### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented lose condition: "All investigators are eliminated (driven insane or killed)."**
   Source: insane/unconscious investigators are NOT eliminated — "He must immediately choose and discard half of his items... moves his investigator to Arkham Asylum. The investigator is restored to 1 Sanity... may take his next turn as normal" (same for St. Mary's Hospital). Devoured investigators before the final battle "draw a new investigator at random" and keep playing. The only stated defeat: "If the Ancient One awakens and defeats all of the investigators in battle... all players lose the game."

2. **MAJOR — Invented lose condition: "The terror track reaches 10 and the town is overrun."**
   Source: terror 10 is not a loss — "If the terror level reaches 10, the town of Arkham is overrun by monsters and the monster limit is completely removed from the game." Play continues.

3. **MAJOR — Wrong awakening condition: "Too many monsters are on the board (monster limit exceeded)."**
   Source: exceeding the monster limit just routes monsters to the Outskirts ("the monster is placed in the Outskirts area instead"). The related awakening condition is much narrower: "the terror level has reached 10 and there are monsters in play equal to twice the normal monster limit." Summary also omits two of the five awakening conditions (no gate markers left; no monsters left in the cup).

4. **MAJOR — Gate-closing victory condition incomplete: "Close all open gates when no gates remain."**
   Source requires a second condition: "When the last gate is closed, the players must have a number of gate trophies equal to or greater than the number of players." Players following the summary would think closing the last gate alone wins.

5. **MINOR — Combat contradiction: "Successes deal damage; failures let the monster damage you."**
   Source: damage is not incremental against monsters — "If a monster's toughness is greater than 1, partial success has no effect on it. A monster must be completely defeated in one Combat check or it ignores all successes." (Cumulative successes apply only in the final battle vs. the Ancient One.)

6. **MINOR — Setup: "Place initial clue tokens and gate tokens as indicated by setup cards."**
   No "setup cards" exist. Source: place 1 Clue on each of the 11 unstable (red diamond) locations; the first gate/monster come from resolving the initial Mythos card. Summary's separate steps 5-6 do cover the Mythos card, so this is wording-level.

7. **MINOR — Omission:** post-game scoring system (doom track number minus terror level, ±modifiers for loans/elder signs/trophies/survivors) is omitted from Scoring; cosmetic since it only rates the win.

**Verdict rationale:** Win/lose structure is the heart of the Scoring section and the summary gets two lose conditions and one awakening condition wrong, plus omits half a victory condition. These would directly mislead at the table.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Move action wrong: "Move to an adjacent connected space."**
   Source: "Move your investigator token up to two spaces... For each dollar you spend, you move one additional space, up to a maximum of two additional spaces." The summary cuts base movement in half and drops paid movement entirely — a core action every turn.

2. **MAJOR — Research action mischaracterized: "Attempt to discover a clue at your space (test observation)."**
   Source: research does not discover clues — "For each success you roll, you may place one of your clues on the scenario sheet." Clues are gathered via encounters; research converts held clues into scenario progress. As written, the summary inverts the game's central clue economy.

3. **MAJOR — Invented test mechanic: "Compare successes to the test's difficulty."**
   Source: there is no difficulty comparison in 3rd edition — "If you roll at least one success, you pass the test... The total number of successes you rolled is called your test result." This appears to be carried over from 2nd edition.

4. **MINOR/MAJOR — Focus token effect wrong: "Spent during skill tests to add +1 to the result per token spent."**
   Source: "Focus tokens can be spent while resolving a test to reroll dice. For each focus token you spend, reroll one die." (Holding the token also gives +1 to that skill's value — different from adding successes.)

5. **MINOR — Defeat consequence wrong: "Your investigator is eliminated from the game."**
   Source: the player continues — "place one doom on the scenario sheet... choose a new investigator that has not been played yet during this game... place your investigator token at the starting space." The investigator card is gone, but stating elimination without the replacement rule misleads.

6. **MINOR — Invented skill: Evade "test observation or agility."**
   Source: evade is an observation test modified by the monster's evade modifier; no "agility" skill exists in this game (skills shown: will, strength, observation, influence, lore).

7. **MINOR — Anomalies "spawn monsters and spread doom."**
   Source: anomalies are placed by doom (3 doom on a space / 5 in a neighborhood) and redirect future doom to the scenario sheet; nothing about spawning monsters. Removal is by clearing all doom from the neighborhood (ward/anomaly encounters), not "specific scenario actions."

8. **MINOR — Ward understated: "remove a doom token" — source: remove doom equal to test result; removing 2+ also grants a remnant.**

**Verdict rationale:** Component counts are flawless, but three core mechanics (move distance, research, test resolution) are wrong or invented, plus several smaller rule errors. A table playing from this summary would play key actions incorrectly.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — Player Reference damage-track table conflicts with setup.**
   Summary table: "+2 VP | Nothing | +Marauder | -1 VP | -2 VP | -Purple | ..." places "-1 VP" as the 4th space. But setup (in both summary and source) says the marker starts "with the explosion side up on the 3rd space (-1)" — i.e., the 3rd space is the -1 space. The full track layout is not enumerated in the extracted text (it is on the board image), so the table's exact ordering is unsupported and internally inconsistent with the summary's own setup step.

2. **MINOR — Omitted timing rule:** "New survivors just gained by exploration MAY NOT be used during this game round" (source, City resolution). The summary never states this; players could illegally use freshly gained survivors for city actions.

3. **Trivial — Omission:** during Exploration, if the marauder-track marker is already on the last space, the player loses 1 VP per leftover marauder (source: "If the marker is on the last space, the player loses 1 victory point for each leftover marauder"). The summary covers the analogous last-space rule only in Completion.

4. **Trivial — Omission:** final tie-breaker after equipment cards is shared victory ("If the tie still persists players share the victory").

**Verdict rationale:** All checked numbers and virtually all mechanics are faithful. The reference-table ordering and the missing exploration-survivor timing rule are small, table-level slips — MINOR.
