## mosaic-a-story-of-civilization

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Prefilter
OK — extracted file present.

### Step 1 — Summary claims noted
- 9 actions listed (Build City, Develop Technology, Recruit, Construct Wonder, Increase Population, Produce, Tax/Tribute, Take a Leader, Establish Government)
- "The game ends after a set number of rounds or when a trigger condition is met."
- Scoring table includes: Cities/territory, Technologies, Wonders, Military achievements, Population, Golden Age bonuses, Government bonuses
- "Region control is determined by majority of military units."

### Step 2 — Source survey
Source is 58 KB (~1610 lines). Read first 200 lines, then grepped for all key sections.

### Step 3 — Verification

**FINDING 1 (MAJOR) — Invented action: "Take a Leader"**
- Summary: Lists "Take a Leader — Recruit a leader for bonuses." as one of 9 game actions.
- Source (lines 396-408): "On a player's turn, they may perform one of the following eight Actions: 1) WORK 2) POPULATION 3) BUILD 4) WONDER 5) TECHNOLOGY 6) TAX & TARIFF 7) MILITARY 8) GOVERNMENT"
- Leaders are chosen during setup (source lines 22-26: "Each Player will select a Unique Leader that has a special power and starting assets"). There is NO "Take a Leader" action in the game.
- NO SUPPORT FOUND (searched: "Leader action", "Take a Leader", "Recruit Leader", "leader.*action").
- VERDICT: MAJOR — invents a game action that does not exist and misrepresents the action count (9 vs 8).

**FINDING 2 (MAJOR) — Wrong action count and action descriptions**
- Summary describes 9 actions; source states exactly 8 actions.
- Summary lists "Recruit" as a separate action and "Establish Government" as another, which roughly correspond to the source's MILITARY (recruit + move) and GOVERNMENT actions.
- Summary does not list the WORK action (Produce Stone, Food, or Ideas) as a distinct action — instead has "Produce" which partially overlaps but also conflates distinct mechanics.
- The MILITARY action covers both recruitment AND movement of military units; the summary splits these improperly.
- Source: "7) MILITARY: Recruit new Military Units and/or move Military Units"
- Summary: "3. Recruit — Add military units to your forces." (separate from movement, which is not listed as an action at all)

**FINDING 3 (MAJOR) — Invented end-game scoring sources: Military achievements and Population**
- Summary (scoring table): "Military achievements: Based on controlled regions" and "Population: Based on total" listed as end-game VP sources.
- Source END GAME SCORING section (lines 882-910): End-game VP sources are CITIES AND TOWNS, WONDERS, GOLDEN AGE AND CIVILIZATION ACHIEVEMENT TILES, PROJECT CARDS, TECHNOLOGY CARDS, MANUFACTORY TOWN CARDS, and UNREST deduction.
- Military does NOT appear in the end-game scoring section. Military units contribute to Influence during Empire Scoring events (mid-game), not at end-game.
- Population does NOT appear in the end-game scoring section. Population drives the WORK action efficiency but is not a VP source at game end.
- NO SUPPORT FOUND for these as end-game scoring sources (searched: "Military.*VP end", "Population.*VP end", "end game.*military", "end game.*population").
- VERDICT: MAJOR — a player using only this summary would incorrectly believe they score VP for military and population at game end.

**FINDING 4 (MAJOR) — Wrong game-end trigger**
- Summary: "The game ends after a set number of rounds or when a trigger condition is met."
- Source (lines 864-878): "Play continues around the table until EITHER: 1) The third Empire Scoring Card is revealed, or 2) 2 of 3 of the following tiles have all been taken by the players: Wonders / Civilization Achievements / Golden Ages. When either occurs, perform Empire Scoring, then finish the current Round up to but not including the starting player, and then one more full Round."
- "A set number of rounds" is incorrect — the game has no fixed round count. It ends on an event-triggered basis.
- VERDICT: MAJOR — players could not determine when the game ends from the summary.

**FINDING 5 (MAJOR) — Wrong definition of "region control"**
- Summary: "Region control is determined by majority of military units."
- Source (lines 833-836): "Most Influence is determined by counting 2 Influence points for each Wonder and City that you own in that region, and 1 Influence [point for each Military Unit]."
- Control/majority is determined by TOTAL INFLUENCE, where Cities and Wonders count for 2 Influence each and Military Units count for 1 each. Military units alone do not determine control.
- Source (line 762-763): "Military Units each count as 1 point of 'Influence' in a region for purposes of majority during Empire Scoring."
- VERDICT: MAJOR — a player who dominates a region with many Military Units but fewer Cities/Wonders than opponents would incorrectly believe they control it.

**Procedural claims verified:**
- "On your turn, perform exactly 1 action" → Source line 394: "perform ONE Action" ✓
- "Technologies may have prerequisites from other tech branches" → Source confirms prerequisites ✓
- "Government type determines tax rate, military capacity, special abilities" → Source lines 769-813 confirm various government types with different bonuses ✓
- "Golden Age tiles reward players who achieve specific milestones" → Source lines 814-823 confirm ✓

**Tiebreaker:**
- Summary: "The player with the most VP wins." (no tiebreaker mentioned)
- Source (lines 907-910): "In the case of a tie, the tied player with the most Wonders is the winner. If there is still a tie, the tied player with the most Money is the winner."
- MINOR omission — tiebreaker not mentioned, but this is secondary to the MAJOR errors above.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (8 actions, 3 Empire Scoring Cards, 2/3 tile threshold, 2 VP/City, 1 VP/Town), SUPPORTED: 4 (actions count wrong; remainder verified)

**Exhaustion rules:** Population deck and Tax & Tariff deck can be exhausted. Source (line 458-460): "Once all Population Cards have been drawn and the deck is used up, all future Population Actions gain 2 Population and cost 15 Food." Summary does not mention this. Minor omission compared to the major errors above.

### Summary of MAJOR findings
1. Invented 9th action "Take a Leader" (leaders are a setup step, not a game action)
2. "Military achievements" listed as end-game scoring source — not in end-game scoring
3. "Population" listed as end-game scoring source — not in end-game scoring
4. Game-end trigger described as "set number of rounds" — actually event-triggered
5. "Region control determined by majority of military units" — wrong; control uses weighted influence (Cities/Wonders = 2 each, Military = 1 each)

Verdict: **MAJOR**
