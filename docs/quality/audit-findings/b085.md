## eldritch-horror

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Victory / Game-End Conditions

**Claim (summary):** "Investigators Lose: The Doom track reaches 0: the Ancient One awakens. Some Ancient Ones have a final battle; others end the game immediately (e.g., Azathoth devours the world). A Mythos card effect specifically ends the game."

**Source (lines 1171–1180):**
> "Investigators lose the game if any of the following occur:
> ^ If the Doom token reaches the '0' space of the Doom track, the Ancient One awakens. The back of the Ancient One sheet lists how investigators can lose the game.
> ^ All investigators have been eliminated from the game.
> ^ A card effect causes investigators to lose the game.
> ^ The investigators are required to draw a Mythos card but cannot because the Mythos deck is empty."

**Finding:** The summary omits two lose conditions: (a) "all investigators eliminated" and (b) "Mythos deck is empty and investigators must draw." These are real lose conditions players would miss.

**Claim (summary):** "Another investigator may take their place from the remaining pool."

**Source (lines 1379–1383):** "If an investigator is defeated, the player controlling that investigator chooses a new investigator at the end of the Mythos Phase."

**Finding:** The summary uses "eliminated" for investigator defeat, but "eliminated" in the source specifically means a harder permanent state that occurs only after the Ancient One awakens (line 1610–1613). Pre-awakening defeat is "defeated," not "eliminated." This is slightly misleading but players can still choose a new investigator.

**Claim (summary):** "If Health or Sanity reaches 0, the investigator is eliminated."

**Source (lines 1358–1367):** "When an investigator has zero Health or Sanity, he is immediately defeated and resolves the following steps: 1. Advance Doom: Advance Doom by 1. 2. Relocation..."

**Finding:** The summary omits that defeat causes Doom to advance by 1. This is a significant gameplay consequence missing from the summary, but since it does not affect how players score or the win condition itself, it is a MINOR omission.

**Win condition verified:** Source line 1163: "Investigators immediately win the game if they solve three of the Ancient One's Mysteries." Summary: "Solve 3 Mysteries." Supported.

### Procedural claims verified

- Action Phase — "each investigator performs up to 2 different actions" — Source line 783: "each investigator resolves up to two actions." Supported.
- Actions listed (Travel, Rest, Trade, Prepare for Travel, Acquire Assets, Component Actions) — Source lines 794–799 lists all six. Supported.
- Encounter Phase: resolve combat against each Monster, then location/token encounter — Source lines 921–933. Supported.
- Mythos Phase: Lead Investigator draws top Mythos card, advances Omen, resolves effects — Source lines 1035–1096. Supported; summary summarizes multiple Mythos card elements as single "resolve effects" step, which is acceptable.
- Combat: Will test (resist Horror), Strength test (deal damage) — Source lines 1456–1474. Supported.
- Tests: 5+ = success, Clue = reroll 1 die — Source lines 1212–1238. Supported.

### Numbers verified

- Improvement tokens add +1 die to a skill — Source lines 1244–1248: "+1" side up, "+2" if improved again. Summary says "+1 die" only. Minor (omits +2 possibility after second improvement).
- Travel tickets: max 2 — Source line 829: "If a player ever has more than two travel tickets, he must choose two to keep." Supported.
- 3 Mysteries to win — Supported.
- 12 Investigator sheets — Source line 222. Supported.

**NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8, SUPPORTED: 7**

**Summary of findings:** Two lose conditions omitted (Mythos deck empty; all investigators eliminated), Doom advance on defeat omitted. These are MINOR omissions that would not cause players to misapply rules at the table in most situations, but they do create incomplete information about when the game ends.

## elfenland

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Transportation Requirements Table

**Claim (summary), Magic Cloud row:**
> `| Magic Cloud | 2 cards | 1 card | -- | 1 card |`
> (Plains=2, Woods=1, Desert=cannot, Mountains=1)

**Source (lines 105–106):**
> "The Magic Cloud cannot travel through the desert and requires 2 Travel Cards for the plains and woods."

**Finding:** The summary shows Magic Cloud costs only 1 card in Woods, but the source explicitly states 2 Travel Cards are required for both plains AND woods. The source example (lines 390–393) confirms this: "On the road in the woods from Dag'Amura to Kihromah the type of transportation is a Magic Cloud. Therefore, Chris must play four Magic Cloud Travel Cards to travel from Dag'Amura to Kihromah and back." (4 cards for a round trip = 2 cards per road.) This is a direct contradiction.

**Impact:** Players using the Magic Cloud on a woods road would play 1 card (per the summary) when they should play 2. This changes every game that involves Magic Cloud movement through woods — MAJOR.

### Other transport rows verified

- Giant Pig: Plains=1, Woods=1, Desert=cannot, Mountains=cannot — Source lines 101–102: "cannot travel through the desert or in the mountains." Summary correctly marks Desert and Mountains as "--". Plains and woods cost 1 each — source doesn't give explicit cost for allowed terrains other than via implication (no "2 cards" mentioned). Supported.
- Elfcycle: Plains=1, Woods=1, Desert=cannot, Mountains=2 — Source lines 103–104: "cannot be used in the desert and requires 2 Travel Cards for the mountains." Summary: Desert=--, Mountains=2. Supported.
- Unicorn: Plains=cannot, Woods=1, Desert=2, Mountains=1 — Source lines 107–108: "cannot travel through the plains and requires 2 Travel Cards for the desert." Summary: Plains=--,Desert=2. Supported.
- Troll Wagon: Plains=1, Woods=2, Desert=2, Mountains=2 — Source lines 109–110: "requires 2 Travel Cards in the woods, in the desert, and in the mountains." Summary matches. Supported.
- Dragon: Plains=1, Woods=2, Desert=1, Mountains=1 — Source lines 111–112: "requires 2 Travel Cards to fly over the woods." Summary: Woods=2. Supported.

**Source (lines 451–454):** "Elfenland ends after the fourth round. The player who collected the most Town Pieces wins. If two or more players have the same number of Town Pieces, the player holding the most Travel Cards in his hands wins." Supported.

**Claim (summary):** "Early victory: If a player collects all 20 Town Pieces before the end of round 3, they win immediately."
**Source (lines 455–456):** "If one player collects all of his Town Pieces before the end of the third round, he wins." Supported.

- End of round: return all but one counter — Source lines 418–420. Supported.

## eminent-domain

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Research Action Description

**Claim (summary):** "**Action:** Remove 1 card in your hand/discard from the game."

**Source (lines 233–235):**
> "Remove up to 2 cards in your hand from the game. This may include the Research card you are playing. Put removed cards back in the box; they will not be used for the remainder of the game."

**Finding 1:** The summary says "1 card" but the source says "up to 2 cards." This changes how many cards can be culled in a single Research Action — a meaningful deck-building decision.

**Finding 2:** The summary says "in your hand/discard" but the source only says "in your hand" (which may include the Research card being played). Cards in the discard pile cannot be removed via the Research Action. Players using the summary would believe they can target discard pile cards, which is not supported.

**Impact:** Both errors would cause players to misapply the Research Action every time it is used. Deck-thinning is the core mechanic of the Research role, so misrepresenting it is MAJOR.

| Players | Trigger |
|---------|---------|

| 3 standard | 1 stack depleted |

> GAME END TRIGGER
> 2 player standard: 1 stack depleted
> 3 player standard: 1 stack depleted
> 3 player extended *: 2 stacks depleted
> 4 player standard: 2 stacks depleted

Supported (the summary correctly omits the extended variant row, which is fine as it's a variant).

Source: "Look at one fewer Planet cards than the number of [survey symbols] played... choose 1 to add (face down) to your Empire." Supported. **Colonize Action** — "Tuck 1 Colonize card as Colony OR Settle 1 planet." Source lines 189–208. Supported. "Planets cannot be Settled when Following." Source line 206: "Planets cannot be Settled when Following a Colonize role." Supported.

**Produce Action** — "Produce 1 resource on any face-up planet with empty resource slot." Source line 212. Supported.

**Trade Action** — "Trade 1 resource for 1 Influence." Source line 223. Supported.

**Politics Action** — "Remove the Politics card from the game. Take 1 Role card from any stack and add it to your hand." Source lines 156–159. Supported.

**Exhaustion/Reshuffle** — "When deck is empty, shuffle discard pile into a new deck." Source line 80: "Whenever you must draw a card and your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile to create a new deck." Supported.

### Starting Deck

**Source (lines 26–31):** Matches exactly. Supported.

## empire-builder

**Verdict: PASS**

### Victory / Win Condition

**Claim (summary):** "Be the first player whose railroad connects five of the six major cities AND who has at least $250 million in cash. Both conditions must be met simultaneously."
**Source (lines 677–679):** "The winner is the first player whose continuous line of track connects five of the major cities and who has $250 million cash." Supported. "Only cash is counted; the money spent on building railroads does not count toward winning." Supported.

### Building Costs

**Claim (summary):**
- Clear milepost: $1 million
- Mountain milepost: $2 million
- Small city: $3 million
- Medium city: $3 million
- Major city: $5 million
- River: $2 million extra
- Ocean inlet: additional cost (not specified in summary)

**Source (lines 578–584):**
> "Clear: $1 million, Mountain: $2 million, Small City: $3 million, Medium City: $3 million, Major City: $5 million, River: $2 million add'l, Ocean Inlet: $3 million add'l"

**Finding:** The summary says "Ocean inlet: additional cost" without specifying $3 million extra. The source clearly states $3 million additional. This omission means players can't correctly calculate costs near ocean inlets. However, this is in the "Additional costs" section and the note is present at least — MINOR omission only.

All other cost figures match. Supported.

### Train Stats

**Claim (summary):** "Speed 9 mileposts per turn, carries up to 2 loads."
**Source (lines 117–119):** "a freight, which goes at a speed of 9 mileposts per turn and carries up to 2 loads at a time." Supported.

**Claim (summary):** Using another player's track costs "$4 million per turn to each player whose track you use."
**Source (lines 552–554):** "A player may run on his opponent's track, but must pay $4 million per turn to each opponent on whose track he has run during his turn." Supported.

### City Access Limits

**Claim (summary):** "Only 2 players can build into a small city; only 3 into a medium city."
**Source (lines 86–87):** "only two players can build into a small city (and only three players can build into a medium city)." Supported.

### Turn Structure

**Claim (summary):** "Build track (spend up to $20 million per turn) ... Move train (up to train's speed) ... Draw a card when you deliver a load."
**Source (lines 429–433):** "each player can: [run train, operate — loading, unloading, movement]; [build] spend up to $20 million per turn either to build track or to upgrade his train." Supported.

**Claim (summary):** "Play 2 rounds of turns for initial track building (no train movement)."
**Source (lines 441–444):** "players take two turns with no train movement to start building their rail empires." Supported.

### Setup

**Claim (summary):** "Each player receives $40 million, a pawn, a crayon of matching color, and 1 Loco card."
**Source (line 399–401):** "he takes the money and gives each player $40 million to start the game...Loco cards." Source lines 244–245: "Give every player $40 million and a pawn and crayon of the same color. Every player gets a Loco card." Supported.

**Claim (summary):** "Deal 3 cards to each player. Return any Event cards to the dealer and draw replacements until each player has 3 Demand cards."
**Source (lines 239–241):** "Deal 3 cards to each player. Return all the Event cards to the dealer and get new cards, until every player has three Demand cards to start with." Supported.

### Exhaustion / Reshuffle

**Claim (summary):** No explicit reshuffle rule stated.
**Source (line 413–415):** "When the deck becomes exhausted during play, the dealer reshuffles the discard pile to form a new deck." The summary does not mention reshuffling. This is a MINOR omission — the game does reshuffle, but it doesn't affect scoring or core mechanics.
