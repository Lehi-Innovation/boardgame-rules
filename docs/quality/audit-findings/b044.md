## bunny-bunny-moose-moose — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the extracted source is not a rulebook. It is a scrape of the Czech Games Edition product web page (navigation menus, shop links, complaint policy, Space Alert mission generator text). It contains only a flavor description and a components list. Every gameplay claim in the summary is therefore unsupported by the source.

### Numbers checked (8 — all the summary's verifiable numbers)
| # | Summary | Source | Status |
|---|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | 6 player cards | "6 player cards (1 in each color)" | Supported |
| 2 | 15 trail cards | "15 trail cards" | Supported |
| 3 | 111 scoring cards | "111 scoring cards" | Supported |
| 4 | 12 wooden figures (1 rabbit + 1 moose per color) | "12 wooden figures (1 rabbit and 1 moose in each color)" | Supported |
| 5 | 1 poem sheet, 1 rulebook | identical | Supported |
| 6 | 3-6 players | "3-6" | Supported |
| 7 | 20 min | "20 min" | Supported |
| 8 | Figures start on the **fifth** trail card; poem has **6** lines; narrator rotates **2-3** times | nothing in source | **Unsupported** |

### Findings
1. **Entire Setup / Turn Structure / Actions / Scoring sections have no basis in the source.** Examples with no source support: "All rabbit and moose figures start on the fifth trail card", "Cards are laid in six piles (one per line)", the scoring-card-type table (multiplier x2, reversal card, tongue card), "the last rabbit figure(s) and last moose figure(s) on the trail each move forward one space", bush penalty ("move both of their figures back one space"), "game continues until each player has narrated 2-3 times", victory by "rear figure". The source contains zero gameplay text — only the marketing blurb ("players try to look like an animal the hunter won't shoot... making rabbit ears or moose antlers").
2. This is an extraction failure (wrong document downloaded/extracted), so the summary content cannot have come from this source and cannot be verified against it.

## bunny-kingdom — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — component counts and harvest scoring are accurate, but the Construction Phase is misdescribed with an invented "verify" step, and several real construction rules (deferral, Camp priority interrupts, one-building-per-territory) plus the 2-player draft procedure are missing — these would mislead at the table.

| 3 | 24 Building tokens (12 Farms, 6 Sky Towers in 3 pairs, 6 Camps) | identical | Supported |

### Findings
1. **Construction Phase misdescribed / invented step.** Summary: "Phase 2: Construction — After all cards are played, verify that all Buildings and Rabbits are correctly placed." Source: "During this phase, you can place your Buildings on the New World board... You are never required to place a Building. You can leave them in front of you to place later." There is no "verify" step; the actual phase content (optional board placement, deferral across rounds) is replaced. The summary's Actions section instead has buildings placed at card-play time, which contradicts the source's two-step play-then-construct flow.
2. **Camp priority system omitted (invented simplification).** Summary: "Place a Camp token and a Rabbit on any empty (uncontrolled) Territory." Source adds a whole interactive mechanic: "Each Camp has a priority number... you must announce its priority number... Any player who has a Camp with a lower priority number can interrupt you and play their Camp before you."
3. **One-building-per-territory and no-moving rules omitted.** Source: "A Building cannot be moved once it has been placed" and "There can never be more than one Building (City or token) on a Territory." Neither appears in the summary; players could illegally stack buildings.
4. **2-player game procedure omitted while implying normal play.** Source: 2p uses a 10-card Reserve, draws 1 Reserve card before each pick, and "instead of choosing 2 cards to play, choose 1 card that you will play and 1 that you will discard." Summary lists "2 players: 10 cards each" under normal dealing and keeps the standard "select 2 cards" draft — contradicting the source for 2p.
5. Minor omissions: playing a Territory card on a Camp removes the Camp ("remove their Rabbit and the Camp token before placing your own"); Setup omits "Place one Strength 1 City on each City space on the board" (though neutral cities are mentioned); tie rule ("tied players share the victory") omitted; Farm breakdown (3 Basic, 2 Trading Posts, 7 Luxury) omitted.

## burg-appenzell — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — core numbers match, but the summary adds multiple rules with no basis in the source (tower entry ban, safe elevated spaces, 5/6-cheese variant, an elaborate end-game winner-determination procedure, invented component counts), and omits the initial mouse placement in a corner tower that the source states explicitly.

### Numbers checked (10)
| # | Summary | Source | Status |
|---|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | 34 sliding tiles | "34 sliding tiles" | Supported |
| 2 | 21 with cheese (3 of each of 7 kinds) | "21 with cheese (3 of each kind)"; 7 kinds via collecting tiles | Supported |
| 3 | 10 without cheese | "10 without cheese" | Supported |
| 4 | 3 with mousetrap and hole | "3 with mousetrap and a hole" | Supported |
| 5 | 4 tower battlements | "4 foldable tower battlements" | Supported |
| 6 | 25 collecting tiles, 7 kinds of cheese | "25 collecting tiles... 7 different kinds of cheese" | Supported (but see finding 4) |
| 7 | 16 mice in 4 colors, 4 per player | "16 mice in 4 colors... all 4 mice of one color" | Supported |
| 8 | 5x5 grid, 12 slide slots | "grid board (with 5x5 holes)", "one of the 12 slots" | Supported |
| 9 | Up to 4 actions; Slide once per turn | "up to 4 actions... Slide action only once per turn" | Supported |
| 10 | Win at 4 different cheeses; rooms of 2/3/4 squares | "collected 4 different kinds of cheese"; "rooms consist of either 2, 3 or 4 squares" | Supported |

### Findings
1. **Setup omission: initial mouse placement.** Source: "Each player puts one of their mice in an unoccupied corner tower." The summary never says this — its setup ends with mice "in front of them", leaving no way to get mice into the chateau.
2. **Invented tower rule (also self-contradictory).** Summary Run action: "Entering into a tower or leaving is not allowed (towers are entry/exit points only at the start)" — yet the same bullet says "(a tower counts as a space)". Neither claim is in the source, and the entry/exit ban conflicts with mice starting in towers.
3. **Invented end-game winner determination.** Summary: when a player's third mouse falls in the dungeon, "the winner among the other players is the one who owns the most kinds of cheese... the player among the tied cheese collectors who has been waiting longest since their last turn wins (excluding a player who has only one mouse left)." Source says only: "The game also ends when a players third mouse plops into the cellar dungeon." The whole tiebreak procedure is fabricated relative to the source.
4. **Invented component details/counts.** "25 collecting tiles (... including 5 'roof' tiles)" and a separate "16 roof tiles" line — the source gives no roof count and no "5 roof tiles" among collecting tiles.
5. **Invented rules without source basis:** "On higher elevated spaces, mice are safe from traps, but there is no cheese there"; the 5/6-cheese long-game variant "recommended for the two or three player games"; "Rooms with a mouse in the cellar dungeon are also considered empty and are covered"; Uncover adjacency defined as "shares a side (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally adjacent)" (source never defines neighboring; the phrasing is also self-contradictory). Some of these may exist in the full printed rulebook, but the extracted source (an abridged transcription) does not support them.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — turn structure, actions, and scoring are faithful, but the player-count tile removal in Setup is wrong: the 2-player removal is attributed to 3 players, and the 2-player removal (worker and jungle tiles, enumerated in the source) is left as "specified tiles". ### Findings
1. **Player-count tile removal misassigned (wrong setup numbers).** Summary: "3 players: Remove 1x 1-1-1-1 and 1x 2-1-0-1 per player; 4 players: No removal; 2 players: Remove specified tiles..." The source's column layout (OCR-flattened) lists three labels (2/3/4 players) followed by items "1x 1-1-1-1", "1x 2-1-0-1", "1x 1-1-1-1", "and", "none" — i.e., 2 players remove 1x 1-1-1-1 and 1x 2-1-0-1; 3 players remove only 1x 1-1-1-1; 4 players none (this also matches the published game). The summary gives 3-player games the 2-player removal (each player loses an extra tile/turn) and leaves the 2-player worker removal unstated.
2. **2-player Jungle tile removal omitted despite being enumerated in the source.** Source: "For the two-player game... put back in the box: 2x single plantation, 1x water, 1x market selling price 3, 1x Sun worshiping site, 1x gold mine value 1, 1x temple." Summary only says "Remove specified tiles... from the Jungle" — unusable at the table.
3. Not penalized (overview sheet not in extraction): the water-track values (-10, -4, -1, 0, +2, +4, +7, +11, +16), max-5 cacao storage, and per-tile action details are on the separate Jungle tile overview sheet, which the extraction does not include; these claims are consistent with the rest of the source and were not counted against the summary.

## caesar-cleopatra — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the summary invents large parts of the game. Setup, turn structure, action cards, and vote resolution all contradict the source; core systems (separate influence/action decks, initial placement, hand refill to 5, vote-card draw each turn, 5/8 card maximums, philosopher cards, Orgy cards, game-end conditions) are missing or replaced with fabricated rules.

### Numbers checked (10)
| # | Summary | Source | Status |
|---|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | 135 cards total | "135 cards" | Supported |
| 2 | 50 Roman / 50 Egyptian: 37 influence + 13 action each | identical | Supported |
| 3 | 35 other: 6 influence bonus, 21 patrician (5 groups), 8 vote of confidence | identical | Supported |
| 4 | Patrician rows: 5 Senators, 5 Praetors, 5 Quaestors, 3 Censors, 3 Aedilis | identical | Supported |
| 5 | Influence bonus: 2 VP for 3+ cards of your group; return 4 to box | "influence bonus of 2 victory points... at least 3 cards"; "Place the 4 unused cards... in the box" | Supported |
| 6 | Vote cards: "Place 1 face-down on each of the 5 patrician groups. Return 3 to box." | "Shuffle the eight vote of confidence cards and place them face-down on the table next to the patrician cards" (a deck; nothing returned) | **Wrong — invented** |
| 7 | Starting hand: 7 cards from a shuffled 50-card pile | Set aside two each of 1-5; place 5 face-down (one per group); keep 5 as starting hand; separate influence deck + ordered action deck | **Wrong — invented** |
| 8 | Hand limit 10, discard excess under draw pile | "she refills her hand to 5 cards" — no 10-card limit exists | **Wrong — invented** |
| 9 | Draw option: "Draw 3 cards" | Passive turn: discard up to 5, refill hand to 5 | **Wrong — invented** |
| 10 | Influence cards numbered 1-5 | "influence cards (with the numbers 1-5 or letter 'P')" | Supported (philosopher "P" omitted from components) |

### Findings
1. **Setup contradicted (vote cards, decks, hand).** See rows 6-7 above. The source has two separate decks per player ("This is her influence deck... her action deck"), an initial placement step ("Each player places 5 of the cards (valued 1 through 5) face-down on her side of the patrician groups, one card to each group"), and a 5-card starting hand. The summary's single shuffled 50-card pile and 7-card hand match nothing in the source.
2. **Turn structure invented.** Source active turn: "1) may play one action card, and 2) must play one or two influence cards [1 face-down OR 2 face-up], then 3) must draw cards to refill her hand [to 5], and 4) must draw the next vote of confidence card." Summary instead offers "Place any number of cards face-up" and an alternative "Draw 3 cards" turn with a 10-card hand limit — all unsupported, and the mandatory vote-of-confidence draw (the game's engine) is absent from the turn.
3. **Action cards largely invented.** Source's 6 types: Assassination (4, removes a **face-up** influence card), Spy (2, "Search your opponent's hand, select one card and remove it"), Castling (2, redistribute two of your groups face-down), Scout (2, "Turn all influence cards from one of your opponent's groups face-up"), Wrath of God (1, clear one group), Veto (2, cancel opponent's action card). Summary's list: Assassin removing "face-down or face-up" cards (wrong), Scout "look at an opponent's face-down card" (wrong), Spy "peek at a vote of confidence card" (wrong), "Worm: force opponent to reveal a face-down card" (no such card), "Philosopher/Diplomat" as action cards (Philosopher is a value-0 influence card; no Diplomat exists). Castling, Wrath of God, and Veto are missing.
4. **Vote resolution contradicted.** Summary: "All influence cards used are discarded." Source: "The winning player discards her highest numbered influence card... The losing player discards her lowest numbered influence card... All remaining influence cards for this group are left face-up and the game continues."
5. **Missing core systems:** the special vote at 8 total cards ("A special vote of confidence is called... a total of 8 cards from both players"); per-side max 5 / per-group max 8 influence cards; philosopher reversal rules (player with the **smallest** sum wins); Orgy cards and reshuffle; game-end conditions (all patricians won / no influence cards / stalemate); tiebreaker ("the player who won the most patrician cards wins"). Summary's "Votes of confidence are triggered when specific conditions are met (varies by group)" is also unsupported — triggers do not vary by group.
6. Minor: majority and whole-group bonuses are each worth 1 VP per the source's example ("9 points (5 for the cards, 1 for the majority, 1 for having all cards in the group, and 2 for the influence bonus)"); the summary leaves them as vague "additional VP"/"a bonus". Bonus cards exist only for Senators/Praetors/Quaestors (2 each) — not noted.

## calico — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — components and basic turn flow are accurate, but the summary contradicts the source on cat scoring (claims each cat can be attracted only once; source scores "2 Millies... 2 Tibbits"), misstates the rainbow button rule, gets the standard setup wrong, and omits the design-goal dual-completion scoring tiers.

### Numbers checked (12)
| # | Summary | Source | Status |
|---|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | 4 dual-layer Quilt Boards | "4 Dual-layer Quilt Boards" | Supported |
| 2 | 108 Patch Tiles (6 sets of 18) | "108 Patch Tiles (6 sets of 18)" | Supported |
| 3 | 6 Black & White Patch Tiles | "6 Black + White Patch Tiles" | Supported |
| 4 | 24 Design Goal Tiles (4 sets of 6) | "24 Design Goal Tiles (4 sets of 6...)" | Supported |
| 5 | 5 double-sided Cat Scoring Tiles | "5 Double-sided Cat Scoring Tiles" | Supported |
| 6 | 80 Cat Tokens; 52 Buttons (8 each color + 4 rainbow) | "80 Cat Tokens"; "52 Button Tokens (8 of each color + 4 rainbow)" | Supported |
| 7 | 6 colors x 6 patterns = 36 combos | "one set of 36 Patch Tiles. A set is each color-pattern combination represented one time" | Supported |
| 8 | Beginner cats Millie/Tibbit/Coconut; 2 B&W tiles per cat tile; hand of 2; market of 3 | all identical in Beginner Setup | Supported |
| 9 | Beginner goals: not-equal, AA-BB-CC, AAA-BBB | "place the ≠, AA-BB-CC, and AAA-BBB Design Goal Tiles" | Supported |
| 10 | Buttons: group of 3+ same color, 3 points per button | "a group of three Patch Tiles or more, of the same color... Each button is worth 3 points" | Supported |
| 11 | Standard: "Use all 6 Cat Scoring Tiles" | "Separate the Cat Scoring Tiles into groups by the number of dots... Randomly select 1 Cat Scoring Tile from each group" (3 of 5 tiles used) | **Wrong** |
| 12 | Standard goals: "choose which 3... from your set of 6" | "shuffles their 6 Design Goal Tiles face down and reveals 4. From the 4 revealed tiles each player chooses 3" | **Wrong** |

### Findings
1. **"Each cat can only be attracted once per board" — contradicted.** Source: "If you wish to attract another cat of the same type, you must create a new separate pattern area that does not touch a previously scored one," and the scoring example credits "2 Millies 6VP / 2 Tibbits 10VP". Players following the summary would forgo repeat cats.
2. **Rainbow button rule wrong.** Summary: "If all 8 buttons of a color are taken, use rainbow buttons instead." Source: "If you sew at least one button of each color onto your quilt, you may take a rainbow button... also worth 3 points." The rainbow button is a one-of-each-color reward, not a substitute supply (the substitute rule in the source is for **cat tokens**, using the back-side cat's tokens).
3. **Standard setup contradicted** (rows 11-12 above): 3 cats chosen by dot groups (1/2/3 dots) with random sides — not "all 6" (only 5 tiles exist); design goals are 3 chosen from 4 randomly revealed — not from all 6.
4. **Design goal dual-completion scoring omitted.** Source: "each Design Goal Tile must be completed using either the colors OR the patterns... You can complete a Design Goal Tile twice (once with colors, once with patterns) and receive only the higher point value" (example: 7 vs 11 points). The summary only says "Point values vary by goal", losing the two-tier color/pattern system.
5. **Invented cat-requirement example.** Summary: "some need a specific arrangement of patterns (e.g., 3 tiles of pattern A and 3 of pattern B adjacent, any colors)". Source: each cat has two preferred patterns and a required group size or shape using **one** of those patterns ("A group of 3 or more of..." etc.); no cat requires a mixed two-pattern arrangement.
6. Minor omissions: color groups of 6+ do not yield a second button ("If you expand your color group to a size of 6 or more tiles, you do not gain another button"); printed edge tiles on the board count toward groups; tiebreakers (most cat tokens, then most buttons, then shared); 22 turns; the Family / Solo / Lower-variance variants are only gestured at.
