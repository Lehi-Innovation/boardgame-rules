| 2 grooves | "so that the Spindle rests in the 2 grooves" (line 249) |

### Findings
1. **Invented rule (draw/replay).** Summary: "If all tops stop spinning simultaneously or are knocked out at the same time, the round is a draw and no points are awarded. Replay the round." — No draw, tie, or replay rule appears anywhere in the source (checked English, French, and Spanish sections). Invented, though it fills a gap rather than contradicting anything.
2. **Invented rule (early launch redo).** Summary: "Players must launch only when the Referee calls 'Go!' — early launches may result in a redo at the Referee's discretion." — Source only says "The Referee calls 'Go!' and all the players pull their Battling Tops Spinners into the Arena" (lines 250-252). No penalty/redo rule exists.
3. Trivial: setup step "places it at the start of their scoring corner" is an elaboration; source only says each player chooses a score peg (scoring corner does exist on the arena).

Turn structure, launch procedure, and scoring otherwise track the source closely.

## belfort — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — component counts and setup are accurate, but the summary contains multiple wrong numbers and contradicted rules in the Actions and Collection phases plus an invented scoring claim and a wrong tiebreaker. These would mislead players at the table.

### Numbers checked (component/setup numbers all supported)
| Claim in summary | Source evidence |
|---|---|
| 5 districts, pentagon + scoring track | lines 105, 241-243 |
| 50 property cards (10 types, 5 each) | lines 140, 894-895 |
| 12 guild tiles; Basic/Resource/Interactive | lines 238, 194-195 |
| 35 Dwarves / 35 Elves (7 per color, 5 Master backs) | lines 213-222 |
| 22 Gnomes | line 223 |
| 60 property markers (12/player), 5 scoring markers | lines 189-190, 204 |
| 46 gold, 6 multiplier chips, 30 wood, 30 stone, 20 metal | lines 179-187 |
| Start: 3 Elves, 3 Dwarves, 1W/1S/1M/5G, deal 5 keep 3 | lines 262-270 |
| Guild setup Beginner 3R+2B / Normal 1R+2B+2I | lines 250-251 |
| Gnomes 9/14/18/22 for 2/3/4/5 players | line 257 |
| 7 rounds, 5 phases, 3 scoring rounds | lines 349-350, 814-817 |
| Recruiter's Desk: 2 Gold; 1/2/3 planks for 2-3/4/5 players | lines 332-337 |
| Guild fee 1 Gold (supply if unowned / owner if owned / free if yours) | lines 455-459 |
| Collection table (1 Wood/Elf, 1 Stone/Dwarf, 1 Metal/pair, 1 Gold/worker; +1 bonus, no ties) | lines 473-520 |

### Findings
1. **Wrong number — Gnome hire cost.** Summary (Actions): "Hire a Gnome — Pay 1 Gold to place a Gnome from the supply onto one of your built properties." Source: "Once per turn a player may pay **3 Gold** to the supply, take a Gnome from the stockpile, and place it on an unoccupied Gnome Lock" (lines 719-724). Cost is 3 Gold, not 1; the once-per-turn limit is also omitted.
2. **Contradicted rule — Income.** Summary: "Income: Collect 1 Gold per Gnome assigned to your buildings." Source: "each player collects 1 Gold from the supply for each **Income Coin** showing on their built Property cards" (lines 592-593). Income comes from Income Coins on properties, not from Gnomes.
3. **Contradicted structure — Actions phase.** Summary: "players take turns performing one action each cycle. Continue until all players pass." Source: "First, the player with Crest 1 completes **all of the Actions** he wants to... followed by the player with Crest 2, and so on until all players have taken their turn" (lines 616-621). Each player takes one full turn of unlimited actions; there is no one-action-per-cycle round-robin and no passing loop.
4. **Invented scoring claim + omitted values.** Summary: "Points increase in later scoring rounds." Source gives fixed values for every scoring round: district majority 5/3/1 points (3rd place only in 4-5p games, lines 818-821); worker majority 3/1 points (lines 776-779). No escalation exists. The summary also omits all actual point values and the tie rule (tied players bump down to the next rank, lines 780-785, 822-825).
5. **Wrong tiebreaker.** Summary: "Tiebreaker: Most Gold remaining." Source: "the tied player with the most resources (Wood, Stone, Metal and Gold) wins; Metal counts as 2 resources... If there is still a tie, then the tied players share the victory" (lines 888-890).
6. **Omitted actions.** Build Walls (3 Wood + 3 Stone, no card needed, lines 670-676) and Build Guilds (pay tile cost to own the Guild, lines 685-691) are absent from the Actions section, even though Walls/Guild markers count toward district majorities (line 819) and guild ownership is referenced in the fee rules.
7. **Invented detail.** "Build a Property... Draw a replacement property card" — building grants no card draw; cards are only acquired by paying 1 Gold (once per turn, as the last action, lines 736-747). The 1-Gold cost and last-action restriction are also missing from the summary's "Recruit from Property Cards" action, and the 5-card hand limit (lines 727-728) is omitted.
8. **Misstated rule — Master worker bonus.** Summary: "Master Workers count as 2 for collection bonuses." Source: Masters count as 2 "for collection purposes only" and "only count as 1 Worker each when determining who wins the bonus" (lines 524-532). Also "Upgrade a Worker" is presented as a freestanding action; upgrading actually occurs when a Gnome is placed on a built Gardens/Pub (lines 901-914).
9. Minor: Mine bonus goes to "most Workers in the Mine" (lines 504-505), not "most pairs"; unpaid taxes cost 1 point per unpaid Gold (lines 597-599) — omitted.

## berlin-85-the-enemy-at-the-gates — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR** — components, sequence, victory, and all spot-checked numbers are correct; several secondary subsystems are misdescribed or omitted, but none distort the core play loop.

### Numbers checked (12+ supported)
| Claim in summary | Source evidence |
|---|---|
| 22" x 32" map | "The 22\" x 32\" game map" (line 195) |
| 200 die-cut playing pieces | "one sheet of 200 die-cut playing pieces" (lines 192-193) |

| Up to 16 Game-Turns | "the scenario is 16 Game-Turns long" (lines 2121, 2186); Medicine Wheel runs GT7-16 (lines 2259-2261) |
| Victory at end of GT16 or when Pact controls city | lines 186-189, 452-455 |
| Untried units; artillery has no untried side | lines 221-228, 1402-1414 |
| Every 3rd turn is night | "Every third Game-Turn is a night Game-Turn" (lines 390-391) |
| Three scenarios: Operation Unity / Werewolf / Medicine Wheel | lines 2040, 2131, 2204 |
| NATO emergency reinforcements from a specified turn | "Beginning with Game-Turn 2" (line 1652) |
| Designer James F. Dunnigan; SPI 1980 | "Designer: JamH F. Llunnill:lln" [OCR] (line 2389); "Copyright 1980, Simulations Publications, Inc." (line 2) |
| Honors of War can force NATO surrender | C/S results force surrender on die rolls (lines 1850-1865) |

(S&T issue "#79" is not stated in the extracted text — external metadata, not contradicted.)

### Findings
1. **Misstated rule — firestorm spread.** Summary: "Firestorms spread through connected urban hexes." Source: fires originate only when POL hexes suffer collateral damage (10.71, lines 1365-1369) and spread only to adjacent **POL** hexes not separated by a water hexside (10.74, lines 1393-1398) — not through urban hexes generally.
2. **Misstated rule — air superiority.** Summary: "Air Superiority is contested each turn" and air support is "subject to restrictions and anti-air fire." Source: the Warsaw Pact has air superiority by default; NATO gains it only on a Reinforcement Table roll of 11-12, for one Game-Turn (lines 1249-1258). No anti-air fire rule exists anywhere in the source ("anti-air"/"flak" — zero matches); that detail is invented.
3. **Drift — Honors of War trigger.** Summary: "Crossing the threshold on the Surrender Points Record Track triggers the 'Honors of War' mechanic." Source: it is offered at the Warsaw Pact player's option, once per turn after Turn 1, and only when at least +6 Surrender Points are accumulated (lines 1822-1830, 1871-1873, 1928-1930); each non-surrender offer costs the Pact 20 VPs (lines 1876-1879, omitted).
4. **Omission — supply (14.0).** Unsupplied units have halved movement (lines 546-550) and suffer a 2-column combat shift (lines 1593-1597); supply tracing/sources are a core system never mentioned in the summary. The "They Shall Not Pass" defender option (line 490) is also omitted.
5. **Scenario characterization drift.** Werewolf is described as a "limited assault"; it is actually the same Pact assault with the NATO 10th Jagdbrigade secretly pre-deployed in the city (lines 2154-2173). "Medicine Wheel (NATO counteroffensive)" is acceptable (a NATO mass raid out of Berlin, lines 2209-2220).
6. **Minor sequencing drift.** Source specifies the Warsaw Pact player-turn always comes first (lines 457-459); summary says generic "First Player-Turn." Emergency reinforcements are checked at the start of a Game-Turn before the Pact Movement Phase, after any Honors of War offer (lines 1665-1667, 1822-1823), not in an end-of-turn interphase. Functionally near-equivalent.
7. Supported elsewhere: ZOC lock-in ("only three ways to exit a hex in an Enemy Zone of Control," lines 643-647) matches the summary's "units cannot leave enemy ZOC during movement"; CRT results include retreats, exchanges (E1), and eliminations (lines 808-835); artillery Barrage/Combined/Adjacent/FPF and unit integrity match TOC 8.1-8.7; paratroop scatter/survival/first-turn restrictions match 18.x; poison gas is optional, Pact-artillery-only (lines 1131-1191); U-Bahn rules match 15.0 (trivial drift: it runs under both East and West Berlin, and both sides may use it).
