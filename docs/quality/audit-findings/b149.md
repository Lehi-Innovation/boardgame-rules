## mountains-of-madness

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Wrong component count for Equipment cards

- Summary: "30 Equipment cards (5 types)"
- Source (line 16): "48 Equipment cards"
- The summary understates the equipment deck by 18 cards.

### Finding 2 — Win condition wrong: no "reach the City" requirement; wrong injury threshold

- Summary: "**Win**: Successfully navigate all encounters, reach the City at the top, and escape with enough relics while keeping total Madness/Injuries below the threshold."
- Source (lines 892-895): "If the expedition gathered as many or more Relic cards than there are Injury cards in the Equipment deck, Equipment discard, and all players' hands combined, you won the game!"
- Errors: (a) you don't "reach the City" to win — you reach the Edge of Madness then traverse 3 Escape tiles; (b) there is no separate "Madness/Injuries below threshold" condition — only Relics ≥ total Injuries across the whole game counts; (c) "enough relics" is under-specified — the actual threshold is Relics ≥ Injuries in deck+discard+all hands.

### Finding 3 — Lose conditions wrong: invented "player injury limit" and "collective Madness" loss

- Summary: "**Lose**: Too many Injuries (any player reaches their injury limit), too much collective Madness, or failing to escape the mountain."
- Source (lines 875-882): Three actual lose conditions:
  1. "If you must return your last Leadership token to the box, you immediately lose the game."
  2. "If you must draw an Injury card and the Injury deck is empty, you immediately lose the game."
  3. Escaped but fewer Relics than total Injuries.
- There is NO individual player injury limit. There is NO "too much collective Madness" loss — excess Madness causes Leadership token loss, which can then cause game loss. The summary invents "player injury limit" as a standalone loss condition.

### Finding 4 — Turn structure missing MOVEMENT and DRAW phases

- Summary describes 4 steps: Reveal, Discussion, Play cards, Resolve.
- Source (lines 408-413): "Each turn has 5 phases: MOVEMENT, ENCOUNTER, RESOLUTION, DRAW, REST (optional)."
- The MOVEMENT phase (where the Leader moves the Airplane to a new space) and DRAW phase (where equipment cards are dealt to players) are both completely absent from the summary.

### Finding 5 — Cards played not simultaneously; players play during entire Encounter phase

- Summary: "**Simultaneously play cards**: All players play cards face down."
- Source (lines 489-496): "At any time during this Phase, any player may play Equipment and/or Injury cards from their hand face down on the Sled Board... All players can continue to play cards from their hand face down on the Sled Board, without communicating in any way with the other players, until the Sand Timer runs out completely. You can play cards in the order of your choice, even playing multiple cards or adding cards after others have played."
- Cards are not played simultaneously — they are played asynchronously at any time during the Encounter phase.

### Finding 6 — Madness cards described as behavioral during "ALL future" discussions, but applies only while Sand Timer runs

- Summary: "When a player gains a Madness card, they must follow its restriction during ALL future 30-second discussion phases."
- Source (lines 241-248): "Madness cards affect your ability to communicate during the Encounter Phase (while the Sand Timer is running) by imposing certain restrictions on you... When the Sand Timer is not running, you must act as though you do not recall acting strangely."
- The "ALL future" phrasing is correct but the summary omits that restrictions stop the moment the Sand Timer runs out (not merely during the 30s discussion).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (30 Equipment cards, 48 Equipment cards, 5 phases, 3 escape tiles), SUPPORTED: 2 (48 Equipment cards, 3 escape tiles). Summary's "30" and phase count are wrong.

## mow

- Summary: "The game ends when any player reaches 100 flies. The player with the fewest flies wins."
- Source (lines 15-17): "as soon as a farmer has accumulated 100 flies at the end of a round, the game ends. The winner is the farmer with the fewest flies."

- Summary table: 15 cows (0 flies), 13 cows (1 fly), 11 cows (2 flies), 3 cows (3 flies), 6 specials (5 flies).

### Verification of turn structure

- Summary: Play a cow higher or lower than current range OR take the herd; draw 1 replacement.

- Match confirmed.

### Verification of green special cow direction change

- Summary: "Playing a green special cow lets you optionally change the direction of play."
- Source (lines 60-61): "a farmer who plays a special cow with 5 flies from the green packet can, if he wishes, change the direction of play."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of yellow special cow "choose next player"

- Summary: "Playing a yellow special cow lets you choose who plays next."
- Source (lines 6-7): "a farmer who plays a special cow with 5 flies from the yellow packet can, if he wishes, choose who the next player will be."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of 2-player variant

- Summary: "3 simultaneous herds in a star layout... When the deck empties and one herd is taken, the round ends (the other 2 herds are not scored)."
- Source (lines 18-26): Three herds in a star shape, "When the deck is empty and one of the farmers takes one of the three herds, the round ends. Neither farmer collects points for the two remaining herds."
- Match confirmed.

### Exhaustion rule

- Summary: "When the draw pile is empty, play continues until a player takes the last herd."
- Source (line 13): "Once the last cow is taken from the deck, the game continues until a farmer takes the last herd."
- Match confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (15 cows, 13 cows, 11 cows, 3 cows, 6 specials, 100 flies), SUPPORTED: 6

## mr-jack

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented Detective win condition: "1 suspect remaining"

- Summary: "**Detective wins** if: All characters except one are flipped to Innocent (that one is Jack)"
- Source (lines 147-162, "End of game", THREE possible ways): The detective wins ONLY if "The detective moves a character onto the same hex as Jack and confronts him: If the accusation is right: the detective wins the game." There is NO win condition for "1 suspect remaining" in the source.
- The source's third end condition is "Jack is not caught: At the end of the eighth round, if Jack is not caught, he wins the game!" — not a detective win.
- This invents a win condition from Mr. Jack Pocket and applies it to the original game.

### Finding 2 — Characters DO pass through other characters (summary says opposite)

- Summary: "Characters cannot pass through other characters, police cordons, or buildings."
- Source (lines 229-230): "The characters do not block movement. But ending movement on a hex on which another character stands is not possible, unless the detective means to accuse a character!!!"
- The source explicitly states characters do NOT block movement. The summary invents this restriction.

### Finding 3 — Escape timing mis-stated

- Summary: "Jack can attempt to escape by moving his character to one of the 4 exit hexes... during a round when his character is **invisible**."
- Source (lines 136-137): "Note: if Jack is invisible at the **end of a round**, and **only then**, he can try to leave the area during the **following round**."
- The summary implies Jack escapes during any round when invisible; the source requires Jack to be invisible at the END of the previous round before he can attempt escape on the NEXT round.

### Verification of turn selection order

- Summary: "Odd rounds: Detective picks 1, Jack picks 2, Detective picks last 1."
- Source (lines 97-104): "During the odd-numbered rounds... The detective chooses a character... Jack then chooses two characters... And finally, the detective plays the last face-up character."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of visibility rules

- Summary: "Visible: A character on a lit hex OR adjacent to another character."
- Source (lines 65-70): "characters on a lit hex... are visible... Likewise, if two characters are on adjacent hexes, even if not lit, they are considered visible... a character who is neither on a lit hex... nor on a hex adjacent to another character, is considered to be invisible."
- Match confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 characters, 4 exit hexes, 8 rounds, 6 gaslights, 2 cordons, 2 manholes. SUPPORTED: all component counts. But procedures (movement blocking, escape timing, detective win) are wrong.

## mr-jack-in-new-york

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented Detective win condition: "1 suspect remaining"

- Summary: "**Detective wins**: Only 1 suspect remains after a witness call."
- Source (lines 165-175, "GAME END", THREE possible ways): Detective wins only if "The Detective moves a character onto the same space as Jack and accuses him: if the accusation is correct: the Detective wins the game." There is no "1 suspect remains" win condition in the source for this game either.
- This is the same contamination from Mr. Jack Pocket as in the original Mr. Jack game.

### Finding 2 — Park tiles' invisibility not mentioned (core mechanic omission)

- Summary does not mention that characters in Park tiles are always invisible.
- Source (lines 101-102): "Characters on a Park tile are always invisible, even if there are characters on the adjacent spaces or if the Park is next to a lighted space."
- Parks are dynamically created/changed during play (Mrs Emma Grant's power converts tiles to parks), so this is a core visibility rule affecting Jack's strategy and detective deductions.

### Finding 3 — Informant token: summary incorrectly describes as "tokens" with generic effects

- Summary: "**Informant tokens**: Characters can pick up informants for special effects."
- Source: There is 1 single Informant token (not plural), and its effect is specifically: the visitor takes an Alibi card (not "special effects"). The Informant flips to Mute/Silent after use and resets at round start.
- This is a minor inaccuracy but imprecise.

### Verification of turn selection order

- Summary: "1/2/1 split between Detective and Jack, alternating who starts on odd/even rounds."
- Source (lines 141-149): Odd rounds: Detective 1, Jack 2, Detective 1. Even rounds: Jack 1, Detective 2, Jack 1.
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of escape conditions

- Summary: "Jack must reach specific edge hexes while invisible to escape."
- Source (lines 168, 314-322): Jack can escape via "an exit or a port space, that is not blocked by an Investigations tile" (land exit or port/steamer). Must have been invisible at end of previous round.
- The summary omits the port/steamer escape route and the requirement to have been invisible the previous round.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 characters, 8 rounds, 6 gaslights, 7 metro tiles, 4 building sites, 2 steamers. SUPPORTED: component counts all match. But detective win condition is invented.

## mr-jack-pocket

**Verdict: PASS**

### Verification of victory conditions

- Summary: "**Investigator wins**: Only 1 Suspect tile remains face up after an Appeal for Witnesses."
- Source (lines 144-147): "For the Investigator: Only one Suspect remains after an Appeal for Witnesses. That suspect is obviously the guilty party!"
- Match confirmed.

- Summary: "**Mr. Jack wins**: Accumulates 6 or more hourglasses (from Turn tokens taken + Alibi card hourglasses) before being identified."
- Source (lines 149-151): "For Mr. Jack: After an Appeal for Witnesses, he has at least six hourglasses between the Turn tokens and those on any Alibi cards acquired during the course of the game."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of special case (both win same turn)

- Summary: "On turn 8: Investigator wins if Mr. Jack can be seen; Mr. Jack wins if hidden. Before turn 8: Game continues -- Investigator wins the first turn Mr. Jack can be seen; Mr. Jack wins if hidden through turn 8."
- Source (lines 152-159): "If this occurs at the end of the eighth turn: the Investigator wins if Mr. Jack can be seen. Mr. Jack wins if he cannot be seen. If this occurs before the eighth turn, the game continues and there is a merciless race... The Investigator wins as soon as a turn ends in which Mr. Jack can be seen. Mr. Jack wins if he remains hidden until the end of the eighth turn."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of turn structure (odd/even)

- Summary: "Odd turns: Investigator throws 4 Action tokens (random sides showing). Investigator picks 1, Mr. Jack picks 2, Investigator executes last."
- Source (lines 63-71): "Odd Turns – the Investigator starts: He throws the four Action tokens in the air... He selects one of the four actions... Then, Mr. Jack chooses two actions from the remaining three... Finally, the Investigator plays the remaining available action."
- Match confirmed.

- Summary: "Even turns: Flip all 4 Action tokens to their opposite sides. Mr. Jack picks 1, Investigator picks 2, Mr. Jack executes last."
- Source (lines 73-79): "Even Turns – Mr. Jack starts: He turns over the four tokens to reveal the four actions that were hidden during the previous turn. He selects one of the four actions... Then the Investigator chooses two... Finally, Mr. Jack plays the remaining available action."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of Joker action

- Summary: "**Joker**: Investigator: move any 1 Detective 1 space clockwise. Mr. Jack: move any 1 Detective 1 space clockwise OR do nothing."
- Source (lines 83-90): "If the Investigator chooses this action: He moves a Detective token of his choice one space clockwise. If Mr. Jack chooses this action, he can either: Move a Detective token of his choice one space clockwise, or Leave the three Detective tokens where they are."
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of Appeal for Witnesses

- Summary: "**Can be seen**: All Suspect tiles NOT in any Detective's line of sight are flipped to Empty side. Investigator takes the Turn token."
- Source (lines 115-118): "If Mr. Jack can be seen, any area tiles with the suspect side up that are not in the line of sight of one of the detectives are turned over to show their empty side. The Investigator takes the current Turn token, thus depriving Mr Jack of it…"
- Match confirmed.

### Exhaustion / deck handling

No explicit reshuffle or deck depletion rule is mentioned in the summary. The source does not describe deck depletion as a game mechanic (Alibi cards are limited to 9 and actions are tokens). No invented exhaustion rule found.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 hourglasses, 8 turns, 9 suspects, 4 action tokens, 8 turn tokens, 3 detective tokens. SUPPORTED: all 6.

## mr-president

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented components: board, map, campaign tokens, money/resource tokens

- Summary: "1 game board (US map with states and electoral votes)", "Campaign tokens", "Money/resource tokens", "Player reference cards."
- Source: The game has: 52 Campaign Cards (4 sets of 13), Information Cards (one per player), 51 Electoral Cards (1st edition) / 51 State Cards (2nd edition), and a chalkboard/scoreboard. There is NO game board showing a US map, NO campaign tokens, NO money/resource tokens.
- This is a pure card game; the summary invents physical board components.

### Finding 2 — Invented turn structure: allocating resources to states, playing cards for effects, rolling dice

- Summary turn structure: "1. Allocate campaign resources to states. 2. Play campaign cards for effects. 3. Roll dice to resolve campaign events. 4. Adjust electoral vote standings."
- Source: Each round, State/Electoral cards are revealed; all candidates simultaneously choose and play Campaign Cards face down; highest value wins that round's electoral votes. There is NO resource allocation to states, NO dice rolling, NO "campaign events."
- The actual mechanism is a pure simultaneous card-play auction.

### Finding 3 — Win condition partly wrong: "closest to 270 wins" contradicts source

- Summary: "The candidate with 270 or more electoral votes wins the presidency. If no candidate reaches 270, the player closest to that threshold wins."
- Source (1st edition, lines 113-116): "If no candidate or Party wins a majority of electoral votes (270 or more), the game must be replayed. Optional: If so decided at the start of the game, the candidate or Party with a plurality (most) of electoral votes may be declared the winner."
- The default outcome if nobody reaches 270 is a REPLAY, not "closest wins." The summary presents the optional variant rule as the default.

### Finding 4 — Game described as 3M 1967 publication but source is from 1965

- Summary: "originally published by 3M in 1967"
- Source (line 173, 321): "Copyright @ 1965 by MINNESOTA MINING AND MANUFACTORING COMPANY" and "Copyright @ 1965 by MINNESOTA MINING AND MANUFACTURING COMPANY"
- Publication year is 1965, not 1967.

### Verification of electoral vote majority threshold

- Summary: "270 or more electoral votes wins"
- Source (1st edition, line 113): "a majority of electoral votes (270 or more)"; (2nd edition, line 269): "majority (270 or more) of electoral votes becomes Mr. President"
- The 270 threshold is correct.

### Verification of player count

- Summary: "2-4"
- Source: "from two to four players (candidates)" — match confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 270 (electoral threshold), 52 Campaign Cards, 51 Electoral Cards/States, 13 cards per set, 12 rounds, 4 players. SUPPORTED: 270, 51, 52, 13 all confirmed. But the entire game mechanic (board, tokens, dice, resource allocation) is invented.

## ms-monopoly

**Prefilter note**: WARN NOT_RULES — source is the German-language rulebook (SPIELANLEITUNG). Content is verified as a genuine rulebook for Ms. Monopoly (contains rules for play, Zentralen/Headquarters, LOS/GO mechanics, etc.). Source is confirmed rulebook; audit proceeds with German-language source.

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Game-end scoring omits mandatory rent payout from bank

- Summary: "The game ends when the last invention is bought. The wealthiest player (most total cash + invention values) wins."
- Source (lines 79-85): "Sobald die letzte Erfindung gekauft wurde, endet das Spiel. Abschließend erhalten alle Spieler die Miete für alle Ihre Erfindungen von der Bank. Der Spieler mit dem meisten Geld gewinnt!" [Once the last invention is bought, the game ends. Finally, all players receive rent for all their inventions from the bank. The player with the most money wins!]
- The summary omits that all players receive rent for ALL their inventions from the bank at game end — a significant final scoring step.
- The summary also adds "invention values" as a scoring element, but the source only mentions "most money" (Geld), not invention face-value calculation.

### Finding 2 — Starting money amounts not specified in summary; source provides totals

- Summary: "Distribute starting money (women receive more than men at the start)."
- Source (lines 312-313): "(Summe = b1500)" for men, "(Summe = b1900)" for women.
- The summary omits the specific amounts (1900 for women, 1500 for men). This is an omission from the Player Reference table.

### Finding 3 — Summary says "Building and mortgage rules follow classic Monopoly conventions" but source shows Headquarters replace houses AND hotels entirely

- Summary: "Building and mortgage rules follow classic Monopoly conventions."
- Source (lines 49-59, ZENTRALEN BAUEN section): One Zentrale (Headquarters) maximum per invention; Zentralen cannot be sold to the bank or other players. Standard Monopoly allows houses (1-4) then hotels (1). Here there is only one Headquarters per property (no progression from houses to hotels). This is NOT classic Monopoly conventions.
- The source (line 140): "BAUEN SIE ZENTRALEN STATT HÄUSERN UND HOTELS" [Build Headquarters instead of houses and hotels] — one type replaces both.

### Verification of GO amounts

- Summary: "Women collect $240; Men collect $200"
- Source (lines 167-170): "Wenn Sie über LOS ziehen oder darauf landen, erhalten Sie von der Bank b240, wenn Sie eine Frau sind. Als Mann erhalten Sie b200." [If you pass or land on GO, you receive b240 from the bank if you are a woman. As a man you receive b200.]
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of game-end trigger

- Summary: "Game ends when the last invention is purchased."
- Source (line 80): "Sobald die letzte Erfindung gekauft wurde, endet das Spiel." [Once the last invention is bought, the game ends.]
- Match confirmed.

### Verification of Headquarters limit

- Summary: "12 Headquarters (replacing houses/hotels)"
- Source (line 39): "12 Zentralen" — match confirmed.

### Exhaustion rule

The game ends when the last invention is purchased — not a deck/card exhaustion situation. No invented exhaustion rule found.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 240 (women GO), 200 (men GO), 24 inventions, 12 headquarters, 16 Chance, 16 Community Chest, 1900 (women start), 1500 (men start). SUPPORTED: 240/200/12/16/16 confirmed. 1900/1500 present in source but absent from summary.
