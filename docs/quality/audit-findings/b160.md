### Finding 1 — MAJOR: Automatic Victory condition is wrong (mechanism misidentified)

**Summary says:**
> "Automatic Victory: Either side wins immediately by controlling a sufficient number of Victory Point spaces (total VP threshold depends on scenario)."

**Source says (5.2.1–5.2.2):**
> "In all scenarios, the Central Powers win an Automatic Victory if the VP total is 20 or higher during segment E.2 of the War Status Phase of any turn."
> "In all scenarios, the Allies win an Automatic Victory if the VP total is 0 or lower during segment E.2 of the War Status Phase of any turn."

**Source says (4.1 Marker Set-Up):**
> "Place the Victory Point marker on the 10 space of the General Records Track."

**Analysis:** The VP marker starts at 10 and shifts up or down as VP spaces change hands per the Victory Point Table. Automatic Victory triggers when this sliding marker reaches 20 (CP) or 0 (Allies) — not when a player "controls a sufficient number of VP spaces." A player who controls every VP space on the map does not win automatically unless the marker actually reaches the threshold. The summary's characterization ("controlling a sufficient number of VP spaces") is wrong about both the mechanism and the fact that there are two distinct thresholds (20 for CP, 0 for Allies), not a single scenario-dependent one.

### Finding 2 — MAJOR: End-of-game Victory omits Draw ranges and scenario-specific thresholds

**Summary says:**
> "End-of-game Victory: If no automatic victory, the side with more VP at game end wins."

**Source says (5.3.4, 5.4.2, 5.5.2):**

Introductory Scenario:
- Central Powers Victory: 14 or more
- Draw: 11 to 13 (the historical result)
- Allied Victory: 10 or fewer

Limited War Scenario:
- Central Powers Victory: 17 or more
- Draw: 13 to 16 (the historical result)
- Allied Victory: 12 or fewer

Campaign Scenario (Treaty of Brest-Litovsk played):
- Central Powers Victory: 11 or more
- Draw: 10
- Allied Victory: 9 or fewer

Campaign Scenario (No Treaty of Brest-Litovsk):
- Central Powers Victory: 13 or more
- Draw: 10–12
- Allied Victory: 9 or fewer

**Analysis:** "The side with more VP wins" is incorrect. There is no direct comparison of VP totals between players — the single VP marker sits on a shared track. More importantly, each scenario has a defined Draw range: a table of players using the summary would falsely declare a winner in situations that should be Draws. This would affect the final score of every game that ends in the Draw range.

### Finding 3 — MAJOR: Armistice game-end trigger missing entirely

**Summary's Scoring / Victory Conditions section** does not mention the Armistice as a game-end condition.

**Source says (16.2.1):**
> "During the War Status Phase of any turn in which the Combined War Status marker is at 40 or more, an Armistice is declared. The game ends and victory is determined."

**Source says (5.5.1):**
> "The Campaign Scenario ends with any of the following: ... as a result of an Armistice (16.2.1) ..."

**Analysis:** The Armistice is a core game-end mechanism for the Campaign Scenario (the main scenario) and is historically the most common way the game ends (the Design Notes mention "if the war ends in an Armistice (historically on turn 19)"). It is triggered by a separate Combined War Status track reaching 40, independent of VP. A player using only the summary has no knowledge of this game-end trigger and cannot play the Campaign Scenario correctly.

### Finding 4 — MINOR: Turn sequence merges two distinct phases into one

**Summary says (Turn Structure phase 6):**
> "6. Replacement/Card Draw Phase: Receive replacement points and draw new strategy cards for next turn."

**Source says (Sequence of Play F and G):**
> "F. Replacement Phase — F.1. Allied Powers Segment ... F.2. Central Powers Segment ... Any RPs not spent are lost."
> "G. Draw Strategy Card Phase — Each player draws cards from his Draw Pile to bring his hand to seven cards."

**Analysis:** Replacement Phase (F) and Draw Strategy Card Phase (G) are two separate phases in the sequence. Merging them is an omission but the gameplay impact is minor since the order within what the summary labels as one phase is preserved.

### Finding 5 — MINOR: Action options understated (Peace Terms and Automatic Operation omitted)

**Summary says:**
> "On each action round, the active player plays one card for one of four uses: Operations (OPS), Strategic Redeployment (SR), Replacement Points (RP), Event."

**Source says (8.1.3):**
> "Each player must take one of six possible actions: Play a Strategy Card as an Operations (OPS) card. Play a Strategy Card as a Strategic Redeployment (SR) card. Play a Strategy Card as a Replacement (RP) card. Play a Strategy Card as an Event card. Offer Peace Terms if the VP level is within the player's allowable range. Take an Automatic Operation with an OPS Value of one without the play of a Strategy Card."

**Analysis:** Peace Terms and Automatic Operation (a free 1-OPS action requiring no card) are valid actions per 8.1.3. Omitting Automatic Operation is a meaningful omission (it allows play when a player has no desirable cards), though it would not affect scoring. Borderline MINOR given Peace Terms is mentioned elsewhere in the summary under Special Rules.
