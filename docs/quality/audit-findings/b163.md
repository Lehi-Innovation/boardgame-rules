## piepmatz

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter note
`python -m scripts.audit prefilter` emitted `WARN SOURCE_MISMATCH: no title tokens ['piepmatz'] found anywhere in source`. Confirmed: the extracted file (`piepmatz-rules.txt`) is the rulebook for **"Songbirds"** / **"Little Songbirds"**, not "Piepmatz". The word "Piepmatz" does not appear anywhere in the source text. However, the game appears to be the same (or closely related) game, so the content can still be compared — but note that the summary uses the title "Piepmatz" while the rulebook calls it "Songbirds."

Since the source text is about the same mechanical game, the detailed audit proceeds, and multiple MAJOR errors were found regardless.

### Finding 1 — Scoring: mated pairs (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** "Mated pairs: For each species where you have both a male and female bird, score the points printed on those cards"
- **Source (Scoring section):** "1) 5 points per pair of birds: A pair of birds is a male and female bird of the same species **and strength**."
- **Error:** The summary says you score "the points printed on those cards" (card point values vary). The source gives a **flat 5 points per pair**, and crucially requires the male and female to be of the **same strength** to form a pair. These are completely different scoring rules — a table scoring from the summary would be wrong.

### Finding 2 — Scoring: singles tiebreaker for species majority (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** "Singles: For each species where you have only male or only female birds, you score points only if you have the **most** birds of that species compared to all opponents. Ties = **no one scores for singles of that species**."
- **Source:** "Only the player who has the most birds of a species gets points for that species… **If multiple players are tied for most birds, all tied players score their respective birds.**"
- **Error:** The summary inverts the tie rule. According to the source, tied players each score their birds; the summary says tied players score nothing. This directly affects final scores.

### Finding 3 — Tiebreaker (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** "Tiebreaker: most birds in collection."
- **Source:** "In case of a tie, the player with the most **pairs of birds** breaks the tie."
- **Error:** Wrong tiebreaker. The source uses most pairs; the summary uses most birds.

### Finding 4 — Game-end trigger (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** "Game end triggers: The feeder deck **or bird deck** runs out."
- **Source:** "The game end is triggered when you are supposed to draw a card from an **empty feeder deck**." The bird deck emptying is never listed as a game-end trigger.
- **Error:** Invented game-end trigger (bird deck). No support found for "bird deck runs out" as an end condition (searched: bird deck, bird deck empty, run out, trigger, game end).

### Finding 5 — Squirrel bad card effect
- **Summary:** "Squirrel = another player draws 2 random cards from your score pile (removed from game)."
- **Source:** "Shuffle your score pile and **let another player draw two cards from it**. Remove these two cards from play."
- **Verdict:** Matches — phrasing is equivalent. PASS on this claim.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (seed card values 1-3, 5 pts per pair, 72 bird cards, 36 feeder cards, 110 total), SUPPORTED: 4 (all except "points printed on those cards" for pairs)

## pirate-s-cove

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Victory / Game-end
- **Summary:** "After 12 turns, the pirate with the most fame wins."
- **Source:** "At the end of twelve months (game turns), the pirate with the most fame will be declared winner."
- **Supported.** ✓

### Tiebreaker — missing (MINOR)
- **Summary:** No tiebreaker mentioned.
- **Source:** "If two or more players are tied for the most fame, these players must fight to the death. In the final epic battle, the lone survivor is declared most famous pirate!"
- **Finding:** The tiebreaker (a special final battle between tied players) is omitted from the summary. This would not affect scoring but players tied at the end would not know the correct procedure. MINOR because it does not affect how the game is scored during play, only the tie-resolution procedure.

### Combat fame — incomplete but not inverted
- **Summary:** "Winner (last ship standing) gains 1 fame per ship defeated."
- **Source (Scoring section):** "Winning Battles (1 point per player that is defeated **or flees after being hit**)."
- **Finding:** The summary omits that fleeing after being hit also awards fame to remaining opponents. This is a simplification rather than a contradiction — the broad statement "1 fame per ship defeated" would cause players to miss awarding fame when an opponent flees after being hit. This is a table-impacting omission (players skip fame awards). However, it is mentioned somewhat implicitly in the fleeing section: "all opponents remaining on the island receive 1 point of fame upon seeing her flee." Since the two sub-cases (crippled and fled-after-hit) both yield the same 1-fame outcome and the mechanic exists in the detailed combat section (albeit omitted from the summary's explicit fame list), this is borderline MINOR.

### Turn structure: 6 phases
- **Summary:** Phases 1-6 match source (Treasure, Navigation, Combat, Plunder, Upgrade, Move Black Ships). ✓

### Component check
- 112 cards (60 Treasure, 5 Legendary Pirate, 1 Royal Navy, 42 Tavern), 44 doubloons (24×1, 20×5), 6 dice, 7 miniatures. All match source. ✓

### Combat dice
- Summary: "dice = minimum of crew/cannon level. Hits on 5-6." Source: "Rolls a number of dice equal to the lowest of his levels of Crew and Cannons. Each roll of 5 or 6 is a hit." ✓

### 3-player: 2 Legendary Pirate ships
- Summary: "With 3 players, use 2 Legendary Pirate ships." Source: "If you are playing with three players, a second Legendary Pirate black ship is anchored off Cannon Island (#4)." ✓

### Treasure Island upgrade cost
- Summary: "upgrade 1 ship section 1 level at double cost." Source: "upgrade any single section of your ship a maximum of 1 level, at double the normal upgrade cost." ✓

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 10 (112 cards, 60/5/1/42 card breakdown, 44 doubloons, 24×1/20×5, 6 dice, 12 turns, 1 fame per crippled/fled), SUPPORTED: 9 (combat fame omission noted)

## pixie-queen

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Round structure — phase naming mismatch (MINOR within larger MAJOR)
- **Summary:** 5 phases: "Steal Food, Perform Actions, Resolve Actions, Offerings to the Queen, Clean Up"
- **Source:** "I. Stealing / II. Actions / III. Offering / IV. Rewards / V. Penalty"
- **Finding:** The summary's "Phase 3: Resolve Actions" is not a separate phase in the source — actions are performed immediately when chosen. More critically, the summary **merges the Rewards Phase (IV) and Penalty Phase (V) into "Phase 4: Offerings to the Queen"**. Both Rewards and Penalty are distinct phases with separate procedures in the source.

### Final scoring — missing steps (MAJOR)
- **Summary scoring:** "Penalty points minus victory points = final score. Lowest final score wins."
- **Source final scoring (4 explicit steps):**
  1. Penalty points remain.
  2. Victory points move score marker toward/past "0."
  3. **Count for each kind of resource cube which player has most remaining — those players receive 3 penalty points per resource type (apples, honey, bread, silver, gold).** If tied, all tied players receive 3 penalty points.
  4. **For each stone enchaining a slave, that player receives 1 penalty point.**

- **Missing:** Steps 3 and 4 of final scoring are entirely absent from the summary. A player with many leftover resources receives additional penalties; a player with enchained slaves receives stone penalties. These are table-critical: a finished game cannot be scored correctly using the summary.

### Tiebreaker — wrong (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** No tiebreaker stated beyond "lowest final score wins."
- **Source:** "In case of a tie the player who has most loyal servants wins the game. In case there still is a tie see which of the tied players had a loyal servant earlier in the game. That player is declared winner."
- **Finding:** The tiebreaker (most loyal servants, then who earned a loyal servant earliest) is completely absent from the summary.

### Win condition — oversimplified (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** "lowest final score wins" (uniformly).
- **Source:** "In case no player passed the '0' space thus ending with all players in the negative points the player who has least points wins. In case 1 or more players passed into the positive side of the score track the player with most victory points wins."
- **Finding:** Two distinct win conditions depending on whether any player is in positive territory. When players are in the positive zone, the winner is determined by **most VP** (not least penalties). The summary states a single rule ("lowest final score") that is wrong when any player goes positive.

### Action count
- **Summary:** "21 different actions."
- **Source:** Actions listed 1-21 in the action overview (Steal food, Steal silver, Promotion, Trade, Turning silver into gold, Food tile, Double offer, Look into bag, Mercy, Penalty points, Gold & silver track, Casino, Ring, Rob a pixie, Mine, Trade tiles, Dump resources/take 1 food, Copy action, Additional offering, Alter play priority, Temporary priority).
- Count: 21 actions. ✓

### Game-end triggers
- **Summary:** "maximum of 7 rounds."
- **Source:** "The game ends when: All reward tiles with golden numbers are taken (3 times 5 tiles); Or 7 rounds have been played and there are no more round tiles."
- **Finding:** Summary omits the reward-tile exhaustion trigger. The game can end before 7 rounds if all golden-number reward tiles are taken. MAJOR omission of a game-end condition.

### Pass count penalty
- **Source:** "In the rare case there is no possible action or you no longer wish to perform any action you are allowed to pass. The Queen however will punish you severely for your lack of trying. You receive 5 penalty points!"
- **Summary:** Not mentioned at all. Omission of a key mid-game penalty rule.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (7 rounds max, 5 penalty points for pass, 5 penalty for empty hand, 5 penalty for wrong food, 3 penalty per resource type at end, 2 max penalty per round for slaves, 60 penalty = pit of oblivion, 3×5 tiles = game-end trigger), SUPPORTED: 3

## pizza-box-football

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Backyard Brawl — initial possession reversal (MAJOR)
- **Summary:** "Each player rolls 1 die; higher roller starts with the ball first." (Implied to apply to all modes.)
- **Source (Backyard Brawl):** "Each player rolls 1 die. The **lower** roller will start with the ball first in all 6 game rounds."
- **Error:** Direction reversal. In Backyard Brawl specifically, the LOWER roller starts with the ball. The summary (and Red Zone Shootout) uses "higher roller" — but Backyard Brawl is explicitly the lower roller. This directly contradicts the source and would cause the wrong player to start every Backyard Brawl game.

### Red Zone Shootout — initial possession
- **Summary:** "Each player rolls 1 die; higher roller starts with the ball first." 
- **Source:** "Each player rolls 1 die. The higher roller will start with the ball first." ✓

### Game modes — confirmed
- Summary lists 4 modes: Red Zone Shootout (5+ min), Backyard Brawl (30-40 min), Professional (30-40 min), Smashmouth (60 min).
- **Source table** (from layout): Red Zone Shootout = 5+ min ✓. Backyard Brawl = 30-40 min ✓. Professional and Smashmouth ordering and time matches visual layout of table.

### Scoring values
- TD=6, FG=3, Safety=2, XP=1, 2PT=2. Source confirms each. ✓

### Turn sequence (Play Sequence)
1. Both secretly choose play type. 2. Reveal. 3. Defense rolls die. 4. Offense rolls Play Dice (+ Yardage Dice). 5. Determine yardage/outcome.
- Source: "steps 1-5 shown on the card" — the sequence as described matches the Play Sequence Card description. ✓

### Solitaire mode
- Summary mentions solitaire. Source: "All instructions below are written for 2 players; however all versions may be played solitaire (without defense rolls)." ✓

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7 (TD=6, FG=3, Safety=2, XP=1, 2PT=2, 3 black Play Dice, 5 white Yardage Dice), SUPPORTED: 6 (Backyard Brawl possession reversal is wrong)
