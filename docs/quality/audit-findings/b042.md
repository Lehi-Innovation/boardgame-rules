## brew — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Wrong trained-Creature limit (3, not 4).**
   - Summary (Actions > Training Creatures): "Place it near your Character board (max 4 active Creatures)."
   - Source: "You may never have more than 3 trained Creatures at a time. ... If you train a new Creature, but you are already at your limit of 3, you must release a Creature of your choice."
   - The "4" figure comes from a Character power exception (Wren: "You may have up to 4 trained Creatures" / "(MAX 4)" sidebar), which the summary misattributed to the base rules. Players would hold an illegal 4th creature.

2. **MAJOR — Tiebreaker contradicted.**
   - Summary (Scoring): "Tiebreakers not specified (shared victory if tied)."
   - Source: "If there is a tie, the tied player with the most leftover ingredients (including Energy Berries) is the winner. If there is still a tie, the tied players share victory!"
   - The rulebook explicitly specifies a tiebreaker; the summary asserts it does not.

3. **MINOR — Potion power list incomplete (3 of 7 listed).**
   - Summary lists only Move, Change, Swap under "Potion Powers."
   - Source also includes: REMOVE an Element die/any die, REROLL dice, RETURN a Forage die (grants extra turn), PLACE an extra die. Presenting 3 as the full list understates the system.

4. **MINOR — Final scoring values omitted/vague and one source missing.**
   - Summary: "VP from Creature bonuses (paired with matching-season Forests)" and "VP from remaining ingredient tokens (varies)."
   - Source: Creatures score exactly 3 VP if in a matching Forest, 1 VP otherwise; leftover ingredients score exactly 1 VP per 3 tokens (not "varies"); and "VP tokens: Score all VP tokens you have collected during the game" is missing entirely from the summary's Final Scoring list.

5. **Trivial — Release-on-overflow rule not stated.** Source: training past the limit forces an immediate release; summary never describes the mechanism (its Releasing Creatures section covers only scoring).

## britannia — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Movement rates wrong (both numbers).**
   - Summary (Actions and Player Reference table): "Infantry: May move 1 area per turn... Cavalry: May move 2 areas per turn."
   - Source: "Normal armies ... may move up to two land areas per turn." "Cavalry units and Roman infantry may move three land areas per turn." Armies with leaders also move three. Every movement number in the summary is one short.

2. **MAJOR — Invented leader combat rule.**
   - Summary: "Leaders in combat: Roll separately; leader killed only on a 6 if with other units." (repeated in Player Reference: "Leaders: killed on 6 only (if with other units)").
   - Source: "Leaders are not armies, so no die is rolled for them. A leader is immediately eliminated in battle if combat losses leave the leader without other units of his nation in the area... No dice roll is required." Leaders add +1 to each accompanying army's roll. The summary's mechanic has no basis in the source.

3. **MAJOR — Invented fort/burh combat bonus.**
   - Summary: "Forts/Burhs: Provide defensive bonuses (+1 to defender's die rolls or similar)."
   - Source: "In battle, Roman forts and Saxon burhs act as normal armies" (they roll their own dice; no +1 modifier exists). Also missing: Roman armies must be eliminated (6s) before a fort can be killed.

4. **MAJOR — Combat hit numbers incomplete/wrong sequencing.**
   - Summary: attacker rolls, 5-6 hits; "Defending units then counterattack."
   - Source: "Die rolls are considered to be simultaneous"; normal army kills on 5+, but Roman/cavalry armies kill on 4+, Roman/cavalry are only eliminated on 6, and a defender in difficult terrain is only eliminated on 6 (summary instead invents "typically +1 to defense rolls" for terrain). Core combat differentiation (the Roman 4+ advantage) is missing.

5. **MAJOR — Standard 4-player nation assignment is wrong (colors and groupings).**
   - Summary: Red = Romans, Romano-British, Scots, Irish; Blue = Brigantes, Jutes, Saxons, Norse; Yellow = Welsh, Picts, Dubliners, Norwegians; Green = Belgae, Angles, Danes, Normans.
   - Source: Yellow = Dubliners, Norwegians, Romans, Romano-British, Scots; Green = Caledonians, Danes, Jutes, Welsh; Red = Brigantes, Irish, Norsemen, Saxons; Blue = Belgae, Picts, Angles, Normans.
   - No color group in the summary matches the source; the Caledonians are omitted entirely.

6. **MAJOR — Bretwalda mechanism wrong; King thresholds and VP values omitted.**
   - Summary: "Bretwalda/King: Controlling the most English areas in certain rounds earns the title and VP."
   - Source: Bretwalda (Rounds 8-10) is elected by vote — one vote per English area occupied, majority of votes cast required — worth 4 VP. King (Rounds 11-14) requires occupying twice as many English areas as any other nation (minimum 4), worth 8 VP plus an extra infantry; Round 16 kingship is worth 10 VP and only Harold, William, Harald Hardrada, Svein Estrithson are eligible. None of these mechanics or values appear in the summary.

7. **MAJOR — Nation-turn phase structure misstated; scoring rounds omitted.**
   - Summary turn: Place units / Movement / Combat / Overpopulation / "Score victory points."
   - Source: five phases are Population Increase, Movement, Battles/Retreats, Raider Withdrawal, Overpopulation. Raider Withdrawal (a distinct phase enabling raiders to return to sea after fighting) is missing, and "hold" scoring is not a nation-turn phase — it occurs at end of round, only in scoring rounds 5, 7, 10, 13, 16, which the summary never identifies.

8. **MINOR — Overpopulation mischaracterized.**
   - Summary: "Each area has a population limit. After movement and combat, excess units must be removed."
   - Source: overpopulation is nation-wide — if armies on land exceed twice the number of land areas occupied, remove the excess. Per-area caps are stacking limits (3 normal / 2 difficult, plus one overstack), which the summary omits.

9. **MINOR — Roman forts after withdrawal wrong.**
   - Summary: "Roman forts remain on the board after Roman withdrawal and benefit Romano-British."
   - Source: in Round 6 eight surviving forts in/south of York and Cheshire are *replaced* with Romano-British armies; "Any other Roman forts (destroyed or intact) are removed from the board."

10. **MINOR — Arthur misdescribed.**
    - Summary: "Arthur can appear anywhere in Round 6-7."
    - Source: Arthur (plus two cavalry) enters in Round 7 only, placed in an area with Romano-British armies or a vacant area south of Cumbria/Pennines/Bernicia — not anywhere.

11. **MINOR — Roman entry location.** Summary: "Romans off-board in Gaul" / "Enter Round 1 from Gaul." Source: 16 Roman armies are placed in the English Channel sea area (Gaul is only flavor text).

## broadsides-and-boarding-parties — VERDICT: MINOR

Note: the extracted source is the Universal Head rules summary of the 1984 Milton Bradley game, not the original rulebook; comparisons are against that text.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — Components card count wrong (self-contradicted).**
   - Summary (Components): "30 Cards per player (3 each of: Remain in Place, Move Forward, Turn Starboard, Turn Port, Damaged Mast and/or Hull)."
   - Source: "Take ... 15 cards (3 each of Remain in Place, Move Forward, Turn Starboard, Turn Port, and Damaged Mast and/or Hull)." It is 15 per player (30 total); the summary's own Setup step correctly says "15 plotting cards (3 of each type)."

2. **MINOR — Irreparable hull damage: "same section" vs "same side."**
   - Summary: "2 hits to the same hull section = irreparable damage."
   - Source: "When one of your hull sections is hit, place a damage marker... When one of your hull sections on the same side is hit a second time, place a second damage marker on top of the first. ... Two hits represent irreparable damage."
   - The source keys the second hit to the same *side* of the ship, not the same section; the summary's reading makes irreparable damage harder to reach than the source states. (Source wording is slightly awkward, so flagged with moderate confidence.) The summary also omits "A third hit to a hull section has no effect."

3. **MINOR — Omitted Captain protection in melee.**
   - Source: "He [the Captain] cannot be eliminated until all other crewmembers in that section have been eliminated."
   - Summary's boarding combat rules omit this; it matters in the decisive Captain-hunting fight.

4. **Trivial — Crew combat bonus phrasing.** Summary: "Add +1 for each extra crewmember you have." Source: "+1 if you have 1 more crewmember... +2 if you have 2 more" — equivalent given the 3-per-section cap. Also omitted (trivial): crew may return to their own ship after boarding.

MINOR.
