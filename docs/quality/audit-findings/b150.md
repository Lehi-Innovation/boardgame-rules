## my-city

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Wrong win condition (campaign)

**Summary:** "The player with the most cumulative victory points after all 24 episodes wins the campaign."

**Source (lines 87-88):** "The player who has colored in the most progress symbols after 24 episodes is the overall winner of My City."

The campaign winner is determined by **progress symbols**, not cumulative VP. VP on the scoring track resets to 10 each episode. A player who scores second-most in an episode earns one progress symbol (except in 2-player games where second place earns nothing). Cumulative VP is never tracked.

### Finding 2 — Progress symbol scoring details omitted/wrong

**Summary:** "Scoring Elements (Evolve Over Episodes): Trees, Rocks, Empty spaces, Color groups" — no mention of progress symbols.

**Source (lines 82-88):** "Whoever gets the most or second-most points in an episode is allowed to color in two or one progress symbols, respectively. These progress symbols appear as circles in two rows at the top edge of the game board. The player who has colored in the most progress symbols after 24 episodes is the overall winner."

The core scoring economy (progress symbols) is absent from the summary's scoring section, and the summary replaces it with a fabricated "cumulative VP" system.

### Finding 3 — Color groups: summary omits this is Eternal Game only

**Summary (Scoring Elements):** "Color groups: Connected groups of same-colored buildings may score bonuses." — presented as a general scoring rule.

**Source (lines 343-348):** "Try to construct as many contiguous buildings of the same color as possible. During the end-of-episode assessment, you will get extra points for each of the three colors. For each color, you will add up the number of buildings composing the largest contiguous group of this color and receive that number of points." — This appears only in the Eternal Game section.

The legacy campaign rules do not include color-group scoring; this is an Eternal Game rule improperly generalized.

### Procedural claims verified

- Episode end trigger: source lines 129-130 confirm episode ends when all players have ended participation or all cards revealed. Summary matches.
- Passing penalty (-1 point): source line 175 confirms. Summary matches.
- Scoring token starts at 10: source line 56 confirms. Summary matches.
- Trees: +1 per visible tree (source line 135). Summary matches.
- Rocks: -1 per visible rock (source line 137). Summary matches.
- Empty light green space: -1 (source line 139). Summary matches.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5, SUPPORTED: 4 (color groups wrong context)

## my-first-castle-panic

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — "Super Monster" is invented; "Marcher" is omitted

**Summary (Monster Types table):** "Super Monster: Requires 2 hits (2 matching cards on separate turns) to catch."

**Source:** No "Super Monster" appears anywhere in the extracted text. The source lists four monster types: regular, Shover, Runner, and **Marcher**. Marcher (source lines 107-116): "After putting the Marcher on the START space, move all the monsters (including the Marcher) 1 more space closer to the castle and then draw 1 more monster token and place it on the board."

The summary invents a mechanic (2-hit monster) that does not exist, and omits the Marcher entirely. A table using this summary would never trigger the Marcher's chain-draw rule.

### Finding 2 — Runner placement described incorrectly

**Summary:** "Runner: Placed on the 2nd space instead of START."

**Source (lines 98-106):** "Runner: This monster is in a hurry! Instead of putting this monster on the START space, place it on the space in front of the monster closest to the castle. If the closest monster is in front of the wall or castle, that means the Runner hits the wall or castle!"

The Runner is not placed on a fixed "2nd space" — it's placed ahead of whichever monster is currently closest to the castle. This is a qualitatively different (and more dangerous) rule.

### Finding 3 — "More Help Variation" misdescribed

**Summary:** "The More Help Variation: Allow any player to play a matching card on any turn, not just when asked."

**Source (lines 136-138):** "More Help: If no player has a card that matches on your turn, draw a card from the Castle deck. Play the card if it matches. Keep it in your hand if it doesn't."

The summary's description of More Help is a different house rule from what the source actually describes.

### Procedural claims verified

- Win condition (all monsters caught, castle standing): source line 121-123 confirms. Summary matches.
- Lose condition (castle knocked flat): source line 82-83 confirms. Summary matches.
- Shover (moves all +1): source lines 93-96 confirm. Summary matches.
- Wall mechanic (monster hits wall → both flat, monster to dungeon): source lines 78-81 confirm. Summary matches.
- No hand limit: source line 33 confirms. Summary matches.
- Reshuffle discard if deck runs out: source line 32-33 confirms. Summary matches.

## mykerinos

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Tiebreaker in region control reversed/wrong

**Summary:** "Ties in a region are broken by the player who placed cubes there first."

**Source (lines 208-212):** "Draws: If two players or more have placed the same number of cubes in the area, take a look at the passing scale to settle the draw and rank them. In this case, the player who occupies the space with the lowest figure (that is, the player who passed first) wins the draw."

The tiebreaker is who **passed first** in the passing turn order — not who placed cubes first. These are completely different criteria. A player placing cubes early is likely still excavating; the tiebreaker rewards those who stopped (passed) earliest.

### Finding 2 — Museum scoring mechanism not described

**Summary:** "Museum position: Based on row/column in Museum grid."

**Source (lines 329-346):** Museum scoring is based on multiplying your patron room position by the number of patron cards you own: "For each wing, a player earns points according to the number of corresponding 'patron' cards they have, and according to the position of the player in the rooms: If the player is in room '5', they earn five prestige points [per card]... If the player is neither in any room of the wing... they only score one point [per card]."

The summary omits the card-counting multiplier entirely. A player who knows their Museum position but not how many patron cards matter cannot score correctly.

### Finding 3 — "Series" scoring omitted

**Source (lines 347-349):** "For each series of five different 'patron' cards they own, a player scores five points."

No equivalent appears in the summary's scoring table. This is an entire VP source missing.

### Finding 4 — Cube allocation numbers wrong

**Summary:** "On your turn, place 1-3 cubes (depending on patron abilities)."

**Source (lines 74-79):** Each player receives 11 cubes (3-player) or 8 cubes (4-player) at the start of each season to form their personal stock. On action A (start new excavation) they place 1 cube; on action B (extend) they place 2 cubes. Patron Colonel Tangerine lets you place 3 cubes (on extend). The "1-3 cubes" framing is misleading — placement is determined by which action is chosen (1 for new, 2 for extend), not by patron abilities generically.

### Procedural claims verified

- 4 seasons: source line 88 confirms. Summary matches.
- Winner: most prestige points: source line 350 confirms. Summary matches.
- Second-place choice (parcel or Museum): source lines 222-227 confirm. Summary matches.
- Tiebreaker game-end: "most cubes in personal stock" source line 351-352 confirms. Summary does not include this tiebreaker (MINOR).
- 2-player rules modify grid: source line 358 confirms. Summary correctly notes "2-player rules modify the grid size."

## myrmes

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented VP source: "Points per controlled hex"

**Summary (Scoring table):** "Pheromone territory: Points per controlled hex."

**Source:** Pheromones score VP at the moment of **placement** (not per controlled hex per turn or per end-game count): "If the pheromone scores victory points (all pheromones of a size greater than 2), the player immediately scores the points indicated on the pheromone." (lines 360-362). There is no "points per controlled hex" — the VP is earned once, immediately on placement of pheromones larger than size 2. The "per hex" framing implies ongoing/end-game territory scoring that does not exist.

### Finding 2 — Invented VP source: "Nest development: Points for expanded tunnels"

**Summary (Scoring table):** "Nest development: Points for expanded tunnels."

**Source:** Placing a new tunnel exit in the Atelier earns **one cube of earth** (resource), not VP (source lines 440-447): "the player who has placed a nurse in this atelier can place a new tunnel exit... and earns a cube of earth which they place in their stockpile." No source support found for tunnels earning VP. Searched: tunnel, expand, nest, atelier point, dig, development.

### Finding 3 — "Special achievements: Bonus tiles" unsupported

**Summary (Scoring table):** "Special achievements: Bonus tiles."

**Source:** No "bonus tiles" or "achievement" scoring category exists. The source mentions "Sub-colony earns 2 victory points for its owner" (each harvest) and objective tiles (6/9/12 VP at completion). No VP tiles separate from these. Searched: achievement, bonus tile, special achievement.

### Procedural claims verified

- 3 game years, 12 seasons: source line 77 confirms ("played over the course of 3 game years. Each year is divided into 4 seasons"). Summary matches.
- Winter food cost (4/5/6 cubes): source lines 530-535 confirm. Summary correctly identifies winter cost.
- Worker phase is turn-based (not simultaneous): source line 242-244 confirms ("played by each player in turn, starting with the first player and going clockwise"). Summary says "Assign workers to tasks" without noting sequential order.
- Objectives: 6/9/12 VP: source lines 492-496 confirm ("6 points for level 1 objectives, 9 points for level 2 objectives, and 12 points for level 3 objectives"). Summary says "Varies by difficulty" — not wrong, just vague.
- Unfed ants die: source line 537 ("loses 3 victory points for each missing cube") — actually they lose VP not ants. The summary says "Unfed ants die" but source says "loses 3 victory points for each missing cube." This is a MAJOR error — players would expect ants to die but the actual penalty is point loss only.

### Finding 4 — "Unfed ants die" is wrong; penalty is VP loss only

**Summary:** "Unfed ants die."

**Source (lines 536-537):** "If a player doesn't have a sufficient amount of cubes, that player loses 3 victory points for each missing cube." Soldiers can reduce food cost (line 538-540) without dying.

No ant death mechanic appears in the source for winter starvation. The penalty is purely VP loss.

## mysterium

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Crow token mechanic completely wrong

**Summary:** "The Ghost may spend a Crow token to give an extra Vision card to struggling Psychics."

**Source (lines 198-202):** "Creating a vision might be difficult. As the Ghost, you may want to discard your hand to replace it with more inspiring cards. If you want to do so, place one of your Crow Tokens on your Screen. Discard up to 7 cards and draw the same number of cards to replace them. You can discard cards at any time; however, you can only use the Crow a limited number of times."

Crow tokens are used to **replace the Ghost's own hand**, not to give Psychics extra Vision cards. The summary invents a mechanic that does not exist and omits the actual mechanic. A Ghost who reads the summary would never think to use Crow tokens to refresh their hand.

### Procedural claims verified

- 7-hour maximum: source line 135 confirms. Summary matches.
- Characters first, then Location, then Object: source lines 258-262 confirm. Summary matches.
- Psychics solve suspect, location, and object each: source lines 133-134 confirm. Summary matches.
- Final phase: Ghost picks 3 Vision Cards for true culprit: source lines 346-349 confirm. Summary matches.
- No tie in final vote: "vote of the Psychic with the highest Clairvoyance Level determines which group" (source line 381). Summary says "Majority vote wins." — the majority mechanism matches but the tiebreaker is missing from the summary (MINOR).
- 2-3 player game: Clairvoyancy tokens not used: source line 388 confirms. Summary matches.
- Ghost draws Vision Cards back to 7 after distributing: source line 163-164 confirms ("the Ghost immediately draws new Vision Cards until they have 7 cards in hand"). Summary says ghost distributes face down then draws replacements — partially matches.

### Clairvoyancy token votes (direction of scoring)

**Summary:** "Correct approve/reject votes advance a Psychic's Clairvoyancy Level."

**Source (lines 250-256, 267-269):** When a Psychic's guess is correct, Psychics who placed an **approve** token move their Clairvoyancy Level Token forward; those who placed a reject token do not advance. When a Psychic is wrong, Psychics who placed a **reject** token move forward; approvers do not. This matches the summary's description.

## mysterium-kids-captain-echo-s-treasure

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Win/Lose framing is entirely wrong; game uses a scoring system, not binary win/lose

**Summary:** "Win: Players find all required treasure before the Moon reaches the end of its track. Lose: The Moon reaches the final space and not enough treasure has been found."

**Source (lines 109-131):** "When the Moon token moves to the last space, the sun begins to rise. The final round begins. After the final round, the players calculate their score. Score one point for each complete star symbol on your faceup Treasure tiles."

The source then gives two score rating tables (Calm Nights and Stormy Nights) with brackets: 0-3 points ("Not bad..."), 4-6 points ("Look at all that loot!"), 7-8 points, 9 points ("Congratulations"), etc. There is no binary win/lose condition. All games play out to completion and are scored. The summary's framing of a threshold "all required treasure" win and a lose condition is fabricated.

### Finding 2 — "Win" threshold "all required treasure" has no source support

No passage in the source defines a required number of treasure to "win." The game always plays to completion of the Moon track and then scores points. Searched: "all treasure," "find all," "required," "must find" — no matches.

### Procedural claims verified

- 5 rooms, one Noise card per room: source lines 30-32 confirm. Summary matches.
- Moon token advances after each round: source line 80 confirms. Summary matches.
- Ghost draws 1 random token and clues that room's card: source lines 56-59 confirm. Summary matches.
- Two-card rounds (some Moon spaces show 2 cards): source lines 96-108 confirm. Summary matches correctly.
- Ghost passes role to left after each round: source line 81 confirms. Summary matches.
- "If players cannot agree, player to Ghost's left decides": source line 68-69 confirms. Summary matches.
- 7 standard Treasure tiles (not symbol) + 3 symbol tiles for hard mode: source lines 33-53 confirm. Summary matches.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7, SUPPORTED: 7 (procedural numbers correct; win condition fabricated)
