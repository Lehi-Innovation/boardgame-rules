## barbarossa — MAJOR

### Numbers checked
| # | Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|------------------|-----------------|-----|
| 1 | Components: 6 event tiles, 4 magicians, 4 hats, 4 elfstones, 4 clay, 12 curse tokens, 13 arrows, 1 die | component list matches verbatim | yes |
| 2 | 3 Curse Tokens per player at start | "a pencil, and three Curse Tokens" | yes |
| 3 | Elfstones start on space 12; max 13 | "on space '12' of the Jewel Scale"; "can not exceed 13 Elfstones" | yes |
| 4 | 4 players: 2 objects each; 3 players: 3 each | "If there are 4 players ... two objects ... three players ... three objects" | yes |
| 5 | First guess 5 pts, second 3 pts, max 2 arrows | "first player to guess ... 5 points ... second ... 3 points ... not get more than two arrows" | yes |
| 6 | Curse tokens: 5 used → take 3 back once; max 6 per player | "Once five Curse tokens have been used ... take three Curse tokens back ... six Curse Tokens over the course of the game" | yes |
| 7 | End scoring: -5 no-arrow object, -2 one-arrow object | "-5 points for each object with no arrows ... -2 points for each object with one arrow" | yes |
| 8 | Game ends at end space (star) or 13th arrow | "reaches the end space (with the star) ... If the 13th arrow is placed" | yes |
| 9 | Sculptor table: 5-6 arrows → no move; 7-8 → 1 forward; 9-10 → 2 forward; 11 → 1 fwd; 12 → no move; 13 → 1 back | source pairs "5-6 / 1 forwards" and "7-8 / 2 forwards" and "12-13 / 2 backwards" | **NO** |

### Findings
1. **(MAJOR — wrong scoring table) Sculptor scoring bands shifted.** The source table (OCR with single-digit row labels dropped, but multi-character labels adjacent to their values) reads: "1-2 / 2 backwards / 1 backwards / No move / **5-6 / 1 forwards** / **7-8 / 2 forwards** / 1 forwards / No move / 1 backwards / **12-13 / 2 backwards**". The consistent reconstruction is 9 rows: 1-2 → 2 back; 3 → 1 back; 4 → no move; 5-6 → 1 forward; 7-8 → 2 forward; 9 → 1 forward; 10 → no move; 11 → 1 back; 12-13 → 2 back (symmetric, value count matches exactly, and the visible labels "5-6", "7-8", "12-13" sit directly before their values). The summary's table (1-2 → 2 back; 3-4 → 1 back; **5-6 → no move; 7-8 → 1 forward; 9-10 → 2 forward; 11 → 1 forward; 12 → no move; 13 → 1 back**) contradicts all three directly visible label/value pairings, gets most bands wrong, and drops the 2-backwards penalty at 12-13 entirely. This is the game's core risk/reward mechanism (sculpt difficulty incentive); a player using the summary's table would score sculptors incorrectly in most cases.
2. **(MINOR — house rule presented as official) End-game scoring scope.** Summary: "Optional Rule (Recommended): **Regardless of how the game ends**, apply the following end-game scoring." In the source, the official optional rule is attached to ending #2 (13th arrow); "regardless of how the game ends" is Richard Vickery's house rule ("Richard's Notes ... we apply the 13th arrow rule before declaring a winner"). The summary folds a translator's house rule into the official rule.
3. Note: summary says "five types of spaces" but lists four — the source also announces five and describes four (fifth space evidently lost to OCR); mirrored faithfully, not counted.
4. Note: Overview trivia (Riddle-Master inspiration, 1988 Spiel des Jahres) is not in the source; externally accurate and non-gameplay, not counted.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — central sculptor-scoring table contradicts the source at every verifiable anchor point.

## battle-cattle-the-card-game — MAJOR

### Numbers checked
| # | Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|------------------|-----------------|-----|
| 1 | 6 large Cow Cards, 110 playing cards | "six large Cow Cards; and 110 playing cards" | yes |
| 2 | Deal 5 cards to each player | "deal five to each player" | yes |
| 3 | Draw 1 card per turn; hand limit 7 at end of turn | "a player draws enough cards to bring his hand **up to six** cards"; no hand limit / end-of-turn discard exists | **NO** |
| 4 | Armor card stops 3 points | "reduce the damage from that attack by 3 points" | yes |
| 5 | Breach at 12+ damage on a side | "takes 12 or more points of damage, it is breached" | yes |
| 6 | Body destroyed at 5+ damage | "takes 5 or more points of damage, it has been disabled" | yes |
| 7 | Kill = 10 points | "10 points for each opponent he has reduced to hamburger" | yes |
| 8 | Sole Survivor bonus = 10 points | "the winner of the duel. He scores **20 points**" | **NO** |
| 9 | "Each cow still alive at duel end: 5 points" | "If the duel ended in a **tie**, each player still in the duel at the end gets **10 points**" | **NO** |
| 10 | Game won at 60 points (Ace) | "scored 60 points to become an Ace" / "reaches 60 points at the end of a round ... declared Chief Beef" | yes |
| 11 | Called Shot can hit any side | "four Called Shot cards which will let you attack any side" | yes |

### Findings
1. **(MAJOR — invented turn mechanic) Draw phase and hand limit.** Summary: "Draw 1 card from the draw pile (hand limit of 7 cards at end of turn) ... discard down if over." Source: "In his turn, a player draws enough cards to **bring his hand up to six cards**" and "A player may not draw cards at any time except the beginning of his turn. At that time, he draws enough cards to bring his hand up to six." There is no draw-1 rule and no 7-card end-of-turn limit anywhere in the source. Both numbers are invented.
2. **(MAJOR — wrong scoring values) Duel scoring.** Source: duel winner scores **20 points**; tie → each surviving player gets **10 points** (only on a tie, which occurs after the deck is exhausted twice). Summary gives "Sole Survivor bonus 10 points" and "Each cow still alive at duel end: 5 points" — both values wrong, and the 5-point line generalizes the tie rule into something that always applies. (The 5-point Prestige Bonus by majority vote — the only 5-point award in the source — is omitted entirely.)
3. **(MINOR — contradicted option) "Pass" as a turn action.** Summary: "play one Attack card ... OR play a Special ... OR pass." Source: "He must then either: Attack; Play a Special; or **Discard** ... the player must place one or more cards in the discard pile." Doing nothing is not allowed; the mandatory discard is a real cost the summary removes. Also only Fireproof Armor and Machine Gun Jams! may be played as the turn action ("Other Special cards can only be played on someone else's turn"), not any "your turn only" card such as Hoof Shot (which is actually played *with* an Attack card).
4. **(MAJOR — omitted core subsystem) Hooves.** The source has a full hooves system: Hoof Shot redirects damage to hooves; Moo-ve costs 2 points of own-hoof damage; hooves destroyed at **9+ points** (not a breach, armor cannot protect them); a cow without hooves cannot Ram, Moo-ve, T-Bone, Steer Clear, or Escape but can still shoot and win. The summary never mentions hooves, their 9-point threshold, or these restrictions, yet cites Skid kill-credit (which only makes sense with Moo-ve). T-Bone (follow-up attack on the same side), Steer Clear, Machine Gun Jams!, Fireproof Armor, and Autocannon Backfires are likewise absent.
5. **(MINOR — contradiction) Reshuffling.** Summary: "When the draw pile is empty, shuffle the discard pile to form a new draw pile." Source: reshuffle once; "If the players go through the deck a second time, **do not reshuffle**. All players still in the duel share equally in a tie." The summary's unconditional reshuffle removes the duel's time limit.
6. **(MINOR — omissions) Escaping misdescribed** (it is an end-of-turn announcement taking effect next turn if hooves intact, not "Special cards allow movement"); 2-3 player setup (remove one each of Machine Gun Jams!, Fireproof Armor, Autocannon Backfires!, Dud Milk Bomb!) omitted; first player is "to the left of the dealer", not random; 60-point win is checked at end of a round with a highest-points tiebreak, not strictly "first to accumulate".

**Verdict: MAJOR** — invented draw/hand-limit numbers, wrong duel scoring (20→10, 10→5), missing hooves subsystem, contradicted reshuffle rule.
