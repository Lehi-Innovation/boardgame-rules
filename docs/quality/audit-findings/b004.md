## 1989-dawn-of-freedom — VERDICT: MAJOR

The summary repeatedly imports Twilight Struggle mechanics that do not exist in 1989, gets core numbers wrong, and contradicts the rulebook on a game-losing rule. Several core scoring systems are missing.

### Finding 1 (MAJOR): Invented "Headline Phase"
- Summary (Turn Structure): "**Headline Phase** — Each player simultaneously selects 1 card to play as a Headline Event. Both are revealed and resolved." Also in Player Reference: "2. Headline Phase (simultaneous Event play)".
- Source 4.5 turn sequence: "1. Deal Strategy Cards / 2. Play Action Rounds / 3. Make Extra Support Check (if applicable) / 4. Verify Held Cards / 5. Celebrate New Year's Eve Party (if applicable) / 6. Advance Turn Marker / 7. Calculate Final Scoring (after turn 10)."
- The word "headline" appears nowhere in the extracted text (grep confirmed). This is a Twilight Struggle mechanic that does not exist in 1989.

### Finding 2 (MAJOR): Invented "Realignment" and "Remove Opponent Support" operations; Tiananmen Square Attempt omitted
- Summary (Actions): lists Ops options as "Place Support Points", "Remove Opponent Support: Spend Ops to remove enemy SPs", and "Realignment: Attempt to remove opponent influence through contested rolls."
- Source 6.0: "the player must choose to use all of the Operations points on one of the following Options: Support Point Placement, Support Checks, or a Tiananmen Square Attempt."
- "Realign" appears nowhere in the source (grep confirmed). There is no direct ops-for-removal spend; removal happens only via Support Checks (6.2). The third real option (Tiananmen Square Attempt, 6.3) is missing from the Actions list.

### Finding 3 (MAJOR): Support Check mechanics are wrong
- Summary: "Support Checks apply when placing into spaces controlled by the opponent — roll dice against the space's Stability Number" and (Special Rules) "When placing SPs in spaces controlled by the opponent, roll 2d6. If the roll exceeds the space's Stability Number, the placement succeeds."
- Source 6.1.3: placing an SP in an opponent-controlled space simply "costs two Operations points" — there is no roll for placement.
- Source 6.2.2–6.2.5: Support Checks are a separate operation. "Each Strategy Card played for Support Checks gives two Support Checks... regardless of the card's Operations value"; resolve by "multiply the Stability Number of the target space by two", then "roll a die, and add the Ops value of the card" with +1/–1 per adjacent friendly/opponent-controlled space; success removes opponent SPs equal to the difference (surplus adds friendly SPs). One die, not 2d6; target is doubled Stability, not Stability; it is not a placement mechanic.

### Finding 4 (MAJOR): Wrong number of Action Rounds
- Summary: "Players alternate playing cards (typically 6 rounds each)."
- Source 4.1: "Each player takes seven Action Rounds per turn, except the Communist may take eight Action Rounds when the Honecker Event is in effect." Also omitted: "The Communist always takes his Action Round first" (4.5.2) and hand size of 8 (4.5.1).

### Finding 5 (MAJOR): Penalty for holding a Scoring card contradicted
- Summary: "Scoring cards not played: If a player fails to play a Scoring card during the turn it was drawn, the opponent gains VP."
- Source 4.5.4: "If a player is holding a scoring card at this stage in the turn, that player loses the game." Source 10.2: "that player loses and his opponent is declared the winner. If both players hold Scoring Cards, the game is considered a draw." Losing the game is not "the opponent gains VP".

### Finding 6 (MAJOR): Power Struggle trigger and ordering are wrong
- Summary: "After country scoring, if the Democrat controls more Battleground spaces than the number of times the Communist has scored Power in that country, a Power Struggle occurs."
- Source 8.0: "Whenever a country's Scoring card is played, a Power Struggle in that country is immediately conducted" — unconditional, no battleground-vs-power-count test. And the order is reversed: country scoring is step 5 of the Power Struggle sequence ("5. Scoring: Finally, the country is scored..."), i.e. the struggle comes first, scoring last.

### Finding 7 (MAJOR omission): Communist "Scoring for Power" VP engine missing
- Source 8.4.6: if the Communist retains Power, he "scores a number of Victory Points equal to the country's Power Value multiplied by the number of times the Power Struggle card has been played" (example: Poland Power Value 3 → –3, –6, –9 VPs). Source 10.4.1: at Final Scoring the Communist gets a bonus per country where he retains Power (–4 / –8 / –12 / –16 / –20 / –24). Neither appears in the summary; this is one of the Communist's main VP sources. Also omitted from the Power Struggle procedure: card deal counts (8.1.1: "6 cards for the first space they control, 2 cards for each additional"), Raise the Stakes (8.2: discard 3 cards for +1 to both aftermath rolls), the Support Loss and Victory Point die rolls (8.4.1–8.4.3, with –2 Petition / +2 Rally modifiers), and the Democrat-topples-power threshold (8.4.4: modified VP die roll of 4+).

### Finding 8 (MINOR/MAJOR-adjacent): Tiananmen Square Track mechanism and benefits distorted
- Summary: "Players advance on this track through certain Events. The track provides benefits including... bonus dice in Power Struggles."
- Source 6.3.1: advancement is an Operations option (die roll + Ops vs printed number, one attempt per turn, 6.3.3); only one Event ('Tank Column/Tank Man*', 6.3.4) advances the marker. The listed awards (6.3.5) are: +1 to track rolls; play 1-Ops cards as 2-Ops; draw 3 keep 1; remove 2 opponent SPs; discard-and-redraw each turn; free 2-Ops Support Check at end of turn; play a card for Ops without triggering opponent's Event; play a card for both Ops and Event. No "bonus dice in Power Struggles" exists (grep confirmed).

### Finding 9 (MINOR): Power Struggle initiative rule garbled
- Summary: "Power Struggle Initiative: Determined by the rank of cards played. Higher rank = initiative."
- Source 8.3.1: "The player who played the Scoring card begins the Power Struggle with the initiative." 8.3.2 Step 3: the defender seizes initiative by rolling "equal to or higher than the rank of the Power Struggle card that the attacker used" (Rally rank 1 = automatic counterattack). Rank sets the difficulty of the defender's die roll; higher rank makes it harder for the opponent, not "higher rank = initiative".

### Finding 10 (MINOR): Country scoring details omitted/soft errors
- Summary's Domination ("More Battleground spaces + more total controlled spaces + Presence") vs source 9.2: must control more spaces AND more Battlegrounds AND "at least one non-Battleground and one Battleground space". Control matches 9.2.
- Omitted: 9.3.2 "Each player scores 1 additional VP for each Battleground space that he controls" and 9.3.3 net-difference scoring.
- Setup: summary's "Place Power markers showing Communist Power in each country" is not in source setup (3.1–3.5); power markers are placed only after Power Struggles (8.4.6). "Early War deck" should be "Early Year" (Twilight Struggle terminology drift).
- Victory: ±20 auto-victory supported (2.1.10, 10.1: "The instant one player reaches a score of 20 VP") but summary omits that auto-victory does not apply during Final Scoring (10.1, 10.4.2), that all countries are re-scored at Final Scoring (10.4.1), the zero = draw rule (10.4.3), and the New Year's Eve Party end (10.3).
