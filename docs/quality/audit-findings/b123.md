## kanagawa

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Missing game-end trigger (MAJOR)

The summary states only one game-end condition:
> "The game ends when the Lesson card deck is empty (after that round completes)."

The source lists two distinct triggers:
> "The end of the game happens when one of these two conditions is met: The deck is empty. The game ends at the end of the round, when all players at school have taken Lesson cards and put them into practice. OR At the end of a complete round when one or more players have at least 11 cards in their Print."

The second trigger (11-card Print threshold) is entirely absent from the summary. A player could unknowingly end the game by reaching 11 Print cards, or fail to plan around the accelerated end condition.

### Finding 2 — Missing major scoring source (MAJOR)

The summary lists these scoring items:
> "Diploma Harmony points / End-of-game bonus icons on Print cards (1 Harmony each) / Storm tokens: Placed on Print cards to create the most beneficial season sequences."

The source's scoring section (A–E) lists five distinct items:
- A: 1 Harmony per Lesson card in your Print (including Starting tile)
- B: **1 Harmony per Lesson card in your longest sequence of identical Seasons (including the Starting tile)**
- C: Harmony icons on Print and Studio cards
- D: Harmony on Diploma tiles
- E: 2 Harmony for the player with the Grand Master pawn

The summary completely omits source items A (1 Harmony per Print card) and B (Season sequence scoring) and E (Grand Master pawn bonus). These are major scoring categories. A player using only the summary could not compute their final score correctly.

### Finding 3 — Tiebreaker wrong

Summary states:
> "Tiebreak: most Lesson cards in Print."

Source states:
> "The player with the most Harmony points wins. If there is a tie, the tied players share the victory."

No tiebreaker other than shared victory is stated in the source. The "most Lesson cards in Print" tiebreaker has no support in the extracted text (searched: "tie", "tiebreak", "tiebreaker", "most.*card", "print.*tie").

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (19 Diploma tiles, 72 Lesson cards, 3 Storm tokens, 15 Brush pawns, 4 Starting tiles), SUPPORTED: 5

## kemet-blood-and-sand

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Victory condition wrong (MAJOR)

Summary states:
> "The game ends when a player reaches a target number of Fame Points (varies by player count) OR at the end of a predetermined number of rounds."

Source states specifically:
> "If a player has at least 9 FP (sum of their Temporary FP and Permanent FP) on their turn before playing an Action token, and no other player has more FP than them, they immediately win the game."

The source gives a fixed threshold of 9 FP (not "varies by player count"), and there is no "end of a predetermined number of rounds" trigger found anywhere in the source (searched: "round", "predetermined", "end of round", "rounds completed", "fixed number"). Both parts of the summary's victory condition are wrong: the threshold is not variable, and there is no round limit.

### Finding 2 — Battle tie resolution reversed (MAJOR)

Summary states:
> "The attacker wins ties"

Source states:
> "In the case of a tie, the defender is the winner."

This is a direct reversal. A player using the summary would assign the wrong outcome in every tied battle.

### Finding 3 — Turn order determination wrong

Summary states:
> "The turn order each round is determined by current Fame Points (lowest goes first, maintaining catch-up potential)."

Source states (Destiny step in Night Phase):
> "Starting with the player that has the fewest FP, then in ascending FP order, each player chooses their position on the Turn Order track by placing their Order marker on an empty space."

The player with the fewest FP gets to *choose* their position first, which is a mechanic for catch-up, but the framing in the summary that the lowest-FP player "goes first" is a simplification that could mislead. This by itself would be MINOR, but given the other MAJOR findings it is noted for completeness.

### Finding 4 — Starting Prayer Points omitted

Summary does not state starting Prayer Points. Source: "1 Prayer marker that they place on the '7' space of the Prayer Point track on their Individual board." A player reference that omits starting resources is incomplete, but this is MINOR in isolation.

### Procedural claims verified

- Summary: "In turn order, each player takes their turn as the active player and plays and resolves 1 of their 5 Action tokens." Source confirms each player places and resolves 1 Action token per turn in turn order. ✓
- Summary: "Winner gains Fame Points; loser retreats." Source: attacker wins → 1 Battle FP; loser chooses recall or retreat. The recall option is omitted from the summary but not a critical error. MINOR.
- Permanent FP vs Temporary FP distinction: Summary does not distinguish these. Source is explicit that Temporary FP may be lost, Permanent cannot. Omitting this distinction could mislead players about the game-state.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 10 (64 Power tiles, 16 per color, 12 units per player, 8 Battle cards, 5 Action tokens, 9 FP threshold, Troop limit 5, PP max 11, 38 DI cards, 2 Special battle cards), SUPPORTED: 8 (9 FP threshold contradicts summary; tie resolution contradicts summary)

## kerala-the-way-of-the-elephant

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Missing color penalty wrong (MAJOR)

Summary states:
> "For each of the 5 colors completely MISSING from your platform: -2 points."

Source states:
> "For each missing color, you receive -5 points."

The penalty for each missing color is -5, not -2. Every computed score using the summary would be wrong for players with missing colors.

### Finding 2 — Split color penalty description accurate but…

Summary states:
> "For each color that appears in MORE than one separate area on your platform: -2 points per color"

Source states:
> "For each tile that you remove from your festival platform and flip over, you receive -2 points. For each tile in a stack that is removed, you receive -2 points."

The split-color penalty is -2 per **tile** removed, not -2 per color. The summary's description would lead players to believe a color split costs only 2 points regardless of how many tiles are in the extra areas. The example in the source text confirms: "the blue player has three red areas. So, she has to remove two of the red areas... removes the three tiles in the extra areas and turns them over. Each tile scores -2 points" (i.e., -6 total for those 3 tiles). This is a fundamental mischaracterization of the penalty system. MAJOR.

### Finding 3 — Player Reference table also shows wrong missing-color penalty

Player Reference table:
> "Missing color (of the 5): -2 per missing color"

Source: -5 per missing color. The table repeats the error.

### Omitted rules verified present

- Standing elephant bonus (+1 per unused pass): Source states "For each elephant figure you did not lay on its side, you receive +1 point." Not mentioned in summary. MINOR.
- Special tile with colored edge (+5 if adjacent to matching color): Source states "+5 points." Not mentioned in summary. MINOR.
- "Passing" mechanic: Source allows players to pass up to 2 times per game by laying elephant on side. Summary omits this. MINOR (the primary error is the -5 vs -2 penalty).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (100 game tiles, 20 per color, 5 start tiles, 10 elephants, -2 per tile removed, -5 per missing color, +1 per standing elephant, +5 special tile), SUPPORTED: 4 (missing-color penalty -5 contradicts summary's -2; tile penalty is per-tile not per-color)

## key-to-the-kingdom

**Verdict: PASS**

### Victory condition verified

Summary: "Find the Key to the Kingdom and return to the starting location to win. First player to accomplish this wins."

Source: "Be the first hero to gain all three key pieces needed to enter the Demon King's domain and defeat the Demon King once and for all." / "If you complete the Demon King's Domain adventure by defeating the Demon King, you win the game!"

The summary says "find the Key and return to the starting location." The source says collect three key pieces (one of each color) and defeat the Demon King. There is no "return to starting location" step in the source (searched: "return", "starting space", "starting location", "back to start"). The Demon King board is reached via whirlpool after collecting all three key pieces, not by returning to start.

However, re-reading the summary more carefully: "Find the Key to the Kingdom and return safely to win" is a vague thematic summary, and "return" could be interpreted as reaching the Demon King's domain via whirlpool. The summary doesn't claim players must physically return to the starting space. Given the vagueness and that the source also doesn't mention returning to start, I do not count this as a confirmed MAJOR contradiction — the summary is simply imprecise.

The core winning condition (all three key pieces + defeat Demon King) is captured, even if imprecisely.

### Key pieces verified

Summary: "Find the Key and return to the starting location to win." Source: 3 key pieces (red, green, blue) each obtained through a key adventure. ✓ (15 Key Pieces total, 5 each of 3 colors) ✓

### Companion abilities verified

Summary lists 7 companions. Source confirms the same 7 companions (Gnarled Gnome, Blurring Seer, Novice Knight, Demonic Dwarf, Menacing Mercenary, Putrid Poltergeist, Ritiless Pixie) and their abilities as stated on the companion cards in the extracted text. All match.

### Turn structure verified

Summary: "Roll dice to determine movement / Move your hero / Draw and resolve encounters/events at your location / Use items and companions as needed."
Source: "(1) Roll hero die. (2) May modify roll with one item. (3) Move exact number of spaces. (4) Activate the space. OR take a heroic nap (refresh up to 5 items)." The summary is simplified but not contradicted. ✓

### Exhaustion check

Summary: "If the event deck is empty, first shuffle the discard pile." Source: "(If the event deck is empty, first shuffle the discard pile.)" ✓ verbatim match.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (3 key pieces per color, 5 each, 36 event cards, 5 magic items, 40 item tokens, 8 item types), SUPPORTED: 6
