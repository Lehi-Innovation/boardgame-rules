### Finding 1 — Injury Table Wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Injury table (d6): 1-2 = Flesh Wound, 3-4 = Down, 5-6 = Out of Action"

**Source (lines 1262-1263):** "Roll a D6 and consult the Injury Table again (1-2 = flesh wound, 3-5 = down and 6 = out of action)."

**Also confirmed at lines 1243-1257 (Injury Table):** 1-2 = Flesh Wound, 3-5 = Down, 6 = Out of Action.

The summary's "3-4 = Down, 5-6 = Out of Action" is wrong. The correct split is 3-5 = Down and only 6 = Out of Action. This means the summary overstates the chance of being Out of Action and understates the chance of going Down. Every game where the injury table is consulted would be played wrong.

### Finding 2 — Turn Sequence Missing Recovery Phase (MAJOR)

**Summary (Player Reference):** "Turn sequence: Movement > Shooting > Hand-to-Hand Combat"

**Source (lines 629-634):** "your turn is divided into four distinct phases. Each phase is completed before beginning the next. So, begin by moving all your models, then shoot with all models that can do so, then fight hand-to-hand, and finally recover any models that have gone down due to injuries."

**Source (lines 636-639):** "Your opponent works through each phase, moving, shooting, fighting hand-to-hand and recovery, after which his turn is over."

The Recovery phase is the 4th mandatory phase of every turn — it is where Down warriors roll to recover or go Out of Action, and where Pinned models stand up. Omitting it from the turn sequence would leave players unable to recover downed warriors each turn.

### Finding 3 — Pinning Rule Wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Pinning: Models hit by shooting may become pinned and must pass a Leadership test to act normally."

**Source (lines 1374-1380):** "A warrior that is pinned at the start of its turn will automatically recover from being pinned in the recovery phase at the end of the turn."

**Source (lines 1388-1395):** "If a model has at least one other warrior from the mob within 2" then he can try to escape pinning at the start of his turn... Roll a D6, and if the score is equal to or less than the model's Initiative value he has recovered his wits immediately."

The summary says Leadership test; the source says automatic recovery in the recovery phase (no test required), or optional early recovery using Initiative (not Leadership). This is a double error — wrong attribute and wrong procedure. Players using the summary would roll Leadership instead of Initiative, and wouldn't know pinning resolves automatically in the Recovery phase without any test.

### Finding 4 — Cover Modifier Values Wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary (Player Reference):** "Shooting modifiers: -1 for long range, -1 for cover, -1 if shooter moved, -1 for small target"

**Source (lines 4689-4698 — HIT MODIFIER CHART):**
- "-1 Target in partial cover"
- "-2 Target in cover"
- "-1 Firing from a vehicle which used its thrusters"
- "-1 Small target"

The summary collapses the two-tier cover system into a single "-1 for cover." The actual rules give -1 for partial cover but -2 for full cover. The summary also misstates "-1 if shooter moved" — the source penalty only applies when firing from a vehicle that used its thrusters (not all movement). This would cause players to under-penalise shots at models in full cover and over-penalise shots from infantry who ran.
