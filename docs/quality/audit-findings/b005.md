## 51st-state-ultimate-edition — MINOR

The extracted source is the **Master Set** rulebook (identical file to `51st-state-master-set-rules.txt`), not an Ultimate Edition document. Core rules in the summary are faithful to it.

### Findings

1. **Unsupported expansion claims (Overview/Components).** Summary: "including the base game and six expansions (New Era, Winter, Scavengers, Allies, Moloch, and No Man's Land)" and "200+ cards including base set and all expansions". Source lists exactly "200 CARDS … 88 51ST STATE BASE CARDS, 12 CONNECTION CARDS, 50 NEW ERA CARDS, 50 WINTER CARDS". Scavengers/Allies/Moloch/No Man's Land appear nowhere in the source (grep confirmed). Content claims are invented relative to the source; no rules impact.
2. **Solo mode omitted.** Source has full solo rules (virtual player drafting, attack pile of 3 cards, 2 VP per claimed Connection / razed Location, achievement table 30–80+). Frontmatter says player_count 1-4, but the summary contains none of the solo rules. Omission of an entire variant; base multiplayer rules unaffected.
3. Trivial: Develop with a Development token also waives the shared-Type requirement ("If you do this, the cards don't need to share a common Type") — summary lists the token only as a Brick substitute.

### Numbers checked (all supported unless noted)
- End trigger 25 VP; +1 VP per Location at end; tie-breakers Goods → Locations → shared ✓ (source lines 5–7, 416–418)
- Defense values: Production 3 / Feature 4 / Action 5 ✓ (lines 137–139)
- Draft: players+1 cards, twice; clockwise then counter-clockwise from last player ✓ (lines 153–163)
- Develop: 1 Brick (or Dev token), +1 VP ✓; Take Connection: 2 Workers ✓ (line 355); Send Worker: opponent gains 1 Worker ✓
- Shield: max 1 per Location, +1 Defense, free placement ✓ (lines 299–303, 365–376)
- Components: 13 Ammo/Bullet ✓, 9 Development ✓, 10 Shield ✓, 4 Multiplier ✓, min 30 Brick/Fuel/Gun/Iron ✓, min 38 Workers ✓; "200+ cards" vs source "200 cards" (drift, see finding 1)

## 7-ages — MAJOR

Components, setup, victory conditions and end trigger are accurate, but the **Turn Structure/Actions sections misrepresent the core of the game**.

### Findings

1. **Wrong action sequence (invented phases, wrong order).** Summary lists a 9-step sequence: "1. Start Empires, 2. Discard/Draw, 3. Progress, 4. Trade, 5. Civil Wars & Revolts, 6. Maneuver, 7. Combat, 8. Production, 9. Glory". Source (page 2): "The actions are taken in this order: (1) start empire (2) production (3) trade and progress (4) manoeuvre (5) destiny (6) civilise (7) discard empire." Production is action 2, not 8; "trade and progress" is a single action; "Civil Wars & Revolts" is not a phase (Civil War exists only as event card 67); "Combat" is not a separate phase (conflict is resolved inside the manoeuvre action); Glory is harvested in the End of Turn, not as action 9; the civilise and discard-empire actions are missing entirely.
2. **Core action-selection system omitted.** Source: players secretly place one action marker face-down on each empire card (plus possibly one extra), with empire maximums of 7 (2P), 5 (3P), 4 (4P), 3 (5+P), and wild-card markers with a glory penalty for repeating an action. None of this is in the summary, which says only "Players take actions simultaneously using cards."
3. **Invented mechanic: dice.** Summary: "Combat is resolved through unit comparisons and die rolls." There are no dice in the game (grep for die/dice/roll: zero matches). Conflict: "each player involved draws a card from the deck" plus front-line/support values, elite, age, terrain modifiers.
4. **Misstatement:** "Each game turn represents an age." In the source, ages are bands of the 49-space progress track that individual empires occupy; turns are not ages.
5. End of Turn free progress (+1 progress per empire) omitted.

### Numbers checked (all supported)
- 2 A1 (594x840mm) maps; 6 countersheets (576 x 5/8", 300 x 1/2" counters); 110 cards ✓ (lines 35–38)
- 15 empire colours ✓ (line 48); deal 7 cards each ✓ (line 51); 2–7 players ✓
- End trigger: beyond 49th progress level (Age of Aquarius) or internet, +7 bonus glory ✓ (lines 60–64)
- Win: most glory; tie → most advanced empire; still tied → both win ✓ (lines 53–59)
- First-player auction by highest card, ties replay ✓ (lines 66–82)

## 7-wonders-architects — MAJOR

Core turn rules, card-type rules, battle resolution, construction costs, and scoring are all faithful. However, the Wonder-effects reference table is wrong for two of seven Wonders, and the Conflict-token setup numbers are unverifiable.

### Findings

1. **Ephesus effect wrong.** Summary: "Ephesus — Take the top card from decks to your left and right." Source: "Take the top card from the central deck and place it in front of you. / Ephesus" (lines 58–60). The effect-to-name alignment (effect text followed by Wonder name) is confirmed by Alexandria ("any deck", line 55–57), Babylon (progress token, 70–71) and Giza (no effect/more VP, 72–74), all of which the summary itself matches.
2. **Olympia effect wrong.** Summary: "Olympia — Add 1 Shield to your total." Source: "Take the top card from the decks to your left and your right and place them in front of you. / Olympia" (lines 61–63); "Add 1 Shield to your total Shields." belongs to Rhodes (lines 64–65). The summary gives the same +1 Shield effect to both Olympia and Rhodes, and Ephesus' real central-deck effect appears nowhere — two Wonders' owners would play wrong rules.
3. **Conflict-token counts unverifiable/likely invented.** Summary table: 2-3 players → 3, 4-5 → 4, 6-7 → 5. The source table lost its values to OCR (lines 169–172 show only "Number of players / Number of Conflict tokens / 2 - 3 / 6 - 7"). The 4-5 row and all token counts are not in the source; since the game ships 6 Conflict tokens and setup says to remove the remainder, the top row likely uses 6, making 3/4/5 suspect. Flagged as unsupported rather than confirmed-wrong.
4. Minor: Urbanism/Crafts/Jewellery Progress tokens are restricted to Grey cards showing **specific resource icons** ("When you take a [icon] or [icon] Grey card…", lines 76–88; icons lost to OCR); the summary drops the restriction, making the tokens read stronger than they are. Also omits "Certain Wonders will give you an option for the order of construction" (line 215–216).

### Numbers checked (all supported unless noted)
- 235 cards / 8 backs; 7 Wonders x 5 pieces; 7 holders; 8 trays; 15 Progress tokens; 6 Conflict tokens; 28 Military Victory tokens; 1 Cat pawn ✓ (lines 37–44)
- Stage costs 2 diff / 2 same / 3 diff / 3 same / 4 diff ✓ (lines 206–210); must build if able, incl. multiple stages ✓
- Science: 2 identical or 3 different → must take Progress token; choose from 4 (3 face-up + stack top) ✓

4. Trivial omissions: VP track wraps at 90 (+90 to score); forge and market helper spaces exist from game start.

## a-column-of-fire — MAJOR

Components, setup, dice system, half-year structure, character/trading-house mechanics, and the 50-VP end trigger are accurate. But the summary gets the game's **primary scoring system (selling goods) wrong**, presents a country/goods table contradicted by the source, omits the entire end-game bonus scoring, and invents a protection-tile use.

### Findings

1. **Selling-goods VP wrong (contradicted rule).** Summary: "VP earned equals the trading house site number." Source: "The trading house site number has no bearing on the sale of goods for victory points" (lines 204–205) and "The player earns four victory points for one type of good, while for the other type, the player earns two victory points" (lines 646–647). Site numbers matter only for religious-conflict VP. The source calls selling goods the primary VP source, so this misleads on the central scoring mechanism.
2. **Country/goods table contradicted.** Summary table: England=Book+Ore, France=Cloth+Wine, Netherlands=Book+Cloth, Spain=Ore+Wine. Source examples: England sells cloth (4 VP) and wine (2 VP) (lines 660–663); France sells book (4) and ore (2) (lines 912–914); Netherlands sells wine for 4 (line 676); Spain sells cloth for 2 (lines 923–925). Every row of the summary's table conflicts with these examples.
3. **End-game bonus scoring omitted entirely.** Source (lines 601–619): at game end add (a) 2 VP per trading house, (b) 1 VP per unused protection tile, (c) 1 VP per two unsold goods tiles (rounded down), (d) VP on advantage tiles (five 1-pt, five 2-pt, two 3-pt; usable only at end, cannot trigger the 50-VP end). A missing scoring source worth ~10+ VP.
4. **Invented protection-tile use.** Summary: protection tiles "can protect your trading house during religious conflicts." Source uses (lines 756–771): neutralize a negative event; one event type converts a tile to VP; avoid placing a religion stone when drawing a character. Nothing protects trading houses in conflicts.
5. **Conflict timing wrong.** Summary: conflict "erupts at end of the 2nd half-year." Source detailed rule: "at the end of the player's turn during which the spaces became covered … It makes no difference if the turn occurs during the 1st or 2nd half of the year" (lines 690–699). (One earlier source sentence loosely says "at the end of the 2nd half of the year," but the dedicated Religious Conflicts section is explicit.)
6. Smaller omissions: neutral stones ignored in conflicts and tied conflicts avoided (lines 705–742); tie-breakers (most unsold goods → highest religion die) (lines 628–632); pay-VP-to-skip action spaces and mandatory movement (lines 557–582); minority-religion compensation VP (lines 798–803); 1-VP cost for declining a character / 3 VP to free a die (lines 805–815). Component wording "24 dice (4 per player…)" garbles "4 of each of 6 colors; 6 dice per player" (the Setup section states 6 dice correctly).

### Numbers checked (all supported unless noted)
- 80 cards = 52 character + 16 event + 4 summary + 4 religion + 4 Loch Leven ✓ (lines 44–51)
- 56 goods (14 each) ✓; 30 religion stones (10 each) ✓; 24 dice ✓; 16 trading houses, 16 protection, 28 advantage, 4 cross, 2 torches ✓ (lines 52–73)
- VP disk starts on space 2 ✓ (line 115); black die 1–5 with second 3 (no 6) ✓ (lines 128–130)
- Die colors: White=England, Blue=France, Orange=Netherlands, Brown=Spain, purple wild ✓ (lines 120–126)
- End: 50+ VP, finish year through player right of starting player ✓ (lines 237–239, 590–597)
- Sale VP "= site number": CONTRADICTED (finding 1); goods-by-country table: CONTRADICTED (finding 2)
