## d-day-dice — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component counts, dice mechanics, resource tables, and the 5-phase turn structure are all accurate, but the victory/endgame procedure is misstated in a way that contradicts the source and would mislead players at the table.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Victory procedure contradicts source.** Summary: "When all Units are in the Bunker, play one more full turn. If all Units survive that final Combat, players win." Source (l.590-601): "All players must enter the Bunker and survive with at least 1 Soldier to win... When a Unit successfully enters the Bunker, it is considered 'safe' and no longer in harm's way (it stops rolling dice)... The game ends when the last Unit successfully enters the Bunker." Each Unit faces the Bunker's Combat only on the turn it enters (the map sections confirm: "survive entering the Bunker and the combat that immediately follows", l.769-771); there is no collective extra turn once all Units are inside. Following the summary, early-entering Units would keep playing turns and/or everyone would take an additional round of Bunker DEF combat — likely turning won games into losses.
2. **MAJOR (supporting) — Invented rule:** "Units cannot stay more than 1 turn in the Bunker" (summary l.135). No basis in the source; under the real rule the question never arises because Units that enter are removed from harm's way ("safe", stops rolling dice; on Gold Beach explicitly "removed from play", l.866-868).
3. **MAJOR (supporting) — Omitted endgame mechanic:** Every surviving Specialist of a Unit that entered the Bunker is placed beside it and "reduces the DEF of the Bunker by 1 point for the remaining Units" (l.597-599, with simultaneous-entry exception). This cooperative assist is central to staggered Bunker assaults and is entirely missing.
4. **MINOR — MGF wording.** Summary: "Machine Gun Fire affects all Units in the same Sector individually." Source: "one player rolls a regular six-sided die (d6)... adds this result to the DEF of the Sector" (l.536-537) — one shared roll applies to all Units present (unlike Landmines, where each Unit rolls its own die, l.566-569). "Individually" could prompt per-Unit MGF rolls.
5. **Trivial omissions:** second explicit losing condition is actually covered under Phase 4; rule-conflict precedence order (l.621-629), optional rules, and per-map specials reasonably omitted from a summary.

## dark-moon — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Setup, turn sequence, status-card table, actions, and win conditions are accurate, but the summary fabricates the dice facings (contradicted by the source's own examples) and omits/misrepresents two core systems (quarantine restrictions, shield testing).

Contradicted by source examples: rolls include **+3 and +4** (l.572 "(-1)(+1)(+4)(-2)", l.584 "(-1)(+3)(+2)", l.587 "(+3)(+4)") — impossible under the claimed facings | **N** |

*Source is internally inconsistent on Status cards (component list says 7: l.11-13; card notes say "STATUS CARDS (8)" l.125; setup says "Shuffle the four Infected Status cards" l.195). Summary follows the component list — not counted as a summary error.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Fabricated die facings.** Stated twice (Components and Player Reference). The OCR lost the actual faces, and the summary invented specific values that the source's own play examples contradict (dice showing +3 and +4). Anyone teaching or analyzing the game from this summary gets wrong probabilities.
2. **MAJOR — Quarantine system misrepresented.** Summary: "Quarantined players have their die limit reduced by 2. They are still in the game and still take turns." Source (l.649-659): on their turn quarantined players "may only use the actions CALL VOTE, ISSUE ORDER, and REVEAL AS INFECTED" and "do NOT draw Task cards at the end of their turn." Choosing Task cards is the Infected's main weapon (l.974-977), so playing quarantine as "normal turns minus 2 dice" guts the game's core counter-traitor mechanic.
3. **MAJOR — "Testing the Shield" omitted entirely.** Any time Shield tokens are drawn, the active player rolls a die against the track's Failure Condition; on failure they must draw a Fatigue or Outpost token (l.537-544, l.812-820, l.908-911). A recurring damage engine feeding all three Infected win conditions, absent from the summary.
4. **MINOR — Voluntary Blood Testing misattributed.** Summary: "The 3-4 player game removes the 'Voluntary Blood Testing' card from Status cards." Source (l.172-179) says to remove the card in 3p/4p games but never says it is a Status card; status cards are dealt by team counts. Unsupported attribution.
5. **MINOR — Final Event deck change omitted.** When the Final Event begins, Complication Tasks are removed and a Malfunction-only Task deck is formed (l.634-638).
6. Verified accurate: 9-step turn order (l.253-261), all six actions including Issue Order details and vote mechanics, Commander die voting ban (l.303), tie-breaking (l.313), Repair Outpost always available (l.289-291), Sabotage token behavior (l.715-729), Task deck reshuffle without revealing discards (l.367-372).

## dark-souls-the-board-game — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Components, setup, sparks, and victory/defeat conditions are accurate, but the summary inverts the encounter activation order, invents treasure-chest costs/placement, and misstates the Aggro token and Block rules.

### Numbers checked (12: 11 supported, 1 fabricated)

| # | Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Enemies: 2 Sentinel, 3 SK Swordsman, 3 SK Greatbowman, 2 Large Hollow, 3 Hollow, 3 Crossbow Hollow | l.161-166 | Y |
| 2 | Treasure: 13 Starting, 40 Class-Specific, 60 Common, 10 Legendary, 18 Boss | l.195-199 | Y |
| 3 | Encounter deck: 12 each of Level 1/2/3 | l.221-223 | Y |
| 4 | Dice: 5 Black, 4 Blue, 2 Orange, 4 Green Dodge | l.233-236 | Y |
| 5 | Cubes: 32 Damage red, 32 Stamina black, 16 Level Up white | l.251-253 | Y |
| 6 | Condition tokens: 5 each (Bleed, Poison, Frostbite, Stagger) | l.254-257 | Y |
| 7 | Tiles: 1 Bonfire, 6 Exploration, 1 Mini Boss, 1 Main Boss | l.263-266 | Y |
| 8 | Sparks: 1P=5, 2P=4, 3P=3, 4P=2 | l.284-291 | Y |
| 9 | Setup: shuffle 6 tiles, lay out 4; Fog Gate on farthest tile | l.278-283 | Y |
| 10 | Max 3 models per node; 1 boss per node; push when exceeded | l.395-403 | Y |
| 11 | Ember: reduce damage by 1 when suffering 3+ from one attack | l.506-508 | Y |
| 12 | "Treasure Chests: spend Souls to draw from Treasure Deck (2 Souls per draw)" | Contradicted: opening a chest is free and gives 2 cards ("open the chest... immediately drawing two cards", l.778-785); buying treasure at Blacksmith Andre costs **1 soul** per card (l.627-628; 2 souls only in campaign play, l.1557-1558) | **N** |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Activation order inverted.** Summary: "Character Activations: Each character activates once per round... Enemy Activations: After all characters activate, enemies activate." Source (l.834-846): encounters start "with the enemy models," and ALL enemies activate between EACH single character activation ("1. All enemies activate. 2. Knight activates. 3. All enemies activate. 4. Warrior activates..."). With the summary's order, enemies act once per round instead of once per character — drastically changing difficulty and tactics.
2. **MAJOR — Invented treasure-chest mechanics.** Beyond the fabricated "2 Souls per draw" cost (see #12 above), the summary claims "If all enemies are defeated, place treasure chests and gravestones for rewards." Source: chests and gravestones are terrain placed on terrain nodes during encounter SETUP (l.727-788); chests open free for 2 treasure cards after victory; gravestones block movement and give boss info (l.756-760). Also omits actual soul rewards: winning a non-boss encounter adds 2 souls per character; a boss encounter adds 1 soul per character per remaining spark (l.859-862).
3. **MAJOR (supporting) — Aggro token rule wrong.** Summary: "Goes to the character who attacked most recently." Source: a character gains the Aggro token at the start of their activation, whether or not they attack (l.996-998); at encounter start it goes to the character who led the way in (l.831-832).
4. **MINOR — Block does not cost Stamina.** Summary: "Block: When attacked, spend Stamina to block." Source (l.1163-1175): block/resist rolls are free — only Dodge costs 1 Stamina (l.1188). The summary makes defense look more expensive than it is.
5. **MINOR — Core Stamina recovery omitted.** Characters gain 2 Stamina at the start of each activation (l.997) — the only routine Stamina recovery besides Estus; without it the game is unplayable as described.
6. **MINOR — Boss Heat Up mechanic omitted.** At the Heat Up point, a random Heat Up behaviour card is shuffled into the deck (l.1304-1310, l.1356-1360); summary covers behaviour decks and arcs but not this.
7. **MINOR — Other small drifts:** traps are placed on basic nodes (not terrain nodes) and never affect enemies (l.790-804); Embers are discarded only when defeated and forced to rest (l.508-509), not on every rest; conditions auto-expire at end of activation/encounter rather than "persist until cleared" (l.969-972); solo game starts with 16 souls in the cache (l.596-597), omitted; Firekeeper level-up costs (2/4/8 souls, l.658-663) omitted; dice color ranking ("better/best") unsupported (faces not in extracted text) but plausible.
8. Verified accurate: win = defeat Main Boss (l.700-701); lose = sparks at 0 then a character dies (l.296-300); endurance bar shared Stamina/Damage with death when full (l.879-889); post-mini-boss reset adds transposed + legendary treasures (l.329-336); boss arcs, behaviour cards, Health dials (l.1284-1326); movement Walk 0/Run 1 Stamina partially captured.
