## empires-age-of-discovery

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Colony scoring timing (MAJOR)

Summary: "End-Game Scoring (after turn 8) — Victory points come from: Regions: Player with the most colonists in each region scores VP"

Source (lines 564–565): "New World colonies score victory points at the end of Age I (end of turn 3), Age II (end of turn 6) and Age III (end of turn 8, the end of the game.)"

The summary presents colony VP as end-game only. The source clearly states colonies score three times (end of each age). This is a fundamental scoring omission — players using the summary would miss the Age I and Age II colony scoring rounds.

### Finding 2 — Invented "Trade Goods VP" scoring source (MAJOR)

Summary: "Trade Goods: Bonus VP for most trade goods."

Source (lines 586–606): VP sources listed as Discoveries, Colonies, Capital Buildings, Economy. No general "bonus VP for most trade goods" exists. The only trade-good-related VP is through the Mercantilism capital building (1 VP per good owned by that building's owner) — which is a building-specific benefit, not a general rule.

No support found (searched: "trade good" AND "victory point", "trade goods" AND "bonus", "most trade goods").

### Finding 3 — Missing "Discoveries" VP source (MAJOR)

Summary's VP list omits Discoveries entirely.

Source (lines 588–590): "Discoveries: The red number on the discovery counter or discovery card. The cards and counters owned by a player are scored at the end of the game only."

A player following the summary would not score discovery counters at game end.

### Finding 4 — Capital building cost amounts omitted

Summary: "paying their cost" with no amounts given.

Source (lines 405–406): "$10 in Age I, $14 in Age II and $20 in Age III."

This is a strategic number omission (MINOR on its own), but in context of the other findings, the overall verdict is MAJOR.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (starting money, colony VPs 6/2, age counts, building costs by age, income table values), SUPPORTED: 5 (starting $10, colony 1st=6 VPs/2nd=2 VPs, 8 turns, age structure)

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Dive token exhaustion rule inverted (MAJOR)

Summary (Special Rules): "Dive tokens and Ocean boards are limited; when stacks run out, those actions become unavailable at those depths."

Source (lines 534–535): "Discard spent tokens into a discard pile. If the Dive token supply ever runs out, shuffle the discards into a new supply."

Direct contradiction: the summary says dive becomes unavailable when tokens run out; the source says to shuffle discards into a new supply. Players following the summary would incorrectly stop taking Dive actions.

Summary does not mention the extra round in solo mode (minor on its own, noted for completeness).

## entdecker-exploring-new-horizons

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — All three unit costs wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "Scout: Costs nothing. Fort: Costs 1 gold. Settlement: Costs 2 gold."

Source (lines 429–432):
```
Settlement . . . 6 gold pieces
Fort . . . . . . 3 gold pieces
Scout . . . . .  1 gold piece
```

All three unit costs are wrong. Scout is not free (costs 1 gold). Fort costs 3, not 1. Settlement costs 6, not 2. This makes the whole economy of the game unusable from the summary.

### Finding 2 — Island scoring mechanism wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "The player with the most scouts (forts = 2, settlements = 3) on the island scores the full points."

Source (lines 562–573): The Main Discoverer is determined by unit type hierarchy: "A Fort is more valuable than any number of scouts. A settlement is more valuable than any number of scouts or forts. So, if any player has a settlement on the island, he is the Main Discoverer. If there are no settlements, then the player with the most forts is the Main Discoverer."

The summary describes a weighted-count system (forts = 2 scouts, settlements = 3 scouts). The source uses a completely different hierarchical dominance system: any settlement beats any number of forts/scouts; most forts beats any number of scouts. A single settlement always beats 10 forts in the summary's wrong system vs. source's actual system.

### Finding 3 — 3rd and 4th place scoring missing

Summary: "Most scouts = full points, second = half points"

Source (lines 583–586): "the player with the second best units on the island scores half this number of Discovery Points. Then the third best receives half again as many points. (One quarter of the full value of the island.) If all four players are on the island, than the fourth player receives half again (one eighth) as many points."

Summary omits 3rd-place (1/4) and 4th-place (1/8) scoring positions.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreaker wrong

Summary: "Ties: player who placed their unit first on the island wins."

Source (lines 574–577): "If there is a tie, such as when two players both have settlements on the island, then the next most valuable type of unit will determine who wins the tie. If both players have exactly the same units on the island, then they are both Main Discoverers."

The summary invents a "placed first" tiebreaker. The source uses unit-type hierarchy further, then grants dual Main Discoverer status if units are exactly equal. No "first placed" tiebreaker exists.

### Finding 5 — Game-end tiebreaker missing

Source (line 650): "In the case of a tie for the most points, the winner is the tied player with the most gold."

Summary omits the game-end tiebreaker.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (unit counts by player count, unit costs 3x, produce token values 5/10/15, starting gold 7, tile costs 1/4, island scoring fractions, scout value equivalents), SUPPORTED: 6 (player counts/scouts, starting gold=7, tile costs 1 hidden/4 open, produce values 5/10/15)
