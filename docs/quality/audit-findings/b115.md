## here-i-stand

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter note
`SOURCE_SUSPECT` warning triggered because the game title tokens "here" and "stand" were not found in the first 3000 characters of the extracted file (which begins with a table of contents). Manual inspection confirms the source is the Here I Stand rulebook by Ed Beach, GMT Games, 2nd Printing — fully applicable.

### Finding 1 (MAJOR): Victory conditions describe a non-existent per-power VP target system

**Summary claims:**
> "At the end of the game, the power closest to its VP goal (or that has exceeded it) wins."
> (Victory Determination Phase section): "After the final turn, compare each power's VP to their target. The power closest to (or farthest above) their target wins."

**Source (Section 23.3, line 4273):**
> "Standard Victory: If any of the powers have reached 25 VP or more, the game ends with a Standard Victory. The power with the highest VP total is declared the winner. If two or more powers tie for highest total, the power with the highest total in the previous turn is declared the winner."

There is no per-power "VP goal" or "target" in the game. All powers share the same 25 VP threshold for Standard Victory. The winner is determined by highest VP total, not by who is "closest to their target." This would cause players to score the game completely incorrectly.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 1 (25 VP threshold), SUPPORTED: 1

### Finding 2 (MAJOR): Domination Victory entirely omitted

**Summary claims:** Victory Determination Phase checks only for automatic victory or end-of-game comparison to VP targets. No mention of Domination Victory.

**Source (Section 23.3, line 4284):**
> "Domination Victory: If during the Victory Determination Phase, if a power's VP total is less than 25 but is at least 5 VP greater than each of the other powers, it wins a Domination Victory. This victory can only occur in the Victory Determination phase of Turn 4 or later. Do not check for a Domination victory during Turns 1, 2, and 3."

A complete victory type is absent from the summary. Players using the summary would not know to check for Domination Victory at all, and the game could be lost/won without any player knowing.

No support found (searched: "domination", "5 VP greater", "5 VP ahead") — entirely absent from summary.

### Finding 3 (MAJOR): Standard Victory triggers mid-game, not only at end — summary implies end-of-Turn-9 only

**Summary claims:**
> "After the final turn, compare each power's VP to their target. The power closest to (or farthest above) their target wins."

This description implies victory is only determined at the end of the last turn. But:

**Source (Section 23.3, line 4273):**
> "If any of the powers have reached 25 VP or more, the game ends with a Standard Victory."
> "If after checking for standard and domination victories, there is still no winner, and this is not yet Turn 9, advance the turn marker on the Turn Track to the next box and start another turn."

Standard Victory is checked every turn (Phase 9) and can end the game at any turn when a power reaches 25+ VP. Time Limit Victory (end of Turn 9, highest VP) is the only end-of-game scenario, but the summary describes an end-of-game mechanism that does not exist (comparing each power to its individual "target").

### Finding 4 (MAJOR): Turn structure omits three named phases — including New World Phase

**Summary Turn Structure (6 phases listed):**
1. Card Draw Phase
2. Diplomacy Phase
3. Spring Deployment
4. Action Phase
5. Winter Phase
6. Victory Determination Phase

**Source (Section 7 Sequence of Play, lines 866–910):**
Nine phases per turn:
1. Luther's 95 Theses Phase (Turn 1 only)
2. Card Draw Phase
3. Diplomacy Phase
4. Diet of Worms Phase (Turn 1 only)
5. Spring Deployment Phase
6. Action Phase
7. Winter Phase
8. New World Phase — "Resolve Voyages of Exploration / Resolve Voyages of Conquest"
9. Victory Determination Phase

The New World Phase (Phase 8) is entirely absent from the summary's turn structure, even though the summary has a full "New World" Actions section. Players would not know when to resolve voyages. The Turn 1-only phases (Luther's 95 Theses and Diet of Worms) are also absent, which matters because Turn 1 is structurally special.

No support found for New World Phase in Turn Structure (searched: "New World Phase", "phase 8", "eighth phase") — absent.

### Finding 5 (MAJOR): Spring Deployment description is incorrect

**Summary claims:**
> "Spring Deployment: Players may redeploy military units within their home territories."

**Source (Section 7 Sequence of Play, lines 891–893):**
> "5 Spring Deployment Phase: Powers move one formation of land units from capital to a controlled space"

The summary's description is wrong in multiple ways: (1) it is not "within their home territories" — the destination is any controlled space (not restricted to home territory); (2) it is limited to "one formation" from the capital, not general redeployment; (3) "may" implies optional, but the source describes it as a specific action definition. This would mislead players about the scope and constraints of Spring Deployment.

### Finding 6 (MAJOR): Political Control action requirements understated / incorrectly described

**Summary claims:**
> "Spend CP to place control markers on spaces adjacent to friendly-controlled spaces."

**Source (Section 12.2, lines 1546–1555):**
> "A power may spend 1 CP on the Control Unfortified Space action to gain political control of a space if the following requirements are all met: The space is independent or controlled by an enemy power. The space is unfortified. The active power has an LOC to the space. Either (a) land units controlled by the active power occupy the space, or (b) land units controlled by the active power are adjacent to the space and enemy land units are not adjacent."

The summary omits: the LOC requirement, that the active power's units must be present or adjacent, that the space must be unfortified, and that the space must be enemy-controlled or independent. The summary implies simply spending CP on any adjacent space is sufficient, which is fundamentally wrong.
