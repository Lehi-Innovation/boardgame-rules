# Audit: age-of-renaissance

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Summary audited: `rules/age-of-renaissance.md`
Source: `extracted/age-of-renaissance-rules.txt` (fan-written rules restatement, updated Jan 2017; complete and well-structured)

The summary largely describes the mechanics of the original *Civilization* game (trade sessions, calamity cards, commodity-set trading), not the rules in the source. Most of the Turn Structure and Actions sections have no basis in the source text.

## Major Findings

### 1. Invented turn structure (UNSUPPORTED)
- Summary Turn Structure / Player Reference: "1. Revenue Collection, 2. Expansion, 3. Trade Card Collection, 4. Trade Session, 5. Calamity Resolution, 6. Purchase Advances, 7. End of Turn."
- Source ch. 5 (Turn sequence) lists the seven phases as: 5.1 Determine Order of Play (secret bid of cash for tokens), 5.2 Draw card phase, 5.3 Buy / discard cards, 5.4 Play cards, 5.5 Purchase, 5.6 Expansion, 5.7 End of turn.
- Only the count (7) matches. "Trade Session", "Calamity Resolution", and "Revenue Collection" (at start of turn) do not exist; income is collected at the *end* of the turn (5.7.3).

### 2. Invented mechanic: player-to-player commodity trading (UNSUPPORTED)
- Summary Actions/Trade: "Commodities are traded between players to build valuable sets... Calamity cards may be hidden among traded cards... Commodity sets increase in value exponentially."
- Source 5.4.1: there is no trading between players. When a commodity *card* is played, "each player holding market(s) providing this commodity are paid according to the value of the commodity and number of markets controlled." There are no calamity cards (the source has Commodity, Event, and Leader cards — 3.13.3) and no hidden-card trading. This is the original Civilization's trading rule, not this game's.

### 3. Turns conflated with epochs (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary: "Each turn (epoch) follows this sequence..."
- Source 3.2: "The game is also divided into 3 EPOCHS, each with a different deck of HISTORY CARDS." Epochs span many turns; an epoch ends when its card deck is exhausted. A turn is not an epoch.

### 4. Victory conditions wrong; misery omitted (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary: "Victory is determined by total advancement score, combining: Advance card point values, Territory control, Economic standing, Position on the progress track."
- Source 6.2: "Each player adds the value of all acquired advances and cash on hand, then subtracts misery to gets the final score."
- Territory control and a "progress track" are not scored; the misery deduction — a core scoring element (and elimination condition: "A player who moves past the '1000' square is immediately out of play", 3.11) — is absent from the entire summary.

### 5. Advance costs wrong (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary: "Cards cost commodity sets and/or treasury."
- Source 5.5.2: advances cost money only ("All advances cost money"), reduced by leader-card discounts and credits from previously bought advances. Commodity sets are not a currency and do not exist.

### 6. Wrong advance counts/categories
- Summary: "24+ advance cards" and Player Reference: "Three categories of advances."
- Source 3.12.2: "all advances have a shortcut letter assigned to them, from A to Z (26 advances in total)"; 3.12.1: "There are advances in six different CATEGORIES: Science, Religion, Commerce, Communication, Exploration and Civics." Also, advances are not cards in this game.

### 7. Invented expansion mechanics (UNSUPPORTED)
- Summary: "Population grows through token placement... Cities built in areas with sufficient population."
- Source 5.6: expansion is placing market-influence tokens to dominate provinces (market number 2–5), with conflicts resolved by a 3-dice roll (5.6.3.9). There are no cities or population in this game.

### 8. Components list largely invented
- Summary lists "Player tokens and cities", "History card deck (commodity and calamity cards)", "Advance cards", "Province markers".
- Source ch. 2: 2-part mapboard; per player 36 square tokens, 25 dominance markers, 12 market indicators, 1 ship, 2 status markers; 3 shortage/surplus markers; 6 player mats; a pad of Advance logs; 64 History cards; 3 dice; play money (1/5/10/20/100). No cities, no calamity cards, no advance cards, no province markers.

### 9. Major omissions (core systems absent)
- Misery index, stabilization costs ($0/1/3/6/10/15/21/28/35 for 0–8 cards, 5.5.3), capital bid ($0–$40, 4.4), secret token bid for turn order (5.1), trade fleets/shipping display (galleys, seaworthy vessels, ocean navigation), leader cards and patronage, market competition resolution, shortage/surplus rolls. None appear in the summary.

## Numbers Spot-Checked (summary has few; all checked)

| # | Claim in summary | Source evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3-6 players | "three to six players each lead a trading nation" | OK |
| 2 | 7 steps per turn | 7 phases per turn (ch. 5) — count only; names all differ | COUNT OK, CONTENT WRONG |
| 3 | "set number of epochs" | 3 epochs; 1- or 2-epoch short game possible (3.2, 4.1) | PARTIAL |

| 7 | Turn order modified by advance effects | 'Renaissance' [Q] allows swapping places in turn order (5.1) | OK |
| 8 | Calamity immunity from advances | Closest: 'Laws of Matter' [C] immune to 'Alchemy' event card — but no "calamities" exist | WEAK |

## Conclusion

The frontmatter and a handful of generic claims are fine, but the Turn Structure, Trade, Expansion, Advances, and Scoring sections describe a different game (Civilization). Player trading, calamity resolution, commodity-set purchasing, and population/cities are invented; the actual scoring (advances + cash − misery), the misery system, secret bids, and trade fleets are missing. MAJOR.

# Audit: agricola

**Verdict: MINOR**

Summary audited: `rules/agricola.md`
Source: `extracted/agricola-rules.txt` (full rulebook + appendix; some OCR column-merging in card art areas, but rules text intact)

## Findings

### Minor 1: Pasture capacity stated per pasture instead of per space (WRONG AS WRITTEN)
- Summary (Actions, Build Fences): "each pasture holds 2 animals, doubled with a stable".
- Source (Action D): "Up to 2 animals may live on each square of the pasture: Pastures that occupy 1 farmyard square can hold 2 animals; 2 squares can hold 4 animals; 3 squares can hold 6 animals etc." Also stables multiply per stable: "Each space within a pasture may have its own stable: the pasture's capacity is then multiplied by 4, 8, etc." (Appendix 1.1).
- A multi-space pasture holds 2 per space; the summary understates capacity. Could mislead at the table, but the doubling-with-stable part is correct.

### Minor 2: Starting player bonus misdescribed
- Summary (Special Rules): "The starting player action also provides a small bonus (typically food or resources)."
- Source (Appendix 1.1): "Starting player and/or Minor Improvement: The player takes or retains the Starting player token. He may play one Minor Improvement from his hand." The bonus is a Minor Improvement, not food/resources.

### Minor 3: Starting food omitted from Setup
- Source: "Players choose a starting player who receives the Starting player marker and 2 Food. The other players each receive 3 Food." The summary's setup never mentions starting Food.

### Minor 4: Small breeding caveat omitted
- Summary: "If you have 2+ of any animal type, gain 1 of that type." Source adds "but only if the lamb, the shoat (piglet) or the calf can be accommodated in the farmyard"; otherwise it runs away and may not be immediately cooked. Edge case omitted.

### Trivial notes
- Setup step 1 "Reveal round cards 1-14 in order" is sloppy: round cards are sorted by stage, stacked face-down, and revealed one per round (source, Cards A).
- Summary's Replenish phase merges source Phase 1 (draw Round card) and Phase 2 (Replenish); acceptable compression.
- Component list omits the 2 Deck cards and 3 Claim markers (both listed in source); summary's "360 Cards" total is still correct.
- Family-member max of 5 (and 15-point cap) not stated; implied by component count.

# Audit: agricola-all-creatures-big-and-small

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Summary audited: `rules/agricola-all-creatures-big-and-small.md`
Source: `extracted/agricola-all-creatures-big-and-small-rules.txt` (complete official rulebook, clean extraction)

## Major Findings

### 1. Breeding Phase entirely missing (MAJOR OMISSION)
- Source Gameplay: "Each round is made up of 4 phases. 1. Refill Phase... 2. Work Phase... 3. Home Phase... 4. Breeding Phase: Animals breed at the end of each round. If you have at least 2 animals of the same type, you receive another one of the same type. Important! You can get at most 1 sheep, 1 pig, 1 cow and 1 horse per breeding phase."
- The summary's Turn Structure lists only 3 steps (Replenish, Place workers, workers return home) and breeding is mentioned nowhere in the entire summary. Breeding every round is the game's central engine; omitting it would fundamentally break play.

### 2. -3 penalty condition wrong (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary (Scoring): "Missing animal types | -3 per missing type".
- Source (Game End and Scoring): "you lose 3 points for each animal type you have 3 or fewer of."
- A player with 1-3 of a type still takes the -3; the summary's version would mis-score most games.

### 3. Half-Timbered House capacity wrong (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary (Special Rules): "The Half-Timbered House replaces the cottage and holds 1 animal of each type."
- Source (Notes On The Special Buildings): "The Half-Timbered House is built over the cottage... You can keep up to 2 animals of the same type in the Half-Timbered House."

### 4. Invented scoring rule (UNSUPPORTED)
- Summary (Scoring): "Empty farmyard spaces | -2 each (in some variants)".
- No such rule or variant exists in the source. Unused spaces only matter in that farm expansions that are not fully used score 0 instead of 4.

### 5. Feeding trough / pasture capacity model wrong (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary: "Pasture (fenced area) | 2 animals | 4 with trough" and "Feeding troughs double capacity of the space they occupy."
- Source: "Each fully enclosed pasture can hold up to 2 animals per space" and "Feeding troughs double the animal number that can be kept both in pastures as well as in buildings" — one trough doubles the *whole pasture* (example: "8 animals in a size two pasture"; two troughs on a 2-space pasture: "4x2=8; 8x2=16").
- Also the summary's table says the cottage gets no trough benefit ("-"), but the source states "The feeding trough also doubles the number of animals that can be kept in the cottage."

## Minor Findings

- Farm expansion scoring value omitted: source gives exactly "Each farm expansion where all 3 farm spaces have been used is worth 4 points"; summary says only "Additional points".
- Animal bonus thresholds vague: summary "Bonus animals (4+ of each type) | Variable bonus"; source: bonus table is on the box sides, plus "After the 13th sheep, the 11th pig, the 10th cow or the 9th horse, you score 1 extra point for each additional animal." Cannot fully verify "4+", but the cap rule is omitted.
- Tiebreaker omitted: "In the case of a tie, the player who was not the start player in round 1 is the winner."
- Building costs omitted throughout (stall 3 stone + 1 reed; stable upgrade 5 wood or 5 stone; special building costs) — the summary gives no costs at all.
- Scoring "Special buildings | Points as printed" — source: "Buildings are worth the points indicated on them. (Buildings are stalls, stables and special buildings.)" — stalls/stables also score, not just special buildings.
