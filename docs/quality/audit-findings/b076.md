## dragon-rage — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (supported)
1. Defender forces 4 cavalry, 8 infantry, 4 archers, 4 militia, 1 hero, 1 wizard — source: "4 cavalry, 8 infantry, 4 archer, 4 militia, 1 hero, and 1 wizard" ✓
2. 2 Dragons for invader ✓
3. 9 turn phases, in the listed order ✓
4. Reinforcement turns 10, 14, 18, 22, 26, 30+ — source: "On turns 10, 14, 18, 22, 26, 30, and so forth" ✓
5. VP hexes marked 1-5 in blue ✓
6. Victory thresholds: <16 "featherless chicken", 16 "dragon whelp", 18 "destroyer of cities", 20+ "the death from above", all VP "the bane of Esirien" ✓
7. City wins if 10 turns pass with no VP destroyed / no invaders within walls ✓
8. Stacking: max one troop counter plus hero or wizard per hex; hero may enter a dragon's hex (ends move), wizard may not ✓
9. Hero wounded once = wounded marker; "killed" again = destroyed ✓
10. Whirlwind destroys all VP in its hex ✓

### Findings

**[MAJOR — contradicted rule] Gate/door smashing mechanic is wrong.**
- Summary: "Gates (large, red): 3 hits to smash; Doors (small, blue): 1 hit to smash."
- Source: doors are smashed automatically with no roll or cost ("A Dragon automatically smashes open a door by simply facing it at any time during movement... No MP cost is required"). Gates are smashed by spending MPs: automatic after 1 MP if unguarded, otherwise roll 1d6 ≤ MPs spent ("If there is no City Defender unit on the other side of the gate, after one MP is spent smashing the gate automatically shatters. Otherwise... If the roll is equal to or less than the MPs spent, the gate is smashed").
- The "3 hits / 1 hit" numbers appear nowhere in the source and would mislead at the table.

**[MAJOR — invented rule / cross-contamination] Courtyard restriction does not exist in this game.**
- Summary (Special Rules): "Only 1 dragon of a given color may be in the courtyard at the same time."
- No "courtyard" rule exists anywhere in the Dragon Rage source. This sentence is verbatim a rule from DragonLance (audited in this same batch: "Only 1 dragon of a given color may be in the courtyard at the same time"), indicating cross-contamination between summaries.

**[MAJOR — omission] Dragon damage / hit-location system entirely missing.**
- Source devotes a full section to the defender's core combat system: head is one group of 8 hit points (hit on 6+), wings are two groups of 6 (hit on 4+), legs four groups of 3 (hit on 5+), belly one group of 6 (hit on 4+, attacked only from underneath; belly destroyed = dragon dies instantly); damage dealt = attacker's combat strength; head destroyed = berserk (dies on 5+ after each invader melee phase); each leg group lost = −1 MP. The summary reduces all of this to "Combat results use die rolls against unit combat values."

**[MINOR — omission] Dragonfire limit omitted.**
- Source: "A Dragon can only breathe fire twice in a game" plus range rules (one of three front hexes, extends one hex further; escape rolls, +2 in towers). Summary: "Flame breath attack resolved during the Dragonfire Phase" with no limit or mechanics.

**[MINOR — omission] Wizard magic under-specified.**
- Source: wizard has exactly 10 spell points for the whole game and four spells (Boost Morale 1-3 SP, Fog 2 SP, Whirlwind 2 SP, Lightning 3 SP). Summary names only Fog and Whirlwind and never states the 10 SP cap.

**[MINOR — omission] Reinforcement composition omitted.**
- Source: each reinforcement group is 4 infantry arriving at sea-front dock hexes and/or the 1413 wooden bridge. Summary says only that "reinforcement units" arrive.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong smashing numbers, an invented (cross-contaminated) courtyard rule, and omission of the core dragon damage system.

## dragonlance — MINOR

### Numbers checked (supported)
1. 24 magic spell cards ✓
2. 5 dragons per player, 6 colors (source: 30 dragons, 5 per horde) ✓
3. Movement roll 1d10; draw a magic card on a roll of 1 or 2 ✓
4. Max end-of-move altitude 10 (may exceed mid-move) ✓
5. Gates passable only at altitude 4 or less ✓
6. Only 1 dragon of a color in the courtyard ✓
7. Tower entry: adjacent courtyard hex at altitude 10, costs 1 MP ✓
8. DragonLance +5 to attack and defense ✓; capture attempt gives Lancer additional +3 defense ✓
9. Spell values: +6 spells (Cloudkill, Cone of Cold, Fireball, Incendiary Cloud, Lightning Bolt, Power Word, each with one immune color), Meteor Swarm +9 (no immunity), Magic Missile +3, Globe of Invulnerability +10, Haste 2d10 ✓
10. Loser drops altitude by the roll difference; crash at 0 or below ✓
11. 2-3 player advanced option: 2 hordes each; 2-player: 3 hordes each ✓
12. 1 card played per dragon controlled per turn; no hand limit; any player's turn ✓

### Findings

**[MINOR — wrong component count] Dice.**
- Summary Components: "1d10 die". Source components list: "2 Ten-sided dice." Trivial at the table but a factual component error.

**[MINOR — omission] DragonLance return on Lancer crash.**
- Source: "If the Lancer is reduced to zero or less altitude then he has crashed and is eliminated — return the DragonLance to the Tower," and likewise a Lancer that descends to zero in its lair from damage crashes instead of winning. Summary never says the Lance returns to the tower; players would not know what to do with the Lance after a Lancer crash.

**[MINOR — omission] Flying over the tower.**
- Source allows flying over the tower at altitude 11+ (ending at ≤10). Not in the summary; small.

Everything else (capture-attack resolution, Fear/Dispel/Passwall/Teleport details, courtyard blocking, single-dragon discarded movement points, advanced game scope) is faithful.

**Verdict: MINOR** — accurate throughout on numbers, but a small component error plus omitted Lance-return/crash endgame rule.

## dragonwood — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (supported)
1. 64 Adventurer cards (5 suits 1-12; 4 Lucky Ladybugs) ✓
2. 42 Dragonwood cards; 2 turn summary cards; 6 custom dice ✓
3. Setup removal: 2p = 12 cards, 3p = 10, 4p = 8 ✓
4. 2 dragons shuffled into the bottom half of the deck ✓
5. Landscape = 5 face-up cards; Events redrawn at setup ✓
6. Deal 5 Adventurer cards per player ✓
7. Hand limit 9 (discard after drawing if over) ✓
8. Lucky Ladybug: discard, draw 2 more ✓
9. 1 die per card played, max 6 dice ✓
10. End-game bonus: most captured creature cards = 3 VP, ties 2 VP each; Orange Dragon tiebreak ✓
11. Dragon Spell variant: 3 same-color consecutive cards → 2 dice, 6+ defeats a dragon, else wound; dragons only ✓
12. Game end: both dragons defeated OR two Adventurer decks played through (one more turn each) ✓

### Findings

**[MAJOR — contradicted rule] Failed creature capture: played cards are NOT discarded.**
- Summary (Actions → Creatures): "If you fail, the Creature stays in the Landscape and you discard 1 card from your hand as a wound. **Either way, all played Adventurer cards go to the discard pile.**"
- Source: "However, if your roll doesn't beat the Creature, it remains in the Landscape. As a penalty, you must discard 1 card from your hand as a 'wound' for not defeating it. **(Take the rest of the Adventurer cards back into your hand.)**"
- On a failed attempt the player keeps the played cards (minus the 1-card wound). The summary's rule makes failed attempts cost the entire played set — a fundamental change to the game's risk economy that would mislead every game.

**[MINOR — omission] "Simple setup" variant** (play with entire Dragonwood deck; game also ends when last Dragonwood card revealed) is omitted. Trivial.

All other mechanics (capture methods Strike/Stomp/Scream, single-card plays counting as any method, Enhancements not capturable by Enhancements, 1 capture attempt per turn, Event handling, Landscape replenishment) match the source.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — one core contradicted rule on failed capture attempts.
