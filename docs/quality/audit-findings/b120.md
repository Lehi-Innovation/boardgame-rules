## in-the-year-of-the-dragon

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Per-round scoring is wrong (Phase 4)

Summary: "Phase 4: Scoring — Each player scores 1 VP per person tile in palaces."

Source (lines 528–533): "each player moves his scoring marker forward on the scoring track, earning 1 point apiece for: — each of his palaces (regardless of whether they are inhabited or how many floors they have), — each dragon on the fans of his court ladies, and — each dragon on his privileges."

The per-round scoring is 1 VP per **palace** (not person tile) + court lady dragons + privilege dragons. The summary's "1 VP per person tile" is the **end-game** bonus (source, line 499: "each person tile in a player's palaces is worth 2 victory points"), not the per-round scoring. This is a complete misattribution of end-game scoring to the per-round scoring phase, and wrong on the number too (2 VP per person at end, not 1).

### Finding 2 — Military action description is wrong

Summary: "Military: Gain 1 warrior power + 1 per sword on your Warriors (for Mongol defense)"

Source (lines 302–308): "Military Parade: When a player chooses this action, he moves his person marker 1 space forward on the person track for the helmet shown on the action card as well as 1 space for each helmet shown on any warriors (red) in his palaces."

The Military action advances the **person marker** (for turn order), not "warrior power." The symbols are **helmets**, not swords. The Military action does not directly protect against Mongols — the Mongol Invasion event scores VPs based on helmets and punishes the player with the fewest; there is no "military strength" concept.

### Finding 3 — Mongol Invasion event description is wrong

Summary: "Mongol Invasion: Each player must have enough military strength or lose 1 person and 1 palace floor"

Source (lines 447–458): "Each player moves his scoring marker forward on the scoring track as many spaces as the total number of helmets on all warriors in his palaces. Additionally, the player or players with the fewest helmets must each release 1 person from any 1 of their palaces."

The event awards **VPs to everyone** proportional to helmets; only the player with the **fewest** helmets releases 1 person. There is no "enough military strength" threshold, no palace floor loss, and it is not a pass/fail test.

### Finding 4 — Research action base value is wrong

Summary: "Research: Score 2 VP + 2 VP per scroll on your Scholars"

Source (lines 309–316): "he moves his scoring marker 1 space forward on the scoring track for the book shown on the action card as well as 1 space for each additional book shown on the scholars (white) in his palaces."

The action card contributes **1 book (1 VP)**, not 2. The example confirms: 1 younger scholar (2 books) + 1 older scholar (3 books) = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 points total. The summary's claim of "2 VP base" is inconsistent with the source.

### Verification checklist

- **Victory/end conditions:** Source lines 514–517 state "the player whose marker is farthest on the scoring track at game's end... tiebreaker: person track." Summary matches these correctly.
- **End-game scoring per person:** Source says 2 VP each. Summary's "End-game scoring" section mentions this. However, the summary misplaces it as per-round scoring.
- **Monks scoring:** Source lines 501–503: "multiply the number of Buddhas on the monk by the number of floors in the palace the monk inhabits." Summary omits monks entirely from end-game scoring — MAJOR omission for scoring.
- **Privilege costs:** Source: 2 yuan (small), 6 yuan (large). Summary matches.
- **Drought:** Source says 1 rice per inhabited palace; summary says "1 rice tile or lose 1 person" — wrong. The source says lose a person from each **unsupplied palace**, not a binary choice of rice or person.
- **Dragon Festival:** Source: most fireworks = 6 VP; second most = 3 VP. Summary matches.
