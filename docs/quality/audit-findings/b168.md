## puzzle-strike-bag-of-chips

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1: Starting deck composition — invented "wound chips", omitted "Crash Gem"

Summary (Setup): "Each player selects a character and takes their 3 character chips plus starting chips (wound chips and 1-gem chips)."

Source (lines 232–236): "Take your 3 Character chips, 1 Crash Gem chip, and 6 1-gem chips and put them in your bag. This is your starting deck."

**The summary invents "wound chips" as part of the starting deck (there are none) and omits the 1 Crash Gem chip.** A player setting up per the summary would start without the foundational offensive chip (the Crash Gem) and with wound chips clogging their bag from turn 1. This would make the game unplayable as designed.

### Finding 2: "1 free buy" framing vs. mandatory buy

Summary (Turn Structure, Buy Phase): "You get 1 free buy."

Source (lines 289–291): "During the buy phase, you buy chips from the bank. You must buy at least one chip per turn."

The buy is **mandatory**, not optional. A player reading the summary may think they can skip buying. (Minor on its own, but combined with Finding 1 this confirms the summary was not written from the source.)

### Finding 3: Summary omits Height Bonus draw mechanic

Summary: Cleanup phase only says "Discard hand and played chips to your discard area. Draw a new hand."

Source (lines 329–338): "Then draw 5 chips. The Height Bonus: 'If your gem pile has 3 / 6 / 9 in it, then draw an extra +1 / +2 / +3 chips.' In other words, if your gem pile totals 3, 4, or 5, draw an extra chip. If it totals 6, 7, or 8, draw two extra chips instead. If it totals 9, draw 3 extra chips instead."

The Height Bonus is a named, central mechanic explicitly referenced in the Quick Start Rules ("draw 5 chips, plus any chips from the 'height bonus' from having a full gem pile"). Omitting it from the summary means players cannot replicate cleanup correctly.

### Finding 4: Winning condition in multiplayer — last player standing vs. lowest pile

Summary: "In a multiplayer game, last player standing wins."

Source (lines 341–346): "In a 3p or 4p game, the player with the lowest gem pile total wins the game. If there is a tie for lowest total, the tied players each take one more turn, check for a lowest, and repeat until there is one winner."

The summary describes a last-player-standing (elimination) format, but the source says the winner is the **player with the lowest gem pile total at the moment someone busts** — not the last survivor of sequential eliminations. These produce different outcomes in 3–4 player games.

### Procedural claims verified (correct)
- Losing condition: "If your gem pile contains 10 or more gems at the end of your turn, you are eliminated." ✓  
  Source (line 340–341): "Whenever a player ends his turn with his gem pile totaling 10 or more, he loses the game." ✓
- Ante: "+1 gem to your pile each turn." ✓  
  Source (lines 270–272): "At the beginning of your turn, take a 1-gem from the bank and put it in your gem pile." ✓
- Turn phases: Ante, Action, Buy, Cleanup — correct order ✓  
  Source (line 267–268): "Ante, Action, Buy, Cleanup. Do each phase in order." ✓
- 1 action per turn: Source (line 277–279): "Each turn you may play one Action chip—any chip with a banner." ✓ (though "1 free action" framing is misleading since it's limited to 1 not free)

### Exhaustion / reshuffle
Summary (Cleanup): "When your bag is empty, shuffle your discard pile into a new bag."
Source (lines 283–287): "If an effect would make you draw a chip when your bag is empty, then put the chips from your discard pile (not including the chips you played to the table this turn) into your bag, shake your bag to shuffle them, then continue drawing." ✓ (mechanic is correct)

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4, SUPPORTED: 3 (starting deck composition wrong — 6 1-gems and 1 Crash Gem, not wounds)

## quarriors

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1: Missing second game-end condition

Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions): "Score Glory when your creatures survive a full round (from your turn to the start of your next turn). First to reach the target wins immediately."

The summary entirely omits the second game-ending condition described in the source.

Source (lines 342–346): "If there are four or more empty Creature Power Cards (cards with no Quarry dice on them) after a player captures a Quarry die the game immediately ends. Only Power Cards that match the Creature Dice are counted: empty Spell Power Cards or Basic Resource Cards are not counted to determine if the game ends. If the game ends because of empty Power Cards, the player with the highest Glory wins the game! If there is a tie, the tied player with the most dice in their Ready Area wins. If they are still tied, they share the victory!"

This is an entire alternate game-end trigger with its own winner determination procedure. A table cannot score or end the game correctly using only the summary.

### Finding 2: Turn structure — 6 phases in summary vs. 5 in source

Summary (Turn Structure):
1. Score Creatures
2. Draw and Roll 6 dice
3. Ready Spells and Summon Creatures
4. Attack
5. Capture
6. Cleanup (Move to Used Pile)

Source (lines 157–165): "Your turn is divided into 5 phases, which you must complete in this order: 1. Score Creatures; 2. Draw, Roll, and Ready Dice; 3. Attack Your Rivals; [4. Capture — optional]; 5. Move Dice to Your Used Pile."

The source explicitly combines Draw/Roll AND Ready Spells/Summon Creatures into a single Phase 2. The summary invents a 6-phase structure by splitting these into separate phases 2 and 3, renaming all subsequent phases. While the underlying steps are not themselves wrong, the phase structure misrepresents the official rulebook and is confirmed by the source's own summary on line 451–455 ("Score Creatures; Draw 6 dice, Roll (active pool), Ready dice; Attack; Buy a die; Move dice").

### Finding 3: Glory targets unverifiable from extracted text

Summary:
| 2 | 20 |
| 3 | 15 |
| 4 | 12 |

Source (lines 337–341): "If any player earns enough Glory to reach the goal shown on the chart to the right, the game immediately ends and that player wins! Glory Goals / # of Players / Required Glory" — the actual numbers in the table were not extracted (the table appears twice at lines 338–341 and 804–807, both times with only the headers present and the values missing from the extracted text).

These numbers cannot be confirmed or denied from the extracted text. Given the rulebook is from 2018 and these appear to be standard values, the numbers are plausible but are not supported by evidence in the source. Flagged as unsupported; not escalating to MAJOR on its own since the numbers are not contradicted.

### Procedural claims verified (correct)
- Summary: "Draw 6 dice from your bag and roll them."
- Source (lines 200–201): "draw out 6 dice and add them to your Active Pool. Now roll all of the dice in your Active Pool." ✓

- Summary: "If your bag has fewer than 6 dice, draw what remains, move your Used Pile into your bag, then draw and roll the rest."
- Source (lines 202–204): "If there are less than 6 dice left in your bag, draw all of the dice that are there and add them to your Active Pool. Then take all of the dice in your Used Pile and return them to your bag, mix them, and continue drawing until you have drawn a total of 6 dice." ✓

- Summary: "Creatures that survive a full round score their Glory value."
- Source (lines 370–372): "If your Creature survives until the first phase of your next turn, you earn Glory, as shown on the matching Power Card." ✓

- Summary: "You may capture at most 1 die per turn."
- Source (lines 317–322): "you may spend any Quiddity you have left to capture one Quarry die from the Wilds. You are never required to capture dice." ✓

- Summary: "When your bag is empty, move your Used Pile into the bag and shuffle."
- Source (lines 117–118): "If you need to draw dice and your bag is empty, put all the dice in your Used Pile into your bag, mix them, and draw the dice you need." ✓

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5, SUPPORTED: 4 (Glory targets not in extracted text)
