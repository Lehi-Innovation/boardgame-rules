## frosthaven

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter Note
`SOURCE_SUSPECT` flag: the word "frosthaven" does not appear in the first 3000 chars of the extracted text. However, the first 60 lines confirm the source is the 2023 Cephalofair Games LLC rulebook with a table of contents matching Frosthaven exactly (Card Selection, Battle Goals, Outpost Phase, etc.). Audit proceeded on the full source.

### Finding 1 — Wrong number of battle goals drawn (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Draw 2 battle goals, keep 1."

**Source (line 476–478):** "Deal three battle goals to each character. Decide which one to keep for each character and discard the others."

The number of battle goals dealt is 3, not 2. This is a concrete wrong number in the setup procedure.

---

### Finding 2 — Impair condition definition is completely wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary (line 104):** "Impair (cannot gain positive conditions)"

**Source (lines 1992–1996):** "Impair: Impair can only be gained by characters. The character cannot use or trigger any items, but bonuses previously gained from items are still active. Impair is removed at the end of the character's next turn."

Impair prevents **item use**, not gaining positive conditions. The summary's description does not resemble the actual rule in any way. A table using the summary would completely misapply Impair.

---

### Finding 3 — Summons act BEFORE the summoner, not at end of summoner's turn (MAJOR)

**Summary (line 114):** "Summons act at the end of the summoner's turn, focusing on the nearest enemy."

**Source (lines 2176–2178):** "A character summon's turn in the initiative order is always directly before their summoner and is separate from the summoner's turn."

**Source (lines 2193–2194):** "Summons never take a turn during the round in which they are summoned."

The summary inverts the timing: summons act *before* the summoner, not after/at the end of the summoner's turn. This meaningfully changes turn order (e.g., a summon could clear a path before the summoner acts). Additionally, the "focusing on the nearest enemy" wording oversimplifies: summons obey automated monster rules (fewest movement points to reach an attack position), and if they cannot find a focus, the summoner may direct them toward themselves.

---

### Finding 4 — Ward described as ongoing damage-halving effect; actually single-use (MAJOR)

**Summary (line 102):** "Ward (halve damage suffered, rounded down)"

**Source (lines 1935–1940):** "Ward: The next time the figure suffers damage from any source, they instead suffer half that amount of damage (rounded down), and ward is then removed."

The summary implies Ward is a persistent passive that halves all damage while active. The source states Ward only applies to the **next** single instance of damage, then is removed. This is the same single-instance pattern as Brittle (which the summary correctly describes as "double next damage source"). Using the summary, a player would treat Ward as ongoing armor rather than a one-shot shield.

---

### Finding 5 — Looting is an explicit ability, not automatic at end of turn (MAJOR)

**Summary (line 66):** "At end of turn, loot any tokens on your hex."

**Source (lines 2242–2249):** "'Loot X' is an ability that allows a figure to loot all loot tokens and treasure tiles within range X, including any in their current hex. … If there are no loot tokens or treasure tiles within the specified range, the ability cannot be performed."

**Source (line 3254):** "Looting can only be performed during [turns, as an ability]."

There is no automatic looting at end of turn. Loot must be explicitly performed via a "Loot X" ability listed on a played ability card. The summary's claim would cause players to pick up tokens they never actually earned via a loot action.

---

### Finding 6 — End of Round step described as removing conditions; it does not (MINOR/borderline MAJOR)

**Summary (line 74):** "End of Round — Remove end-of-round conditions and effects."

**Source (lines 3157–3172):** The End of Round step does: (1) trigger end-of-round effects from scenario/items/cards, (2) shuffle decks with shuffle icons, (3) move round-bonus cards to discard/lost, (4) allow short rests, (5) advance elements toward inert.

Conditions (Immobilize, Disarm, Stun, Muddle, Strengthen, Ward, Invisible) are each removed "at the end of the figure's next turn," not at end of round. The summary conflates "round bonus cards discarded" with "conditions removed at end of round." A table following the summary would remove all conditions at end of round, significantly weakening negative and positive conditions alike.

Verdict for this finding: MAJOR — misapplying condition timing removes conditions far too early for multi-turn conditions.

---

### Finding 7 — Exhaustion from card starvation condition is ambiguous/misleading

**Summary (line 126):** "Also exhausted if they have fewer than 2 cards available at the start of a round (hand + discard)."

**Source (lines 2685–2688):** "If the character does not have at least two cards in their hand (to play) **or** at least two cards in their discard pile (to rest) at the start of a round."

The source uses OR: you must have ≥2 in hand OR ≥2 in discard to avoid exhaustion. Having 1 in hand and 1 in discard (total = 2) still triggers exhaustion because neither condition alone is met. The summary's phrasing "hand + discard" could be read as "2 total across both piles" which would incorrectly let a character with 1+1 = 2 total avoid exhaustion. This is a MINOR vs MAJOR borderline; it would mislead edge-case situations.
