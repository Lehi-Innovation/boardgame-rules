# Audit b065: d-day-at-omaha-beach

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Compared `rules/d-day-at-omaha-beach.md` against `extracted/d-day-at-omaha-beach-rules.txt` (full 3,313-line read). Component counts and time scale are accurate, but the Scoring section contains a wrong victory-check turn and invented VP sources, the Actions section attributes barrage to the wrong units, the turn structure omits/misorders phases, and several Special Rules claims contradict the rulebook.

## Major findings

### 1. First Victory Check at wrong turn (Scoring)
- Summary: "**First Victory Check (Turn 24):** Check control of key terrain objectives."
- Source (sec. 20 / 20.2): "...until either of two Victory check points, one at the end of Turn 16 and one at the end of Turn 32." / "20.2 First Victory Check: At the end of Turn 16, total up your Victory Points as described in 13.2."
- Turn 24 appears nowhere as a victory check. Also, the check is a full VP count against thresholds (25+ = decisive victory and game ends; 10 or fewer = decisive defeat; 11-24 = continue play), not a "control of key terrain objectives" check.

### 2. Invented VP sources, real ones omitted (Scoring)
- Summary (First Waves): "count VP for controlled terrain, German positions eliminated, and units landed safely."
- Source (13.2): VP only for control: "1 VP for each WN position you control... 1 VP for each German reinforcement position you control... 5 VPs for each draw under your control. You win the game if you have 19 or more Victory Points."
- There are no VPs for German positions *eliminated* or for *units landed safely* — both are invented. The 19-VP win threshold is omitted.
- Summary (Second Victory Check): "Final scoring for controlled hexes, exits secured, and German units destroyed."
- Source (20.3): 1 VP per controlled VP-marked reinforcement position (42 hexes), 4 VP per draw open to traffic, 1 VP per non-infantry/non-HQ unit on high ground, 1 VP per armor/infantry step exited with communication, 1 VP per ranger step exited via F/G, **minus** 1 VP per uncontrolled WN hex and per unit left in the KG Meyer box; win at 40+ VP. "German units destroyed" is again not a VP source; the penalty VPs and the 40-VP threshold are omitted.

### 3. Barrage attributed to wrong units (Actions)
- Summary: "**Barrage:** HQs and units with heavy weapons can conduct barrage attacks at range."
- Source (sec. 7 action list): "Barrage (tank units only)"; 8.4: "A tank unit may conduct an action to barrage..."; 8.14: "HQ units and Generals do not directly participate in an attack." Artillery barrage only becomes available in the extended game (17.3). HQ barrage is an invented mechanic.

### 4. Disruption rule contradicted (Actions / German Fire)
- Summary: "Disrupted units that take additional hits are eliminated."
- Source (6.33): "An already disrupted unit that incurs another disruption result is not further affected." Elimination happens only via step loss (6.32: a unit with one step that loses a step is removed). As written, the summary would cause players to wrongly eliminate disrupted units.

### 5. Core action economy omitted/misstated (Actions)
- Summary: "**Free Actions:** Units can perform one free action per turn (reform from disruption, for example)."
- Source (sec. 7 / 7.1): "Each turn, you may select two US units or stacks in each division, each of which may conduct one action." Free actions are limited to specific cases (ranger infantry, hero/inspired, climb/disrupted markers, HQs/Generals, units in command of an HQ/General, infantry self-preservation moves). The defining constraint of the game — 2 actions per division per turn (3 in the extended game, per 14/17) — is never stated.

### 6. Turn structure: phase omitted and phase misordered (Turn Structure)
- Summary sequence: 1. US Amphibious Operations, 2. German Fire, 3. US Actions, 4. US Combat Actions, 5. Engineer Beach Obstacle Demolition.
- Source (sec. 4): I. US Amphibious Operations; II. **Event Phase** (skip on Turn 1); III. German Fire; IV. **US Engineer Phase** (before actions); V. US Action Phase (movement, attack and barrage all in this phase); VI. End of Turn.
- The Event Phase — which drives German reinforcements, depth markers, heroes, and naval fire markers — is missing entirely from the sequence; the Engineer phase is placed last instead of before US actions; "US Combat Actions" is presented as a separate phase when attacks/barrages occur within the single US Action Phase. The extended game also adds a *second* Event Phase and a US Engineer & HQ Phase (sec. 15), not mentioned.

### 7. Communication rules misstated (Special Rules)
- Summary: "**US Communication:** Units must trace communication back to the beach to receive benefits."
- Source (12.3): "US units are not affected by being in or out of communication during the game. At the end of a scenario, US communication is required in order to control hexes for victory purposes."
- Summary: "**German Communication:** ...cutting communication degrades their fire."
- Source (12.2): communication is required to receive reinforcements/depth markers, withdraw, and perform certain extended-game actions. Fire is unaffected by communication status. Both claims would mislead play.

## Minor findings

- **Stacking:** summary says "Maximum units per hex vary by terrain type"; source 7.6 is a flat limit of one or two US units per hex at the end of the US Action Phase (leaders exempt), with no terrain variation.
- **Attack resolution:** summary says "Cross-reference attack weapons and the attack table on the event card." The US Attack Table is on a player aid (2.4), and attacks are resolved by strength comparison + weapon requirements with no card draw (8.3); cards are drawn only for barrages and infiltration.
- **Catastrophic loss specifics omitted:** source defines it precisely — eight regular infantry units of one division reduced to one step or eliminated (13.1); fourteen during turns 17-32 (20.1). Summary gives only a vague "too many US units eliminated."
- **Scenario inventory:** Overview claims "two scenarios"; source sec. 3 lists four (Easy Fox introductory, The First Waves, full D-Day at Omaha Beach, Beyond the Beach). Easy Fox is never mentioned.
- **Victory levels:** "Decisive Defeat to Decisive Victory" is accurate only for the Turn-32 check; the First Waves scenario is a simple win at 19+ VP.
- **Play time frontmatter:** "240-480 min" vs rulebook's stated six to eight hours for the full scenario (First Waves ~3 hours; Beyond the Beach 4-5). Likely BGG metadata; not a table-misleading issue.

## Verified accurate (sample)

- Solitaire / 2-player cooperative split: 1st Division east, 29th Division west (Intro).
- No dice; card-driven German fire/events (sec. 2, designer's notes).
- Climb Bluff limited to infantry/HQs/Generals; Climb Cliff infantry only (7.4, 7.5).
- Optional variants list matches 22.1-22.4 and 23 (German Armor).
- Extended-game additions (two-hex movement, tank road movement, artillery barrage, command posts, engineer bases) match secs. 17-19.
- Bocage as strong German defensive terrain (2.1, Terrain Effects Chart).
