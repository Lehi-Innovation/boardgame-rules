## city-of-heroes-collectible-card-game — VERDICT: MAJOR

Source: `extracted/city-of-heroes-collectible-card-game-rules.txt` (CoH CCG Technical/Comprehensive Rules v0.545)

The summary appears to be written largely from general knowledge of the City of
Heroes franchise rather than from the extracted rules document. It invents
components and a turn-cycle structure, contradicts the source on archetypes,
gets the starting hand wrong, and omits nearly all of the game's actual core
systems (clicks/charging, tactics modes, reacts, missions, the nine-action menu).

### Numbers checked

The summary contains very few numbers; all were checked, plus key source numbers
the summary should reflect:

1. "5 archetypes" (Components/Overview) — SUPPORTED. Source 203.5a: "The
   archetypes in City of Heroes are: Blaster, Scrapper, Defender, Controller,
   and Tanker."
2. Starting hand "5 cards for the first active player, 7 for the other"
   (Setup step 3) — WRONG. Source 103.1: "each player draws a starting hand of
   5 cards." Both players draw 5; the 7-card hand for the second player is
   invented.
3. "Reduce your opponent's hero to 0 health to win" — SUPPORTED. Source 101.1:
   hero "health total zero or less when priority is passed" = that player loses;
   105.1 last hero standing wins.
4. player_count "2" (frontmatter) — PARTIAL. Source 104.1 explicitly supports
   multiplayer games of 3+ players ("A game with three or more players is
   called a multiplayer game"). Minor metadata issue.
5. Source: minimum deck size 40 cards (constructed, 506.2) — OMITTED from summary.
6. Source: minimum deck size 25 cards (limited, 506.3) — OMITTED.
7. Source: max 3 copies of an individual card (506.2/506.3) — OMITTED.
8. Source: 10-card maximum hand size (505.1e) — OMITTED.
9. Source: click powers charge 1, 2, or 3 clicks; max 3 clicks (203.12a, 204.3a);
   max 2 toggle powers turned on (304.3b) — OMITTED (the whole click system is
   missing).
10. Source: Rest action draws 2 cards (302.1g); summary's "Draw Cards" action
    gives no number — OMITTED detail.
11. Source: inspiration values (+2 Accuracy, +2 Defense, heal 2, draw 3,
    +1 damage; 403.2a–f) — OMITTED (inspirations missing entirely).

Numbers verdict: of the 3 substantive numbers in the summary body, 1 is wrong
(starting hand 5/7), and the summary omits essentially every load-bearing number
in the source.

### Invented components

Summary Components list: "Villain cards", "Location cards", "Equipment cards",
"Clock track", "Damage tokens" — NONE of these exist in the source. Source card
types (glossary, "card type"): "Hero, Sidekick, Power, Enhancement, Edge, and
Mission." Missions "represent the forces and influence of villains" (202.8) but
there is no Villain card type; there are no location or equipment cards; "clock"
in the source is an abstract effect-duration counter (407.3a), not a physical
track; health is a tracked value, not damage tokens. The summary also omits the
real card types: Sidekick, Enhancement, Edge, Mission.

### Invented mechanics (Turn Structure / Actions)

- Summary: "Repeat until both players pass consecutively. Advance the clock and
  reset for the next cycle" and a "Pass" action ("If both players pass
  consecutively, the current engagement ends"). UNSUPPORTED. The source's action
  list (302.1a–i, glossary) is: Power Up, Act, Move, Assemble, Recharge,
  Mission, Rest, Defeat, Power Down — there is no Pass action, no "engagement",
  and no clock-advance cycle. Source 301.1: "There are no phases or other turn
  structure in the game."
- Summary: "The clock track creates time pressure and can trigger special
  events" (Special Rules). INVENTED — no such component or mechanic.
- Summary: "Villain cards may appear as third-party threats both players must
  deal with." INVENTED — missions are played voluntarily by a player (302.1f)
  and completed for rewards (504.1b); they are not third-party threats.
- Summary setup: "Set up the clock track." INVENTED.

### Contradicted rules

- Summary: "Activate an Ability: Use your hero's innate archetype ability" and
  "Archetype abilities are unique and define the core strategy." CONTRADICTED.
  Source 203.5b: "These archetypes have no inherent abilities in City of
  Heroes." (Innate abilities exist per individual super card, 401.4 — not per
  archetype.)
- Summary Scoring + Player Reference table: "Tankers have the highest health,
  Blasters have the lowest but deal more damage, Defenders can heal..."
  UNSUPPORTED by the rules document and contradicted in spirit by 203.5b;
  nothing in the source assigns stats or specialties to archetypes.
- Summary setup: "Each player selects a hero card." Source 103.1: players search
  their deck for the hero AND its starting powers, which begin in play attached,
  in Toe-to-Toe mode — materially different.

### Major omissions

- The click/charging system (204.x, 203.12) — the game's core action-economy
  mechanic — is entirely absent.
- Tactics modes (Toe-to-Toe / Run-'n'-Gun) and chasing costs (303.x, 306.2c).
- React abilities and react triggers (305.x) — the actual interaction system
  (the summary's "react immediately" claim loosely gestures at this but the
  system is never described).
- Attack resolution: accuracy vs. defense comparison (306.2e).
- Missions, sidekicks, enhancements, edges, inspirations (202.x, 403.x).
- Deck construction rules (506.x).

## city-of-the-great-machine — VERDICT: MAJOR

Source: `extracted/city-of-the-great-machine-rules.txt` (CrowD Games rulebook, 2021)

The summary describes a plausible-sounding but largely fictional version of the
game built around an invented "awaken/oppress citizens" mechanic. It misstates
the Heroes' win condition, inverts the hidden-information system, invents Great
Machine phases and actions, and omits the actual core systems (Riots, Guards,
Access cards, Trust/Bond economy, the five-phase round, Directives, City
Events).

### Numbers checked

The summary body contains almost no numbers; all were checked, plus key source
numbers the summary should reflect:

1. player_count "1-4" (frontmatter) — UNSUPPORTED. Source setup: 1 Great
   Machine player + 1–3 Revolutionaries = 2–4 players. No solo mode appears in
   the source (see invented solo rules below).
2. "1 modular city game board (districts)" — PARTIAL. The city is built from
   9 separate District tiles (source components: "5 Minor Districts, 4 Major
   Districts"), not one board; "modular" is correct.
3. Source: Heroes win after 3 successful Riots ("they must lead three
   successful Riots"; "The Revolutionaries immediately win on the 3rd
   successful Riot") — OMITTED/REPLACED by a wrong condition (see below).
4. Source: 3 Servants, 3 Heroes in play ("the conflict of 3 Servants ... and
   3 Heroes") — OMITTED.
5. Source: 18 wooden Guard figures; 2 Guards placed per District at setup —
   OMITTED (Guards never mentioned in the summary at all).
6. Source: 9 Access cards per Hero (54 total), 1 randomly forfeited at setup —
   OMITTED (Access cards never mentioned).
7. Source: Master Plan track marked I–XII; starts at III (or II/I as handicap);
   GM wins at XII — summary says only "track reaches the end" (compatible but
   numberless).
8. Source: Discontent track = 18 slots (6 stages x 3) + red-circle 19th slot —
   OMITTED.
9. Source: GM income 6 Bonds at Zero Stage up to max 12 Bonds at Stage 5/Riot
   Stage; Guard movement 1 Bond per District exited, Servant 2 Bonds; Servant
   actions cost 2 Bonds — OMITTED (the entire Bond economy is missing).
10. Source: City Event deck of 13 cards (6 + 6 + 'The Countdown'); Countdown
    revealed in the 13th round — OMITTED.
11. Source: 30 Famous Citizen tokens (5 each of 6 types), 27 placed, 3 arrested
    at setup; max 3 published Directives — OMITTED.

Numbers verdict: the summary contains no correct substantive numbers and omits
every load-bearing number in the source.

### Wrong win condition (Heroes)

Summary: "Heroes win: Raise the Discontent track to the revolution threshold"
(and Overview: "win by raising the city's discontent to trigger a revolution").
WRONG. Source: "The objective of the Revolutionaries is to bring about a
Revolution. To do this, they must lead three successful Riots." Reaching the
end of the Discontent track (Riot Stage) "counts as 1 successful Riot" — it is
not itself a win. The two actual paths: 3 Riots in 3 districts, or 2 Riots +
Riot Stage. The summary's Player Reference table repeats the error
("Discontent reaches revolution | Heroes"). The Riot action — the Heroes' core
win mechanism — is never mentioned. The Great Machine win condition (Master
Plan reaches the end) is correct.

### Invented mechanics

- "Awaken Citizens: Convert oppressed citizens to awakened" / "Oppress
  Citizens: Convert awakened citizens back to oppressed state" / "Citizen
  tokens (with awakened/oppressed states)" / "Citizens can fluctuate between
  awakened and oppressed states." INVENTED. The word "awaken" never appears in
  the source; Citizens have face-down / identified / revealed / arrested states
  and an active/inactive status driven by the Discontent stage vs. the number
  on their token. The closest real actions are Incitement (raise Discontent
  by 1) and Arrest (remove a revealed active Citizen).
- Great Machine turn step 1 "Advance Master Plan: Move the Master Plan marker
  forward." WRONG as a routine phase step. The Master Plan advances only via
  City Event conditions in the Closing Phase, detainment Option A, failed
  Riots, or 'The Countdown' card.
- Great Machine turn step 2 "Deploy Servants: Place servant units on the
  board." INVENTED. All 3 Servants are placed in the Grand Citadel at setup
  and are never deployed/added during play.
- "Hidden Movement: The Great Machine's master servant moves secretly" and
  Special Rules "The Great Machine uses hidden movement for its master
  servant." INVENTED and INVERTED. There is no "master servant" and Servants
  move openly (paying Bonds). The hidden information belongs to the HEROES:
  they secretly choose Access cards each round, and Citizen identities are
  hidden. The Raid action is the GM guessing the Heroes' destinations.
- "Build Machines: Construct devices that enhance control" / Hero "Sabotage:
  Destroy Great Machine installations." INVENTED. No machines or installations
  are built; the nearest real mechanics are publishing Directives (Tower of
  L.A.W.) and Heroes breaking Guards via the Attack a Guard action (Assault
  die roll vs. Trust paid).
- Hero actions "Recruit: Gain citizen allies for special actions" and "Rally:
  Organize awakened citizens for greater effect." INVENTED. Actual Hero actions:
  Incitement, Attack a Guard, Identify Famous Citizens, Move Famous Citizens,
  Start a Riot, plus Major District special actions.
- "Solo rules allow one player to control all Heroes against an automated
  Great Machine." UNSUPPORTED. Source: with 1 Revolutionary, that player
  controls all 3 Heroes, but the Great Machine is always a human player; no
  automated GM appears in the source.
- Setup step 6 "Distribute starting cards and tokens per scenario." There are
  no scenarios in the source.

### Major omissions

- The five-phase round structure (Opening Phase: reveal City Event + receive
  Trust/Bonds; Access Phase: Heroes secretly pick Access cards; Great Machine's
  Phase; Heroes' Phase in Initiative order; Closing Phase) — entirely absent.
- Guards (18 figures, 2 per District, movement cost of 1 Trust per Guard when
  Heroes exit a District, breaking/repairing, Escape die) — never mentioned.
- The Trust/Bond economy (income, payment for movement and actions) — absent.
- Access cards and forfeiting; Detainment with Option A/B and Punitive
  Measures ('No Trust!' / 'No Access!'); Raids.
- Riots and Riot resolution (active Citizens vs. Guards + Traitors); Traitors
  and the 5 social classes (1 Artists, 2 Scientists, 3 Merchants,
  4 Industrialists, 5 Nobles).
- Directives (draw/publish/cancel, max 3 published) and City Event cards /
  'The Countdown'.
