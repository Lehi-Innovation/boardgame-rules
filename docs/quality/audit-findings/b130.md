## lego-heroica

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Die face "Skull" damage amount is wrong

Summary: "Skull: A monster attacks you (lose 1 health or use a potion to block)"
Source: "Skull: Lose Health equal to the monster's strength AND move back 1 space away from the monster."

The summary says Skull causes "1 health" loss; the source says you lose Health EQUAL TO THE MONSTER'S STRENGTH. Monsters have different strengths, so this can be more than 1. Players using the summary would systematically understate damage taken.

### Finding 2 — Two Shield faces conflated into one

Summary (Player Reference): "Shield: Defeat an adjacent monster" (single entry)
Source has TWO shield faces:
- "Heroica Shield [melee]: Defeat the monster OR use your Hero's Melee skill."
- "Heroica™ Shield [ranged]: Move up to 4 spaces OR use your Hero's Ranged skill."

The summary completely omits that one Shield face is a ranged movement option (up to 4 spaces). Players would not know that rolling this Shield result can mean moving instead of fighting.

### Finding 3 — Sword and Sword & Skull die faces omitted entirely

Source lists: "Sword: Defeat the monster." and "Sword & Skull: Defeat the monster AND lose Health equal to the monster's strength AND move back 1 space away from the monster."

Summary's die table has only "1-4 spaces / Shield / Skull" — the Sword and Sword & Skull faces are not mentioned anywhere, including the Actions section. Players cannot play the game without knowing these results.

### Finding 4 — Combat roll description incorrect

Summary: "Battle monsters: When you land on or move through a space with a monster, battle occurs. Roll the die; Shield = defeat monster, Skull = take damage, Number = no battle effect (must stop)."

Source: Movement numbers (1, 2, 3) during combat mean "Move that many spaces" — these are movement results, not "no battle effect (must stop)." Actually the source indicates that if you end adjacent to a monster you MUST fight, then roll the die. A number result on the combat die still means movement (move forward to escape), not simply stopping.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (starting health=4 ✓, weapons buy=3 gold ✓, sell=2 gold ✓, 4 health restored on Shield heal ✓), SUPPORTED: 4
Finding-supported pairs: 4

## letters-from-whitechapel

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Detective action structure misrepresented (phase conflation)

Summary: "Each detective may perform one of two actions: 1. Move: Move their policeman up to 2 spaces along connections. 2. Search for Clues: ... 3. Arrest Attempt: ..."

Source (sections): There are TWO SEPARATE phases per iteration of the chase:
- Phase 2 "Police: Hunting the Monster" — ALL detectives move their pawns (up to 2 Crossings). This is mandatory movement.
- Phase 3 "Police: Clues and Suspicion" — THEN each detective's pawn EITHER searches for clues OR executes an arrest.

Source: "After consulting together, the detectives move their Policeman pawns." (phase 2, movement happens regardless)
"...each detective states, for each Policeman pawn he controls, that the Policeman is either looking for clues or executing an arrest." (phase 3)

The summary implies detectives choose EITHER Move OR Search/Arrest. In reality they move ALL pawns in phase 2 AND choose clues/arrest in phase 3. These are separate phases, not alternatives.

### Finding 2 — Special Movement uses per night table is wrong

Summary (Player Reference): "Coach: Uses: 3 / Alley: Uses: 2"
Source: Coach and Alley tokens vary per night:
- Night 1 (Aug 31): 3 Coach, 2 Alley
- Night 2 (Sep 8): 2 Coach, 2 Alley
- Night 3 (Sep 30): 2 Coach, 1 Alley
- Night 4 (Nov 9): 1 Coach, 1 Alley

The summary's "3" and "2" are only correct for Night 1. On Nights 2–4 Jack has fewer tokens. Players using the summary would give Jack too many tokens on later nights.

### Finding 3 — Jack's victory condition incomplete (Double Event not mentioned)

Summary: "Jack wins by successfully returning to his hideout on all 4 nights after committing each murder."
Source: "Jack the Ripper wins the game if he kills five victims without being caught."
Also: "On the third Night, September 30th, there are two murders."

The summary never mentions the Double Event — that Night 3 requires TWO murders. Jack must kill 5 victims total across 4 nights (1+1+2+1), not just one per night. A player reading the summary would not know Night 3 requires two murders.

### Finding 4 — False Clue rule presented as core rule, is optional

Summary: "False Clue markers: Jack has 3 per game."
Source: "Optional Rule: If the False Clue optional rule is being used, Jack can collect and use False Clue markers." Also: "Jack receives a False Clue marker every time he reveals five Clue markers in the same Night."

The summary says Jack "has 3 per game" as a given. Source says it is an optional rule, and Jack earns markers during play (every 5 clues revealed), not as a fixed starting allocation.

### Verification of core claims

- Four nights: Source confirmed ✓
- Jack chooses hideout at start, keeps it all game: confirmed ✓
- Jack cannot move through a Crossing occupied by Policeman: confirmed ✓
- Arrest: detective announces one adjacent circle, if Jack is there Jack is arrested: confirmed ✓
- Jack loses if no legal moves: confirmed ✓
- Looking for clues: detective announces circles one at a time, Jack replies; Clue placed if match: confirmed ✓

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (4 nights ✓, 5 victims total ✗ omitted from summary, coach Night1=3 ✓ but Night2-4 missing, alley Night1=2 ✓, 15-move limit ✓, 3 false clue markers ✓ as count but rule is optional, move up to 2 crossings ✓, arrest = 1 circle ✓), SUPPORTED: 5

## lewis-clark-the-expedition

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Village Actions table is largely wrong

Summary Village Actions table:
| Character | Action |
| Lumberjack | Collect Wood — count Wood badges... |
| Hunter | Collect Food — same badge-counting mechanism |
| Blacksmith | Collect Equipment — same badge-counting mechanism |
| Fur Trader | Collect Fur — same badge-counting mechanism |
| Interpreter | Gather all Indians from Village to Powwow area... |
| Commander | Move your Scout forward on the Route |

These are the STARTING CHARACTER ACTIONS, not Village Actions. The actual Village Actions table in the summary is completely wrong:

Summary (Village Actions):
- Logging: Take 1 Wood per Indian
- Hunting: Take 1 Food per Indian
- Fur Trading: Take 1 Fur per Indian
- Smithing: Take 1 Equipment per Indian
- Canoe Manufacturing: Pay 1 Wood to get 1 Dugout Canoe per Indian
- Horse Trading: Pay 1 Fur and 1 Food to get 1 Horse per Indian

Source Village Actions:
- Hunting: "Take 1 Food AND 1 Fur from the Stock" (not just Food)
- Craftsmanship: "Take 1 Equipment AND 1 Wood from the Stock" (not just Equipment)
- Gift: "Take 2 Fur OR 2 Wood" (entire action missing from summary)
- Farewell: "Discard 0 to 3 Character cards from your hand... Discard all Journal cards, Refill Journal with 5 new cards" (entire action missing)
- Horse Trade: "Pay 3 different resources to take a Horse from the Stock. Each of the three resources must be different from the other two!" (summary says "Pay 1 Fur and 1 Food")
- Canoe Manufacturing: "Pay 2 Wood to take a Canoe from the Stock" (summary says "Pay 1 Wood per Indian")
- Shamanism: "Pay one Food to activate a face-up Character Action" (entire action missing)
- Expedition Upgrade: "Pay 3 Wood... take either one Boat tile + 2 primary resources, or one Boat tile + 1 Indian" (entire action missing)

The Village Actions table has 4 of 6 listed actions with wrong costs/effects, and 4 actions entirely omitted. This would completely mislead players about how the Village works.

### Finding 2 — Scout movement cost table is wrong

Summary movement table:
| Terrain | With Transport | Without Transport |
| River | 1 Canoe/space | 2 Indians/space |
| Mountain | 1 Horse/space | 2 Indians/space |

Source (Commander action description): 
- "Pay one Food from your Boats and move your Scout forward 2 River spaces."

The summary says 1 Canoe = 1 space (River), but source says 1 Canoe = 4 River spaces.
The summary says 1 Horse = 1 space (Mountain), but source says 1 Horse = 2 Mountain spaces.
The Food movement option (1 Food = 2 River spaces) is entirely missing from the summary.
No source passage found for "2 Indians per space" alternative movement (searched: "indian.*space", "without.*canoe", "without.*horse").

## lifeboats

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Source mismatch — summary describes a completely different game

The extracted source (`lifeboats-rules.txt`) is the 2001 Jeff Siadek game "Lifeboats": a game for 6 players controlling one of 6 named characters (Lady Lauren, Sir Stephen, Captain, Mate, Frenchy, Kid) in a single Edwardian lifeboat. Actions each turn are: Row, Change Seats, Mug Somebody, Special Action, or Do Nothing. The game ends when 4 birds are sighted.

Scoring from source: "Character is alive: Survival value / Character is in boat: total VP value of cash, jewels and paintings in hand / Loved one survives: their Survival value / Hated one dies: their Size value."

The summary describes an entirely different "Lifeboats" game: multiple lifeboats, officers and sailors, 3 islands with a scoring table, colour-card voting, Captain's Hat override cards, 3-6 players. The summary's overview: "Players must rescue their officers and sailors from a sinking ship by transporting them via leaking lifeboats to nearby islands. Players vote on which boats spring leaks."

None of the summary's mechanics (colour-card voting, sailors/officers, 3-island scoring table, Captain's Hat cards, Leak tokens) appear anywhere in the source text. The source's mechanics (Row action, Mug Somebody, Navigation deck, love/hate scoring, character Size, wounds) do not appear in the summary.

The summary was written about a different edition/version of a game named "Lifeboats" (likely the 2009 Days of Wonder edition) while the extracted PDF is the 2001 Jeff Siadek game.
