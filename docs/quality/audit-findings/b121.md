## isla-dorada

**Verdict: PASS**

### Source

Extracted file: `extracted/isla-dorada-rules.txt` — a third-party rules summary from Esoteric Order of Gamers (Universal Head), not the original FFG rulebook. Still covers full rules.

### Victory / Game-End

- Summary: "Game ends when the last exploration marker is placed (after round 16 minimum). Complete the round, then score."
- Source (p.1): "After the final exploration marker is placed on the board, the game ends when the round is completed." ✓
- Summary: "1 point per remaining gold / Points from played treasure cards / Points from Bonus cards (+3 each) / Points from destiny card / Negative points from played curse cards."
- Source: "1 point for each remaining gold; Points for treasure they have found (played faceup treasure cards and Bonus cards); Points for their destiny card; and Negative points for any curse cards they played. The player with the highest total wins." ✓

### Procedural Claims Verified

1. "Bidding continues until all pass after a bid. Highest bidder becomes leader, discards cards equal to bid." — Source: "Bidding continues until all other players have passed after a bid. The highest bidder becomes the new expedition leader, takes the leader token and must discard a number of adventure cards equal to their bid." ✓
2. "Ovetos: Playable when bidding 4+; ends bidding immediately." — Source: "A player who bids 4 or more can play an Ovetos to end the bidding round immediately." ✓
3. Cable News round 4: "each player draws 2 treasure cards, keeps 1" — Source: "During round 4, each player receives 2 cards from the treasure deck, keeping one in hand and discarding the other." ✓
4. Cable News round 9: "each player draws 3 treasure cards, keeps 2" — Source: "During round 9, each player receives 3 cards from the treasure deck, keeping 2 in hand and discarding the other." ✓
5. "Maximum hand size: 10 adventure cards" — Source: "any player with more than 10 adventure cards in hand must discard cards of their choice to reduce their hand size to 10." ✓

### Numbers Verified

- 16 exploration markers ✓, 10 starting gold ✓, Kayak value 2 ✓, Drako value 2 / cost 1 gold ✓, Zeppelin value 5 / cost 3 gold ✓, Bonus card +3 ✓, 2 destiny cards deal/keep 1 ✓, 1 curse card deal/keep 1 ✓, 2 starting treasures kept from 3 dealt ✓

### Bolt-Symbol / Bigfoot / Leviathan

- Summary: "Bigfoot blocks land paths; Leviathan blocks water paths." Source: Bigfoot placed on "any jungle, mountain, or sand path" and "can be moved to block any land path." Leviathan "on any water path...can be moved to block any water path." Summary's shorthand is accurate. ✓

### Minor Note

Summary states "Cards in hand and gold amount are public knowledge; card contents are secret." Source says "The number of cards in a player's hand and their amount of gold…are public knowledge, but goal and adventure cards must be kept hidden." The summary collapses "number of cards" into "Cards in hand," which is slightly loose but not misleading at the table given the following clarification clause.

**Verdict: PASS**

### Source

### Victory / Game-End

- Summary: "Standard Victory: Control three of the four planets."

- Summary: "Final Conflict: If neither player controls all three planets after three planets are resolved, a Final Conflict occurs on the fourth planet."

- Summary: "repeating cycles of Character Battle Phase and Draw Phase until only one player has characters at the site. That player wins."

### Procedural Claims Verified

1. "Force Phase — Both players secretly select a Force card from their Force deck and reveal simultaneously. 2. "Support Step (defending player acts first)" — Source: "this time you do not begin with Player 1; instead, the defending player takes the first game action." ✓
3. "Weapons Step (attacking player acts first)" — Source: "Players alternate taking game actions, but during this step the attacking player goes first." ✓
4. "Power Step: Compare power of attacker vs. defender (not supporters). Ties go to the attacker." — Source: "Compare the power of the attacker and the defender. (Do not add the power of supporting starships...) The one that has the most power wins the battle. (If they tie, the attacker wins.)" ✓
5. "Control Check on the second turn at a planet. Total power of your characters at the site. Double if your Hero is there." — Source: "If this is the second turn at the same planet, then it's time to determine who controls that planet. Total the power of all your characters at the site. (Do not count cards at the system.) Double your total if your Hero is there." ✓

### Victory / Game-End

- Summary: "When the last Perp in the Perp Pack is used, the game continues for one complete round of Game Turns (ending when the Turn Start Card returns to the player who had it when the last Perp was used). - Source: "When the last Perp in the Perp Pack is used, the game ends after one complete round of Game Turns—as soon as the Turn Start Card would be passed to the player who had it when the last Perp card was used.
