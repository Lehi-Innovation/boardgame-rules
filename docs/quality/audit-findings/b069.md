## descent-journeys-in-the-dark — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component counts and core turn/combat numbers are excellent (22/22 checked numbers supported), but the Hero Orders section invents two entirely different mechanics (Aim and Dodge are re-roll effects in the source, not stat bonuses or wound cancellation), Guard gets an invented range restriction, the hero-death penalty directly contradicts the source (heroes lose half their money, NOT their items), and the spawn rules contain an invented "within 6 spaces of a hero" placement requirement. These would all mislead players at the table.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Aim order: invented mechanic.**
   - Summary (Hero Orders): "Aim: +1 range and +1 damage on one future attack (exhaust to use)."
   - Source: "Before the hero rolls dice for an attack, he may use the aim order to declare that he is making an aimed attack. This allows him to re-roll any number of dice after rolling for the attack (including any dice showing the 'miss' result). The hero must keep the second result." (l.1317-1321)
   - Aim is a re-roll, not a +1/+1 bonus. The summary's version resembles a power enhancement, not the order. Also omitted: aim removal conditions (taking wounds, moving, changing equipment, l.1329-1332).

2. **MAJOR — Dodge order: invented mechanic.**
   - Summary: "Dodge: Roll power dice; cancel 1 wound per power enhancement rolled (when attacked)."
   - Source: "A hero that has placed a dodge order may, when attacked, force his attacker (usually the overlord player) to re-roll any number of dice rolled for the attack. The hero player may only do this once for each attack, and must accept the second result." (l.1338-1343); "A dodge order stays with a hero until the start of his next turn, which means that he can 'dodge' multiple attacks." (l.1349-1351)
   - No power-dice rolling or wound cancellation exists anywhere in the source. Also omitted: aim and dodge cancel each other ("If an aimed attack is made against a target that has placed a dodge order, both abilities are ignored," l.1352-1354; reinforced at l.1670-1674).

3. **MAJOR — Guard order: invented restriction, wrong trigger.**
   - Summary: "Guard: Interrupt a nearby enemy's movement to make 1 attack (within half your range)."
   - Source: "At any point during the overlord player's turn (not during a hero's turn), a hero may use his guard order to immediately 'interrupt' the overlord player's turn and make one attack (following all the normal rules for line of sight and attacking)." (l.1366-1370)
   - There is no "within half your range" rule and no "nearby" requirement in the source — the interrupt attack follows all normal attack rules. The trigger is also broader than "an enemy's movement": it is any point during the overlord's turn (the guarding example, l.1469-1480, shows interrupting a declared attack).

4. **MAJOR — Hero death penalty contradicts the source.**
   - Summary: "The hero is moved to town, loses all items not in pack, and the Overlord removes conquest tokens equal to the hero's conquest value."
   - Source: "A hero that is killed loses half of his money, rounded down to the nearest 25 coins (e.g., a hero with 125 coins would be left with 75 coins after dying), but does not lose any of his items or skill cards." (l.1539-1542; reaffirmed l.1738-1740)
   - The summary inverts the penalty: it removes equipment the source explicitly protects, and omits the money loss that actually applies. (Conquest loss equal to the hero's conquest rating is correct, l.1534-1535.)

5. **MAJOR — Spawning: invented "within 6 spaces of a hero" requirement.**
   - Summary: "Monsters must be placed within 6 spaces of a hero, not in line of sight of any hero, and in a legal empty space."
   - Source spawn restrictions (l.1050-1088): not in a space containing a figure or obstacle; not in an unrevealed area; not in any hero's line of sight (monsters don't block LOS for spawn purposes); limited by available figures. No proximity requirement exists anywhere in the source (grep for "6 spaces"/"within ... spaces" returns only large-monster size text). This invented restriction would wrongly forbid legal overlord spawns.

6. **MODERATE — Large monsters: invented overlap rule.**
   - Summary: "Can end overlapping friendly monster spaces."
   - Source large-monster rules (l.1395-1422) say nothing of the kind, and the general movement rule requires all figures to "end their movement in an empty space" (l.727-728). Only familiars may end movement in occupied spaces (l.1262-1265) — this looks like a conflation. The summary also omits the actual special movement (Hell Hound/Dragon half-body or sidestep movement; four-space monsters move like regular figures).

7. **MINOR — "Excess fatigue becomes wounds" unsupported.**
   - Summary (Fatigue): "Cannot exceed maximum fatigue; excess fatigue becomes wounds."
   - Source: nothing converts fatigue to wounds; "Once a hero is out of fatigue tokens, he may not spend fatigue again until he has used the rest order or another method to regain some of his fatigue." (l.1678-1680, 1693). The wounds conversion appears imported from Descent 2nd Edition.

8. **MINOR — Pits: invented "begins activation" damage; omits armor bypass.**
   - Summary: "Deal 1 wound when a figure enters or begins activation in a pit space."
   - Source: a figure falls in only when it "moves so that the figure is only occupying pit spaces," suffering "one wound that cannot be reduced by armor" (l.1575-1579). There is no start-of-activation damage; repeated wounds each turn would be wrong. The armor-ignoring detail, climb-out cost (2 MP), and jump cost (3 MP/space) are omitted.

9. **MINOR — Town: "heal (costs movement)" not in source.**
   - Source town section (l.1721-1745): shopping costs 3 movement points; dead heroes are restored to full wounds/fatigue automatically at the temple. The purchasable list (l.1807-1825, prices partly lost to OCR) covers potions, treasure draws, skill cards, and training tokens — no healing service that "costs movement." Low impact, but unsupported as written. (The 3-MP shop cost is itself omitted.)

10. **MINOR — Omissions (no entire phase/system missing):** overlord converts 2 rolled surges to 1 threat (l.866-868); 50-coin bounty for killing a master monster (l.1519-1520); single re-roll limit per attack (l.1670-1674); conquest award amounts (+3 glyph, +2 to +4 chest, l.1231-1244); movement-actions cost table (drink potion 1 MP, open chest 2 MP, etc.); lingering effects (burn/poison/web/stun mechanics) and the abilities glossary — acceptable compression for a summary, listed for completeness.

### Mechanics verified correct
- Round structure (hero turns in chosen order, then overlord; turn tokens green/red), hero turn 3 steps (refresh/equip/action), overlord turn 3 steps, monster activation (move up to speed + 1 attack, splittable), attack sequence and miss/range logic, melee/ranged/magic trait power dice, power enhancement = +1 range or +1 damage, damage minus armor = wounds, LOS blockers (walls, closed doors, figures, blocking obstacles), movement through friendly figures with empty-space ending, fatigue spending (1 = 1 MP or 1 power die), glyph activation/teleport/monster-end-restriction, water blocks movement not LOS / rubble blocks both, rune-locked doors need runekey, conquest loss = killed hero's conquest value, Rest order (restore fatigue to max at start of next turn), conquest gains from glyphs/chests/quest tasks, training tokens raise traits.
- Note: the source itself is internally inconsistent on the attack step order (header list l.753-759 puts spend-before-success; body text l.821-874 puts success-before-spend) and on "2 other" vs "up to three miscellaneous" equipped items (l.607 vs l.1223). The summary follows the body text and the bullet list respectively; not counted as errors.
