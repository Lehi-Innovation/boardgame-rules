## amun-re — VERDICT: MAJOR

Source: SuperMeeple/TMG 2016 edition rulebook (Knizia). Extraction is clean and complete (components list, full phase rules, scoring summary, favor card reference).

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented "sacrifice track" (component + scoring source).**
   - Summary (Components): "Sacrifice track markers"; (Scoring): "**Temple Offerings:** Bonus points based on position on the sacrifice track."
   - Source: there is no sacrifice track anywhere. Temples score per the current Offering tile: "+1, +2, +3, or +4 VP for each Temple depicted among Provinces you control. The scoring value of each Temple is determined by the Gold shown on the current Offering tile." Components list has "4 Offering tiles", no track or track markers.

2. **MAJOR — Pyramid scoring misstated.**
   - Summary: "**Province Pyramids:** Points for the most pyramids in each province."
   - Source: "+1 VP for each Pyramid in a Province you control. Double Pyramids count as 2 Pyramids." and "+3 VP for each Pyramid in your single Province with the fewest Pyramids." Nothing scores "the most pyramids in each province"; the only majority scoring is the per-Nile-side +5 VP (which the summary does capture as "Pyramid Majorities").

3. **MAJOR — Final Scoring for Gold omitted entirely.**
   - Source: "FINAL SCORING ... +6 VP to the player with the highest combined value of Gold cards, +4 VP to the player with the second highest value, +2 VP to the player with the third highest value" (plus tie handling). The summary's scoring section ends at era scoring and never mentions end-game VP for remaining Gold — a whole scoring source missing, and it changes how players should manage money.

4. **MINOR — "Set Collection: Points for controlling provinces with matching symbols" overstated as a base scoring category.**
   - Source: the closest analog is the yellow Favor cards (+3 VP each if condition met: all provinces same Nile side, all/none next to Nile, all in Upper or Lower Egypt, ≥7 Favor symbols, ≥9 Farmers). These are card-driven bonuses, not an automatic "matching symbols" scoring category.

5. **MINOR — Invented penalty for lowest offering.**
   - Summary: "The player who sacrificed the least (or nothing) may get a penalty."
   - Source: no penalty exists. Rewards are 3 / 2 / 1 for 1st / 2nd / all other players; only Theft-card players get no Reward ("players that played a Theft card cannot collect a Reward"). The summary also undersells the reward ladder (says only the top sacrificer gets a bonus).

6. **MINOR — Terminology drift: "Power cards" vs source "Favor cards" (39 Favor cards).** Recognizable, but every rule reference in the source PDF says Favor.

7. **Trivial — starting money (20 Gold) and Theft card not mentioned in Setup ("Give each player starting money").**

## andean-abyss — VERDICT: MAJOR

Source: GMT 2nd Edition rules of play (Volko Ruhnke, COIN Vol. I). Extraction is complete through section 8 plus key terms index and Available Forces chart.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Government victory condition wrong (Aid invented into it).**
   - Summary (Scoring table): "Government | Support + Aid exceeds threshold".
   - Source 7.2: "Government: Total Support exceeds 60." Aid is a resource-income input (6.3.1), never part of the victory check. Margin (7.3) is "Total Support – 60".

2. **MAJOR — AUC victory condition invented.**
   - Summary: "AUC | AUC bases in departments with no FARC bases".
   - Source 7.2: "AUC: AUC has more Bases than FARC." Margin: "AUC Bases – FARC Bases." Nothing about departments without FARC bases.

3. **MAJOR — Cartels victory condition misrepresented as a sum.**
   - Summary: "Cartels | Resources + bases exceeds threshold".
   - Source 7.2: "Cartels have more than 10 Bases and have Resources above 40" — two independent thresholds (margin is the *lower* of Bases–10 and Resources–40), not an additive total.

4. **MINOR — AUC Special Activities missing Ambush.**
   - Summary lists "Assassinate, Extort"; source 4.4: "AUC may choose from Extort, Ambush, or Assassinate" (4.4.1: AUC Ambushes the same as FARC).

5. **MINOR — Cartels Rally row includes "cultivate (place drug crops)".**
   - Cultivate is a Special Activity (4.5.1: relocate or place 1 Cartels Base), not part of the Rally Operation; Rally itself (3.3.1) is the same place-guerrillas/base action all Insurgents share.

6. **MINOR — 2nd Eligible option restrictions omitted.**
   - Summary: both acting factions "choos[e] from: Event / Operation (+ Special Activity) / Pass". Source 2.3.4: the 2nd Eligible's menu depends on what the 1st did (Limited Op only if 1st did an Op; LimOp or Event if 1st did Op + Special Activity; full Op + SA if 1st did Event). A core COIN tension, simplified away.

7. **MINOR — Propaganda Round phases compressed.**
   - Summary: "Reset eligibility, collect resources, redistribute aid, check victory." Source 6.0 sequence: Victory, Sabotage, Resources, Support (Civic Action / Agitation / Election / FARC Zones / Elite Backing), Redeploy, Reset. Support and Redeploy phases (and El Presidente elections) are unmentioned; "redistribute aid" is not a thing (Aid simply adds to Government earnings, 6.3.1).

8. Supported claims verified: hidden/underground guerrillas must be Activated (Swept) before Assault can remove them (1.4.3, 3.2.3-3.2.4); pass keeps a faction Eligible and acting makes it Ineligible next card (2.3.1, 2.3.3, 2.3.7); only first 2 Eligible factions act per card (2.3); victory checked when a Propaganda card is played (6.1); Pop-0 spaces/LoCs never hold Support or Opposition (1.6.2); closest-to-condition (highest victory margin) wins after final Propaganda (7.3); non-player flowcharts enable solo/2-player (8.0).

## andor-the-family-fantasy-game — VERDICT: MAJOR

Source: KOSMOS 2020 English rulebook. Extraction is complete (components, setup, day/night sequence, fog/mine token tables, all 10 bridge-guard tasks).

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented loss condition / "sun marker on a day track" time system.**
   - Summary: Turn step 4 "**Advance Sun:** Move the sun marker forward"; "The group must complete the adventure before the sun reaches the end of the day track"; "Lose: Sun marker reaches the end of the day track"; Components: "Sun marker and day tracker"; Setup: "Set the sun marker to the beginning of the day track."
   - Source: there is no sun marker or day track. Time is per-hero **sun discs** ("Each step costs time i.e. you have to give up one of your sun discs"; archers have 9 instead of 6); when all heroes are out of discs, night falls. The loss condition is the **dragon reaching Rietburg**: "If he reaches Rietburg... you will lose the game. If you find all the wolf cubs before the dragon reaches Rietburg, you've won."

2. **MAJOR — The dragon/Gor threat system (the game's entire clock) is omitted.**
   - Source: each night the dragon advances 1-3 spaces on the dragon bar (red die), Gors move one space toward Rietburg, a Gor reaching Rietburg sounds the alarm (dragon advances again), and new Gors spawn per the dragon's cloud space; players push the dragon back by winning Gor fights or burning wood at the watchtower (max 3 times). None of this appears in the summary; in its place is the invented sun-track clock.

3. **MAJOR — Combat model wrong.**
   - Summary: "roll dice. Compare your roll (plus bonuses) to the monster's strength."
   - Source: there are no monster strength values or roll totals. You must place dice showing **sword symbols** on the sword storage spaces by the Gor: "In a forest, 2 dice must display swords... in the mountains... you will need 3 dice with swords." One roll per turn, battles can continue across turns/heroes, sword tokens substitute for rolled swords, and the magician wins instantly on a flash. ("Heroes can assist each other" is supported.)

4. **MAJOR — Invented modular board.**
   - Summary: "Game board tiles (modular, double-sided)", "Players explore a modular board", "The modular board means different layouts each game."
   - Source components: "1 Game board" — a single fixed board. Variety comes from task-card combinations, not board layout.

5. **MINOR — Invented archer ability.**
   - Summary: "Archer: Can attack from distance."
   - Source: "The archers... have 9 sun discs instead of 6" — endurance, not ranged attack. (Dwarf's actual signature ability — cave shortcuts and extra torch faces — is only loosely captured as "better at mining".)

6. **MINOR — Invented "letter" tutorial cards.**
   - Summary: "Rules are taught progressively through 'letter' cards that introduce new mechanics during play."
   - Source: progression is via numbered **task cards** in recommended challenge combinations (tasks 1-2, then 3-4, etc.); the only "letters" are Task 9's letter task tokens (find the letter in your hero's color), which teach nothing. Likely conflated with the original Legends of Andor legend cards.

7. **MINOR — Component errors:** "4 Hero figures" (source: 18 characters incl. **8 Heroes**, 1 Bridge Guard, 7 Gors, 1 Troll, 1 Dragon); "Bridge tokens" (no such component; there is a bridge guard figure and a bridge printed on the board); "Monster tokens" (Gors are figures; the tokens are fog/mine tokens). Night marker, well tokens, torch tokens, sun discs, dragon bar/die all unmentioned.

8. **MINOR — Turn order:** summary says "Players take turns in any agreed order"; source: night-marker holder starts, then clockwise ("move around the circle clockwise and take it in turns").

9. Supported claims verified: cooperative, all win/lose together; heroes are magician/warrior/archer/dwarf; find the 3 wolf cubs in the dwarven mine to win; mandatory fog reveal on the target space; heroes can share a space and assist in battle; multiple adventures/challenges of increasing difficulty.
