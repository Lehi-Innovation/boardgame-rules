### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Borodino victory conditions are largely invented.**
   - Summary: "geographic objectives (controlling the Fleches, Great Redoubt, Utitza)
     and enemy casualties. Each side has specific VP targets; the side meeting its
     objectives wins. Levels of victory (Decisive, Substantive, Marginal)."
   - Source (scenario rules 14-16): VP come from **Group Collapse** — French get 3 VP
     per collapsed Russian Activation Group (10 VP for the Russian Imperial Guard);
     Russians get 4 VP per collapsed French group (10 VP for the French Imperial Guard)
     — plus a single geographic objective: "The Coalition receives 10 VP for occupying
     hex 3417. The French receive 10 VP for occupying hex 3417." Winner: "The player
     with the most VP wins – the larger the margin, the greater the victory."
   - The terms "Fleches", named victory levels "Decisive/Substantive/Marginal", and any
     multi-objective list do not appear anywhere in the extracted text (grep-verified).
     There are no per-side VP targets; it is a simple most-VP comparison. The actual VP
     schedule (3/4/10 VP per collapse, 10 VP for hex 3417) is omitted entirely. This
     misleads on the core win condition.

2. **MAJOR — Invented "Combined Arms" bonus.** Summary (Actions / Shock Combat):
   "Combined Arms: Infantry and cavalry attacking together gain bonuses." Source 9.23
   says the opposite: "Infantry, Charging Cavalry and non-Charging Cavalry may **not**
   combine their strengths when attacking the same unit. Each attack is resolved
   separately." No combined-arms bonus exists anywhere in the rules.

3. **MAJOR — Reserve mechanic misdescribed.** Summary (Actions): "Units designated as
   Reserve activate during the special Reserve Phase, **not during normal activation
   draws**." Source 5.5: in the Reserve Phase each player may give one eligible Orders
   Command an *extra* activation (must be in the Overall Commander's Orders Range, not
   adjacent to enemy); the play note states "it is possible for an Orders Command to get
   three Activations in one turn," and 5.57 explicitly forbids Reserve-activating
   commands that had no AM in the Pool. There is no "reserve designation" that removes
   units from normal activation draws.

4. **MINOR — Schevardino victory condition embellished.** Summary: "Russian win by
   holding the redoubt **or inflicting sufficient casualties**." Source: "The player who
   occupies the Schevardino Redoubt at the end of the 5th Turn wins." No casualty
   condition exists. Also, the summary's Setup ("For each scenario... Place Activation
   Markers per scenario") contradicts the Schevardino scenario, which "uses no AM, and
   it does not use the Orders system."

5. **MINOR — "Rout" listed as a shock result.** Summary: "Results include Disorder,
   Withdrawal, Rout, or Elimination." The Shock Resolution Table has Disorder, Retreat,
   Breakthrough/Pursuit, Counter-Shock; 1.0 states Rout was *replaced* by Withdrawal
   (the summary itself says so later under Rally and Recovery — internal contradiction).

6. **MINOR — "Commitment rating" does not exist.** Summary: "Individual units have a
   Commitment rating determining their ability to engage." Commitment is a die roll
   checked against the unit's **Cohesion** rating (9.41/9.43), not a printed rating.

7. **MINOR — Unsupported fieldworks claim.** "Once captured, they provide reduced
   bonuses to the occupier" has no basis in the extracted text (the Terrain Effects
   Chart itself is not in the extraction, so this is flagged as unsupported rather than
   contradicted).

8. **MINOR — Omissions.** Scenario special rules absent from summary: Russian
   Destruction AM (burn villages / destroy bridges), Napoleon's variable movement
   (1D10 MP), Kutuzov MA 4, Russian Militia restrictions, Russians barred from Extended
   Movement, optional French Flanking Maneuver, Collapse threshold of 2/3 of units
   (10.52). The structural rules coverage (sequence of play, orders, fire, shock,
   charge, rally/recovery) is otherwise faithful.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the actual VP system is replaced with invented objectives and
victory levels, plus an invented Combined Arms bonus that directly contradicts 9.23 and
a misdescribed Reserve mechanic.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — 4-player team configuration wrong.** Summary (Special Rules / Team Mode):
   "Teams: 4 players **(2v2)**, 5 players (2-team + 1 individual), 6 players (3 teams of
   2 or 2-2-1-1)." The source configuration table lists for 4 players only **2-1-1**
   ("The dealer and the player opposite them play individually, while the other players
   play as a team") — there is no 2-2 option at 4 players; 2-2-2 at 6 players is noted
   as "The only example where the dealer plays in team." The 5- and 6-player entries
   match (2-2-1; 2-2-2 / 2-2-1-1). A 4-player table following the summary would set up
   team mode incorrectly.

2. **Trivial — omission.** Source: the player who leads the first trick "will become the
   dealer for the next round"; summary does not state how the next dealer is chosen.
   Also the Second Bottle / "sold bottle is always the one with the highest value"
   wording is faithfully captured.

No invented mechanics found; trick resolution, bottle transfer, end-of-round scoring,
and all three variants (Team, Second Bottle, Devil's Eye) match the source. Verdict:
**MINOR** on the strength of the 4-player team configuration error alone.
