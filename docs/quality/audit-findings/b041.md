# Audit Findings — Batch b041

Games: bowl-bound, brass-birmingham, brass-lancashire
Method: compared `rules/<slug>.md` against `extracted/<slug>-rules.txt`. Spot-checked numbers (components, costs, VP, thresholds), looked for invented mechanics, contradictions, and major omissions. OCR artifacts (notably icon glyphs dropped from the Brass rulebooks) were accounted for before flagging.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — Variable yardage mischaracterized.** Summary: "Some chart entries show variable yardage (e.g., '5-15' yards). Roll additional dice to determine exact yardage within the range." Source: variable entries are the coded symbols DS, X, T1, T2, T3, resolved either as fixed defaults (DS=6, X=12, T1=28, T2=55, T3=83) or by formula rerolls (e.g., "X = 40 minus the normal offensive [total]") (lines 338-352, 1495-1500). No chart entry shows a numeric range like "5-15"; the example is invented and "within the range" misstates the mechanic. The general gist (extra dice rolls determine yardage) is correct.
2. **MINOR — Invented penalty examples.** Summary lists "offensive holding ... defensive offsides" as penalty types. Neither term appears anywhere in the source (grep: no matches for holding/offside). OFF 5A and pass interference are confirmed (lines 13-14, 443-455). Also, "loss of down" as a penalty outcome is not in the source (source outcomes: down counts, down replayed, automatic first down — lines 371-377, 422).
3. **MINOR — Weather effect on fumbles unsupported.** Summary: weather modifies "passing accuracy, kicking distance, and fumble frequency." Source weather rules (lines 1094-1139) modify run/pass/kick-return yardage and make passes incomplete in strong wind; nothing modifies fumble frequency.
4. **Trivial — Overtime.** Summary: "Overtime rules follow college football conventions." Overtime is never mentioned in the source, but the source's blanket directive to apply official college football rules makes this a harmless extrapolation.

Structure is otherwise faithful: sequence of play, Priority Chart usage, special teams, advanced options (RZDZ, QB Sneak, Hail Mary, Hurry-Up, Wild Card / Run-Pass / Nickel Zone / Red Zone defenses, kicking options, elected safeties, alternate timing, power ratings) all match the source.

## brass-birmingham — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Link scoring criterion is wrong.** Summary (Scoring, step 1): "Each Link tile scores 1 VP per **beer barrel icon** in adjacent locations." Source: "For each of your Link tiles, score 1 VP for each [icon] displayed in adjacent locations" (lines 520-524) — the icon glyph is lost to OCR, but it is the location **link icon**, not a beer barrel (beer barrels in Birmingham are physical components placed beside Merchant tiles and on Breweries, not printed location icons; the identical sentence appears in Brass: Lancashire, a game with no beer at all). Scoring links by beer barrel icons would produce near-zero link VPs at most locations and badly misscore the game.
2. **MAJOR-contributing — Rail Network cost incomplete/misleading.** Summary: "Rail: costs 5 pounds + 1 coal. 1 or 2 Links per rail route." Source: 1 rail Link for £5; "a maximum of 2 rail Links for **£15**, if you also **consume 1 beer**" (must come from a Brewery, not Merchant beer), plus 1 coal per link (lines 1070-1082). The summary omits the £15 double-build price and the beer requirement, implying 2 links cost £10 + 2 coal. Also "per rail route" should be per Network action.
3. **MAJOR-contributing — Merchant Beer Bonuses omitted entirely.** Source (lines 938-962): consuming a Merchant's beer during a Sell grants that Merchant's bonus — Develop (Gloucester), +2 income spaces (Oxford), VPs (Nottingham, Shrewsbury), £5 (Warrington). The summary mentions Merchant beer nowhere (it even restricts beer sources to "your breweries or connected breweries"), dropping a whole scoring/income subsystem.
4. **MINOR — Invented "Scout pile" in setup.** Summary setup step 4: "Draw 1 additional card facedown (Scout pile) in Birmingham variant." Source: "Draw 1 additional card from the Draw Deck and place it face down in your player area; this is your [Discard Pile]" (lines 389-391; OCR-truncated, but it seeds the Discard Pile — nothing called a "Scout pile" or a "variant" exists).
5. **MINOR — Canal/Rail era building limits omitted.** Source: Canal Era max 1 of your Industry tiles per location; Rail Era allows multiple (lines 1116-1129). Absent from the Birmingham summary (it is present in the Lancashire summary).
6. **MINOR — Overbuilding condition understated.** Summary: "(must be your own tile or a lower-level tile)." Source: own tiles — any industry, replaced by higher level of same industry; opponents' tiles — only Coal Mines/Iron Works and only when **no cubes of that resource exist anywhere on the board or in its Market** (lines 1092-1111). The no-cubes condition is omitted.
7. **MINOR — Other omissions:** income capped at level 30 (lines 688-689); no loan below income level -10 (lines 985-989); Potteries with the lightbulb icon cannot be Developed (lines 1031-1040); Sell beer is required "usually," per amount on tile, not unconditionally (lines 901-905).

Everything else checked out: turn order by least money spent, no income after final round, coal closest-mine rule and £8 empty-market price context, iron any-source rule, end-of-Canal-Era teardown, flip table, farm breweries restricted to Brewery/Wild Industry cards.

### Verdict rationale
Wrong link-scoring rule (an end-of-era scoring source) plus the omitted Merchant Beer Bonus system and the wrong double-rail-link cost would mislead players at the table. MAJOR.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Link scoring "beer barrel icon" is impossible in this game.** Summary: "Each Link tile scores 1 VP per **beer barrel icon** in adjacent locations." Source: "score 1 VP for each [icon] displayed in adjacent locations" (lines 501-504; icon lost to OCR). Brass: Lancashire contains **no beer, beer barrels, or breweries anywhere** (components list lines 46-68; industries are Cotton Mills, Ports, Shipyards, Iron Works, Coal Mines). The "beer barrel icon" was evidently copied from the Birmingham summary and is an invented scoring criterion for a core scoring source.
2. **MAJOR — Distant Cotton Market mechanics misdescribed and payoff omitted.** Summary: "Draw a tile; if the arrow points down, the market price drops" / "move the market marker down one space" / "If the marker passes the red line, cotton cannot be sold." Source (lines 884-913): flip the topmost DCM tile and move the marker "by the **number of spaces shown** in the bottom centre of the flipped tile" (tile values vary, e.g. 0/-1/-3 per line 1136-1137) — not arrows, not always one space; then "**Advance your Income Marker** ... by the number of spaces displayed on the Income icon to the left of the Distant Cotton Market Marker" — the income payoff that is the whole point of distant sales is omitted from the summary; the cutoff is the "**X" space** (if the marker reaches it, the Sell action ends immediately), not a "red line." The summary also omits that distant-market sales require connection to a market trade icon (lines 889-892).
3. **MAJOR-contributing — Double Action Build omitted.** Source: "Double Action Build — May be used instead of both of your turn's actions. Discard any 2 cards and regard them as any 1 Location card" (lines 817-825; reiterated lines 1150-1154). This action (Lancashire's counterpart to Birmingham's Scout/Wild cards) is entirely absent from the summary's Actions list and player reference.
4. **MINOR/MODERATE — Loan restriction omitted; Deck tile purpose misstated.** Source: "You may not take a loan during the final 4 rounds of the Rail Era"; the Deck tile (Nathan Rothschild side) marks the last loan round (lines 926-949). Summary says only that the Deck tile "signals the final rounds" and gives no loan cutoff. Also omits the -10 income floor for loans (lines 966-968).
5. **MINOR — Overbuilding misstated.** Summary: "overbuild ... opponent's lower-level **Coal Mines** in your network." Source: opponents' **Coal Mines or Iron Works**, and only when no cubes of that resource remain anywhere on the board or in its Market (lines 1035-1042). Iron Works and the no-cubes condition are dropped; "in your network" is not a stated overbuild condition.
6. **MINOR — Port selling ownership omitted.** Source: cotton may be sold to an unflipped Port "owned by any player"; flipping it advances **its owner's** income (lines 874-883). Summary's "Flip a connected Port tile to sell cotton through it" implies your own port and omits the owner's income gain.
7. **MINOR — Other omissions:** restricted Shipyard tiles cannot be built until developed (lines 854-865); income capped at level 30 (lines 655-656); empty-market prices (£5 coal / £5 iron, lines 750-753, 780-783).

Correct elsewhere: shipyards flip immediately when built; era/round structure; turn order; end-of-Canal-Era teardown including DCM reset and Deck tile reinsertion; end-of-Rail-Era cash scoring; negative income shortfall; Canal Era 1-tile-per-location; coal/iron consumption rules.

### Verdict rationale
An invented link-scoring criterion referencing a resource that does not exist in this game, a misdescribed Distant Cotton Market (core sell system) with its income payoff omitted, and a wholly omitted action (Double Action Build) would mislead players at the table. MAJOR.
