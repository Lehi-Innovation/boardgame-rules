## el-grande

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1: Grande piece incorrectly described as immovable and counting for scoring

**Summary claim:** "The Grande always remains in its home region and counts for scoring there."

**Contradiction 1 — Grande cannot move:** The source explicitly allows Grande movement via action cards.
- Source lines 464-469: "Grande. (two cards) You may move your Grande into a different region, unless it is in the King's region, in which case it cannot move. You may choose any region except the Castillo and the King's region."

**Contradiction 2 — Grande does NOT count for standard scoring:** 
- Source lines 160-163: "The Grandes serve only to identify the home region and are **not counted** when determining the majority in a region."
- The Grande counting as 1 is a VARIANT rule only: Source lines 510-511: "Home Region Advantages [Variant]: The Grande piece counts as 1 for determining who has the most in a region."

The summary conflates the optional variant with the base game rule. In the base game, the Grande is purely a marker, does not count for scoring, AND can be moved.

### Finding 2: Home Bonus (2-point scoring source) omitted from summary

**Summary:** No mention of the Home Bonus anywhere in the summary.

**Source lines 180-182:** "If a player has the most Caballeros in the region where their Grande stands when that region is scored, they receive an additional two points. They do not receive the bonus if they are tied for first in this region."

This is a distinct scoring source that would affect the final score of every game. Missing from the summary's Scoring section, Player Reference, and Special Rules.

### Victory / Game-End Verified

- Source lines 185-187: "Whoever has progressed the furthest along the scoring track is the winner." — CONFIRMED.
- Summary: "General scoring occurs after rounds 3, 6, and 9."

### Turn Structure Verified
- Summary: "Highest to lowest power card determines turn order."
- Source lines 83-84: "The player who played the highest power card goes first, and then play proceeds from player to player in decreasing value." — CONFIRMED.
- Summary: "Starting with the leader (youngest in round 1, then whoever played lowest last round)."

### King's Bonus Verified

## elder-sign

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1: Monsters do NOT need to be defeated before other adventure tasks

**Summary (Special Rules):** "Monster markers: Appear via Mythos/Adventure effects. Must be defeated (complete their task) before completing Adventure tasks."

**Contradiction from source:**
- Source lines 436-438: "Order of Tasks: Typically players can complete tasks on Adventure cards in any order. The only exception is when an Adventure card has an arrow on its left side."

Monster tasks are integrated as additional tasks on an Adventure card. The standard rule is that tasks may be completed in any order. There is no rule requiring monster tasks to be completed before other tasks — that ordering restriction exists only when an arrow is present on the card. The summary's claim would cause players to complete monster tasks first when the rules allow free ordering.
