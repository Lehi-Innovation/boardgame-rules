## panzer-leader

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn structure Phase 1 is wrong: "Direct Fire" does not exist as Phase 1

Summary Turn Structure (Phase 1): "Direct Fire Phase — The active player may fire with any or all eligible units at enemy units within range and line of sight."

Source (Section IV, lines 103–112):
> "1. Combat Phase — Allied player executes and resolves minefield attacks, direct and indirect fire combat, and writes 'orders' for the next turn's indirect fire combat."

The source Phase 1 is a full **Combat Phase** that encompasses minefield attacks, direct fire, indirect fire, and writing indirect fire orders. The summary's "Direct Fire Phase" is a severe narrowing of what Phase 1 actually covers. No "Direct Fire Phase" by that name exists.

### Finding 2 — Air Phase (Phase 2) entirely omitted from the core turn sequence

Summary lists 4 phases: Direct Fire → Movement → Defensive Fire → Close Assault. There is no Phase 2 "Air Phase."

Source (lines 107–109):
> "2. Air Phase — Allied player moves any aircraft units desired. German player executes and resolves anti-aircraft attacks. Allied player resolves fighter bomber attacks."

The Air Phase is a standard, mandatory Phase 2 of every player segment. The game has full Airpower rules (Section XII, lines 1078–1130) with fighter-bombers and observation aircraft. The summary incorrectly buries airpower as a Special Rules note: "Air support: Some situations include air strikes as special events." The source says aircraft are standard units that act in Phase 2, not situational special events.

### Finding 3 — "Defensive Fire Phase (Opportunity Fire)" listed as a core phase; source labels it an Optional Rule

Summary Phase 3: "Defensive Fire Phase (Opportunity Fire) — The non-active player may fire at enemy units that moved during the Movement Phase."

Source (lines 172–173):
> "No combat, enemy or friendly, takes place during the movement phase (EXCEPTION: See OVERRUN RULE and **OPPORTUNITY FIRE OPTIONAL RULE**)."

Opportunity Fire is explicitly labeled an Optional Rule in the source. The summary presents it as the mandatory Phase 3 of the standard turn structure, which is false. Players using the summary would treat optional content as required.

### Finding 4 — Phase ordering in source is Combat → Air → Movement → Close Assault; summary has a different order

Correct order per source: Phase 1 Combat, Phase 2 Air, Phase 3 Movement, Phase 4 Close Assault.
Summary order: Phase 1 Direct Fire, Phase 2 Movement, Phase 3 Defensive Fire (Opportunity Fire), Phase 4 Close Assault.

The movement of Phase 3 (Air) to a buried special-rules note and the insertion of Opportunity Fire as a mandatory phase would cause players to sequence the turn incorrectly.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 3 (192 German counters, 192 Allied counters, 7 cards), SUPPORTED: 3

## panzerblitz

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn sequence is reversed: summary says Movement then Combat; source says Combat then Movement

Summary Turn Structure:
> "1. Movement Phase — Move any or all units up to their Movement Factor."
> "2. Combat Phase — Resolve attacks with eligible units against enemy units within range and line of sight."

Source (page 11, lines 332–334):
> "B- Attack takes place before the movement portion of a player's turn. Only the player whose turn it is may attack, the other player is considered the 'defender.'"

Source Sequence of Play (lines 898–921) confirms: Steps 1–3 are minefield attacks, announcing attacks, and resolving normal combat; Steps 4–5 are moving vehicular and non-vehicular units. Combat explicitly precedes movement.

The summary inverts this sequence. Players following the summary would move first, then fight — the opposite of the source.

### Finding 2 — Stacking limit misstated for Russian side

Summary: "Stacking: Multiple friendly units may occupy one hex (varies by situation; typically 3 units max)."

Source (lines 299–300):
> "A- The Russians may stack two units per hex.
> B- The Germans may stack three units per hex."

The "typically 3 units max" is only correct for Germans. Russians are limited to 2. This is a firm rule, not situationally variable.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (6-minute turn, 3 mapboard sections, 2 Russian stack limit, 3 German stack limit), SUPPORTED: 3 (6-minute turn and mapboard sections correct; Russian stack limit wrong)

## parks

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Tiebreaker is wrong: summary says "most unspent tokens"; source says "most Parks visited, then shared"

Summary Scoring / Victory Conditions:
> "Tiebreaker: most unspent tokens."

Source (lines 294–296):
> "The player with the most points at the end is the winner of Parks! If tied, the win goes to the player who visited the most Parks. If still tied, the win is shared."

The source tiebreaker is (1) most Parks visited, then (2) win is shared. There is no mention of unspent tokens as a tiebreaker anywhere in the source.

### Finding 2 — Gear purchase requires Sunshine tokens specifically, not generic tokens

Summary Actions (Trail End Actions):
> "Buy Gear: Spend tokens shown on a Gear card to claim it and gain its ongoing ability."

Source (lines 235–238):
> "Select one of the three available Gear cards on the board and turn in **Sunshine** equal to its cost."

Gear cards cost Sunshine tokens, not generic tokens of any type. This is a fundamental constraint on how Gear is purchased that the summary omits.

### Finding 3 — First Hiker Marker: summary says it passes to player who "reached Trail End first each Season"; source says it goes to the first player to Reserve a Park

Summary Special Rules:
> "The First Hiker Marker passes to the player who reached the Trail End first each Season."

Source (lines 227–232):
> "The first player to place their Hiker here [the Reserve a Park area] also gains the First Hiker Marker as a benefit by placing their Hiker in the far right slot."

The marker goes to the first player to choose the Reserve a Park action at Trail End (by taking the far-right Reserve slot), not simply the first to arrive at Trail End. Any Hiker arriving at Trail End could choose Visit a Park, Buy Gear, or Reserve a Park. Only the first to specifically Reserve a Park gets the marker.

### Finding 4 — Campfire resets at wrong time: summary says "start of each season"; source says "when a Hiker reaches Trail End"

Summary Campfire rule:
> "Your Campfire resets (re-lights) at the start of each season."

Source (lines 206–208):
> "A player's Campfire will relight when one of their Hikers reaches Trail End."

The campfire relights mid-season whenever any of a player's hikers reaches Trail End — not only at the start of a season. This distinction matters because a player could relight their campfire in the middle of a season by sending one hiker to Trail End, then use the campfire again for their remaining hiker still on the Trail.

Source also confirms (lines 216–218): "When a Hiker arrives here [Trail End], the player immediately relights their Campfire if it is extinguished."

### Finding 5 — First Hiker Marker is worth 1 point; summary omits this from scoring

Source (line 232): "First Hiker Marker is worth 1 POINT at the end of the game."
Source (lines 290–293): game end scoring includes "The [First Hiker Marker] also gives 1 Point to the player who has it."

Summary Scoring section lists: Park cards, Photos, Personal Bonus — no mention of the 1-point First Hiker Marker bonus.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 9 (48 Park cards, 10 Season cards, 12 Year cards, 36 Gear cards, 15 Canteen cards, 10 Hikers, 5 Campfires, 28 Photos, 12 Wildlife tokens), SUPPORTED: 9

## patchwork

**Verdict: PASS**

All key claims verified against source:

- Turn order (furthest behind goes next; tie → top token goes first): confirmed (source lines 3–6).
- Action A (advance to just in front of opponent, 1 button per space): confirmed (source lines 15–17).
- Action B (5 steps: choose from 3 patches clockwise of neutral token, move neutral token, pay buttons, place patch, advance time token): confirmed (source lines 18–81).
- Special Tile (first to fill 7x7 gets 7 points): confirmed (source lines 144–145).
- Scoring formula (remaining buttons + 7 if special tile − 2 × empty spaces): confirmed (source lines 106–113).
- Tiebreaker (player who reached final space first wins): confirmed (source lines 108–109).
- 5 buttons starting amount per player: confirmed (source lines 43–45).
- Components (33 patches, 5 special patches, 2 quilt boards, 1 neutral token, 1 special tile, button tiles): confirmed.
- Campfire/special patch rule (special patches are the only way to fill single spaces): confirmed (source lines 92–94).

One neutral-token phrasing difference: summary says "Place it where the chosen patch was" while source says "Place the neutral token next to the chosen patch" — functionally equivalent (the chosen patch is removed, the neutral token fills that gap adjacent to the next patch). Not a gameplay error.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 11 (5 buttons start, 33 patches, 5 special patches, 7x7 square, 7 point tile, 2 points per empty space, 32×1-button, 12×5-button, 5×10-button, 1×20-button tiles, 9×9 board), SUPPORTED: 11
