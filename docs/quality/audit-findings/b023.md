## assault-on-hoth-the-empire-strikes-back — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR — the summary appears to be written from movie knowledge rather than the rulebook; it invents units, mechanics, and victory conditions while omitting the actual game systems.**

1. **Wrong component counts.** Summary: "55 stand-up playing pieces", "2 decks of move cards (Rebel and Imperial)", "8 picture dice". Source (2): ".59 counters .60 plastic stands .40 markers .Ten dice (eight fire dice and two normal) .42 cards". The pieces count is wrong (55 vs 59), the 40 markers and 2 damage dice are omitted, and the cards are not two per-faction "move card" decks but a shared Action Deck, a shared Event Deck, and 1 Hero Card (2.4).
2. **Invented turn structure.** Summary: "The game alternates between Rebel and Imperial turns. Each turn: 1. Draw move cards from your deck. 2. Play cards to activate and move units..." Source (4/5): a turn is a series of Action Rounds; each round, the top card of the *shared* Action Deck is drawn and "all of one player's units of the listed type may perform the action indicated on the card." There are no alternating player turns and no per-player decks.
3. **Invented units/mechanics — ion cannon and transports on the board.** Summary: "Fire ion cannon: The Rebel ion cannon can fire at Imperial units to disable them temporarily"; "Evacuate transports: The Rebel player attempts to move transports off the evacuation edge of the board"; Player Reference lists "Ion Cannon" and "Transport" as Rebel units. The words "ion cannon" never appear in the source; Rebel forces are "Laser Towers ... Rebel light and heavy Trooper units ... Snowspeeders" (1, 3.2). Transports are never board units — they escape via "Transports Away!" Event Cards drawn from the Event Deck (8).
4. **Invented units — probe droids.** Summary: "The Imperial player sets up AT-AT walkers, AT-ST walkers, snowtroopers, and probe droids"; "Probe droids serve as Imperial scouts." Source Imperial setup (3.1): "four Walkers numbered 1-4, two AT-STs, eight light Snowtroopers, and four heavy Snowtroopers." No probe droids exist in the game.
5. **Invented rule — weather.** Summary: "Weather and terrain affect movement rates." Terrain does (rough costs Walkers 3 MP, 6.1); no weather rule exists anywhere in the source.
6. **Wrong/incomplete victory conditions.** Summary: Imperial wins by destroying "the Rebel power generator"; Rebel wins by evacuating "a required number of transports and key personnel." Source (10): Imperial wins by destroying the *Shield Generator* "before the fifth Transport Away! card is pulled from the Event Deck"; Rebel wins "if the 5th Transport Away! card is drawn" first, or alternatively "if he destroys all Imperial forces currently on the map." The 5-card threshold, the Power Grid rule (destroying it drops the Shield Generator's armor from 3 to 2), and the Rebel alternate win are all missing; "key personnel" is invented.
7. **Major omissions.** The entire Luke Skywalker / 10 Force Points system (9), harpoon attack resolution (5 dice same macrohex / 4 dice otherwise, instantly destroys a Walker, 7.4), the Walker/Snowspeeder damage tables (7.3), the fire-strength/armor-strength dice system with range and terrain modifiers (7.2), macrohexes, the Base/tunnel system (6.5), and Walker facing/field of fire (6.3, 7.1) are absent or reduced to vague allusions.
8. **Numbers:** of the checkable numbers in the summary, 55 (pieces) is wrong, 8 dice is incomplete (10), "2 decks of move cards" contradicts 42 cards/3 types; player count 2 is correct. Source numbers absent: 5 Transports Away!, 10 Force Points, 5 MPs per move, 3 Snowtroopers per Walker, armor 3/2 for Shield Generator.

## asteroyds — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR — numbers and core racing rules are essentially perfect; the shooting mechanic used by three game modes is never explained.**

Numbers checked (all supported):
1. Components 1 board / 6 spacecraft / 1 stopwatch / 3 dice / 48 transparent tokens / 36 asteroids / 6 Pilot tokens / 24 Target tokens / 6 status charts = source "Box Contents" verbatim.
2. Racing time limit "50 seconds for beginners and 20 seconds for experts" = source.
3. Red die: red asteroids + red gates + white/red move 2 spaces, stop on last available space = source 2.1).
4. White die: 1 space, "the move is void" if blocked = source.
5. Blue die: 1 space, pushes one asteroid if its destination is free, cannot push craft/pod/platform = source.
6. Priority: smallest priority number moves first and completes its whole move = source (example: asteroid 6 beats gate 24).
7. Damage: asteroid-hits-craft 1 (0 shielded); craft vs fixed object or asteroid 2 (1 shielded) = source Collisions 1–3.
8. Destroy asteroid: once per game, 4 damage (3 with shield), must have the capacity or "pathetically crash" = source.
9. Victory: through the 4 gates; round finishes; tie → leftmost planning column, then least damage = source "Victory/Draw".
10. Drones: behave as asteroids (2 red / 1 white), priority over asteroids, pushed by blue, 4 different symbols to win, tie → least damage; multiple players may shoot the same drone in one round = source "Drones".
11. All 6 pilot powers (Hector, Dolorosa, Lawrence, Yayenek, M'Hand, 1634) = source verbatim.

Findings:
1. **Omission — how shooting works.** Source "Advanced rules: Shooting": "their craft projects a laser beam forward. That beam cannot reach the other spacecraft but it is obstructed by any other object in its way"; players "can program as many shots as they wish for their turn." The summary's Targets/Shooting/Drones modes all require shooting but the summary never explains the beam mechanic, that it cannot hit other craft, or the unlimited-shots rule. (The "Shoot" pictogram is also missing from the planning-action list.)
2. **Minor drift — time limits.** Summary states 50s/20s as the global planning limit; source gives 1 minute / 30 seconds for the Targets and Drones modes.
3. **Minor omission — Targets handicap** (unbalanced teams need only 4 of 6 targets) is not mentioned.

## at-the-gates-of-loyang — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR — component numbers and round structure are excellent, but a core action (Buy a Two-pack) is completely misdescribed, and the Regular-customer penalty rule is wrong.**

Numbers checked (supported):
1. 237 wooden tokens; vegetables 48 Wheat / 46 Pumpkins / 44 Turnips / 36 Cabbages / 32 Beans / 25 Leeks = source. (Trivial mislabel: summary calls all 237 "vegetable tokens"; the 237 includes the 4 Scoring markers and 2 Starting player tokens — vegetables alone are 231.)
2. 24 1-Cash, 14 5-Cash; 120 cards = 4 Storehouse/Cart + 4 Turn Summary + 6 Loan + 36 Private fields (4 Home fields, 9 spaces) + 70 Action cards (6 Common fields, 14 Market stalls, 14 Regular, 14 Casual, 22 Helpers) = source component list, all exact.
3. Setup: 10 Cash; Shop stock 3/2/2/2/1/1; 8 Private fields as 2 shuffled sets of 3/4/5/6 spaces; start plant only wheat/pumpkin/turnip, max 2 players per type = source.
4. 9 rounds; deal 4 Action cards; play exactly 2 (1 hand + 1 Courtyard); Common field costs 2 Cash; Loan = 5 Cash, −1 step at game end; tiebreaker most Cash = source.

Findings:
1. **Wrong rule — Two-pack.** Summary Action H: "Buy a Two-Pack (Once Per Round) — Purchase 2 vegetables of the same type as a bundle." Source: "A Two-pack consists of two Action cards which are drawn from the Draw pile. Its cost is the greater of the number of either Helpers or Market stalls that the player has in his 'played' area." The summary describes vegetables, not Action cards (it has apparently confused the Two-pack with the Haggler helper: "buy 2 vegetables of the same kind in your Shop for the price of 1"). An entire core action — including its cost formula, keep 0/1/2, stacking, and reveal rules — is contradicted/missing.
2. **Wrong rule — Regular customer penalty.** Summary: "Failed delivery: If you cannot deliver ... turn the marker to its red side and pay a 2-Cash penalty"; Special Rules: "penalty of 2 Cash per round for each unsatisfied Regular customer." Source: on a missed delivery "he turns the Satisfaction marker to the red side"; the 2-Cash penalty is paid only "If the player does not deliver to a Regular customer when the red side is already facing up" — i.e. the first miss is free. Players following the summary would pay penalties they don't owe.
3. **Wrong rule — Sow action.** Summary: "Buy a vegetable from your Shop and place it on an empty field." Source Action A: "The player takes 1 vegetable from his Cart and places it on an empty field." Buying is a separate action; also the per-field planting restrictions ("The top border of each field shows which vegetables can be planted on it") are omitted.
4. **Wrong number — Helpers.** Summary: "22 different Helpers exist." Source: "There are 20 different Helpers. Each Helper appears on one card, with the exception of the Trader, who appears on three" (22 cards, 20 kinds).
5. **Omission — Casual customer ±2 modifier.** Source Action G: price −2 Cash if you have more Casual than Regular customers, +2 if fewer. Entirely absent (the summary's own Components note that Loan card backs are "Income modifiers for Casual customers" hints at it, but the rule is never stated).
6. **Minor:** invented "satisfied delivery: flip the marker to the completed side" (the marker is only turned red on a miss; only the Saleswoman turns it back); Storehouse upgrade (2 Cash, capacity 1→4) omitted; first Path step costs 1 Cash per round omitted; second tiebreaker (most vegetables) omitted; 4-player phrasing ("both pairs simultaneously" vs Starting+Second simultaneously, then partners).

## aton — PASS

**Verdict: PASS — faithful and complete; every checked number matches.**

Numbers checked (all supported):
1. 72 cards values 1–4, 36 per color; 58 priest counters (29+29); 2 white exchange counters; 2 scoring counters = source component list.
2. 4 temples × 12 squares; scoring strip to 40; Kingdom of the Dead 8 squares; 4 cartouches per side = source board description.
3. 1st cartouche: higher card gets double the difference; equal = no points = source (example: 1 vs 4 → 6 points).
4. 2nd cartouche: lower value goes first; tie → 1st-cartouche values; still tied → draw cards until lower, returned to bottom of pile = source.
5. Removal = card value − 2; positive removes opposing, −1 removes own, not in round 1; insufficient → remove only those available = source.
6. 3rd cartouche: temples numbered ≤ card value = source.
7. 4th cartouche: place counters = card value, 1 per unoccupied square; none in stock → place none; overflow to Kingdom of the Dead; Kingdom overflow → back to stock = source.
8. Scoring: Temple 1 difference; Temple 2 = 5 P; Temple 3 = own count; Temple 4 = 3 P per occupied blue square (1 blue per temple); black squares 8 P; bonus squares +1/+2; no majority → no points (incl. black) = source.
9. Post-scoring: each removes 4 own counters, leader first (red if tied), one per temple if possible, else highest-numbered temple = source.
10. End conditions: immediate win during 1st/4th cartouche on 40 points / all 12 squares of a temple / all yellow / all green; exchange counter once per game = source.

Minor note (trivial): the summary's "If the game ends via scoring (Kingdom of the Dead fills), complete scoring is carried out" slightly compresses the source's "the game ends after the scoring round in which a player has reached 40 or more points" — a Kingdom-of-the-Dead scoring where nobody reaches 40 does not end the game. The summary's own post-scoring cleanup steps imply continuation, so this is unlikely to mislead.

3. Up to 3 actions, each once per turn except Diplomatic Blitz = source VIII.
4. Movement: adjacent region; tanks/planes 2 regions; tanks can't pass through unowned regions, planes can; amphibious units may not move before/after; planes unblockable = source X.
5. Land battle: commit 4 (all if fewer); max committed 4/5/6... per round; defender acts first; 1 die per unit, 2 per tank, never fewer than 2 dice; hits matched to rolling player's units, one die per unit; eliminate Infantry→Tanks→Artillery→Planes; no retreats = source XIII.
6. Naval: max 8 cards per row; 2 regular dice + modifiers; hit applied to first ship in battle line (carrier-vs-carrier exception); destroyer/sub/carrier 1 hit, battleship 2 (sideways, removed but repaired); retreat = lose = source XV.
7. Modifiers: BB vs DD +2; Sub vs BB +2; DD vs Sub +2; Carrier vs non-carrier +1; +1 per adjacent carrier (max 2); stack = source verbatim.
8. Diplomatic Blitz: adjacent 8+, non-adjacent 9+; success = free infantry + Economics card; failure → others roll 9+, highest wins, ties re-roll = source XVI.

Trivial notes: summary's battle setup says "Each side commits up to 4 units" — source requires choosing four (or all reserves if fewer); summary omits the naval tie rule (tied match-up = no hit). Neither is likely to mislead materially.

## attacktix-battle-figure-game-star-wars — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR — faithful overall; one Special Rules bullet contradicts the rulebook's FAQ on hand-knocked figures.**

Numbers checked (all supported):
1. Quick Battle: 3 figures, save the Emperor for master battles; line up about 3 feet apart; youngest first = source Level 1.
2. Move any number of figures, any direction, up to tix on base; attack with 2 different figures; reload missiles at start of next turn = source.
3. Equal Points: 100 standard, try 200 or 500 = source.
4. Master Battle: battle area about 3 feet; starting line 2 tix from edge; returning figures placed 2 tix from your starting line; red/black grind guess for first turn = source Level 2.
5. Special powers: only when window is white; trigger as soon as the figure is knocked down; target by name, Attacktix class, or Star Wars class; if target unavailable, no effect = source.

Findings:
1. **Contradiction — hand-knocked own figures.** Summary Special Rules: "Self-inflicted casualties: If you accidentally knock over your own figure, it is defeated." The summary's Scoring section states the shoot case correctly, but this bullet as written would cause wrong play on a hand bump.

## attangle — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR — rules content is faithful, but one edge-case rule is invented and the stack-ownership definition is missing.**

Numbers checked (all supported unless noted):
1. 36 playing pieces, 18 black / 18 white = source Components.
2. Pool of 18 in front of each player, may not be hidden = source ("It may not be hidden" → "visible pool").
3. White starts; passing not allowed; place or capture = source.
4. Place anywhere vacant except center d4; center vacant the whole game = source.
5. Capture moves exactly 2 attackers; straight lines; any of the 6 directions; may cross the center; no jumping; may not move except to capture; not onto vacant spaces or own pieces; converge from different directions; top 2 pieces must be the current player's = source.
6. After capture: take back topmost piece to pool; no stack may exceed 3 after the take-back; stacks never split; capture mandatory when pool is empty = source.
7. Win: first to create the third triple stack; triple stacks can never be captured or moved and remain on the board = source + Q&A.
8. Stack heights only 2 or 3 = source Q&A.
9. Board "(4 spaces per side, 37 intersections)" — not stated in the source (only "a hexagonal board" with center "d4"); consistent with a hex-4 board but unverifiable from this text.

Findings:
1. **Invented rule — draw.** Summary: "If a player cannot place (pool is empty) and cannot capture, the game is a draw (though this is extremely rare in practice)." The source contains no draw rule and never addresses this situation; both the rule and the "extremely rare" claim are unsupported.
2. **Omission/drift — stack ownership.** Source: "A player always owns a stack by controlling the topmost piece." The summary never states this, and its Overview says the goal is "3 triple stacks ... of their own color," which could be misread as needing all 3 pieces of one color — captured stacks are normally mixed. The win actually depends on controlling the top of the stack.
3. **Trivial wording:** "any combination of single pieces and double stacks" is loose — source allows exactly "2 single pieces or a single piece plus 1 double stack" (two double stacks is not an option); the summary's own parenthetical lists the correct two combinations.
