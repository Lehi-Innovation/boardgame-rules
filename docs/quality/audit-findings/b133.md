## luftwaffe

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — extracted source present and is the Luftwaffe rulebook.

### Finding 1 — Turn structure is entirely invented

The summary describes a 7-phase "raid" structure: "Planning Phase → Approach Phase → Interception Phase → Combat Phase → Bombing Phase → Return Phase → Assessment Phase." None of these named phases (Approach Phase, Interception Phase, Combat Phase, Bombing Phase, Return Phase, Assessment Phase) exist in the source.

**Summary:** "Each raid follows these phases: 1. Planning Phase … 2. Approach Phase … 3. Interception Phase … 4. Combat Phase … 5. Bombing Phase … 6. Return Phase … 7. Assessment Phase"

**Source (section 5.3):** The actual quarter sequence is: 1) Reinforcement Phase, 2) Redeployment Phase, 3) Planning Phase, 4) Economic Phase (Advanced), 5) RAF & Ploesti Phase, 6) Operational Turns (up to 20 individual German/American turns of free-form hex movement and combat), 7) Replacement Phase, 8) Repair Phase.

The source says: "The term 'turn' refers to the operational phase of an individual player turn during which that player is moving his units and initiating combat." (section 5.1). There are 20 such turns per quarter. Units move freely on a hex map; there is no fixed approach/interception/return sequence — German fighters choose when to intercept; the American player chooses routes.

No support found for "Approach Phase," "Interception Phase," "Return Phase," or "Assessment Phase" under any name. These are invented procedural phases.

### Finding 2 — Victory conditions are wrong for all game modes

**Summary:** "American VP: Destroying strategic targets, reducing German war production. German VP: Shooting down American bombers and fighters. The VP balance at campaign end determines the winner. Decisive, substantive, or marginal victory levels exist for both sides."

**Source errors:**

(a) **Basic Game** (section 4.0): Victory is binary/ternary based solely on bombed aircraft factory city count. "The German player wins if the American player bombs eight or fewer cities that have aircraft factories by the end of play." "If the American player bombs nine, 10 or 11 cities that have aircraft factories, the game is a draw." "The American player wins if he bombs 12 or more cities that have aircraft factories." No VP balance. German does not track VP or shooter kills.

(b) **Tournament Game** (section 19.0): "The American player wins by bombing all targets of the designated type before the end of the game. The German player wins by avoiding the American victory condition." Again, no German VP for shooting down bombers.

(c) **Advanced Game** (section 26.0): Only the American player scores VP — "The German player doesn't receive victory points. Essentially, the German player wins by successfully defending the Reich's industrial infrastructure." (26.16). VP thresholds: 241+ = American Strategic Victory; 201-240 = American Tactical Victory; 161-200 = American Marginal Victory; 121-160 = Draw; 81-120 = German Marginal Victory; 41-80 = German Tactical Victory; 0-40 = German Strategic Victory. There are 6 named levels (Strategic/Tactical/Marginal for each side) plus Draw — not "decisive, substantive, marginal" as the summary states ("substantive" does not appear in the source at all, and "decisive" does not appear in this context).

(d) The summary says "German VP: Shooting down American bombers and fighters." The source: the German player earns no VP for shootdowns in any mode. German success is purely defensive — preventing the American from bombing enough targets.

### Finding 3 — Game structure: "raids" vs "quarters"

**Summary:** "The game is played over multiple raids representing a three-month bombing campaign (Advanced/Tournament rules require up to 10 separate raids)."

**Source:** "Luftwaffe 2 is played in a series of 'Quarters,' each of which represent three months." (5.1). "The Tournament Game consists of 10 quarters, each with 20 turns." (18.1). The unit is "quarters" (each with 20 individual turns), not "raids." A "raid" is an American mission within a quarter — there can be multiple per quarter.

### Finding 4 — Fuel shortage penalty direction is missing

The summary omits the fuel shortage penalty rule, which materially affects victory calculation. Source (4.4): "For each American unit that fails to re-cross the R Line due to fuel shortage, the German player 'saves' one bombed city at the end of the game." This can change who wins the Basic Game.

### Numbers checked
- Basic Game win threshold: 12+ cities bombs → American wins (source 4.3: confirmed)
- Basic Game draw threshold: 9-11 cities (source 4.2: confirmed)
- Tournament Game: 10 quarters (source 18.1: confirmed)
- Advanced Game: 241+ = American Strategic Victory (source 26.2: confirmed)
- Advanced Game points: 1943 = 3 pts/target, 1944 = 2 pts, 1945 = 1 pt (source 26.1: confirmed, not in summary)

## luxor

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — extracted source present and is the Luxor rulebook.

### Finding 1 — Invented "adjacent" rule for treasure collection

**Summary (Actions → Collecting Treasures):** "To collect a treasure tile, you need the required number of adventurers on that tile: 1 adventurer: collect matching treasures with 1 required; 2+ adventurers: collect treasures requiring multiple adventurers. Treasure tile: Collect it if you have enough adventurers on that tile (or adjacent)."

The phrase "or adjacent" is invented. The source never mentions adjacency for treasure collection.

**Source (p. 5 / line 277-278):** "The player checks if they have at least as many of their own adventurers on this tile as the number of depicted adventurer icons." The word "adjacent" does not appear anywhere in the source in connection with treasure collection.

This is a MAJOR error: a player following the summary would believe they can collect a treasure tile using adventurers from a neighboring tile, which the rules never permit.

### Finding 2 — Horus tile action omits key option

**Summary (Actions → Step 2 Perform an Action):** "**Horus tile:** Take a Horus card of the matching level."

**Source (lines 249-254):** "Horus tile: As long as any are available, the player takes either • a key from the general supply or • the top card from that stack on the Horus board showing the same number of eye icons as shown on the tile."

The summary omits the key option entirely. A player following the summary would never learn they can take a key from a Horus tile — this affects strategy and game play. Verdict contribution: MAJOR (combined with Finding 1).

### Finding 3 — Tiebreaker missing from victory conditions

**Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions):** "Highest VP wins." No tiebreaker stated.

**Source (lines 412-413):** "In case of a tie for the most VP's, the tied player owning the more valuable sarcophagus is the winner. If none of them owns a sarcophagus, these players enjoy a shared victory."

The summary omits the tiebreaker entirely. This is MINOR on its own (edge case), but the game ends with exactly 2 sarcophagi in play (5 VP and 3 VP), so ties would be broken by sarcophagus ownership in a non-trivial fraction of games. Categorized as MINOR relative to Finding 1 and 2 which are MAJOR.

### Procedural claims verified (minimum quota)

1. **Card play rule:** Summary: "Choose your leftmost or rightmost hand card." Source (line 127): "A player may play the leftmost or rightmost card of their hand only." ✓ Supported.
2. **Draw card placement:** Summary: "Draw from the draw pile and insert it in the middle of your remaining 4 cards." Source (line 131): "When drawing a new card to their hand, a player must sort it in the middle of their 4 remaining hand cards." ✓ Supported.
3. **Discard reshuffle:** Summary: "If draw pile is empty, shuffle the discard pile." Source (lines 132-133): "When the draw pile is exhausted, shuffle the discard pile and use it as new draw pile." ✓ Supported.
4. **Anubis waking:** Summary: "When an upright adventurer passes an Anubis statue, the lying-down adventurer there stands up." Source (lines 200-206): "The first time a player's adventurer passes an Anubis statue, they place their adventurer lying down at that statue onto the stairs at the start in upright position." The summary omits "onto the stairs at the start" — woken adventurer returns to start stairs, not to any in-game position. MINOR omission.
5. **Horus card — no draw:** Summary: "Exception: If the player had taken a Horus card during their turn, they do not draw a new card." Source (lines 302-305): "Exception: If the player had taken a Horus card during their turn, they do not draw a new card now." ✓ Supported.
6. **Game end trigger:** Summary: "The instant two adventurers (any players') enter the chamber tomb, finish the current round." Source (lines 158-160): "The game ends after the first two adventurers have entered the chamber tomb with the aid of keys. The game round is played completely." ✓ Supported.
7. **Sarcophagi VP:** Summary: "VP from claimed sarcophagi." Source (lines 326-328): "The first adventurer to enter the chamber tomb claims the sarcophagus worth 5 VP's, the second adventurer to get there takes the second one worth 3 VP's." ✓ Supported (specific values in source, summary omits exact values but doesn't contradict).
8. **Keys VP:** Summary: "VP per unused key." Source (line 333): "Each key still owned by a player is worth 1 VP." ✓ Supported.

### Victory/scoring terms verified

**"Treasure sets"** — Summary: "Treasure sets: Escalating VP for sets of different treasure types." Source (lines 338-342): "A set consists of one tile each of the three different treasure types: Vase, statue, and jewelry. A wild card treasure tile may be used as substitute for any type, but a set may not contain more than 2 wild card treasure tiles." ✓ Supported. (Specific VP table is partially obscured in OCR but confirmed as escalating from source description.)

**Exhaustion check:** Source confirms discard pile is reshuffled when draw pile is empty (lines 132-133). Summary states this correctly. No invented exhaustion rule.
