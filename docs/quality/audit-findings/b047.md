### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Game-end conditions effectively missing.** Source: "Play ends and the Ritual Spell is cast when one of two things happens: 1. The Bell, Book and Candle have all be[en] 'locked', OR 2. The Large Black Pawn reaches the space marked 'Ritual Spell'... Move any unlocked disks to the close[st] locked position." Summary says only "The game ends based on the Ritual Spell Countdown mechanism" — the all-three-locked trigger is absent, and the countdown itself is never explained (see #2). A table could not end the game from this summary.
2. **MAJOR — Keep/Tower spells invented; actual new spells omitted.** Summary: Keep = "More powerful versions of Wall spells", Tower = "Contains the most powerful spells and opportunities." Source instead introduces two specific new spells in the inner areas — "+1 COMBAT Automatic: Add 1 to your Combat value" and "COUNT DOWN Optional – no die roll: Move the Large Black Pawn down one space" — and notes the Keep has "no 'Learn Ritual' or 'Learn Country' spells on this level." The Count Down spell drives end-condition #2 and is entirely missing; "more powerful versions" has no basis in the source.
3. **MAJOR — Co-winner rule omitted.** Source final scoring: "The player with the most positive points and all players who have more than half the high score are winners." Summary: "Players with the highest point totals win" (only the intro-page phrasing; the shared-win rule changes who wins).
4. **Major omission — Talk Chips mechanic.** Each player gets exactly one talk chip; playing it at the start of your turn triggers a "time out" where any players may leave the table to negotiate privately — the game's only sanctioned private-negotiation mechanism. The summary lists talk chips as a component but never explains them.
5. **Omission — disc locking and start-space rules.** "If moving the disk will put it back in the start space, move it beyond to the first space on the other side... If a disk moves all the way to one end, it is 'locked' and cannot be moved again." Absent from the summary (and locking feeds end condition #1).
6. **Omission — inner-area movement rules.** Tower barrier drops only "When all three of the Ritual Indicator disks have been moved" (summary just says "accessible via Keep"); entering the Keep is your movement for the turn into an unoccupied space; "Once you move into the Keep, you cannot move back to the Castle Wall"; "Once in the Wizard's Tower, you must remain there until the end of the game." All omitted.
7. **Omission — rampage edge cases.** "The Monster is controlled by an item that nobody owns? The Monster rampages!" and "If a player controlling an item dies, the item is lost and the country has no ruler." Omitted.
8. Trivial: starting spaces must be *unoccupied* "+1 Spell" spaces and give no credit; frontmatter designer "Mark Acres" does not appear in the source credits (RiddleMaster Games Design Group) — metadata, not a rules issue.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — invented characterization of Keep/Tower spells, missing game-end triggers (including the never-explained Count Down spell), and a missing co-winner rule would mislead players; component numbers themselves are accurate.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Wall/tower damage to monsters omitted (contradiction by omission).** Summary: "When a monster moves into a wall, the wall is destroyed and the monster stays in that space. Source: "The Wall is then removed from the board, and the Monster that attacked the Wall takes 1 point of damage" and "If a Monster moves into the same space in the Castle ring as a Tower, the Tower is removed and the Monster takes 1 point of damage." This is how Goblins die to walls and how Trolls are worn down — a core mechanic that materially changes difficulty. The multi-monster rule (only 1 of the simultaneous attackers takes the damage, players' choice) is also missing.
2. **MAJOR — Wrong hand sizes.** Summary's chart says 1 player = 7 (source solitaire: "draws up to a hand of 6 cards"); 5p=4 and 6p=3 are also almost certainly wrong (published chart: 5p=5, 6p=4) though the chart graphic didn't extract. Hand size drives the whole card economy.

4. **Minor — invented boss example.** Summary: bosses have "special effects that trigger when drawn (e.g., extra movement, destroying walls)." No boss destroys walls (Goblin King draws 3 tokens; Orc Warlord and Troll Mage move monsters; Healer heals 1). "Extra movement" is supported; "destroying walls" is not.
5. **Minor omission — Castle-ring targeting restriction.** "Once a Monster is in the Castle ring, it can only be affected by Castle cards that have the symbol on them" (Barbarian, Tar, Drive Him Back!). Largely implied by the summary's ring-matching rule, but never stated.
6. Trivial: Tar described as "skips one movement phase" vs. source's fuller "does not move during the Move Monsters phase nor during the Draw 2 new Monsters phase... removed on the next player's turn"; optional rules and Overlord turn detail compressed (variant existence is correctly noted).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — monsters taking 1 damage when destroying walls/towers is missing, the hand-size chart contains wrong numbers (1p contradicted by the source), and the Giant Boulder token is omitted.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Base-Catan costs imported over the variant's actual costs.** The variant's resource for pasture is **cattle**, not wool, and its cost table differs from base Catan:
   - Oxcart (road): source "1 brick and 1 cattle" — summary gives "1 brick + 1 papyrus" (a transplant of base Catan's brick+lumber with papyrus assumed to be the wood-equivalent).
   - Development card: source "1 stone, 1 grain, and 1 papyrus" — summary gives "1 stone + 1 grain + 1 wool" (base Catan's ore+grain+wool).
   - Settlement: source "1 brick, 1 cattle, 1 grain, and 1 papyrus" — summary "1 brick + 1 papyrus + 1 grain + 1 wool" (right only if "wool" is read as cattle).
   Players following the summary would build roads and buy development cards with the wrong resources every time.
2. **Minor — special-card names drifted to base Catan.** Source: "Longest Trade Route" and "Largest Mercenary Army" (mercenary cards, not knights). Summary uses "Longest Road"/"Largest Army (3+ knight cards)". Thresholds and VP values are correct.
3. **Minor omission — oases.** Foreign trade is improved at harbors **and oases** (3:1 oasis, special 2:1 oasis); the summary mentions harbors only.
4. **Omission — Great Pyramid scenario substance.** The scenario occupies roughly half the rulebook with materially different rules: papyrus boats (2 papyrus + 1 cattle) required to cross the Nile, pyramid blocks (1 stone + 1 cattle, only after building a boat across the Nile), Pharaoh's Curse/-Blessing (start at 1 VP), Vizier's Favor card, variable robber threshold (gold block 6-9 instead of the fixed 7), and different end conditions ("A player reaches **11** victory points" OR all 12 gold blocks used / pyramid finished → most VP wins, with pyramid-block tiebreakers). The summary reduces this to one line and its global "Win condition: First to 10 VP" is wrong for the scenario. The one-line framing ("cooperative-competitive... collectively build a Great Pyramid") is at least flagged as a scenario, mitigating severity.
5. Trivial: starting resources described as "hexes adjacent to your second settlement" — the beginner setup says the star-marked settlement (the second-settlement rule is the Great Pyramid scenario's setup); god cards ("Help from the Gods", 10 cards + 4 overview cards, A/B sides) summarized only generically, consistent with the source's "optional for base game" framing.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — two building costs are flat-out wrong (base-Catan imports) and the variant's cattle resource is misnamed wool throughout; these are exactly the at-the-table errors the variant rulebook exists to define.

## catan-event-cards — MAJOR

(Source PDF is the two-player **Catan Card Game** rulebook; the summary describes that game.)

### Numbers checked (11/12 supported; 1 contradicted)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 120 cards total | "120 cards" | Yes |
| 2 | 9 red / 9 blue starting cards | "9 Red Shield Cards... 9 Blue Shield Cards" | Yes |
| 3 | 11 region, 7 road, 5 settlement, 7 city cards | "11 Region Cards... 7 Road Cards... 5 Settlement Cards... 7 City Cards" | Yes |
| 4 | 10 event cards | "10 Event Cards" | Yes |
| 5 | 62 expansion cards (yellow/green/red text fields) | "62 Expansion Cards (include all cards with yellow, green, and red text fields)" | Yes |
| 6 | 1 event die, 1 production die, 1 windmill token, 1 knight token | component list matches | Yes |
| 7 | Expansion cards divided into 5 roughly equal stacks | "Divide this deck into five roughly equal 'expansion stacks'" | Yes |
| 8 | Settlement cost: 1 each brick, lumber, grain, wool; 1 VP | "A new settlement costs four resources: one each of wool, lumber, brick, and grain"; "worth 1 Victory Point" | Yes |
| 9 | City cost: 3 ore + 2 grain; 2 VP | "A city costs five resources: 3 ore and 2 grain... worth two Victory Points" | Yes |
| 10 | Settlement holds 2 expansion cards; city holds 4 | "Each settlement has two building sites... Each city has four building sites" | Yes |
| 11 | First to 12 VP wins (lower total by agreement for beginners) | "The first player to reach 12 Victory Points is the winner"; "New players may want to play to a lower Victory Point total" | Yes |
| 12 | Road cost: 1 brick + 1 lumber | **Contradicted.** "It costs three resources to build a road: 2 brick and 1 lumber" | **No** |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Road cost wrong (base-Catan import).** Summary: "1 brick + 1 lumber per road." Source: "It costs three resources to build a road: **2 brick** and 1 lumber." The classic board-game road cost was transplanted over the card game's cost.
2. **MAJOR — Windmill Token rule invented.** Summary: "Goes to the player with the most grain production (commerce bonus)." Source: awarded for the "trade advantage" — "you must have at least one city in your principality" AND "more Commerce Points than your opponent" (windmill icons on Buildings). Nothing to do with grain production; the city requirement is also load-bearing ("if you have more Commerce Points but no city, you cannot claim the token!").
3. **MAJOR — Knight Token effect wrong.** Summary: "The knight token provides extra strength and VP." Source: the token is worth exactly 1 VP and is awarded to the player with the higher total Strength Points; it confers no strength itself. (The summary also conflates strength with "tournament bonus" — Tournament Points, the red number, are a separate stat used only for the Tournament event.)
4. **MAJOR — Core Draw/hand-limit system omitted.** The source turn has 5 steps; the summary gives 2 ("Roll both dice", "Actions"). Missing entirely: step 4 Draw — refill to your hand limit (3 cards, increased by Abbey/Library) by free random draw from any stack **or** paying any 2 resources to search a stack without reordering it; the starting draft of 3 expansion cards from a chosen stack; and the rule that Action Cards cannot be played "until the total score of both players combined is at least seven Victory Points" (Scout excepted). The summary's "Draw expansion cards: Pay gold or resources to draw" misstates this (random draws are free; searching costs 2 resources, not gold).
5. **Error — improved trade rates misattributed.** Summary: "3:1 with the supply, or use harbor/counting house for better rates." Source: 3:1 base rate is correct, but better rates come from **Trade Fleets** ("Each Trade Fleet allows you to trade two of the pictured resource for one other") and the **Mint** (1 gold : 1 resource); the Harbor instead grants +1 Commerce Point per Trade Fleet.
6. **Error/invented — resource hand limit.** Summary: "Players can only hold a limited number of resources; excess must be discarded." Source: the hand limit applies to cards; resources are capped at 3 per Region Card (overflow is simply lost), and the Brigand Attack event makes a player with more than 7 total resources "forfeit all the ore and wool" (not discard excess). The summary's statement matches base Catan's 7-card discard, not this game.
7. Minor omissions: starting position includes 1 of each resource (region cards turned to the 1 side); brigands ignored on the first two turns; Garrison protection; Plague/city counter-cards; "no two roads adjacent" placement rule; event-die specifics compressed (the five events are otherwise correctly named in spirit).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong road cost, invented Windmill criterion, wrong Knight Token effect, and the omitted draw/hand-limit system (plus the 7-VP Action Card gate) would mislead at the table; component counts and settlement/city costs are accurate.
