## battlelore — MAJOR

Source is the full BattleLore Player's Guide (Days of Wonder, 2006). The extracted text has no component manifest page, so some component counts (60 Command cards, 42 terrain tiles, "over 100" miniatures) cannot be verified from the source; they are not flagged. Everything flagged below is directly supported or contradicted by the extracted text.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented battle dice symbols; the hit system is wrong.** Summary's Player Reference dice table: "Sword (green): Hit on infantry / Helmet (blue): Hit on heavy/special / Horse (red): Hit on cavalry / Bow: Hit on archers (ranged)", and Turn Structure: "Matching troop symbol (sword, bow, horse): Hit if it matches the target's troop type." The source has no sword/horse/bow dice symbols at all. Actual die faces are three **colored helmets**, Sword-on-Shield, Flag, and Lore: "the attacker scores one Hit for each colored helmet rolled that matches the banner color of the unit targeted. Other colors rolled are always a Miss." Hits depend on the target's banner color (Green/Blue/Red = training level), not on infantry/cavalry/archer type. A player using the summary's table would resolve every combat in the game incorrectly.
2. **MAJOR — Movement allowances wrong.** Summary table: Infantry (Green) 1 hex; Archers 1 hex; Cavalry (Red) 2 hexes. Source: movement is by banner color, not unit role — Green foot "up to 2 hexes and battle"; Blue foot "1 hex and battle or 2 hexes and not battle"; Red foot "1 hex and battle"; mounted Green 4 / Blue 3 / Red 2 hexes, all may battle. The summary halves Green infantry/archer movement, omits the Blue-foot option entirely, and presents 2 hexes as the speed of all cavalry when Blue Regular Cavalry moves 3 and Green mounted moves 4.
3. **MAJOR — Bonus Strike misstated.** Summary: "Bonus Strike: Hit on any unit" (stated in both dice tables). Source: the Sword-on-Shield symbol "Provides a Bonus Strike, when specified on the attacking unit's Weapon card. Otherwise, it is a miss" — e.g., the Common Bow "hits exclusively on helmets" (never on Sword-on-Shield), the Short Sword's Sword-on-Shield hits are ignored once by mounted targets, and the Crossbow ignores them at point blank. "Hit on any unit" is only true of the Long Sword.
4. **MAJOR — Creature Critical Hits inverted.** Summary: "Critical Hits: Some attacks deal extra damage." Source: Critical Hits are the *only* way to kill a Creature — "Creatures are immune from the wear and tear of normal combat. Instead, they are eliminated only when they receive a Critical Hit" (hits scored against a creature are set aside and re-rolled; only a second banner-color match kills it). The summary turns a defensive immunity mechanic into a generic bonus-damage mechanic.
5. **Minor — "Bold" misdefined.** Summary: "Bold: Some units may attack before the defender's normal response." Source: "A unit that has the capability to ignore a flag rolled against it in battle is a Bold unit" — Bold is about ignoring retreat flags (and thereby qualifying to battle back), not striking first.
6. **Minor — Battle Back condition omitted.** Summary: "Surviving units that were attacked in melee may immediately attack back." Source: "any defending unit that may ignore a flag (i.e. any unit whose morale is Bold)... is entitled to Battle back" and it must not have vacated its hex. The summary implies all survivors battle back; only Bold/supported units do.
7. **Minor — Lore token storage contradicted.** Summary (War Council): "Lore Masters determine... how many Lore tokens can be stored." Source: "Unlike Lore cards, there is no limit to the number of Lore tokens a player may have at a given time." Lore Master level caps Lore *cards* in hand (level + 1), not tokens.
8. **Minor — Terrain table drift.** Summary: "Hills: Combat bonus" — source Elevated Terrain imposes dice *caps* (max 3 attacking from a hill, 2 attacking up a hill), no bonus. "River... may prevent battle after crossing" — source: "A unit may enter a Ford hex and still battle" (max 2 dice). Forest/Waterways/Landmark rows are broadly supported.
9. **Minor — Omissions.** Base battle dice per troop type (Green 2 / Blue 3 / Red 4 dice) never stated despite "roll battle dice equal to the unit's combat strength"; archers' -1 die when firing after moving; Common Bow range 4 / Crossbow range 3; unit sizes (Foot 4 figures, Mounted 3, Creature 1); Goblin Rush; Support (adjacent to 2 friendlies = Bold). None of these appear in the summary.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the dice/hit system and movement allowances (the two most-used rules in the game) are wrong, Bonus Strike and Creature Critical Hits are misstated, and two checked numbers (24 vs 60 Lore cards; 4-6 vs 4-7 banners) fail.

## battles-of-the-third-age — MAJOR

Note on source coverage: the extracted text is the **Twilight of the Third Age** rulebook only (the strategic-expansion half). The "Battles of the War of the Ring" tactical rulebook is not in the extracted text, so summary claims that are purely about the tactical mode (e.g., scenario victory conditions) were not flagged unless contradicted. Component counts and all strategic rules below are fully covered by the source.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Siege Engines wrongly excluded from the strategic game.** Summary (Special Rules): "Siege Engine Rules: Apply only in the Battles of the War of the Ring tactical mode, not in the standard strategic game"; (Actions): "Towers and Trebuchets add new tactical options during sieges in the Battles of the War of the Ring mode." The source's Section 4 "SIEGE ENGINES" is part of the Twilight of the Third Age strategic rules: the 6 Towers and 6 Trebuchets are listed among the Twilight components ("The remaining components are used only with the Battles of the War of the Ring rules" — i.e., everything *not* in that list), and the rules are strategic-game rules: "The Shadow player may use a Muster die to place a Siege Tower in a region containing a Shadow Army besieging a Stronghold"; Free Peoples Trebuchets are placed with a Muster die in a controlled Stronghold. The summary tells players to skip an entire strategic-game subsystem.
2. **MAJOR — Galadriel's entry mechanic wrong.** Summary: "Galadriel: Enters play through Event cards." Source: "If Sauron or the Elves are 'At War,' and Gandalf the White is not in play, use a **Muster die** to play Lady Galadriel in Lórien." No Event card involved.
3. **MAJOR — The Balrog's entry mechanic wrong.** Summary: "The Balrog: Activated by the Shadow player through Event cards." Source: "If the Witch-king is not in play, play the Balrog and one Sauron unit (Regular or Elite) in Moria using a **Muster die**" (also advancing the Elf, Dwarf, and North nations on the Political Track — omitted). Also note the Balrog is Level 0 and per the Appendix "If a Character's Level is 0, he cannot move even when attached to an Army," so "can emerge from Moria" is doubly misleading.
4. **MAJOR — Faction entry mechanic wrong.** Summary: "New factions (Hillmen, Corsairs, Ents) are activated through specific Event cards." Source: all three factions enter play with a **Muster die** under conditions (Hillmen: Isengard "At War"; Corsairs: Southrons & Easterlings "At War"; Ents: Saruman in play + Companion in Fangorn). Only the Ents' subsequent movement/attacks use Event cards (the three Ents Awake cards).
5. **MAJOR — Invented Smeagol "taming" mechanic.** Summary: "Joins the Fellowship when Frodo and Sam separate. The Free Peoples player can attempt to 'tame' Gollum. If tamed, Smeagol becomes a companion; if untamed, he may betray the Ring-bearers during Hunt resolution." None of this is in the source. Actual rule: "When you draw a Sméagol tile from the Hunt Pool, the Hunt Damage is 0 and Sméagol enters play as a special Companion always serving as the Guide of the Fellowship." There is no taming attempt, no betrayal-during-Hunt mechanic, and the expansion's premise is the opposite of "joins when Frodo and Sam separate" (it explores him being tamed *before* the breaking of the Fellowship). The real edge rules (if eliminated, the "We Shall Get It" Shadow card enters play; if he's the last Companion, replace with Gollum) are omitted.
6. **Minor — Smeagol Hunt Tiles description partly invented.** Summary: "They may cause Smeagol to reveal his treacherous nature or provide the Free Peoples player with useful guidance." Source: a drawn Sméagol tile has Hunt damage 0 and either brings Sméagol into play or (once he is guiding) is placed back in the Hunt Pool; (after he leaves the game, discard and redraw). No treachery effect on the tiles.
7. **Minor — Witch-King characterization unsupported.** Summary: "enhanced combat abilities and the power to lead Nazgul more effectively." Source: his abilities are Hunt-focused — each Nazgûl in the region with the Fellowship's last known position "grants one Hunt re-roll," and he may move to the Fellowship's declared region. Nothing about enhanced combat; Leadership is 2 (same stat category as Galadriel).
8. **Minor — Omissions.** Siege Engine Superiority (the core siege-engine mechanic: -1/+1 Combat roll modifiers in round 1, extending/preventing battle extension, the tie/claim rule, max 2 of each per region) is entirely absent; Galadriel's actual abilities (cancel grey Eye tiles with an Elven Ring; recruit in besieged Lórien; cannot leave Lórien); Dunlendings' "one hit eliminates two Dunlendings"; Corsair Ship movement/transport/attack rules; Ents' five-dice 5+ attack and Entwood chain rules; the Appendix's revised War of the Ring rules.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — all 14 checked numbers are correct, but the entry mechanics for both new characters and all three factions are wrong (Event cards vs. Muster dice), the Smeagol system is largely invented, and the siege-engine subsystem is wrongly declared inapplicable to the strategic game with its actual rules omitted.
