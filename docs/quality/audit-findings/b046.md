| Roll 3 dice to replenish | l.157-159 | Y |
| Discs reset every 5th round | l.86-88 | Y |
| Win at 10 castles placed (2p) | "game ends when a player has 10 castles in play" (l.309) | Y |

**Finding 1 (MAJOR — invented game-end condition + omitted real one):**
Summary (Scoring): "If the game ends with all discs played (after every 5th round, discs reset), the player with fewer remaining castles wins."
Source (l.308-317): "The game ends when a player has 10 castles in play in the territories. That player wins! The game also ends when there are fewer than 4 territories... The winner is the player who has the most castles in play in the territories at that time."
The disc-exhaustion ending is invented — discs simply reset every 5 rounds and never end the game. The real second end condition (fewer than 4 territories remain on the table due to merging) is entirely missing. A player using the summary would never end a game that stalls before 10 castles, or would end it at the wrong time.

**Finding 2 (minor — omitted bidding constraint):** Source l.76-77: "The numbers on the two discs must be different" (discs are played sequentially in turn order). The summary implies simultaneous play and omits the must-differ rule, keeping only the last-disc tie exception.

**Finding 3 (minor — merging trigger loosely stated):** Summary: "Whenever two adjacent territories are both controlled by the same player, they merge." Source (l.214-221): merging triggers when a player *builds a castle* in a territory adjacent to territories containing his castles; a newly merged territory is not re-checked for majority. Paladin-majority alone does not merge territories. Also omitted: a merged territory counts as one step for Emperor movement (l.231-233).

**Finding 4 (minor — 3p/4p compression):** Summary calls the gray discs "neutral" for 3 players; source: gray is simply the third player's set (l.326-329). 3p specifics (8 castles to win, 9-paladin reserve, play 4 paladins, relative majority — l.326-338) are summarized only as "modified rules"; 4p shared-castle partnership is correct (l.349-350).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong/invented game-end condition with the real alternate ending omitted.

Summary: "Mines earn $3 per adjacent mountain you own."
Source (l.364-368): "Income: $3 per neighboring mountain. Several mines may benefit from the same mountain. Once you own a mountain, it no longer benefits other players' mines." Unowned mountains do count; ownership only blocks *other* players. **Finding 2 (minor — omitted house-equivalence for income):** Source: a ranch "is considered a house to calculate drugstore, bank and saloon incomes" (l.352-353, drugstore example counts the ranch twice, l.359-362) and a church "is considered a house" for the same incomes (l.393-394).
