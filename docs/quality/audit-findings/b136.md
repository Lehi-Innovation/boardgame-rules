## mage-wars-arena

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Wrong component count: spell cards

Summary: "322 spell cards"
Source (components list): "336 Spell Cards" (repeated three times: components list line 238, line 267, and "336 SPELL CARDS" header at line 306)
→ Wrong number by 14 cards.

### Finding 2 — Wrong component count and type: attack dice

Summary: "2 attack dice (custom d12)"
Source (components list): "9 Attack Dice" + "1 Effect Die (d12)" — the attack dice are separate custom dice from the d12 Effect Die.
→ Both the count (2 vs. 9) and the type (described as d12, but only the Effect Die is a d12; attack dice have blank/normal/critical faces) are wrong.

### Finding 3 — Invented turn structure: "First Stage" and "Second Stage"

Summary: "Action Phase (two stages per round) — First Stage: Each player's Mage and creatures may take a full action or quick action. Second Stage: Repeat, allowing each creature that hasn't acted to take its action."
Source: "The Action Stage is divided into three parts" — First Quickcast Phase, Creature Action Phases (alternating one creature per player until all have acted), Final Quickcast Phase. There is no "First Stage" / "Second Stage" division.
→ The summary invents a two-stage sub-structure that does not exist.

### Finding 4 — Major omission: Ready Stage (5 of 6 phases missing)

Summary describes only "Initiative Phase" and "Action Phase (two stages)." It completely omits 5 of the 6 Ready Stage phases:
- Phase 2: RESET (reset all action markers, quickcast markers, ready markers)
- Phase 3: CHANNEL (each Mage gains mana equal to channeling value)
- Phase 4: UPKEEP (pay upkeep costs or destroy spells; conditions like Burn/Rot resolve)
- Phase 5: PLANNING ("Each Mage prepares for the round by choosing up to 2 spells from his spellbook. Both players simultaneously choose their spells.")
- Phase 6: DEPLOYMENT (Spawnpoints cast their prepared spells)

Source: "The Ready Stage is divided into 6 phases. Both players must complete each phase before the next one can begin."
→ Without the Planning Phase, players cannot know when to select spells. Without Upkeep, they cannot run the economy. These are core phases; their omission makes the summary unplayable.

### Finding 5 — Initiative Phase misrepresented

Summary: "The player with initiative chooses who acts first this round."
Source: "the player with the initiative marker must give it to his opponent. In this manner the initiative marker alternates back and forth between players. The player with the initiative acts first during the Action Stage."
→ The player with initiative does NOT choose who goes first; they automatically go first and pass the marker to the opponent. There is no choice.

### Finding 6 — Flying rule: invented "hovering" exception

Summary: "Flying creatures can only be attacked by ranged attacks or other flyers unless they are 'hovering.'"
Source: "Non-Flying creatures cannot make a melee attack against a Flying creature unless they have the Reach trait." (No mention of "hovering" anywhere in the extracted text — searched: hover, hovering, grounded.)
→ The source says the exception is the Reach trait; the summary invents a "hovering" state instead. No support found (searched: hover, Hover, hovering).

### Finding 7 — Guard description: incorrect trigger (movement)

Summary: "A creature may guard, gaining a free counterattack against enemies that move or attack in their zone."
Source: "While a creature is guarding, all of its quick action melee attacks gain the Counterstrike trait." (Counterstrike is only triggered when the creature is the target of a melee attack: "If the attacker made a melee attack on the defender, and the defender has a quick melee attack with the Counterstrike trait, the defender may choose to counterstrike.")
→ The guard does NOT trigger on enemy movement into the zone. Only melee attacks trigger Counterstrike.

### Finding 8 — Spellbook point total not verified as "120"

Summary: "Each player selects a Mage and builds a 120-point spellbook before the game."
Source: "You may not spend more Spell Points than the Maximum Spell Point total listed on your Ability Card." The actual maximum is defined per-Mage on their Ability Card; no universal value of 120 appears in the extracted rulebook text. No support found (searched: 120, "spell point total", "maximum spell").
→ Cannot be verified from source; likely a specific mage's value used as a universal. UNCERTAIN — not counted as a separate MAJOR finding since the primary issues above are already clear.

### Minimum Verification Quota

**Victory/game-end:** "Destroy the opposing Mage. When a Mage's damage equals or exceeds their life total, they are defeated." Source: "Mage Wars is played over a series of rounds, which continue until one Mage takes enough damage to be destroyed. The last Mage standing wins the game!" — consistent in substance.

**Procedural claims verified:**
1. Initiative alternates each round — WRONG in summary (see Finding 5)
2. Each creature gets one Action Phase — supported: "each creature gets to act one time during each Action Stage"
3. Mages start in corner zones — supported: "Place your Mage Card in the corner zone closest to you"
4. Upkeep pays for spell maintenance — supported: "Some spells have an upkeep cost. This is a mana cost which must be paid each Upkeep Phase, or the spell is destroyed." OMITTED from summary.
5. Planning phase selects up to 2 spells — supported but entirely OMITTED from summary.

**Numbers checked:** spell cards (322 vs 336 — WRONG), attack dice count (2 vs 9 — WRONG), effect die (1 d12 — matches summary's d12 claim but the attack dice are not d12s).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 3, SUPPORTED: 1

## magic-maze

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Vortex action description is wrong

Summary: "Vortex: Teleport a hero to any space occupied by another hero of matching color portal"
Source: "The player with the Use a Vortex action (and only this player) can move any Hero pawn from wherever it is to any Vortex space of its colour."
Also (rules reminder section): "The Use a Vortex action allows you to move the Hero pawns from anywhere to any Vortex space of that hero's colour."
→ The summary says the Vortex moves a hero to a space *occupied by another hero*. The source says it moves to any *Vortex space* of its colour (a special designated tile space). These are completely different — a Vortex space need not have another hero on it.

### Finding 2 — Communication rule: wrong timing and missing restriction

Summary: "When the timer is flipped, players may briefly discuss strategy before the timer starts again."
Source: "Each time the sand timer is flipped over, the players are allowed to speak for as long as they wish while the sand continues to flow. No actions can be performed during this discussion time. As soon as any player performs any action, all communication must cease again!"
→ Two errors:
  (a) Summary says communication happens "before the timer starts again" (implying the timer is paused or has not yet started). Source says it happens "while the sand continues to flow" — the timer is running during discussion.
  (b) Summary says "briefly" — source says "as long as they wish."
  (c) Summary omits the critical rule: "No actions can be performed during this discussion time."

### Finding 3 — Communication rule: gesturing and signaling always forbidden

Summary: "Players may NOT communicate (no talking, gesturing, or signaling) except during sand timer pauses."
Source: "During most of the game, you cannot communicate. You are not allowed to speak, point at something, make signs or signals, or make sounds. The only permitted forms of communication allowed are as follows: Staring intensely at another player. Taking the 'Do Something!' pawn..."
"Each time the sand timer is flipped over, the players are allowed to speak for as long as they wish while the sand continues to flow."
→ The exception ("speak for as long as they wish") refers specifically to *speaking*, not all communication. Staring and "Do Something!" pawn are always allowed. The source does NOT say gesturing/signaling are permitted during sand timer flips — only speaking is specifically permitted.

### Minimum Verification Quota

**Victory/game-end:** "All 4 hero pawns exit the mall before the sand timer runs out." Source: "All of the players win the game if all of the Hero pawns succeed in leaving the shopping mall in the limited time allotted for the game, each having stolen an item." — consistent. Lose condition: "if the sand timer ever completely runs out before the Hero pawns escape, all of the players lose the game" — summary says "The sand timer runs out at any point during the game" — consistent.

**Theft condition:** "All 4 heroes must be on their Item spaces simultaneously to trigger the theft." Source: "When all four Hero pawns are simultaneously on their respective Item spaces, they steal the items." — consistent.

**Sand timer spaces:** "Each sand timer space can only be used once." Source: "Each Sand Timer space can only be used once." — consistent.

**Exit rule:** "A hero can only use an exit matching their color." Source (Scenario 2): "Each Hero pawn must escape through the exit of its own colour." — consistent.

**Escalator:** "Move a hero via an escalator connection." Source: "The player with the Take the Escalator action (and only this player) can move a Hero pawn from one side of an escalator to the other." — consistent.

**Procedural claims verified:**
1. Vortex disabled after theft — supported: "Once the theft has occurred, the Vortex system will be automatically shut down" — consistent.
2. Vortex action description — WRONG (see Finding 1).
3. Communication timing — WRONG (see Finding 2).
4. No turns, real-time — supported: "In this game, there are no 'turns': you act whenever you notice that one of your actions may be useful."
5. Heroes can't share spaces — supported: "There can never be two Hero pawns on the same space."
