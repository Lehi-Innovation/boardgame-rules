## heroes-of-normandie

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Suppression mechanics completely wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "Suppressed units (marked with token) cannot fire or move. Tokens removed during Supply Phase."

Source (lines 1802–1805): "Each marker imposes a −2 penalty to ALL of a unit's die rolls. Each marker also imposes a −2 penalty to a unit's movement value. If this value drops to 0, the unit is immobilized (not if suppressed during a movement)."

Suppressed units are NOT prevented from firing or moving — they suffer −2 penalties to rolls and movement. Immobilization only occurs if the movement penalty reduces a unit to 0 movement. The summary inverts the entire mechanic: players using the summary would never fire or move suppressed units, losing activations they should be taking (at a penalty).

### Finding 2 — Supply Phase: only ONE Suppressed marker removed per unit, not all (MAJOR)

Summary (Supply Phase): "Remove suppressed tokens." (implies all removed)

Source (line 694): "Discard one and only one Suppressed marker per unit."

Confirmed by the "Courageous" special ability (line 1102): "Courageous units can remove 2 Suppressed markers instead of one during the Supply Phase" — this only makes sense if the normal rule is exactly one.

A unit hit by Hull damage (which places TWO suppressed markers) would incorrectly have both removed under the summary, instead of just one per Supply Phase. This affects gameplay timing and how long suppression lasts.
