## junta

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — extracted text present and matches game.

### Finding 1 — Political Game Phase Order Is Wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure):**
> 1. Accept Development Aid → 2. Budget Allocation → 3. Vote on Budget → 4. Assign Offices → 5. Bank Deposits → 6. Assassination Attempts → 7. Return from Exile → 8. Coup d'État → 9. Coup Consequences

**Source (junta-rules.txt, line 437–453):**
> Phases of the Political Game. Each turn has the following phases:
> 1. Each player draws two Politics cards. The markers on the two rows of Phases are reset.
> 2. El Presidente assigns offices.
> 3. El Presidente draws eight banknotes from the Development Aid deck.
> 4. El Presidente proposes a budget, and the Parliament votes on its adoption.
> 5. Every player chooses a secret Location for this turn.
> 6. Assassination attempts are declared and resolved.
> 7. If the bank is open, money can be transferred or withdrawn.
> 8. A coup d'état can be proclaimed.
> 9. A coup creates Coup Consequences.

The summary's phase order is wrong in at least two major ways:
- Phase 1 of the source (draw Politics cards + reset) is **omitted entirely** from the summary.
- Phase 2 of the source (Assign Offices) is moved to **phase 4** of the summary, after Development Aid and Budget/Vote — but the source clearly does Assign Offices *before* Development Aid is drawn.
- Phase 5 of the source (Choose Location) is **omitted entirely**.
- "Return from Exile" is listed as a numbered phase (phase 7) in the summary, but it is **not a numbered phase** in the source; returning from exile is handled within the Location/Assassination section as a sub-rule.

Players following the summary's phase order would assign offices *after* the budget is proposed, which contradicts the source (offices are assigned first, then the Presidente draws aid and proposes a budget). This would cause incorrect gameplay.

### Finding 2 — Political Game Phase 1 (Draw Cards) Omitted

**Summary:** Does not mention players drawing 2 Politics cards at the start of each turn.

**Source (line 439, 455–463):** "1. Each player draws two Politics cards… At the beginning of a round, every player draws two Politics cards… Each player can have at most six cards in his hand."

The hand-management mechanic (drawing 2 cards per turn, 6-card limit) is entirely absent from the summary.

### Victory / Game-End Condition — SUPPORTED
**Summary:** "When El Presidente can no longer draw sufficient funds from the Development Aid deck, the game ends. The winner is the player with the most money in their Swiss bank account."

**Source (line 84–87):** "When El Presidente can no longer draw sufficient funds from the deck of Development Aid, the game ends. The winner is the player with the most money in his Swiss bank account at that point."

This is correct. ✓

### Numbers Checked
- Development Aid: 96 banknotes (source line 188–190) — not mentioned in summary (minor omission)
- 8 banknotes drawn per turn (source line 442–443) — not mentioned in summary (procedural omission, compounded by phase reordering)

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 2, SUPPORTED: 2

## junta-viva-el-presidente

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — extracted text present and matches game.

### Finding 1 — Victory Condition Entirely Wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions):**
> "The game continues for multiple rounds. The player who accumulates the most wealth through building their estate, collecting money, and outmaneuvering rivals wins. Two versions exist: Basic Game and Expert Game."

The summary says **"most wealth"** wins, with no game-end trigger described.

**Source (extracted, lines 425–432):**
> "As soon as at least 1 player has 5 or more Victory Points at the end of a Spend Money Phase, the game ends. Every building (including the Main House) counts as 1 Victory Point. In addition, the Yacht and Super Car each give 1 Victory Point to the player who has them in their hand. The player with the most Victory Points wins. In case of a tie, the tied player with the most money in hand wins."

The summary omits completely:
- The **5 VP** game-end trigger
- The **Victory Points** scoring system (buildings = 1 VP each, Main House = 1 VP, Yacht/Super Car = 1 VP each)
- The correct tiebreaker (most money **in hand**, not most wealth generally)

Expert Game end condition (source line 462): "The game ends when someone has 6 or more Victory Points." Also omitted.

A table using the summary cannot score a finished game or know when the game ends.

### Finding 2 — Hand Limit Omitted from Summary

**Summary:** "6. Check Number of Cards: Verify hand limits and discard as needed."

**Source (lines 415–420):** "In this Phase, you may possess at most 4 cards. If you have more than 4 cards in your hand, you must discard as many cards as it takes to get you down to 4."

The summary mentions "verify hand limits" but does not state the limit is 4. This is a minor omission but worth noting since it's uncheckable without the source.

### Finding 3 — Defense Value Formula Omitted

**Summary:** Does not describe how Defense Value is calculated.

**Source (lines 300–309):** "The defender then adds all the results of their rolls, adding to it, however, the number of buildings they have. The sum is the Defense Value. Every building (including the Main House) raises the Defense Value by 1. The Presidente may not roll his Militias! Instead, each presidential Militia is treated as if it had rolled a 1. So, his Defense Value equals the number of his buildings plus the number of his Militias."

This is a significant procedural omission but since the summary is a summary, it could be considered minor. Combined with the MAJOR victory condition error, the verdict is MAJOR.

### Procedural Claims Verified
1. Draw Cards phase: "Presidente draws cards equal to total players + 2; each Golpista draws 1 card clockwise" — Source (lines 135–140): "He takes as many cards from the Deck equal to the total number of players plus 2… Then the Golpistas each take 1 card, proceeding in a clockwise direction." ✓
2. First round exception: "In the first round, each Golpista draws 2 cards" — Source (line 140–141): "Exception: In the first round of the game, each Golpista takes 1 additional card, for a total of 2 cards." ✓
3. Militia defend Presidente: die set to 6 — Source (lines 186–191): "For that, turn the die or dice to the number 6. The number 6 always refers to the defense of the Presidente, independent of who happens to be Presidente at the moment." ✓
4. Attack on Presidente resolved first — Source (line 231–234): "If someone attacks the Presidente, that battle is always resolved first." ✓
5. If no one attacks Presidente, all players receive Promises immediately — Source (lines 242–245): "If no one attacks the Presidente, all the players immediately receive their Promises—before the battles between the Golpistas are carried out." ✓

### Numbers Checked
- 5 VP game-end trigger (Basic Game) — summary omits ❌
- 6 VP game-end trigger (Expert Game) — summary omits ❌
- 4-card hand limit — summary omits value ❌
- 4 million Pesos: 1 Building — source (line 397): "4 Million Pesos: 1 Building" ✓
- 2 million Pesos: 1 Militia — source (line 401): "2 Million Pesos: 1 Militia" ✓
- 1 million Pesos: 1 Card — source (line 404): "1 Million Pesos: 1 Card" ✓
- Max 4 Militias per player — source (line 403): "Each player may possess at most 4 Militias." ✓

## kalah

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — extracted text present. Source is a blog post/article titled "How To Play Mancala by In The Playroom" explicitly covering the Kalah variant.

### Finding 1 — Seeds Per Pit: Wrong Number (MAJOR)

**Summary:**
> "Place 4 seeds in each of the 12 small pits."
> Player Reference: "Seeds per pit (start): 4"
> Components: "48 seeds/stones (4 per pit in the standard version)"

**Source (lines 41–43):**
> "To begin, place an equal number of game pieces (usually six) into each of the small holes on both sides of the game board."

Source repeats this (lines 108–109): "To set up mancala, place an equal number of seeds (usually six) in each of the 12 small holes on the board, six on each player's side."

The source consistently states **6 seeds per pit** as the standard setup. The summary states **4 seeds per pit**. This directly contradicts the source. A game started with 4 seeds per pit instead of 6 would have 48 seeds in play rather than 72, fundamentally changing the game's dynamics.

### Victory / Game-End Conditions

**Summary:** "The game ends when a player has no seeds left in ANY of their 6 pits on their turn. The other player captures all remaining seeds on their side and places them in their store."

**Source (lines 71–75):** "The game of mancala ends when all the small pockets on one player's side are empty. At this point, the second player collects all the remaining seeds on their side and places them into their mancala store." ✓

**Winner:** Summary: "Count the seeds in each store. The player with the most seeds wins." Source: "The players then count the seeds in their respective stores. The player with the most seeds is declared the winner." ✓

### Procedural Claims Verified
1. Pick up all seeds from one pit, sow counterclockwise — Source (lines 49–54): "On your turn, choose one of your small holes with seeds in it and pick up all the seeds. You will now distribute these seeds, one by one, into the following holes in a counter-clockwise direction, including your own store but skipping your opponent's store." ✓
2. Extra turn if last seed lands in your store — Source (lines 54–55): "If the last seed you distribute lands in your own store, you earn a free turn and may continue playing." ✓
3. Capture: last seed in your empty pit → take opposite pit's seeds + that seed — Source (lines 56–59): "If the last seed you distribute lands in an empty hole on your own side, you capture all the seeds in the opposite hole on your opponent's side, as well as the last seed you just placed. Place all captured seeds into your own store." ✓
4. Skip opponent's store — Source (line 53): "skipping your opponent's store" ✓
5. Each player's store is on their right — Source (line 38–39): "Each player's store is on their right-hand side." (Summary says "store to their right" ✓)

### Numbers Checked
- Seeds per pit: summary says 4, source says 6 ❌
- 12 small pits (6 per side) ✓ (source line 36)
- 2 stores (1 per player) ✓ (source lines 37–39)

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4, SUPPORTED: 3
