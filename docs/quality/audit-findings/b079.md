### Finding 1 — Scoring values comprehensively scrambled (MAJOR)

The summary's four core scoring values are all wrong, and the adventurer/paladin values are swapped relative to each other, while the room and monster values are also incorrect.

**Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions):**
> "+1 per unconquered room"
> "+3 per monster remaining (not ghosts)"
> "+5 per imprisoned adventurer (eliminated during combat)"
> "+2 per imprisoned paladin (if applicable)"

**Source (extracted, lines 2169–2174):**
> "2 points for each unconquered room in your dungeon."
> "1 point for each monster in your Monster Lair. (They should all be face up again after Combat.) No points for ghosts."
> "2 points for each adventurer in your Prison, except for paladins who are each worth 5 points."

The summary has inverted the room (+2 → +1), monster (+1 → +3), adventurer (+2 → +5), and paladin (+5 → +2) values. The adventurer and paladin scores are swapped with each other, and the room and monster values are both wrong. Every scoring calculation by a player using this summary would be incorrect.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (room, monster, adventurer, paladin per-item scores), SUPPORTED: 0

### Finding 2 — Title points wrong for Lord of Imps and Lord of Riches (MAJOR)

**Summary:**
> "Lord of Imps (most imps): +1"
> "Lord of Riches (most food, gold, and unused traps): +1"

**Source (lines 2202–2208):**
> "Titles are given to players who have the most in selected categories. If one player has the most, then the title is exclusive and worth 3 points. If 2 or more players have the most, then they have to share the title and only get 2 points each. In a 2-player game, exclusive titles are worth 2 points and shared titles are worth 1."

All titles are worth 3 (exclusive) or 2 (shared) in a standard game, and 2 (exclusive) or 1 (shared) in a 2-player game. The summary assigns "+1" as the value for Lord of Imps and Lord of Riches, which is only the 2-player shared value and is presented as the universal value for those titles. Players would systematically underscore these titles in multiplayer games.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 title point values (Dark Deeds exclusive, Halls exclusive, Imps, Riches), SUPPORTED: 2 (Dark Deeds +3, Halls +3 are correct), WRONG: 2

### Finding 4 — Fatigue damage stated as fixed 2; source says it varies by Combat Card (MAJOR)

**Summary (Combat Phase):**
> "Fatigue and Conquering: Adventurers take 2 fatigue damage (to front adventurer first)."

**Source (lines 1969–1972):**
> "Before conquering the tile, the party accumulates fatigue. Fatigue is damage dealt by the effort of conquering. The amount of damage is specified on the Combat Card. The damage is dealt to the first adventurer, but it is dealt one point at a time."

The amount of fatigue is determined by the Combat Card drawn each round, not a fixed 2. The value 2 appears only in the training scenario (the illustrated 2-counter example). Players using the summary would apply 2 fatigue every round regardless of the card drawn.

### Finding 5 — Pay Day placed after Adventurers Phase; source shows it is part of Event Phase, which precedes Adventurers (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure — Building Round Phases):**
> "4. Event: Reveal and resolve the round's Event Tile."
> "5. Adventurers Arrive: (Spring, Summer, Fall) New adventurers appear."
> "6. Pay Day: Pay wages for monsters."

**Source (lines 2434–2443):**
> "Event Phase: ... Pay Day: For each monster (and ghost), either pay its cost, or let it go and gain 1 Evil. ... Taxes: ... Special Event: ..."
> "Adventurers Phase [listed after Event Phase]"

Pay Day is one of the three Event Tiles; it occurs within the Event Phase, which comes before the Adventurers Phase. The summary presents Pay Day as a separate step after Adventurers, reversing the order and implying it is structurally separate from events.

### Finding 6 — Missing combat steps: Fast Spells and Slow Spells

**Summary (Combat Phase combat steps):**
> "1. Planning ... 2. Traps ... 3. Monsters Attack ... 4. Healing ... 5. Fatigue and Conquering"

**Source (lines 1883–1968):**
> Battle steps listed as: Trap Step → Fast Spells Step → Monsters (and Ghosts) Step → Slow Spells Step → Healing Step → Conquering Step

The summary omits the Fast Spells Step (between Traps and Monsters) and the Slow Spells Step (between Monsters and Healing). These are full steps on the Dungeon Board reference. Players would not know to resolve wizard spells from the Combat Card at all.
