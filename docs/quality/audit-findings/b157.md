## pacific-victory-war-in-the-pacific-1941-45

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn structure: "yearly turns" is wrong; turns are 3-month (quarterly) cycles

Summary: "The game plays over multiple yearly turns (1941-1945). Each year consists of multiple action rounds."

Source (sidebar, p.1): "There are 15 Game Turns (GT), each representing three months of action. GTs are printed on the west edge of the mapboard."

Source (section 1.0): "Each Game Turn has five Phases."

A player following the summary would misunderstand the pacing of the entire game. The 1941 scenario covers Dec/41–Jun/45 = 15 quarterly turns, not ~4 yearly turns.

### Finding 2 — Turn phases are misnamed and misstructured

Summary (Turn Structure): "1. Initiative 2. Strategic movement 3. Combat 4. Production 5. Supply check"

Source (sections 1.1–1.5): The five phases are:
1. Initiative (1.1)
2. Player-1 Move (1.2) — includes both Operation and Strategic movement
3. Player-2 Move (1.3) — same
4. Combat Phase (1.4)
5. Logistics Phase (1.5) — includes both supply check AND production

The summary collapses the two distinct move phases (Player-1 Move, Player-2 Move) into one "Strategic movement" phase and incorrectly splits Logistics into separate "Production" and "Supply" phases. This misrepresents the two-player sequential movement order.

Source (1.5): "Both players, simultaneously: (A) Check supply (7.1) status of units… (B) Determine available Production Points (PP) and build new units or add steps to existing units."

### Finding 3 — Invented action: "Strategic bombing"

Summary (Actions): "Strategic bombing: Attack enemy production and infrastructure"

No such action exists in the source. Searched: "strategic bomb," "bomb," "bombing," "infrastructure," "production attack." The word "Strategic" in the source refers exclusively to a movement type (Strategic Movement, section 4.12); there is no strategic bombing mechanic that attacks production or infrastructure. Raids exist (section 5.9) but they are direct combat attacks on units, not infrastructure.

No support found (searched: "strategic bomb", "bomb.*product", "atom", "nuclear", "infrastructure").

### Finding 4 — Invented special rule: "Atomic bomb"

Summary (Special Rules): "Atomic bomb: May be available to Allies in 1945 under certain conditions."

No support found (searched: "atom", "nuclear", "atomic", "bomb"). The 1943 scenario special rules include Kamikazes and Allied submarines, but no atomic bomb rule of any kind.

### Finding 5 — Decisive victory trigger is invented; source uses VP threshold only

Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions): "Automatic victory can occur if one side captures critical locations (e.g., Japan takes India or Australia; Allies take Tokyo)."

Source (8.1): Decisive Victory is determined purely by the Japanese VP (JVP) total at game end or during play: 25+ JVPs = Japanese Decisive; 0–5 JVPs = Allied Decisive. Section 8.11 states: "The game ends immediately if a player holds a Decisive Victory after any Game Turn." No location-capture trigger (India, Australia, Tokyo) appears anywhere in the source.

No support found (searched: "automatic", "India.*victory", "Australia.*victory", "Tokyo.*victory", "critical location", "capture.*victory").

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (VP thresholds 25+, 16-24, 13-15, 6-12, 0-5), SUPPORTED: 5 (all VP threshold numbers verified)

## pairs

**Verdict: PASS**

### Verification summary

**Victory / game-end conditions:**
- Summary: "There is no winner. The first player to reach the target score loses."
- Source: "There is no winner, just one loser. The game ends when one player reaches the target score." SUPPORTED.

**Target score table:**
- Summary: 2p=31, 3p=21, 4p=16, 5p=13, 6+=11
- Source gives formula: "Take 60 points, divide by players, then add 1." Confirms 4p=16 explicitly in example. All summary values are consistent with the formula. SUPPORTED.

**Deck composition:**
- Summary: "55-card triangular deck: numbers 1 through 10, with 1x1, 2x2, 3x3, ... 10x10 (55 cards total)"
- Source: "1x1, 2x2, 3x3, and so on." Sum 1+2+...+10=55. SUPPORTED.

**Burn rule:**
- Summary: "Burn (discard) 5 cards face down... Do this every time you reshuffle."
- Source: "Shuffle the deck and burn (discard) five cards, facedown… Each time you reshuffle, you will burn five cards again." SUPPORTED.

**Hit / Fold mechanics:**
- Summary: "Hit: Draw 1 card. If it pairs with a card in your stack, score those points."
- Source: "If you pair up, you score that many points." SUPPORTED.
- Summary: "Fold: Take the lowest card currently in play (from any player's stack) and keep it as points."
- Source: "you take the lowest card in play and keep it for points. You may choose this card from all players' stacks, not just your own." SUPPORTED.

**Exhaustion / reshuffle rule:**
- Summary: "When the deck runs out, pause, reshuffle the discard pile, burn 5, and continue where you left off."
- Source: "When the deck runs out, reshuffle the discards. Pause the game, shuffle, and resume where you left off. Remember to burn five cards each time you shuffle." SUPPORTED.

**Scoring cards:**
- Summary: "Keep scored cards face up aside (they leave the deck until game ends)."
- Source: "Players keep their scoring cards aside, faceup. These cards will not return to the deck until the game is over." SUPPORTED.

**Procedural claims (5 verified):**
1. Deal 1 card face up each to start a round — source: "deal one card faceup to each player." SUPPORTED.
2. Lowest card goes first — source: "The player with the lowest card will go first." SUPPORTED.
3. Play proceeds clockwise — source: "play passes to the left." SUPPORTED (left = clockwise from dealer).
4. Pair scoring equals the card rank — source: "if you catch a pair of 8's, you score 8 points." SUPPORTED.
5. Round ends immediately when pair caught or fold taken — source: "As soon as one person catches a pair or folds, the round is over." SUPPORTED.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7 (55 cards, losing scores for 2/3/4/5/6+ players, burn 5 cards), SUPPORTED: 7

## paladins-of-the-west-kingdom

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — "King's Favors: Variable VP" is not a scoring source; King's Favours are action spaces only

Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions table): "King's Favors | Variable VP"

Source (round structure section): "King's Favours: These open up new action spaces for players to use each Round. Each King's Favour may only be used once per Round (they cannot be cleared using the Pray action)."

The end-game scoring section (lines 552–574) lists these scoring sources: Completed King's Orders, Attribute Tracks, Commissions (if 5+), Fortifies (if 5+, plus VP on specific Walls), Develops (if 6+), Garrisons (if 5+), Absolves (if 5+), Converts, Debts (paid/unpaid), Silver/Provisions (1 VP per set of 3). King's Favours do not appear.

This is a MAJOR error: players would incorrectly believe King's Favour cards award VP at end of game.

### Finding 2 — Worker color system is substantially wrong; "Silver" and "Gold" workers invented; "Black" worker flexibility is reversed

Summary (Special Rules): "Worker colors: Blue, Green, Red are standard; Black workers are more flexible but may lower virtue; Silver and Gold workers have premium effects."

Source (components and action descriptions): Workers are: Labourers (white), Scouts (green), Merchants (blue), Fighters (red), Clerics (black/dark), Criminals (purple). No Silver or Gold workers exist anywhere in the source.

Source on flexibility (actions section): "Criminals (purple) act as wilds and may be placed onto both transparent and coloured action spaces." Criminals are the flexible wild workers, not black workers.

Source on Clerics (Pray action): "This action requires 1 Cleric (Black)…" Clerics are specific-type workers used for specific actions (Pray, Garrison, Attack), not flexible general-purpose workers.

The summary invents two worker types (Silver and Gold) and incorrectly attributes the wild/flexible property to black (Cleric) workers when the source gives that property to purple (Criminal) workers.

### Verification of other scoring sources

**Attribute Tracks VP:**
- Summary: "Faith/Strength/Influence tracks: VP based on position." Source scoring example: "16 VP for their Strength, 13 VP for their Influence and 6 VP for their Faith." SUPPORTED.

**Debt scoring:**
- Summary: "Unpaid Debt = VP or penalty." Source: "If left unpaid, Debts will lose players 3 VP at the game's end. Paid Debts… earn players 1 VP at the game's end." SUPPORTED.

**Resources:**
- Summary: "Remaining resources: Minor VP." Source: "Silver / Provisions (1 VP for every set of 3)." SUPPORTED.

**Game end condition:**
- Summary: "After 7 rounds, calculate VP. Highest VP total wins."
- Source: "The game is concluded at the end of the seventh Round… The player with the highest total score is declared the winner." SUPPORTED.

**Tiebreaker:**
- Summary does not mention tiebreakers.
- Source: "In the case of a tie, the tied player who scored the most Victory Points from the King's Orders is the winner. If still tied, the tied player with the least Suspicion is the winner. If still tied, all tied players share the victory." Tiebreaker omission is MINOR (would not cause a wrong score, just unresolved ties).

**Procedural claims (5 verified):**
1. Each round: draw 3 Paladins, choose 1, place 1 atop draw pile, place 1 at bottom — source (round step 4): exactly described. SUPPORTED.
2. Players take turns placing workers (1 action at a time, clockwise) until all pass — source: "Starting with the first player… each player must decide to either take an action or pass. This continues until all players have passed." SUPPORTED.
3. King's Orders: 4 VP (1st), 6 VP (2nd), 8 VP (3rd) — source: "The first King's Order will score players an additional 4 VP… The second will score 6 VP and the third, 8 VP." SUPPORTED.
4. Commission requires 5+ to score: source scoring example confirms "5 VP for Commissioning 6 Monks." Source: "Commissions (if 5 or more completed)." SUPPORTED.
5. Workers placed on action spaces stay there (blocking) until cleared — source: "Each action also requires a certain number of Workers and often specific types (colours) of Workers." Action spaces fill up. SUPPORTED.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (7 rounds, VP sources per scoring section, 4/6/8 VP for King's Orders, 3 VP debt penalty, 1 VP paid debt, 5+ threshold for Commissions/Fortifies/Garrisons/Absolves, 6+ for Develops, 1 VP per set of 3 resources), SUPPORTED: 8
