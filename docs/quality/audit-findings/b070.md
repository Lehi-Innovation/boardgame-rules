### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented victory condition.** Summary (Special Rules): "If all heroes are knocked out simultaneously, the Overlord wins." No such rule exists anywhere in the source. Source "Winning the Game": "Each quest provides specific victory conditions for both the overlord player and the hero players." The Knocked Out section says knocked-out heroes stand up on their next turn; nothing ends the game. This invented blanket rule would cause groups to end games incorrectly.

2. **MAJOR — Wrong Stand Up mechanic.** Summary (Actions table): "Stand Up — If knocked out: stand up, recover damage to half health." Source: "To stand up, the player rolls two red power dice, recovers damage equal to the [hearts] rolled, recovers fatigue equal to the [surges] rolled, replaces his hero token with his hero figure." There is no "half health" recovery anywhere in the source; the dice-roll recovery is the actual mechanic (also used by Revive). This is a wrong recovery amount that directly affects play.

3. **MAJOR — Contradicted large-monster rule.** Summary: "Large Monsters ... Can move through but not end on heroes. Are affected by terrain in any space they occupy." Source movement rules: figures may move "through friendly figures" only, and "a monster treats all other monsters as friendly figures" — monsters (large or small) cannot move through heroes. Terrain claim is also contradicted: source says large monsters are only affected by pits "if they end their movement and all spaces they occupy are pit spaces," only defeated by lava "if all spaces it occupies are lava spaces," and may place their base freely on water when ending adjacent to it — i.e., NOT affected by terrain in any space they occupy.

4. **MINOR — Invented conditions.** Summary lists eight conditions: "Poisoned, Diseased, Immobilized, Stunned, Bleeding, Cursed, Terrified, Weakened." The source names only four condition types (Disease, Immobilize, Poison, Stun — base game has 16 Condition cards = 4 types). Bleeding, Cursed, Terrified, and Weakened are expansion conditions with no basis in this source.

5. **MINOR — Monster activation oversimplified.** Summary: "Each monster gets 1 move action (up to Speed) and 1 attack action per activation." Source: "He may perform up to two actions with each monster ... Each monster may only perform one attack action per activation," and monster actions include move, attack, monster action, open/close door, special. A monster may e.g. double-move or move + open a door; the summary's fixed "1 move + 1 attack" misrepresents this.

6. **MINOR — Omitted hero actions.** The hero action list omits "Open or Close a Door" (an explicit standard action; doors are a core component) and lists "Use a Skill" only obliquely under "Special."

7. **MINOR — Invented Rest restriction.** Summary: Rest "Cannot be performed twice." Source explicitly says a hero "may even perform the same action twice" with no Rest exception (repeating Rest is merely pointless). Harmless at table but unsupported.

8. **Trivial — Search wording.** Summary: "Adjacent to a Search token." Source: "adjacent to or in a space that contains a search token."

Everything else checked out: combat sequence (X = miss; range must equal/exceed distance; damage = hearts minus shields; surge spending), LOS corner-to-corner rule, knocked-out basics, master/minion distinction, campaign flow (XP for skills/Overlord cards, shop, Interlude transition to Act II), attribute names.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — Test mechanic omitted.** The summary references tests only in passing ("Produces varied effects (tests, items, map expansion)") but never explains how tests work: roll two black dice, apply the hero's stat modifier (might/agility/insight/will), pass thresholds, or the "negates" mechanic. Tests recur constantly during explore interactions; a player relying on the summary would not know what to roll. (Hero stats are also never named.)

2. **MINOR — Equipment loadout limits omitted.** Source campaign rules: each hero equips "Two weapons (creating one attack card), One armor, One trinket, Three consumables" before a quest. The summary covers weapon upgrades and crafting persistence implicitly but never states these equip limits.

3. **Trivial — Enemy targeting tie-break compressed.** Summary: "If that hero is unreachable, the closest hero with the least health becomes the target." Source resolves in two steps: closest hero first, then least health among ties (heroes choose if still tied). The compressed phrasing matches the source's own fallback sentence ("moves toward the closest hero who has the least health") and is unlikely to mislead.

4. **Trivial — Fatigue overflow wording.** Summary: "excess goes to a card or is suffered as damage per app instructions." Source: "for each fatigue token that a hero cannot place on a card, they suffer one damage instead." Slightly garbled but same outcome.

5. **Trivial — Explore range.** Summary says terrain/tokens must be "adjacent"; source says "adjacent to or in the same space as the hero's figure."

Also omitted (noted, not penalized as they are reference-level subsystems handled in play by the app/Rules Reference): elevation/staircase/overhang rules, underlay effects (embers/poison/spikes/water), keywords (Charge/Reveal/Shift), barricade combat bonuses, city/crafting economy details. Setup, hero roster (including Chance and Kehli unlocking later), round structure, combat (hero and enemy), conditions, wounds/injuries, win/loss conditions, and the Heroic Adventure Rule all verified accurate.
