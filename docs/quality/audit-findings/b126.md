## l-wenherz

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — source is confirmed as genuine condensed Löwenherz rules by Eric Postpischil.

### Summary overview check
Summary describes the action mechanism as: players "simultaneously choose which of the three actions they want" and resolve conflicts "via negotiation." This is partially supported, but the summary omits the entire card-based action selection structure (decision cards/action cards system) that drives the game. The summary also mischaracterizes and omits key scoring elements and the actual game-end condition.

### Game-end condition — MAJOR FINDING
Summary: "The game ends when a specific condition is met (e.g., certain number of walls placed or VP threshold reached)."
Source: "Play turns until the The King is dead! action card is revealed, which ends the game immediately." (line 34)

The summary does not state the actual game-end trigger (the "King is dead!" action card being revealed). The parenthetical "(e.g., certain number of walls placed or VP threshold reached)" is entirely fabricated — neither of those is a game-end condition in the source. This is MAJOR because players cannot know when the game ends.

### Scoring — MAJOR OMISSION
Summary: "VP are scored based on: Number of castles in your controlled territories; Number of mines in your controlled territories; Power card bonuses."
Source: "Points come from: Gaining territory (especially towns), mines (when mining cards come up), and parchment cards." (lines 4-5) / "Score 3/5/7/9/12 points for a region of 1-4 / 5-10 / 11-20 / 21-30 / more spaces plus 5 per contained town." (line 65) / "Score 1 power point for each space taken over plus 5 power points for each town gained." (line 74) / "Give each player 1 power point for each hill space in their regions." (line 106) / "Score points for parchment cards." (line 107)

Critical errors:
1. Summary says "castles" score VP — source says regions/territories score points, based on *size* (number of spaces enclosed), not on the number of castles. Castles are a prerequisite for a region, not a VP source in themselves.
2. Summary says "mines" — source says "mines (when mining cards come up)" which means silver mine *action cards* trigger hill-space scoring (not from owning mine tokens). Summary implies it's from mine tokens in territories, which is not how it works.
3. Summary completely omits town scoring ("+5 per contained town" when forming a region, and "+5 power points for each town gained" when expanding) — towns are the single most referenced scoring item in the source.
4. Summary completely omits hill-space end-game scoring ("+1 point for each hill space in your regions").
5. Summary says "Power card bonuses" instead of "parchment cards" — parchment cards are the correct term (lines 5, 101).

These omissions/errors would cause players to miscount every score: they would not count town bonuses (5 pts each), would not score hill spaces at end-game, and would attempt to count castles instead of region sizes.

### Action system — MAJOR INACCURACY
Summary: "Bid for Actions: Players simultaneously choose which of the three actions they want. Resolve Conflicts: If multiple players chose the same action, they must negotiate or the action is lost."
Source: "If exactly 2 players choose an action, they attempt to negotiate by offering each other ducats. If they agree, one pays the other and performs the action. If the players cannot agree or more than 2 players chose an action, they duel." (lines 46-48) / "Players secretly select any number (may be 0) of money cards (including treasure cards) from their hands and reveal them simultaneously. The winner pays the bank and takes the action. The other players keep their money." (lines 50-52)

Summary says "action is lost" if negotiation fails — source says they *duel* (bid money) and the winner performs the action. The action is never simply "lost" (except on a second tie in the duel). This is a significant procedural error that would leave players without a conflict resolution mechanism.

Summary also says "Actions: Gain Power Cards" — source has "Crown and Scepter Action" to draw *politics* cards, not "Power Cards". The summary uses "Power Cards" inconsistently (also for the cards providing VP bonuses). Source distinguishes action cards, politics cards, alliance cards, renegade cards, parchment cards, and treasure cards.

### VP Sources — MAJOR
Summary VP Sources table: "Castles + Mines in controlled territories + Power card bonuses"
Source: Points from territory size (3/5/7/9/12 by spaces), towns (5 per town in region when formed and when expanded), hill spaces (1 each at game end), parchment cards.

Castles do not score VP. Summary omits towns entirely. Hill-space end-game scoring is omitted.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (region scoring table 3/5/7/9/12, town bonus 5, expansion town bonus 5, hill-space bonus 1, tiebreaker = ducats), SUPPORTED: 5 (numbers are accurate where stated; errors are in *which* things score, not the point values themselves)

## la-citt

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Prefilter
WARN SOURCE_SUSPECT — prefilter flagged "citt" absent from first 3000 chars. However, reading the extracted file confirms this is a complete, genuine La Città rulebook. The title "La Città" uses accented characters and the rulebook refers to it as "La Città" at line 79. The prefilter warning is a false positive from accent-stripping in the title match.

### Victory / Game-end
Summary: "The game ends after a set number of years (varies by player count)."
Source: "The game ends after 6 game years." (line 316) — The game always ends after 6 years regardless of player count. The summary's "varies by player count" is unsupported; no source text states the year count changes by player count.

This is MINOR because: (a) the "6 game years" rule is stated in the source's goal section (line 428-430: "at the end of the game (after 6 game years)") and players using only the summary would not know the exact number; however (b) the error doesn't affect gameplay once the number is known — it's a metadata error, not a scoring/mechanical error. It would not cause players to score incorrectly if they know it's 6 years from another source.

### Scoring
Summary: "1 point per citizen figure in your cities"
Source: "Each citizen figure is worth 1 victory point." (line 324)
✓ Supported.

Summary: "Bonus points for largest cities"
Source: "Each city that has at least one arch of each of the 3 services (culture, education, and health), is worth 3 victory points." (lines 325-327)
MISMATCH: Summary says "bonus for largest cities" — source says the bonus is for cities with "at least one arch of each of the 3 services" (all-services bonus), not for size. This is a substantive error but the correct rule IS stated in the source and is clearly worded. A player who only reads the summary would never know to score the all-services bonus correctly.

This IS a MAJOR-level error (wrong scoring rule) but it is partially mitigated because the Player Reference section correctly states: "Win: Most victory points from buildings + mine majority + cathedral + coins + councilor bonuses" — wait, this is from the Kutná Hora summary, not La Citt. Let me re-check the La Citt summary…

Re-reading la-citt summary scoring: "1 point per citizen figure in your cities; Bonus points for largest cities; The player with the most points wins." The "largest cities" claim is unsupported and replaces the actual 3-service bonus.

However, the summary omits the PENALTY: "Each player who lost one or more citizen figures in the last round because he could not feed them, loses 5 victory points." (lines 330-332). This is a scoring rule that would directly cause wrong scores.

Summary tiebreaker: none mentioned.
Source: "If two or more players tie with the most victory points, the player among them who has the most gold, is the winner." (lines 333-335). Missing tiebreaker.

Also, the summary's Player Reference states "Service Types: Health = Blue arches; Education = Black arches; Culture = White arches" — this is verified ✓.

Summary population caps table is verified ✓.

### Re-assessment
The "bonus for largest cities" replacing the "3-service bonus" is a factual error in the scoring section. The penalty (-5 VP for unfed citizens last round) is missing. The tiebreaker (most gold) is missing. Together these would cause players to compute wrong final scores. This rises to MAJOR: a player following the summary would not award the 3-arch bonus correctly and would not apply the -5 VP starvation penalty.

**Revised verdict: MAJOR**
