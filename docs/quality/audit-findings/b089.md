## eye-know

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — True/False betting payout table is doubled (2:1 shown instead of 1:1)

The source OCR table structure (lines 55–74), read with the column interpretation that "True or False" followed by 1,2,3,4,5 represents the bet amounts while "Multiple Choice" (2,4,6,8,10) and "Open-Ended" (3,6,9,12,15) represent payouts per bet level, strongly implies T/F pays 1:1 (1,2,3,4,5 for bets 1–5 chips). The Player Reference in the summary itself confirms this: "Payout ratio | T/F 1:1, MC 2:1, Open 3:1 (per chip bet)."

However, the summary's **body betting table** contradicts the Player Reference:

**Summary body table:**
```
| 1 chip | 2 chips | 2 chips | 3 chips |   ← T/F pays 2 chips = 2:1 ratio
| 2 chips | 4 chips | 4 chips | 6 chips |
| 3 chips | 6 chips | 6 chips | 9 chips |
| 4 chips | 8 chips | 8 chips | 12 chips |
| 5 chips | 10 chips | 10 chips | 15 chips |
```

**Summary Player Reference:** "Payout ratio | T/F 1:1, MC 2:1, Open 3:1 (per chip bet)"

**Source (lines 55–74):**
```
YOUR BET / Pays
True or False: 1 chip, 2 chips, 3 chips, 4 chips, 5 chips
Multiple Choice: 2 chips, 4 chips, 6 chips, 8 chips, 10 chips
Open-Ended: 3 chips, 6 chips, 9 chips, 12 chips, 15 chips
```

The source's T/F column values (1,2,3,4,5) match a 1:1 payout ratio (matching the Player Reference), while the body table claims T/F pays 2,4,6,8,10 (a 2:1 ratio — identical to Multiple Choice). The body table doubles the T/F payouts. A player using the body table would award twice as many chips for T/F answers as the source intends. The Player Reference and source agree; the body table is wrong.

Additionally, the summary notes T/F and MC pay identically (both 2,4,6,8,10), which would make T/F trivially optimal (easier questions, same payout), contrary to normal game design and contradicted by the Player Reference.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 15 (all cells in payout table), SUPPORTED: 10 (MC and OE correct; T/F wrong × 5)
