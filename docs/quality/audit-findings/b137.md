# Audit Findings — Batch b137

## magic-realm

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Victory/Win condition fundamentally misrepresented (CRITICAL)

**Summary:** "Meeting or exceeding targets earns victory points. The player with the most points wins."

**Source (rules 4.7/1–4.9):**
> "4.7/1 He counts up his score in each category and subtracts the requirement he recorded for that category. If the result for a category is less than zero, multiply it by three as a penalty."
> "4.7/2 Basic score: He divides each category by its factor (round fractions down, to his disfavor) to convert to victory points."
> "4.7/3 Bonus score: He multiplies his basic score in each category by the number of victory points he assigned to that category, and the result is his bonus score in that category."
> "4.7/4 Total score: He adds together all of his basic scores and his bonus scores, and the result is his total score."
> "4.8 Each character who has a total score of zero has fulfilled his victory requirements and thus can fairly be said to have won the game. There can be several winners in a game, or none."
> "4.9 The character who has the most points is the most successful. He is the 'victor'. There will be a victor even if nobody 'wins'!"

The summary completely omits the core scoring mechanics: (a) shortfall in any category is multiplied by 3 as a penalty, (b) basic score and bonus score are calculated via a two-step formula, (c) "winning" requires achieving a total score of zero or above (meeting requirements), NOT just having the most points, and (d) the player with the most points is the "victor" — a distinct concept from "winner." There can be multiple winners or none. A table using only the summary's description would score the game entirely wrong.

### Finding 2 — Turn structure omits "Evening" phase; Sunset misdescribed (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure):** "Sunset: Monsters prowl; encounters are resolved." The Player Reference table lists only 5 phases: Birdsong, Sunrise, Daylight, Sunset, Midnight — missing Evening entirely.

**Source (rule 1.3/1):**
> "Each game day is broken up in to six periods of time: Birdsong, Sunrise, Daylight, Sunset, Evening and Midnight."

**Source (rule 1, Turn Summary):**
> "2. SUNRISE: The characters mix their ATTENTION chits together, blank side up. Then one die is rolled to determine which denizens are prowling today, and the MONSTER ROLL chit is put in the prowling row of the APPEARANCE CHART."
> "4. SUNSET: One ATTENTION chit is taken from each clearing that contains a character. The chits are mixed together, blank side up."
> "5. EVENING: The ATTENTION chits are picked randomly one at a time. When a chit is picked, the characters in that clearing can rearrange their belongings and trade with each other."

Monster prowling is determined at **Sunrise** (die roll), not at Sunset. Sunset is when attention chits are collected for Evening trading. Evening is an entire phase (character trading/rearranging) that the summary omits entirely. The Player Reference table also lists only 5 phases, missing Evening.

### Finding 3 — Number of daily phases understated (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Each player secretly records their planned activities for the day (up to 2 basic phases, more with special abilities)."

**Source (rules 6.3/1–6.3/2):**
> "6.3/1 He always gets at least TWO phases - these are termed his 'basic' phases."
> "6.3/2 If he spends the whole day outside of the caves, he gets TWO additional 'sunlight' phases. If he starts the day in a cave clearing or records a move into a cave clearing, he does not get these extra 'sunlight' phases. Thus, characters can normally do TWO activities per day in caves, FOUR activities per day outside of the caves."

Characters get 2 basic phases PLUS 2 additional "sunlight" phases when outside caves, for a normal total of 4 phases per day. The summary saying "up to 2 basic phases, more with special abilities" omits the sunlight phases entirely and understates the normal number of daily activities by half.

### Finding 4 — Fame earned from killing monsters is incomplete; Notoriety claim misleading (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Fame (earned by killing monsters)" and "Notoriety (earned by killing natives and causing havoc)"

**Source (rule 23.6):**
> "When a character kills a monster, he immediately gains recorded FAME and NOTORIETY points. He gets the bounty points for the monster, multiplied by the number of monsters he has killed that day."

**Source (rule 35.1/1):**
> "When a character kills a monster, he gains its FAME bounty and NOTORIETY bounty, multiplied by the number of kills he made that day. When he kills a native, he gains its NOTORIETY bounty, multiplied by the number of kills he made that day, and he gains the native's GOLD."

Killing monsters earns BOTH Fame AND Notoriety (not just Fame). Killing natives earns Notoriety AND Gold (not Fame). The summary's category descriptions are incomplete and misleading about which bounties come from which source, which would cause players to misunderstand how to score in each category.

### Finding 5 — Activity called "Enchant" but source calls it "SPELL" (MINOR)

**Summary Actions table:** "Enchant: Cast an enchantment spell (for magic-using characters)"

**Source (rule 39, heading):** "ENCHANTING (The SPELL Activity)"

The activity is named "SPELL" in the rules. It performs enchantments (enchanting MAGIC chits or tiles) rather than casting spells — spell-casting is a separate action. The summary's description of "Cast an enchantment spell" conflates enchanting and spell-casting. This is a minor naming/description error.

### Finding 6 — Combat direction rule incorrectly described as "must match" (MINOR)

**Summary:** "combatants simultaneously select attack and maneuver directions... direction (must match to hit)"

**Source (rule 20.6/1, 22.6/2):**
> "Each attack direction intercepts one of the maneuver directions: Thrust intercepts CHARGE, Swing intercepts DODGE and Smash intercepts DUCK. If an attack intercepts the direction of its target's maneuver, then the attack hits the target."

Attacks hit when the attack direction **intercepts** the maneuver direction via a specific cross-mapping (Thrust→CHARGE, Swing→DODGE, Smash→DUCK), not when directions "match." The distinction could mislead players about when attacks land.
