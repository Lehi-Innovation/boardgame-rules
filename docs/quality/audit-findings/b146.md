## modern-art

**Verdict: PASS**

### Victory / Game-End Conditions
- Summary: "The player with the most money after four rounds wins."
- Source (line 341): "When the fourth rounds ends and all the paintings are sold, all players reveal their money. The player with the most money is the winner."
- Supported.

### Round-End Trigger
- Summary: "When a 5th painting of any single artist is played, the round ends immediately (the 5th painting is NOT auctioned)."
- Source (lines 200-202): "When the fifth painting from any single artist is played, the round ends. The fifth painting is NOT auctioned, and is not owned by any player at the end of the round."
- Supported.

### Auction Types
All five auction types verified against source. Open, One Offer, Hidden, Fixed Price, and Double Auction procedures match the source text.

Notable edge: Hidden Auction tie-breaking. Summary says "Ties: closest to auctioneer clockwise wins." Source (lines 130-132): "If two or more players tie for the highest bid, then the player sitting closest to the auctioneer in clockwise order wins the auction." Matches.

### Artist Value Tiers
- Summary: "1st most: 30k; 2nd most: 20k; 3rd most: 10k; All others: 0."
- Source (lines 227-234): "First: 30 k€, Second: 20 k€, Third: 10 k€, All others: nothing."
- Supported.

### Cumulative Value
- Summary: "In subsequent rounds, an artist's value is the sum of all value tiles in their column across all rounds (but only if they rank in the top 3 that round; unranked artists' paintings are worthless regardless of past tiles)."
- Source (lines 269-275): "When an artist is one of the top three in a round, any paintings from that artist are worth the total value of all Artist Value tiles in that artist's column. Paintings by artists who do not rank in the top three are still worthless, even if they have Artist Value tiles from previous rounds."
- Supported.

### Tie in Artist Rankings
- Summary: "Ties: the artist closer to the left side of the board ranks higher."
- Source (lines 222-224): "If two or more artists have the same number of sold paintings, the artist who is closer to the left side of the game board (the closest to Manuel) is ranked better."
- Supported.

### Double Auction
- Summary: "If another player plays the second card, they become the new auctioneer and receive all payment."
- Source (lines 172-175): "The highest bidder buys both paintings and the new auctioneer receives all the money (the player who played the Double Auction painting doesn't take anything)."
- Supported.

### Card Dealing Table (Rounds 2-4)
- Summary states specific numbers (3p: 6/6/0; 4p: 4/4/0; 5p: 3/3/0).
- Source contains the table headers but the actual cell values are missing from the OCR (lines 288-297 show column/row headers but no values).
- Cannot verify or contradict — applying benefit of doubt as OCR failure.

### Numbers Checked
Starting deal (3 checked), artist values (4 checked), starting money (1 checked) = 8 numbers verified as supported.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8, SUPPORTED: 8

5. **Marae Tiebreaker:** Summary: "If scores are equal in Marae, the player who placed the Moeraki-ball wins." Source (line 228): "In case of equal scores, the player wins who has placed the Moeraki-ball!" Supported.

- Summary says "All tribal tiles in the Tane's line of sight up to the sidelines are removed (including own tiles)."

### Note on "Basic Game" nomenclature

The summary merges these, calling Master's Game elements into the Basic game description. This is a minor structural error that does not mislead about play at the table if the Tane section is separately labeled. Verdict remains MINOR due to the Basic/Master tier confusion in setup description.

## mole-rats-in-space

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Victory Condition
- Summary: "Win: All 4 equipment pieces collected AND all mole rats reach the escape pod."
- Source (line 62): "If all four pieces of equipment have been collected and all mole rats make it to the escape pod, you all win!" Supported.

### Lose Conditions
All four lose conditions verified against source. Supported.

### MAJOR FINDING: Turn Order — Discard and Draw Steps Reversed

Summary Turn Structure (steps 2 and 3):
> "2. Discard your card — Place it in the discard pile.
> 3. Draw a new card — Take the top card from the draw pile. If no cards remain and you cannot win with cards in hand, all players lose."

Source (lines 156-169):
> "Each turn has three steps:
> PLAY YOUR CARD — Move the creatures (mole rats and snakes) shown on your card.
> DRAW A NEW CARD — Take the top card from the draw pile. If there are no more cards to draw, and you can't win the game with the cards that you have left in your hands, you all lose the game.
> DISCARD YOUR CARD — Put your card into the discard pile."

The source order is: Play → Draw → Discard.
The summary order is: Play → Discard → Draw.

Steps 2 and 3 are reversed. This matters because:
- The lose condition check ("no cards to draw and can't win") is evaluated at the DRAW step, which is step 2 in the source but step 3 in the summary.
- With the correct sequence, after drawing you have 1 new card in hand before discarding the old card. With the summary's sequence, you have 0 cards in hand at the draw check (having already discarded), which changes the evaluation timing.

This would mislead players about the sequence of each turn.

### Landing Rules
- Summary: "Landing on Equipment: Collect it into space pack. Cannot pass without collecting."
- Source (line 76-77): "Equipment: Collect it! Pick up the equipment token and place it in the mole rat's space pack." Supported.
- Summary: "A snake on an equipment space blocks collection; move the snake off first." Source (lines 107-109): "If a snake lands on a piece of equipment, nothing happens. Leave the snake there. You must move the snake off the piece of equipment before you can collect it." Supported.

### Snake Bites
- Summary: "Two or more snakes on one space count as one bite." Source (lines 84-86): "Two or more snakes on one space count as one bite." Supported.

### Procedural Claim: "Bump" on Landing on Another Mole Rat
- Summary: "Landing on Another mole rat: Bump it 1 adjacent space."
- Source (lines 78-81): "A Mole Rat: Give a boost! Mole Rats cannot share spaces. Move the mole rat already on this space to an adjacent space in either direction (you choose)." Supported.

### 2-Player Setup
- Summary: "2-player game: Remove 3 specific cards from the deck (43 cards total)."
- Source (lines 264-267): "Remove these 3 cards from the deck before playing the 2-player game. There will be 43 cards in the deck." Supported.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 10 (4 equipment pieces, 12 snakes, 4 medkits, 46 cards, 3 removed for 2p, 43 remaining, 4 mole rats, 1 snake/bite rule, 2nd bite = lose, challenge envelope = 1), SUPPORTED: 10

## mombasa

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Victory Condition
- Summary: "After 7 rounds, final scoring includes... The player with the most VP wins."
- Source (lines 1080-1081): "add up the scores of the 7 categories for each player. The player with highest score (who has earned the most money) wins the game. In the case of a tie, the victory is shared."
- Summary omits the tie rule (victory is shared). Minor omission on tiebreaker.

### MAJOR FINDING 1: Invented Scoring Category — "Bonus tiles | As printed"

Summary Scoring Table:
> | Source | Points |
> | Bonus tiles | As printed |

Source (lines 1054-1081) lists exactly 7 scoring categories:
1. Cash on hand (pounds)
2. Mombasa company shares
3. Cape Town company shares
4. Saint-Louis company shares
5. Cairo company shares
6. Diamond track (last value passed)
7. Bookkeeping track (last value passed)

There is no "Bonus tiles" scoring category anywhere in the final scoring section. The source explicitly says (lines 1078-1079): "Also book tiles (no matter if the ink jar marker has entered them or not) are not worth anything now." Bonus tiles grant in-game advantages, not end-game points. This is an invented scoring category.

### MAJOR FINDING 2: Fabricated Coins-to-VP Conversion

Summary Scoring Table:
> | Coins | 1 VP per 5 coins |

Source (lines 1060): "First, write down the cash (the number of pounds of actual coins) each player has in front of him."

There is no "1 VP per 5 coins" conversion rate. Each pound (coin) counts as 1 point directly. The summary's formula is fabricated. With 5-pound coins in the game worth 5 pounds each, this formula would also be ambiguous as to whether "5 coins" means 5 individual tokens or 5 pounds of value. Either way, no conversion ratio exists in the source.

### Share Value Calculation
- Summary: "Company shares | Track position x company value (number of trading posts)."
- Source (lines 1062-1064): "count the number of uncovered coin icons in Mombasa's company base AND multiply this number by the total number of shares."
- The summary's description is slightly misleading ("number of trading posts" vs "number of uncovered coin icons in the company base"), but approximately correct. Not a MAJOR error.

### Turn Structure
Phase 1 (Planning): Summary says "selects 3 action cards." Source (lines 394-395): "At the start of the game, you have 3 action slots available." Players can unlock up to 5 slots. The summary only mentions 3, omitting the additional slots. Minor omission.

Phase 2 (Actions): Summary describes 6 possible actions. Source (lines 431-437) lists 6 actions: goods cards, expansion cards, bookkeeper card, diamond merchant card, bonus marker, end action phase. Matches.

Phase 3 (Cleanup): Summary: "Slide action cards to discard areas on the player board. Retrieve previously discarded cards."
Source (lines 923-926): "Pick up exactly 1 of your resting decks... Then clean up your action area by turning the cards of your action area face up again and then moving each card from its action slot up to its linked resting deck."
The summary is roughly correct but omits the critical distinction that you retrieve ONE resting deck (not all cards) and that the cleanup involves moving cards to resting decks, not a discard pile. Minor imprecision.

### 7 Rounds
- Summary: "over 7 rounds." Source (line 348): "Mombasa is played over 7 rounds." Supported.

### Starting Coins
- Summary does not specify starting coins amount.
- Source (lines 191-192): "1 pound from the bank (which he places next to his player board)." Each player starts with 1 pound. Minor omission.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (7 rounds, 4 companies, 60 trading posts, 15 per color, 44 action cards, 36 starting + 4 expansion + 4 track = 44 ✓, 72 book tiles, 60 coins, 1 scoring category invented, 1 conversion ratio fabricated, 4 bonus tiles as not a scoring source, 7 scoring categories), SUPPORTED: 10 (2 major errors)

## monopoly

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Victory Condition
- Summary: "The last player remaining after all others are bankrupt wins."
- Source (lines 278-279): "The last player left in the game wins." Supported.

### MAJOR FINDING: Wrong Income Tax Amount

Summary:
> "Income Tax: Pay $200 (or 10% of total assets in some editions)."

Source (lines 151-153):
> "INCOME TAX: If you land here you have two options: You may estimate your tax at $900 and pay the Bank, or you may pay 10% of your total worth to the Bank."

The summary states $200 as the fixed Income Tax amount. The source for this specific rulebook says $900. These are different amounts by $700 — a significant difference that affects gameplay every time a player lands on Income Tax. The $200 figure comes from the original classic Monopoly rules, while this rulebook edition uses $900. This would mislead players at the table.

### Starting Money
- Summary: "Each player receives $1,500: 2x $500, 2x $100, 2x $50, 6x $20, 5x $10, 5x $5, 5x $1."
- Source (line 67): "Each player is given $1,500 divided as follows" — the OCR is garbled but total of $1,500 is confirmed. The breakdown cannot be fully verified from the extracted text due to OCR corruption.

### GO Salary
- Summary: "Collect $200 salary each time you land on or pass GO."
- Source (lines 108-110): "Each time a player's token lands on or passes over GO...the Banker pays him/her a $200 salary." Supported.

### Jail Exit Rules
- Summary: "pay $50 before rolling, use a Get Out of Jail Free card, or roll doubles (max 3 attempts; after 3 failed attempts, pay $50 and move the last roll)."
- Source (lines 171-179): Throw doubles on any of your next three turns; use Get Out of Jail Free card; purchase from another player; pay $50 before rolling on either of your next two turns. "If you do not throw doubles by your third turn, you must pay the $50 fine."
- Summary omits: the $50 fine can only be paid before rolling on "either of your next two turns" (not three), and after the third turn without doubles you MUST pay. The "3 attempts" for rolling doubles is correct. Minor procedural imprecision — MAJOR finding remains the Income Tax error.

### Railroad Rents
- Summary: "Rent depends on how many you own (1 = $25, 2 = $50, 3 = $100, 4 = $200)."
- Source does not state these values explicitly (Title Deed cards not included in text), but these are standard and no contradicting text found.

### Speed Die
- Summary: "1, 2, or 3: Add to total of white dice. Bus: move value of one die, other, or their sum. Mr. Monopoly: move sum of white dice, then advance to next unowned property or next rent-owed property."
- Source (lines 28-47): Matches summary descriptions. Supported.
- Summary: "Three-of-a-kind (all same number): move anywhere on the board." Source (lines 51): "If you roll a three-of-a-kind (all of the dice show the same number), you can move anywhere you want on the board!" Supported.

### Mortgaging
- Summary: "Unmortgage by paying mortgage value + 10% interest."
- Source (lines 242-243): "In order to lift the mortgage, the owner must pay the Bank the amount of the mortgage plus 10% interest." Supported.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 11 (Income Tax $900 vs $200, GO $200, jail $50, 3 turn doubles limit, railroad rents 4 values, 32 houses, 12 hotels, 16 Chance, 16 Community Chest, starting $1500, 10% mortgage interest), SUPPORTED: 9 (1 major error on Income Tax amount)

## monopoly-gamer

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Victory Condition
- Summary: "The game ends after the final Boss Fight, and the player with the highest score (coins + properties + boss cards) wins."
- Source (lines 188-191): "The game ends immediately when the final Boss Fight is over. The player with the highest score wins! To find out how much you scored, add up the points from your Property cards, Boss cards, and Coins." Supported.

### MAJOR FINDING 1: Wrong Coin Scoring Rate

Summary Scoring Table:
> | Coins | 1 point per coin |

Source (lines 191-193):
> "Every five Coins is worth 10 points. Stack your golden Coins in sets of five for easy counting."

This is 2 points per coin, not 1 point per coin. The summary's rate is wrong by a factor of 2. This would cause every player's score to be dramatically underestimated for coins at end-game. MAJOR.

### MAJOR FINDING 2: Auctions Exist but Summary Claims They Do Not

Summary:
> "There are no auctions; if you don't buy a property, no one else gets the opportunity on that turn."

Source (lines 234-238):
> "Don't want to buy it or can't afford it? The Property goes up for auction. Bidding starts with you and moves to the left. Bids start at 1 Coin and may be raised by as little as 1 Coin. The highest bidder wins the auction, and payment goes to the Bank. If all players pass on bidding, the Property remains unowned."

Monopoly Gamer does have auctions when a player cannot or does not buy a property. The summary directly contradicts the source. MAJOR.

### Boss Fight Procedure
- Summary: "When you land on or pass GO: 1. Flip top Boss card. 2. Pay the roll cost. 3. Roll numbered die. If roll equals or exceeds required number, win."
- Source (lines 16-79): "When you land on or pass GO, you trigger a Boss Fight. At the end of your turn: Turn over the top Boss card... pay the roll cost... Roll the numbered die. If your roll is equal to or higher than the required roll, you win!" Supported.

- Summary: "Lose: The next player clockwise may pay to fight, and so on."
- Source (lines 88-93): "The player to your left may fight the Boss by paying the roll cost and rolling the numbered die, or they may pass to the next player." Supported.

- Summary: "If all players pass, the Boss escapes (removed from game). Any coins on the Boss card are discarded."
- Source (lines 98-100): "If all players pass, the Boss gets away and is removed from the game." Source also (lines 60-63): "When the card requires you to pay the Boss, place your payment on that Boss card." No explicit statement about coins being discarded when the Boss escapes is found under those words, but it is implied. Cannot confirm "discarded" wording exactly — minor.

### Starting Coins (Basic Game)
- Summary: "Each player starts with a set number of coins." (No number given.)
- Source (lines 311-315): "Each player starts the game with: 5 Golden Coins (worth 1 each) + 1 Five Coin (worth 5 each)" = 10 coins total = 10 points at game start.
- Omitting the starting amount is a notable gap, but the MAJOR errors on coin scoring and auctions dominate.

### Boss Fight Trigger
- Summary: "If you landed on or passed GO, trigger a Boss Fight at the end of your turn."
- Source (line 333-334): "At the end of your turn, if you landed on or passed GO, flip over the top Boss card to start a Boss Fight." Supported.

### Power-Up Die
- Summary describes it as "rolled along with the numbered die" to "activate your character's special power-up ability."
- Source (lines 268-280): "Roll this along with the numbered die. On your turn, you'll move AND you'll activate a Power-up Ability." Supported.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 10 (8 Boss cards, 16 Title Deed cards, 90 coins, 50+40=90 ✓, 4 characters, coin scoring rate 1 vs 2, starting coins 10, Jail cost 5 coins, auction starting bid 1 coin, 6 to roll for jail-free exit), SUPPORTED: 8 (2 major errors)

### Ticket Booths

### Owned Amusement Rent

- Source (lines 47-48): "if that person owns both Amusements of the same color, you must pay double the amount shown!" Supported.
