## claustrophobia — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (the summary contains almost no numbers; all that exist were checked, plus key source numbers verified as omitted)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 players (frontmatter) | "It allows two players to recreate the battles" | Yes |
| 2 | Round has 3 phases (Initiative / Demon / Combat Resolution) | "divided into 4 phases to be carried out in order: Initiative Phase, Human Player's Action Phase, Threat Phase, Demon Player's Action Phase" | **No** |
| 3 | "Roll action dice (number depends on surviving warriors)" | "rolls as many Action Dice (six-sided) as they have active warriors" | Yes |
| 4 | Designer "Croc" | "A game by CROC" | Yes |

Source numbers the summary omits entirely (nothing to verify against): 36 tiles, 11 Troglodyte / 1 Demon / 5 Human minis, 16 Event cards, 15 Advantage cards, 6 Gift cards, 6 Object cards, 20 TP tokens, 3 Dice of Destiny per Threat phase, 1 TP per Troglodyte, 5 TP per Demon, max 3 warriors per side per tile, 6 Lines of Action per warrior, DEF cap of 6.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Round structure is wrong.** Summary: 3 phases, with "Phase 3: Combat Resolution — Attacks are resolved with combat dice" as a standalone phase. Source: 4 phases (Initiative, Human Action, Threat, Demon Action); combat is resolved *inside* each player's Action phase ("During their activation, a warrior can either move first and then engage in combat, or engage in combat first, then move"). There is no separate combat phase. The Player Reference table propagates the same wrong structure.
2. **MAJOR — The Board of Destiny system (the heart of the Demon turn) is replaced by an invented "threat level" economy.** Summary: "Gain threat points based on current threat level" and "The Demon player's threat level increases as the game progresses, creating escalating danger." Source: the Demon player "rolls 3 Dice of Destiny (six-sided), and positions them in the Destiny square or squares of their choice... and immediately applies the relevant effects" (TP gain via "Sounds From The Deep", stat boosts, event draws, etc.). There is no "threat level" stat and no built-in escalation. The Board of Destiny is never mentioned in the summary.
3. **MAJOR — Invented cost: "Play Event Cards: Pay threat points for powerful one-time effects."** Source: Event cards are drawn via the "Dark Destiny" Destiny-square result and "can be kept in his hand or used at the time indicated in its description" — no TP cost to draw or play them. TP are spent only to bring warriors into play (1 TP per Troglodyte, 5 TP per Demon).
4. **Minor — "Spawn points."** Summary says troglodytes are placed "on spawn points." Source: they may enter on any tile with at least one unexplored opening and no Human warriors (plus the Lair special tile). Loose but directionally similar.
5. **Minor — "Line of sight matters for some abilities and ranged attacks."** Source has no line-of-sight concept; ranged combat only requires "the attacker's tile and the target's tile must be connected by an opening."
6. **Minor — Exploration placement.** Summary implies the Human player places the new tile; source: the Human player draws the tile and "gives it to the Demon player," who places it.
7. **Minor omissions:** Tunnel Size Rule (max 3 warriors/side/tile) and Blocking Rule; troglodytes die to a single hit while Demons take Health-based wounds; warriors die when all 6 Lines of Action are canceled; Exhausted state.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong phase structure plus an invented threat economy in place of the Dice of Destiny/Board of Destiny system, the Demon player's core mechanic.

## cleopatra-and-the-society-of-architects — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (summary body contains very few numbers; all checked, plus key omissions noted)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3-5 players (frontmatter) | "For 3 - 5 players" | Yes |
| 2 | 60 min (frontmatter) | "60 minutes" | Yes |
| 3 | "Take 1-3 cards from the market" | "picks all the card(s) from the column of cards (called a 'Market stall') of his choice" — a stall can accumulate any number of cards | **No** |
| 4 | Game ends "when the last major palace element is built" | "the moment 5 out of the 6 categories of construction pieces in the Quarry have been fully built" | **No** |
| 5 | Choose one of two actions per turn | "MUST perform one (and ONLY ONE) of the following two actions" (Market or Quarry) | Yes |
| 6 | Tie for most corruption → all tied eliminated | "The player or players with the most Amulets are fed to Cleopatra's favorite Crocodile" | Yes |

Source numbers omitted by the summary (no values given to verify): 110-card deck (75 standard / 23 tainted resources / 11 characters), 89 amulets, 108 talents, start with 3 cards + 3 Nile Merchants + 5 Talents, 10-card hand limit, piece costs/values (e.g. Column wall = 1 Artisan + 1 Wood for 3 Talents; Obelisk = 3 Artisans + 2 Woods + 2 Stones for 12; Throne+Pedestal = 3 Artisans + 2 Marbles + 2 Lapis for 12; Sphinx 2/5 Talents; Mosaic 4 + 1/palm; multi-build bonus +2/+5; Merchant = 3 Talents at game end).

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Market mechanic is wrong.** Summary: "Take 1-3 cards from the market (combination of face up and face down)." Source: you take **all** cards from **one stall** (column), then replenish each stall with one card from the deck. The accumulation of cards in unpicked stalls is the central market tension; "1-3 cards" is both numerically and structurally wrong.
2. **MAJOR — Offering mechanic is inverted/invented.** Summary (Actions and Special Rules): "Spend resources to make offerings, which reduce your corruption" — presented as a player action. Source: an Offering to the Great Priest is **triggered** when all 5 dice show the Great Priest symbol; all players then **secretly bid Talents** (not resources); only the highest bidder "discards 3 Corruption Amulets"; the 2nd/3rd/4th/5th bidders **gain 1/2/3/4 amulets**; all bid Talents are lost. In the summary's version every player can shed corruption at will; in the real game most participants gain corruption.
3. **MAJOR — Building costs wrong / invented split.** Summary: "Wall Sections: Require marble" and a separate "Columns: Require lapis lazuli." Source has a single piece type, "Column walls," costing 1 Artisan + 1 Wood (no marble, no lapis). Marble is used for the Throne+Pedestal, lapis for Door frames and Throne.
4. **MAJOR — Invented components:** "Ankh tokens" and "Offering tokens" appear nowhere in the source component list (Statues of Anubis, Cleopatra figure, 110 cards, 89 amulets, Nile Merchants, 108 Talents, Mosaics, 5 Dice of the Great Priest, 5 Pyramids of Corruption, Summary Sheets).
5. **MAJOR — Core systems omitted:** (a) Sphinxes (6 pieces, one of the 6 building categories, 2/5 Talent scoring) are absent from both Components and the building table; (b) the Statues of Anubis / Sanctuary system (claiming enclosed garden areas to shed 1 amulet per square at game end) — the game's main legitimate corruption-mitigation route — is never mentioned; (c) the 10-card hand limit with corruption penalties; (d) end-of-game corruption for tainted cards left in hand; (e) Nile Merchants (wild resources, +3 Talents if unspent).
6. **Minor — "Face up (safe)" is wrong.** Tainted-ness is printed on the card (corruption symbol); the face-up/face-down mix comes from shuffling half the deck face-up. A face-up card can be tainted.
7. **Minor — Victory tiebreak omitted:** "the Architect with the smallest number of Corruption Amulets left is declared the winner."

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the market, offering, and building-cost mechanics are each contradicted by the source, plus invented components and omission of the Sanctuary/Sphinx systems.

## codenames — MINOR

**Source caveat:** `extracted/codenames-rules.txt` is the Czech Games Edition product webpage (component list + marketing description), not the rulebook. Only the component counts and the broad game loop can be verified against it.

### Numbers checked (10/10 supported where the source speaks)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 200 cards with 400 codenames | "200 cards with 400 codenames" | Yes |
| 2 | 16 agent tiles in two colors | "16 agent tiles in two colors" | Yes |
| 3 | 1 double agent tile | "1 double agent tile" | Yes |
| 4 | 7 innocent bystander tiles | "7 innocent bystander tiles" | Yes |
| 5 | 1 assassin tile | "1 assassin tile" | Yes |
| 6 | 40 key cards | "40 key cards" | Yes |
| 7 | 1 card stand | "1 card stand (made of RE-Wood®)" | Yes |
| 8 | 25 cards in 5x5 grid | "lay out 25 cards, each featuring a single word" | Yes |
| 9 | 4-8+ players (frontmatter) | "Number of players 4-8+" | Yes |
| 10 | 15 min (frontmatter) | "Length 15 min" | Yes |

Core loop also matches the source: one-word clue + number; correct guess → keep guessing; opposing team's card or bystander → turn ends; assassin → game over; first team to identify all agents wins.

### Findings

1. **Minor — Much procedural detail is unsupported by this source** (which is a product page, not a rulebook): the 9/8 agent split and key-card starting-team indicator, double-agent tile placement, max guesses = clue number + 1, minimum 1 guess, "unlimited" clues, clue-validity rules. These all match the official Codenames rulebook (no contradiction with the source), so they will not mislead, but they cannot be verified from `extracted/`.
2. **Minor — The "zero" clue rule is stated incorrectly (vs. the official rulebook; source is silent).** Summary: with a clue of zero "they still get 1 guess" / "the team still gets exactly 1 guess." Official rule: when the clue number is 0, the usual guess limit does not apply — operatives may guess as many words as they want (minimum one). This is the only substantive rules error found.
3. Trivial: "(8 per team)" is an inference from "16 agent tiles in two colors" (consistent with the 9/8 split plus double agent).

**Verdict: MINOR** — components and core loop are fully faithful to the (thin) source; the one real error is the zero-clue guess limit, a niche situation.

## cold-war-cia-vs-kgb — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (12/12 supported)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 12 agent cards (6 CIA, 6 KGB) | "12 agent cards (6 CIA, 6 KGB)" | Yes |
| 2 | 21 objective cards (15 nations, 6 events) | "21 objective cards (15 nations, 6 events)" | Yes |
| 3 | 24 group cards | "24 group cards" | Yes |
| 4 | 6 military, 6 economic, 6 media, 6 political | "(6 military, 6 economic, 6 media, 6 political)" | Yes |
| 5 | 2 domination tokens (1 CIA blue, 1 KGB red) | "2 domination tokens (1 CIA, 1 KGB)"; "blue for the CIA, red for the KGB" | Yes |
| 6 | 1 balance token | "1 balance token" | Yes |
| 7 | 2 score cards / 2 score tokens | "2 score cards (1 CIA, 1 KGB) / 2 score tokens" | Yes |
| 8 | Six phases per game turn | "Each game turn is divided into six phases" (names match) | Yes |
| 9 | 100 VP to win | "The first player to accumulate 100 victory points wins" | Yes |
| 10 | Both at 100+ → higher total wins; tie → play on | "the player with the greater total wins. If there's a tie, play additional game turns" | Yes |
| 11 | Balance token: lowest VP; tie → loser of last Cease-fire | "player with the lowest victory point score takes the balance token. If there's a tie, the player who lost last turn's Cease-fire" | Yes |
| 12 | First turn: balance token assigned by fist-guess | "one player hides the token in one of his closed fists..." | Yes |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Three of four group powers are misassigned, one invented (also in the Player Reference logic of the Actions table).** Source rules text, confirmed by the worked example (Opposition=political steals Industry; Industry=economic re-states Newspapers; Newspapers=media peeks at deck; Mafia=economic readies Newspapers):
   - **Economic** = "switch the state of another group card in play" (ready↔mobilized, one card, cannot affect another economic card). Summary gives Economic the *media* power ("Look at top card of group deck; recruit it, discard it, or leave it").
   - **Media** = "look at the top group card on the group deck, and then choose to discard it, put it faceup in front of him, or leave it." Summary gives Media the *political* power ("Steal a group from opponent or give them one of yours").
   - **Political** = "switch the allegiance of another group card in play." Summary gives Political an invented power: "Ready all your mobilized groups."
   - **Military**: summary says it "cannot destroy another military card"; source says a military card "can never destroy **itself**" — destroying other military cards is legal.
2. **MAJOR — Influence tie-breaker is wrong.** Summary: "If tied, the player with the balance token wins." Source: ties are broken by the objective's **bias icons** — "Address the most important (i.e., largest) faction first. The player with the highest value group from that faction wins the tie." The balance token only decides who acts first in the Influence Struggle.
3. **MAJOR — The leave cycle is inverted, twice.** (a) Summary Planning: "Agents on leave from last turn return to headquarters **before** selection." Source: on-leave agents "can't be chosen as Agent X"; they return only "Once both players have chosen their Agents X." (b) Summary Debriefing: "Return Agent X cards to headquarters (unless terminated)." Source Détente: "each player's Agent X **goes on leave**" (except the Deputy Director). As summarized, the game's central agent-rotation restriction disappears entirely.
4. **MAJOR — Invented "Domination bonus."** Summary Scoring: "If you hold both your domination token and claim the objective, gain its VP." No such conditional bonus exists; the winner simply claims the objective and scores its VP, and the domination token merely marks the provisional winner during Cease-fire/Debriefing.
5. **MAJOR — Garbled "bonus objective" rule.** Summary Debriefing: "If the winner's Agent X has initiative, the winner also claims a 'bonus objective' from the bottom of the objective deck." Every agent has an initiative number (1-6) used only for agenda resolution order; only the **Director** (initiative 6) claims the bottom-of-deck objective, and only "if his side places its domination token."
6. **Moderate — Phase content misplaced:** agendas are applied in the Debriefing phase in initiative order (after Agent X reveal there), not in Cease-fire as the summary states; civil disorder also makes the opponent "immediately claim the objective" (omitted), and if both players cause it the objective goes to the bottom of the deck unclaimed (omitted).
7. **Moderate — Agent details:** Deputy Director is not "no effect" — he "always manages to survive termination... and never goes on leave." Assassin's agenda is conditional ("If his side places its domination token") and sends the objective to the bottom of the deck (both omitted). Double Agent sends "a rival agent from his opponent's **headquarters**" on leave, not the opponent's Agent X.
8. **Minor omissions:** Influence Struggle ends when both players pass consecutively; balance token stays put if both caused civil disorder; event-card VP are lost when their ability is used.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — every component count is right, but the faction powers, tie-breaker, leave cycle, and two scoring rules are wrong or invented; this summary would badly mislead at the table.

## colonial-twilight-the-french-algerian-war-1954-62 — MAJOR

### Faction victory conditions (special COIN attention)

| Summary | Source (7.2/7.3) | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| "French Victory: Total Support + Commitment >= a threshold" | "Government: Total Support (1.6.2) plus Commitment (1.8) **exceeds 35**" | Direction correct; threshold value (35) omitted; ">=" vs "exceeds" unverifiable since no number given |
| "FLN Victory: Total Opposition + FLN Bases >= a threshold" | "FLN: Total Opposition (1.6.2) plus number of FLN Bases on the map **exceeds 30**" | Direction correct; threshold value (30) omitted |
| "Checked during each Propaganda Round" | "Check victory at the start of each Propaganda Round **except the first** (6.1)"; "Do not conduct a Victory Phase on the first Propaganda Round" | **Contradicted** (first-round exception missing) |
| "If neither side achieves their condition when the final Propaganda card is resolved, the side closest to their threshold wins" | "the player with the highest victory margin wins" (margin = amount beyond/short of condition) | Equivalent — correct |
| (no tie rule) | "Ties go to the FLN" | Omitted |

Unlike many COIN summaries, the *attribution* of conditions to factions is correct. But the actual numbers (35/30), the first-Propaganda exception, and the FLN tie rule are missing.

### Numbers checked (summary has almost no numbers; all checked, key source numbers verified as omitted)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1-2 players (frontmatter) | "2-player board game... rules can run a FLN non-player faction, enabling a solitaire game" | Yes |
| 2 | French ops menu: Train, Garrison, Sweep, Assault | "The Government player chooses from Train, Garrison, Sweep, and Assault" | Yes |
| 3 | FLN ops menu: Rally, March, Attack, Terror | "The FLN player chooses from Rally, March, Attack, or Terror" | Yes |
| 4 | Gov special activities: Deploy, Search, Neutralize, Resettle (4 items) | "The Government player may choose from **Deploy, Troop Lift, or Neutralize**" (3 items) | **No** |
| 5 | FLN special activities: Extort, Subvert, Ambush, Agitate (4 items) | "The FLN may choose from **Extort, Subvert, or Ambush**" (3 items) | **No** |

Source numbers omitted by the summary (nothing to verify): victory thresholds 35/30, 71-card deck, 91 wooden pieces, Train cost 2 Resources/space placing up to 4 Algerian cubes, Garrison 2 Resources total moving up to 6 Police, FLN op costs 1 Resource/space, Attack removes up to 2 pieces on die ≤ #guerrillas, 12 Terror markers, Pass = +1 FLN / +2 Government Resources.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented Government special activity "Search: Reveal hidden guerrillas."** No such special activity exists (activation of Underground guerrillas is done by the Sweep and Garrison *Operations*). Meanwhile the real special activity **Troop Lift** ("Move any French Troops (only) among any three Algerian spaces") is missing entirely.
2. **MAJOR — Special-activity menus padded with non-special-activities.** "Resettle" is one of the two options of the **Deploy** special activity (4.2.1), not a separate SA; "Agitate" is an option within the **Rally** Operation (3.3.1) and the Propaganda Support Phase (6.3.2), not an FLN special activity. As listed, both factions' SA menus (4 items each vs. the actual 3) are wrong.
3. **MAJOR — Subvert target is wrong.** Summary: "Convert **French** police to FLN guerrillas." Source 4.3.2: "Remove a total of up to two **Algerian** cubes (Troops or Police)... or in one space replace one **Algerian** Police cube with an Underground Guerrilla." Subvert cannot touch French pieces — a distinction central to the game's French/Algerian force split.
4. **Moderate — Garrison is wrong.** Summary: "Move troops and police between sectors." Source 3.2.2: Garrison "moves **Police**" only ("Move up to six Police cubes"), then Activates guerrillas in one selected space. Troops move via Sweep or Troop Lift.
5. **Moderate — Terror effect overstated.** Summary: "Shift population from support to opposition." Source 3.3.4: Terror "Set[s] the space to **Neutral**" and places a Terror marker; shifting to Opposition is done by Agitation, not Terror.
6. **Moderate — Victory Phase exception and thresholds omitted** (see victory table above): no check on the first Propaganda Round; thresholds 35/30 never stated; "Ties go to the FLN" omitted.
7. **Minor — Propaganda Round phases compressed.** Summary lists victory/resources/support/reset; source 6.0-6.5 has Victory, Resources & Commitment, Support, **Redeploy**, Reset. Redeploy (Government troops to Cities/Bases, FLN guerrillas within Wilaya) is folded into "reset pieces."
8. **Minor:** "Wooden cubes (... FLN guerrillas, FLN bases)" — pieces are wooden but not all cubes (source: "91 blue, black, and green wooden playing pieces, some embossed"). Turn structure, eligibility adjustment, dual-use events ("Shaded Text (see Dual Use 5.2)"), Pivotal Events omission, and the non-player FLN solo system are otherwise consistent with the source.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — victory-condition attribution is correct (rare for COIN summaries), but the special-activity menus contain an invented activity and omit a real one, and Subvert/Garrison/Terror are each contradicted by the source.
