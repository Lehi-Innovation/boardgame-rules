### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Entire phase omitted: "Phase 1: Evaluate vacant spaces."** Source: "Each turn consists of 3 phases... Phase 1: Evaluate vacant spaces ... If not all spaces on their side of the icon bar are occupied, the active player has to forfeit 1 VP of their personal supply for each vacant space on their side." The summary's Turn Structure is "1. Roll action dice 2. Use dice 3. End of turn" — the VP-forfeit phase is completely missing. This is the game's central economic pressure (the start player forfeits 2 VP on turn one), a missing phase and a missing VP-loss source.
2. **MAJOR — Game-end condition omitted.** Source gives two triggers: "The personal supply of VP's of one of the players is exhausted" OR "The general supply of VP's is exhausted." Summary only states "When any player's VP personal supply reaches 0, the game ends immediately."
3. **Minor (unsupported claim).** Summary: "(The player at 0 can still win if their opponent has fewer through transfers.)" — no such statement in the source; source only says "The player with most VP's is the winner."
4. **Minor — Draw Cards misstated.** Source: "draw as many cards from the face down draw pile as shown by the die. You keep 1 of these cards and place the remainder... on the... discard pile." Summary just says "draw cards from the draw pile," omitting both die-count and keep-only-1.
5. **Minor — Collect Money amount omitted.** Source: "take as many Sestertii from the general supply as shown by the die"; summary: "collect Sestertii from the bank" with no amount.
6. **Minor — Bribery rules omitted.** Source: activating the card next to the bribery icon costs Sestertii equal to the die used, and "a player may assign only 1 die to the 'bribery' icon" per turn (all other icons may take multiple dice). Summary only says "Place a die on the bribery icon for special effects."
7. **Minor — Playing onto occupied spaces omitted/contradicted.** Source ("Adding cards"): "If you play a card on a place already occupied by a card, put the original card on the discard pile." Summary says new cards go "next to an available icon," implying occupied spaces are off limits.
8. **Trivial omissions:** reroll if all three action dice match; beginner variant; virtual VP rule when general supply is short.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — an entire turn phase (vacant-space VP forfeit) and one of the two game-end conditions are missing; several action rules are under-specified. Components and setup numbers are all accurate.

## argent-the-consortium — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (the summary contains very few numbers; all that exist were checked)

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 5 rounds | "Gameplay proceeds over a series of 5 rounds"; "At the end of the 5th Round, the game ends!" | Yes |
| 2 | Player count 2-5 | 3/4/5-player setups (8/10/12 rooms) plus "Argent for Two" variant | Yes |
| 3 | Most votes wins, ties to tiebreakers | "the player with the most votes wins! In the case of a tie for Votes, the player with the most Influence wins!"; Marks then IP break voter ties | Yes |

The Components section gives no counts at all (source has e.g. 36 oversized spell cards, 108 standard cards, 38 vault cards, 18 voter cards, 35 mage pawns, 60 marks, 42 loyalty badges, 50 mana, 27 INT/27 WIS, 19 merit badges, 15 board tiles). Not wrong, but no numbers to verify. Setup quantities (6 Gold, 2 Mana, 2 INT, 2 WIS, 5 drafted mages, 10 dealt voters + 2 white-border voters) are all omitted.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Mage powers are wrong for 3 of the 4 colors listed (also repeated in the Player Reference table).**
   - Summary: "Blue mages: Cannot be wounded by other mages." Source: **Green** (Natural Magick) "can never be Wounded"; **Blue** (Divinity) "can never be affected by Spells cast by rival candidates (they are still susceptible to Sorcery Mages, Vault Cards, and Supporters)." Blue absolutely CAN be wounded by Red mages.
   - Summary: "Green mages: Provide bonus resources when placed." Invented — no such power; Green's power is wound immunity.
   - Summary: "Grey mages: Can be placed in already-occupied spots." Source: Grey (Mysticism) lets you "also place a Mystic after the Spell is completed" when you take the Cast a Spell action. Nothing about occupied spots.
   - Red ("can wound opposing mages") is roughly right but omits the 1-mana cost and taking the slot ("you may choose to spend 1 mana to Wound a Mage and take their slot"). Purple (deploy as Fast Action) is omitted entirely.
2. **MAJOR — Invented round-end mechanic.** Summary Placement Phase: "Continue until all players pass." Source has no passing: "Once the last Bell Tower Offering is taken, players will no longer be able to take actions in the round" / "The Errands Phase ends immediately as soon as the final Bell Tower Offering card is taken." The Bell Tower variable-round-length mechanic is a flagged "Core Idea" and the summary replaces it with a nonexistent pass rule; "Take a Bell Tower Offering" is also missing from the action list (source lists 5 actions: Place a Mage, Cast a Spell, Use a Vault Card, Use a Supporter Card, Take a Bell Tower Offering).
3. **MAJOR — Marks mechanic wrong, and voter-peeking attributed to the wrong system.** Summary: "Marks: Placed on rooms to reserve future benefits" — contradicted; source: "When you gain a Mark, place it onto one of the Consortium Voters and secretly look at that Voter's card." Meanwhile the summary credits voter-peeking to "Research Vault: Peek at consortium voter cards" — Vault cards are gear/treasures ("recover valuable gear from the University's vaults"), not voter intel. Marks are also the primary vote tiebreaker, omitted from the summary's tiebreaker note.
4. **Minor — Unsupported voter criteria examples.** Player Reference lists "Most mages" and "Most merit badges" as voter criteria; neither appears in the source's voter categories or resource/vote table (which lists IP, Mana, Gold, Research, INT, WIS, Marks, Treasures, Consumables, Supporters, Departmental Control, Diversity). Source even notes Mages "do not give you any additional benefit to claim Departmental Control Voters."
5. **Minor — Round phases relabeled.** Source: Setup Phase → Errands Phase → Resolution Phase; mages return to offices during Resolution, and refresh happens in the next round's Setup. Summary's "Placement / Resolution / Cleanup" is a reasonable approximation but drops Fast Actions/Reactions and the consolation "take 1 IP instead" rule.
6. **Minor — Unverifiable claim.** "Bell Tower: First player can change based on bell tower actions" — no support in the extracted text (OCR of Bell Tower card texts is fragmentary, so not counted as definitively invented).
7. **Minor — Components list oddities.** "Research points" listed as a physical component (Research is an abstract resource; the tokens are INT/WIS); "Treasure cards" listed separately from Vault cards (Treasure is a Vault card type); "Bell tower track" (they are Offering cards).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong mage powers (the asymmetric-worker core of the game), an invented pass-to-end-round rule replacing the Bell Tower mechanic, and a contradicted Marks mechanic would all mislead players at the table.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Contradiction: workers are never removed.** Summary: "After earning seals, remove workers from around the completed building and return them to owners." Source: "Once the workers are placed on the board, they remain on the square on which they have been introduced for the whole game. They are never moved or removed." (Tactical hint 7 confirms one worker can cash in on multiple payouts.) This inverts a defining mechanic.
2. **MAJOR — Contradiction: neutral workers do NOT earn seals.** Summary: "Neutral workers also earn seals, which go to the player who completed the building." Source: "Important: You do not get any seals for neutral workers!" The actual bonus for the completing player is different: "The player whose turn it is gets the seal of the completed building in addition" — which the summary omits.
3. **MAJOR — Invented deployment rule.** Summary: "You may also place neutral workers from the pool (1 neutral for every 2 of your own workers placed)" (repeated in Player Reference as "neutrals at 2:1 ratio"). No such ratio exists. Source: you place "any number of his own and / or neutral workers from their pool behind the screen"; neutral workers are acquired by covering tent camps when laying out a building ("one neutral worker per square showing a tent camp that he has just covered") — a mechanic the summary omits entirely.
4. **MAJOR — Architect banner benefit omitted.** Source: "If they play it, they get two workers of their colour from the general pool" and may then optionally run a personal evaluation. Summary describes the banner as conversion-to-gold only; the 2-worker gain (per source hint 4, sometimes the main reason to play a banner) is missing.
5. **Moderate — End-game trigger misstated.** Summary: "The game ends when all castle pieces have been placed or a specific trigger occurs." Source: "The end of the game begins as soon as a player places the last castle piece on the second floor," then every player (trigger player last) gets one final turn using third-floor pieces. The summary's condition is wrong and the final-round structure is missing.
6. **Moderate — Placement/deployment constraints misstated.** (a) Building placement may also be adjacent to "any worker already placed" — summary only allows adjacency to a building/castle lot. (b) Source: "All workers that a player introduces during their turn have to touch one and the same building" — summary says "adjacent to buildings" with no same-building constraint.
7. **Minor omissions:** placing the card's seal on the new building; redrawing back up to 4 cards (face-up row or deck, with replenish); simultaneous multi-building completions evaluated separately; exchanging 5 seals for a value-5 seal; tie = shared win; first-game neutral-worker allotment (3/2/1 by player count).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — all component/setup numbers check out, but the completion/payout rules contain two direct contradictions of the source, plus an invented neutral-worker ratio and a missing banner benefit; these are the heart of the game.
