### Findings
1. **MAJOR — Wrong civilization card taxonomy.** Summary: "24 civilization cards across 3 types (Crafts, Sciences, Arts/Civics)" and Player Reference lists exactly those 3 types. Source 6.1: "There are five groups of civilization cards... Arts (blue - triangle), Crafts (orange - square), Sciences (green - circle), Civics (red - hexagon), and Religion (yellow - star)." The five-group structure matters mechanically (33.24 requires cards "from all five groups (i.e., all five colors)" to advance epochs).
2. **MAJOR — Incomplete victory conditions.** Summary lists only "Position on the A.S.T., Civilization card point values, Other scoring factors." Source 35.1 enumerates five sources: A. Civilization cards, B. Commodity cards (sets + face value), C. Treasury tokens, D. A.S.T. position (**100 points per space**), E. Cities (**50 points per city**). Three of five scoring sources and all point values are missing — a player could not compute the winner from the summary.
3. **MAJOR — Missing turn phases.** Source 18.1–18.2: "There are 13 phases in a turn": Taxes, Population expansion, **Census**, **Ship construction and maintenance**, Movement, Conflict, City construction, **Remove surplus population (check city support)**, Trade card acquisition, Trade, Resolve calamities, Civilization card acquisition, A.S.T. movement. Summary lists 10, omitting Census (which sets movement order, 21.1), Ship Construction (cost 2 tokens, 22.1), and Surplus Population Removal — and folds "overpopulation" resolution into a Conflict step placed *before* city construction, while the source removes surplus *after* city construction (26.1).
4. **Contradiction — conflict trigger.** Summary: "Combat occurs when opposing tokens share an area." Source 24.1: conflict occurs only when mixed-nation tokens exceed the population limit; "If the population limit... is not exceeded, the tokens will co-exist without conflict."
5. Minor: Components omit ships (each set has 55 tokens, 9 cities, 4 ships per 5.1); tax rate (2 tokens per city, 19.1) and city-build thresholds (6 tokens on city site / 12 without, 25.2) not stated.

**Verdict: MAJOR** (wrong card-group structure, 3 of 5 scoring sources missing, 3 of 13 phases missing, misstated conflict trigger)

| Gold: +1 strength or reroll a die per gold | both options match | Yes |

### Findings
1. **MAJOR — Game end conditions missing entirely.** Source: "The game ends when there are no more swords and no more companions in the stockpile. The ongoing round is finished..."; plus "In the rare event that none of the adventurers can move anymore the game ends immediately." The summary never states when the game ends (Scoring section only says "Highest score wins").
2. **MAJOR — Contradicted sword rule.** Summary: "Swords are not lost in victorious combat; only herbs and gold spent are consumed." Source quick reference states generally: "Items used in a battle are out of the game." The sentence the summary appears to have misread — "You do not have to give up any swords in order to win a fight" — means swords are *optional* in combat (the next sentence: "An adventurer can defeat a fog creature using only its own combat strength level, companions, herbs and/or gold"), not that used swords are retained. The sword-restock rule ("If there are no sword tiles left in the stockpile..., shuffle the discarded ones in the box") confirms used swords cycle to the box.
3. **Invented numbers — fog creature strengths.** "values: 4x strength-4, 4x strength-3, 3x strength-2, 2x strength-1, 1x strength-0" misreads the tile *counts* as strength values; the source's example creature has strength 12.
4. **Unsupported rule.** "If the terrain card deck runs out, shuffle the discard pile" — not in source. The actual exhaustion rules (companions: take from box; swords: reshuffle discarded ones) are omitted instead.
5. Minor omissions: 2-player game adds starting areas outside row 5 / column E; option to move one adventurer one field or not at all; excess herb points are lost (no "change").

**Verdict: MAJOR** (missing game-end trigger, sword-retention claim contradicts source, invented component values)

## afrika-korps — MAJOR

Source: Avalon Hill / Wargame Academy Amplified 3rd Edition rulebook (full, 13 pp).

### Numbers/facts checked (10 checked, 9 supported, 1 wrong)

|---|---|---|
| ~700 miles El Agheila–El Alamein | 1.1 "approximately 700 miles between El Agheila and El Alamein" | Yes |
| Blue = Allied, Red = Axis | 1.2 | Yes |
| Attack/Defense/Movement factors on units | 1.25–1.27 | Yes |
| Unit sizes XXX/XX/X/III/II = Army Group/Division/Brigade/Regiment/Battalion | 1.21 table matches | Yes |
| Nationalities A/NZ/I/SA/It + unmarked German/British | 1.22 | Yes |
| German Home Base W3, Allied Home Base J62 | 1.1 map features | Yes |
| Fortresses Bengasi H2, Tobruk G25 | "FORTRESS: Bengasi (H2), Tobruk (G25)" | Yes |
| Game turn = half a month; Axis moves first | 3. "passage of half a month"; 3.1 Axis first | Yes |
| Coast road enhanced movement | 17.1 "up to ten additional hexes per turn on the coast road" | Yes |
| Unit types incl. "Artillery" | 1.23 figure lost to OCR; game's units are infantry/armor/recce (21.1) — artillery unverifiable | Doubtful |

### Findings
1. **MAJOR — Invented supply mechanic.** Summary: "Units must trace a supply line back to their home base. Unsupplied units have halved combat values and cannot advance after combat." Source: supply is provided by discrete *supply unit counters*; attacks at 1-2 or better require attackers "within five hexes of a friendly supply unit" (14.2) and the supply unit is *consumed* (14.1); attacks at 1-3 or worse need no supply (14.3); and 14.7 explicitly contradicts the halving claim: "Supply is not necessary to defend; there is no change or reduction of a unit's defense factor due to lack of supply." Nothing in the source halves combat values or traces supply to home base ("halv" appears nowhere). Real penalty for isolation: elimination when trapped in enemy ZOC without supply (11.9).
2. **MAJOR — Wrong victory conditions.** Summary: "Axis wins by capturing both fortress cities... and advancing to El Alamein... Allied player wins by preventing these objectives or by driving the Axis back to El Agheila." Source 4.1/4.2: a side wins by eliminating all enemy combat units, OR by controlling **both fortresses AND both home bases simultaneously for two consecutive player turns**, by/through the last October 1942 turn (Allies also win by simply avoiding Axis victory through that turn). "Advancing to El Alamein" and "driving back to El Agheila" are not victory conditions; the home-base requirement, elimination condition, and October 1942 time limit are all missing.
3. **Major omissions:** Zones of Control (section 7.1 — the mechanism that triggers and governs all combat, retreats, and supply lines) never mentioned; stacking limit (3 combat units/hex, 6.1); Automatic Victory (7-1 / 5-1 surrounded, 9.1); supply units as capturable counters (15); Rommel HQ unit (22, two-hex movement bonus); sea movement via Tobruk/home base (23.4); replacements at fixed attack-factor rates (20.2–20.3).
4. Minor: "Qattara Depression: Impassable to most units" — impassable to all units on full hexes (5.6); setup described as units "entering from" home bases, whereas initial forces are placed per the March 1941 Situation Chart (2.3) and reinforcements land at controlled ports (19.2).

**Verdict: MAJOR** (supply system invented and contradicted by 14.7; victory conditions wrong; ZOC core system absent)

Robber (lose all money), blank token (nothing), visa/horseshoe rules (worthless until Star found; alternative win by beating the Star carrier to a start city), and the primary win condition all match the source.

### Findings
1. **Unsupported addition — "2005 official rule amendment".** "The 2005 official rule amendment eliminated the need for sea travel tickets, allowing free movement across water to prevent unwinnable scenarios for cash-poor players." Nothing like this is in the source. Specific date + mechanical claim from outside the source; flagged as edition guidance so unlikely to mislead badly, but it is invented relative to the source.
2. **Unsupported inference — broke-player rule.** "If a player runs out of money, they cannot use paid transportation... can only travel by land" — not in the source (and in tension with the summary's own point 1).
3. **Unsupported but accurate embellishments:** start cities named as Cairo/Tangier (source only says "starting cities"); 1951/Finnish origin; "tokens remain face-up"; "no limit to players in a city"; "determine first player by any agreed method". None contradict the source.
4. Note: the source itself warns it "may have some errors"; the summary nonetheless tracks it faithfully on every checkable number.

**Verdict: MINOR** (all numbers match; several invented/unsupported additions — notably the "2005 rule amendment" and the no-money restrictions — none contradicting the source)
