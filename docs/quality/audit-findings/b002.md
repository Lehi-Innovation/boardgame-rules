# Audit: 1830-the-game-of-railroads-and-robber-barons (batch b002)

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Summary audited: `rules/1830-the-game-of-railroads-and-robber-barons.md`
Source: `extracted/1830-the-game-of-railroads-and-robber-barons-rules.txt` (Mayfair edition; covers Base Game, 1830 Classic, variants, and 1830+ scenarios)

## Major Findings

### 1. Invented rule: corporation "closes" on unaffordable forced train purchase (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary (Special Rules & Edge Cases): "If the president cannot afford any train, the corporation closes."
- Source 7.6.2/7.7: "If together the railroad and its president do not have enough money to buy a train, both the railroad and its president spend all of their money and the bank pays the rest. The railroad goes into receivership until this 'loan' is repaid." The railroad keeps operating under a manager.
- 1830 Classic sidebar: "the president is forced to sell shares to make up the difference. If there is still not enough money after the president sells all of his shares, he goes bankrupt and the game ends."
- A grep for "close/closes/closed" confirms closure only ever applies to private companies. Corporations never close in any ruleset in this book. This would badly mislead players in one of the game's most pivotal situations.

### 2. Invented phase structure: "Phase 7 = first 7-train" and "Phase D = first diesel" (UNSUPPORTED + self-contradictory)
- Summary Player Reference table: "7 | First 7-train bought | + Grey" and "D | First diesel bought | + Grey | 4-trains". The Trains table likewise lists "7-train ... Phase 7" and "Diesel ... Phase D".
- Source 3.0: "The game progresses through seven phases. The start of each new phase is triggered by the purchase of a new train type: 2-train, 3-train, 4-train, 5-train, 6-train, diesel." Phase 7 is triggered by the first diesel (3.7); there is no 7-train phase and no "Phase D".
- 7-trains do not exist in the Base Game/Classic at all ("26 trains are used: 6x 2-trains, 5x 3-trains, 4x 4-trains, 3x 5-trains, 2x 6-trains, 6x diesels"). In 1830+ scenarios, "Once the first 6-train is bought, 7-trains and diesels are available" — they still trigger no phase.
- Gray tile timing is also wrong in the table: source 3.6 says gray tiles become playable in Phase Six (first 6-train), not at a 7-train phase. The summary even contradicts itself — its Actions section correctly says "Grey tiles: Upgrade brown tiles (Phase 6+)".

### 3. Private company sale price to corporations is backwards (CONTRADICTED)
- Summary (Actions): "Private companies can be sold to corporations at face value or less."
- Source 4.1: "The price paid may not be less than half or more than twice the face value of the company and must be publicly declared."
- Selling a private to your own corporation at up to double face value is a core 1830 money-extraction tactic; the summary forbids exactly what the rules allow.

## Minor Findings

- Stock Round turn order: summary gives Sell then Buy; source (2.0, 6.0, C-1.2) specifies Sell-Buy-Sell (you may sell again after buying).
- Final wealth: summary says cash + share value only; source 8.0 also adds "the face value of any owned private companies still in existence" (rare edge case — only if game ends before a 5-train is bought).
- Forced train purchase applies only "if a railroad with a legal train route connecting at least 2 cities has no train" (7.6.2); summary's "A corporation must own at least 1 train" drops the legal-route condition (source note: "If a railroad does not have a legal train route to run, it need not own or buy a train").
- Omission: corporation flotation/capitalization — corporations float when 60% of shares are bought and receive 10x par value as initial capital (6.3). This core economic system is absent from the summary.
- Omission: per-phase train ownership limits (4 trains in phases 2-3, 3 in phase 4, 2 from phase 5 on; 7.6.1) — the summary's phase table has no train-limit info.
- Frontmatter `play_time: 180-360 min` vs rulebook "3 to 5 hours" — likely BGG metadata, not treated as an error.

## Conclusion

Component counts, starting cash, terrain costs, train obsolescence, and operating-round progression are accurate. However, the summary contains one invented rule (corporation closure on failed train purchase — actually receivership/bankruptcy), an invented phase structure (7-train phase and "Phase D", with gray tiles delayed a phase), and a contradicted private-company sale rule (face value or less vs half-to-double face value). These would mislead players at the table: MAJOR.
