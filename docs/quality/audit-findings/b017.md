# Audit b017: android-netrunner

**Files compared:**
- Summary: `rules/android-netrunner.md`
- Source: `extracted/android-netrunner-rules.txt` (full FFG core set rulebook, 2813 lines; OCR quality good, symbols rendered as `[` = click, `<` = credit)

**Verdict: MAJOR**

All spot-checked numbers are accurate and no invented mechanics or contradictions were found. The verdict is driven by omissions of core systems that would mislead or block play at the table: the agenda scoring procedure (advancement requirement), the Discard Phase / maximum hand size for both players, and the trace subsystem.

## Invented mechanics / contradictions

None found. Every action, cost, and rule in the summary's Turn Structure, Actions, and Making a Run sections has direct support in the source.

## Major omissions (basis for verdict)

1. **Agenda scoring procedure entirely missing.** The summary lists "Advance a card" as an action and says the Corp wins by "scoring 7 agenda points," but never explains *how* an agenda is scored: each agenda has an advancement requirement, and "When the number of advancement tokens on an agenda is equal to or higher than its advancement requirement... the Corporation can score it... right before his turn begins, or after he completes an action. Scoring an agenda does not cost a click" (source lines 891-901). A Corp player using only this summary cannot execute their primary win path.
2. **Discard Phase missing from both turns.** Both turns end with a Discard Phase ("The Corporation discards down to his maximum hand size," lines 716-717, 924-935; Runner equivalent, lines 954-955, 1026-1032). The summary presents the Corp turn as draw + 3 clicks and the Runner turn as 4 clicks only, and never states the maximum hand size of 5 (lines 328, 355, 925-926). This is an entire phase of the turn structure, and it also underpins flatline-by-hand-size.
3. **Trace mechanic entirely missing.** Traces (TraceX vs. link strength, open credit spending by both sides, tie favors the Runner) are a core subsystem with a dedicated rulebook section (lines 1374-1397) referenced by many cards and ice subroutines. The summary never mentions traces or link.

## Minor omissions / drift (would not alone change verdict)

- **Flatline definition incomplete:** source also flatlines the Runner if "he has a maximum hand size of less than zero at the end of his turn" (lines 1428-1431); summary gives only the damage-exceeds-grip case.
- **Per-server access rules omitted:** R&D = top card, HQ = one random card, Archives = all cards, remote = all cards (lines 1213-1226). Summary says only "access cards in the server."
- **Jack out and bad publicity run credits omitted:** Runner may jack out at each approach except the first (lines 1135-1139); Runner gains 1 credit per Corp bad publicity at the start of each run (lines 1117-1118, 1151-1163). Bad publicity tokens are listed in Components but the mechanic is never explained.
- **Damage wording drift:** summary says damage "discards" cards; source says the Runner randomly "trashes" cards (lines 1416-1421), and the rulebook explicitly distinguishes trashing from discarding (lines 943-947). Could matter for prevention effects.
- **Unique cards:** summary says only 1 copy "installed" at a time; source says "active" at a time, with the newer copy trashing the older (lines 689-695).
- **Component counts omitted:** source itemizes 134 Corp cards, 114 Runner cards, 51 credit/advancement tokens, 8 five-credit tokens, 6 brain damage, 12 bad publicity/tag, 23 generic tokens (lines 139-186); the summary lists types only, with no counts (no wrong numbers, just absent).
- **"Must spend all clicks" rule omitted** (lines 707-708).
- Deckbuilding rules (3-copy limit, influence, agenda point density requirements, lines 1662-1700) are omitted entirely, defensible for a play summary.

## Conclusion

The summary is numerically accurate everywhere it commits to a number (14/14 checks pass) and invents nothing, but it omits the agenda scoring procedure (the Corp's win mechanism), the Discard Phase / hand-size limit of both turns, and the trace system. Under the rubric (major omissions = entire phases, scoring sources, core systems), this is **MAJOR**.
