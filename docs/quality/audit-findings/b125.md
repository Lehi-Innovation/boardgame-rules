## knights-of-camelot

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Basic Scenario VL threshold wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "First Player Knight to reach minimum Wound Level 10 and Virtue Level **4 or lower**, then successfully petition King Arthur to join the Round Table."

Source (line 3747–3749): "The purpose of the Basic Scenario is to become an Arthurian Knight. Before seeking the court of King Arthur, a PK must gain, through adventure, a total of 100 or more CP and reduce his VL to **5 or less**."

The VL threshold is wrong: summary says VL 4 or lower; source says VL 5 or less. A player following the summary would play 20–33% longer to lower VL needlessly.

Note: WL 10 = 100 CP is technically equivalent (WL = CP/10), so that part is correct. Only the VL threshold is wrong.

### Finding 2 — Advanced Scenario win condition fundamentally misidentified (MAJOR)

Summary: "**Advanced Scenario**: Join the Round Table, then be granted and complete the Quest for the Holy Grail."

Source (lines 3772–3775): "Advanced Scenario — Start: Each PK begins the game with 40 CP and 4 VP. The purpose of the scenario is again to be the first PK to become an Arthurian Knight. Each PK should follow the same steps to become an Arthurian Knight outlined in the Basic Scenario."

The Holy Grail quest is a completely separate, standalone scenario ("Quest of the Holy Grail", lines 3776–3819) — not part of the Advanced Scenario. The summary has merged two distinct scenarios into one. Players using the summary would not know how to play either scenario correctly.

### Finding 3 — Fabricated designer credits (MAJOR)

Summary frontmatter: `designer: "Tom Dalgliesh, Ron Gibson"`

Source (lines 4241–4242): "ORIGINAL CREDITS — DESIGN: Glenn & Kenneth Rahman"

"Tom Dalgliesh" and "Ron Gibson" do not appear anywhere in the extracted rulebook. These names were fabricated (possibly from another game). This meets the "Fabricated metadata" pattern listed in the SKILL.

### Finding 4 — Turn Structure omits Luck phase and Prayers & Requests phase (MAJOR)

Summary Turn Structure lists 4 steps:
1. Movement
2. Encounter Roll
3. Resolve Encounter
4. End of Turn: Check Comrade Retention / Heal Wounds

Source (lines 1154–1175, Player Turn Sequence) lists 5 distinct phases:
1. Luck (roll Luck-of-the-Day)
2. Movement & Encounters
3. Comrade & Companion Retention
4. Prayers & Requests
5. Healing

The first phase — rolling the Luck-of-the-Day — is entirely absent from the summary's turn description. The "Prayers & Requests" phase (phase 4) is also missing from the Turn Structure, even though it is the phase where Holy Grail questing and king petitioning occur. A table of players following the summary's turn sequence would not know when to roll Luck-of-the-Day or when to make Prayers & Requests.
