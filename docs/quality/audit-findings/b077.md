## duck-duck-go

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Buoy collection: passing through DOES count (contradicted rule)

Summary: "You must end on the buoy space -- passing through does not count."

Source: "The first time your ducky passes over or ends its movement on any one of the three target buoy spaces, take a matching buoy token and put it in front of you." (extracted line 122-123)

The source explicitly allows collecting a buoy token by passing over the space, not only by ending movement there. The summary's prohibition on pass-through collection is directly contradicted by the rulebook.

Note: The bonus movement (play another card) does require ending on the buoy space — "If your ducky finishes its movement on a buoy space, you get to keep going!" (line 124) — but token collection does not. The summary conflates the two rules and incorrectly restricts buoy collection to ending-movement-only.

### Procedural claims verified (5+)

1. Turn order: "lowest numbered card moves first" — source line 52-53: "The number on each card is the order in which duckies move, from lowest to highest." MATCH.
2. Hand size: "draw back to 3 cards" — source line 79: "Players then draw enough cards to refill their hand back up to three cards." MATCH.
3. Collision: "ducky stops in last valid space, remaining movement lost" — source line 110-114: "the move ends immediately. Leave your ducky in the space it was in when it bonked." MATCH.
4. Win condition headline: "first to touch all 3 buoys and reach drain" — source line 143-145: "The first ducky to reach the drain space after touching all three buoy spaces wins." MATCH.
5. Reveal: "simultaneously flip and reveal" — source line 71-72: "simultaneously flip and reveal them." MATCH.

### Victory condition verified

Source line 143-145: "The first ducky to reach the drain space after touching all three buoy spaces wins. It is not necessary that your ducky end its turn on the drain space – the game ends as soon as the ducky moves into the space." Summary: "The first ducky to touch all 3 buoys (collect all 3 buoy tokens) AND reach the finish drain wins." MATCH on win condition headline — but the buoy-collection mechanic error (Finding 1) undermines correct play.

## dune

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — 2-3 player stronghold requirement (contradicted rule)

Summary: "2-3 Player Games: Need 4 strongholds to win."

Source: "For a three player game...an Alliance needs 4 strongholds to win, as in the standard Alliance rules. For a two player game...the number of strongholds needed to win is increased to 4." (extracted lines 399-407)

For a 3-player game, only the Alliance win condition requires 4 strongholds — which is the same as the standard Alliance rule. A solo player in a 3-player game still wins with 3 strongholds. The summary incorrectly implies all wins (solo and alliance) in 3-player games require 4 strongholds.

### Finding 2 — Faction alternate win conditions mischaracterized as "Advanced Game only"

Summary: "Turn Limit: If no one wins by end of turn 10, the game ends (no winner, or apply faction-specific alternate win conditions in the Advanced Game)."

Source (faction rules section, standard game):
- "SPACING GUILD SPECIAL VICTORY CONDITION: If no faction has been able to win the game by the end of play, you have prevented control of Dune and automatically win the game." (line 1428-1431)
- "FREMEN SPECIAL VICTORY CONDITION: If no faction has won by the end of the last turn and if you (or no one) occupies Sietch Tabr and Habbanya Sietch and neither Harkonnen, Atreides nor Emperor occupies Tuek's Sietch, your plans to alter Dune have succeeded and you and any allies win the game." (lines 1214-1219)
- "BENE GESSERIT PREDICTION: If the faction you predicted wins...in the turn you predicted, reveal your prediction and win alone." (lines 1350-1353)

These faction special victory conditions appear in the standard faction rules, not in the "Advanced Game" section (which starts at line 970 and covers only double spice blow, advanced combat, income, and enhanced Karama). The summary's claim that alternate win conditions only apply "in the Advanced Game" is wrong — the Spacing Guild automatically wins if they are in play and no one else wins by the end of turn 10.

### Victory condition main text

Source line 396-397: "The winner is the First Player to occupy at least 3 strongholds with at least one of their forces during the Mentat Pause Phase." Summary: "Standard Win: Occupy 3 strongholds with at least 1 force during the Mentat Pause Phase." MATCH.

Source line 867-868: "No more than two players may be in an Alliance and the win condition is now 4 instead of 3 strongholds." Summary: "Alliance Win: Allied players jointly occupy 4 strongholds." MATCH.

### Procedural claims verified

Subsequent turns: source lines 430-433 describe First Storm, but for dial range 1-3 subsequent turns: this is verified in the Quick Start Guide section. MATCH.
2. CHOAM Charity — "any player with 0 or 1 spice may claim charity to bring total to 2" — Source line 509-510: "Any player with 0 or 1 spice can collect spice to bring their total to 2." MATCH.
3. Bidding — "1 card per eligible bidder face-down...highest bidder pays to Spice Bank" — Source line 506-507: "deals cards from the Treachery Deck face down in a row, 1 card for each player who is allowed to bid." Source line 534-535: "pays the number of spice they bid into the Spice Bank." MATCH.
4. Revival — "up to 3 forces/turn, additional at 2 spice each" — Source lines 601-607: "revived must be done at a cost of 2 spice per force...A player can never revive more than 3 forces per turn." MATCH.
5. Spice harvest — "3 spice per force if occupying Arrakeen or Carthag, 2 spice per force otherwise" — Source lines 837-839: "collection rate is 3 spice per force if the player occupies Carthag or Arrakeen. It is 2 spice per force if the player does not occupy Carthag or Arrakeen." MATCH.

### Tiebreakers

No tiebreaker rules found for standard game — not in the summary either. N/A.

### Exhaustion rules

## dune-imperium

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Tiebreaker: "troops in supply" vs. "garrisoned troops" (wrong number/concept)

Summary: "ties broken by most spice, then most Solari, then most water, then most troops in supply."

Source line 681-683: "If the case of a tie, tiebreakers are, in order: amount of spice, Solari, water, and garrisoned troops."

"Troops in supply" and "garrisoned troops" are distinct game concepts. Supply troops have not yet been recruited/placed (they sit in reserve). Garrisoned troops are on the game board in the garrison area waiting to be deployed to Combat. Source line 458-459: "Take a troop from your supply and place it in your garrison on the game board." The tiebreaker favors players who have deployed and accumulated troops on the board, not players who held back reserves. The summary's error would produce incorrect tiebreaker decisions at game end.

### Finding 2 — Invented mechanic: troop deployment during Reveal turn

Summary: "Reveal Turn: Reveal all remaining cards in hand. Gain their Reveal box effects (typically Persuasion to buy cards, and swords for Combat strength). Deploy troops from garrison to Combat. You may buy cards from the Imperium Row or Reserve using Persuasion."

Source: No passage supports deploying troops from garrison to Combat during a Reveal turn. All troop deployment from garrison to Combat is tied to Agent turns on Combat spaces:
- Lines 462-476: "You may deploy them to a Conflict when you send an Agent to a Combat space...you may deploy any or all troops recruited during your current turn...plus up to two more troops from your garrison."
- Lines 577-591: The Reveal Turn section covers only Acquiring Cards (Persuasion spending). No deployment step.
- Line 555-560: "Set Strength" counts troops already in the Conflict area; it does not allow deploying troops.

The summary inserts a troop-deployment step into the Reveal turn that does not exist in the source. Players following this would deploy garrison troops during Reveal turns, bypassing the requirement to visit a Combat board space with an Agent.

### Victory condition

Summary: "Game End: At the end of a round, if any player has 10+ VP or the Conflict Deck is empty, the game ends."
Source line 254-255: "if any player has reached 10 or more Victory Points (or if the Conflict Deck is empty), the game ends, and whoever has the most Victory Points wins." MATCH.

### Procedural claims verified

1. Round Start: "Reveal top Conflict card; each player draws 5 cards." Source lines 326-329: "Each round begins by revealing a new Conflict card...Next, each player draws five cards." MATCH.
2. Faction Influence: "1 VP at 2 Influence, Alliance token at 4 Influence." Source line 300: "When you reach 2 Influence with a Faction, you gain a Victory Point." Source line 305: "The first player to reach 4 Influence...earns an Alliance with that Faction." MATCH.
3. Conflict Deck setup: "4 Conflict III (all) on bottom, 5 random Conflict II in middle, 1 random Conflict I on top." Source lines 158-163: "Shuffle the four Conflict III cards and place all of them face down...Shuffle the 10 Conflict II cards, then deal five face down on top...Shuffle the four Conflict I cards, then deal one face down on top." MATCH.
4. Score marker starting position: "4-player: start at 1; otherwise: 0." Source line 226-227: "In a 4-player game, place it on the 1 space. Otherwise, place it on the 0 space." MATCH.
5. Makers phase: "Place spice bonus tokens on unoccupied Maker board spaces." Source lines 668-670: "Check each of the three board spaces with a Maker icon...If the space does not have an Agent, place 1 spice from the bank on that space." MATCH.

### Exhaustion rules

Summary does not state a reshuffle rule. Source line 266-267: "any time you're unable to draw a card because your deck is empty, you reshuffle your discard pile to form a new deck, then continue to draw as needed." The summary omits this; it does not invent a false rule. MINOR omission only.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 10 (VP thresholds, Influence thresholds, card counts, score start, draw count, Conflict Deck construction), SUPPORTED: 8; 2 errors documented above.
