# Audit b055: civilization

**Files compared:** `rules/civilization.md` vs `extracted/civilization-rules.txt`
**Verdict: MAJOR**

The summary gets the high-level shape right (5 phases in correct order, 4 victory conditions with correct thresholds), but it contains several invented mechanics, a wrong core number (four tech levels instead of five), conflates the game's central units-vs-figures distinction, and omits an entire city action (Harvest a Resource) and the Building New Cities mechanic.

## Major findings

### 1. Wrong number: "Four technology levels" (source: five)
- Summary (Actions > Research): "Four technology levels unlock progressively powerful abilities."
- Source (glossary): "Tech Pyramid: Techs are organized in the tech pyramid into five different levels." Tech pyramid section: "Level I ... up to level V"; "The highest level tech is Space Flight, at level V."
- The summary even contradicts itself — its own victory table says "Research a Level 5 technology (Space Flight)."

### 2. Invented mechanic: producing a unit places a figure on the map
- Summary (Actions): "**Build Unit:** Place a military unit figure in your city's outskirts." Components: "Unit figures (infantry, cavalry, artillery, aircraft)."
- Source: "To produce a unit ... the player draws a unit card at random from the appropriate deck and adds it to their standing forces pile." Units are square cards with "no physical location on the map" (Armies vs. Units sidebar). Only **figures** (armies cost 4 production, scouts cost 6) are placed in the outskirts when produced. The rulebook devotes a sidebar to exactly this distinction; the summary erases it and would directly mislead at the table.

### 3. Omitted city action: Harvest a Resource (and producing figures)
- Summary lists the city action choices as "build a building, build a unit, build a wonder, or devote to the arts."
- Source lists three action types: "A. Produce a Figure, Unit, Building, or Wonder; B. Devote to the Arts; C. Harvest a Resource." Resource harvesting feeds the resource-ability system on tech cards ("Resources are used to power resource abilities found on tech cards") — an entire core system missing from the summary.

### 4. Invented mechanic: resources "needed for certain buildings and wonders"
- Summary (Special Rules): "Resources on the map provide trade goods needed for certain buildings and wonders."
- Source: buildings and wonders cost **production** only; resources are spent solely to activate resource abilities on tech cards (e.g., Currency: spend one incense for three culture). Wonder discounts come from knowing a tech (Colossus: 15 production, or 10 with Metalworking), not from resources.

### 5. Invented mechanic: city size limits buildings/units
- Summary (Special Rules): "A city can support a limited number of buildings and units based on its size."
- Source: "Normally, any number of a given type of building may be built in a city as long as suitable terrain exists," with the exception of limited buildings (star-marked, one per city) and wonders (one per city). There is no city-size mechanic, and units are not kept in cities.

### 6. Invented mechanic: villages "may attack players"
- Summary (Special Rules): "Barbarian villages appear on explored tiles and may attack players."
- Source: villages are static facedown markers; a battle only occurs when a player's army **enters** a village square, with the player to the left running three rank-1 barbarian units as defender. Villages never attack.

### 7. Omission: Building New Cities and Changing Governments in Start of Turn
- Summary's Phase 1 says only "Activate start-of-turn abilities."
- Source: the Start of Turn Phase is where players build new cities (sacrifice a scout, 5 placement restrictions, max 2 cities / 3 with Irrigation) and change governments (including the Anarchy transition rule). The summary never explains how new cities are built anywhere — a core expansion mechanic is missing.

## Minor findings

- Turn framing: "On your turn, perform these phases" — source runs each phase for all players (first player then clockwise) before the next phase begins; Trade and Research are simultaneous.
- "Determine starting player (varies by civilization)" — invented; source: "Choose the player who can trace their family history back the farthest, or select at random."
- "Great Persons (from culture cards)" — great people are markers gained from great-people spaces on the culture track (or villages), distinct from the 47 culture event cards.
- "Dial trackers (trade, culture, coins)" — there are only trade and economy (coin) dials; culture is tracked by a marker on the culture track.
- "Scouts explore face-down map tiles" — any figure (the source's example uses an army) can discover a facedown tile by spending one movement; scouts cannot enter hut/village squares, only armies explore those.
- "Build Wonder: Begin constructing..." implies multi-turn construction; wonders are produced in a single city action paying full cost.
- "cavalry" — source calls the type "mounted."
- General: the summary drops nearly all exact numbers (army 4 / scout 6 production; unit costs 5/7/9/11, aircraft 12; tech trade thresholds 6/11/16/21/26; travel speed 2; stacking limit 2; battle hand size 3; wall +4; capital defense +12; max 27 trade; loot of up to 3 trade/3 culture/1 resource).
