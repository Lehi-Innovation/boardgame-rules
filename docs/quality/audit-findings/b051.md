## chandragupta — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — The summary reads like generic GBoH boilerplate rather than this rulebook: the turn sequence is misordered (Rout/Rally placed first; in the source it is Phase D, after all leader activations), a nonexistent component ("Chaos cards") is listed for a game that contains no cards at all, two special rules are invented ("Supply and fatigue rules apply in longer scenarios"; "Simple GBoH" alternative), and the elephant rule directly contradicts the source ("if they rout" vs. "Elephants do not rout, but Rampage"). The core TQ/Cohesion-Hit system — which the source calls "by far, the most important number a combat unit has" — is never mentioned.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Turn structure misordered and partially invented.**
   - Summary (Turn Structure, repeated in Player Reference): "1. Rout and Rally Phase ... 2. Command Phase ... 3. Movement and Combat ... 4. End Phase" / "Turn phases: Rout/Rally > Command > Movement/Combat > End".
   - Source (3.0, l.468-535): A. Leader Activation Phase → B. Orders Phase (Movement & Missile Fire Segment, then Shock Combat Segment) → C. Momentum Phase OR return to A → D. Rout and Recovery Phase → E. Camp Orders Phase → F. Withdrawal Phase. "After all leaders have been activated and are 'Finished,' each player checks for Rout and Recovery and Army Withdrawal, after which the game turn is concluded" (l.473-475).
   - Impact: Rout/Recovery happens at the END of the turn, not the start; the summary also flattens the activation-by-activation interleaving (each leader moves AND fights during his own Orders Phase) into separate game-wide "Command" and "Movement and Combat" phases, and omits the Camp Orders Phase entirely. A player following the summary would run the turn in the wrong order.

2. **MAJOR — Invented component: "Chaos cards."**
   - Summary (Components): "Chaos cards".
   - Source component list (2.0, l.105-113): "2 22"x34" map sheets (back-printed); 1 22"x17" map sheet; 3 counter sheets (840 counters); 1 Rules Book; 1 Scenario Book; 4 player aid cards; 1 ten-sided die; plastic counter storage bags." There are no cards of any kind in the game, and "Chaos" appears nowhere in the source.

3. **MAJOR — Invented special rules.**
   - Summary: "Supply and fatigue rules apply in longer scenarios." — No fatigue rules exist anywhere in the source; "supply" appears only as Missile Supply (8.19, l.1906: units run low/out of missiles), which has nothing to do with scenario length. Player Reference also lists "fatigue" as a combat modifier — unsupported.
   - Summary: "The Simple GBoH rules provide a streamlined alternative for faster play." — "Simple GBoH" is never mentioned in the rulebook (it is a separate GMT product). No streamlined variant exists in the source; the actual optional rules (14.0) are Dharmayuddha and Random Selection of Guilds/Tribals, both unmentioned.

4. **MODERATE — Elephant rule contradicted.**
   - Summary: "Elephants can cause panic among friendly units if they rout." (and unit table: "can panic").
   - Source: "Elephants do not rout, but Rampage (10.14) instead" (11.21 Exception #2, l.2845-2846). The Rampage mechanic (10.14, l.2461-2511) has the elephant itself charge 3 hexes per directional die roll, inflicting 1-2 Cohesion Hits on any unit in its path, friend or foe, and killing leaders outright — there are no "panic" checks taken by friendly units. The gist (a broken elephant endangers your own troops) is real, but the stated trigger ("if they rout") contradicts an explicit rule and the mechanic is misnamed.

5. **MODERATE — Core combat system mischaracterized; TQ/Cohesion omitted.**
   - Summary: "Combat results based on differential between attack and defense values modified by terrain, facing, and unit condition."
   - Source: units have no attack/defense values — they are rated for Size, Troop Quality (TQ), and Movement (2.21, l.131-134). Shock is resolved by determining the CRT column via the Clash of Spears and Swords Chart (weapon-system matchup) plus Position/Weapon Superiority (9.16-9.17, l.2051-2181), and results are Cohesion Hits compared against TQ ("TQ Rating is, by far, the most important number a combat unit has", l.324-327; "a unit Routs when hits >= TQ", l.2841-2842). The summary never mentions TQ or Cohesion Hits at all.

6. **MINOR — Component list drift.** "Dice" (plural) vs. "1 ten-sided die" (l.112, l.269); "Rules booklet and playbook" plus a separate "Scenario booklets" line vs. exactly one Rules Book and one Scenario Book — there is no playbook.

7. **MINOR — Unsupported Sanskrit unit names.** The four-fold table gives "Patti / Rathin / Hasti / Ashva"; only "Hasti" appears in the source (10.1, l.2395). "Chaturangabala" also never appears (source says "four-fold" division, l.35-36). External-knowledge flavor, not a rules error, but the source's own unit taxonomy (Types/Classes plus the Indian Maula/Bhrta/Sreni/Tribal Grades, 2.22 — central to command and combat effectiveness) is omitted from the summary.

8. **MINOR — Victory conditions drift.** Summary lists "Control of key terrain objectives" and "Elimination of enemy leaders" as typical victory conditions. Source 13.11 (l.3356-3362): a player wins solely by causing the opponent's army to Withdraw (accumulated Rout Points >= the scenario's Withdrawal Level); killed leaders merely add RPs (13.12), and terrain control becomes a victory condition only under the optional Retire to Camp rule (12.4x, l.3342-3345). "Rout points" and "scenario specifies thresholds" are supported.

9. **MINOR — Hex scale precision.** Summary: "Each hex represents 70 yards"; source: "approximately 60–70 paces (yards)" (l.459). Turn length 15-20 minutes matches (l.460).

### Mechanics check
- Supported: leader-driven activation ("Combat units may not move or fire missiles without getting an Individual Order or a Line or Wing Command from a leader", l.537-538 — matches the summary's "Leaders drive everything"); Momentum and Trump; missile vs. shock combat split; morale-style checks after combat (TQ checks); chariots fragile in terrain (10.21, l.2561-2565: cohesion hits for entering certain terrain) and routed chariots eliminated (10.24); terrain affecting movement and combat; scenario-based setup.
- Not supported / wrong: Chaos cards; turn-phase order; "Simple GBoH" variant; supply/fatigue rules; fatigue as a combat modifier; elephants "routing"; attack/defense-value differential combat.
- Major omissions: TQ and Cohesion Hits (the game's central currency); the Grade system (Maula/Bhrta/Sreni/Tribal) that the source calls "important in terms of command and combat effectiveness" (l.156-159); Line/Wing/Nayaka command structure (the game's signature addition); ZOCs and facing; Camp Orders/fortification rules; Dharmayuddha optional rule.
