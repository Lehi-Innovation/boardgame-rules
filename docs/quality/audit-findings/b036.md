Audited 2026-06-11. Game: `rules/bloody-april-the-battle-of-shiloh-1862.md` compared against `extracted/bloody-april-the-battle-of-shiloh-1862-rules.txt`.

## bloody-april-the-battle-of-shiloh-1862 — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — The summary weaves an invented core system ("Brigade Combat Effectiveness") through five sections, misstates the melee resolution mechanism (odds-based CRT vs. the source's Melee Differential), misrepresents the Sequence of Play (3 generic phases instead of the actual 9-phase player-turn; claims the second player-turn runs "phases in reverse" when the source says the same order; omits Defensive Fire), and gets the turn length wrong (30 min vs. the source's 20 min daylight / 1 hour night). Several component claims are unsupported.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented system: "Brigade Combat Effectiveness" (BCE).**
   - Summary: appears in five places — Overview ("tracks... Brigade Combat Effectiveness"), Components ("Brigade Combat Effectiveness Track"), Turn Structure ("Command Phase: Check Brigade Combat Effectiveness"), Actions ("Brigade Combat Effectiveness tracks overall brigade morale"), and a whole Special Rules bullet ("Each brigade has a BCE rating that degrades as its units take losses. When BCE drops below threshold levels, all units in the brigade suffer penalties...").
   - Source: the phrase never appears. The rules outline (l.78-248) has no such section, and every occurrence of "Effectiveness" refers to officers' Effectiveness Radii used for command control ("Leaders have Effectiveness Radii, Rally Ratings and Command Ratings", l.602-605; Initial Command Phase "checks the Effectiveness Radii of all his officers to see whether the infantry units in that officer's command may move and fire", l.718-723). Unit-level degradation is handled by Morale and Rout (13.0), Stragglers (15.0), and Battle Fatigue (19.0) — there is no brigade rating, no track, and no threshold mechanic.
   - Impact: players would look for a nonexistent track and skip the real command-radius system the summary never explains.

2. **MAJOR — Sequence of Play misrepresented.**
   - Summary: each player-turn = "A. Command Phase / B. Movement Phase / C. Combat Phase", and "2. Second Player-Turn: Same phases in reverse."
   - Source [4.0] (l.691-784) and the Abbreviated Sequence (l.7960-7980): A. Union Alert Phase, B. Picket and Patrol Phase, C. Initial Command Phase, D. Movement Phase, E. Defensive Fire Phase (the **non-phasing** player fires first), F. Offensive Fire Phase, G. Retreat Before Melee Phase, H. Melee Phase, I. Final Command Phase. The second player "now becomes the Phasing Player and proceeds to follow the Sequence, Phases B through I" (l.780-784) — the **same order**, not reversed.
   - Specific errors: (a) "phases in reverse" is contradicted; (b) Defensive Fire — a core TSS-system mechanic where the defender fires before the attacker — is omitted entirely; (c) Retreat Before Melee (a named phase with its own rules, 12.8) is omitted; (d) straggler recovery, rally, and resupply are assigned to the (initial) "Command Phase" when the source puts them in the Final Command Phase ("may attempt to rally... round up stragglers and resupply units needing ammunition", l.775-778); (e) "check for special events" in the Game-Turn Record Interphase is unsupported — the interphase only advances the marker (l.785-789).

3. **MAJOR — Melee resolution mechanism contradicted.**
   - Summary: "Melee is resolved using odds-based CRT with modifiers..."
   - Source: melee uses a **differential**, not odds — Case 12.6 is "Determining the Melee Differential" (l.139) and the Sequence of Play states "The Melee Differential is determined and any retreats and/or casualties are taken as a result of such melee" (l.772-774).

4. **MAJOR (wrong number) — Game-turn length.**
   - Summary: "each game turn represents roughly 30 minutes."
   - Source: "The Game-Turn Marker(s) should be advanced one space (20 minutes for daylight turns, one hour for night turns)" (l.786-788).

5. **Wrong number (probable, OCR caveat) — Hex scale.**
   - Summary: "Each hex represents approximately 200 yards."
   - Source [2.5] GAME SCALE: "Each hexagon on the map represents approximately 1lJ yards of actual terrain" (l.608-610). The OCR glyphs ("1lJ") are garbled but begin with "11"/"1l" — consistent with ~110-115 yards and inconsistent with "200". Reported as probable error rather than certain due to OCR. (The next sentence is clean: each infantry/cavalry strength point = approximately 100 effective men, l.610-612 — not mentioned in the summary.)

6. **Unsupported components — invented or not in source.**
   - "36-page rules booklet": no page count appears anywhere in the source; the only "36" is "a complete game of Bloody April will take anywhere from 36 to 60 hours" (l.794-796) — a likely hallucination seed. (That play-length statement also dwarfs the frontmatter's 180-480 min, though frontmatter is BGG metadata.)
   - "Two dice": the inventory [2.6] (l.614-622) lists six 200-piece countersheets (1200 counters total), one historical situation briefing booklet, two identical charts & tables booklets, two roster sheet booklets, two game-map sections, one game box — no dice (typical for SPI). The game does use one- and two-dice rolls (l.1098, 3181, 3494), so this is a minor invention.
   - "Morale Table, Fatigue Table, Brigade Combat Effectiveness Track" in the charts list: the source's chart list [2.2] (l.285-296) is Fire and Melee Combat Results Tables, Terrain Effects Chart, Range Effects Chart, Stacking Restriction Chart, Density/Fire Adjustment Chart, Straggler Table, Leader Casualty Table, Fire Coordination Table — plus on-map Turn Record Track and Casualty/Captured Tracks. No Morale Table, no Fatigue Table, no BCE Track. (Straggler Table and CRTs in the summary are supported.)

7. **MODERATE — Invented artillery ammunition-type mechanic.**
   - Summary: "Different ammunition types (shot, shell, canister) have different effects at different ranges."
   - Source: range-varying effects come from **weapon types**, not ammunition choice — [9.2] (l.1782-1831) lists types (Rifled Muskets, Muskets, Carbines, Pistols, Henry Repeating Rifles, Six-Pounder Smoothbores, 12-pdr Napoleons, rifled guns, Howitzers, Parrotts, Siege Guns, 8" Naval Shell Guns) whose strength multiplier varies by range on the Range Effects Charts. "Canister" never appears; the Ammunition rules (11.0) cover only supply, depletion, and supply wagons. The summary's "fire arcs, ranges, and ammunition limits" and wagon tracking are supported; the shot/shell/canister selection is not.

8. **MINOR — Victory conditions: mostly right, one embellishment.**
   - Summary's casualties + Pittsburg Landing possession framing matches [30.1]: "levels of Victory are usually measured by two items: losses and physical possession of or proximity to [Pittsburg Landing]" (l.6721-6726); Confederate Total Victory = "No Union combat units remaining west of the Tennessee" (l.6806-6813) supports "driving the Union into the Tennessee River". "Specific victory point schedules are provided for each scenario" is fair ([30.2] gives a separate schedule for the Confederate Attack scenario).
   - But "control of... the road network" is unsupported: the standard conditions use the Landing hexes, Strength/Gun Points within 10 hexes, and Casualty Points (l.6806-6844); the Confederate Attack scenario scores control of **named fields** (Review Field, Barnes Field, etc., l.6855-6865), not roads.

9. **MINOR — Omitted systems.** Zones of Control (10.0, incl. withdrawal fire — which the sequence invokes), Stacking (8.0, incl. density effects on fire), Special Cavalry Rules (17.0, incl. cavalry charge and retreat before melee), Fire Coordination (24.0), and the Pinned/Prone states are absent from the summary. For a game whose rules outline runs 33 sections, some compression is expected, but ZOCs and stacking are core to play.

Pattern note: the summary reads like a plausible reconstruction of "a TSS-family Shiloh game" from general knowledge rather than from this rulebook — generic 3-phase turn structure, an invented brigade-effectiveness system (a mechanic from later GBACW-era designs), odds-based melee, and round-number scales (200 yards, 30 minutes) that the source contradicts. Future audits of old SPI/wargame titles should specifically verify the Sequence of Play outline and game-scale case against the extracted text, since these are dense, OCR-survivable, and frequently hallucinated.
