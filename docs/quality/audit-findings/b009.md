# Audit Findings — Batch b009

Audited: a-study-in-emerald, abba-monopoly, abyss, accasta, acquire, ad-astra, adaptoid
Method: compared `rules/<slug>.md` against `extracted/<slug>-rules.txt`. Spot-checked 10+ numbers per game (component counts, costs, VP values, thresholds), checked Turn Structure/Actions claims for source support, and looked for contradictions and missing phases/scoring sources.

## a-study-in-emerald — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (all supported)
9 City cards, 9 Royalty cards, 66 Game cards, 6 Secret Identity cards, 5 player sets, 10 Agents, 10 Influence cubes, 1 VP disc, 10 Starting cards per player, 6 Zombie pieces, 1 Sanity die, 15 Sanity tokens, 2 Track markers, cards per city (3 for 2-3p / 4 for 4p / 5 for 5p), 5 cubes to Limbo + 5 available, 3 Sanity tokens per player, 2 starting agents, hand of 5, 2 actions per turn — all match the source. Component/setup numbers are clean; the problems are in mechanics and scoring.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Primary game-end trigger (VP target) omitted entirely.** Source: "The game ends when one of the following conditions has been met: A player reaches or exceeds a set number of victory points, which are: two players: 28 ... three players: 24 ... four players: 22 ... five players: 20". The summary's "Game End Triggers" lists only sanity loss, track end, and "specific card effects" — the score-target end condition (and all four thresholds) is missing.

2. **MAJOR — Wrong game-end rule for sanity loss.** Summary: "A player loses all 3 sanity tokens (goes insane) -- game ends immediately." Source: losing all sanity tokens (or all agents) only forces a reveal; "If you reveal your identity to be that of a Loyalist: You immediately add Agent pieces to the board to bring your total up to three... The game then continues as normal. If you reveal your identity to be that of a Restorationist: The game ends immediately." The game ends only when a *Restorationist* is forced to reveal. The summary also omits the agent-loss reveal trigger.

3. **MAJOR — Final scoring penalty contradicted.** Summary: "The player with the **lowest VP on each side** applies a penalty to all players on that side" (no amount given). Source: "look at which player has the lowest victory point score. All players on the same side (Loyalist or Restorationist) lose five victory points. In the case of a tie for the lowest score then a Loyalist player will be regarded as having a lower score." It is the single overall-lowest player, penalizing only that one side, and the penalty is a specific −5 VP — not a per-side penalty as summarized.

4. **MAJOR — Invented mechanic: assassination advances the Restorationist track.** Summary ("Assassination"): "Successful assassination: Restorationist track advances; Royalty card removed." Source procedure for assassinating Royalty: take the Royalty card, place it near your Secret Identity card, roll the Sanity die, remove cubes to Limbo (worked example: "he places the Royalty card near his Secret Identity card and increases his victory point total by four"). No track movement occurs on assassination anywhere in the source; tracks move only via the Move Markers action and card effects (e.g., Sigmund Freud, Matvei Golovinski).

5. **MAJOR — Agent movement restriction invented.** Summary: "Move Agents: Play movement cards. Move agents between connected cities." Source: "you can move one of your Agent pieces from one City space to any other City space on the board"; play aid: "move one agent from any city to any other city." There is no adjacency/connection restriction.

6. **MAJOR — Assassination requirements/targets misstated.** Summary says only "Must have agents in that city" and presents assassination as targeting Royalty only. Source: "You can only perform the assassination action in a City space where you have more pieces (including Influence cubes) than any other single player" (also flagged in the play aid's "Easy to Forget Rules"), and you may target "either another agent or a Royalty card". Also omitted: each of your agents in the city is worth 1 Bomb point; the card enabling the assassination is removed from play (one-use); your cubes in the city go to Limbo afterwards.

7. **MODERATE omission — Move Markers scoring.** Source: "After moving the markers... ALL players score whatever the difference is between the positions of the two markers"; markers cap at 10 (triggering game end). The summary's Move Markers action says only "advance the ... track markers" — a recurring in-game scoring source is missing, as is the 10-cap.

8. Supported items worth noting: multiple claims in one Claim action (source confirms), claim domination rule ("MORE pieces... at least one... an Influence cube"), free actions, interrupt replacement draw, Limbo mechanics — all faithful.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — missing the main score-target end condition, wrong end-trigger and team-penalty rules, an invented assassination/track mechanic, and an invented movement restriction would all mislead at the table.

## abba-monopoly — MINOR

### Numbers checked (all supported)
$1,500 start (2×$500, 2×$100, 2×$50, 6×$20, 5×$10, 5×$5, 5×$1); 2 dice; 32 houses / 12 hotels; $200 GO salary; Income Tax $200 or 10% (decide before adding up); $50 jail fine; 3 doubles → Jail; doubles within 3 turns to leave Jail; max 4 houses then 1 hotel per property; mortgage interest 10%; buildings sell back at half price; Short Game: 3 free Title Deeds, 3 houses before hotel, flat $200 tax, ends at first bankruptcy; Time Limit Game: 2 Title Deeds dealt and paid at printed price. All match the source.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — ABBA-specific theming is unsupported by the source.** The extracted text is the generic Parker Brothers MONOPOLY rulebook (1994/1997 US edition; "EQUIPMENT... a board, 2 dice, tokens, 32 houses and 12 hotels") with no ABBA content. The summary's claims that "ABBA singles replace streets, studios replace houses", the six named tokens ("Napoleon hat, platform boot, vinyl record, money bag, telephone, star guitar"), "32 studios... 12 record labels", and the "Studios = Houses | Record Labels = Hotels" reference row have no basis in the source text. They may be true of the licensed edition, but they cannot be verified from this PDF and are invented relative to it.

2. **Trivial** — frontmatter `player_count: "2-8"` is not stated in the source (it only says "When more than five persons play, the Banker may elect to act only as Banker").

3. All mechanical content checked (rent rules incl. double rent on complete unimproved color-group and the "ask before the second following player rolls" rule, auctions, even-build rule, building shortages, mortgage transfer with 10% interest now and again later, bankruptcy to player vs Bank, jail rules) is faithful to the source.

**Verdict: MINOR** — game mechanics are accurate; the ABBA component naming/theming is unsupported by the source document but would not mislead anyone about how to play.

## abyss — MINOR

### Numbers checked (all supported)
71 Exploration cards = 65 Allies + 6 Monsters; per-Race distribution 1×5, 2×4, 3×3, 3×2, 4×1 (13 cards × 5 Races); 35 Lords; 6 Guilds with correct colors; 20 Locations; 20 Monster tokens (2×4, 9×3, 9×2); 10 Key tokens; Court of 6 Lords; 1 Pearl per player at setup; Ally purchase costs 1/2/3 Pearls; 1 Ally max per opponent per turn; full Threat Track reward table (spaces 1–6) verbatim-equivalent to source; Court refill trigger at 2 Lords remaining (+2 Pearls); 3 Keys to control a Location; game end at 7th Lord or unfillable Court; Monster token IP values 2/3/4; tiebreakers (Pearls, then highest-value Lord). All match.

### Findings

1. **MINOR omission — Location selection procedure.** Source: "When you take control of a Location, you may: Take one of the available face up Locations... or Draw 1, 2, 3, or 4 Locations and choose one from among those drawn. Then, place the rest face up beside the board, making them available for later turns." The summary's Turn Structure and Special Rules never explain how a Location is chosen — only that control is mandatory at 3 Keys. This is a gap in a core system, though nothing stated contradicts the source.

2. **Trivial — "20 Location tiles (double-sided)".** The source shows Locations have a front and a back ("Front of a Location / Back of the Locations") like any tile; "double-sided" (implying two playable faces) is not supported. Harmless.

3. Everything else checked — exploration procedure, Council, recruitment cost rules (exact number of Races, mandatory Race, Pearls as point substitutes, no change), affiliation of lowest-value Ally, arrow vs semi-permanent Powers, end-of-game affiliation of lowest Ally per Race, and all five Clarifications (Commander, Assassin, Trainer, Master of Magic, Traitor/Schemer) — is faithful, often near-verbatim. The "5 Ally spaces" on the Exploration Track is consistent with the source's worked example (7th card revealed lands on the last space with 2 cards bought).

**Verdict: MINOR** — accurate throughout; one procedural omission (how Locations are picked: face-up vs draw 1–4) in an otherwise faithful summary.

## accasta — MINOR

### Numbers checked (all supported)
37 board spaces; 40 pieces total; 20 per player; 9 castle spaces per side; movement ranges 1/2/3 (Shield/Horse/Chariot); max 3 pieces of the same color per stack; safe stack = 3 captured pieces; win = 3 stacks in enemy castle; 2 players; 6 movement directions; S/H/C notation letters. All match.

### Findings

1. **MINOR — Win-condition timing unsupported.** Summary: win by controlling 3 stacks in the enemy castle "at the **beginning** of their turn" (repeated in Player Reference: "Win = 3 stacks in enemy castle at start of your turn"). Source says only: "Players attempt to end up with at least 3 stacks of their own in the enemy's castle to win" — no timing qualifier appears anywhere in the article. The added timing rule may come from outside knowledge but cannot be verified from this source.

2. **MINOR — Setup placement contradicted.** Summary: pieces "are placed on their side of the board on the 9 castle spaces **and adjacent spaces**." Source: "The two players are provided with 20 pieces each, which are placed on two sides of the board on 9 specially marked spaces, inside a 'castle'" — all 20 pieces start on the 9 castle spaces (stacked; the sample game confirms starting stacks like a1:CHS). "And adjacent spaces" has no basis.

3. All movement/stacking/splitting/multiple-move/release/safe-stack/recapture rules, the no-release-in-own-castle rule (with both stated rationales), no-passing, and White-moves-first are faithful to the source.

**Verdict: MINOR** — two small unsupported/contradicted setup-and-timing details in an otherwise faithful summary.

## acquire — MINOR

### Numbers checked (all supported)
108 building tiles; 7 HQ buildings + 11 banners; 175 stock cards (25 × 7 chains); 6 stockholder info cards; 1 player aid card; money pack 30×$100, 40×$500, 50×$1,000, 35×$5,000; $6,000 start (4×$1,000 + 3×$500 + 5×$100); 6-tile starting hand; buy up to 3 stocks/turn; safe chain at 11+ tiles; game-end chain size 41+; 2-for-1 trade in mergers; founder's 1 free stock; Classic = top 2 / Tycoon = top 3 stockholders. All match.

### Findings

1. **MINOR omission — Classic Mode tie-for-secondary rule missing.** Source: "If there is a tie for secondary shareholder [Classic Mode], split the bonus among the tied players, rounding up to the nearest hundred." The summary's bonus table covers secondary ties only for Tycoon Mode.

2. **MINOR omission — "Only Two Stockholders in Tycoon Mode" rare case missing.** Source: "If there are only two stockholders when resolving stockholder bonuses in Tycoon Mode, the tertiary bonus is ignored." Not in the summary; a table could plausibly mis-assign the tertiary bonus.

3. **Trivial — "divide by 2" vs "divide it evenly".** For Classic primary ties the source says "add the primary and secondary bonuses together, divide it evenly" among the tied players (could be 3+); the summary says "divide by 2". (For Tycoon the source itself says "divide by two".)

4. **Trivial — unsupported "Hotel Chain Tiers" section.** The Player Reference's "Budget tier / Mid-range tier / Premium tier" description does not appear in the source (info-card price tables were not extracted); the source only says prices depend on chain name and size. Accurate to the real game but unverifiable here.

5. Everything else checked — turn order (place/buy/draw), founding (no 8th chain), merger procedure (don't count merging tile, mergemaker breaks ties and resolves stock first clockwise, keep/sell/trade, multi-merger largest-to-smallest), unplayable-tile discard, end-game declaration being optional, final scoring, founding-with-zero-stock cash payment, incorrect-placement rule, and the full two-player stock-market variant — is faithful.

**Verdict: MINOR** — accurate core rules and numbers; a couple of edge-case bonus rules omitted plus one unsupported reference section.

## ad-astra — MAJOR

### Numbers checked
Supported: 1 planning board; 150 resource cards (25 × 6); 55 action cards (11 per player); 25 pawns of each type, 5 per player with only 4 usable ("each player is only allowed 4 pawns of each type"); 11 alien artifact cards incl. 2 optional; 43 planets incl. 5 alien; 9 stars (Sol + 8 red/yellow in 4 shapes); 5 scoring tokens; 1 first-player token; 5 reference sheets; 12 planning spaces for 3–4p / 15 for 5p; 4 cards each in 3p, 3 cards each in 4–5p; 1 starting resource card of each type; production 1 per colony/starship and 2 per factory; movement costs free from deep space / 1 to deep space / 1 within a system / 2 from another system; max hand 10; colony on alien planet +3 VP; factory on alien planet +6 VP; terraforming +4 VP; scoring values 1/colony, 2/factory, 2/starship, 3/terraformed planet, 1/system, 1 per discarded resource (identical min 2; different 2–6); +3 bonus, none on tie; 50 VP / last-planet end conditions; bank trade 2 identical → 1 any. Not supported (see below): terraformer and starship build costs.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Terraformer cost wrong.** Summary: "Terraformer | Energy + Food" (Player Reference table: Food 1, Energy 1 — total 2 cards). Source (resource icons are stripped by OCR, but the worked example is decisive): the Red player's terraforming strategy hinges on water — "Red will need to explore to find the water he needs" — and at action 9 he "has now enough [x] and [y] to start terraforming. He spends **2 of each** to terraform his starting planet". The cards he had accumulated were food (his factory's production at action 1) and water (2 cards at action 8 from a starship+colony on the planet "he's looking for"). So the terraformer costs 2 + 2 of two resources including water — not 1 Energy + 1 Food. Both the resource mix and the quantities in the summary are contradicted.

2. **MAJOR — Starship cost contradicted by the source's example.** Summary: "Starship | Water + Energy + Food + 1 any Ore". Source example, action 7: "The Blue player, as planned, builds a starship in deep space, spending his **three initial metal resource cards**, plus one of the [ore] cards he produced before" — i.e., four ore cards (Blue's strategy notes he "has access to metals from the start" and that "To build his starship, he will need another ore card", which is why he played an ore Production card). Under the summary's cost, no "three initial metal cards" would be spent — and Blue then builds his colony with "his initial [two] cards and his last [produced] card" (water + food + ore, matching the colony cost), leaving his energy card unspent. The summary's starship cost cannot be reconciled with this.

3. **Unverifiable (not flagged as error)** — Factory cost "Water + Food + Energy": the source shows three cost icons, all stripped by OCR; the card count (3) matches but the types cannot be confirmed. Colony cost "Water + Food + 1 any Ore" is supported ("plus any one ore" survives in the text, and the example is consistent). The terraform restriction (summary: "non-alien planet"; source: "can only terraform a [icon] or [icon] planet") is also icon-mangled; the example (Red terraforms his own food-producing starting planet) keeps me from flagging it confidently.

4. Everything else checked — planning/action phase procedure, owner-first then clockwise (with the Identical/Different Resources left-of-owner exception), end-of-round hand limit and card retrieval (scoring cards back only after all 3 played), production choice binding all players, bank-shortage clockwise distribution, alien planets never producing, movement sequence (owner 1, others 1 each, owner the rest), peeking at face-down planets, no landing on opponents' pawns, Sol unreachable, only 6 of 8 systems per player's movement cards, build (owner any number, others 1), colony/factory exclusivity and colony pawn returned, trade rules, scoring-card first-player rule, artifact secrecy/one-use/Repositorium Alienum immediate — all faithful.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — two of the four building costs (terraformer, starship) are contradicted by the rulebook's own worked example; building costs are exactly the kind of number players consult a reference for.

**Verdict: PASS**
