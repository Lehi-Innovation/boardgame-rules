## pendulum

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK

### Finding 1 — Timer durations swapped (MAJOR)

Summary:
> | Green | 45 seconds | Quick actions (gain resources, basic moves) |
> | Black | 2 minutes | Medium actions (provinces, stratagems) |
> | Purple | 3 minutes | Long actions (major strategic moves, achievements) |

Source (extracted line 112–114):
> 1 purple 3-minute timer
> 1 green 2-minute timer
> 1 black 45-second timer

Green and Black are completely swapped. The Green timer is 2 minutes (not 45 seconds) and the Black timer is 45 seconds (not 2 minutes). This inverts the action weights attributed to two of the three timers throughout the summary.

### Finding 2 — Victory condition wrong (MAJOR)

Summary:
> To win, a player must meet the **minimum threshold** on all 4 victory tracks. Among players who meet all thresholds, the player with the **highest legendary achievement** wins. If tied, the player with the most votes wins.
> Players who fail to meet any threshold cannot win regardless of other track positions.

Source (extracted lines 977–995):
> The game ends after the council phase of the 4th round. If any player has advanced all of their victory tracks into the section on the parchment at the end of the tracks, this player is declared the winner and the new Timeless Ruler. If multiple players qualify as the winner, the tie is broken by the player with the most victory points in the parchment (for example, the player pictured has a score of 5). If still tied, the winner is the top player in [privilege] order.
> If no player has advanced all of their VP tokens into the parchment section of the victory point tracks, then the winner is one of the players who have their legendary achievement victory point. Look at each of these players' lowest-scoring victory track (i.e., the track furthest from the parchment). The player whose lowest-scoring track is closest to the parchment is the winner. If tied, compare the next victory point track, and so on. If still tied, the winner is the top player in [privilege] order.
> If no player has a legendary achievement victory point, there is no winner, and the kingdom descends into civil war for an age.

Errors in the summary's victory condition:
- The win condition is getting ALL tracks into "the parchment section" (a specific zone at the end of each track), not "meeting a minimum threshold." The summary never mentions the parchment.
- The legendary achievement victory point is not used as a tiebreaker among players who meet the primary condition; it is required only if NO player reaches the parchment — and even then, those players' lowest track positions are compared, not their "highest legendary achievement" score.
- The tiebreaker stated as "most votes wins" is invented. Source says ties are broken by most VP in the parchment, then privilege order. Votes are never mentioned as a tiebreaker.
- The summary implies the legendary achievement track is the final determining factor, but the source is explicit: it is a prerequisite only in the fallback case, and the actual comparison is the lowest-scoring track's proximity to the parchment.

### Finding 3 — "Typically 4 rounds" understates the rule

Summary: "The game ends after the final Council phase (typically 4 rounds)."
Source (line 341–348): "Pendulum is played over 4 rounds of real-time activity. After each round a council phase takes place... After the council phase of the 4th round, the game ends."

The game is always exactly 4 rounds; "typically" misstates a fixed rule. Minor in isolation but adds to overall inaccuracy.

- Province cards: 4 face-up in display (confirmed line 95)

- 15 common workers / 10 grande workers / 20 VP tokens / 150 resource cubes / 50 vote tokens / 10 ten-vote chips — all confirmed
- 3 timers: purple 3-min, green 2-min, black 45-sec — timer durations in summary are wrong (green/black swapped)

## pentago

**Verdict: PASS**

### Prefilter
OK

### Victory condition
Summary: "Get five of your marbles in an unbroken row — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally."
Source: "The object is to get five marbles in a row before your opponent does... A winning row of five marbles can occur vertically, horizontally or diagonally, anywhere on the board and will span two or three game blocks." ✓

### Win-without-twist rule
Summary: "If you create five in a row by placing your marble (before twisting), you win immediately — you do not need to twist."
Source: "should one player be able to win by simply placing a marble on the board for a row of five, without twisting a game block, then that game is over, without that player having to twist a game block, even if doing so would have resulted in the opponent also getting five-in-a-row." ✓

### Draw conditions
Summary: "If a player's twist simultaneously creates five in a row for both players, the game is a draw. If the board fills completely with no five in a row, the game is a draw."
Source: "A tie can occur if both players have played all marbles and neither player has five-in-a-row. A tie can also occur if one player twists his/her marbles into a row of five, but also creates a row of five for the opponent." ✓

### Neutral block rule
Summary: "If a 'neutral' game block exists (empty or with only one marble in the center), the twist portion is optional."
Source: "A neutral game block is one that is empty or has only one marble in the middle of it. Twisting a neutral game block will have no effect on the positional nature of the game board so the twisting part of a move is optional while there are neutral game blocks." ✓

### Mandatory twist
Summary: "You must always twist a non-neutral game block if one exists (the twist is mandatory once all blocks have 2+ marbles)."
Source: "once such time that all game blocks have at least one marble on it that isn't in the middle, then you have to twist a game block as part of your turn." ✓

### 180-degree rule
Summary: "A 180-degree (double notch) twist is NOT allowed."
Source: "A 180 degree (double 'notch') twist is not allowed." ✓

### Components
Summary: "18 marbles of each color (2 colors)"
Source: does not state 18 per color explicitly — this is not contradicted by source. Acceptable.

### Numbers checked
Twist = 90 degrees (✓), 4 quadrants (✓), 5 in a row to win (✓), 6x6 board (✓ implied), neutral = empty or 1 marble in center (✓).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5, SUPPORTED: 5

## pickomino

**Verdict: PASS**

### Prefilter
Flagged SOURCE_MISMATCH (title tokens "pickomino" not found in source). However, the source text is unambiguously the Pickomino rulebook: it describes barbecue worm helpings, 8 dice with 1-5 and worm symbols, values 21-36, Reiner Knizia as author, Rio Grande Games 2005 publisher, 2-7 players. The game is also published as "Heckmeck am Bratwurmeck." Content audit proceeds.

### Victory condition
Summary: "The game ends when no face-up tiles remain on the grill. Each player totals the worm icons printed on all their collected tiles. The player with the most worms wins. Tiebreaker: the player with the single highest-numbered tile wins."
Source: "The game ends as soon as there are no more open worm helpings on the grill. Now, each player adds up the worms that are on their barbecue worm helpings. The player with the most worms has won the game. If there is a tie, the player that has the most valuable single worm helping (the one with the highest number) has won the game." ✓

### Taking from the grill
Summary: "From the grill: If the grill has a face-up tile matching your exact total, take it. If not, take the highest face-up tile on the grill that is less than your total."
Source: "If, the collected dice include at least one worm symbol and add up to the number of a visible barbecue worm helping, the player can take it. If the portion is on the grill, he takes it from there... If the sum of his collected dice equals the worm helping that is lying open in his own stack or if it is not lying on the grill, the player takes another helping from the grill that has a lower value, if available."
Summary captures the overall behavior. ✓ (The nuance that you take lower when your exact tile is already in your own stack is omitted but doesn't change table play.)

### Snatching
Summary: "You can only snatch with an exact match; you cannot snatch with a higher total."
Source: "You can only snatch a worm helping from another player if you have the exact count." ✓

### Bust penalty
Summary: "Return your top tile (if you have one) to the grill, then flip the highest face-up tile on the grill face down (permanently removed from the game). Exception: if the returned tile IS the highest on the grill, it stays face up and no tile is flipped."
Source: "After a failed attempt, not only does the player get no worm helping, he also must return one he has previously collected that is one of the top of his stack of worm helpings (if he has one) and place it back on the grill... When a player needs to return a worm helping, he places it face-up back on the grill. Then, he turns over (face down) the highest available worm helping on the grill... If the returned helping is the highest number on the grill, it remains face-up." ✓

### Worm die value
Summary: "Each worm die counts as 5 points when totaling your score."
Source: "Each collected worm is worth 5 points." ✓

### Bust conditions
Summary: "A turn fails if: You roll and get only symbols you have already collected / You end your turn without any worm dice among your collected dice / Your total does not reach any visible tile on the grill or any opponent's top tile."
Source: "A turn is over unsuccessfully, when a player gets a dice roll that has only numbers and worms which he has already collected... The attempt has also failed if at the end of the player's turn, he wasn't able to collect any worm(s)... The same applies if the player is unable to reach the score of a visible worm helping." ✓

### Shorter game variant
Summary: "Shorter game variant: Returned tiles are also placed face down, even if they are the highest tile on the grill."
Source: "If you want to shorten the game, you can use the following alternative: the returned helping is also placed face-down, even if it is the highest number on the grill." ✓

### Numbers checked
16 tiles (21-36 ✓), 8 dice ✓, 6 symbols per die ✓, worm = 5 pts ✓

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4, SUPPORTED: 4

## pictureka

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK

### Finding 1 — Cow's Creative Combo win condition wrong (MAJOR)

Summary:
> **Cow's Creative Combo (ages 8+, creative)**
> **Goal:** Combine multiple cards to complete missions.
> ...
> First to 3 mission cards wins.

Source:
> Be first to collect **4** mission cards by completing the missions on your own cards.
> The first player to collect **4** mission cards wins the game.

The summary states 3 mission cards; the source says 4. This is a MAJOR error — players using the summary will end the game one card early.

### Finding 2 — Cow's Creative Combo setup wrong (MAJOR)

Summary:
> Deal **5 picture cards face up** to each player. Shuffle mission cards into a draw pile.

Source:
> Shuffle and deal **3 picture cards and 1 mission card, facedown, to each player.**
> On the first turn only, the first player takes 2 cards from the picture card draw pile, so they have **5 picture cards and 1 mission card** in their hand.

The summary conflates the first-turn draw with the initial deal. Starting deal is 3 picture cards + 1 mission card, facedown. Only the first player draws 2 extra on their very first turn to reach 5. Describing 5 face-up cards to everyone at setup is doubly wrong (count and visibility). This will cause players to start with the wrong hand.

### Finding 3 — Cow's Creative Combo matching requirement wrong (MAJOR)

Summary:
> Players combine **2+** of their cards to match either mission.

Source:
> Your job is to match **3 picture cards** to either one of the missions on your mission card.
> Do any **3 picture cards** match either of your missions?
> Lay down the mission card and **3 matching picture cards**, and explain yourself!

The summary says "2+" but the source is explicit: exactly 3 picture cards must match. Using "2+" would make the game significantly easier.

- Alphabetti win: most cards when final letter is found ✓
- Matchureka win: first to collect 4 mission cards ✓
- Matchureka setup: 20 picture cards in 5x4 grid ✓

- Penguin Wild Card works in all games ✓
- False shout = group-chosen punishment ✓

8 cards per player in 8-Away ✓; 4 piles of 2 ✓; 20 grid cards in Matchureka ✓; Matchureka win = 4 mission cards ✓; Creative Combo win = 4 (summary says 3 — WRONG); Creative Combo starting cards = 3+1 mission (summary says 5 — WRONG); matching cards = 3 (summary says 2+ — WRONG)
