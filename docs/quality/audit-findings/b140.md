## maria

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Player roles: Saxony omitted from Frederick's control (MAJOR)

Summary: "One player leads Austria (Maria Theresa), another France and Bavaria, and the third Prussia and the Pragmatic Army (Britain/Hanover/Netherlands)."

Source (lines 69–74): "The 3 players take the roles of Maria Theresa, Frederick, and Louis XV. They control the following powers: • Maria Theresa: Austria (white) • Frederick: Prussia (blue), Saxony (green), Pragmatic Army (grey) • Louis XV: France (red), Bavaria (orange)"

Finding: The summary completely omits that Frederick controls **Saxony**. Saxony is a distinct minor power with its own TC income, generals, pieces, and an entire defection sub-system (section 19.3) where Saxony can switch from Prussian ally to neutral to Austrian ally, changing the game fundamentally. Omitting Saxony from Frederick's portfolio would mislead players about who controls Saxon pieces and about the Saxony political track. The Player Reference table also lists only "Prussia + Britain/Hanover/Dutch" for that player, cementing the omission.

### Finding 2 — France/Bavaria victory condition wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "France/Bavaria: Conquer the Austrian Netherlands and/or install a Bavarian emperor."

Source (lines 718–721): "The game ends immediately when a player fulfils his victory conditions. The victory conditions for each player are: • Louis XV: Control of 9 fortresses in Austria."

Finding: The summary invents two victory conditions for Louis XV that do not appear in the source's victory conditions section. The source gives Louis XV a single, specific threshold: control of 9 fortresses in Austria. "Conquer the Austrian Netherlands" and "install a Bavarian emperor" are not stated as victory conditions. (The Imperial Election exists as an advanced-game mechanic but is a victory-box marker mechanism, not a standalone win condition.) Any table using the summary would apply the wrong win condition for France/Bavaria.

### Finding 3 — Austria victory condition wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "Austria: Survive as a major power; retain control of key territories (Silesia, Bavaria, Bohemia)."

Source (lines 718–725): "• Maria Theresa: It is the end of turn 9, and no other player has won." (Introductory game.)
Source (lines 734–736): "Basic goal of the game: A major power wins as soon as it has emptied its pool of victory markers." (Advanced game.)

Finding: The summary describes Austria's win condition as "retain control of key territories (Silesia, Bavaria, Bohemia)" — this is not stated anywhere in the source. The introductory game has Maria Theresa win by default at the end of turn 9 if no opponent has won; the advanced game has all major powers working to empty their victory marker pool. No territory-retention condition exists. Silesia is Austrian home country that Prussia seeks to annex, not a territory Austria must "retain" under the victory rules.

### Finding 4 — Turn structure invented (MAJOR)

Summary turn structure: "1. Card Draw: Each player draws cards to refill their hand. 2. Activation Rounds: Players alternate activating armies. 3. Supply Phase. 4. Political Phase. 5. Winter Phase."

Source (lines 209–233): "Each turn consists of 5 stages, which are carried out in strict order: 1. Major Powers conduct politics (advanced game only). 2. Austria places its hussars. 3. Action stage of France and Bavaria. 4. Action stage of Prussia and Saxony. 5. Action stage of Austria and Pragmatic Army." Each action stage has 5 phases: "1. Tactical Cards [draw]. 2. Supply. 3. Movement. 4. Combat. 5. Retroactive conquests."

Finding: The summary's turn structure is fabricated. There is no "Card Draw" stage (TC drawing happens at the start of each action stage, not as a separate global stage). There are no "Activation Rounds" where players alternate. "Political Phase" is advanced-game-only and comes at the start of the turn. "Winter Phase" is not part of each turn — it occurs after every 3 turns as a separate event. The actual turn structure — politics, hussar placement, then three sequential action stages for each player faction — is entirely different.

### Finding 5 — Advanced game victory tiebreaker omitted (MINOR)

Summary does not mention the tiebreaker rule for the advanced game.

Source (lines 1138–1148): "If more than one power has an empty victory pool, award victory to the power with an empty pool that could have played the most additional markers from its pool if it had them to play. If still tied, among the tied powers, Maria Theresa wins all ties, while Louis XV loses all ties. If no player has emptied its victory pool after the fourth winter, the game ends by default. Every major power totals its four winter scores. The winner is the power with the lowest total score."

Finding: Not checking this further as the game is already MAJOR; noted for completeness.

### Numbers checked

Summary numbers checked: 9 turns (introductory game), 12 turns (advanced game), 90 min, 3-5 hours, 4 suits (clubs/spades/hearts/diamonds).

Source support:
- "introductory game (9 turns to play, about 90 min.)" ✓

## marrakech

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Prefilter flagged SOURCE_MISMATCH (no "marrakech" tokens) but the source clearly is the Marrakech rulebook — it mentions "Marrakesh Square," Assam, rugs, the correct game object and rules. This is a spelling variant (the game is Marrakech, the city is Marrakesh). Proceeding with full audit.

### Finding 1 — Bankruptcy rule: player NOT eliminated per source (MAJOR)

Summary: "If a player cannot afford payment, they pay all they have and the difference is forgiven."

Source (lines 90–92): "If a player runs out of money, they pay what they can and are out of the game. Their remaining rugs are returned to the game box. Rugs already placed remain on the board and become neutral. Neutral rugs do not incur payments."

Finding: The summary says the difference is "forgiven" and the player continues. The source says the player is **eliminated from the game** (their remaining rugs removed from the box, placed rugs becoming neutral). This is a fundamental rule error — players using the summary would never eliminate an insolvent opponent.

### Finding 2 — Variant turn order misdescribed (MINOR)

Summary: "In the variant, players choose Assam's direction after rolling the die (instead of before)."

Source (lines 76–83): "During each turn, players take the following actions in this order: 1. Roll the die 2. Move Assam 3. Pay the opponent if required 4. Lay one of their own rugs 5. Rotate Assam a quarter turn. In this way, each player dictates to the next the direction in which Assam moves."

Finding: The variant does not have players choose direction after rolling during their own turn. Instead, orientation is set at step 5 (end of turn) by each player for the **next** player's turn. The summary's framing ("players choose Assam's direction after rolling") is technically inaccurate but the practical effect (direction determined by previous player, not current) is close enough to be minor given that the key insight — direction is set separately from movement — is present.

### Procedural claims verified

1. "Orient and Move Assam" — source step order: choose direction before rolling ✓ (standard rules)
2. "Roll the die" → move that many spaces in a straight line ✓
3. "If Assam reaches the edge, follow the about-turn mosaic pattern (does not count as a move)" — source: "If Assam leaves the market, he follows the about-turn symbolized by the mosaics. Going through the mosaics doesn't count as a move." ✓
4. "Pay Other Merchants (if required)" — ✓
5. Payment = "number of connected rug squares of that color touching the space Assam landed on" — source: "The amount owed is equal to the number of connected squares covered by rugs of the same color (including the one Assam landed on)." ✓
6. "Lay One Rug adjacent to Assam's current space" ✓
7. "Cannot completely cover an existing rug in one placement" — source: "Existing rugs cannot be entirely covered in one go." ✓

### Victory / game-end verified

Summary: "The game ends when the last rug is placed. Score: Each visible rug half = 1 point, Each dirham = 1 point. Highest total wins. Ties broken by most dirhams."

Source (line 68–69): "The game ends once the last rug is laid. Each visible half of a rug and each dirham count as one point. The player with the highest score wins. In the event of a tie, the player with the most dirhams wins." ✓

## marrakesh

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Actions table entirely fabricated (MAJOR)

Summary Actions table:
| Sector Color | Action |
| Red | Trade goods at the market |
| Blue | Construct buildings |
| Green | Hire workers / gain resources |
| Yellow | Influence scoring tracks |
| Purple | Acquire special abilities |

Source: The game's sectors are named areas on the player board: Main Square (Jemaa el-Fnaa), Souk, Madrasa, Date Orchard, Mosque, Palace, Medina, Sahara, and River. Keshi colors (red, blue/black, white, gray, beige, brown, pink, turquoise, yellow, orange, purple, green) correspond to these sectors but the actions are nothing like the summary describes.

Source (lines 409–498 and the OVERVIEW sections): Red keshis are water vendors; gray keshis are scholars used in the Madrasa to acquire scrolls; black (cleric) keshis advance the mosque staircase; white (noble) keshis advance the palace staircase; beige (guard) keshis are deployed in the Medina to buy city gates; brown (caravan) keshis discover oases in the Sahara; green (date picker) keshis yield dates; pink (entertainer) keshis feed the Main Square bonus mechanism. No action named "Trade goods at the market," "Construct buildings," "Hire workers / gain resources," "Influence scoring tracks," or "Acquire special abilities" appears in the source.

No support found for the action table (searched: "trade goods," "construct," "hire workers," "influence scoring," "acquire special," "sector color").

Finding: The entire 5-row action table is invented. Players relying on this summary would have no idea how any of the actual sectors work.

### Finding 2 — Scoring section fabricated (MAJOR)

Summary scoring: "Points are earned throughout the game from: Completed buildings, Trade goods delivered, Area majorities in market sectors, Special ability bonuses, End-game scoring for collections, remaining resources, and position on various tracks."

Source (lines 825–849): Final scoring consists of exactly:
1. Completely filled sectors: 10 points per sector (except souk) completely filled with 8 keshis + 1 water vendor.
2. Oasis tiles: points for the 3 claimed oases of highest value, calculated per tile requirements.
3. Remaining resources: (all resources + goods keshis) ÷ 2, rounded down.

Season scoring includes: River evaluation (winner of river track gets a bonus from river tile) and Providing for Citizens (pay provision tiles or lose points).

None of the summary's scoring categories ("Completed buildings," "Trade goods delivered," "Area majorities") appear in the source. The actual primary end-game scoring sources (filled sectors = 10 pts, oasis scoring capped at 3 oases) are entirely absent from the summary.

No support found (searched: "buildings," "trade goods delivered," "area majority," "area majorities").

### Finding 3 — Tiebreaker correct (verified)

Summary: "Ties broken by most dirhams." — No, wait. Summary says "The player with the most victory points after the final round wins." and does not mention a tiebreaker explicitly in the scoring section.

Source (lines 845–849): "In case of a tie for the most points the tied player leading on the palace staircase is the winner. A further tie is broken in favor of the player leading on the mosque staircase. If still there is no winner, the remaining tied players share the victory."

Finding: The summary does not state any tiebreaker. Omission is noted but the primary scoring errors are already MAJOR.

### Turn structure partially verified

Summary says: "1. Cylinder Selection: Each player secretly selects 3 cylinders. 2. Reveal and Place Assistants. 3. Tower Drop. 4. Draft Cylinders. 5. Execute Actions."

Source (lines 295–370): Four phases per round: (1) Select keshis & deploy assistants (simultaneously select 3, reveal, deploy, then toss into tower). (2) Claim keshis & deploy (draft in turn order, up to 2 of one color per turn). (3) Use your assistants (in turn order, each uses 3 assistants — take keshi OR perform sector action). (4) River bonus & end of round.

The summary's 5-step structure conflates phase 1 (select + deploy + toss tower all happen together) and omits the "River bonus & end of round" phase. The description of "Use placed cylinders to boost associated action strengths, then perform actions" is imprecise: cylinders don't boost action strength per se; having more keshis of a color in a sector makes that sector's action more powerful when you actually perform it. The omission of the River phase is MINOR but the fabricated action table and scoring are MAJOR.
