# Audit b003: 1960-the-making-of-the-president

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Summary audited: `rules/1960-the-making-of-the-president.md`
Source: `extracted/1960-the-making-of-the-president-rules.txt`

The summary's component list and turn skeleton are faithful, but it contains a wrong victory
threshold, a contradicted initiative rule, and several smaller invented or mischaracterized
mechanics, plus omission of the core card-economy system (rest cubes / Campaign Strategy
banking / hand sizes).

## Findings

### MAJOR 1 — Wrong victory threshold: "270" vs source "269 of 537"

- Summary (Scoring): "The player with the majority of electoral votes (270+) on Election Day wins."
- Summary (Key Numbers): "270 electoral votes needed to win"
- Source (Final Tally): "There are a total of 537 electoral votes available, which means any
  player who takes at least 269 of them has won the game!" The 1960 map has 537 EV, so the
  majority is 269, not the modern 270/538. The Unpledged Electors section also references
  "fewer than 269 electoral votes." Source elsewhere states the operative rule is simply
  "The player with the most electoral votes wins the game." The 270 figure is a real-world
  anachronism contradicting an explicit number in the rulebook's victory condition.

### MAJOR 2 — Initiative rule contradicted (two errors in one bullet)

- Summary (Special Rules): "Initiative: Determined by bag draws. The trailing player in cubes
  drawn gets initiative (plays first that turn)."
- Source (Initiative Checks): "draw cubes from the bag one at a time until two cubes of the
  same color have been drawn; the player who had two of their own cubes drawn gains the
  initiative." There is no "trailing player" rule — initiative goes to the player whose color
  comes up twice first.
- Source (Initiative Phase): "The player with the initiative may select the start player, who
  will play first during each Activity Phase," with a tip noting "it is generally advantageous
  to force your opponent to play first." So initiative does NOT mean you play first — you
  choose who plays first, and choosing the opponent is often correct. The summary would
  mislead players on both who wins initiative and what it grants.

### MINOR 3 — Invented: "Maximum momentum is tracked on the board"

- Summary (Special Rules): "Maximum momentum is tracked on the board."
- Source: No momentum maximum and no momentum track exists anywhere in the text (board
  features listed: Rest Cube Zones, Card Stacks, Persistent Events, Issues, Prevention Event,
  Turn/Phase tracks, Advertising, States). Momentum is limited only by Momentum Decay
  ("discard half of their momentum markers, rounded down"). Invented mechanic.

### MINOR 4 — Debate rewards misstated

- Summary (Turn Structure, Debates): "winning issues earns momentum and support."
- Source (Winning An Issue): winner is "awarded a bonus of state support cubes": first issue
  2, second issue 3, last issue 4 — no Support Checks required. No momentum is awarded for
  winning debate issues. "Momentum" here is unsupported, and the 2/3/4 escalation is omitted.

### MINOR 5 — Endorsements on Election Day mischaracterized

- Summary (Scoring): "endorsement markers in a region can flip states — each endorsement
  affects undecided or weakly-held states."
- Source (Endorsements and Undecided Voters): only states "that currently contain no state
  support cubes for either player" tip; an endorsement in the region wins such a state,
  otherwise the state's printed edge (blue=Kennedy, red=Nixon) decides. Endorsements never
  flip "weakly-held" (occupied) states, and the edge-indicator fallback is omitted entirely.

### MINOR 6 — Candidate Card mischaracterized; 5 CP value omitted

- Summary (Actions): "Each player has a unique Candidate card with special abilities."
- Source (C. Play a Candidate Card): playing it is worth a flat 5 CP, "resolved in exactly
  the same way as playing any other card for Campaign Points," then flipped to its Exhausted
  side. It is not a special-ability card; the 5 CP value and one-use/exhaust mechanic are
  omitted (exhaustion not mentioned anywhere in the summary).

### MINOR 7 — Core card economy omitted/garbled

- Hand size: source deals 6 cards per turn on turns 1–5 and 7 cards on turns 7–8; the
  summary's Key Number "5 cards per player per turn" describes only cards played (5 Activity
  Phases) and never mentions the 6/7-card deals.
- Campaign Strategy Phase: summary says only "Campaign Strategy decisions, then Rest."
  Source: players bank exactly 1 card facedown on turns 1–5 (yielding the 5 Debate cards)
  and exactly 2 cards on turns 7–8 (yielding the 4 Election Day cards, each granting 3
  Support Checks in the card's state). This banking system — the engine behind both the
  Debates and Election Day — is absent.
- Rest cubes: source has players collect the rest cubes printed on every card played and
  deposit them into the Political Capital Bag (which drives all Support/Initiative Check
  odds). Summary says only "Players may 'rest' by holding cubes in their Rest Zone for
  future use," omitting that rest cubes come from card play and go into the bag.
- Setup: summary says "deal starting hands per the setup rules"; source deals cards during
  each turn's Initiative Phase, not at setup.

### MINOR 8 — Cost/threshold omissions in Actions

- Triggering an event costs 1 momentum marker; preempting costs 2 (source: "spending one
  momentum marker" / "spending two momentum markers"). Summary gives no costs.
- "Carrying" a state = 4+ state support cubes (the trigger for opponent Support Checks);
  summary uses "carried" without defining the threshold.
- Positioning on Issues: first cube in an issue 1 CP, each additional cube in the same issue
  2 CP — omitted.
- Candidate token travel: 1 CP per regional boundary crossed (2 between West and East;
  Alaska/Hawaii special boundaries) — candidate movement is absent from the Actions section.
- Support Check exemption: media support in a region exempts a player from Support Checks
  when campaigning there — stated only obliquely ("facilitates campaigning").

## Verdict rationale

Component counts and the high-level turn skeleton are accurate, but the summary gets the
victory threshold wrong (270 vs 269), states the initiative rule backwards on two counts,
invents a momentum cap, and misstates debate rewards and Election Day endorsement effects,
while omitting the Campaign Strategy card-banking / rest-cube bag economy that connects the
campaign turns to the Debates and Election Day. These would mislead players at the table:
MAJOR.
