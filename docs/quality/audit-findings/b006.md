## a-d-e-l-e — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component numbers are all faithful, but the Actions section substantially misrepresents the crew action menu (invents actions, omits the core Special Action delivery mechanic), and the Player Reference contradicts the source on what wounds do.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Wound effect contradicted.** Summary (Player Reference): "Must manage life track (damage reduces available cubes)." Source (7.1): "At a certain point, wounded crew members start losing carrying capacity. To reflect this, put a token on one of the spaces for objects in the personal board." Wounds reduce inventory/carrying capacity, not action cubes. A player using the summary would remove action cubes when wounded — a significant rules change.

2. **MAJOR — Core "Special Action" mechanic omitted.** Source (7.0): "The special action is a little different from the others. It cost you 3 action cubes to perform, but you may, as an option, try it for just 2. If you do, you must succeed on a Spirit roll." This is the only way to deliver mission objects (the win condition), use terminals, and clear certain hazards. The summary never mentions it or its 3-cube/2-cube cost.

3. **MAJOR — Crew action list misrepresented.** Summary lists actions: Move, Search, Pick Up/Drop Object, Use Object, Share Information, Interact with Terminal. Source (7.0) lists exactly five: Special Action, Unlock Door, Search, Advance, Use Object. "Share Information" is not an action — communication is free and public (7.2: "Crew members can talk as much as they want... loud enough for everybody to hear"), and same-location tile/object sharing is also not a cube action. "Pick Up" is part of the Search action; dropping is forced, not an action. "Unlock Door" is omitted. Summary also asserts crew "use cubes... to share information," which is unsupported.

4. **MINOR — Spirit roll system omitted.** Source (7.0): "Every time a situation requires a Spirit roll, the player affected must roll his d6. If the result is equal to or less than his Spirit, the roll is a success." This core resolution system (used by events, Fire hazards, risky special actions) appears nowhere in the summary.

5. **MINOR — Mission win conditions left vague.** Summary: "meet the disconnect condition" / "meet escape conditions." Source (4.0 chart): Deactivate ADELE requires "There must be no impact token on the board"; Escape requires "The turn marker cannot be beyond the 'no return' marker." The specific conditions are omitted (not contradicted).

6. **MINOR — Setup of the 5 lab objects misplaced.** Summary: "Distribute 5 face-down objects on ship locations." Source (2.0): "Place those 5 objects face down on the box printed on the ship" (a single stack in the laboratory, used by Create Objects, 9.2), distinct from the 20 objects placed one per location.

7. **MINOR — Components list omits the 1 Energy Cylinder** (source components list includes "1 Energy Cylinder").

8. **MINOR — Turn structure compressed.** Source (3.0) has six named phases in order: Event, Planning, ADELE, Resolution, Actions, Upkeep. Summary gives only a one-paragraph gloss ("phases for crew actions and ADELE actions, with events triggering between them"), losing the planning-behind-screens / simultaneous-reveal structure (partially recovered in Special Rules).

## a-distant-plain — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component counts are accurate, but three of the four faction victory conditions are wrong, two factions' Special Activity lists are wrong (including an activity assigned to the wrong faction), faction colors are wrong, and the Pass reward is misstated.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Coalition victory condition wrong.** Summary: "Total Support + Patronage >= threshold; Coalition Casualties below threshold." Source (7.2): "Coalition—Total Population in Support plus the number of pieces in the Coalition Available Forces box exceeds 30." Patronage is not part of the Coalition condition, and there is no casualties threshold (casualties only affect Aid, 6.3.4, and which pieces are Available).

2. **MAJOR — Government victory condition wrong.** Summary: "Total Support + Patronage >= threshold." Source (7.2): "Government—Total COIN-Controlled Population plus Patronage exceeds 35." It is Controlled Population, not Support.

3. **MAJOR — Warlords victory condition wrong.** Summary: "Total Bases + Resources >= threshold." Source (7.2): "Warlords—Total Uncontrolled Population within Afghanistan exceeds 15 and Warlord Resources exceed 40." Bases are not part of it; Uncontrolled Population is omitted entirely. (Taliban condition — Opposition + Bases — is correct, threshold 20 omitted.)

4. **MAJOR — Taliban Special Activities wrong.** Summary: "Infiltrate, Suborn, Extort." Source (4.4): "The Taliban choose from Extort, Infiltrate, or Ambush." Suborn is a Warlords activity (4.5.3); Ambush is omitted.

5. **MINOR/MAJOR — Coalition Special Activity misnamed.** Summary: "Surge, Transport, Airstrike." Source (4.2): "The Coalition may choose from Surge, Air Lift, or Air Strike." "Transport" is the Government's activity (4.3.2) with a different procedure (LoC movement with Warlord tolls) than Air Lift (move among any 3 spaces).

6. **MAJOR — Faction colors wrong.** Summary Player Reference: Coalition = "Medium blue", Government = "Light blue". Source (1.5): "the Coalition (tan)... the Government (blue)"; (1.4): "Coalition Troops (tan cubes), Government Troops (medium-blue cubes) and Police (light-blue cubes)." Coalition is tan; medium blue is Government Troops.

7. **MINOR — Pass reward misstated.** Summary: "Pass (stay Eligible for next card; gain 1 Resource if desired)." Source (2.3.3): a passing Faction "receives +1 Resource if an Insurgent Faction or provides +3 Resources to the Government if either COIN Faction."

8. **MINOR — 2nd Eligible's restricted options omitted.** Summary presents both eligible factions as freely choosing Event/Operation/Pass. Source (2.3.4): the 2nd Eligible's options depend on the 1st (e.g., "If the 1st Eligible Faction executed an Operation, the 2nd Eligible Faction may execute a Limited Operation" — 1 space, no Special Activity, 2.3.5). The Limited Operation concept is absent from the summary.

9. **MINOR — Control definition imprecise.** Summary: "Controlled by the faction (Government or Insurgent) with the most total pieces there." Source (1.7): COIN Factions *together* or the *Taliban alone* Control a space "if their pieces there exceed those of all other Factions combined." Warlords can never Control; "most pieces" is not the test.

10. **MINOR — Propaganda Redeploy phase incomplete.** Summary: "Government and Coalition may reposition forces." Source (6.5): includes mandatory Government Desertion (remove 1 in 3 cubes, 6.5.1), mandatory Government Troop redeploy (6.5.2), and mandatory Taliban Guerrilla redeploy (6.5.4) — all omitted.

11. **MINOR — Resources phase basis loosely stated.** Summary: "based on control, population, bases, and economic value." Source (6.3): Government earns Aid + Econ of unSabotaged LoCs; Taliban earn per Base; Warlords per Base + Guerrillas on unSabotaged LoCs. Control/population are not earnings inputs.

## a-feast-for-odin — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component counts are excellent (all verified), but the round-phase table contains a wrong start-player rule, a wrong breeding schedule, and an invented phase 11; the scoring table gets animal points wrong and invents both an English Crown effect and a tiebreaker; and "Anytime Actions" invents free actions that actually cost an action space.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Phase 6 (start player) rule wrong.** Summary: "Player with fewest Vikings on action board gets start player moose." Source (Phase 6): "The player who placed Vikings last in the action phase receives the start player moose."

2. **MAJOR — Phase 8 (breeding) schedule invented.** Summary: "Every other round (rounds 3, 5, 7 in long game): sheep/cattle breed." Source: Phase 8 is one of the 12 phases "played one after another" every round; the marginal note "your sheep and cattle can breed every 2 rounds" refers to the pregnancy cycle (turn pregnant one round, birth the next), not to skipping the phase. Players following the summary would skip breeding in even rounds.

3. **MAJOR — Phase 11 invented / mountain strip phase omitted.** Summary: "11. Exploration Board Bonus — Receive bonuses from claimed exploration boards." Source: "Phase 11: Update and Add New Mountain Strips" (remove the leftmost resource from each face-up strip, turn a new strip face up). Exploration board income/bonuses are paid in phases 7 and 10 ("Income from an exploration board is handled like income from your home board"). There is no separate exploration bonus phase.

4. **MAJOR — Animal scoring wrong.** Summary: "Sheep/cattle: 1 point each; pregnant = 1 extra." Source (Scoring): "each sheep is worth 2 points (3 points if pregnant), each cattle is worth 3 points (4 points if pregnant)."

5. **MAJOR — English Crown effect invented.** Summary: "English Crown (special tile) | Points for sets of goods." Source: "The player who owns the 'English Crown' special tile receives 2 additional points." No set-collection effect exists.

6. **MINOR — Tiebreaker invented.** Summary: "Ties broken by most silver remaining." Source: "In case of a tie, there are multiple winners."

7. **MAJOR — Invented "Anytime Actions."** Summary lists as free anytime actions: "Upgrade goods: flip green to blue side (craft to luxury)" and "Exchange weapons for goods." Source: turning green goods blue is the Overseas Trading *action space* — "you can pay 1 silver to turn any number of different green goods tiles to their blue side... you must have at least one knarr" — not an anytime action (the summary's own Special Rules section states this correctly, contradicting its Anytime list). No anytime weapons-for-goods exchange exists; weapons are spent to modify hunt/raid rolls. Actual anytime actions in the source: placing goods, buying ships, changing silver, arming ships with ore, anytime occupation effects.

8. **MINOR — Ore placement overstated.** Summary: "Ore and silver can fill gaps anywhere" / Player Reference "Ore/Silver | Any placement area." Source: houses accept "orange and red food tiles... as well as the usual green and blue goods and silver coins, **but not ore**"; ore also cannot go on the Banquet Table.

9. **MINOR — Emigration restricted to longships.** Summary: "Emigration: Use longships to emigrate" and ship table credits Emigration only to longships. Source (Emigration): "you may turn a knarr or a longship... to its back side and place it on the leftmost space of your 'Banquet Table'" (emigrated knarr 18 pts, longship 21 pts). The emigration cost (silver equal to current round) is also omitted.

10. **MINOR — Green adjacency rule omitted.** Source: "Green tiles cannot be orthogonally adjacent to other green tiles" (and orange/red adjacency bans in houses and at the feast). This central placement constraint appears nowhere in the summary, which only says "Tiles must fit tetris-style; no overlapping."

11. **MINOR — Red goods mislabeled "equipment."** Source components: Food tiles are "front side: farm product [orange], back side: animal product [red]"; "Equipment" is the green craft / blue luxury tile category. The placement rule given for red (stone/long houses only) is nonetheless correct.
