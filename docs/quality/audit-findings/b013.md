## agricola-express — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented scoring rules imported from base Agricola.**
   Summary (Scoring Categories): "Same as base Agricola: ... Unused farmyard spaces (-1 each); Begging cards (-3 each, for unfed family)."
   Source: neither "begging" nor "farmyard" appears anywhere in the extracted text (grep-verified). The score sheet lists: House +3 and +1/2/3 by house type; Fields +3 / Mayor +2 per field; Pastures +3/+1; Babies +1/3/5/7 for 1/2/3/4; Grain +1 per 2; Veggie +1 per 1; animals +1 per 3/2/1 (sheep/boar/cattle); Improvements +1 each; workshop resource scoring. There are no farmyard spaces or begging cards in this dice game. The claim "Same as base Agricola" is itself false — the categories and values differ substantially.

2. **MAJOR — Invented pasture cost.**
   Summary (Actions): "Build Pasture: Use Pasture face + 4 wood to fence a pasture."
   Source: "The player may select the Pasture Farm die face to build a new pasture on his farm" — no 4-wood cost anywhere ("4 wood" absent from the text; the score sheet shows pasture cost as "(-1 [wood])"). The 4-wood figure appears borrowed from base Agricola fencing.

3. **MAJOR — Feeding penalty misstated.**
   Summary: "Unfed members = Begging cards" / "Begging cards (-3 each, for unfed family)."
   Source: "You lose 3 points for each food you are missing." Penalty is -3 per missing FOOD (so an entirely unfed member costs -6), not -3 per unfed member, and no begging cards exist.

4. MINOR — Omissions: occupation costs 1 Food to gain; max 4 babies; room cost (1 Reed + 1 resource of current house type per room); house upgrade costs (2 Brick / 2 Stone); planting a field records 2 grain/vegetable; breeding at harvest (pasture with 2+ animals gains 1); tiebreaker = unused food; solo par = 85. None individually misleading, but the scoring section's lack of the actual score-sheet values compounds finding 1.

5. MINOR — "Animals need proper housing (pastures or stalls)": source allows pasture OR immediate cooking with a hearth; "stalls" do not exist in this game.

## alchemists — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented turn-order mechanic.**
   Summary (Turn Structure): "Choose Turn Order: Players secretly bid on turn order positions using bid cards." (repeated in Player Reference: "Bid for order").
   Source: "Beginning with the player with the starting player marker and proceeding clockwise ... each player chooses an order space" and draws the indicated cards — open sequential selection, no bidding. Bid cards exist only for the Sell Potion discount auction ("Each player has four bid cards with discount values of 0, -1, -2, and -3 ... they all bid secretly"). The summary transplants the sell-potion bid onto round setup.

2. **MAJOR — Test on Student mechanics invented.**
   Summary: "The student reports only the sign of the potion (positive/negative/neutral). Risk: if you give a negative potion, lose reputation."
   Source: the experiment procedure is identical for Test on Student and Drink Potion — "Show the result to the other players ... Put the corresponding result token in your results triangle" — you learn the exact potion either way. The actual difference: after anyone mixes a negative potion, later users of the action must pay 1 gold piece ("the action costs 1 gold piece"); there is no reputation loss for testing on a student. Both halves of the summary's claim are unsupported.

3. **MAJOR — End-game gold conversion wrong.**
   Summary (Scoring 5): "Remaining gold converts to points (ratio varies by player count)."
   Source: "score one-third of a victory point for each gold piece. Or ... buy 1 victory point for every 3 gold pieces" — fixed 3:1 ratio for all player counts. (Also omits: leftover favor cards exchange for 2 gold each before conversion.)

4. **MAJOR — Board sides misidentified.**
   Summary (Setup 1): "Place the game board (apprentice or master side)" and Special Rules: "Master Side: The advanced board side adds more complexity."
   Source: "This side of the board is for a four-player game. The setup for two or three players is the same, except you use the other side of the board." Master/apprentice is a rules variant (3 vs 2 starting ingredients, different conference tiles, different debunking) — not a board side. Following the summary would cause a wrong setup.

5. **MAJOR — Core seal/endorsement system omitted.**
   The summary never mentions: endorsing others' theories (pay 1 gold to bank + 1 gold per existing seal holder, no reputation), or the seal types — 2 gold-starred (5 VP), 3 silver-starred (3 VP), unstarred hedge seals (0 VP but protect against one aspect), incorrect theory = -4 VP per non-hedged seal at the Big Revelation. This is the game's central betting mechanism and primary VP engine; without it the Publish/Scoring sections are not playable.

6. MINOR — Sell Potion: "earn gold and reputation; if wrong, lose reputation and may have to refund." Source: selling never gains reputation; neutral or wrong-sign results lose 1 reputation; there are no refunds — you simply "don't get paid" if the guarantee is missed.

7. MINOR — Declaration order omitted/inverted: source has the LOWEST player on the order spaces declare actions first ("The player lowest on the order spaces must declare his or her actions first"); summary says "In turn order, place cubes."

8. MINOR — "Favor Cards: Earned by placing last in turn order." Source: favor cards come from specific order spaces and from pairs of unused cubes at round end; not specifically from placing last.

9. MINOR — Other notable omissions: action cube counts (6/5/4 for 2/3/4 players; only 3 in round 1), three actions unavailable in round 1, reputation zones (green/blue/red) with bid and price effects, exhibition being final-round-only (replaces Test/Drink), conferences at end of rounds 3 and 5 with reputation penalties, max potions sold = players-1.
