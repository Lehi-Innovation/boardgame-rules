## mystery-of-the-abbey

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Finding 1 — Wrong revelation point value (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Correct trait revelation | +4 points"

**Source (lines 317, 350):** "Each correct revelation is worth +2 points, each false revelation –1 point." / "• Each right revelation +2"

The summary doubles the point value for a correct revelation. At +4 (not +2), every score computed from the summary for revelation-heavy players is wrong.

### Finding 2 — Invented bonus for most suspect cards

**Summary:** "Most suspect cards at game end | Bonus"

**Source:** No support found (searched: "most suspect", "bonus", "most cards", "suspect card.*end", "end.*suspect"). The scoring section (lines 349–355) is exhaustive: right revelation +2, wrong revelation −1, correct accusation +4, false accusation −2. No bonus for holding the most suspect cards appears anywhere.

### Finding 3 — Confessional and Cells room descriptions are imprecise

**Summary:** "Confessional | Look at another player's cards"

**Source (lines 232–236):** "Randomly draw a Suspect card from the hand of the last player who visited this same confessional, as indicated by the color of the monk displayed on the upturned face of the dice." 

The summary says "look at" but the source says you **randomly draw** (take) the card — these are meaningfully different. This is a procedural error but less severe than Finding 1; however combined with Finding 1 this game has multiple errors.

### Verification Quotas

**Victory / game-end conditions:**
- Summary: "The game ends after the final Mass (Compline) or when a player makes a correct accusation."
- Source (line 333): "The game ends and the player who correctly named the culprit scores 4 points." Game-end on correct accusation: confirmed. Game-end after Compline: implied by "eight Mass cards ... Compline" being last. Supported.

**Tiebreaker:**
- Summary: "In the event of a tie, the player who found the culprit is the winner."
- Source (line 335): "In the event of a tie, the player who found the culprit is the winner." — Confirmed.

**Scoring numbers checked:**

|---|---|---|
| Correct revelation +4 | +2 (line 317, 350) | WRONG |
| Incorrect revelation −1 | −1 (line 351) | Correct |
| Correct accusation +4 | +4 (line 352) | Correct |
| Incorrect accusation −2 | −2 (line 353) | Correct |
| Most suspect cards: Bonus | Not in source | INVENTED |

**Procedural claims verified:**
1. Move 1–2 rooms per turn: Source line 198–199: "Move your pawn one or two steps." Confirmed.
2. Chapter Hall used for revelations: Source lines 289–291: "You may make a Revelation or bring an Accusation." Confirmed.

## navegador

### Verdict: MINOR

### Finding 1 — Missing tiebreaker (MINOR)

**Summary:** No tiebreaker mentioned.

**Source (lines 556–559):** "A possible tie is won by the player who currently holds the Navegador or, if not the case, who is the next player who would receive him."

The summary says "Highest total VP wins" but does not mention this tiebreaker. This would not prevent players from scoring correctly, but could cause confusion in a tied game. Rated MINOR since it doesn't affect how the game is scored during play.

### Verification

**Victory / game-end conditions:**
- Summary: "The game ends after the last sea region is explored or the last building is taken; one final round is played."
- Source (lines 41–44): "After the last sea region has been explored, or the last building has been taken, one final round of turns is executed." Confirmed (Nagasaki = last sea region per detailed rules, consistent with summary section).
- Summary: "each player, including the one who triggered the end, takes a single final turn"
- Source (lines 541–544): "each player, including the one who triggered the end, takes a single final turn." Confirmed.

**Scoring claims:**
- Summary: "VP scored from 5 categories, each multiplied by privileges: Colonies, Factories, Explorers, Shipyards, Churches"
- Source (lines 562–566): "every colony counts as 4 VPs, every factory as 2 VPs, every explorer as 5 VPs, every shipyard as 7 VPs, and every church as 5 VPs." Confirmed (these are base VP values before privilege multiplication).
- Summary: "Additional 1 VP per: worker, ship, full 200 Cruzados"
- Source (lines 582–585): "Each player receives 1 VP per his worker in Lisboa, ship on the game board and full amount of 200 Cruzados cash." Confirmed.

**Procedural claims verified:**
1. Rondel: first 3 spaces free, each additional costs 1 ship. Source lines 143–147: "any of the next three fields ahead can be chosen free of charge. If the game stone moves further than three fields, each additional field has to be paid for with 1 of the player's ships." Confirmed.
2. Colony requires: 1 ship in region, 2 workers in Lisboa. Source lines 282–284: "at least one own ship in the same region, and two workers in Lisboa." Confirmed.
3. Workers action: 50 Cr each up to 1 per church; additional cost 100/200/300 per phase. Source lines 172–176: "one new worker per church owned for the price of 50 Cruzados each. Additional workers each cost 100 Cruzados per game phase." Confirmed.
4. Exploring: claim explorer disk, receive cash bonus, lose 1 ship. Source lines 238–252: Confirmed (2 ships needed to enter; 1 is lost).
5. Navegador card: extra sailing action before turn; passed right after use; cannot be used in first round. Source lines 92–99 and 507–531: Confirmed.

**Exhaustion rule:** No deck/bag exhaustion mechanic in this game. Not applicable.

## neuroshima-hex

### Verdict: PASS

### Verification

**Victory / game-end conditions:**
- Summary: "The game ends when: Both players run out of tiles, OR An HQ is destroyed (that player loses immediately)."
- Source (lines 229–244): "If any of the players draws his last tile from the deck, he can finish his turn. Then the opposing player finishes his turn and the Final Battle begins. The game ends after the Final Battle or when an HQ's Hit points are reduced to zero. If one of the HQs is destroyed, the game ends and the player whose HQ survived wins." Confirmed.
- Summary: "If no HQ is destroyed, the player whose HQ has more remaining hit points wins. Ties are possible."
- Source (lines 243–250): "If none of the HQs are destroyed and the Final Battle has been fought, the player whose HQ has more Hit points left is the winner. If both HQs have the same number of points remaining, each player takes one more turn … Then, an additional Battle begins. If both HQs still have the same number of points remaining after this additional Battle, the game ends in a draw." The summary simplifies tie resolution to "Ties are possible" — accurate in spirit but omits the extra turn procedure. This is a minor simplification, not a contradiction.

**Scoring numbers:**
- Summary: "Each HQ starts with 20 hit points." Source (line 100): "each HQ has 20 'hit points'." Confirmed.
- No VP table to check; victory is determined by HQ HP.

**Procedural claims verified:**
1. Player 1 draws 1 tile first; Player 2 draws 2 tiles. Source (lines 125–132): Confirmed.
2. Regular turn: draw up to 3 tiles, mandatory discard of 1, use remaining. Source (lines 141–147): Confirmed.
3. Battle triggered by Battle tile OR full board. Source (lines 191–196): Confirmed.
4. Battle initiative order: 3 first, then 2, then 1, then 0. Source (lines 199–200): Confirmed.
5. Units with same initiative act simultaneously. Source (line 202): Confirmed.

**Multiplayer (3–4 players) claim:**
- Summary: "the last HQ standing or the one with most HP wins."
- Source (lines 619–649): 3-player Deathmatch: if an HQ is destroyed, player is out; game continues. When game ends, winner is last HQ standing or most HP. Confirmed in substance.

**Exhaustion rule:**
- Summary does not claim any deck reshuffle. Source (line 217): "A Battle tile cannot be used if any of the players has drawn his last tile from the deck." No reshuffle is ever stated in source. Summary is silent on this, which is correct.

**Exhaustion rule:** No deck exhaustion mechanic in this game's summary. Action cards refilled each round per source (lines 514–528). Summary does not claim any special reshuffle rule. Correct.
