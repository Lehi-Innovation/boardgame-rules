## dragon-dice — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — The Scoring section invents a capture mechanic ("the terrain and your army there are removed from the battlefield" once you are "the only player with units at that terrain") that directly contradicts the source's Eighth Face rules, and in doing so the summary omits the entire eighth-face control system (doubled saves/maneuvers, free action choice, enemy melee restriction, reversion to face 7 on loss of control). The Player Reference also asserts a fixed terrain-face-to-action mapping that the source does not support. All spot-checked numbers are correct.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented capture mechanic; contradicts the source's Eighth Face rules.**
   - Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions): "Capturing terrain: When face 8 is showing and you are the only player with units at that terrain, you control it. The terrain and your army there are removed from the battlefield."
   - Source (l.964-966): "If a terrain is maneuvered to its eighth face (the number 8 is showing), the acting army immediately controls or 'captures' that terrain. ... An army that has captured a terrain receives several special advantages for as long as it retains control of that terrain die" — nothing is removed from play. The glossary confirms (l.3185-3186): "'Capture a Terrain': This phrase denotes when an army successfully maneuvers the terrain to an eighth face."
   - The source further contradicts removal: the controlling army keeps playing at that terrain with advantages (l.968-975), enemy armies can still be there ("enemy armies at the terrain are restricted to only melee attacks", l.972-973), and "A terrain at its eighth face immediately turns back to its seventh face whenever the controlling army: abandons the terrain, loses control (is out-maneuvered) by an enemy army, or all of the units in the controlling army are killed" (l.983-986).
   - The condition "you are the only player with units at that terrain" is also invented — capture requires only a successful maneuver to face 8 (l.964, l.3185).
   - Impact: severe. Players following the summary would remove a captured terrain and the capturing army from the game, making captures permanent and uncontestable and removing the capturing army from further play. Under the real rules a capture can be lost, must be defended, and confers ongoing advantages.

2. **MAJOR (consequence of #1) — Eighth-face control advantages omitted entirely.**
   - Source (l.964-986): a controlling army (1) doubles all save results, (2) doubles all maneuver results, (3) may take melee, missile, or magic regardless of the terrain icon while enemies there are restricted to melee, and (4) uses the eighth-face Special Location Icon (City, Standing Stones, Temple, Tower — each defined at l.993-1012, e.g. Temple blocks enemy black magic and forces a burial; Tower gives unlimited missile range).
   - None of this appears in the summary (the Initial Effects phase mentions City/Temple icons only in passing). This is a core ongoing system — the game's central reward structure between the first and second capture — and is incompatible with the summary's "removed from the battlefield" claim.

3. **MODERATE — Unsupported fixed terrain-face mapping in Player Reference.**
   - Summary: "Terrain faces: 1-2 = far (missile), 3-4 = medium (magic), 5-6 = close (melee), 7-8 = very close (melee/capture)"
   - Source: faces 1-8 abstractly represent distance ("a low value ... means the armies are very far apart, while a large value means the armies are very close", l.200-203) and "the face showing on that terrain dictates its action" (l.561), but no uniform face-to-action mapping is ever given. On the contrary, the special-terrains section implies normal terrains' "action icon progression on the die is set by ... their elemental colors" (l.2716), i.e. it varies per terrain type. The specific 1-2/3-4/5-6 assignment has no basis in the extracted text. (Low practical harm since the physical die shows its icon, but it is presented as a rule.)

4. **MINOR — Starting-distance roll omits the frontier terrain.**
   - Summary (Setup 7): "Roll each home terrain die (reroll 8s, reduce 7s to 6)."
   - Source (l.385-387): "Each player rolls their home terrain die ... The player that selected the frontier terrain rolls this die [the frontier terrain]." The frontier terrain is also rolled; reroll-8 / 7-to-6 is correct.

5. **MINOR — Promotion source incomplete.**
   - Summary: "Promotion: Replace a unit with a one-size-larger unit of the same race from DUA."
   - Source (l.223-225): "...replace it with a unit one health larger in size of the same race from your dead unit area **or summoning pool**" (the summoning pool matters for Dragonkin, l.226). Also "rare promotes to a monster or champion" is captured correctly.

6. **Trivial — Order-of-play roll described as "maneuver results"**; source counts "maneuver and ID icon results" (l.367-368) — equivalent in effect since IDs count as the result rolled for, and the summary states the ID rule elsewhere.
