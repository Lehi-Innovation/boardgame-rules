## ogre

### Prefilter
OK

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Findings

#### F1 — Turn structure omits Recovery phase (MAJOR)

Summary (Turn Structure):
> "Each turn consists of two player turns. The first player completes all phases, then the second player does the same: 1. Movement Phase … 2. Combat Phase"

Source (section 4.02):
> "4.02 Phases of a turn. The turn sequence for each player in a two-player game is: 1. Recovery. (a) All the player's units which were disabled before the last enemy turn by ramming or enemy fire now recover automatically. Turn the counters right-side-up. (b) Roll one die for each of his units disabled by forest, rubble, or swamp… 2. Movement phase. … 3. Disable check. … 4. Fire phase. … 5. Second (GEV) movement phase."

The summary reduces a 5-phase turn to 2 phases and entirely omits: (1) the Recovery phase — a mandatory first step that recovers disabled units, including a die-roll recovery for terrain-disabled units; (2) the Disable check (step 3); and (3) the Second (GEV) movement phase as a distinct phase. The Recovery phase matters because units disabled by terrain require a die roll to recover and may remain disabled for many turns. Players cannot play correctly without knowing this phase.

The summary lists "G.E.V. second movement" as a Special Rule rather than as an integrated phase of the turn sequence, which is also misleading.

#### F2 — Victory conditions oversimplified / inaccurate for basic scenario (MAJOR)

Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions):
> "Ogre wins: By destroying the Command Post. Defender wins: By destroying or immobilizing the Ogre before it reaches the Command Post."

Source (basic scenario victory conditions):
> "All defending units destroyed: complete Ogre victory. Command post destroyed and Ogre escapes from the south end of the map: Ogre victory. Command post and Ogre destroyed: marginal Ogre victory. Command post survives, but Ogre escapes: marginal defense victory. Command post survives, Ogre destroyed: defense victory. Command post and at least 30 points of attack strength survive, Ogre destroyed: complete defense victory."

The summary implies a binary win condition. In fact the scenario has six graduated victory outcomes. Most critically: the summary omits the Ogre escape condition (the Ogre must escape from the south end of the map to score a full victory — destroying the CP alone does not win unless the Ogre also escapes). The summary also invents "immobilizing the Ogre" as a defender win condition; the source does not list this as a victory condition, though a lone immobile Ogre is described as "helpless."

#### F3 — Defender win condition inaccurate: "immobilizing" not supported

Summary: "Defender wins: By destroying or immobilizing the Ogre before it reaches the Command Post."

Source: No scenario victory condition lists "immobilizing" as a defender win. The source says "An Ogre is not destroyed until all its firable weapons and tread units are gone." An immobile but armed Ogre is still a fighting threat, not a scenario win for the defender.

### Numbers checked: 2 (30 attack strength threshold from source, graduation of 6 victory outcomes). Supported: 1 (30 AP threshold). Not supported: "immobilize = defender win."

## olympos

### Prefilter
OK

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Findings

#### F1 — Development / Wonder cost wrong: 2 AP stated, source says 7 AP (MAJOR)

Summary (Action B / Player Reference):
> "Development costs **2 AP** on the Time Track."
> "Development: Pay resources → Take Discovery tile + Bonus → Advance 2 AP"

Source (sections on Discovery and Wonders):
> "Cost: **7 action points**. The player therefore moves his token 7 spaces along the Time Track. This ends his turn." (for Discoveries)
> "Cost: **7 action points**. The player therefore advances his token 7 spaces on the Time Track. This ends his turn." (for Wonders)

The summary states 2 AP for all Development actions (both Discoveries and Wonders). The actual cost is 7 AP for both — more than triple what the summary states. This governs the core time-economy of the game; underestimating this cost would fundamentally break game play.

#### F2 — Star tokens listed as universally worth 3 Prestige each — wrong, only applies with Engineering discovery (MAJOR)

Summary (Scoring):
> "Star tokens (from Tribes): 3 Prestige each"

Source (end-game scoring section, lines 476-502):
Star tokens are NOT listed in the end-game scoring section at all. The only scoring reference to Star tokens and 3 points is:
> "Engineering: the player benefits from a Star and earns **3 points for each of his Star tokens** at the end of the game."

Star tokens are worth 3 points at game end ONLY if the player has the Engineering discovery. Without Engineering, Star tokens score 0 points. The summary presents this as a universal scoring rule, which would mislead players into thinking Star tokens always score.

#### F3 — Time Track final position scoring omitted (MAJOR)

Summary (Scoring section): Does not mention the final position on the Time Track as a scoring source.

Source:
> "The player's final position on the Time Track earns between **0 and 5 points**."

This is a distinct scoring source listed first in the scoring section and is entirely absent from the summary's Scoring / Victory Conditions section.

#### F4 — Tiebreaker omitted (MINOR)

Summary: "Highest total Prestige wins." (no tiebreaker stated)

Source: "In the event of a tie, the player with the most discovery and Wonder tiles wins."

#### F5 — Setup: discovery tier setup for non-4-player games (MINOR)

Summary says "Discard tier-5 tiles in 4-player games." Source confirms this for 4-player. For 3 players, both tier-4 and tier-5 tiles are removed. The summary does not mention this variant difference, which is acceptable given the summary is for the 4-player default.

### Numbers checked: 11 (Development cost 7 AP × 2; Expansion create settler 2 AP; land 1 AP; sea 2 AP; combat 1/2/3 AP; Wonder 8-12 VP; Discovery 2 VP; territory 1 VP; Atlantis 2 VP; Time Track 0-5 pts). SUPPORTED: 9 (all except Development/Wonder costs and Star token scoring).
