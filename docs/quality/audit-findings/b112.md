## hamburgum

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Prefilter note
`scripts.audit prefilter` raised `SOURCE_MISMATCH` because the word "hamburgum" never appears in the extracted text. However, reading the first 50 lines confirms the source is the correct rulebook: it describes the game set in Hamburg 1890, names Stefan Feld, describes the same components (workers, wall segments, clerics, Hans Hummel statues, 280 cards, etc.), and the game-end condition "The player with the most victory points at the end of the game will be the new Mayor of the city" matches the summary exactly. The title in the PDF is simply "Hamburg" rather than "Hamburgum." The source is valid; detailed checks proceed.

### Finding 1 — Component count: "22 categories" (MINOR)

**Summary:** "280 playing cards (12 each of 22 categories in 5 colors, plus 20 Zoo and 20 Park cards)"

**Source (lines 40-64):** lists Art, Government, Bank, Church, Circus, Civic, Emigration, Entertainment, Craft, Harbor, Medicine, Industry, Military, Mysticism, Police, Secret Society, Theater, Commerce, Underworld, University — that is **20 categories** at 12 cards each, plus Zoo (20) and Park (20) separately. 20 × 12 + 20 + 20 = 280. The summary miscount of "22 categories" is internally inconsistent (it then lists Zoo and Park separately, implying they are not among the 22). The total card count (280) is correct.

**Impact:** Component miscount; does not affect gameplay.

### Finding 2 — Wall scoring phrasing (MINOR, borderline)

**Summary (Action 4):** "Building the 3rd segment on either side scores 3 points per segment at game end."

**Source (lines 476-478):** "If you have built the third segment of the left or right half of your city wall, you will gain 3 points for each of these segments at the end of the game."
**Source (final scoring, line 608):** "Each player who has built the third wall segment on either side of their city wall gains 3 points per side."
**Source (example, line 626):** "She has built one of her third city wall segments = 3 points."
**Source (glossary, lines 1138-1139):** "3rd wall segment: This is the special segment in the center of both halves of the wall, marked with '3 points'"

The summary's phrasing "3 points per segment" is technically consistent with "3 points for each of these [3rd] segments" from the action text — each side has one such special segment worth 3 points. The final scoring description and example confirm it is 3 points per side (not per each of the 5 segments on a side). The phrasing is ambiguous but not definitively wrong; a reader could misunderstand it as scoring 3 VP for every segment beyond the 3rd. Classified MINOR because the glossary and example make the intent clear.

### Victory & end condition

**Summary:** "Control a majority of nobles at the end of the scenario."
**Source (lines 429-430):** "The object of the game is to control a majority of Nobles at the end of the scenario." ✓
