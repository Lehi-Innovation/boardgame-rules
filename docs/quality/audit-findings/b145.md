## middle-earth-quest

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Victory & Game-End Conditions

**Finding 1 — Victory conditions fundamentally misrepresented.**

Summary: "The game ends after a set number of story stages; heroes win if they have thwarted enough of Sauron's plots."

Source: "The object of the game is for Sauron or the hero players to advance their story marker to the Finale space of the Story Track and complete the requirements on their Mission card." The Finale is triggered when any story marker reaches the Finale space (or all three Sauron markers reach The Shadow Falls space). Winning requires completing a secret Mission card during the Finale, or winning a combat against the Ringwraiths if no Mission is completed. There is no "set number of story stages" as an end trigger, and winning is not defined by "thwarting enough plots."

Summary: "The game tracks Sauron's and the heroes' progress on separate tracks; the side further ahead at game's end wins."

Source: This is completely wrong. The game does not end with a "who is further ahead" comparison. It ends in the Finale: the dominant side reveals its Mission card, and if it meets the Mission requirements it wins immediately. If neither side wins via Mission, both sides fight a combat (one hero vs the Ringwraiths), and victory goes to whichever side wins the combat. There is no "side further ahead wins" resolution.

**Finding 2 — Turn structure invented.**

Summary describes: "(1) Sauron's Shadow Phase, (2) Hero Phase, (3) Story Phase."

Source: The actual turn order is "(1) Sauron Turn; (2) Hero 1 Turn; (3) Hero 2 Turn; (4) Hero 3 Turn." There is no separate "Story Phase." Story advancement occurs within Sauron's turn during the "Story Step." The "Shadow Phase" is not a phase name used in the source. Sauron's turn consists of: Hero Rally Step → Story Step → Plot Step → Event Step → Action Step → Hero Draw Step. The summary's three-phase structure does not match the source.

**Finding 3 — Sauron's actions misrepresented.**

Summary: "Play shadow cards: Deploy minions, create obstacles, trigger events. Advance plots: Move plot markers toward completion. Corrupt: Spread corruption to heroes and locations."

Source: Sauron's Action Step offers three action types: "Place Influence," "Draw Shadow and Plot Cards," and "Command Monsters and Minions." Shadow cards are drawn as an action and played separately (one per player's turn). The summary's "Corrupt" action does not exist as a named action. Influence spreading (extending Shadow Strongholds) is the actual mechanic behind "corrupting locations," but it is not called "Corrupt" and is done via the "Place Influence" action.

**Finding 4 — Sauron player count incorrect.**

Summary: "3 hero figures and multiple minion figures" — Components section.

Source: "5 Plastic Hero Figures" and "5 Plastic Minion Figures." Three hero figures is wrong.

**Finding 5 — Mission card mechanic omitted.**

Summary never mentions Mission cards. Source states each side secretly draws a Mission card that determines how they can immediately win during the Finale. This is the primary win mechanism and its absence makes the victory section unplayable from the summary alone.

**Finding 6 — Ringwraith finale combat omitted.**

Summary says nothing about the Finale combat. Source: "If a side triggers the Finale, but fails to complete its Mission, then the winner of the game is determined by a final fight between a hero and the Ringwraiths." This is the tiebreaker/fallback victory mechanic.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (player counts, hero/minion figures, story marker counts, damage tokens), SUPPORTED: 2 (damage tokens, story markers count)

## minecraft-builders-biomes

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Action Structure — directly contradicted

**Finding 1 — "Same action twice" rule inverted.**

Summary: "On your turn, take exactly 2 of these 4 actions (you may take the same action twice)."

Source: "During your turn you must choose and perform two different actions. You cannot perform the same action twice."

The summary explicitly states players may take the same action twice; the source explicitly forbids it. This would change how the game is played every turn.

**Finding 2 — Action list wrong: "Collect blocks" is a choosable action, not a mandatory post-action.**

Summary: "After taking your 2 actions, mine 2 blocks from the resource cube."

Source's "LIST OF ACTIONS" includes: (1) Collect blocks, (2) Explore the Overworld, (3) Build, (4) Fight a mob, (5) Collect a weapon. "Collect blocks" is the first named action in the list and is one of the 5 actions players choose from. It is NOT a mandatory post-action. The summary also omits "Collect a weapon" from the action list entirely, and splits "Explore the Overworld" (which includes moving 0–2 spaces plus revealing 4 corner stacks) into separate "Explore" and "Move" actions that do not exist in the source.

**Finding 3 — Third scoring round criteria completely wrong.**

Summary: "Third scoring (after third layer): Score for the largest connected group of buildings with the same biome OR material combination."

Source: "The third scoring (C: Type) follows the same rules. Select the largest continuous group of structures of a particular type: decoration, dwelling, animal house, or bridge."

The summary describes a "biome OR material" criterion for the 3rd scoring round. The source says the 3rd round scores by **type** (decoration, dwelling, animal house, bridge). This is a totally different scoring dimension — players using the summary would score the wrong characteristic in round 3.

**Finding 4 — Mob scoring missing.**

Summary mentions "Experience points from defeated mobs" at game end but describes only the XP gained when defeating mobs during the game. Source: "The game ends after the third scoring round. Now, it's time to score each mob in your collection with icons of the following type: You gain that many experience points for each space on your player board with that characteristic. As opposed to the scoring rounds A, B, and C, the mob cards count all spaces of that characteristic. The spaces don't have to be connected." This end-of-game mob scoring (based on matching board spaces) is a distinct scoring phase that the summary omits.

**Finding 5 — Tiebreaker missing.**

Summary: "Winner: Most total experience points." Does not mention tiebreaker.

Source: "If there is a tie, the player with the most blocks in their supply wins the game. If there is still a tie, the tied players all win."

**Finding 6 — "Explore" action mechanics wrong.**

Summary: "Explore: Flip 2 face-down tiles adjacent to your skin's position."

Source: "First, move your character zero, one, or two spaces... Next, reveal the top cards of the four corner stacks around your position." The Explore action reveals 4 stacks (the corners of the crossing you end on), not 2 tiles. There is no "Move" action separate from Explore.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 11 (player count 2-4, 64 resource blocks, 16 wood/14 sand/12 stone/10 obsidian/12 emerald blocks, 5 weapon tokens per player, 4 hearts per player, 3 scoring rounds, scoring XP values from examples: 4xp/space biome, 3xp/space material, 5xp/space type), SUPPORTED: 8 (most block counts, player count, 3 scoring rounds)

## mission-red-planet

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Card Recycling — invented mechanic

**Finding 1 — "After round 5, all discarded cards return to hand" is invented.**

Summary: "Used character cards go to a discard pile. After round 5, all discarded cards return to hand."

Source: "Note: Once a character is used, it cannot be used again until the player plays the Recruiter card, which returns all of his played cards to his hand." And the Recruiter's effect: "9 Recruiter: The player takes all of his played character cards back into his hand, including the Recruiter card."

There is no "after round 5, all cards return" mechanic in the source. Cards only return to a player's hand when that player uses the Recruiter card. This is a fundamental misstatement of the card management system — players who follow the summary will have wrong hand sizes throughout the game and won't know the Recruiter's core function.

### Production — partially wrong

**Finding 2 — "Tied players split points (rounded down)" is wrong.**

Summary: "If tied for majority, tied players split points (rounded down)."

Source: "If two or more players tie for the most astronauts in a zone, the tied players evenly distribute the point tokens from that zone. Any remaining point tokens stay in the zone." The source describes distributing tokens (whole tokens), with remainders staying in the zone for future production — not "rounded down." The example confirms: "There are two players tied in a zone with three point tokens, so the two tied players each receive one of those point tokens, and the third point token stays in the zone." This also means second-majority players get nothing in a tie, which the summary doesn't clearly state.

### Character Card Details — partially wrong

**Finding 3 — Travel Agent description incorrect.**

Summary: "3 Travel Agent: Place 2 astronauts on docked ships."

Source: "3 Travel Agent: If there is not a docked ship with room for at least three more astronauts, the player ignores this effect and skips his turn." The source's clarification shows the Travel Agent effect is mandatory and must be fully resolved (placing 2 astronauts on one ship) or skipped entirely — but the summary says nothing about the 3-astronaut-capacity condition.

### Numbers check

Summary: 10 rounds, 86 point tokens (40 ice, 23 celerium, 23 sylvanite), 36 ship cards, 30 event cards (13 missions, 13 discoveries, 4 actions), 54 character cards, 132 plastic astronauts, 20 destination tokens, 11 resource tokens, 9 bonus points for Ice Monopoly.

Source confirms: 10 rounds (round tracker), 86 point tokens (40/23/23), 36 ship cards, 30 event cards (13/13/4), 54 character cards (9 per color), 132 plastic astronauts (22 per color), 20 destination tokens (2 per zone), 11 resource tokens, 9 points for Ice Monopoly. All checked numbers match the source.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 13 (rounds, point token counts, ship cards, event card types, character cards, astronauts, destination tokens, resource tokens, ice monopoly bonus, ice/celerium/sylvanite values), SUPPORTED: 10 (all component counts and resource values supported; card-recycling round number not supported)

## mississippi-queen

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Running out of coal — contradicted

**Finding 1 — Summary says coal depletion does NOT eliminate players; source says it does.**

Summary: "A player who runs out of coal can no longer change speed or make extra turns, but continues moving at their current speed." And under Special Rules: "Running out of coal does not eliminate you, but severely limits maneuverability."

Source: "When the last coal point is used, remove the black wheel and return it to the box. If an extra coal point is needed later, then that player drops out of the game and his paddle-wheeler is removed from the river."

Running out of coal and then needing one more coal point eliminates the player entirely. The summary directly contradicts this by saying elimination does not happen.

### Passenger pickup rule — wrong

**Finding 2 — "Adjacent to dock at speed 1" is wrong; you must move TO the boarding platform.**

Summary: "You must end your movement adjacent to a passenger dock. Your speed must be exactly 1 to pick up a passenger."

Source: "To collect a passenger, a paddle-wheeler must move to the boarding platform shown extending from an island at a speed of 1." The source requires the paddlewheeler to move ONTO the boarding platform, not just be adjacent to it. Being adjacent at speed 1 does not satisfy the requirement.

### Components — wrong coal count

**Finding 3 — Coal component description wrong.**

Summary Components: "12 coal tokens (2 per player)."

Source: There are no coal tokens. Coal is tracked on individual black wheels, one per player, starting at 6. Each player has 6 coal, not 2. The summary's component list says 2 per player (contradicting the setup section which says "Each player receives 6 coal tokens").

### Turn order (introductory vs. passenger game)

**Finding 4 — Summary conflates two game modes for turn order.**

Summary: "Players take turns in order determined by position (leader goes first)."

Source: The introductory game uses clockwise order from starting positions ("The player with a paddle-wheeler on space 1 starts, others following in clockwise order"). The passenger game uses position order with the lead paddlewheeler moving first ("the lead paddle-wheeler moves first each turn rather than movement being in clockwise order"). The summary applies the passenger-game rule to both modes without distinction.

### Finishing rule — partially wrong

**Finding 5 — Passenger game finish requires speed 1 at landing platform, not just "cross finish line."**

Summary: "The first player to cross the finish line with both required passengers aboard wins."

Source: "The first paddle-wheeler to reach the landing platform with two passengers wins. As when collecting passengers, the paddle-wheeler must be traveling at a speed of one."

The summary omits the speed-1 requirement for finishing, which is a mandatory rule. The tiebreaker "player furthest past the finish line wins" has no support in the source (the source says the first to reach an entry point wins; no "furthest past" rule is described).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (3-5 players, 6 coal per player, speed range 1-6, 2 passengers required, 1 free speed change, 1 free turn, speed at finish = 1, passenger pickup speed = 1), SUPPORTED: 6 (player count, speed range, 2 passengers, 1 free speed change, 1 free turn, passenger pickup speed = 1)
