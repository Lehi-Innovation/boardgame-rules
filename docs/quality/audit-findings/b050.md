## chancellorsville — MAJOR

`rules/chancellorsville.md` vs `extracted/chancellorsville-rules.txt`. All component counts and almost all two-player numbers are accurate, but the summary omits the attacker's post-combat resolution (a core combat outcome that applies to nearly every attack), garbles the solitaire victory conditions, and omits the entire solitaire BOT system despite billing the game as 1-2 players and including a Solitaire Victory section.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR OMISSION — attacker post-combat resolution missing.**
   - Summary (Combat / Player Reference): describes the dice results table and defender retreats only; says nothing about what happens to the attacker after combat.
   - Source: "If the defender is not forced to retreat, the attacker must retreat back to the location they passed through or came from to begin the attack." (l.695-699) and "If the defender is destroyed the attacker MUST occupy the location attacked." (l.702-703)
   - Impact: double 6s is the only result that forces a defender retreat, so in most combats the attacker must bounce back — players using this summary would leave the attacker stacked with the defender, which the game does not allow (combat MUST occur when units share a location, l.336-339). This breaks combat resolution at the table in nearly every attack.

2. **ERROR — solitaire victory conditions garbled.**
   - Summary: "Confederate BOT must hold 2 objectives or destroy 3 Union formations."
   - Source (19.1, l.871-887): when the human plays Confederate, it is the human "Confederate player [who] must destroy 3 Union formations for an automatic win," and "The BOT [the Union BOT] must hold just 2 objectives (like the player in the two player game) to win." When the human plays Union, the Confederate BOT has no "hold 2 objectives" condition — it wins simply "if the Confederate player stops the Union BOT from winning" (i.e., Union fails to hold all 3 objectives or destroy 3 formations).
   - Impact: a solo Union player could believe holding 2 of 3 objectives denies the Confederate BOT; the source says Union must hold all 3 (which the summary's previous sentence states, making the section internally contradictory).

3. **MAJOR OMISSION — entire solitaire BOT system absent.**
   - The summary lists BOT activation cards in Components and gives Solitaire Victory conditions, but omits the BOT rules entirely. Missing core solo systems, all in the source:
     - BOT uses a completely different combat results table applied per die: 1 = attacker -2; 2 = attacker -1; 3 = defender -1; 4 = defender -2; 5 = defender -2; 6 = defender -3 + retreat (l.1083-1091) — vs the player's doubles-based table.
     - BOT terrain modifier is +1 die for green double-headed arrows, not +2 (l.1077-1079); BOT always attacks with minimum 1 die (l.1080-1082).
     - BOT never pays activation costs and never receives Momentum cubes; Tactics deck never cycles on BOT turns (l.946-953).
     - Player redoubts subtract BOT attack dice (1 die per redoubt consumed) instead of setting die faces; vs the BOT, player-set redoubt dice are set to 1 (l.1093-1115).
     - Hidden Movement Track mechanics (l.1001-1019), no screens/Hidden Movement Map in solo (l.891-892), reinforcement decks unused in solo (l.921-922), three difficulty settings (Standard/Hard/Hardest, l.925-935), player always takes first turn in solo (l.936-939).
   - Impact: a solo player would have to run the BOT with the two-player combat table and modifiers — wrong values for every BOT attack.

4. **MINOR OMISSIONS (two-player):**
   - Re-hiding: sighted formations no longer adjacent to enemies return to the Hidden Movement Map (l.149-152); summary only covers revealing.
   - Retreat tie-breaks: retreat to the location with the fewest friendly formations (l.726-727); a formation that cannot retreat is destroyed; if only one of two can retreat, the higher-cohesion formation is saved (l.744-748); Confederate retreat from 22 goes 21/23 then 12 (l.734-737).
   - Objective control reversion timing: Confederate occupation at the end of a Confederate turn removes the Union control marker (l.186-191).
   - Major activation may not attack once then build a redoubt / transfer reinforcements (l.410-416).
   - Momentum from rolled 3s is collected only after all rolling including re-rolls (l.692-694).
