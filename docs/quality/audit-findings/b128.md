## last-night-on-earth-the-zombie-game

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Combat dice are reversed (MAJOR)

Summary states:
> "Hero attacks Zombie: Roll 1d6. On a result of the Hero's fight value or higher, the Zombie is killed."
> "Zombie attacks Hero: Roll 2d6 (Zombie dice) vs. Hero's 1d6 (fight dice). If Zombie total >= Hero total, the Hero takes a wound."

Source (extracted, lines 770–772):
> "- Heroes roll 2 Fight Dice
> - Zombies roll 1 Fight Dice
> - Zombies win on a tie."

The summary has the dice counts reversed. Heroes roll 2 dice (taking the higher); Zombies roll 1 die. The summary invents a "1d6 Hero attack" and "2d6 Zombie attack" system that does not exist; the actual system is symmetric (both roll, compare highest die). Any player using the summary's combat table would run fights completely wrong.

### Finding 2 — Zombie Turn step order is wrong (MAJOR)

Summary Zombie Turn:
> 1. Draw Zombie Card
> 2. Move Zombies
> 3. Spawn New Zombies (Roll 2d6)
> 4. Fight

Source (extracted, lines 485–491):
> 1. Move the Sun Track Marker
> 2. Draw New Zombie Cards
> 3. Roll to Spawn New Zombies
> 4. Move Zombies
> 5. Fight
> 6. Place Newly Spawned Zombies

The summary omits Step 1 (Sun Track advance happens at start of Zombie Turn, not Hero Turn) and Step 6 (place newly spawned Zombies after fight), and shows Move before the spawn roll, when the source shows the roll precedes movement.

### Finding 3 — Sun Track advance is in the wrong turn (MAJOR)

Summary Hero Turn step 4:
> "Advance Sun Track: Move the Sun Track Marker 1 space."

Source (extracted, lines 459–460):
> "At the start of each following Zombie Turn the Sun Track Marker is moved one space forward on the track (counting down)."

And confirmed by the 6-step Zombie Turn (Step 1: Move the Sun Track Marker). The summary places Sun Track advancement in the Hero Turn; the source places it as step 1 of the Zombie Turn.

### Finding 4 — Zombies auto-win on killing Heroes: wrong threshold (MAJOR)

Summary does not list a global Zombies auto-win for killing Heroes (only scenario-specific losses). Source (extracted, lines 934–940):
> "In addition to the normal Objectives, in any Scenario (unless specifically stated otherwise) the Zombies automatically win the game if:
> - They kill Four (4) Heroes (This includes turning Heroes into Zombies in the Advanced Game)"

The summary's scenario "Die, Zombie, Die!" entry says "Zombie Players win if the Heroes fail to kill enough Zombies, or by killing 2 of the Heroes." The source (extracted, line 366–368) confirms killing 2 Heroes wins the *scenario* Die Zombies, Die!, but the global rule is 4 Heroes. The summary conflates the scenario-specific condition (2) with what is actually a scenario-specific threshold, while completely omitting the global rule (4 Heroes = automatic Zombie win in any scenario).

### Finding 5 — Spawn mechanic description is incorrect (MAJOR)

Summary:
> "Spawn New Zombies: Roll 2d6. For each Spawning Pit, if the roll meets the spawn requirement, place a new Zombie on that pit. Max 14 Zombies on the board."

Source (extracted, lines 479–507 and 557–577):
The spawn roll (2d6) determines whether *additional* Zombies can be placed — if the roll exceeds the number of Zombies the player already controls on the board, they may place new ones at the end of the turn (step 6). There is no "spawn requirement per pit" — the mechanic is a single threshold check (roll > current Zombie count), not per-pit requirements. The summary also places spawning in the wrong step (step 3 of 4 in the summary; source: roll in step 3, placement in step 6 after fighting).

## le-havre

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Feeding cost source is wrong (MAJOR)

Summary:
> "Players must pay food equal to the number shown on their Food production token."

Source (extracted, lines 453–455):
> "Each player must pay the amount of food shown on the Cooking pot symbol on the Round card."

The summary states that the feeding requirement comes from the player's own Food production token, but the source states it comes from the Round card's cooking pot symbol. Food production tokens track what ships *provide* to reduce the feeding requirement; the required amount is set by the Round card. A player following the summary would look at the wrong place for their feeding obligation.

## leaders-the-combined-strategy-game

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn phase list is completely wrong (MAJOR)

Summary phases per round:
> 1. Resource Phase: Collect resources from controlled territories.
> 2. Diplomacy Phase: Negotiate alliances, trade deals, or declare war.
> 3. Action Phase: Move units, build structures, recruit military forces.
> 4. Combat Phase: Resolve conflicts.
> 5. Event Phase: App-driven events.

Source (extracted, lines 193–200):
> "Each turn consists of 6 phases which must be carried out in the following order:
> 1. Withdraw units
> 2. Enter production points
> 3. Events
> 4. Headquarters
> 5. Command units
> 6. Battle"

The summary invents 5 phases that do not exist in the source (Resource, Diplomacy, Action, Combat, Event). The actual 6 phases are: Withdraw units, Enter production points, Events, Headquarters, Command units, Battle. The event phase (Events) occurs 3rd in the actual order, not 5th; there is no "Resource Phase" or "Diplomacy Phase" as separate phases; resource management ("production points") happens as part of Phase 2; diplomacy happens within Headquarters (Phase 4). Any player following the summary would play the turn structure completely wrong.

### Finding 2 — Victory conditions are wrong / fabricated (MAJOR)

Summary:
> "The player who achieves the dominant position (most territories, strongest military, most resources) at the end of the game wins. Specific victory conditions are managed and announced by the companion app."

Source (extracted, lines 354–368):
> "END OF THE GAME AND PATHS TO VICTORY
> As soon as a player fulfills the conditions for victory specified in the scenario, he/she has won the game…
> - Military: A player wins when his/her total on the indicator when the production points are entered reaches the number specified by the scenario.
> - Research: A player wins when he/she has completed all their research strands (military, technological, economic).
> - Missions: A player wins when he/she has completed enough successful missions to be awarded the rank of 'LEADER'."

The summary's vague "dominant position" description is fabricated — there is no end-of-game "dominant position" scoring. The game has three distinct mid-game victory paths, any one of which wins immediately when achieved. The summary presents it as a points-at-game-end system, which is completely wrong.

### Finding 3 — Retreat rule

Source (lines 334–337):
> "If a player decides to execute a retreat, half of their units (rounded up) in the respective area are lost. The remaining units are returned to base camp and may not be deployed again until the player's next turn."

Summary does not mention retreats at all. This is a significant omission for an action in Battle. However, since the summary's turn structure is entirely fabricated, this falls under the MAJOR verdict already reached.

### Finding 4 — Starting player compensation rule (omitted)

Source (lines 163–173): Players receive infantry units in base camp to compensate first-player advantage: Player 1 gets 1, Player 2 gets 2, through Player 6 gets 6 units. The summary contains no mention of this setup mechanic.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 3 (player count max, starting infantry compensation, retreat rounding), SUPPORTED: 1 (player count = up to 6 supported by source line 7)
