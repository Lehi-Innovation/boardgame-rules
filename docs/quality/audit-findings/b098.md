## fog-of-love

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Source is ~1150-line rulebook extract. Read first 200 lines fully; used targeted grep for key claims.

### Finding 1 — MAJOR: Personality Dimensions Listed as Wrong Pairings; Invented Term

Summary: "There are 6 personality dimensions, each tracked on a scale: Discipline (vs. Curiosity), Sincerity (vs. Extravagance), Sensitivity (vs. Gentleness)"

Source (lines 315-338): Lists 6 independent dimensions — each with a positive and a negative aspect (not pairings with other dimensions):
- Sincerity / Deceitful
- Extroversion / Introverted
- Sensitivity / Thick-skinned
- Curiosity / Closed-minded
- Gentleness / Rough
- Discipline / Disorganized

Source (line 344): "(j) A personality dimension. There are 6 personality dimensions."
Source (lines 351-352): "It contains six personality dimensions (Discipline, Curiosity, etc.), each with two aspects represented by an up and down arrow."

The summary's framing is wrong in three distinct ways:
1. "Discipline (vs. Curiosity)" — these are NOT a paired dimension; they are two separate dimensions.
2. "Sincerity (vs. Extravagance)" — "Extravagance" does not appear anywhere in the source. The sixth dimension is "Extroversion" (searched: "Extravagance", "extravag" — no matches found).
3. "Sensitivity (vs. Gentleness)" — these are two separate dimensions, not opposites.

A player using the summary would think there are 3 dimension-tracks (Discipline/Curiosity, Sincerity/Extravagance, Sensitivity/Gentleness) and would play the game with a completely wrong understanding of the personality system — unable to correctly place personality tokens or evaluate trait goals.

### Finding 2 — MAJOR: Destiny Setup is Wrong

Summary (Setup step 7): "Deal Destiny cards (each player picks one secretly)."

Source (lines 152-154): "Initially you have a handful of destiny cards, but as the game progresses you'll secretly discard most of these until you select a final destiny at the end of the game — in the finale."
Source (lines 930-935): "Both players start with the same destinies in hand. An important part of the game is deducing which destinies your co-player has kept or discarded...At the end of the game, at the finale, you will have to choose a single final destiny."

Players do NOT pick one destiny at setup. They start with a hand of multiple destinies and narrow them down over the course of the game, choosing their final one at the finale. The summary describes the opposite of how destiny works — it removes the key deduction mechanic entirely.

### Finding 3 — MINOR: Features Selected in Turns, Not Simultaneously

Summary (Setup step 2): "Each player selects 3 Feature trait cards for their partner... Players reveal these simultaneously."

Source (lines 472-486): "In turn, select a feature for your co-player's character. First player starts. Place the feature on your co-player's side of the board...Continue this back and forth until you each have selected and placed 3 features for the other player's character."

Features are NOT revealed simultaneously — they are chosen and placed alternately in turns. The summary's "revealed simultaneously" is incorrect, but the practical impact is lesser than the other errors.

### Numbers/Procedural Verification

- Summary: "6 personality dimensions" — Source confirms 6. SUPPORTED.
- Summary: "110 scenes, 38 traits, 60 features, 36 occupations, 14 destinies" — Source (lines 175-179) confirms all. SUPPORTED.
- Summary: "Personality tokens (35 per player plus 5-value tokens)" — Source (lines 185-186) says "70 personality tokens (35 pink and 35 blue) [plus] 10 personality tokens with a value of 5." SUPPORTED.
- Summary: "Both players start with the same destinies" — Source (line 930) confirms. SUPPORTED.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (6 dimensions, 110 scenes, 38 traits, 60 features, 36 occupations, 14 destinies, 35+35 tokens, 5-value tokens), SUPPORTED: 8, CONTRADICTED: 1 (dimension pairings), MAJOR_ERROR: 2 (dimension structure/naming, destiny setup)
