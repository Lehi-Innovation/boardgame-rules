## alhambra — MAJOR

The base-game summary is highly faithful (all spot-checked numbers correct), but the 2-player variant is an invented mechanic that contradicts the source, and a scoring-relevant end-game rule is omitted.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented 2-player "Dirk's turn" mechanic.**
   - Summary: "When it is Dirk's turn, draw a tile from the bag matching the next currency in order and place it in Dirk's area."
   - Source (2-player rules, decoded): "There is an imaginary third player... Dirk does not build an Alhambra but **does collect building tiles. Dirk does not have any turns.** At the beginning of the game **6 building tiles are drawn randomly from the bag** [for Dirk]... Directly after the first scoring round... Dirk is given 6 more tiles... After the 2nd scoring round Dirk is... given **a third of the tiles remaining in the bag (rounded down)**." Also: players "can also give the building tile to Dirk" when buying, and "only 72 money cards are used" (one of each removed).
   - The summary's per-turn, currency-driven draw mechanic appears nowhere in the source; the actual schedule (6 / 6 / one-third of bag), the give-a-tile-to-Dirk option, and the 72-card deck are all missing. Source also says Dirk scores buildings "but **not** for an external wall" — summary says "count Dirk's building tiles just like a regular player" without the wall caveat.

2. **MINOR — Omitted end-game rule affecting final scoring.** Source (decoded, "Game end" section): "The remaining building tiles from the building market are given to the players who have the most money of the respective currency in their hand... If two or more players have the same amount of money then that building tile remains on the building market." These free tiles count toward 3rd-scoring majorities; the summary jumps straight from game end to the 3rd scoring round.

3. **Trivial — omissions:** the "100"-tiles (used when a player exceeds 100 VP) and the end-game tie rule ("If two players are level the game is a draw") are not mentioned.

## alien-frontiers — MAJOR

The facility table is wrong for most facilities, a facility is invented, the Relic Ship acquisition is wrong, and the victory condition directly contradicts the source.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Victory condition contradicted.**
   - Summary: "The first player to place all of their colonies (typically 8) onto the planet wins immediately. There is no point scoring -- it is a race to place all colonies."
   - Source: "Gaining more influence... (measured in Victory Points)"; "You score 1 victory point for... Each of your colony tokens on a territory; Each territory you control; Having the Alien City card; Having the Alien Monument card; Controlling the territory with the Positron Field"; "The game ends as soon as one player places their last colony on a territory... **the player with the most victory points wins**." There is a scoring track and elaborate tiebreakers. The summary inverts the core win condition.

2. **MAJOR — Colony count wrong.** Summary: "typically 8". Source: 4 players = **six** colony tokens; 3 players = seven; 2 players = eight; 5/6 players = six; only the optional Long Game uses eight for all.

3. **MAJOR — Facility requirements/effects wrong in 6 of 9 table rows:**
   - Solar Converter: summary "Gain fuel equal to die value"; source "You gain fuel equal to **one half the value** of each ship... Round up."
   - Lunar Mine: summary "Any 1 die / Gain ore equal to die value"; source: each new ship "must be equal to or greater than the highest value ship currently docked"; "You gain **one ore for each ship**."
   - Orbital Market: summary "Any 2 dice / Trade fuel for ore or vice versa"; source: "two ships of **equal value**"; trade is one-way — "pay fuel equal to the value of one of your docked ships to receive one ore."
   - Colonist Hub: summary "Matching triple / Place 1 colony"; source: Colonist Hub takes ships of any value on an advancement track (seven circles), launch by "paying one fuel and one ore." The matching triple belongs to the **Colony Constructor** ("three ships of equal value and pay three ore"), which the summary omits entirely.
   - "Colony Ship — Straight (sequential) — Place 1 colony": **invented facility**; no such facility exists in the source. The 3-die straight is the Raiders' Outpost requirement.
   - Raider's Outpost: summary "Matching pair / Steal resources or an Alien Tech card"; source: "a set of **three sequentially numbered ships**"; steal "a total of **four resources**... or one Alien Tech card."
   - Alien Artifact: summary "Any 1 die / Draw Alien Tech card"; source: any-value ships only cycle the display; "To claim one of the Alien Tech cards... docked ships with a **total value of 8 or more**." Cards are claimed from a face-up display, not drawn.
   - Terraforming Station: requirement (a 6) correct, but summary omits the cost ("pay one fuel and one ore") and that the ship is "completely consumed."

4. **MAJOR — Relic Ship acquisition wrong.** Summary: "claimed from the Alien Artifact facility." Source: "Burroughs Desert — Purchase the Relic Ship for 1 fuel and 1 ore" (a territory-control bonus).

5. **MINOR — Docking obligation misstated.** Summary: "You must place at least 1 die per turn." Source: "you **must dock all of your ships** at orbital facilities on your turn, if possible" (undockable ships go to the Maintenance Bay, which the summary never mentions).

6. **MINOR — Omissions:** Maintenance Bay facility; 8-resource hand limit at end of turn ("more than eight total resource tokens... must return excess"); setup detail (3 Alien Tech cards on display + 1 dealt per player; turn-order compensation of fuel/ore); colony placement scoring of 1 VP immediately.

## altiplano — MAJOR

Components and most numbers are excellent, but the summary contradicts the source on action-phase turn structure and movement, and invents two scoring sources.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Action phase wrongly described as simultaneous.**
   - Summary (Turn Structure, phase 3): "Move your figure to locations... and execute planned actions there. **All players act simultaneously.**"
   - Source: "Now players, **in turn and in clockwise order**, carry out actions. Beginning with the starting player, one player carries out one action; then the next player has his turn... This continues until all players have passed." (Phases 1 and 2 are simultaneous; phase 3 is not.) This changes the game fundamentally (action order and passing matter).

2. **MAJOR — Movement mechanic wrong / roads misdescribed.**
   - Summary: movement is "1 step per turn or more with roads"; Road location = "Get corn, build roads for movement."
   - Source: each player has a **cart**, usable "once per round for free... up to 3 steps"; extra moves cost "1 food for each step"; additional carts allow "up to 3 steps" plus 1 food. Road construction (1 stone + 1 wood) has nothing to do with movement: "By constructing the first road, you increase the number of your **planning spaces** to 5... the second road, you obtain 1 corn... Additional road construction either increases the number of your planning spaces or gives you corn."

3. **MAJOR — Invented scoring sources.**
   - Summary scoring list includes "**Extensions purchased**" and "Remaining goods and **coins**."
   - Source scoring: goods values (0-4 pts each), boats 2 pts, houses 4 pts, completed orders, house bonuses (+1 per matching good), warehouse row points, missions. Extensions are never scored. Coins explicitly score nothing: "Coins aren't worth any points in the end"; they are only the tiebreaker ("In case of a tie, the tied player with the most coins wins").

4. **MINOR — Scoring scope/omissions.** Summary omits the house bonus (+1 point per good of the boosted type) and presents warehouse storage as the scoring vehicle, whereas the source scores **all** goods tiles (bag, container, planning/action spaces) by value, with completed warehouse rows giving additional row points.

5. **MINOR — Phase 4 misdescribed.** Summary: "Clean-up Phase: Return used tiles to your container." Source: tiles go to the container immediately when actions are carried out (phase 3); the bag is refilled from the container during phase 1. Source phase 4 ("New round") is starting-player rotation, cart reset, and extension-strip refill — including the rule that if no extension was bought, the bottom card is removed from the game (omitted).

6. **Trivial:** game-end description omits that "the current round is completed and then **one more complete round** is played."

## amerigo — MAJOR

Round/phase structure numbers are right, but 4 of 7 cube-color action mappings are wrong, the per-phase action structure is wrong, a component is invented, and end-game scoring is substantially wrong.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Cube color → action mapping wrong for 4 of 7 colors.**
   - Source: Blue = Move ships; Black = Load cannons; **Red = Plan** (take village/landscape tiles from supply); **Brown = Progress**; Green = Build; **Yellow = Buy production tokens**; **White = Special action** (change player order / wildcard action).
   - Summary: Red = "Buy progress tokens" (wrong — that is Brown's effect; Red is Plan); Brown = "Trade goods for victory points" (no such action exists); Yellow = "Plan/store actions for later use" (wrong — Yellow buys production tokens); White = "Produce goods from settled islands" (no production action exists; commodities are gained by building over commodity spaces: "If a player builds over a commodity space, he takes that commodity token").

2. **MAJOR — Per-phase action structure wrong.**
   - Summary: "Each player takes actions equal to the number of cubes that fell" / "All players take that many actions of that type."
   - Source: "During each phase, each player takes **exactly one action**"; "The **largest number of cubes of a single color** determines the number of action points (AP) that can be spent on a single available action"; colors of emerged cubes determine which actions are choosable. Also omitted: taking gold instead of an action (cubes ÷ 3, rounded up), and spending gold for +1 AP each.

3. **MAJOR — Scoring wrong/incomplete.**
   - Summary end-game list: tile placement VP, island bonuses, "Goods traded", progress token bonuses, "**Remaining cannons**", pirate penalties.
   - Source final scoring: (1) **Gold — 1 VP per gold** (after converting chests and 1x tokens); (2) **Goods — commodity tokens × production tokens per type** (no trading anywhere); (3) Progress — "E" tokens plus highest VP value reached on the progress track; (4) **Special action track** VP. Remaining cannons score nothing — "If a player still has cannons remaining... he keeps these for the next game round." The summary invents cannon scoring and goods "trading" and omits gold and the two track scorings.
   - Pirate attacks are intermediate-scoring events each round (lose VP equal to full pirate strength if cannons insufficient) — summary's description is acceptable at this altitude.

4. **MINOR — Invented component / setup step.** "Production wheel" appears in Components and Setup ("Set up the production wheel"); no such component exists in the source component list (it has a storage board with production token spaces). Also, ships are not placed during setup ("Choose starting positions for player ships"); source: "The first time a player chooses this action [Move Ships], he places his two ships on any spaces of his choice on the frame."

5. **MINOR — Omissions:** 3 VP for first trading post on an island (discovery); island completion payout = trading posts × current time marker (summary says only "bonus points"); trading posts as the prerequisite to build on an island; gold compensation for turn order at setup; 12-cap on gold/cannon tracks.
