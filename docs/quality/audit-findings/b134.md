## lyngk

### Verdict: MINOR

### Finding 1 — Contradictory component/setup note (minor numerical confusion)

**Summary (Components section, line 28):** "Total: 48 pieces on the board + 5 pieces set aside for claiming colors."

**Source:** "Place 8 pieces of each color and the 3 white pieces randomly on the board, so that each spot (i.e. where the lines intersect) is occupied." (English rules, section C, line 351-353); "Line up the remaining pieces (1 ivory, 1 blue, 1 red, 1 black and 1 green) next to the board." (line 354-355)

Calculation: 8 × 5 active colors = 40 + 3 white = 43 on board; 5 aside = 48 total. The summary states "48 pieces on the board" which is wrong (43 go on the board). However, the setup instructions immediately above this note correctly state "Place 8 pieces of each of the 5 active colors plus the 3 white joker pieces" giving the right 43, so players following the setup step would not be misled. This is a contradictory note, not a procedure error.

### Procedural verification

**Victory condition:** Summary: "The player with the most completed 5-piece stacks wins." Source: "The winner is the player with the most stacks of 5 differently colored pieces." (line 521-522). MATCH.

**Game-end trigger:** Summary: "The game ends when no more moves are possible for either player." Source: "The game ends when the last possible move has been made." (line 520-521). MATCH.

**Tiebreaker:** Summary: "In case of a tie, count 4-piece stacks on the board, then 3-piece stacks, and so on." Source: "In case of a tie, the winner is the player with the most stacks of 4 pieces on the board. If that still does not determine who wins, then count the stacks of 3 pieces, and so on." (lines 523-526). MATCH.

**Neutral single piece movement:** Summary: "Single neutral piece: May only move onto another single piece of a different color. Cannot jump onto a stack." Source: "A single neutral piece (i.e. of a color that has not been claimed yet) can only move onto another single piece of any other color. … A single neutral piece cannot jump onto a stack." (lines 431-437). MATCH.

**Neutral stack movement:** Summary: "Stack with neutral top piece: May jump onto a single piece or onto a stack of equal or lesser height." Source: "A stack with a neutral piece on top of it may jump onto any single piece or onto a stack of at most the same height. It cannot jump onto a higher stack." (lines 446-451). MATCH.

**Claimed piece movement:** Summary: "Claimed piece/stack (top is a claimed color): May move onto any piece or stack regardless of height." Source: "A single piece of a claimed color or a stack with a claimed color on top of it may be moved onto any other piece or stack." (lines 452-456). MATCH.

**Completed stack (claimed top):** Summary: "the owning player removes it and places it on their side. It scores 1 point." Source: "they must remove the stack and put it on their side of the board … A removed stack is worth one point at the end of the game." (lines 457-461). MATCH.

**Completed stack (neutral top):** Summary: "When a 5-piece stack is completed with a neutral color on top, it remains on the board as an obstacle and scores for neither player." Source: "When a player completes a stack of 5 pieces with a neutral color on top, then the stack remains on the board as an obstacle. This stack does not count as a point for any of the players." (lines 462-465). MATCH.

**LYNGK-rule:** Summary: "Pieces of the same claimed color are 'connected' if they can reach each other via regular moves. A player may use a claimed-color piece as a LYNGK-point (waypoint) during a move: move toward it but do not land on it; instead, continue from that point to another piece." Source: "pieces of one and the same claimed color are connected, but only under the condition that they can be moved towards each other with a regular move. … you may move a piece of a claimed color towards another piece of that color, but it may not be put on top of it; instead you use the reached piece as a LYNGK-point, meaning that you must make a second move from there." (lines 481-496). MATCH.

**Joker as LYNGK-point:** Summary: "Joker pieces may not serve as LYNGK-points." Source: "It is not allowed to use a joker piece as a LYNGK-point." (lines 512-513). MATCH.

**Variant (Stack of 6):** Summary: "Completing a 6-piece stack with your claimed color on top wins the game immediately (sudden death)." Source: "The player who succeeds in making a stack of 6 pieces, with one of his claimed colors on top, wins instantly." (lines 568-570). MATCH.

**Passing rule:** Summary: "Passing is not allowed unless no legal move exists." Source: "It is not allowed to pass, unless a player has no more possible moves." (line 468). MATCH.

**Exhaustion/reshuffle:** No deck or supply depletion rules; not applicable to this abstract game.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (piece counts, stack sizes, joker count, tiebreak order), SUPPORTED: 7 (one contradictory note on total vs board count)

## macao

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Finding 1 — City Quarter Cost: WRONG (invented flat cost)

**Summary (Actions 3.2):** "Pay 1 action cube to place an ownership marker on an adjacent empty city quarter (connected to where your disc is on the wall or to a quarter you already own). Take the goods tile from that quarter."

**Source:** "In order to take possession of a quarter, a player must put exactly the AC shown in this quarter from his supply of action cube supply back into the general supply. Then, he exchanges the tile on the quarter for one of his ownership marker." (lines 301-304)

The source says the cost is "exactly the AC shown in this quarter" — each quarter has its own printed cost (specific number and colors of action cubes). The summary replaces this with a flat "1 action cube" cost, which is completely wrong. The cost is variable per quarter, not a uniform 1 cube.

### Finding 2 — Adjacency Rule for City Quarters: UNSUPPORTED (possibly invented)

**Summary:** "adjacent empty city quarter (connected to where your disc is on the wall or to a quarter you already own)"

**Source (lines 298-318):** No adjacency or connectivity requirement is mentioned for taking possession of a city quarter. The rule simply states you pay the shown AC cost. No support found (searched: adjacent, connected, disc, wall, nearby, contiguous, neighbor, chain).

This adjacency/connectivity constraint does not exist in the source rules. Any quarter with the correct AC cost can be taken.

### Finding 3 — Missing "Move Forward on Wall" Action

**Summary Actions section:** Lists 3.1 Activate Cards, 3.2 Acquire City Quarter, 3.3 Move Ship, 3.4 Deliver Goods, 3.5 Acquire Prestige. The "Move forward on wall" action is entirely absent.

**Source (lines 345-352):** "3.3) Move forward once on the wall (AC colors are unimportant). The player can move his disc forward along the wall once per round. For each one AC (any color) that the player pays from his action cube supply to the general supply, he moves his disc forward one space along the wall. After moving his disc one space, he can move it further by returning any two AC for each one further space he moves his disc along the wall."

The wall movement action (numbered 3.3 in the source, which shifts ship move to 3.4 and deliver goods to not a numbered action) is a core mechanic of Macao that determines turn order and the city wall track. It is completely absent from the summary's Actions section.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreaker: CORRECT but verify

**Summary:** "Ties broken by gold coins remaining."
**Source (lines 459-462):** "If players tie with the most, the player among those who is further along the wall is the winner."

The tiebreaker is wall position, not gold coins. The summary has an entirely wrong tiebreaker.

### Finding 5 — Final Scoring: Goods Tiles Miscounted

**Summary (Scoring):** "Each goods tile still on the player's tableau may score bonus points based on activated card effects."

**Source (Game End, lines 440-457):** The final scoring section lists: (1) punish markers (−3 PP each), (2) "game end" activated cards, (3) city scoring (2 PP per marker in longest connected string). There is no mention of goods tiles scoring at game end. Goods tiles score when delivered to ports during the game. No support found for goods tiles scoring at end via card effects (searched: goods, ware, tile, end).

### Finding 6 — Deliver Goods Cost Claim Unverifiable (minor)

**Summary Player Reference:** "Deliver goods: free (when at correct port)"
**Source (line 366-371):** "he may unload any goods tiles he has on his ship that match the goods wanted by that port. He places the tile(s) on the highest empty spaces available at the port (delivering goods does not cost the player AC!)." MATCH — delivery is free.

### Procedural verification

**Victory condition:** Summary: "The player with the most prestige points wins." Source: "The winner is the player who has moved furthest along the prestige track — earned the most prestige points." (lines 459-460). MATCH.

**Punish marker cost:** Summary: "each punish marker deducts 3 PP." Source: "for each punish marker a player has in his player area, he moves his disc on the prestige track backward 3 spaces." (lines 444-445). MATCH.

**City scoring:** Summary: "each goods tile still on the player's tableau may score bonus points." Source city scoring: "each player scores points for his largest connected string of ownership markers in the city. For each marker in the string, the player scores two prestige points." (lines 450-453). Summary omits the city scoring entirely from the scoring summary section and mentions only goods tiles (which aren't scored at end).

**Punish marker for unactivated cards:** Summary: "each unactivated card on a player's tableau costs 3 PP." Source: "for each card a player still has on his tableau, he must take a punish marker" (line 441-442), and each punish marker = −3 PP (line 444-445). This is correctly summarized.

**Rounds:** Summary: "12 rounds." Source: "The game lasts for exactly 12 rounds." MATCH.

**Wind rose rotation:** Summary: "Rotate the wind rose one space clockwise. The cubes now at the arrow become the player's 'action cube supply' for this round. If no cubes are at the arrow, the player receives a punish marker (−3 PP)." Source: "each rotates his wind rose one space in the clockwise direction … The pile of AC, which the arrow now points to … become the player's 'action cube supply'… if, after rotating his wind rose, the arrow points to a space with no AC, the player … receives a punish marker." (lines 233-242). MATCH.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (12 rounds, 30 quarters, 6 joker tiles, 24 goods tiles, 36 coins, 300 AC, -3 PP, 2PP/marker, 16 wall spaces, 8 ports, dice count, player count), SUPPORTED: 10 (city quarter cost and tiebreaker wrong)

## mag-blast-third-edition

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Finding 1 — Attack Targeting Zone: WRONG (opposite vs same)

**Summary (Phase 5: Attack):** "The target must be in the zone directly opposite the firing ship's zone."

**Source (lines 344-349):** "After declaring which ship will fire, draw a blast card from your hand and place it on the target ship. Note that you may only target an enemy ship **in the same zone as the firing ship**; if you wish to target an enemy command ship, the enemy fleet's corresponding zone must be empty."

The summary says you target ships in the "opposite" zone; the source says you target ships in the "same" zone. This is a direct reversal and is the core attack mechanic. Every attack declared using the summary's rule would be aimed at the wrong zone.

### Procedural verification

**Victory condition:** Summary: "The last player with a surviving command ship wins." Source: "When your command ship is the only one left in the game, you win!" (line 26-28). MATCH.

**Elimination:** Summary: "When a command ship takes damage equal to or exceeding its hull strength, it is destroyed and that player is eliminated." Source: "If your command ship has damage cards beneath it equal to or greater than its hull strength, it is destroyed, you are eliminated from the game." (lines 81-86). MATCH.

**Turn structure:** Summary lists 5 phases: Discard, Draw, Reinforce, Maneuver, Attack. Source: "A turn in Mag•Blast is divided into five phases … 1. Discard 2. Draw 3. Reinforce 4. Maneuver 5. Attack." (lines 211-220). MATCH.

**First turn skip:** Summary: "On the first turn of the game, all players skip the Discard and Draw phases." Source: "NOTE: On the first turn of the game, all players skip phases 1 and 2 as described below, instead beginning their turns in Phase 3: Reinforce." (lines 206-208). MATCH.

**Reinforce cost:** Summary: "3 resources of one type, OR 1 of each type (1 Star + 1 Circle + 1 Diamond)." Source: "Three resources of one type (for example, 3 stars or 3 circles) or One resource of each type (1 star, 1 circle, and 1 diamond)." (lines 261-266). MATCH.

**Zone maximum (maneuver):** Summary: "Max 3 ships per zone." Source: "you may not end this phase with more than three ships in any one zone." (lines 289-291). MATCH.

**Reshuffle:** Summary: "If the action deck runs out, reshuffle the discard pile." Source: "If the action deck runs out of cards, simply reshuffle the action discard pile to form a new deck." (lines 241-243). MATCH.

**Command ability uses:** Summary: "Some abilities may be used only 3 times per game." Source: "Some command abilities can be used three times per game." (line 99-100). MATCH.

**Sound effect rule:** Summary: "When firing a blast, you must make an appropriate sound effect. If you forget, the blast misses!" Source: "When you fire a blast, you must make some sort of sound effect … If you forget to make a sound, the blast misses and is discarded without effect." (lines 352-355). MATCH.

**Attacking command ship:** Summary: "You can only fire at a command ship through an empty zone (no fleet ships defending it)." Source: "if you wish to target an enemy command ship, the enemy fleet's corresponding zone must be empty." (lines 347-349). MATCH — though note the summary adds "(no fleet ships defending it)" as clarifying text; the source just says "corresponding zone must be empty."

**Components (fleet ships):** Summary: "12 Scouts, 12 Cruisers, 10 Destroyers, 8 Carriers, 6 Dreadnoughts, 3 Minesweepers, 3 Gunships." Source: "12 Scouts, 12 Cruisers, 10 Destroyers, 8 Carriers, 6 Dreadnoughts, 3 Minesweepers, 3 Gunships." (lines 35-41). MATCH.

**Action cards breakdown:** Summary: "50 Blasts: 18 Laser, 22 Beam, 10 Mag; 22 Miscellaneous; 13 Squadrons; 8 Direct Hits; 8 Direct Hit Effects." Source: "50 Blasts: 18 Laser blasts, 22 Beam blasts, 10 Mag blasts; 22 Miscellaneous; 13 Squadrons; 8 Direct Hits; 8 Direct Hit Effects." (lines 43-50). MATCH.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 11 (component counts, zone limit, ability uses, hull strength threshold, blast counts, player count, turn phases), SUPPORTED: 11
