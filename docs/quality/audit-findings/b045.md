## can-t-stop — PASS

**Numbers checked (all supported):**
| Claim in summary | Source evidence |
|---|---|
| 4 dice | "four dice" (line 9) |
| 3 white cubes | "three white cubes" (line 9) |
| 4 player colors (red, green, blue, yellow) | "red, green, blue, or yellow" (line 10) |
| Roll two dice for first player, highest, reroll ties | "Players roll two dice to see who goes first. Reroll any ties. Highest total starts." |
| Sums between 2 and 12 | "chosen two sums between 2 and 12" |
| Claim 3 columns to win | "As soon as any player claims three columns, they win" |
| Example: 2,4,4,6 = two moves in column 8 | "if you roll 2, 4, 4, 6, you could make two moves in column 8 (4+4 and 2+6)" |
| End turn with 3 columns claimed = win | "If you end your turn with three columns claimed, you have won" |

**Mechanics:** Placement rules (advance existing white cube / place ahead of own colored cube / bottom space), must-play-all-legal-moves, bust = lose all turn progress, stop = replace white cubes with own color, claiming closes the column for everyone, space-sharing — all match the source closely. No contradictions or invented mechanics found.

**Observation (not graded as a finding):** The Player Reference table of column lengths (2→3 spaces … 7→13 spaces … 12→3 spaces) does not appear in the extracted text. The source is a homemade paper-board edition and the column lengths would only appear on the board graphic; the values given match the canonical Can't Stop board, so this is treated as legitimately sourced from the board image rather than invented. Likewise "If you only have 0 or 1 available white cubes and your roll requires a new column, you cannot make that move" is a sound inference from the three-white-cube limit.

**Verdict: PASS**

## candamir-the-first-settlers — MAJOR

**Numbers checked (component list fully supported):**
| Claim in summary | Source evidence |
|---|---|
| 48 wooden pieces (4 figures, 40 VP cubes, 4 disks) | identical (line 3) |
| 48 exploration tiles; 12 potion tiles; 8 equipment markers; 4 endurance markers; 16 experience markers | identical (lines 4-9) |
| 90 resource/ingredient cards; 29 movement cards; 22 adventure cards; 8 character cards; 1 die; 2 card holders; 1 shuffle board | identical (lines 10-16) |
| 10 VP markers per player, 1 destination token, 1 figure | "10 victory point markers (cubes), 1 destination token (disk), and one adventurer" |
| 2P remove "3"+"4" tiles, 3P remove "4" tiles | matches setup text |
| Adventure decks stacked 3/2/1, reveal top 3 | matches setup text |
| Each player starts with 1 lumber | "Give each player 1 'lumber' card" |
| First to place 10 VP cubes wins | "The first player to place all 10 of his victory point cubes on the game board is the winner!" — summary's "first to earn 10 VP wins" is an acceptable paraphrase |

**Finding 1 (MAJOR — wrong ability set):** Summary: "Each character has 4 abilities: Strength, Agility, Charisma, Stamina" and encounters are resolved with "Strength, Agility, Charisma, or Stamina." Source: "Your character has four abilities: Strength, Prowess (fighting skill), Agility, and Charisma." "Stamina" does not exist; Prowess (used against bears, the most common test) is missing. Endurance is a separate movement-points track, not a test ability.

**Finding 2 (MAJOR — core endurance system omitted):** The source's movement engine — endurance marker starting at 4, max moves per turn = current endurance, losing 1-2 endurance on failed tests, endurance 0/-1 ending the journey, forfeiting a turn to reset to 4 — is entirely absent. Summary instead invents: "The number of movement cards you draw depends on the distance to your destination," which has no basis in the source.

**Finding 3 (MAJOR — invented turn structure / village mechanics):**
- Summary movement cards show "Safe passage / Encounter / Arrival." Source: cards show directional events; you reveal one card per space moved; there is no "Arrival" card — you arrive by reaching your goal token.
- Summary village action "Trade with founders: Deliver specific combinations of resources/ingredients to Candamir, Osmund, or Jared in exchange for VP." Source: you *build goods* (chest = 1 ore + 1 hide + 1 lumber; sword = 2 ore + 1 lumber; window cover = 2 hide + 1 lumber) and place VP cubes on wish lists; ingredients are never delivered for VP — they are brewing inputs only.
- Summary village action "Buy equipment: Spend resources to gain equipment." Source: equipment is only *found* on exploration tiles ("If you land on an exploration tile that has a picture of a shield… you discover some valuable equipment"). It is never purchased.
- Summary: adventures are completed in the village for VP. Source: adventures trigger during movement (the "?" on a movement card), are resolved by ability tests, reward resources/ingredients — not VP. Only the "Hero of Catan" special VP (first to 3 completed adventures) exists, and it is omitted from the summary.
- Summary turn = fixed 5-step sequence. Source: optional trade, then EITHER explore OR build-and-brew — the trade step and the either/or choice are missing.

**Finding 4 (MAJOR — wrong failure consequence):** Summary: "Failure: Suffer consequences (lose resources, return to village)." Source: "If you fail to match the target on a test, then you will immediately lose 1 or 2 endurance." You never lose resources or get sent back to the village on a failed test (you even still move into the chosen space).

**Finding 5 (contradiction):** Summary: "You cannot look at face-down exploration tiles." Source: "you may secretly look at any 2 face down exploration tiles" before placing your goal token (3 with the Scout skill).

**Finding 6 (omissions):** Brigitta (the fourth settler, with her own wish list and potion/mead rewards) is omitted — summary names only three founders. Also omitted: special VP for most seeds/sheep/house (first to 3 cubes on a settler's list, can be stolen), hand limits (5 resources / 5 ingredients), 3:1 bank trading, potion/mead effects (+2 to tests; +2 endurance; mead attacks other players' endurance), individual character skills, camps. Some omission is expected in a summary, but combined with the inventions above the village/VP economy is misrepresented.

**Finding 7 (minor, unsupported):** "Adventure cards increase in difficulty as the game progresses (deck 1 is easiest, deck 3 hardest)" — not stated in the source.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong ability (Stamina vs Prowess), missing endurance system, invented mechanics (buying equipment, village adventure delivery for VP, "Arrival" cards, distance-based card draws), wrong failure penalty, and a direct contradiction on peeking at tiles.

## captain-sonar — MAJOR

**Numbers checked:**
| Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|
| 4 damage destroys a sub / 4 damage spaces | "first team whose submarine suffers 4 damage loses"; "four damage spaces" | YES |
| Torpedo range 1-4, not diagonal | "one to four spaces away… (not diagonally)" | YES |
| Direct hit 2 damage / adjacent 1 damage | "DIRECT HIT, TWO DAMAGE" / "INDIRECT HIT, ONE DAMAGE" | YES |
| Silence: up to 4 spaces, one direction, no announcement | "up to four spaces in a straight line" | YES |
| 2-8 players, multiple roles below 8 | team lineup table | YES |
| "5 double-sided map boards (10 different maps)" | Source: per team 5 Captain sheets + 5 Radio Operator sheets, "1 for each scenario"; two-sided = light side for turn-by-turn, dark side for real-time — the SAME 5 maps in two mode variants, and they are role sheets, not map boards | NO |
| "2 transparent Captain screens" | Source: "2 screens" placed end-to-end in the table center (privacy screens) + "2 transparent sheets for the Radio Operators"; there is no transparent Captain screen | NO |
| Components omit "8 erasable marker pens" count; "2 sets of 12 role sheets" structure lost | components list | PARTIAL |
| Surfacing in turn-by-turn: "the enemy gets a free turn" | "The enemy team takes THREE turns in a row" | NO |
| Charge gauges: Torpedo 3, Mine 3, Drone 4, Sonar 3, Silence 6 | Not in extracted text (gauge sizes are on the First Mate sheet graphic); values match the published game, so not flagged as invented | N/V |

**Finding 1 (MAJOR — wrong surfacing penalty):** Summary: "In turn-by-turn mode, the enemy gets a free turn." Source: "The enemy team takes three turns in a row. If the team surfaces during those turns, any remaining turns are lost." One vs three turns drastically changes how costly surfacing is.

**Finding 2 (MAJOR — Engineer breakdown mechanic inverted):** Summary: "The Engineer manages 6 breakdown circuits in 4 zones… When all symbols in a circuit are crossed off, that system breaks down and cannot be used until repaired (by crossing off a full radiation symbol set or surfacing)." Source says the opposite on both counts: (a) "If at least ONE symbol corresponding to a system is crossed out, that system cannot be activated" — a single mark disables, not a full circuit; (b) "When all four symbols on a circuit are crossed out, they REPAIR themselves" — completing a circuit is the self-repair mechanism, not the breakdown; (c) crossing off all radiation symbols is not a clean repair: "the submarine suffers 1 damage… then erases ALL breakdowns." The "6 breakdown circuits" figure is also unsupported (the source mentions six radiation symbols and orange/yellow/gray circuits of four symbols each). A table using the summary would play the Engineer role completely wrong.

**Finding 3 (MAJOR omission — breakdown damage rules):** Complete Area Breakdown ("When all symbols in a single control panel have been crossed out… the submarine suffers 1 damage") and radiation-full = 1 damage are missing, removing an entire damage source. The Blackout rule (Captain with no legal course MUST surface) is also omitted.

**Finding 4 (moderate — turn-by-turn action structure):** Summary: "On your team's turn, the Captain chooses one action: move, surface, or use a system." Source: surfacing replaces a course, but systems are activated "after each of his team's course announcements" — i.e., in addition to moving, with the constraint "The Captain MUST announce a course between two systems' activation" (also omitted).

**Finding 5 (minor):** Drone sector context omitted (map divided into 9 sectors real-time / 4 sectors turn-by-turn); sonar's "each piece of information must be of a different type" omitted; mine restrictions (cannot drop on own route; sub cannot enter own mine space; triggering needs no gauge) omitted; 2-3 player games must use turn-by-turn omitted; First Mate may activate drone/sonar himself (summary's reference table credits all activation to the Captain). Silence "0-4 spaces" — source says "up to four spaces"; including 0 is defensible.

**Supported well:** roles and their duties, real-time wait-for-OK rule, torpedo/mine outcomes and self-damage, drone yes/no truthfulness, sonar one-true-one-false, Radio Operator transparent-sheet tracking, scenario-specific systems.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong surfacing penalty (1 vs 3 enemy turns), inverted breakdown/repair system plus missing breakdown damage, and a misdescribed component set.

**Mechanics:** Flick-from-where-it-lies in disc-number order, mark a lap when crossing the start line, spacing-disc rules (own disc only; not if gap exceeds disc width), knocking other discs allowed if you touch only your own, off-track = restore pre-shot positions and lose the turn, upside-down disc = miss a turn to flip it (and stays face down if knocked off), bank shots, snapping technique (pushing forbidden), professional rules (no spacing, obstacle disc: own disc into obstacle = lose turn and return disc; others' discs and obstacle stay where they lie), continue racing for finishing order — all directly supported. The summary correctly does NOT invent tournament point values (the source's points table was lost to OCR — "Position / Points" with blank rows). "Index finger" is a sensible rendering of the translator's "goat-finger (???)".

**Finding 1 (MINOR — wrong designer in frontmatter):** `designer: Bill Payne`. The source credits no designer (only "Produced by Goldsieber", "Translated by Frank Branham"); Carabande (BGG 538) was designed by Jean du Poël. The name appears fabricated. No effect on play, but it is an unsupported factual error in the file.

**Verdict: MINOR** — rules content is faithful throughout; only the invented designer attribution keeps this from PASS.
