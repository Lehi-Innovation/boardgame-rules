### Finding 3 — Victory condition: Nightmare/Hell requires ALL incarnations (not just one) (MAJOR)

Summary: "Victory: Exorcise the incarnation of Wu-Feng (in Nightmare/Hell: all incarnations)."

Source (lines 174–175): "In order to claim victory over the ghosts and save the village, the players must exorcise the incarnation of Wu-Feng." — and the main goal text refers to a single incarnation.

The source at line 387–388: "The players collectively win if they manage to exorcise the incarnation of Wu-Feng." — again singular.

However for Nightmare/Hell there are multiple incarnations in the deck: "For 3 or 4 players: 10 ghosts, 1 incarnation, 10 ghosts, 1 incarnation, 10 ghosts, 1 incarnation, 10 ghosts, 1 incarnation, the rest of the ghost deck."

The source does NOT explicitly say "exorcise ALL incarnations" — it says "exorcise the incarnation of Wu-Feng." Each incarnation entry says "Exorcising this incarnation is the only way for the players to win the game." The summary's claim that Nightmare/Hell require "all incarnations" is an inference: the source does not explicitly state this, but since multiple incarnations exist and each has "conditions that must be met to win," a reasonable reading is that the LAST incarnation wins the game — not necessarily all. The source is ambiguous but the summary's summary is directionally plausible.

Actually re-reading: "In both the Initiation and normal levels, such reward only impacts on the final score, since the game ends up immediately when the unique incarnation is exorcised. In the nightmare and hell levels, such reward is extremely profitable for the group to go on." — This confirms that in Nightmare/Hell, exorcising one incarnation does NOT end the game; you must keep going. So "all incarnations" is correct. This is SUPPORTED.
