## mansions-of-madness-first-edition

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — source text present and matches the game.

### Finding 1 — Completely wrong combat system (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Attack: Roll dice equal to your combat stat + weapon bonus vs. monster's defense."
Also in Player Reference: "Action (1): Explore, Attack, Puzzle, Use Item, Cast Spell, Run"

**Source (pp. 21-23):** "combat is always resolved by the keeper drawing Combat cards from the deck matching the monster's class (color) until a relevant card is drawn. All text on the card is then read aloud and resolved." … "The keeper draws Combat cards until he draws one matching the actual type of weapon being used by the investigator."

**Evidence:** The source describes a card-draw system: the keeper draws from a typed Combat deck (humanoid/beast/eldritch) until a card matching the investigator's weapon type appears, then the investigator makes a single attribute test as described on that card (e.g., test Marksmanship). There is no "combat stat," no "weapon bonus," and no "monster's defense" to roll against. Results (damage dealt, effects) are printed on the drawn card. The summary invents a dice-pool combat mechanic that does not exist.

Source confirmation: "Regardless of who is attacking, combat is always resolved by the keeper drawing Combat cards from the deck matching the monster's class (color) until a relevant card is drawn."

Source example: "Jenny Barnes decides to attack the only monster within range (monster 'D'). The keeper draws the top card of the humanoid Combat deck… Since this card matches Jenny Barnes' weapon type, she resolves it. This card requires her to test her Marksmanship. She passes this test and deals 3 damage to the zombie."

### Finding 2 — Investigator movement is fixed 2 steps, not "speed value" (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Move: Move up to a number of spaces equal to speed."

**Source:** "During each investigator's turn, he receives two Movement Steps and one Action Step." … "The player may move his investigator figure to an adjacent space. The player may perform this step up to two times during each of his turns."

**Evidence:** There is no "speed" stat in First Edition. Every investigator gets exactly 2 Movement Steps. The summary imports a stat-based movement system that does not exist in this game.

### Finding 3 — Insanity mechanic wrong; "secret insane objective" does not exist in First Edition (MAJOR)

**Summary (Special Rules):** "Insanity: Investigators who lose all sanity may gain a secret insane objective (potentially working against the team)."

**Source:** "If an investigator ever has zero sanity, then he is not killed, but is instead driven insane. The keeper may play insanity Trauma cards on an insane investigator as a keeper action." … "An investigator only remains insane as long as he has 0 sanity."

**Evidence:** In the First Edition, going insane means the Keeper can play Trauma (insanity) cards on you — which are cards the Keeper holds and plays as actions. There is no "secret insane objective" that investigators gain. The secret-condition-working-against-the-team mechanic is a Second Edition feature, not present in this rulebook. Searching the source for "insane.*objective," "secret.*objective," and "condition" returns no relevant results; the only insanity mechanic is Keeper-controlled Trauma cards.

### Finding 4 — Turn structure omits Keeper Turn elements (MINOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure):** The Keeper phase description lists: "Receives threat tokens … May play Keeper Action cards … Activates each monster … Draws and resolves Event cards."

**Source (Keeper Turn):** Steps are: (1) Investigator Trading Step, (2) Gain Threat Step, (3) Keeper Action Step, (4) Monster Attack Step, (5) Event Step.

**Evidence:** The "Investigator Trading Step" — where investigators in the same space may trade items — is omitted entirely from the summary. This is a functionally significant phase since trade is the primary way investigators share items. Minor because trade is not a Keeper action, but its complete omission could confuse players.

### Victory / Game-End Verification

**Summary:** "Each scenario has unique victory conditions for both sides. Investigators win by completing their objective (e.g., finding the ritual components, stopping the ceremony, escaping the mansion). Keeper wins by completing their objective (e.g., the ritual succeeds, all investigators die or go insane, time runs out)."

**Source:** "At the start of the game, the keeper receives one of the selected story's three Objective cards. This card details what he needs to do to win the game… In addition to completing his story objective, the keeper also wins if all of the investigators are eliminated." … "Should the investigators and keeper both fail to complete their objective before the last Event card is resolved, all players lose the game (investigators and keeper alike)!"

**Evidence:** The general framing is approximately correct (scenario-dependent objectives), but the summary omits a critical rule: if neither side completes their objective by the time the last Event card is resolved, **both** sides lose. This mutual-loss condition is absent from the summary.

The summary also says the keeper wins if "all investigators… go insane" — but the source says the keeper wins if all investigators are **eliminated**. Going insane ≠ eliminated in this game (insane investigators continue playing at 0 sanity).

### Numbers Checked

- Investigators: 2 Movement Steps + 1 Action Step per turn — WRONG in summary (speed-based movement)
- 5 scenarios included — confirmed by source ("five unique stories")
- Custom d10 die — confirmed in source ("1 Ten-sided Die")
- 8 Investigator figures — confirmed in source
- 24 Monster Figures — confirmed in source

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5, SUPPORTED: 4

## mansions-of-madness-second-edition

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
Flagged SOURCE_SUSPECT by prefilter. Confirmed by reading: the extracted text begins with a fictional prose prologue ("The House on the Hill") before the actual rulebook for Mansions of Madness: Second Edition. The game text (starting at line 65) is genuinely the Second Edition rulebook (confirms app-driven, cooperative, Fantasy Flight, © 2016). The prefilter false-alarmed on the prologue title. Proceeding with full audit.

### Finding 1 — "Driven insane" listed as a loss condition; source says insane investigators continue playing (MAJOR)

**Summary (Victory Conditions):** "Investigators lose if all are eliminated, driven insane, or the scenario's doom conditions are met."

**Source:** "When an investigator becomes Insane, he gains an Insane Condition and discards all of his facedown Horror cards." … "An investigator's Insane Condition might alter how that investigator wins and loses the game." … "If an Insane investigator has suffered Horror equal to his sanity, that investigator is eliminated."

**Evidence:** Per the source, going Insane is NOT a loss condition. An investigator who becomes Insane gains a secret Insane Condition card, continues playing, and is only eliminated if they subsequently suffer horror equal to their sanity again. Some Insane Conditions give the investigator an alternative win condition (the Pyromania example: "At the start of your turn, if 6 or more rooms contain Fire, you immediately win the game"). 

The summary implies being "driven insane" ends the game. Players reading this would wrongly end the game when investigators go Insane, rather than continuing with the secret condition.

### Finding 2 — Dice success threshold (6+ and 5+) is not verifiable from source (MINOR)

**Summary (Actions / Combat):** "Roll dice. Each 6+ is a success (5+ with bonuses). Blanks are failures."

**Source:** The source uses symbolic icons (success symbol, investigation symbol, blank) throughout. The actual face values on the dice are not described in the extracted text. The source says: "The number of success results () rolled is called the test result."

**Evidence:** Source does NOT specify what die face values constitute success or investigation results — these are represented by icons on custom dice. The "6+" and "5+" claim cannot be verified or refuted from this source. Dropping rather than reporting as MAJOR (uncertain finding per audit rules). Flagged as unsupported/unverifiable.

No support found (searched: "6+", "5+", "success.*result.*die", "die face", "six", "five or higher").

### Finding 3 — Summary lists "Search" as an action but omits "Trade" action (MINOR)

**Summary (Actions listed):** Move, Explore, Search, Interact, Attack, Component Action.

**Source:** Lists Move, Explore Action, Search Action, Trade Action, Interact Action, Attack Action, Component Actions.

**Evidence:** The "Trade Action" is present in the source ("The investigator may give any number of Common Items, Unique Items, or Spells to each other investigator in his space") but absent from the summary's action list and Player Reference table. This is an omission — trading is important for cooperative play — but does not change how combat or scoring work. Rated MINOR.

### Victory / Game-End Verification

**Summary:** "Each scenario has unique win/lose conditions managed by the app. Investigators win by completing the scenario's objective (varies: perform a ritual, escape, defeat a boss, solve the mystery). Investigators lose if all are eliminated, driven insane, or the scenario's doom conditions are met."

**Source:** "Investigators win the game when the investigation is complete." … "If the investigators take too long to complete the investigation, the scenario's objective might change or become more difficult to accomplish. If the investigators continue to take too long, investigators will lose the game." … When an investigator is eliminated: "The remaining investigators have a single additional investigator phase to attempt to complete their investigation. At the end of that investigator phase, if the investigators did not complete the investigation, they lose the game."

**Evidence:** General framing is correct (app-managed, scenario-dependent). The "doom conditions" description approximately matches the "too long" lose condition. The "driven insane" loss claim is MAJOR (Finding 1 above). The one-additional-phase-after-elimination nuance is not mentioned in the summary but this is a minor omission.

### Procedural Claims Verified

1. "Each investigator takes a turn consisting of up to 2 actions" — Source: "On an investigator's turn, he performs up to two actions of his choice." SUPPORTED.
2. "Move: Move up to 2 spaces" — Source: "The investigator moves his investigator figure up to two spaces." SUPPORTED.
3. "After all investigators act, one player presses 'End Investigator Phase' in the app" — Source: "one of them presses the end phase button in the bottom-right corner of the screen to end the investigator phase." SUPPORTED.
4. "Horror checks: Investigators near horrifying entities may need to roll horror checks" in Mythos Phase — Source: "The app instructs each investigator to resolve a horror check against a single monster within range." SUPPORTED (but summary says "near" vs source's "within range" of three spaces; not contradicted).
5. "Mythos Phase: The app controls this phase entirely" — Source: confirms app generates all effects. SUPPORTED.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (player count 1-5, 2 actions/turn, move up to 2 spaces, horror check within range 3 spaces, damage=health means wounded, wounded+damage=eliminated), SUPPORTED: 5 (dice threshold unverifiable)

## mare-nostrum

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — source text present and matches the game.

### Finding 1 — Wrong number for Heroes/Wonders victory condition (MAJOR)

**Summary (Victory Conditions):** "Control 4 Heroes/Wonders (any combination of 4 heroes and/or wonders)."

**Source (6.2):** "A player who builds his fifth Hero and/or Wonder wins the game at the end of Phase 3: Build."

**Evidence:** The threshold is FIVE, not four. The summary is off by one. A player who builds their fourth Hero/Wonder would not win; they need a fifth. This would directly mislead players at the table about when this victory condition triggers.

### Finding 2 — Missing phase: "Collect Resources" is Phase 1 but absent from turn structure (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure):** Lists only 3 phases: Trade Phase, Political Phase, Military Phase.

**Source (Round Overview):** "Each round consists of five Phases: Phase 1. Collect Resources (simultaneous) … Phase 2. Trade Resources … Phase 3. Build … Phase 4. Move and Battle … Phase 5. Claim Leadership."

**Evidence:** The "Collect Resources" phase (where players collect Commodities from Caravans/Markets and Coins from Cities/Temples) is entirely absent from the summary's turn structure. This is the first and economically fundamental phase of every round. Without knowing this phase exists, players would not know how to acquire resources at the start of each round.

### Finding 3 — Missing phase: "Claim Leadership" is Phase 5 but absent from turn structure (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure):** Only 3 phases listed. No mention of the Leadership determination phase.

**Source (Phase 5):** "During this phase, the new Trade Leader, Culture Leader, and Military Leader are determined in accordance with each Leader Track." … "The player with the highest level on each Track becomes that Track's Leader."

**Evidence:** The Claim Leadership phase is entirely omitted. This phase is not just bookkeeping — it determines who holds the Director/Leader titles that control the order of play in the next round, and it is a win condition (holding all three). Its omission means players following the summary would not know when or how leadership changes.

### Finding 4 — Wrong terminology throughout: "Directors" instead of "Leaders" (MAJOR)

**Summary:** Uses "Trade Director," "Political Director," "Military Director" throughout — in Setup, Turn Structure, Special Rules, Player Reference, and Victory Conditions.

**Source:** Uses "Trade Leader," "Culture Leader," "Military Leader" consistently throughout (no instance of "Director" found in source; search returned zero matches).

**Evidence:** The titles are completely fabricated. Furthermore, the summary's "Political Director" corresponds to the source's "Culture Leader" — not only is the word wrong, but the middle title is different. Players following the summary would not recognize the actual game terminology.

### Finding 5 — Wrong phase name: "Political Phase" does not exist; source calls it "Build Phase" led by "Culture Leader" (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "2. Political Phase (led by Political Director) — Players simultaneously build improvements."

**Source (Phase 3):** "3. Build (in order determined by the Culture Leader). The Culture Leader selects an Active Player to build first (he can choose himself)."

**Evidence:** Two errors: (1) the phase is called "Build" not "Political"; (2) the leader is the Culture Leader, not a "Political Director." The description is also slightly wrong — building is sequential (Culture Leader picks order) not "simultaneous" as the summary implies.

### Finding 6 — Wrong victory condition: Leadership victory requires holding all 3 Leader titles, not "Build all 3 Directors" (MAJOR)

**Summary (Victory Conditions):** "Build all 3 Directors (hold all three Director positions simultaneously through dominance in trade, politics, and military)."

**Source (6.4):** "A player wins if he claims all three Leader Titles (Trade, Culture, and Military) at the end of Phase 5: Claim Leadership."

**Evidence:** The mechanism is "claiming Leader Titles" based on track levels, not "building" anything. While the parenthetical "(hold all three Director positions simultaneously)" partially explains it, the framing "Build all 3 Directors" implies a construction action, which is wrong. Combined with the wrong terminology (Directors vs Leaders), this is misleading.

### Victory / Game-End Verification

All four victory conditions checked:

1. **Build the Pyramids** — Source 6.1: "The player who builds the Pyramids immediately wins the game." Summary: "Build the Pyramids (collect specific set of resources and build the wonder)." Correct in essence. Source says 12 Resources are required; summary omits the cost but the claim is not wrong.

2. **Control 4 Heroes/Wonders** — Source 6.2: "fifth Hero and/or Wonder" — Summary says "4" — WRONG (Finding 1).

3. **Military victory: 4 Capital/Legendary Cities** — Source 6.3: "A player wins when he occupies or controls 4 Capitals and/or Legendary Cities at the end of Phase 4: Move & Battle." Summary does NOT include this victory condition at all. The summary mentions only 3 victory conditions while the source has 4. The Military (4 Cities) victory condition is completely absent.

4. **Leadership** — Source 6.4: confirmed. Summary approximates it (Finding 6 above).

**ADDITIONAL FINDING: Missing victory condition — "4 Capital/Legendary Cities" win entirely absent from summary (MAJOR)**

**Summary lists only 3 victory conditions.** Source has 4. The "control 4 Capital and/or Legendary Cities" condition is absent entirely.

### Numbers Checked

- 5 Empires (Rome, Carthage, Egypt, Greece, Babylon) — confirmed.
- Each empire starts with 3 Provinces — Source: "Each Empire begins the game controlling 3 Provinces." SUPPORTED.
- 5 Heroes/Wonders for victory — Summary says 4, source says 5. WRONG.
- 12 Resources for Pyramids — Source: "12 Resources to build the Pyramids." Summary does not state the cost, so not contradicted.
- 3 Resources for most units/buildings — Source: "3 Resources to build most Buildings and Units." Summary says "each improvement costs specific resources (e.g., a legion costs wheat + iron)" — the specific example is unsupported, but the general structure isn't contradicted.
- 6 Resources for Market — Source: "6 Resources to build particularly powerful Buildings" (Markets listed). Not in summary.
- 5th Hero/Wonder threshold — WRONG in summary (states 4).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7, SUPPORTED: 4
