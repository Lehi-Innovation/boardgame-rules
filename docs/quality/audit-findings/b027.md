### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Victory conditions wrong/omitted.** Summary: "Ork Victory: The Ork player controls a sufficient number of hive cities as specified on the reference sheet" and "Imperial Victory: The Imperial player prevents the Ork player from achieving their victory conditions through all 8 turns." Source (lines 311-323) states the actual conditions in the rulebook: "The Ork player wins by capturing any three of the five Imperial hives" (captured = flipped to 'smoking ruins' side and stacked with an Ork counter); "The Imperial player wins if he is in control of all five of his hives and the Warlord Ghazghkull counter has been removed from play"; "If neither player has won by the end of the 8th turn, the game is a draw." The summary omits the 3-of-5 threshold, misstates Imperial victory (surviving 8 turns is a DRAW, not an Imperial win; Imperial victory requires all 5 hives controlled AND Ghazghkull eliminated), and omits the draw outcome entirely. This would decide games incorrectly at the table.

2. **MAJOR — Invented combat option.** Summary: "A player may attack enemy units in adjacent areas **or in the same area**." Source: attacks are made against "an enemy stack in an adjacent area" (line 364), and movement may never "enter an area occupied by an enemy counter of any type" (lines 341-342), so enemies can never share an area. Same-area attacks are invented and contradicted.

3. **MODERATE — Supply consequences softened/wrong.** Summary: "Out-of-supply units may be eliminated or have reduced combat effectiveness." Source (lines 539-545): "Counters that are out of supply are destroyed, unless they are in a stack that includes a hive or fortification" (then only destroyed on D6 1-3; hive+fortification = roll twice, eliminated only if both fail). There is no "reduced combat effectiveness" mechanic; out-of-supply units are destroyed outright unless protected.

4. **MODERATE — Ork build rules wrong/omitted.** Summary: "The Ork player may rebuild destroyed units. Ork units return to the Ork territory border." Source (lines 516-527): the Ork player may rebuild **one counter for each two smoking ruins he controls**; Ghazghkull may **not** be rebuilt; rebuilt counters are placed "on a garrisoned smoking ruin or an Ork supply area," not at the territory border. The build-rate limit and placement rules are both missing/wrong.

5. **MINOR — Movement terrain prohibition omitted/misstated.** Summary: "Terrain may cost extra movement points." Source: a counter moves a number of areas up to its move value and "may not enter a Sea or Mountain Area" (lines 339-342); ash wastes force a unit to stop (movement example, lines 384-385). The source has no movement-point-cost system; the prohibition on sea/mountain entry is omitted.

6. **MINOR — Omissions in combat:** may not attack the same enemy-held area more than once per combat phase (lines 390-392); combined attacks allowed except across Ork tribes (lines 393-397); on "one counter eliminated" results the owning player chooses (lines 420-423); the -1 river attack modifier (line 416).

7. **MINOR — Imperial build details omitted:** build point income equals each intact hive/factory's defence value (lines 487-491); the production track delay with new counters moving toward the 'ready' box (lines 504-515); Space Marine Chapters cannot be rebuilt (lines 493-495).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — victory conditions (the game's scoring) are misstated/omitted; plus an invented same-area attack and incorrect supply/Ork-build rules.

All component counts and scoring values check out. Phase structure (3 phases; 7-step resolution order), token types, battle resolution (ties to defender; attacker removes control tokens and places one facedown; defender adds faceup token), raid trigger requirement, special token behaviors, two-player first-player-card rule, and scorched-earth territory control all match the source.

### Findings

1. **MINOR (most consequential) — Army attack placement constraint omitted.** Summary table: Army placement = "Land border (attack)." Source (lines 422-424): "a player can place an army token on a land border **between a province they control and an adjacent province** they wish to attack." The summary never states that army attacks must originate from a controlled adjacent province; a reader could place armies on arbitrary borders (which is exactly what the source's ronin exception highlights as abnormal).

2. **MINOR — Ronin rules omitted entirely.** Source (lines 744-754): a player with no control tokens at the start of upkeep becomes ronin; can place army tokens on any land border, cannot place raid or diplomacy tokens. Edge case, but a complete subsystem with no mention.

3. **MINOR — Scout/shugenja timing omitted.** Source (lines 731-743): both are played "during the placement phase... at the start of their turn, before placing a combat token," and are returned to the box (single-use). Summary describes effects only.

4. **MINOR — Inherent province defense bonuses omitted.** Source (lines 645-651): some provinces (e.g., clan capitals) have a defense bonus printed on the map, applying even if that clan is not in play. Summary only mentions faceup-token and bonus-token defenses.

5. **MINOR — Territory card mechanics abbreviated.** Source (lines 675-692): cards are single-use, returned to the box, not replaced after being played, and returned to the board if territory control is lost before playing; Shadowlands territory cards are played during the placement phase, not upkeep (lines 716-722). Summary's "Claim territory cards" / "Play Territory Cards" steps omit these.

6. **MINOR — Successful defense scope narrowed.** Summary: faceup tokens "accumulate from successful defenses." Source (lines 636-640): a failed attack for any reason AND placing a defensive token in a province that is never attacked also count as successful defenses (granting a faceup token).

7. **Uncertain (not counted in verdict) — Scout card effect.** OCR fragment (lines 91-95) for what appears to be the scout card reads "...any player. Look at the token without revealing it to the other players, **then return the token to its owner's token pool**," which may mean the scout also removes the token from the board. The fragment is scrambled with the "First Player" header, so this cannot be confirmed from the extracted text.

**Verdict: MINOR** — all numbers and core systems faithful; a handful of omitted constraints/edge rules (army adjacency requirement, ronin, card timing) that are unlikely to wreck a game but could permit illegal placements.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Clash rule is wrong and a tie-break rule is invented.** Summary (Turn Structure step 3): "If tied, the player who was hit last beat goes first (or the one closer to their starting side)." Summary (Special Rules): "Clash: If both attacks hit and both are stunned simultaneously, neither attack resolves (both miss)." Source (lines 264-277): a priority tie causes a **Clash**, in which "both players must play a new base face-down from their hands," put it on top of the old base forming a new attack, and re-check priority; if players run out of cards they pick up all clash bases and continue. The summary's tie-break is invented, and its "Clash" definition describes a completely different (nonexistent) mechanic. This misrepresents a core, frequently-occurring system.

2. **MAJOR — Wrong starting positions.** Summary: "one on space 2, the other on space 5 (3 spaces apart)." Source: "Place both players on spaces 2 and 6" (line 176), confirmed by "characters at the start of a match are at range 4" (line 302). Affects which attacks can hit on the first beat.

3. **MAJOR — Stun threshold off by one.** Summary (twice): stunned if damage "equal to or greater than" Stun Guard. Source: stunned only if damage is **greater than** Stun Guard; equal damage does not stun (lines 434-435, 508-513). This changes the outcome of common exchanges (e.g., source Example 3: 2 damage vs Stun Guard 4 — not stunned; under the summary's rule, 4 damage vs Stun Guard 4 would wrongly stun).

4. **MAJOR — Overdrive Finish condition inverted/garbled.** Summary: "available once per duel when opponent is low on life. Costs 7+ life to attempt." Source (lines 741-745): the **user** must have 7 or fewer life points; there is no life cost; if attempted with too much life it becomes a Cancel. The summary's version would have players paying life and checking the opponent's total — both wrong.

5. **MAJOR — Movement rule contradicted.** Summary: "A character cannot move through or land on the opponent's space." Source (lines 410-420): "When moving, you **hop over opponents**, and do not count the spaces they occupy against your movement," with an explicit example of Cadenza moving through Kallistar to switch sides. Moving through (over) the opponent is a core maneuver (the source even warns "Dash prevents opponents from hitting you if you change sides," lines 438-440).

6. **MODERATE — Invented end-of-duel rules.** Summary: 15-beat limit with most-life win; simultaneous-zero tiebreaks ("player with more life remaining wins. If still tied, the active player loses"). Source: the duel "immediately ends" the moment a player hits 0 or fewer life (lines 316-318); the active player resolves damage first, so the summary's simultaneous-KO scenario has no basis; no beat limit exists in this rulebook.

7. **MODERATE — Soak omitted entirely.** Source defines Soak as one of the two Common Effects (lines 501-506; damage reduction, stacks, passive) and uses it throughout the stun examples. The summary never mentions it.

8. **MINOR — Setup omissions/errors.** Source setup (lines 166-195): each character has 1 character card, 5 styles, 1 unique base plus 6 generic bases (7 bases total — summary's table shows only 6 generic bases and calls bases a "shared generic set," missing the unique base, though Finish bases like Snare/Press/Spellbolt are named later in the source); two numbered attack pairs start in discard piles 1 and 2 (omitted — this materially changes the opening hands); "determine first player randomly" is invented (no first player exists).

9. **MINOR — Beat sequence drift.** Source sequence is Select → **Ante Tokens** → Reveal (incl. clash + Start of Beat for both players, higher priority first) → Execute → Recycle (lines 227-231). The summary buries ante in Special Rules (acceptably) but moves Start of Beat effects inside the active player's attack only, and omits Reveal effects, mandatory triggered effects, and that End of Beat effects work even while stunned (line 333-335).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — five core-rule errors (clash, starting positions, stun threshold, Overdrive Finish, movement through opponents) plus invented end-of-duel rules and an unverifiable base-stats table.
