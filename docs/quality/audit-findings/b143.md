## merchant-of-venus

### Verdict: MAJOR

---

### Finding 1 (MAJOR): End-game scoring — Fame-to-Credits conversion completely omitted

The summary lists income sources as "Trading goods, passenger delivery, missions, spaceport ownership" and "selling ship equipment" but entirely omits the end-game Fame → Bonus Credits conversion, which is a mandatory scoring step.

**Summary:** "Credits come from trading, delivering passengers, completing missions, spaceport income, and selling ship equipment."

**Source (End of the Game, step 3):**
> "Award Bonus Credits: Each player now receives an amount of credits equal to his total fame multiplied by 10. If a player's Fuzzy Dice roll had the same result on both dice (i.e. he rolled doubles), he instead receives an amount of credits equal to his total fame multiplied by 20."

This is the third of four mandatory end-game steps. It is absent from the summary entirely. In the scoring example in the source, a player with 35 fame receives c700 in bonus credits — this can be a substantial portion of a player's final score. Omitting it means a table using the summary cannot compute correct final scores.

No support found in the summary for this mechanic (searched: "fame", "bonus credit", "multiplied", "fame.*credit", "final score").

---

### Finding 2 (MAJOR): End-game scoring — "Selling ship equipment" is invented

The summary claims "selling ship equipment" is a credit source at game end. No such step appears in the source's 4-step end-game procedure (Sell Merchant Spaceports → Count Fame → Award Bonus Credits → Determine Final Score).

**Summary:** "Credits come from trading, delivering passengers, completing missions, spaceport income, and **selling ship equipment**."

**Source (End of the Game steps 1–4):** Only "Sell Merchant Spaceports" (c200 each), "Award Bonus Credits" (fame × 10), and totaling credits are listed. No mention of selling ship equipment.

No support found (searched: "sell.*equipment", "sell.*upgrade", "equipment.*end", "ship.*sale.*end").

---

### Finding 3 (MAJOR): Game length — "varies by player count" is wrong; game is exactly 30 rounds

**Summary:** "The game ends after a set number of rounds (varies by player count and variant)."

**Source:**
> "The game is played for 30 game rounds, after which each player counts his assets and the player with the most credits is declared the winner."
> "When the last player ends his turn in round 30, the game ends."

The number of rounds is fixed at 30 regardless of player count. The summary's parenthetical "(varies by player count and variant)" has no support in the standard game rules. (The solo challenge and epic variants also end at round 30 per the source.)

---

### Finding 4 (MINOR): Wrong die name/color

**Summary (Components):** "5 six-sided dice (4 white speed, **1 red navigation**)"

**Source:**
> "5 Six-Sided Dice, including: 4 White Speed Dice, 1 **Purple** Effect Die"
> "There are four white speed dice and one **purple effect** die."

The die is purple (not red) and is called the "effect die" (not "navigation die"), though it is used at navigation spaces among other things. Wrong color and wrong name.

---

### Finding 5 (MINOR): Turn structure — 4 phases collapsed/misdescribed; First Contact Phase omitted

**Summary turn steps:** "1. Move, 2. Encounter hazards/telegates/relics, 3. Take actions at destination"

**Source:**
> "A player's turn consists of four phases, performed in the following order: 1. Movement Phase, 2. First Contact Phase, 3. Transaction Phase, 4. Merchant Spaceport Phase."

The First Contact Phase (revealing new cultures) is entirely absent from the summary's turn description. The Merchant Spaceport Phase is folded into "take actions." While the summary's description is not completely wrong about what happens, it misrepresents the structure and omits that building a merchant spaceport is a dedicated final phase with restrictions (cannot be done if buy actions were taken during the turn).

## merchants-marauders

### Verdict: MAJOR

---

### Finding 1 (MAJOR): Setup — Captain draw procedure wrong

**Summary:** "Each player draws 2 captain cards and chooses 1."

**Source:**
> "Randomly draw a Captain from the Captain Card deck – do not reveal which Captain you draw."
> "Secretly choose a ship for your Captain by placing it in your hand. You can choose between a Sloop and a Flute."

Players draw ONE captain randomly and do not get a choice between two captains. The choice in setup is between ship type (Sloop or Flute), not between captains. The summary inverts this: it says you choose a captain and get the "corresponding starting ship and home port," implying the ship is predetermined by the captain, but actually the ship choice is free (Sloop or Flute).

---

### Finding 2 (MAJOR): Bounty removal — "paying at specific ports" is not in source

**Summary:** "Bounties can be removed by paying at specific ports."

**Source (Removing Bounties section):**
> "Generally speaking, once you get a Bounty your Captain is stuck with it. However, a few Glory Cards and Event Cards let you pay Gold to be pardoned. Likewise a few rumors or missions offer you some form of pardon."

There is no mechanism to remove bounties by paying at specific ports. Pardons come from Glory Cards, Event Cards, rumors, or missions — not from port visits with payment. The summary's claim would lead players to believe they can freely shed bounties at certain ports, which contradicts the source's "generally stuck with it" rule.

No support found (searched: "port.*pay", "pay.*port", "specific port", "particular port", "port.*pardon", "pardon.*port").

---

### Finding 3 (MAJOR): Retirement — "achieve greatness to earn bonus glory" is invented

**Summary:** "Players can retire captains who have achieved greatness to earn bonus glory."

**Source (Retirement section):**
> "If you grow tired of your Captain, or end up broke and with a heavily damaged ship, retiring may be a sensible option. To retire you must start your turn in a port and retire before performing any actions."

Retirement is a consolation/reset mechanism for struggling captains, not a reward for achievement. There is no mention of "bonus glory" from retirement anywhere in the source. This claim is entirely invented.

No support found (searched: "retire.*glory", "glory.*retire", "bonus.*glory", "achieved.*greatness", "retirement.*glory").

---

### Finding 4 (MAJOR): Alternative game-end triggers missing from summary

**Summary (Victory Conditions):** Only mentions "First player to reach 10 Glory Points wins."

**Source (Time Limits and Game Length):**
> "The game ends immediately if either of the following occurs: There are no more Event Cards at the beginning of a turn. A Captain dies, and there are no more Captains left to draw. In both cases, the winning player is the one who has the most Glory Points."

These two alternative game-end triggers are entirely absent from the summary. A game could end via Event Card exhaustion or Captain exhaustion with the leader on fewest glory points winning — this is different enough from the standard win condition to mislead players.

---

### Finding 5 (MINOR): Merchant raid description — mechanics misdescribed

**Summary:** "When scouting, you may find a merchant ship (draw a merchant token)."

**Source:**
> "After declaring your intent to 'scout' for a merchant, you roll your Scouting skill. If successful, you remove the Merchant Token from the Sea-Zone and reveal the nation on its flip side."

Merchant tokens are pre-placed on the board; players don't "draw" one. The raid triggers by rolling Scouting successfully, then removing an existing token. Minor because the outcome (merchant encounter) is the same, but the mechanic is misdescribed.
