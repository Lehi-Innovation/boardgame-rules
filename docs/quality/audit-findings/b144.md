## merv-the-heart-of-the-silk-road

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn structure: variable turns per year (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "The game spans 3 years. Each year consists of multiple turns until all players have placed their master meeple."

**Source (line 286–287):** "Merv is played over three years. In each year there is an action phase, which is divided into four rounds of turns. Thus, over the course of the game, each player will take twelve turns."

Each year has exactly **four rounds** (one per side of the city), not a variable number until meeples are placed. Players take their meeple *around* the city grid over four fixed rounds. This misstates the turn structure entirely.

---

### Finding 2 — Building placement is mandatory when site is empty, not always optional (MINOR–MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure step 3):** "Construct a building (optional) by placing one of your buildings on the selected site."

**Source (line 311):** "If you choose a building site with no building, you must place one of your buildings on it."

The summary says building construction is always optional. The source says it is *mandatory* if the site has no building. (You can choose a site that already has a building to avoid placing one, but the summary omits that distinction.)

---

### Finding 3 — Year-end phase order misrepresented; invasion in year 1 claimed implicitly (MAJOR)

**Summary (Year-End Scoring):** "1. Mongol Attack… 2. Palace Scoring… 3. Mosque Scoring"

**Source (lines 453–475):** "In the second and third year, the action phase is followed by the invasion phase. In the first year, skip straight to the scoring phase." Then: "After the action and invasion phase, you spend favour to score points for your courtiers in the palace, your presence in the city, and your advancements on the mosque track. Palace Scoring… Bonus Scoring [buildings + scoring tile + mosque track]."

The summary presents the Mongol attack as occurring in every year's end sequence, and presents "Mosque Scoring" as a third separate step. In reality: (a) there is no Mongol attack in year 1; (b) the scoring phase is a unified phase covering palace, buildings, and mosque track — not three separate phases.

---

### Finding 4 — Contract prerequisite: prestige vs. influence (AMBIGUOUS — not counted as MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Contracts require reaching a minimum prestige level before they can be fulfilled."

**Source (contract card description, line 22):** "4 Prestige level: the space on the prestige track you must reach or pass to fulfil this contract."

**Source (rules text, line 348–349):** "Before you can fulfil a contract, you must reach or pass its required influence level on the influence track."

The component description says "prestige level/prestige track" and the rules say "influence level/influence track." This inconsistency is in the source itself. The summary matches the component description but not the rules text. Given the ambiguity in the source, this is not counted as a clear error in the summary.

---

### Finding 5 — End-game scoring: "remaining resources and scrolls may score" unsupported (MAJOR)

**Summary (End-Game Scoring):** "Remaining resources and scrolls may score"

**Source (lines 997–1008, "Game End" section):** "The game ends after the scoring phase of the third year. Add up all the end of game points from your caravan cards as follows: [caravan card set scoring only]. The player with the most points wins."

The source lists only **caravan card set scoring** as end-game points. Scrolls score through the palace *during* the yearly scoring phase (hall of knowledge), not as an end-game score. Resources do not score at all. The summary's claim that "remaining resources and scrolls may score" at end-game has no source support.

**Searched:** "resource.*score", "scroll.*score", "remaining.*score", "end.*game.*point" — none support this claim.

---

### Finding 6 — Tiebreaker omitted (MINOR)

**Summary:** "The player with the most points wins." No tiebreaker listed.

**Source (lines 1010–1013):** "Tiebreakers: If two or more players are tied for the most points, the tied player with the most favour wins. If there is still a tie, the tied player with the most influence wins. If there is still a tie, the tied player whose turn is first in the order queue wins."

Three-tier tiebreaker is missing. MINOR (tiebreaker only).

---

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (caravan card set points: 1/3/6/10, building points: 1 each, scoring tile bonus: 3 per building, mosque final-space bonus: 4 per building), SUPPORTED: 4 (direct contract/resource claims checked above)

## mice-and-mystics

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Initiative is randomly shuffled, not sorted by speed (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure, step 1):** "Initiative Phase: Sort initiative cards (mice and minions) by speed."

**Source (lines 766–781, "Determining Initiative Order"):** "To determine in which order the mice and the minions will take their turns, players must first determine an initiative order… Take the initiative cards for each mouse and each minion type that are going to be on the board at the start of that encounter and **shuffle them together** to create an initiative deck. Then, one at a time, take each of the cards from the initiative deck and place them face up along the initiative track…"

There is no "speed" stat. Initiative order is determined by **random shuffle**, not by sorting. The summary invents a "sort by speed" mechanic that does not exist in the source.

**Searched:** "speed", "sort.*initiative", "initiative.*sort", "initiative.*speed" — only "Move value" describes movement speed, not initiative. No sorting mechanism found.

---

### Finding 2 — Turn structure: hero and minion turns are interleaved on the initiative track, not in separate phases (MAJOR)

**Summary (Turn Structure):** "Rounds alternate between hero and minion phases: 1. Initiative Phase… 2. Hero Turns: Each mouse acts in initiative order: Move, then take actions... 3. Minion Turns: Each minion group acts…"

**Source (lines 755–764, "Game round"):** "Mice and Mystics is played over a series of rounds. Each round is made up of turns. The first turn of a round is taken by the card that is **highest on the initiative track**. The next turn is taken by the next highest card on the initiative track, and so on."

**Source (lines 1225–1236, "Taking a Turn with a Minion"):** "When a minion initiative card comes up in the turn order, each minion of that type will move and battle."

There are no separate hero and minion phases. All figures — mice AND minions — take their turns in **initiative track order**, interleaved. A minion group can act before some mice and after others within the same round. The summary's alternating phase structure is incorrect.

---

### Finding 3 — Hero turn structure: move-then-act or act-then-move (not just "Move then actions") (MINOR)

**Summary (Hero Turns):** "Each mouse acts in initiative order: Move, then take actions (attack, search, explore, use abilities)."

**Source (lines 731–733, "Taking a Turn with a Mouse Hero"):** "On its turn, a mouse can either perform 1 of the following actions **then move**, or **move then** perform 1 of the following actions."

The summary only describes move-then-act. The source explicitly permits act-then-move. MINOR (doesn't change outcome catastrophically but misstates the flexibility).

---

### Finding 4 — Search action: roll required, not automatic (MINOR)

**Summary:** "Draw a search card when on a search space."

**Source (lines 1059–1065, "Search"):** "To perform a search action, a player must announce that his or her mouse is searching and **roll an action die**. If a [star symbol] is rolled, that player draws 1 card from the top of the search deck."

Search requires rolling a star symbol to succeed. The summary says simply "draw a search card" implying automatic success. MINOR (affects how the action works but not game-ending conditions).

---

### Finding 5 — Cheese wheel surge threshold not stated; description slightly inaccurate (MINOR)

**Summary:** "when full, a surge occurs (new minions spawn or bad events trigger)"

**Source (line 1259):** "When the minion cheese wheel fills up with **6 or more** cheese tokens, a surge triggers."

**Source (lines 1260–1275):** Surge effects: (1) Move hourglass marker one page closer to chapter end. (2) Follow special surge rule or face-up encounter card effect. The surge advances the chapter timer *as well as* potentially spawning minions.

The summary omits that the cheese wheel threshold is 6 tokens and omits that a surge advances the hourglass marker (which is a key part of the lose condition). MINOR.

---

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (cheese wheel threshold: 6; move value used in example: 2; life value triggers capture; 4 mice per party typically; hourglass marker advances 1 per surge), SUPPORTED: 3 (life value for capture correct, 4 mice typically correct, hourglass advances correct)

## micropul

The Micropul summary accurately reflects the rules.

**Victory/game-end:**

- Source (line 110): "The game end as the last tile is drawn from the core." ✓

**Scoring:**
- Summary: "Each closed group you own: 1 point per micropul in the group (big micropul = 1 point) / Each tile remaining in your supply: 2 points / Each tile remaining in your hand: 1 point"

**Tiebreaker:**
- Summary: "No tiebreaker."
- Source (line 115): "There is no tie-breaker." ✓

**Turn options:**
- Summary: "Lay tile | Take from supply to hand | Claim a group with stone"
- Source (lines 52–54): three options match exactly ✓

**Catalyst effects:**
- Summary: "Single dot = draw 1 | Double dot = draw 2 | Cross = draw + extra turn"
- Source (rules diagram): Single dot → draw 1; Double dot → draw 2; Cross → play again ✓

**Groups and merging:**
- Summary: "If two separately claimed groups merge, that merged group scores zero points."
- Source (lines 101–103): "It's possible for two groups claimed separately to merge into one group by playing a tile which will connect them together. That group will not be worth any points." ✓

**Losing condition (solo/2p):**
- Summary: "If you have no tiles in hand AND no tiles in supply, you lose immediately."
- Source (lines 78–79): "You lose the game if you have no more tiles in hand or in supply." ✓

**Hand limit:**
- Summary: "Max 6 in hand | Supply is unlimited face-down tiles"
- Source (line 83): "You cannot have more than 6 tiles in your hand." ✓

**Starting tile:**
- Summary: "the one with 4 micropul: 2 black, 2 white"
- Source (lines 38–39): "It's the one with 4 micropul, 2 black and 2 white." ✓
