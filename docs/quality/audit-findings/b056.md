## civilization-the-boardgame — MAJOR

The summary appears to be written largely from genre knowledge of "a Civilization game" rather than from this rulebook. The victory currency, turn structure, several components, and several special rules are invented or contradicted.

### Findings

1. **Invented victory currency: "culture points" (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Victory is achieved by accumulating the most culture points" and the entire Scoring section ("Players score culture points from… The player with the most culture points wins").
   Source: the game uses **victory points** throughout: "count how many victory points you have scored. The player with the most victory points wins the game." Culture points do not exist anywhere in the rulebook.

2. **Wrong turn structure (MAJOR).**
   Summary lists six phases: "1. Production, 2. Commerce, 3. Building, 4. Technology, 5. Movement, 6. Combat."
   Source (both Standard and Advanced): "There are four turn phases in a game turn: 1. Movement and battles phase, 2. Trade phase, 3. Production phase, 4. Purchase phase." Movement/battles come first (not fifth/sixth), and building units/settlements and buying technologies all happen inside the single Purchase phase. "Commerce," "Building," and "Technology" phases are invented.

3. **Invented component and rule: Government cards / government types (MAJOR).**
   Summary lists "Government cards" in Components and claims "Government types provide different bonuses and constraints."
   Source explicitly excludes this: "Advantages for various government types and Nationalities have **not** been included in The Board Game, but are currently planned to be included in an expansion pack." Directly contradicted.

4. **Invented mechanic: Barbarians (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Barbarians inhabit unclaimed territories and attack players."
   Source has no barbarians. The closest concept is the "Minor Civilization" exploration marker, where the *winner of a dice-off gains control* of a new village and army — nothing attacks players.

5. **Invented resource/terrain link (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Resources are specific to terrain types (mountains produce ore, plains produce food, etc.)."
   Source: resources (wine, horses, iron, gems, spices, oil, coal, rare metals) come from randomly placed **exploration markers**; terrain markers (desert, mountains, jungle/forest, fertile) only limit/boost settlement size and production. No terrain-to-resource mapping exists, and "ore"/"food" are not resources in the game.

6. **Wrong game-end conditions (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "The game ends when a certain number of technologies have been researched or a set number of rounds are completed."
   Source Standard: game ends "at the end of the game turn when any player owns **three modern era technologies**." Advanced: short/medium games end via a die roll tied to medieval/gunpowder technology purchases; long game ends via total conquest, diplomatic victory (United Nations), military victory (Apollo Program), or technology/space victory (Alpha Centauri Colony Ship, 200 gold). No "set number of rounds" ending exists.

7. **Scoring sources garbled (MAJOR, part of finding 1).**
   Summary: points from "Technologies researched, Wonders built, Cities and territories controlled, Military victories, Special achievements."
   Source Standard: settlements by size (village 1 / town 2 / city 3 / metropolis 4 VP), 2 VP per technology, 3 VP per wonder. Advanced: settlements as above, 2 VP per wonder, 4 VP per seminal discovery, plus situational bonus VP depending on which victory ended the game. Territories (regions without settlements) score nothing; "military victories" do not score (only the Advanced military-victory *ending* grants 1 VP per owned unit).

8. **Components list partially invented (MINOR, in addition to the above).**
   "Civilization tiles and city tiles," "Culture point markers" — none exist; the game uses city *cards* (61), improvement cards (64), coins, exploration markers, plastic miniatures. "Wonder cards" (25) and "Technology cards" arranged by era (15 ancient / 10 medieval / 10 gunpowder / 18 modern) are real.

## clank-a-deck-building-adventure — MINOR

Faithful overall; every spot-checked number matches the source. The main weakness is a simplified endgame Countdown Track.

### Findings

1. **Countdown Track simplified/partially wrong (MINOR).**
   Summary: "Once a player escapes, they trigger additional dragon attacks for remaining players each turn."
   Source: only the **first** player to leave the dungeon **or be knocked out** uses the Countdown Track; on their subsequent turns spaces 2–4 cause instant Dragon Attacks with **1/2/3 extra cubes**, and "On the fifth space, the dragon instantly knocks out all players still in the dungeon." The summary omits the escalating extra cubes and the fifth-space hard end, and ties it to escaping only. Players using the summary would still expect per-turn attacks but not the ~4-turn hard deadline.

2. **Out-of-game player handling omitted (trivial).**
   Source: players who left/were knocked out add no Clank!, are unaffected by cards, and their cubes drawn from the Dragon Bag count as black. Not in summary.

3. **Backpack contradiction-by-omission (MINOR).**
   Summary: "You can only carry one Artifact at a time; once taken, you're stuck with it," and lists Backpacks as market items without explaining them. Source: "You can **normally** carry only one Artifact"; Backpack "Allows you to carry an additional Artifact." Also omitted: Master Key and Backpack are each worth 5 points at game end.

4. **Danger cards omitted (trivial).** Source: each Danger card in the Dungeon Row adds one cube pulled per Dragon Attack. Not mentioned.

5. **Turn-structure rigidity (trivial wording).** Summary presents "Play Cards" then "Take Actions" as sequential steps; source allows taking actions "between cards if you wish."

## clans — MAJOR

Prose rules are highly faithful (all numeric spot checks pass), but the Player Reference epoch table fabricates the favorable/hostile terrain assignments, including one entry directly contradicted by the source — scoring-relevant misinformation.

### Findings

1. **Epoch III terrains contradicted (MAJOR).**
   Summary table: Epoch III — Favorable: **Mountains**, Hostile: **Steppes**.
   Source, Example 4: "The **steppe is a favorable terrain in the 3rd epoch**. Therefore, the village gets 3 bonus points." The summary reverses this: a player consulting the table would avoid steppe villages in epoch III (expecting 0 points) when they actually score huts +3, and would expect mountains to score the bonus with no support in the source.

2. **Epoch II favorable and Epoch IV favorable/hostile unsupported (part of finding 1).**
   The source only states: epoch I favorable = forest, hostile = mountains; epoch II hostile = grassland (+2 bonus); epoch III favorable = steppe (+3); epoch V all favorable. The summary's "Epoch II favorable: Steppes" and entire Epoch IV row ("Grassland favorable / Forest hostile") are invented; they cannot be verified from the extracted text and are presented as authoritative.

## clans-of-caledonia — MAJOR

Frame (5 rounds, market drift, clans, contract competitiveness, money VP, tiebreaker) is right, but the summary invents mechanics, omits an entire phase and a core action, and misstates the two majority scorings.

### Findings

1. **Missing phase (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Each of the 5 rounds has 3 phases" (Actions, Production, Round Scoring).
   Source: "There are five rounds and **four Phases** per round… Phase 1: Preparation Phase — Flip the Scoring tile…, refill… the Export board and **retrieve your Merchants**." The Preparation phase (the only way Merchants return from the Market) is omitted entirely.

2. **Missing core action: Obtain an Export Contract (MAJOR).**
   Source action 2: "Pay the current round cost to take any Export contract… in the first round, players **receive £5** when obtaining an Export contract." The summary's action list has Fulfill but no Obtain; a reader would not know contracts must be acquired, cost round-dependent money, and are limited to one unfulfilled contract at a time.

3. **Invented action structure (MAJOR).**
   Summary invents "Deploy a Worker," "Deploy a Settlement," and "Expand" as three separate actions, and "Upgrade: Upgrade a basic worker to a settlement or advance technology."
   Source has a single **Expand** action ("Place one unit (Sheep, Cow, Cheese dairy, Bakery, Field, Distillery, Woodcutter or Miner)… onto any empty space… neighbouring or within Shipping reach"), and two distinct upgrades: "Upgrade Shipping" (£4) and "Upgrade Technology" (£10, boosts Worker income by £2). Workers never upgrade into settlements.

4. **Invented mechanic: distance-based adjacency cost (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Adjacency costs: Placing units further from your existing network costs more. River and loch crossings cost extra."
   Source: each space has a fixed printed land cost "ranging from £1 to £6"; crossing rivers/lochs is not a money cost but a **capability** gated by Shipping level ("Pay £4 to move your Shipping marker… allows you to expand across rivers and lochs").

5. **Invented mechanic: whisky aging (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Whisky production is a two-step process: grain from fields must be aged in distilleries" and Player Reference "Distillery | Grain | Whisky (after aging)."
   Source: "Each Distillery may process one Grain into one Whisky" in the same Production phase — no aging. A whisky-aging cellar exists **only** as Clan MacKenzie's unique ability.

6. **Production table garbled (MAJOR).**
   Summary Player Reference: "Dairy → Milk", "Sheep Field → Wool", "Cattle Field → Milk".
   Source: "Each **Sheep** produces one Wool. Each **Cow** produces one Milk. Each **Field** produces **two Grain**." (Cheese dairies process milk→cheese — correctly listed.) There is no milk-producing "Dairy" unit, no "Sheep Field"/"Cattle Field," and Field grain production (the input for bread and whisky) is missing from the table.

7. **Majority scorings missing/contradicted (MAJOR).**
   Summary: "Settlements on the map (VP per settlement type)" and no mention of export-count scoring; also invents a "Final scoring tile (end-game condition)."
   Source final scoring: Export Scoring — "12 VP for the player with the most fulfilled Export contracts, 6 VP for the… second most" (2p: 8 VP, winner only); Settlement Scoring — most settlements within Shipping reach: **18/12/6 VP** (2p: 12/0). There is no per-settlement-type VP and no separate final scoring tile. Also omitted: Glory track = 1 VP per step, Basic goods 1 VP each, Processed goods 2 VP each.

8. **Import goods garbled (part of finding 7).**
   Summary: "Hops and cotton bonuses (VP for imported goods used in whisky/cloth production)."
   Source: Hops = 1 VP each; Cotton/Tobacco/Sugar cane = 3/4/5 VP each by rarity (least-imported is worth most). The "used in whisky/cloth production" rationale is invented; tobacco/sugar cane and the rarity mechanism are dropped.

9. **Starting money "varies by turn order" (MINOR).**
   Source: players receive goods and money from their random **Starting tiles**; turn-order-based money (£0/£2/£4/£6) is only the "Without Clans" simplification variant, not the standard rule.

10. **Pass income misstated (MINOR).**
    Summary: "Pass: …Gain money equal to your position on the export track."
    Source: "move your Turn order token to the leftmost available position of the next round and receive the Pass bonus money indicated" (e.g., first passer £16). Wrong basis, though "passing earlier is rewarded" survives.
