## feudum

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Findings

**Finding 1 — Invented "Draw Epoch Card" step in turn structure**

Summary: "Each epoch: 1. Draw epoch card (may trigger events or scoring)."

Source: The actual Round of Play steps (lines 44-54 of extracted text, confirmed in detail at lines 260-281) are:
1. Take Actions
2. Nourish Pawns
3. Roll Progress Die
4. Advance Epoch Marker & Score Epoch (if triggered)
5. End Round/Game & Final Scoring

There is no "Draw epoch card" step anywhere in the source. Epochs are triggered by the Progress Die roll depleting region tile stacks, not by drawing cards. Royal Writ cards are drawn through the Explore or Guild actions, not as a round step.

No support found (searched: "epoch card," "draw epoch," "epoch draw," "card draw.*epoch").

**Finding 2 — "Nourish Pawns" step entirely omitted**

Summary's turn structure makes no mention of feeding pawns.

Source (lines 266-268, 1194-1218): "Step 2: Nourish Pawns — All players must sustain pawns on the board with food and/or wine." ... "Unnourished pawns go back into your personal supply. If losing a pawn takes you out of a guild, retrieve your influence marker from that guild." This is a mandatory step every round with significant consequences (pawn loss → guild status loss). Omitting it would leave players unable to play the game correctly.

**Finding 3 — "Roll Progress Die" step entirely omitted**

Summary's turn structure makes no mention of the progress die.

Source (lines 269-271, 1221-1236): "Step 3: Roll Progress Die — Roll the progress die to determine a region tile to remove." This is the mechanism that triggers epoch transitions and ultimately ends the game. Without this step, players have no epoch advancement.

**Finding 4 — Wrong action list: "Transfer" and "Progress" are invented; "Explore" and "Harvest" are omitted; "Starve" is misrepresented**

Summary lists 11 actions as: Move, Influence, Tax, Transfer, Improve, Conquer, Guild, Repeat, Progress, Migrate, Starve (Defensive).

Source (lines 344-811) lists the actual 11 action cards as:
1. Migrate (summary: Migrate ✓)
2. Move (summary: Move ✓)
3. Influence (summary: Influence ✓)
4. Improve (summary: Improve ✓)
5. **Explore** — summary replaces this with "Transfer" (invented)
6. **Harvest** — summary replaces this with "Progress" (invented)
7. Tax (summary: Tax ✓)
8. Conquer (summary: Conquer ✓)
9. **Defend** — summary replaces this with "Starve (Defensive)" (wrong; Starve the People is a special Noble pawn ability within Conquer, not a separate action card)
10. Repeat (summary: Repeat ✓)
11. Guild (summary: Guild ✓)

Source on Explore (line 564): "5. Explore Action — If you rule an outpost, you may play the explore action. Draw 2 Royal Writ cards..."
Source on Harvest (line 661): "6. Harvest Action — If you rule a farm, you may play the Harvest Action to randomly draw 5 goods from the haversack..."
Source on Defend (line 764): "9. Defend Action — If an opponent attacks 1 of your pawns or feudums, and you preselected the Defend Action, you may flip it over out of turn and add +1 to your defense and score 1 vp."
Source on Starve (line 750): "'Starve the People' (Advanced Game) — If you have a Noble pawn beside a location with an opponent's subject or serf, you may conquer 1 of them... Important: Play this instead of the regular Conquer Action."

No support found for "Transfer" or "Progress" as action cards (searched extensively through all 11 action card descriptions).

**Finding 5 — Guild positions described incorrectly**

Summary: "Each guild has a Leader and Journeyman position."

Source (lines 913-924): "The player with the most status stars places his marker... denoting him as guild master. Players with the second and third most status stars place markers... and are known as the journeyman and apprentice respectively." The positions are Guild Master (not "Leader"), Journeyman, and Apprentice. The summary invents the term "Leader" for "Guild Master" and omits the Apprentice position entirely.

**Numbers in Scoring/VP section — spot check**

Source confirms:
- 5 epochs: ✓
- 11 action cards per player: ✓  
- Guild master/journeyman/apprentice epoch scoring: 5/3/1 vp (not mentioned in summary)
- Epic Voyage sections: 3/5, 7/9, 11/13, 17 vp (not mentioned in summary)
- Large Empires end scoring: each outpost/farm/town ruled = 1 vp; each feudum = 3 vp (not mentioned in summary)

**Exhaustion rules**: Source (lines 1287-1292): "The player with the most vp is heralded... If there is a tie, the player with the most sulfur left in his barrel wins... If there is still a tie, the players share in the victory." Tiebreaker not mentioned in summary — MINOR omission.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8, SUPPORTED: 5

**Summary verdict: MAJOR** — The turn structure invents a "Draw epoch card" step that does not exist and omits mandatory Nourish Pawns and Roll Progress Die steps. Three of eleven action cards are replaced with invented ones (Transfer, Progress replacing Explore and Harvest), and Defend is replaced by Starve which is actually a special ability within Conquer. A group using this summary could not play the game correctly.

## fief-france-1429

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Findings

**Finding 1 — VP sources wrong: Bishops and Cardinals are NOT worth VP**

Summary: "VP sources: Each Fief title = 1 VP, Each Bishop title = 1 VP, Cardinal = 1 VP, Pope = 1 VP, King/Queen = 1 VP"

Source explicitly states VP sources:
- "A Player receives 1 Victory Point (VP) for each Fief Title his family controls" (line 112)
- "The titles of Pope and King bestow 1VP each to a family." (line 118)
- Pope description: "Keeps all of his other Titles and is worth 1 VP." (line 519)
- King description: "Keeps all of his other Titles and is worth 1 VP." (line 593)
- Queen description: "She is worth no VP." (line 608)

Neither Bishop nor Cardinal is stated to be worth VP anywhere in the source. The win examples confirm only Fief titles, Pope, and King count: "A Player could win by controlling 3 Fiefs at the end of a round, or 2 Fiefs AND the Title of King or the Pope, or 1 Fief and both Titles of King and Pope!" (lines 1465-1467).

No support found for "Each Bishop title = 1 VP" or "Cardinal = 1 VP" (searched: "bishop.*VP," "cardinal.*VP," "1VP.*bishop," "bishop.*worth," "cardinal.*worth").

This would cause players to believe they can win with a Bishop + 2 Fiefs (3 VP by summary) when the source requires 2 Fiefs + King or Pope for a 3-VP win.

**Finding 2 — Turn phases wrong order / wrong content / missing phases**

Summary describes 5 phases:
1. Income Phase — Collect income from villages/mills, bishops tithe, fief lords levy tallage
2. Purchase Phase — Buy troops, mills, strongholds, fief titles
3. Card Phase — Draw and play event cards
4. Action Phase — Move troops, attack, siege
5. Political Phase — Marriages, elections (Bishop, Cardinal, Pope, King)

Source has 7 phases in this order (lines 273-319):
1. 'Hear Ye, Hear Ye' — Marriage Alliances, Elect Bishops, Elect Pope, Elect King
2. Draw & Play Cards — Discard/draw Lord and Fortune cards, resolve Disaster cards, play Fortune cards
3. Collect Income — Villages, mills, tithes, tallages
4. Purchase — Troops, buildings, titles
5. Movement — Lords and troops move
6. Battles — Resolve combat
7. End Round — Check for winner

Critical errors:
- Summary Phase 1 (Income) = Source Phase 3: players collecting income before elections is wrong; in the source, all elections happen BEFORE income collection.
- Summary Phase 5 (Political: marriages, elections) = Source Phase 1: these happen FIRST in the round, not last.
- Summary completely omits Source Phase 5 (Movement) as a named phase and Source Phase 6 (Battles) as a named phase.
- Source Phase 2 is "Draw & Play Cards" but summary calls it "Card Phase" and misdescribes it as "Draw and play event cards that may affect the game state" — the actual phase includes both Lord cards (new family members) and Fortune/Disaster cards, with sub-steps.

**Finding 3 — "Purchase Fief Title" condition wrong**

Summary: "If you control all villages and at least one stronghold in a fief, you may purchase the title."

Source (lines 974-976): "A Fief's Title may be purchased by a Player if he controls all of the Villages in the Fief and at least one of these Villages contains a Stronghold." — actually this matches, the wording is equivalent. NOT a finding.

**Finding 4 — Tiebreaker missing / Player Reference wrong**

Summary: "Players may also win through diplomatic agreements." This is a vague misstatement; diplomatic agreements are negotiation mechanics, not a formal victory path.

Source (lines 1480-1484): "A Solo Victory beats an Alliance Victory. If Players are tied, a winner is determined by who has the following in order: The Title of King, then Pope, then Queen Regent, then controlling the most Fiefs, then Bishoprics, then Villages." The full tiebreaker is absent from the summary.

**Finding 5 — Player Reference: "VP to win" table missing Alliance rule**

Summary Player Reference: "VP to win: 3 (standard)"

Source (line 317): "A Player wins with 3VPs. An Alliance wins with 4VPs."

The summary omits that allied players need 4 VP to win, which is a different threshold. The summary does mention alliances in the body ("Marriage alliances allow players to combine VP with their spouse's family") but does not state the 4 VP requirement.

**Procedural checks (verified from source)**

- Income: 1 Denier per controlled village, 2 Deniers per Mill — source (line 912-913) confirms. MATCH.
- Bishops restricted to unmarried male Lords — source (line 264-265) confirms. MATCH.
- Marriage alliances combine VP — source confirms (lines 118-122). MATCH.

**Exhaustion rule**: Source (lines 689-693): "When a Player wishes to draw a new card and the 'Lord' or 'Fortune & Disaster' Draw Deck is empty, reshuffle the corresponding Discard Pile and place these cards face down on the appropriate Draw Deck space." Correctly implied by the summary's general card draw description though not explicit. MINOR omission.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7 (VP thresholds, troop costs, income values), SUPPORTED: 5 (Bishop/Cardinal VP not supported)

**Summary verdict: MAJOR** — Two serious errors: (1) Bishops and Cardinals are falsely assigned 1 VP each when the source grants VP only to Fief Titles, Pope, and King; this directly misleads players about when the winning condition (3 VP) is met. (2) The 7-phase turn sequence is scrambled into 5 phases with elections/marriages moved from Phase 1 to Phase 5 (last), making income collection appear to come before political actions that the source places first.
