(If you are both negative, the smaller number wakes up first and wins. If you are both -5 or worse, you killed each other, and there is no winner.)"

Summary: "A character is defeated when their body points reach zero. The surviving character wins."

The "zero" threshold matches the win condition. However, the summary omits two notable edge cases:
1. If both characters reach zero or below simultaneously, the one with the higher (less negative) total "wakes up first and wins."
2. If both reach -5 or below simultaneously, both are dead and there is no winner.
Neither of these would affect normal play; they are edge cases. The summary is incomplete but does not actively contradict the core win condition for the normal case. Verdict: MINOR.

This somewhat simplifies the double-step process (each player turns to their own move page first, then cross-references), but is not a material error for play.

## lotus

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Tie control rule reversed (MAJOR)

Summary (Special Rules): "Ties in control: no one scores the flower."

Source (lines 302–303): "If there is a tie for control of a flower, all tied players earn their choice of reward."

This is a direct contradiction. The summary says tied players get nothing; the source says ALL tied players earn their reward choice (Scoring Token or Special Power Token). This would cause incorrect scoring at the table.

### Finding 2 — Petal card VP attribution wrong (MAJOR)

Summary (Completing a Flower section): "The controlling player also earns a Special Power token (if any remain of that type). Completed flowers and their cards are removed."

Source (lines 304–308): "Pick the Flower: The player who completed the flower, by placing the last Petal Card, collects all of that flower's Petal Cards and places them in his scoring pile. At the end of the game, each Petal Card collected will be worth one victory point."

The summary omits entirely that the COMPLETER (the player who placed the last petal, regardless of control) collects all petal cards for 1 VP each. Petal cards are a major scoring source — the example shows players finishing with 25 petal cards = 25 points. The summary's final scoring table says "Scoring tokens are worth the number printed on them" but never mentions petal cards as a VP source at all.

### Finding 3 — Scoring tokens description incorrect (MAJOR)

Summary: "Scoring tokens are worth the number printed on them."

Source (lines 300, 327, 497–498): "A Scoring Token worth five victory points at the end of the game" and "Scoring Tokens= 5 Points each."

All scoring tokens are uniformly worth 5 VP. The summary's description ("the number printed on them") implies variable values, which is false.

### Finding 4 — Game-end trigger misdescribed (MAJOR)

Summary: "When any player's deck runs out, finish the current round."

Source (lines 460–462): "The last round of the game is triggered when a player draws the last card of his Player Deck. At that time, players each take one final turn, including the player who triggered the last round."

The trigger is the same (player deck emptied), but the source says players each take ONE FINAL TURN (not "finish the current round"). In a 4-player game these produce different results: "finish the current round" could mean up to 3 more players act after the trigger, whereas "each player takes one final turn" means all players get exactly one more turn each.

### Finding 5 — Controller earns choice, not just Special Power token

Summary: "The player with the most Guardian symbols ... controls it and takes the scoring tokens. The controlling player also earns a Special Power token (if any remain of that type)."

Source (lines 299–301): "The player with control earns a choice of earning either: A Scoring Token worth five victory points at the end of the game, OR A Special Power Token the player may use for the rest of the game."

The source says it's a CHOICE between a Scoring Token or a Special Power Token — not that they get scoring tokens AND potentially a Special Power token separately. The summary's description is wrong on the reward structure.

## lowlands

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn structure massively simplified/wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "The game plays over several rounds. Each round: 1. Place Farmers: Players take turns placing one farmer at a time... 2. Resolve Actions... 3. Flood Phase: Reveal flood cards; compare dike strength to flood level. 4. End of Round: Advance phase marker, refill displays."

Source (lines 216–221): "The game is divided into phases that are grouped into three stages. Each of the three stages consists of six phases resolved in the following order: Turn of the Tide, I Work, II Upkeep, I Work, II Upkeep, III High Tide. After playing through all three stages, you will resolve one final phase: the storm surge phase."

The actual structure is three stages × 6 phases each, plus a terminal Storm Surge phase. There are two Work phases and two Upkeep phases within each stage. The summary's four-step round structure is wrong. Key omissions: the Upkeep phase (which includes breeding, income, and farmer retrieval) is entirely absent, and the Storm Surge phase (where accumulated dike breach penalties are finally resolved) is absent. A player following the summary would never perform breeding or income steps, fundamentally breaking the game.

### Finding 2 — Tiebreaker absent (MAJOR)

Summary (Player Reference): "VP Summary: Sheep, Dike points, Coins, Resource cards, Farm tiles." No tiebreaker stated.

Source (lines 855–861): "In the case of a tie, the tied player who is furthest on the dike track wins the game. If there is still a tie, the tied player whose dike point marker is on top wins the game."

Tiebreaker is entirely missing from summary.

### Finding 3 — Dike breach token penalties in Storm Surge missing from scoring (MAJOR)

Summary does not mention the storm surge phase, dike breach tokens, or the VP penalties they cause.

Source (lines 898–911): During Storm Surge, if the dike breaks, each player discards one sheep per dike breach token. If not enough sheep, pay coins equal to sheep value per shortfall. If not enough coins, lose 1 VP per coin short. These negative VPs must be recorded on the scorepad.

This is a scoring source (negative) entirely absent from the summary.

### Procedural Claims Verified

More dike work = dike points worth more, sheep worth less." — This is broadly correct in direction but abstracts away the actual mechanism (value marker moves right when dike holds, left when it breaks). Not contradicted, just simplified.
