## railways-of-the-world

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented "3 actions per turn" mechanic

Summary: "each player takes 3 actions"

Source: "Turns consist of the following options: 1. Build track … 2. Urbanize a gray city … 3. Upgrade engine … 4. Deliver one goods cube … 5. Take one RR Operations card"

The source lists these as options for a single turn, with no indication that a player takes 3 actions. Each option is numbered as a choice, and the word "Max 1 link may be created per turn" for build track confirms one action per turn. The source never states players take multiple actions per turn. The summary's claim of "3 actions" is invented; there is no support for it under any alternate search terms (searched: "action", "3 actions", "action points", "two actions").

### Finding 2 — Turn structure: Income and expenses described as a separate round phase

Summary describes: "Auction for turn order → Player turns in auction order → Income and expenses (collect income from deliveries, pay bond interest)"

Source: The income/end-of-round step is: "At the end of round 3 players receive money according to their placement on the score track minus $1000 per bond they possess." This happens at end of round 3 (the three-round grouping), not at the end of each individual round. Also, VP are scored on delivery (not "collected" at round end). The summary implies an income step every round; this is wrong.

### Finding 3 — Bond restriction omitted

Summary: "At any time, take a bond (worth $5,000) to fund operations."

Source: "Bonds can only be taken to make a purchase that the player could not afford otherwise."

The restriction — bonds may only be taken when the player cannot otherwise afford a purchase — is omitted from the summary. This would mislead players at the table.

### Finding 4 — Incomplete game-end trigger

Summary: "Game ends when the required number of empty city markers are placed."

Source: "Once all the empty city markers have been placed on the board, finish the current round (completing the three rounds) and then complete one more full round of three."

The game does not end immediately; you finish the current three-round block and then play one additional full three-round block. The summary's simplification misleads about when the game ends.

### Finding 5 — Baron card scoring omitted

Summary lists VP sources as: "Delivering goods (1 VP per link traversed), Railroad Operation card bonuses, Subtract 1 VP per outstanding bond."

Source: "Total up the players scores by points on the score track + their Baron card points if completed, and subtracting one point per bond owned."

Baron card scoring is entirely absent from the summary's Scoring section. Baron cards are set up at the start ("Deal 2 Baron cards (secret objective) to each player. Players get to keep one and discard the other.") and score at game end. This is a major omitted scoring source.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (bond value $5000 ✓, bond penalty 1VP ✓, empty city marker counts for each player count ✓, $1000 bond income penalty ✓), SUPPORTED: 3 (bond penalty of 1VP ✓, $5000 bond ✓, empty city counts ✓; bond restriction not supported, 3-actions not supported)

## rajas-of-the-ganges

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Tiebreaker rule wrong

Summary: "If multiple players would cross simultaneously (unlikely), compare total advancement."

Source: "If there are two or more players whose markers passed each other on their final turn, each of the players involved counts the difference of fame points between his money marker and his fame marker; the player with the most points wins the game. In case of a tie, the first player whose two markers passed each other wins."

The summary's tiebreaker ("compare total advancement") does not describe the actual rule. The actual tiebreaker is: (1) count the fame-point gap between markers — largest gap wins; (2) if still tied, the first player to have crossed wins. A table using the summary's tiebreaker would compute the wrong winner.

### Finding 2 — End-of-game trigger: current stage, not current round

Summary: "The game ends immediately when a player's fame marker and money marker meet or cross on the tracks. That player wins."

Source: "As soon as one player manages to have his fame marker and his money marker converge or pass each other, the end of the game is triggered. The current stage will still be completed – i.e., all players who are sitting between the player who has triggered the end of the game and the current starting player still have a chance to win and may place one more worker."

The game does not end immediately — the current stage (not the full round) is completed, giving subsequent players in the stage one more worker placement. The phrase "That player wins" is also wrong; a later player in the same stage can win.

### Finding 3 — Win condition description: "first player to cross" is misleading

Summary: "The game ends immediately when a player's fame marker and money marker meet or cross on the tracks. That player wins."

Source: "The first player to have his fame marker and his money marker converge or even pass each other on their tracks, has a good chance to win." (emphasis mine — not a guarantee). The source explicitly states later players in the same stage can also cross and potentially win by having a larger marker gap.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (max 10 dice on Kali — not mentioned in summary but confirmed in source; starting money positions space 3/4/5…; fame track worker at space 15, money track worker at space 20; karma max level 3; river bonus at die 1/2/3), SUPPORTED: 5 (all numbers found in source; the error is in the procedural tiebreaker)

## ramses-ii

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Component counts wrong: pyramids

Summary: "Pyramids (covering treasures): 30"

Source: "1 47 pyramids (16 x gold, 16 x red, 15 x blue)"

The source lists 47 pyramids total. The summary states 30.

### Finding 2 — Component counts wrong: treasure tokens described as "treasure tokens (hidden under pyramids): 30" but source has 12 treasure cut-outs

Summary: "Treasure tokens (hidden under pyramids): 30"

Source: "4 12 treasure cut-outs (each with one treasure and three empty spaces)" and "3 1 playing surface with 48 holes"

The source has 12 treasure cut-outs (not 30 separate tokens) placed in a game-box insert, with the playing surface (48 holes) placed on top. The spatial memory mechanic involves sliding pyramids on a fixed playing surface with visible treasure cut-outs underneath, not randomly placed tokens under each pyramid. The summary's description of 30 treasure tokens is incorrect.

### Finding 3 — Turn mechanism description incorrect: player count and when turn ends

Summary: "Move pyramids to locate the matching treasure. If found, collect the search card. If not, replace pyramids and the next player tries."

Source: "The player then slides exactly one neighboring pyramid onto the previously free space. There are three possibilities: 1. No treasure is showing: The player can move another pyramid that borders the free space. 2. A treasure is showing, but not the right one: He's out of luck! His turn is over. Now it's the next player's turn to search for the treasure showing on the card. 3. The correct treasure is showing: [player claims card, next player draws new card]"

The summary says "replace pyramids" when the wrong treasure is found, but the source does not say pyramids are replaced — the board state persists. Also, the summary implies a single move per turn, but when no treasure is showing the player can keep sliding. The description of the mechanism is significantly inaccurate.

### Finding 4 — Game ends when last search card is WON, not when the draw pile is exhausted

Summary: "The game ends when the last search card has been won."

Source: "The game is over as soon as a player has won the last search card." — This matches, but see tiebreaker below.

### Finding 5 — Tiebreaker omitted

Summary: "The player with the most points wins."

Source: "The player with the most points wins the game. If more than one player has the same number of points, all involved players count their cards and the one with the most search cards wins. If they are still tied, all involved players win!"

The tiebreaker (count cards if points are tied; all win if cards are also tied) is entirely missing from the summary.

### Finding 6 — Search card setup omitted: cards are NOT simply shuffled

Summary: "Shuffle search cards and place face-down as a draw pile."

Source: "Sort the 30 search cards according to the number on the back (1, 2, and 3). Shuffle each of the three stacks of cards separately before laying each stack, one on top of the other: Put the stack of cards with the 3 on the bottom, then put stack 2 on top of it, followed by the stack with the number 1 on top."

The summary says to shuffle all cards together. The source requires a specific ordered stacking of three separately-shuffled groups. The special actions (scorpion, duel, etc.) only appear in the 2-back stack, so this ordering is mechanically significant.

### Finding 7 — Player count wrong: up to 5 players, not 2-4

Summary frontmatter: "player_count: '2-4'"

Source: "For 1 to 5 players ages 7 years and up"

The source explicitly supports 1-5 players. The summary's frontmatter says 2-4.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7 (47 pyramids ✗ vs summary's 30; 30 search cards ✓; 12 treasure cut-outs ✗ vs summary's 30 tokens; 21 chips ✓; playing surface 48 holes — not in summary; player count 1-5 ✗ vs 2-4; search card values 1-4 ✓), SUPPORTED: 3

**Victory conditions:**
- Summary: "Raptor wins if 3 baby raptors escape the board OR all scientists are eliminated."

- Summary: "Scientist wins if 3 baby raptors are captured OR the mother raptor has 5 sleep tokens on her."

**Card resolution mechanics:**
- Summary: "Same value → both cards have no effect; the round ends immediately. Different values → player with lower card applies its card effect. Then, player with higher card receives action points equal to the difference."
- Source: "If both cards have the same value, they have no effect. The round ends immediately. In most cases however, both cards have a different value. In that case, the player who played the card with the lowest value goes first and applies the effects of his card … Then, his opponent receives a number of action points equal to the difference between the values of the two cards." ✓

**AP costs:**

- Scientist: Move 1 AP ✓; Stand up frightened 1 AP ✓; Sleep baby 1 AP ✓; Capture sleeping baby 1 AP ✓; Shoot mother 1 AP ✓

- Raptor card 2/6 (Disappearance+Observation): remove mother, place after scientist spends AP, scientist shows card next round ✓

- Scientist card 3 (Jeep x2) / 8 (Jeep x4): straight-line moves, fire removed on path ✓

**Deck exhaustion:**
- Summary: "When a player's deck is empty, shuffle all played cards into a new deck, then draw to 3."
- Source: "If a player's deck is empty, he keeps the cards he has in hand, shuffles all his played cards to create a new deck, and then draws a card to have three cards in hand." ✓

One minor note: the summary says "Raptor wins" with "0 scientists on the board" while the source says "no more scientists on the board" — these are equivalent descriptions. ✓

## the-princes-of-florence

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Auction description wrong: players do not "each buy exactly 1 item"

Summary: "In each round, exactly the number of items equal to the number of players are auctioned. Each player must buy exactly 1 item."

Source: "He is not required to do this; he may pay nothing and take nothing." (referring to the last player who can take an object for 200 Florin) and "After all players have acquired objects or chosen not to acquire objects this round, the Auction phase ends."

The source explicitly says players may choose not to acquire an object. The summary's claim that "each player must buy exactly 1 item" is incorrect and would mislead players at the table.

### Finding 2 — Building adds +4 WV (not +3) per matching building

Summary: "Each matching Building: +3 WV"

Source: "If the player has the Building where the artist or scholar prefers to work, he adds 4 points to the WV for this Work."

The summary says buildings give +3 WV but the source says +4 WV. This is a direct numerical error in the scoring section.

(Note: The summary correctly states "Each matching Landscape: +3 WV" and "Each Freedom: +3 WV" — both confirmed in source. The building value alone is wrong.)

### Finding 3 — Scoring from Profession cards: summary omits the counting rule

Summary (Actions section): "Work Value = base value + bonuses from matching Buildings, Landscapes, Freedoms, Jesters, and Builders."

Source: "For each Profession or Recruiting card the player has in his hand or on the table, he adds 1 point to the WV for the Work."

The summary's "Producing a Work" subsection omits Profession/Recruiting cards as a source of WV. The Player Reference actions table also lists only Buildings, Landscapes, Freedoms, Jesters, and Builders — no mention of Profession cards adding WV. A player computing WV from the summary alone would systematically undercount.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreaker: money wins ties

Summary: no tiebreaker mentioned.

Source: "If two or more players tie with the most PP, the player among them with the most money is the winner."

The tiebreaker (most money wins) is missing from the summary.

### Finding 5 — "Recruit a Builder or Jester" listed as an action, but this is not a standard Action Phase action

Summary lists as one of 6 possible actions: "Recruit a Builder or Jester: Hire one using a Recruiting card."

Source lists the five actions as: Complete a Work, Build a Building, Take a Profession card (once only), Introduce a Freedom (once only), Acquire a Bonus card. Recruiting cards are acquired during the Auction phase, and using a Recruiting card is not one of the Action phase actions — "The use of a Recruiting card in the Action phase does not count as one of the two allowed actions." The summary incorrectly lists it as a separate standard action.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 11 (building WV +4 ✗ vs summary +3; landscape +3 ✓; freedom +3 ✓; jester +2 ✓; profession card +1 — missing from summary; building cost 700 Florin ✓; with 1-2 builders 300 Florin ✓; with 3 builders 0 Florin ✓; take profession card costs 300 Florin ✓; buy bonus card 300 Florin ✓; 7 rounds ✓), SUPPORTED: 9
