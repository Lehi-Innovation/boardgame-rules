## imperial

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK — extracted file is the Imperial rulebook.

### Finding 1 — Wrong Investor card starting position (MAJOR)

Summary: "Investor card to player left of Russia's controller."
Source (lines 351–354): "At the start of the game, the investor card goes to the player seated to the left of the player who controls **Austria-Hungary**. If there is none, the investor card goes to the player to the left of the player who controls Italy."

This is directly contradicted. The summary gives Russia's controller as the reference point; the source gives Austria-Hungary's controller.

### Finding 2 — Wrong rondel movement cost formula (MAJOR)

Summary: "First 3 spaces free; each additional space costs (1 + Power Factor) million."
Source (lines 231–235): "The playing piece may be moved to one of the three spaces ahead at no cost; each additional space costs the player who leads the government of that nation **2 million**, paid to the bank."

The "(1 + Power Factor)" formula is from Imperial 2030, not Imperial. In Imperial the cost is a flat 2 million per extra space regardless of Power Factor.

### Finding 3 — Taxation: invented "unoccupied home provinces" tax source (MAJOR)

Summary: "Count tax revenue (factories + flag regions + unoccupied home provinces). Score power points per tax chart. Government receives personal bonus."
Source (lines 641–645, and quick-reference at line 1019): "The national taxes are comprised of two components: 2 mil. per unoccupied factory; 1 mil. per flag."

There is no "unoccupied home provinces" component in the tax formula. The summary invents a third tax source not present in the rules. (A factory that happens to be in a home province contributes through the "factory" component, not separately as a "home province".)

Additionally, the summary omits that in the Taxation step, the nation must pay 1 million soldiers' pay per army and fleet from the tax revenue (source lines 688–692: "the nation has to pay 1 mil. soldiers' pay to each of its armies and fleets").

### Finding 4 — Investor action: "Government gets 2 million bonus" is misleading/wrong attribution

Summary (Investor row): "Government gets 2 million bonus. Each player may buy 1 bond. Investor card holder gets 1 million from bank."
Source (lines 607–609): "The player who is holding the Investor card (Investor) gets paid 2 million from the bank and then may invest in any nation."

The 2-million payment goes to the **Investor card holder**, not to the government. The summary's Player Reference table reverses this: it says "2M bonus" goes to government and "1M from bank" goes to investor card holder. But the source gives the investor card holder 2 million (and no separate 1 million entry). This contradicts the summary.

The discrepancy in amount (2M vs 1M) would matter — but since this same issue appears in both summaries and Imperial was flagged for it, I note it here but verify whether the summary's overall description is still playable.

There is no separate "government gets 2 million bonus" in the Investor action. The summary's description of Investor is wrong in the same way as Imperial but the Player Reference table for Imperial 2030 says "2 million to government" for the Investor row which is incorrect.

HOWEVER — since the summary's rondel table (the key player-facing reference) says "Investor | Bonus + bond buying round | 2 million to government" — this is wrong. The 2 million goes to the investor card holder, not the government. This is the same MAJOR-class error as in Imperial. But re-reading more carefully: in Imperial 2030's Investor action, interest is paid first to bond holders (step 1), then the investor card holder gets 2M and buys a bond (step 2). The government gets no separate 2M bonus here.

Wait — let me recheck the Imperial 2030 Player Reference table in the summary: "Investor | Bonus + bond buying round | 2 million to government." This is incorrect per source. The 2M goes to the investor card holder.

Despite this error in the Player Reference table, the main body text of the Imperial 2030 summary correctly describes: "The government receives a bonus from the treasury (2 million)" — this is WRONG, AND the Player Reference also says "2 million to government." However, this is a less severe finding than in Imperial because: (1) it's the same systematic error appearing in both summaries suggesting they were generated together, (2) in practice the investor card holder IS often the government, though they are distinct roles, (3) the scoring formula and end condition (the most critical playability elements) are correctly stated.

On balance: this finding would mislead players about who receives the 2M payment on the Investor space. This is a MAJOR error — a wrong direction-of-payment claim in a core action.
