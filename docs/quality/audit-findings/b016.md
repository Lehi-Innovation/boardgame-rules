# Audit b016: android

**Files compared:** `rules/android.md` vs `extracted/android-rules.txt`

## Verdict: MAJOR

The summary reads like a generic reconstruction from theme/memory rather than the source rulebook. It invents an evidence "light/dark" scheme and a scoring source that do not exist, conflates plot cards with twilight cards, fabricates the daily turn structure, and omits the game's core action economy (Time) and twilight-shift system, plus several concrete scoring values.

## Findings

### 1. MAJOR — Invented "light/dark" evidence tokens
- Summary (Components): "Evidence tokens (light and dark)"
- Summary (Actions/Investigation): "Place evidence tokens (light = incriminating, dark = exonerating) on suspects."
- Summary (Special Rules): "the suspect with the most light evidence is guilty."
- Source: "60 Evidence Tokens (1 +5 token, 4 +4 tokens, 6 +3 tokens, 11 +2 tokens, 11 +1 tokens, 7 –1 tokens, 7 –2 tokens, 4 –3 tokens, 1 –4 token, 4 surprise witness tokens, and 4 perjury tokens)" and "Most evidence tokens bear a number ranging from 5 (particularly damning evidence) to –4 (particularly exonerating evidence). When the game ends, the suspect with the highest postive total of evidence is the murderer."
- The phrase "light/dark evidence" appears nowhere in the source (grep confirmed). Light/dark refers exclusively to twilight cards. A player following the summary would misunderstand the entire evidence system.

### 2. MAJOR — Invented scoring source: "Evidence Placed"
- Summary (Scoring): "Evidence Placed: Bonus points for evidence tokens placed during investigation."
- Source (Tallying the Points): scoring sources are exactly: Guilty hunch correct +15 VP, Innocent hunch correct +5 VP, each plot ending (varies), each conspiracy token +4 VP, each Haas token +3 VP, each Jinteki token +3 VP, each trauma counter –1 VP, each favor +0 VP (modified only by puzzle links). There is no VP for placing evidence anywhere in the rulebook.

### 3. MAJOR — Plot cards conflated with twilight cards; twilight system omitted
- Summary (Components): "Plot cards (light and dark) for each detective"; (Personal Storyline): "Each detective has unique light and dark plot card decks."
- Source: these are two distinct systems: "210 Twilight Cards (44 Caprice... )" — "Each detective has two decks of twilight cards – a dark deck and a light deck" — versus "56 Plot Cards (12 Caprice, 12 Floyd, 12 Louis, 12 Rachel, and 8 Raymond)" which are start/crossroads/ending cards resolved via good/bad baggage at the end of days 3 and 6 of each week.
- The entire twilight track / light-shift / dark-shift cost system ("dark shift to the right to play a light card; light shift to the left to play a dark card") is absent from the summary, as is the baggage mechanic that actually drives plot outcomes.

### 4. MAJOR — Fabricated daily turn structure
- Summary (Turn Structure): a four-step daily sequence "1. Move 2. Investigate 3. Resolve Plot Cards 4. Advance Time."
- Source: each round = Daytime Phase (each player in turn receives Time, "usually six," spent 1 Time per action: move, follow up a lead, draw/discard a twilight card, play a light card, get a jump on the case, use a location ability) then End of Day Phase (advance day marker, resolve calendar events, change first player). Plots are NOT resolved daily — "At the end of the third and sixth days of each week, the players resolve their plots." Dark cards are played on other players' turns for 0 Time, not in a dedicated step. The 6-Time action economy — the heart of the game — is never mentioned.

### 5. MINOR/MAJOR — Murder scoring described too vaguely; hunch cards never named
- Summary: "Points based on whether the guilty suspect matches your preferred suspect."
- Source: each player is secretly dealt one guilty and one innocent hunch card at setup; correct guilty hunch = +15 VP, correct innocent hunch = +5 VP; hunches for killed suspects never pay off; obsession (both hunches on one suspect) gives +3 VP bonus if guilt margin >= 5. None of these numbers or the hunch-card mechanic appear in the summary.

### 6. MAJOR — Omitted core systems
- Hits: "Should a suspect ever have three hit tokens on him, he is instantly killed" (evidence/alibis discarded, hunches on him void) — entirely absent.
- Alibis/surprise witnesses/perjuries (each worth –5/+5 guilt) — absent.
- Strong/weak evidence files (lowest value in strong file and highest value in weak file discarded at case close) — absent.
- Conspiracy links modifying end-game VP values, and conspiracy tokens earned for completing a row/column/diagonal — absent (summary only says "complete sections... for VP").
- Trauma counters (–1 VP each), Haas/Jinteki tokens (+3 VP each) — absent from scoring.
- Lead movement (follower hands the lead to the player on his right to relocate), climax (last two days, leads removed) — absent.

### 7. MINOR — Conspiracy action mischaracterized
- Summary: "Collect conspiracy puzzle pieces at various locations."
- Source: uncovering the conspiracy is one of the two options when following up a lead (the other being placing evidence); pieces are drawn from three stacked piles (shift/favor/baggage) by hero-marker position, then added to the central puzzle. Pieces are not "collected" and are not tied to specific map locations.

### 8. MINOR — Favors mischaracterized
- Summary: "Favors provide one-time bonuses or ongoing benefits."
- Source: "Favors do not have any abilities on their own; they are normally spent to pay all or part of the cost to use a location ability or play a card." Four types (corp, political, society, street), gained at specific bank locations.

## Conclusion

Multiple invented mechanics (light/dark evidence, evidence-placement VP), a fabricated turn structure, conflation of two distinct card systems, and omission of the Time economy, twilight shifting, hits, alibis, strong/weak files, and all concrete VP values. This summary would substantially mislead players at the table.

**Verdict: MAJOR**
