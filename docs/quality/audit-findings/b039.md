# Audit b039: border-reivers

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Compared `rules/border-reivers.md` against `extracted/border-reivers-rules.txt` (GMT Multi-Player Rulebook, Ed Beach). Component counts are accurate, but the summary contains several contradicted rules, invented mechanics, wrong numbers in scoring/turn-order, and omits an entire combat type (Battles) and the Default Action system.

## Major findings

1. **Winter turn order contradicted.** Summary: "lowest VP goes first on Turn 1; VP order on Turns 2-3." Source 7.1: Turn 1 order is a **random cube draw** from the Turn Order Container (re-drawn before round 2 per 7.7); Turns 2-3 use **reverse VP order** (last place attacks first). The summary's Turn 1 rule is invented and its Turn 2-3 wording obscures that lowest VP goes first there.

2. **Sixth Summer round contradicted.** Summary: "In the 6th round, pass your drawn card to the next player instead of keeping it." Source 5.6: each family selects one card to act on normally and adds the other to the **Summer Cards Discards pile** — nothing is passed.

3. **Summer draft mechanic misdescribed.** Summary: "each player draws 1 Summer card and must choose one action." Source 5.0: each family is **dealt a hand of 7 cards**, then each round simultaneously selects one card and **passes the remainder** to a neighbor (left T1, right T2, random T3). The pick-and-pass draft is the game's core engine and is absent.

4. **Battle combat type omitted.** Source 7.0 defines four combat types: Raid, Feud, Gaolbreak, **Battle** (Event-card battlefields fought for England or Scotland, with hits scoring card-specified VP and the winner gaining card-specified Notoriety; special Rising of the North rules). The summary's Actions section lists only Raid/Gaolbreak/Feud, and it mischaracterizes the "Battle Awards" step as "VP for successful raids" — source 7.9 is about granting post-combat awards for Battles contested by only one side.

5. **Invented Feud mechanic.** Summary: "Feud Attack: Target an opponent's Family Seat to steal livestock stored from previous feuds." Source: no livestock is stored at or stolen from Family Seats. Feud hits award 3 VP each; the winner gains 2 Notoriety (4 with Ill Week) and the loser drops 1; Feud Cubes in the "Feuds with" box grant +1 attack die each. Nothing is stolen in a Feud.

6. **Invented Notoriety placement rule.** Summary Winter step 3: "Place Notoriety: Advance your Notoriety in the target March." Source 7.3: Notoriety is placed (first combat round only, in reverse turn order) from **Held Notoriety cards, Offices, and Allied Graynes** into the Marches those cards allow — it is not tied to the raid's target March.

7. **Spring scoring sources omitted.** Source 8.2 awards **2 VP per Cube in your Gaol (Reivers in Gaol)** every Spring and allows **Border Ballad cards for VP on Turn 3**; neither appears in the summary's scoring section.

8. **Default Actions omitted.** Source 5.4/5.5: Discarding a Summer card grants a Default Action (add 1 Horse / 1 Sheep / 3 Cattle / 1 Notoriety in Home March / bastle 1 Sheep, 2 from Turn 2). The summary describes Discard as merely placing the card in the discard pile, dropping the entire reward system (also referenced by Spring Bonus Actions).

## Minor findings

- **Economic Update misstated:** summary says "Gain Cattle and Horses based on Sheep on Map total"; source 6.1: check Horse Limit (discard excess), check Hand Size, then gain **Cattle income only** — Horses are never gained here.
- **Cattle-as-currency omitted:** source 5.1/5.2 requires paying Cattle costs to Recruit/Play cards; the summary's Recruit/Play descriptions and Livestock section never mention costs, and the +1 Cattle gained for each Hold (5.3) is also absent.
- **Bastles omitted:** flipped-down Sheep protected from Raids (5.2.6, 7.5) is a core defensive system never mentioned.
- **Component list omission:** "1 Book of Historical Notes" missing from the Components section.
- **Raid loot incomplete:** summary says raids "steal sheep"; Cattle hits also steal Cattle (rolls of 3-4, 1 VP; Sheep hits 5-6, 2 VP — none of these values appear).
- **Traits scope:** summary says "4+ player games"; source says 4- or 6-player game only.
- Bonus Actions are Turns 1-2 only (source 8.3); summary does not state the restriction.

## Verdict rationale

Multiple table-misleading problems: wrong event-draw count, wrong tiebreaker order, end-game scoring missing Horses (the highest-value livestock), contradicted Turn 1 turn order and sixth-round rule, an invented Feud "steal stored livestock" mechanic, and the complete omission of the Battle combat type and Default Actions. MAJOR.
