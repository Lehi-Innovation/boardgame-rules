## gloomhaven-jaws-of-the-lion

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Exhaustion condition from running out of cards (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "A character is exhausted (eliminated for the scenario) when: … They have no cards in hand at the start of a round and fewer than 2 cards in their discard pile (cannot select 2 cards to play)."

**Source (extracted lines 854–858):**
> "If a character is unable to play two cards at the start of a round because they do not have at least two cards in their hand, that character becomes exhausted."

The summary adds a second conjunctive clause — "and fewer than 2 cards in their discard pile" — that does not appear in the source rule. The source condition is solely about hand size. A player with exactly 1 card in hand (and 0 in discard) is exhausted per the source, but the summary would leave them in play (since they don't meet the "no cards in hand" criterion). This would cause players to continue playing an exhausted character for at least one more round.

Note: Short resting (which requires ≥2 in discard) occurs at end of round, so in practice the scenario "1 in hand, 0 in discard" is reachable (e.g., after losing cards to negate damage, or a lost action reduces hand). The summary's condition is wrong and actionably misleading.

### Additional checks (no further MAJOR findings)

- Starting 6 "A" ability cards confirmed (source line 266: "their 6 ability cards marked 'A'").
- Starting attack modifier deck composition (20 cards: six +0, five -1, five +1, one -2, one +2, one ×2, one null) confirmed (source line 597–598).
- Short rest procedure: source (lines 836–844) confirms shuffle discard, randomly lose 1 to lost pile, return rest to hand; pay 1 HP to lose a different random card instead. Summary matches.
- Long rest: initiative 99, heal 2, recover spent items, choose 1 card to lose. Source (lines 1539–1550) confirms. Summary matches.
- Poison condition: source says "All attacks targeting the figure gain a +1 Attack bonus… poison prevents the heal from increasing the figure's current hit point value." Summary says "+1 to all attack damage suffered; healing has no effect." Functionally equivalent, directionally correct.
- Scenario level formula: source (lines 1989–1990) "average level of the characters divided by two and rounded up." Summary matches.
- Damage negation: source (lines 1149–1151) "lose one card from their hand or two cards from their discard pile." Summary matches.
- Conditions (Immobilize, Stun, Muddle, Strengthen) confirmed at source lines 640–648.
- Disarm confirmed at source line 1140.
- Wound confirmed at source lines 1517–1523.
- Curse/Bless as modifier deck cards confirmed at source lines 1524–1536.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12, SUPPORTED: 11 (1 wrong: exhaustion condition)

## glory-to-rome

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Game-end trigger for Sites is wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "All In-Town Sites of any one type are taken"

**Source (lines 665–669):**
> "The game ends when any of the following occur: … A player lays a Foundation which claims the last Site card (see above) that is not Out of Town."

The source trigger is the very last remaining in-town Site card of any type being claimed (a single card event). The summary says "all sites of any one type are taken," which means the game would end when a particular material's N sites run out (e.g., all 3 Rubble sites), even though many other in-town sites remain. These are completely different triggers. Players using the summary would end the game far too early (as soon as one material runs out rather than when the very last in-town site is claimed).

### Finding 2 — Draw pile game-end: fabricated "full turn cycle" qualifier (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "The draw pile is exhausted (after a full turn cycle)"

**Source (lines 665–680):**
> "The game ends when any of the following occur: The draw deck runs out (you may count it). … The game ends immediately – players do not continue with the current turn and do not perform any more actions."

The source says game ends immediately when the draw deck runs out; the summary adds "(after a full turn cycle)" which has no support in the source.

### Finding 3 — Missing game-end conditions (MAJOR)

**Summary claims** only three game-end conditions (sites, draw pile, Catacomb). The source lists five:

**Source (lines 665–678):**
> "• Any player has a completed Forum Romanum Structure and at least one of each Client type in Clientele and one Material of each type in Stockpile (see Appendix: Forum Romanum, p.22). • All other players agree to 'surrender' to a player for any reason."

The Forum Romanum end condition is entirely absent from the summary. The surrender condition is also absent. Crucially, the source notes that "In the event of a Forum Romanum or 'surrender' victory that player wins – there is no need to score Victory Points." Without knowing these conditions, players would not recognize an immediate Forum Romanum win.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreaker reversed (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Ties broken by most materials in Vault."

**Source (lines 693–694):**
> "If there is a tie, the tying player with the most cards in his hand wins."

The tiebreaker is most cards in hand, not most materials in Vault. Every tied game scored by the summary would produce a wrong winner.

### Finding 5 — Legionary demands from wrong players (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Demand materials from other players' hands (matching your card + neighbors in Pool)"

**Source (lines 581–588, also lines 604–607):**
> "If your neighbors (the players sitting left and right of you) have one or more cards of the color you are demanding in their hand then they must give them to you." (Card legend: "Move one card from each of your neighbor's HANDS and POOL into your STOCKPILE")

The source restricts the demand to neighbors (left and right players only). The summary says "other players' hands" — all opponents, not just neighbors. In a 5-player game this doubles or triples the Legionary's potential haul.

## goa

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Expedition card scoring completely wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Remaining expedition cards (1 VP per card)"

**Source (lines 322–329):**
> "Victory points for Expedition cards: 1 symbol / 2 identical symbols / 3 identical symbols / 4 identical symbols — 10 VP / 5 identical symbols — 15 VP"

Scoring is based on sets of identical symbols among retained cards, not 1 VP per card. The 10 VP for 4 identical symbols and 15 VP for 5 identical symbols shows these are set bonuses, not per-card values. The summary's description of "1 VP per card" is not supported anywhere in the source and would make Expedition cards worth far less than their actual value. (VP for 1–3 symbols are OCR-dropped in the extraction but the table structure and the 4/5-symbol rows confirm set-based scoring.)

### Finding 2 — Colony scoring described as "sets of different types" but source is total count (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Completed sets of different colony types"

**Source (lines 317–321):**
> "Victory points for the number of colonies founded during the game: 1 colony 1 VP / 2 colonies 3 VP / 3 colonies 6 VP / 4 colonies 10 VP"

The source awards VP based on total number of colonies founded, not on sets of different colony types. A player who founded 2 Quilon colonies (same type) would earn 3 VP per the source; the summary's "sets of different types" language implies variety is required.

### Finding 3 — Auction order reversed (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "3. Bidding: Starting from the highest-numbered token, the tile owner (token placer) auctions their tile."

**Source (line 169):**
> "Starting with the flag, each tile with an Auction token on it is now auctioned off in order (1, 2, 3...)."

The source auctions from token 1 (flag, placed by the first player) through to the highest token. The summary reverses this, saying auctions start from the highest-numbered token. Players using the summary would have the first player auction last (instead of first) each round.

### Finding 4 — Colony founding cost includes ships incorrectly (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Found Colony: Pay required ships and colonists to claim a colony tile"

**Source (lines 252–254):**
> "To found a colony, a player needs to recruit a minimum number of colonists: 6 colonists for Quilon, 8 colonists for Cochin, 10 colonists for Madras, and 12 colonists for Calicut."

Only colonists are required. Ships are never mentioned as a cost for founding colonies. Players using the summary would incorrectly think they also need to pay ships.

### Finding 5 — Scoring omits "most ducats" VP bonus (MAJOR)

**Summary claims** the VP sources are: colony tiles, development board progress, sets of colony types, expedition cards, tiles with VP values, certain tile bonuses.

**Source (lines 330–333):**
> "Victory points for the player with the most ducats: The player with the most ducats scores 3 victory points. In case of a tie for the most ducats, the tied players score 3 VP each."

The "most ducats" bonus (3 VP) is completely missing from the summary's scoring section. This is a scoring source that would be omitted in every final scoring.

### Additional checks

- Tiebreaker: source (line 309) "In case of a tie, the tied player with the most ducats wins." Summary matches.
- Starting resources: 4 ships, 2 colonists; first player gets 5 ducats, others get 10 ducats. Confirmed (source lines 125–131).
- 8 rounds total (4 Phase A + 4 Phase B): confirmed (source lines 135–140).
- Development board: 5 tracks with progress markers. Confirmed (source line 195–216).
- Actions per round: 3 actions per player. Confirmed (source line 188).
- Auction tokens = player count + 1. Confirmed (source lines 117–120).
- Progress action cost: requires ships AND spices (not just ducats). Source (lines 202–210) "Moving a marker down one space costs ships and spices." Summary says "paying the required cost (ducats, spices, ships, or colonists depending on the track and level)" — the source specifically says ships AND spices together; ducats are not a development cost. This is a MINOR imprecision; dropped as track-dependent costs may vary.
- Expedition deck reshuffle: source (line 393) "If the Expedition draw pile ever runs out, simply shuffle the discard pile to create a new draw pile." Summary does not claim there is no reshuffle, so not an invented rule.
