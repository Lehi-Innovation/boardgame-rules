## conquistador-the-age-of-exploration — MAJOR

### Findings

1. **Wrong counter colors (Components).** Summary: "units for Spain (green), England (red), France (yellow), and optionally Portugal (blue)". Source (lines 200-209): "The Green counters are the Spanish; the Blue counters are the French; and the light Tan counters are the English. The Yellow ships and Brown and Yellow Gold Markers are not tied to a nationality." England=red and France=yellow are invented; "Portugal (blue)" collides with France=blue.

2. **Wrong turn length.** Summary: "Each game turn represents approximately 7 years." Source: turn 6 = "1521-1525" (line 914), "within 15 years (3 turns)" (line 703), "10 years (2 game-turns)" (lines 710-711) — i.e. 5 years per turn.

3. **Wrong campaign length.** Summary: "Campaign game covers 1495-1600 (~15 turns)". Source [21.1]: "It covers 21 Game-Turns of play" (line 2228). Errata also references explorers "in play on turns 18 and 19."

4. **Invented phase ("Victory Point Phase").** Summary lists phase 5 as "Victory Point Phase — Calculate current standings". Source [5.1] sequence is: A. Council Phase, B. Initial Naval Phase, C. Land Phase, D. Final Naval Phase, E. Maintenance Phase. There is no Victory Point Phase; the summary also relocates Maintenance into the Royal Council Phase (it is the final phase of the turn in the sequence of play, though rules section 6.8 is numbered under Royal Council operations).

5. **Contradicted: "Second Naval Phase — Same as First Naval Phase."** Source D: "Ships may not use Hemispheric Naval Movement in this phase, nor is combat allowed" (lines 486-488); the Final Naval Phase instead adds a Discovery Credit segment and banking of returned gold. Not the same as the Initial Naval Phase.

6. **Political Control requirements misstated.** Summary: "Political Points: Earned for controlling areas on the map (must have colonists and/or missionaries present)". Source [19.1] requires all five of: >=50% of all Colonists in the Area, >=75% of all Soldiers, no enemy ports, at least one friendly port, and at least 3 friendly Colonist units plus 2 friendly settlements. Missionaries play no role in control.

7. **Missionaries misrepresented.** Summary: "Missionaries: Convert native populations, reducing uprising risk and enabling political control" (and lists them as a generic unit type). Source [15.6]: Missionaries are Spanish-only and mandatory for Spain in native areas; per [15.66]/[17.32] a Missionary's rating is *added* to the Uprising Level — a plus-rated Missionary increases uprising risk (only minus-rated ones reduce it); plus-rated Missionaries instead help in Combat Against Natives [15.65]. "Enabling political control" is unsupported.

8. **Omitted VP values (Scoring).** Source [20.0]: 2 VP per Ducat in Treasury, 150 VP per Area under Political Control, variable VP per Discovery Table; Spain starts with 25 VP [18.16]. Summary gives only the three categories with no values.

9. **Omitted nationality restriction.** Conquistadors are Spanish-only ([15.51], counter manifest "Conquistador (Spanish only)"); summary does not say so. (Privateers are correctly flagged as English.)

## coraquest — MINOR

### Findings

1. **Omission: Threat token also advances when the Dungeon deck is empty.** Summary (Countdown Phase): "If the Explore marker is still on the Dungeon deck (no card was revealed this round), move the Threat token down one level." Source (lines 494-496): "If the Explore marker is still on top of the Dungeon deck **or the deck is empty**, move the Threat token down one level"; FAQ (lines 658-665) confirms spiders keep spawning after the deck runs out, and "Every round ends with a Countdown Phase, even when the final Dungeon card has been revealed" (lines 514-518). Players following the summary would stop advancing the Threat in the endgame.

2. **Revive cost ambiguity (mirrors a source inconsistency, noted for completeness).** Summary lists Revive under Free Actions, but in Special Rules says Stunned heroes are revived for "(1 Full Action)". The source itself contains both statements (Revive in the Free Action list, lines 125-129; "an adjacent hero spends one Full Action to Revive them", lines 610-612), so this is faithful reproduction of a rulebook contradiction, not invention — but the summary presents both without flagging.

3. **Slightly unsupported component detail.** Summary lists "Status effect tokens (Poison, Stinky, Stunned)". Source mentions only a Poison token (line 629); Stunned is marked by laying the standee on its side (line 599-600); no Stinky token is mentioned.

## cosmic-coasters — MINOR

### Findings

1. **Special power names misassigned/conflated.** The source has four named powers: TELEPORT INHIBITOR (line 28), STINGING DEFENSE (line 88), WARNING SYSTEM (line 89), RAPID TRANSIT (line 90), with four effect paragraphs (lines 68-87) separated from the names by OCR column merging. The summary's table:
   - "Teleport Inhibitor (Stinging Defense)" — presents two distinct powers as one name (with the repel-invaders effect, which is Teleport Inhibitor's).
   - "Warning System | When attacked and you win, opponent's ship is destroyed" — this effect ("When someone attacks you and you win, their ship is destroyed", lines 74-76) is by content Stinging Defense, not Warning System.
   - "(4th power varies)" — the teleport-escape effect (lines 83-87) belongs to the remaining name, Warning System; the summary declares the name unknown instead.
   All four effects are described accurately and Rapid Transit is matched correctly; only the name-to-effect mapping is scrambled. OCR layout makes the mapping ambiguous, but the conflation "Teleport Inhibitor (Stinging Defense)" asserts an equivalence the source contradicts (they are separate headings).

2. **Trivial wording drift on Teleport Inhibitor.** Summary: "Opponents must win a round of RPS before teleporting"; source: "must first play a round of RPS and **not lose**" (lines 70-71). The summary's following clause ("ties allow landing only on empty spaces") restores the correct net behavior, so impact is negligible.
