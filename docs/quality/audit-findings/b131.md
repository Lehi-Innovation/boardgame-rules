## london

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — WRONG TIEBREAKER

Summary: "Tiebreaker: most money remaining."

Source (lines 302-304): "The player with the most prestige wins. If there is a tie, the tied player with the least poverty wins. If there is still a tie, the tied player with the most borough cards wins. If there is still a tie, the tied player with the city card worth the most prestige wins."

The source gives a four-tier tiebreaker: least poverty → most borough cards → city card worth most prestige. The summary invents "most money remaining" which does not appear anywhere in the source's tiebreaker section. This would cause players to incorrectly resolve tied games.

### Finding 2 — GAME-END TRIGGER MISDESCRIBED

Summary: "Game ends when the city deck runs out and a player cannot draw. Finish the current round."

Source (line 279): "If the city deck is empty at the end of any player's turn, each other player takes one final turn."

The source triggers game end at the end of the player's turn when the deck empties, and gives each OTHER player exactly one final turn (not "finish the current round"). "Finish the current round" implies the player who triggers game-end also gets a full remaining turn, which is incorrect per the source.

### Supporting procedural checks (5 verified):
1. Draw 1 card at start of turn: source line 183 confirmed.
2. Develop: discard a card of the same colour to add a card to display: source line 191 confirmed.
3. Buy land: choose face-up borough, pay cost, overlay it: source line 214-216 confirmed.
4. Run city: poverty = one per stack + £10 of loans + each card in hand: source lines 253-257 confirmed.
5. Loans: take £10, pay £15 to repay, unpaid loans = -7 prestige each: source lines 125-130, 297 confirmed.

### Poverty table verification:
Source shows columns: 1-2 → -1, 3 → -2, 4 → -5, 5 → -7, 6 → -9, 7 → -11, 8 → -13, 9 → -15, each point over 10. Summary table matches.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 14, SUPPORTED: 13 (tiebreaker money figure unsupported)

## loony-quest

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Note: Prefilter flagged SOURCE_SUSPECT (title tokens absent from first 3000 chars), but reading the source confirms it is the Loony Quest rulebook (mentions Arkadia, Fedoor, Loonies, Laurent Escoffier/David Franck, all matching the summary). Proceeding to detailed audit.

### Finding 1 — BROOM TOKEN EFFECT COMPLETELY WRONG

Summary: "Broom — Target opponent must draw with eyes closed for the first few seconds."

Source (lines 366-367): "Broom: a player who has a Broom Bonus may pass one of their Penalty tokens to another player of their choice."

The Broom lets you offload a Penalty token. The "eyes closed" description in the summary is entirely fabricated.

### Finding 2 — MOSQUITO TOKEN EFFECT WRONG

Summary: "Mosquito — Target opponent must draw with their non-dominant hand."

Source (lines 358-365): "The player receiving the Mosquito token must balance it on the end of their marker pen. The token must not fall off while they are drawing."

Mosquito is a balancing challenge, not a non-dominant hand challenge.

### Finding 3 — SHIELD TOKEN EFFECT WRONG

Summary: "Shield — Protects against one Prank played on you."

Source (lines 371-374): "Shield: a player who has a Shield may choose to cancel a loss of Experience Points caused by a Trap."

The Shield counters Trap XP losses in scoring, not Pranks during the Prank step.

### Finding 4 — CRAMP PENALTY DESCRIPTION WRONG

Summary: "Cramp — Must hold the pen in a fist."

Source (lines 385-386): "Cramp: a player affected by a Cramp must draw with their arm held totally straight, without bending their elbow."

Holding "in a fist" (summary) vs. arm fully straight without bending the elbow (source) are different constraints.

### Finding 5 — WORLD STRUCTURE WRONG

Summary: "Each World has 4 regular levels, 1 special stage, and 1 Boss level."

Source (lines 73-75): "Each World (except the seventh one) is divided into 6 levels, with a fearsome Boss defending the final level." Lines 31-32 show World 7 has "4 levels. 2 special stages."

Worlds 1-6 each have 6 levels (not 4+1+1). Only World 7 has the 4-level structure.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7, SUPPORTED: 4

## looot

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — HOUSE CAPTURE RULE WRONG

Summary: "When you surround a House with your Vikings on at least 3 of its adjacent spaces, capture it."

Source (lines 154-155): "When you place a Viking adjacent to a House, take 1 tile for that House."

No "3 adjacent spaces" requirement exists. Any single Viking adjacent to a House captures it immediately. This fundamentally changes how Houses are acquired.

### Finding 2 — CASTLE STARTING VP VALUE WRONG

Summary: "Castles: 5 VP each."

Source (lines 269-272): "She starts by adding the value of her bonuses to the starting value of the Castles (4), giving 11 (starting value of 4 + bonus of 3 + bonus of 4)."

Castle starting value is 4, not 5.

### Finding 3 — TROPHY DESCRIPTION MISLEADING

Summary: "Spend Axes to claim the lowest-value available Trophy. Trophy values increase as they're claimed."

Source (lines 217-224): "At the end of your turn, you may claim a Trophy. Be aware that you can only claim one Trophy during the game. Each Trophy is unique. To claim a Trophy, you must have at least as many Axes as shown on the Trophy."

The key rule "you can only claim one Trophy during the game" is omitted from the summary's Trophy action. Also "Trophy values increase as they're claimed" is not stated in the source — the ascending Trophy order is set at setup and taken by whoever claims first.

### Supporting procedural checks (5 verified):

3. Shield allows placement on occupied space + place second Viking: source lines 169-177 confirmed (though summary's description differs slightly from source's "place Viking on occupied space + gain 2 resources + place second Viking").
4. Completing Construction Site by placing required Resources/Buildings adjacent to it: source lines 208-213 confirmed.
5. Unfilled Longships cost -5 VP: source line 285 confirmed.

## lord-of-the-rings-the-confrontation

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — TURN ORDER REVERSED

Summary: "The Fellowship player always goes first."

Source (lines 173-174): "The Sauron player takes the first turn. Play then alternates between the two players for the rest of the game."

The Sauron player goes first, not the Fellowship player. This is a fundamental reversal that affects every game.

### Finding 2 — MULTIPLE FELLOWSHIP CHARACTER ABILITIES STRIPPED (Merry, Legolas, Gimli, Aragorn)

**Merry:**
Summary: "Merry (2) — No special ability."
Source (lines 616-619): "Merry defeats the Witch King immediately before any cards are played. In battles against all other enemies, the usual rules apply."
Merry has a major combat ability against the Witch King.

**Legolas:**
Summary: "Legolas (3) — No special ability."
Source (lines 647-651): "Legolas defeats the Flying Nazgûl immediately, before any cards are played."
Legolas has an instant-defeat ability against the Flying Nazgul.

**Gimli:**
Summary: "Gimli (3) — No special ability."
Source (lines 652-656): "Gimli defeats the Orcs immediately, before any cards are played."
Gimli has an instant-defeat ability against the Orcs.

**Aragorn:**
Summary: "Aragorn (4) — No special ability."
Source (lines 635-646): "Aragorn can move into any adjacent region – forwards, sideways, or backwards – if he attacks at least one enemy character by doing so."
Aragorn has a significant flanking movement ability.

### Finding 3 — CAVE TROLL ABILITY STRIPPED

Summary: "Cave Troll (1) — No special ability."
Source (lines 758-763): "When it comes to playing cards in a battle with the Cave Troll, the Sauron player's card is ignored."
Cave Troll has a card-negation ability.

### Finding 4 — GANDALF ABILITY MISIDENTIFIED

Summary: "Gandalf (5) — Defeated after fighting; removed from game regardless."
Source (lines 621-634): "In a battle against Gandalf, should the battle come to playing cards, the Sauron player must choose and play his card first."
The classic Gandalf's text ability is forcing the Sauron player to reveal their card first — there is no "defeated after fighting; removed from game regardless" text for classic Gandalf. (The Balrog shares a mutual "removed" pattern, but not Gandalf classic.)

### Finding 5 — FLYING NAZGUL ABILITY WRONG

Summary: "Flying Nazgul (3) — May move to any region."
Source (lines 695-703): "The Flying Nazgûl can move to any region on the board, as long as that region is occupied by a single Fellowship character."
The critical restriction ("occupied by a single Fellowship character") is omitted.

### Finding 6 — SETUP PLACEMENT WRONG

Summary: "Fellowship player places all 9 characters in the four Shire regions (any distribution). Sauron player places all 9 characters in the three Mordor regions and Minas Morgul."

Source (lines 155-167): "The Fellowship player chooses four of his characters and places them in the Shire. He then places his remaining five characters in the five regions in front of the Shire (Arthedain, Cardolan, Enedwaith, Eregion, and Rhudaur)... The Sauron player proceeds likewise: He chooses and places four of his characters in Mordor, then places his remaining five characters in the five regions in front of Mordor."

Neither player places all characters in their home territory — each places only 4 in the home zone and 5 in the adjacent forward regions. The summary's setup would place all pieces in the wrong positions.

### Supporting procedural checks (5 verified):
1. Characters move forward one region per turn: source lines 176-180 confirmed.
2. Moving into an enemy-occupied region triggers battle: source lines 240-245 confirmed.
3. Battle: reveal, resolve abilities (Fellowship first), play cards, compare strengths; lower total defeated; tie = both defeated: source lines 253-334 confirmed.
4. Victory conditions: Frodo enters Mordor = Fellowship wins; Frodo killed OR 3 Sauron chars in Shire = Sauron wins: source lines 361-367 confirmed (summary matches these specific conditions).
5. Maximum 4 characters in Shire/Mordor, 1 in mountain regions, 2 in other regions: source lines 186-192 confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 18, SUPPORTED: 13 (turn order, four Fellowship abilities wrong, Gandalf misidentified, Flying Nazgul restriction missing, setup placement wrong)
