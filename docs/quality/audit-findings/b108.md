## godzilla-game

### Verdict: MAJOR

Multiple serious errors in Scoring/Victory Conditions and game-end trigger.

---

### Finding 1 — Game-end condition is wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "The game ends when the Oxygen Destroyer marker reaches the end of the Damage Track."

**Source (lines 402–406):** "If the Oxygen Destroyer marker and the Small Building tokens have passed each other on the Damage Track, the game ends. If the Oxygen Destroyer marker has not passed—or is only ADJACENT to—a Small Building token, a new Round begins."

The game does not end when the OD marker reaches the end of the track. It ends when the OD marker and Small Building tokens (placed when buildings are destroyed) pass each other. Small Buildings fill the Damage Track from the Start end; the OD marker moves from Start toward End. The game ends mid-track when they cross. A player reading the summary would not understand the actual end-game trigger at all.

---

### Finding 2 — Scoring mechanism hidden; Trophy pile mechanic omitted (MAJOR)

**Summary (Scoring section):** "Each Kaiju card has a Dominance Value -- this contributes to determining the most dominant Kaiju. The Kaiju with the most accumulated dominance at game end wins the King of Monsters crown."

**Source (lines 389–391, 272–282):** "If the game ends, each player counts the total Dominance Value on the cards in their Trophy pile. The player who holds the King of Monsters token gains 2 extra [Dominance]. The player with the highest total Dominance Value wins the game." And: "you win the chance to take a Trophy! … Take the card with the highest Dominance Value and place it face down in your Trophy pile."

Dominance is scored only from the Trophy pile — cards stripped from opponents' decks through combat. The summary implies Dominance accumulates from playing your own cards ("each Kaiju card has a Dominance Value"), which is false. Players do not score their own deck's Dominance; they score only cards captured as Trophies. This fundamentally misrepresents how victory is achieved.

---

### Finding 3 — King of Monsters +2 Dominance bonus omitted (MAJOR)

**Summary:** No mention of the King of Monsters token granting bonus Dominance at game end.

**Source (lines 389–390):** "The player who holds the King of Monsters token gains 2 extra [Dominance at game end]."

This is a scoring bonus that directly affects who wins the game. Its omission from the Scoring section is a material error.

---

### Finding 4 — Refresh Phase: King of Monsters token holder draws 6 cards, not 5 (MAJOR)

**Summary (Refresh Phase):** "Draw cards until you have 5 in hand." Player Reference also states "Hand size: 5 cards (refilled during Refresh Phase)."

**Source (lines 347–350):** "Players may choose to discard or keep any number of cards remaining in their hands, before drawing up to a hand of five cards. The player with the King of Monsters token draws up to a hand of six cards."

The King of Monsters token holder has a hand size of 6, not 5. This is incorrectly stated as universal 5.

---

### Finding 5 — King of Monsters token transfer rule omitted

**Summary:** No mention of how the King of Monsters token changes hands during play.

**Source (lines 325–326):** "Whenever you damage the current King of Monsters, whether or not you gain a Trophy, you always take the King of Monsters token."

This is a core game rule — it means dealing any net damage to the token holder transfers the token. Given the token awards +2 Dominance at game end and a 6-card hand, this is a significant strategic and scoring rule absent from the summary.

---

### Procedural verifications:

- Turn phases (OD Phase, Action Phase, Refresh Phase, Event Phase): sequence confirmed correct.
- Action Phase pass/extend mechanic: confirmed correct.
- Starting Energy of 2: confirmed.
- Starting hand of 5: confirmed (applies to non-KOM holder).
- Kaiju card symbols (MOVE, ATTACK, DEFEND, MOMENTUM, RANGE, ENHANCEMENT): all confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8, SUPPORTED: 5 (3 wrong/missing as detailed above)

## gold-mine

### Verdict: MAJOR

Bat Challenge outcome is invented; the actual mechanic is replaced with a fabricated "move to Mine Entrance" rule.

---

### Finding 1 — Bat Challenge outcome is fabricated (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Bat Challenge: … If Challenger wins, Defender must move to the Mine Entrance tile. If Defender wins, Challenger must move to the Mine Entrance tile."

**Source (lines 396–399):** "The winner of the Challenge rolls one die and moves the loser's Miner up to the number rolled in any direction, following normal movement rules."

No source text anywhere supports moving to the Mine Entrance tile as a Bat Challenge outcome. The actual rule is: the winner rolls a die and moves the loser up to that many spaces in any direction. The summary's invented "teleport to Mine Entrance" rule would dramatically change game play — forcing a miner across the board rather than a short local move. No support found (searched: "Mine Entrance", "Bat Challenge", "winner", "loser", "move", "entrance").

---

### Procedural verifications:

**Victory condition:**
- Summary: "Collect the required number of Gold Nuggets and then exit the mine by moving to the Mine Entrance tile."
- Source (lines 413–416): "A Miner exits the mine by reaching the ladder on the Mine Entrance tile. The first player to exit the mine with at least the required number of Gold Nuggets wins the game!" — MATCHES.

**Gold nugget requirements:**
- Summary: 2 players = 10, 3–4 = 7, 5–6 = 5.
- Source (lines 77–80): "2 Players: 10 Gold Nuggets. 3 to 4 Players: 7 Gold Nuggets. 5 to 6 Players: 5 Gold Nuggets." — MATCHES.

**Gold Challenge outcome:**
- Summary: "Winner takes 1 Gold Nugget from the loser and places it on any unoccupied Gold Chamber tile not occupied by either participant."
- Source (lines 278–280): "The winner of the Challenge may take one Gold Nugget from the loser and place it on any Gold Chamber tile that does not contain a Gold Nugget and is not currently occupied by the participants." — MATCHES.

**Gold Challenge bonus move:**
- Summary: "If Challenger wins, they also get a bonus move (roll 1 die, move that far)."
- Source (lines 283–285): "If the winner is the Challenger, he gains a head start by rolling a single die once more and moving up to the number rolled." — Summary says "move that far" (exact number) vs source "up to the number rolled" — minor imprecision, not MAJOR.

**Secret Passage:**
- Summary: "Roll both dice: doubles or sum of 7 = teleport to any other Secret Passage entrance; otherwise move up to the lower die number."
- Source (lines 200–207): "If two of any number (doubles) or a sum of seven is rolled, the water level is low enough and the Miner may move to any other existing Secret Passage entrance. Otherwise, the Miner may choose to move up to the lower number rolled." — MATCHES.

**Challenge resolution (best of 3):**
- Summary: "Challenger and Defender each roll a die 3 times; Winner is whoever wins at least 2 of 3 rolls (ties favor Challenger)."
- Source (lines 265–268): "The Challenger and Defender each retrieve a die and roll three times in a row. Whichever player rolls a higher number for at least two of the dice rolls wins the Challenge—ties go to the Challenger." — MATCHES.

**Gold Challenge location requirement:**
- Summary (Special Rules): "Gold Challenge token requirement: must have at least 1 Gold Nugget AND 1 Gold Challenge token."
- Source (lines 374–381): "Gold Challenges can only occur when the Challenger and the Defender currently occupy the same path on the same tile. Also, a Gold Challenge may only be issued if a Miner has at least one Gold Nugget in his possession." — The summary omits the "same tile and same path" spatial requirement but mentions it in the Challenge table ("Initiated From: Same tile and path as Defender"). Confirmed via table in summary.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7, SUPPORTED: 7 (Bat Challenge outcome is the fabricated error)
