## game-of-thrones-the-card-game — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Three serious procedural errors: (1) initiative winner automatically becomes first player per the summary, but source says they *choose* who is first player; (2) initiative tie-breaking is wrong (summary: current first player decides; source: lowest power total wins); (3) taxation phase description conflates reserve value (which governs hand size) with gold return, omitting the hand-discard step entirely. Plus one unsupported claim about plot deck recycling.

**Prefilter note:** The prefilter emitted `SOURCE_SUSPECT` because "thrones" was absent from the first 3000 chars of the extracted file. Reading confirmed the source is the legitimate Rules Reference for *A Game of Thrones: The Card Game (Second Edition)* — the opening pages are the Golden Rules and glossary intro without the game title in that span. Source is valid; audit proceeded.

---

### Finding 1 — MAJOR: Initiative winner automatically becomes first player (wrong)

**Summary (Plot Phase):** "The player with the highest initiative value on their revealed plot becomes the new first player (ties: current first player decides)."

**Source (line 2020–2022):** "II. Choose first player. The player who won initiative in step I **chooses a player to be the first player**. The chosen player takes the first player token and keeps it until another card or game effect … selects a new first player."

**Contradiction:** The initiative winner does not become first player — they *choose* who is first player. This is a meaningful difference in a multiplayer game (the winner can keep someone else as first player or take it themselves). The summary states it as automatic.

---

### Finding 2 — MAJOR: Initiative tie-breaking rule wrong

**Summary (Plot Phase):** "(ties: current first player decides)"

**Source (lines 2015–2019):** "If multiple players have the same initiative value, the tied player with the **lowest power total** wins initiative. If the players' initiative and power totals are both tied, the tie is broken by **randomly selecting** one of the tied players."

**Contradiction:** Ties are broken first by lowest power total, then randomly — the current first player has no special role in breaking initiative ties. A table following the summary would play this incorrectly every time there is an initiative tie.

---

### Finding 3 — MAJOR: Taxation phase conflates reserve value with gold return; omits hand-discard step

**Summary (Taxation Phase):** "Each player returns unspent gold (exceeding their reserve value on plot card) to the treasury. Discard revealed plot cards to the used pile."

**Source (lines 2293–2302, Appendix I §7):**
- Step 7.2: "Each player simultaneously returns **all** unspent gold in his or her gold pool to the treasury." (No reservation — all gold goes back.)
- Step 7.3: "Each player, in player order, checks his or her reserve. Any player with **more cards in hand** than the reserve value on his or her revealed plot card … chooses and discards cards from his or her hand until that player's hand size and reserve value are equal."

**Contradiction:** The summary describes reserve value as controlling how much gold is kept (wrong), and omits the mandatory hand-discard step entirely. Reserve value governs hand size, not gold retention. A player following the summary would never discard excess hand cards and would misunderstand the economy — both are table-breaking errors.

Additionally, the summary states plot cards are "discard[ed] to the used pile" at taxation. The source shows plots enter the used pile when a new plot is *revealed* on top of the previous one (not at taxation). This is a secondary procedural error but overshadowed by the above.

---

### Finding 4 — MINOR: Plot deck recycling described as shuffle; source says "returned"

**Summary (Special Rules):** "After all 7 are used, shuffle all used plots back into the plot deck."

**Source (lines 2039–2042):** "If a player has no cards in his or her plot deck, that player's used pile now **returns** to his or her plot deck." No shuffle is mentioned anywhere in the source for plot deck recycling. The absence of a shuffle could be strategically meaningful (players know the order plots return in).

No support found for shuffling (searched: "shuffle.*used pile", "shuffle.*plot deck", "plot deck.*shuffle").

### Exhaustion / reshuffle

Summary makes no claim about deck exhaustion or reshuffling mid-game (cards are simply set aside face-up in a pile). Source confirms: "all cards played are left face up in a pile in the middle of the table" (l.204–206). No invented reshuffle rule.

---

### Minor omission (not affecting verdict)

The summary omits the 3-player edge case: "if none of the players holds the multi-colored 1, the player to the right of the dealer plays first" (source l.39–41). This applies only to 3-player games where the multi-colored 1 ends up in the dummy hand. Omission noted but does not affect typical play or scoring.
