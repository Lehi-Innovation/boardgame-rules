## blood-bowl — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — One contradicted core number: the summary requires a 4+ on the Going For It roll, while the source fails GFI only on a roll of 1 (success on 2+). GFI is one of the most frequent risk decisions in Blood Bowl, and the summary triples its failure rate (50% vs ~17%). All other checked numbers are supported; remaining issues are minor omissions/imprecisions.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Going For It threshold contradicted (wrong number).**
   - Summary (Special Rules & Edge Cases): "After using full MA, a player may attempt to move 1-2 extra squares by rolling **4+ on D6** each. Failing = Knocked Down + turnover."
   - Source: "Roll a D6 for the player after they have moved each extra square. **On a roll of 1 the player trips up** and is Knocked Down in the square that they moved to... On any other roll the player moves without mishap." (l.1557-1560)
   - Impact: under the summary a GFI fails half the time instead of 1-in-6. Players would avoid GFI almost entirely (or be wrongly punished). Verified no base-rule 4+ variant exists in the source: the only altered GFI thresholds are special-card/weather effects (e.g., "roll a 5+ to Go For It" is a Dirty Tricks card, l.6052; slippery-pitch weather knocks down on a GFI of 1, l.1344). The "roll 4+" in the source applies to standing up with fewer than 3 MA (l.573) and KO'd recovery (l.599, 875) — both of which the summary already states separately, suggesting a confusion during summarization.

2. **MINOR — Foul action incomplete.**
   - Summary: "Roll 2D6 vs. Armour (with +1 per assist). If doubles are rolled, the fouling player is ejected by the referee."
   - Source: offensive assists add +1 each, but **defensive assists subtract 1 each** ("Each defensive assist modifies the Armour roll by -1 per assist", l.1696-1698), and ejection occurs on doubles on the **Armour and/or Injury roll** (l.1727-1729), not only the Armour roll. The summary's version makes fouls look safer (only one doubles chance) and stronger (no defensive assists).

3. **MINOR — Restart after a touchdown misstated.**
   - Summary: "Both teams set up again (kicking/receiving swap)."
   - Source: "When play is restarted after a touchdown, **the scoring team is always the one to kick off**" (l.880-881). These differ when the kicking team scores (e.g., a defensive touchdown): the source has them kick again; the summary swaps roles. Low frequency but a real difference.

4. **MINOR — Touchdown condition imprecise; opponent-turn scoring omitted.**
   - Summary: "When a player carrying the ball enters the opposing team's End Zone, a touchdown is scored."
   - Source: the player must be **standing** in the End Zone holding the ball **at the end of an Action** — "if the player failed to make a Dodge roll... and thus was Knocked Down in the End Zone then he would not score" (l.918-934). The source also allows scoring in the opponent's turn (e.g., pushed into the End Zone holding the ball), costing an extra turn-marker space (l.862-870). Both nuances are absent.

5. **MINOR — Catching mechanic omitted.**
   - The summary never explains the catch roll (AG roll; +1 accurate pass, +0 scattered pass/kick-off/bounce/throw-in, -1 per opposing tackle zone — l.748-752), even though every completed pass and hand-off depends on it and the turnover list references uncaught balls. Bouncing balls and 2D6-square throw-ins (l.725-736) are also omitted. Interceptions (-2 modifier) and fumbles (pass roll of 1 or less after modification, l.1589-1599) get only a one-word mention in the turnover list.

6. **MINOR — Re-roll limits omitted.**
   - Source: "A coach may not use more than one Re-roll counter per turn" (l.793-794) and team re-rolls are restored at half time (l.799-801). The summary's re-roll section states neither; players could chain team re-rolls in one turn.

7. **Trivial — Tie/overtime rules omitted.** Source: tied match is a draw, with optional sudden-death overtime (coin flip, 8 turns each, carried-over re-rolls, penalty shoot-out tiebreak: D6 + 1 per unused re-roll, l.904-917). Summary stops at "most touchdowns wins."

8. **Trivial — "Special Block dice (with 6 results)".** The die has 6 faces but 5 distinct results (Pushed appears twice). The source has no components list at all (it is a fan-compiled rules text); the summary's component list is reconstructed but every item is mentioned in the rules text (Dugout boxes l.539, 593, 603; eight-sided dice l.161; Scatter template l.160; range ruler l.640; counters l.192-197).

9. **Trivial — Overview phrasing.** "two halves of 8 turns each (16 turns per half total)" is internally confusing but the numbers reconcile with the source ("two halves of sixteen turns each (i.e., eight turns per coach)", l.284, 894).

### OCR note (not counted as errors)

The source's printed Dodging and Pick-up modifier tables read "+1" per opposing tackle zone (l.348-350, 389-392, 480-483) — the minus signs were lost in extraction. The worked example confirms the negative sign ("has to subtract 2 because there are two Orc tackle zones on the square he is moving to", l.463-465), as do the parallel Passing/Catching/Hand-off tables where "-1" survived (l.717, 752, 1548). The summary's "-1 per opposing tackle zone" is correct and was not flagged.
