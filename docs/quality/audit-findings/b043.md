# Audit Findings — Batch b043

Method: compared `rules/<slug>.md` against `extracted/<slug>-rules.txt`. Spot-checked numbers (components, costs, VP, thresholds), checked Turn Structure/Actions for invented mechanics, contradictions, and major omissions. OCR/column-merge artifacts were taken into account.

## broom-service — MINOR

**Numbers checked (15+, all supported unless noted):** 7 rounds; 4 of 10 role cards; 4–10 turns/round; 24 heavy clouds; 24 wands; 60 potions; 10 pawns; 5 VP markers; 15 amulets; 17 landscape tiles (5 mountain/6 forest/6 hill); 8 storm clouds; VP marker starts on 10; wands 1/1/2 (2p: 1 each); event deck 7 of 10, 3 boxed; bewitched reveal 3/2/1 for 2/3/4 players; gatherer brave 3 / cowardly 1; druid brave +3 VP; weather fairy brave +3 VP; bewitched −3 VP; end-game 4 VP per set of 4 resources, 2 VP per set of 3; amulets 4/9/15; tiebreaker most resources. All match the source.

**Findings:**

1. (Minor, components double-count) Summary: "60 playing cards (5 sets of 10 role cards)" *plus* a separate line "10 event cards". Source: "60 playing cards: - 5 x 10 role cards - 10 event cards" — i.e., the 60 total *includes* the 10 event cards (50 role + 10 event). The summary implies 70 cards.
2. (Minor, unsupported/contradicted naming) Summary: "Druids (3 types: Forest, Hill, Peak)". The source never enumerates druid types; it mentions a "valley druid" (example, p. with role play) and a "peak druid" (hill tile rules). "Forest" and "Hill" druids are unsupported, and "valley" contradicts the summary's list. Witch types (Forest, Hill, Mountain) are supported.

Everything else (brave/cowardly mechanic, duty to follow, starting-player cascade, tower/cloud/delivery rules, square vs circular towers, events, variations) is faithful.

## bruges — MAJOR

**Numbers checked:** 165 cards ✓; 50 workers ✓; 20 one-guilder + 24 three-guilder ✓; 45 threat markers ✓; 40 canal tokens ✓; 4+4 emblems ✓; 8 pawns ✓; 12 majority markers ✓; 6 statues (2–7) ✓; 4 fifty/100 tokens ✓; 9 overview cards ✓; 5 dice ✓; pawn on space 5 ✓; 5 starting guilders ✓; hand of 5, play 4 cards ✓; dice 5–6 = threats, 1–2 = reputation cost, 3–4 no effect ✓; 3rd threat marker triggers penalty ✓.

**Findings:**

1. (MAJOR, wrong threat-penalty table) Summary table: Blue = lose 3 VP; Brown = discard 1 canal token; Red = discard 1 person; Purple = lose 3 guilders; Yellow = discard 1 worker. Source (last page): Flood = "return all of your workers"; Plague = "discard one person of your choice"; Raid = "return all of your guilders"; Fire = "discard one of your built houses, or discard one of your built Canal tokens"; Intrigue = "lose 3 points". The source's example explicitly assigns **red = fire** (house OR canal) and **yellow = raid** (ALL guilders), directly contradicting the summary's red and yellow rows. "Lose 3 guilders" and "discard 1 worker" penalties do not exist anywhere in the source (the worker/guilder penalties are *all* workers / *all* guilders). Essentially every row of this table is wrong.
2. (MAJOR, wrong action reward) Summary Action III: "Discard 1 Threat marker... Gain 1 guilder each time you discard a threat" (repeated in Player Reference). Source: "Each Threat marker discard this way gets the player **1 point**; advance your pawn 1 space on the scoring track." Point, not guilder.
3. (MAJOR, wrong canal cost + invented immediate scoring) Summary Action IV: "Pay guilders equal to the matching-color die value (1-6). Place 1 canal token in the next empty canal space. Advance your scoring pawn according to the VP shown on that canal space." Source: "play 1 card matching the color of the **canal space** you wish to build on... pay the number of guilders **shown on that space**" — cost is printed on the space, not the die. Canal VP are not scored immediately: "If a player reaches space 3 of a section, that player scores 3 points **at game end**"; completing a section grants the topmost Statue (scored at game end, max 2 statues) — the statue-on-completion rule is omitted.
4. (MAJOR, contradicted rule) Summary Action VI / Special Rules: person "placed face-up in a house of **matching color**". Source: "The person may be of a **different color** than the house."
5. (MAJOR, missing end-game scoring) Source final scoring includes: points per person in play area (printed value = price/3), **1 point per house**, 3 points per built 3rd canal space, statue points, 4 points per flipped majority marker, reputation step points. Summary's End-Game Scoring omits house points, person base points, and 3rd-canal-space points entirely, and says majority bonus is "VP per the number shown on the Majority marker" where the source states a flat **4 points** each.
6. (Minor omission) Once flipped, majority markers stay flipped for the game and multiple players can end up scoring the same category (source Phase 4); summary omits this.

Setup, phases 1–2, actions I/II/V, draw-pile/extra-pile end trigger, and tiebreaker (most guilders) are accurate.

## bruxelles-1893 — MAJOR

**Numbers checked:** 26 Public Figure cards ✓; 25 Bonus cards ✓; 12 Stock Exchange cards ✓; 76 one-BF + 12 five-BF ✓; 6 compass tiles + 2 needles ✓; 30 buildings (6/color) ✓; 20 discs (4/color) ✓; 35 assistants (7/color) ✓; 30 noble cubes (10/10/10) + 15 jokers ✓; 5 exhibition tiles (1893–1910) ✓; setup 5 assistants + 2 in Courthouse ✓; start money 5/6/7/8/9 BF ✓; 5 rounds ✓; theater costs free/1/2/3 BF ✓. ("30 Works of Art tiles (5 colors + black)" — source says five colors *including* black: blue, pink, yellow, green, black; minor.)

**Findings:**

1. (MAJOR, invented Brussels-board actions) Summary lists Brussels actions as "City Hall: Advance your disc...", "Royal Palace: Advance your disc...", "Stock Exchange", "Free Action". Source: the 4 Brussels actions are **Market** (gain 3 Joker cubes), **Stock Exchange** (money = value on current Stock Exchange card), **Fiftieth Anniversary Park** (use any Art Nouveau action), and **Grand Plaza** (activate Public Figure cards up to your Royal Palace influence). There is no action that advances the City Hall or Royal Palace discs (those advance via Bonus cards / Public Figures). Three of four real actions are missing; two are invented.
2. (MAJOR, omitted escalating cost) Source: each subsequent use of the same Brussels action in a round costs one more Assistant (1, then 2, then 3...; available twice at 1 assistant in 4–5p games). Summary says Brussels actions cost "1 Assistant (free)" flatly.
3. (MAJOR, invented resolution step) Summary Resolution phase item 3: "Compass: The first player resolves the compass, potentially adjusting City Hall or Royal Palace positions." No such step exists; the compass is only used during the Construction action (needle turned by the builder, defines construction-cost types).
4. (MAJOR, wrong first-player rule) Summary End of Turn: "Pass Manneken Pis clockwise." Source Resolution step 2: the new first player is whoever earned the most **Manneken Pis symbols** this round (on Bonus cards/Exhibition tile), ties broken clockwise from current first player.
5. (MAJOR, contradicted auction rules) Summary: "Tied bidders: closest to first player wins. Losers get their money back." Source: on a tie, **each tied player gets the card's central power but the card is discarded**; and all bid money "goes back into the general supply" in the End of Turn phase — losers do not get money back.
6. (MAJOR, missing resolution scoring) Source Resolution steps 3–4 omitted: Bonus card choice (use central power vs. tuck under board row as an endgame multiplier: rows score per 4 BF / per Work of Art / per Public Figure / per Assistant beyond 2) and **Shield majorities** (each shield surrounded by 4 Assistants scores the majority holder VP equal to City Hall influence). Both are core scoring systems.
7. (MAJOR, invented scoring line) Summary Scoring lists "Canal-related scoring". There are no canals in Bruxelles 1893 (likely bleed-over from Bruges). Also "City Hall track position" / "Royal Palace track position" as end-game VP is unsupported. Actual end-game scoring (pay kept Public Figures or lose card + 5 VP; buildings × Architect-track level; tucked Bonus cards; 1 VP per noble material; 5 VP for holding Manneken Pis) is largely missing.
8. (Minor) Construction described as building "on your Architect board"; source: the tile moves from the personal board **onto an Art Nouveau action space**, where it grants the owner secondary actions when opponents use that space (the entire secondary-action system, Appendix 2, is omitted). "Earn VP and advance your Architect track" — source grants 5 VP only if no Joker cubes were used; no Architect-track advance from building.
9. (Minor omission) Pass mechanism: first passer takes the Exhibition tile + 1 BF + 1 BF per Work-of-Art color (max 6); later passers get 1 BF per color (max 5). Omitted, though Workshop correctly references owned Exhibition tiles.

## buck-rogers-battle-for-the-25th-century — MINOR

**Numbers checked (all supported unless noted):** starting units 18 = 1 Leader + 8 Troopers + 1 Transport + 2 Factories + 2 Gennies + 4 Fighters; 6 Territory cards (9/3p, 12/2p); 3 units per placement turn, 6 turns, max 6/zone in setup; victory 15 zones (20/3p, 25/2p); Leader +2; Battler bombard 7+; sabotage 7+; commando 6+; build rates (2 Troopers, 2 Gennies, 1 Fighter, 1 Transport in 1 turn; Battler/Factory/KilSat in 2 turns); movement allowances (Leader 4 any, Trooper/Gennie 1, Transport/Fighter 4, Battler 4 space/orbit); Transport carries 5 Troopers or 1 Factory; defeated-Leader captivity 2 turns; 54 cards (6 Leader, 6 Turn Order, 42 Territory); 45 white + 5 red chips; 5 d10.

**Findings:**

1. (Moderate, contradicted rule) Summary 1-turn builds include "1 Trooper + 1 Gennie". The source's build table (column-merged OCR) reads: "Each Factory can produce 1 of the following in a turn: 2 Troopers / 2 Gennies / 1 Transport / 1 Fighter — **A Factory may not produce 1 Trooper and 1 Gennie.**" The summary presents the explicitly forbidden combination as legal. (Player Reference table avoids the error.)
2. (Minor, OCR-artifact carried over) "356 miniatures per color" / "199 counters per color": the source table header merges "356 Minis | per color" — 356 is the total across colors (per-color values like "120 Troopers → 20" appear adjacent in the OCR). Per-color phrasing is implausible (would be 2,136 minis) but matches the garbled extraction; components-only impact.
3. (Minor omission) "Each unit attacks once per round" drops the source's "each unit **except Leaders** attack once" — Leaders provide the +2 but do not attack in the basic game.

Setup, all 4 phases, combat order of battles (a–e), pass-through fire framing, mutually-checked victory conditions, factory restrictions, Black Market, KilSat rules, advanced Leader abilities (Buck 4+, Wilma immunity, Huer +2 move, Kane free strike, Ardala steal 2 / control Leader, Barney capture), retreat, and commando ops are faithful.

## buffy-the-vampire-slayer-collectible-card-game — MINOR

**Numbers checked (all supported):** 7 challenge cards; 0–8 location cards, 4 playable at setup; hand size 5; refill to 5; fight draw 5/5; challenge draw 5 attacker / 3 defender; discard to 5 in Fight/Challenge Discard; 10 DP Destiny Victory; Park Victory 6 consecutive turns (reset to 0 on shared/empty park); win a fight +1 DP; Main Character defeated −1 DP (incl. on ties); vampires 0 moves day / 2 per night, non-vampires 1 per turn; 1 character move per watch; max 2 items; 1 skill attach per character per turn; 1 item swap per watch, each item once per turn; DP floor 0; Villains go first.

**Findings (omissions; nothing invented or contradicted found):**

1. (Moderate omission, scoring) Failed challenge penalty omitted: source "Losing the challenge... The attacking player **loses 1 Destiny Point**, and the challenge stays in play." The summary's Challenge/Resolve and DP table only cover success.
2. (Moderate omission) Fatigue talent penalty omitted: "When a character is fatigued he gets a **-1 to all his talents**" (active in fights, challenges, and talent requirements; values can go below 0). Matters whenever a fatigued character defends.
3. (Minor omission) Fatigued characters also cannot **move** or Stunt-Double (summary lists only no-start-fight/no-challenge/no-skill-attach).
4. (Minor omission) Challenge alignment restriction: Heroes/Companions may only attempt good (gold) challenges; Villains/Minions only evil (rusty red) ones.
5. (Minor omission) Deck/in-play limits (40/60-card minimums, 3 duplicates, supporting Hero/Villain limit of 4, 50% caps) not summarized — arguably out of a play-summary's scope.

Setup steps 1–9, the 7-step turn sequence with watches/mandatory sub-steps, fight stages A–E (stunt-double, talent stacks, action-icon vs +1 boosts, default Butt-Kicking, tie handling, attacker fatigue), challenge stages A–E (defender boosts only printed talents), ascension (Destiny Requirement, once/turn, no skipping, no level loss), resurrection, Episode Part I/II framing, Golden Rule, Crypt vs discard, recycling, duplicate-challenge and one-event-per-game rules all match the source.
