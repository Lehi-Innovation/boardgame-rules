### Victory / Game-End Conditions

Summary: "End of Game Trigger: If 6 or fewer fresco tiles remain on the board at the beginning of a round, that round is the final round. Players flip their action sheets to side 2 (theater is removed; cathedral can be visited twice). If the fresco is completely restored during any round, that is also the final round."

Source line 515-517: "If there are 6 or less fresco tiles remaining on the board at the beginning of a round this is the final round of the game." — CONFIRMED.
Source lines 553-560: "In the final round the 'theater' can not be visited any more, instead the 'cathedral' may be visited twice." — CONFIRMED.
Source lines 530-533: "In case the fresco is completely restored during a round without the final round being triggered, that is the final round and followed immediately by final scoring." — CONFIRMED.

Summary: "Final Scoring: No income is paid in the final round. Each player gains 1 VP per 2 Thalers remaining, awarded in wake-up time order. The player with the most VP wins."
Source lines 521-529: "No income is paid at the end of the final round. Final scoring: Now all players are awarded victory points for their money. Every 2 Thalers are worth 1 victory point. The player with the highest victory point total is the master of all fresco painters and winner of the game." — CONFIRMED.

### "Tiebreaker" Section

Summary: "Tiebreaker: If master painters land on the same VP space during final scoring, the arriving painter moves forward to the next free space."
Source lines 534-537: "If a master painter is landing on an occupied space of the victory point track during final scoring, the figure is moved forward to the next free space." — CONFIRMED for final scoring.

Note: The summary labels this a "tiebreaker" which is slightly inaccurate — it is a VP-track collision rule, not a tie-breaking mechanism for equal final VP totals. The source never specifies a tiebreaker for equal total VP. This is a minor labeling imprecision that does not mislead gameplay.

Summary: Two phases per round: Phase 1 (get-up time / mood), Phase 2 (planning and performing actions). Then round preparation.
Source line 215-219: "Fresco lasts for several rounds; each round is split into 2 phases: 1. Choosing get up time and adjusting mood. 2. Planning and performing actions." — CONFIRMED.

## friedrich

### Verdict: PASS

### Methodology
Source: ~1089-line extracted text. Read fully (~47KB). Valid rulebook for Friedrich (Anniversary Edition v2.1).

### Victory / Game-End Conditions

Summary: "Attacker Victory: An attacking nation wins if it controls all its objective cities (1st order objectives only if eased victory conditions apply) before the Card of Fate is picked at the end of a turn, or after the Card of Fate resolves (if eased conditions were triggered). The controlling player wins; all others lose."

Source lines 641-649: "If an attacking nation controls all her objectives before the Card of Fate is picked, this nation has won. Do not pick the card. If a nation controls all necessary objectives after the pick (due to eased victory conditions) the nation has won, too. In both cases the game ends with the victory of the player currently playing this nation. If two or more nations fulfill their victory conditions simultaneously both will win. All other players – allies and opponents – have lost." — CONFIRMED.

Summary: "Prussian Victory: If Russia, Sweden, and France have all quit the game due to Cards of Fate, Prussia/Hanover (player Frederick) wins."
Source lines 650-652: "Prussian victory. If Russia, Sweden and France have quit the game due to the Cards of Fate, then the game ends with the victory of Prussia/Hanover (player Frederick)." — CONFIRMED.

Also confirmed from intro (lines 91-95): "The player Frederick wins if no attacking nation has won before the end of the game. The game ends as soon as 3 attacking nations have been forced out of the game by historical events." — CONFIRMED.

### TC Draw Numbers

Summary: "Prussia: 7 TCs (reduced to 4 by certain Cards of Fate). Hanover: 2 TCs (reduced to 1). Austria: 5 TCs (reduced to 4). France: 4 TCs, but must immediately discard 1 (reduced to 3, keeping all)."
Source lines 226-232: "a) Basically Prussia receives 7 TCs per turn, Hanover 2 and Austria 5. Due to certain Cards of Fate, these values can be reduced to 4, 1, and 4, respectively. b) Basically France receives 4 TCs, but has to choose one of these immediately. The chosen TC is discarded facedown... There is a Card of Fate that reduces the French draw to 3, but France can keep all of them." — CONFIRMED.

Note: The summary says France "must immediately discard 1 (reduced to 3, keeping all)" — this is slightly ambiguous but matches the source intent (when reduced by Card of Fate to drawing only 3, France keeps all 3).

### Movement Rates

Summary: "Generals: Up to 3 cities. If the entire move is along main roads, up to 4 cities. Supply trains: Up to 2 cities (3 on main roads)."
Source lines 258-261: "A general may move up to 3 cities, even back and forth. If the entire move is along a main road, the general may move an additional city, up to a total of 4. A supply train moves like a general, but one less city, i.e. 2 cities (and 3 on main roads)." — CONFIRMED.

### Combat

Summary: "The inferior player may play one TC, adding its value to the score. If the score becomes zero or positive, the right to play switches to the other player."
Source lines 405-415: "the inferior player has the right to play a single TC of his suit. He adds the TC value to the initial score... As long as a player has a negative current score, he has the right to play another TC. If a score becomes zero or positive, the right to play TCs switches to the other player." — CONFIRMED.

Summary: "Defeat: The defeated general loses troops equal to the final negative score (but not more than commanded) and retreats the same number of cities. The winner loses no troops and stays in place."
Source lines 416-419: "The defeated general loses as many troops as the final negative score (but not more than he commanded) and is retreated the same number of cities. The winner loses no troops and remains in place." — CONFIRMED.

Summary: "Tie: If a player receives the right to play on a score of zero, they must play a card of the correct suit if they have one (Reserves are optional). If they have none, combat ends as a tie with no losses."
Source lines 421-424: "If a player receives the right to play a TC on a score of zero, he must play a card if he has any of the correct suit (he is not obligated to play a Reserve). If he has none (and if he is not willing to play an existing Reserve), combat ends as a tie. In a tie, neither side loses troops nor has to retreat." — CONFIRMED.

### Retreat — Winner Chooses

Summary: "The **winning player** chooses the retreat path."
Source line 473: "The winning player chooses the retreat path, according to the following conditions" — CONFIRMED. (This is a notable mechanic; confirmed correct.)

Retreat conditions:
Summary: "Must retreat the full distance, ending as far from the winner as possible. Cannot enter a city twice or pass through any other piece. Cannot conquer objectives during retreat. If unable to retreat the full distance, the general loses all troops and is removed."
Source lines 474-485: "The general must retreat the full distance and has to finish his retreat as far away as possible from the winning general... A retreating general may never enter a city a second time. A retreating general may not enter or move through a city containing any other piece... A general can retreat through an objective city, but cannot (re-)conquer it. If a general cannot retreat the full distance, he loses all troops and is removed from the map." — CONFIRMED.

### Recruitment

Summary: "Each troop: 6 TC points. Each supply train: 6 TC points. Each general: free, but must receive at least 1 new troop (6 points)."
Source lines 539-542: "Each TROOP costs 6 points of TC. Each SUPPLY TRAIN costs 6 points of TC. Each GENERAL is for free, but has to receive at least one new troop (for a price of 6 points of TC)." — CONFIRMED.

Summary: "No nation may exceed its starting troop total."
Source line 551: "No nation may ever have more troops than the number she started with" — CONFIRMED.

### Supply

Source lines 493-504: "Inside his home country a general is always in supply... Summary: "Out of supply: flip general face-down. If still out of supply next supply phase, lose all troops and remove from map. Face-down generals have no special restrictions (can move, fight, conquer, recruit)."

However, if he is still out of supply, he will lose all troops and is put off-map... Face-down generals have no special restrictions on them: they can move, fight, conquer, receive new troops, etc." — CONFIRMED.

### Stack Rules

Summary: "2-3 generals in a stack share troops as a common pool (min 2 or 3, max 16 or 24). Troops can be transferred freely between stacked generals at any time. If a stack loses troops, the lowest-ranked general is removed first."
Source lines 355-370: "The 2 (or 3) generals of a stack must treat the sum of their troops as a common pool. In a stack, removal is executed from bottom to top (that is, the lowest-ranked general is removed first)." — CONFIRMED.

### TC Deck Cycling

Summary: "When the first TC deck is exhausted, use the second. After all four are used, combine the two largest discard piles for the new draw deck."

### Objective Protection Radius

Source line 277-278: "It is protected if a general of the defending nation is positioned 1, 2 or 3 cities away." — CONFIRMED.
