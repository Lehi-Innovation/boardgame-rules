## mall-of-horror

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — 3-player character set (MAJOR)

Summary: "Each player selects a color and receives 3 character tokens (Pin-Up, Tough Guy, Gunman)"

Source (Setup step 1): "Each player chooses a color and gets the matching Pin-Up, Tough Guy and Gunman characters and a Voting Wheel. Players in a **3 player game** also get the **Little Girl** character."

The summary omits the Little Girl entirely from both Setup and Components. The source gives the Little Girl special abilities across multiple phases (kills zombies in Phase F before vote, peeks at dice in Phase C, changes destination in Phase E). A 3-player game is thus played with 4 characters per player under the summary but only 3 under the source's setup—affecting character counts, VP, and game-end thresholds.

### Finding 2 — Initial action cards (MAJOR)

Summary (Setup): "Each player rolls 2 dice to place characters in matching locations (continue until all placed)." — no mention of starting action cards.

Source (Setup): "Each player gets one Action card and the remaining cards are placed face down next to the board."

The summary omits that each player starts with 1 action card already in hand. This is a procedural setup error.

### Finding 3 — Chief of Security dice viewing rule (verified correct)

Summary: "Players with Security Camera cards may peek."
Source: "A Chief of Security may view the dice only if he was elected this round. A player may use a Security Camera card to view the dice."

Summary omits that the *newly elected* Chief may also peek—Security Camera is not the only peek method. Minor omission but the Security Camera statement itself is correct.

### Finding 4 — Choose Destinations: CoS must reveal first (omission)

Summary: "Players secretly select locations for their characters using voting wheels."
Source: "A Chief of Security elected this round must choose his destination first and tell the other players. All other players use their wheels to select a destination."

The summary says all players secretly select simultaneously, but the source requires the newly elected Chief to announce publicly first.

### Finding 5 — Condemned location trigger (verified correct)

Summary: "If a location becomes condemned (8+ zombies), it closes permanently."
Source: "An empty location with 8 or more zombies is condemned."
SUPPORTED — both require 8+.

### Finding 6 — Zombie Attack: extra zombie placement (omission)

Summary: "Zombies attack locations with characters. When zombies equal or exceed character count..."
Source: "Place an extra zombie on the location with the most characters. Place an extra zombie on the location with the most Pin-Ups."

The summary omits the extra-zombie-placement rule at the start of Phase F entirely. This changes how many zombies attack each location.

### Finding 7 — Parking Lot zombie feeding (verified with omission)

Summary: "Every zombie in the Parking Lot feeds automatically. All zombies leave after feeding."
Source: "In the Parking Lot (Location 4) every zombie feeds. A vote is taken for each Zombie, one at a time. All Zombies are removed from the Parking Lot after this phase."

Summary says zombies "feed automatically" but source requires a vote for each zombie one at a time. This is a MAJOR procedural error—the Parking Lot requires repeated voting, not automatic feeding.

### Finding 8 — Game end condition (verified correct)

Summary: "The game ends when all surviving characters occupy the same non-Parking Lot location, or only 4 characters remain total."
Source: "The game ends if all remaining characters are at the same location, except for the Parking Lot (Location 4), or there are only 4 remaining characters (6 in a 6 player games)."
SUPPORTED — the 4-character threshold is correct; the 6-player variant (6 characters) is omitted from summary.

### Finding 9 — Victory points (omission)

Summary: "Each character token has a victory point value."
Source: Pin-Up = VP 7, Tough Guy = VP 5, Gunman = VP 3, Little Girl = VP 1.
The summary's Player Reference table lists "Defense Value" and "Special" columns but no VP values. The actual VP numbers are in the source but absent from the summary's reference table.

### Victory/tiebreak (verified correct)

Summary: "The player with the most victory points from surviving characters wins. Ties broken by unused action cards."
Source: "The winner is the person with the most Victory Points (total the numbers on the surviving character tokens). Ties are broken by the number of unused cards."
SUPPORTED.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5, SUPPORTED: 4

The Parking Lot "automatic feeding" vs. "vote for each zombie one at a time" is a MAJOR table-level error. The omission of the Little Girl in 3-player games is MAJOR (affects setup, VP, character counts).

## mamma-mia

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Ingredient card count and types (MAJOR)

Summary: "72 ingredient cards (in 5 types: pepperoni, mushroom, pineapple, green pepper, olive)"
Source: "65 ingredient cards (13 each of salami, pineapple, mushroom, pepper and olive)"

Two errors:
1. Count: 72 vs. source's 65.
2. Ingredient types: summary says "pepperoni" and "green pepper"; source says "salami" and "pepper". "Pepperoni" does not appear in the source.

### Finding 2 — Setup: Mamma Mia! card (MAJOR procedural omission)

Summary (Setup): "Shuffle all ingredient cards to form a draw deck."
Source: "Now shuffle the **Mamma Mia! card** and the ingredient cards together. Place these cards face down as a supply."

The summary omits the Mamma Mia! card from the supply deck entirely. This card is central to the game—it triggers round end and determines who resolves the oven. Without it in the supply, the round-end mechanism doesn't function.

### Finding 3 — Draw rule: supply only vs. supply or waiter (MAJOR)

Summary (Turn): "Draw: Draw cards from the deck until you have 7 cards. You may also take 1 pizza order card from your remaining orders."

Source: "the player draws enough cards to return his hand size to seven cards. The player must, however, draw cards only from the supply **or from his waiter, and not both during a turn**."

The summary says to draw from "the deck" (implicitly only the supply) but doesn't mention the key restriction that a player chooses between drawing from supply OR waiter but NOT BOTH in the same turn. This is a procedural constraint that affects strategy fundamentally.

### Finding 4 — Round end trigger (MAJOR)

Summary: "When the ingredient deck runs out, the round ends."
Source: "As soon as a player draws the **last card from the supply**, the round ends."

These are the same mechanically, BUT the Mamma Mia! card is shuffled into the supply (omitted in summary's setup). When the Mamma Mia! card is drawn mid-round it does not end the round—only drawing the last card does. The summary's setup error cascades here.

### Finding 5 — Resolution: oven flip direction (verified correct)

Summary: "Turn the entire stack over (bottom becomes top)."
Source: "The Mamma Mia! player takes the cards from the oven and turns them over in his hand so they are face down." (Then turns them over one at a time.)
SUPPORTED — bottom becomes top is implied by reversing the stack.

### Finding 6 — Tiebreaker (MAJOR)

Summary: "Ties are broken by fewest unfilled orders remaining."
Source: "If two or more players tie with the most, the player among them with the **most ingredients left in his hand** is the winner."

The summary inverts the tiebreaker: source uses "most ingredients in hand," summary says "fewest unfilled orders remaining." These are different metrics.

### Finding 7 — Special pizzas (omitted)

Summary does not mention Pizza Bombastica, Pizza Minimale, or Pizza Monotoni.
Source dedicates substantial text to these three special orders each player has (3 of their 8 orders). This is a MINOR omission in isolation, but combined with other errors contributes to an incomplete picture.

### Finding 8 — Unused ingredient handling between rounds (verified correct)

Summary: "After round resolution, all unused ingredients are reshuffled to form a new draw deck."
Source: "The Mamma Mia! player puts all the unused ingredient cards from the oven face up as the new oven stack for the next round." AND "The Mamma Mia! player shuffles the used ingredients cards (that were used to complete properly baked pizzas) together with the Mamma Mia! card to form a new supply."

The summary is WRONG: unused ingredients become the new *oven stack* (not reshuffled into the draw deck); it is the *used* ingredients that are reshuffled (with the Mamma Mia! card) into a new supply. This is a MAJOR process error.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (72 vs 65 ingredient cards, 40 orders, 8 per player, 7-card hand limit), SUPPORTED: 3

The ingredient count (72 vs 65), ingredient name errors (pepperoni/green pepper vs. salami/pepper), tiebreaker reversal (fewest unfilled orders vs. most ingredients in hand), and wrong unused-ingredient reshuffling procedure are all MAJOR errors.

## man-o-war

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn sequence (verified correct)

Summary: "1. Initiative Phase, 2. Magic Phase, 3. Movement Phase, 4. Shooting Phase, 5. Boarding Phase, 6. End Phase"
Source: "1. Initiative phase, 2. Magic phase, 3. Battle phase, 4. End phase"

The source's "Battle phase" is described as: "players take turns moving and fighting with a single MOW, IND or squadron of SOL ships." Each ship "moves, shoots and then performs any boarding action" within the battle phase.

The summary splits Battle into Movement/Shooting/Boarding as separate phases. While the source does these in sequence per ship, they are all within a single Battle phase. This is a structural simplification rather than a hard contradiction—the *ordering* within a ship's activation is correct.

### Finding 2 — Victory conditions (verified correct)

Summary: "Victory conditions vary by scenario but typically involve destroying or capturing a certain number of enemy ships..."
Source: "If all of one side's ships are sunk or captured the game ends; the other player is the winner. Optionally, play to a time limit and count up battle honours: each player totals the number of below the waterline hits for all enemy ships they sunk or captured."

SUPPORTED — the summary accurately describes the optional scenario-based system.

### Finding 3 — Battle honours for admirals and wizards (omission)

Summary does not mention battle honours for admirals/wizards.
Source: "An admiral captured or killed is worth 5 battle honours. Wizards are worth 1 for a wizard champion, 2 for a master wizard and 3 for a wizard lord."

This is a scoring source omission for the optional battle honours game. MINOR since the main victory condition (all enemy ships sunk/captured) is correct.

### Finding 4 — Boarding combat (verified correct)

Summary: "Crew fight in melee — compare crew values and roll dice. The winner captures or damages the enemy ship."
Source: "Both sides roll a dice and add the number of crew counters on their ship. The defender adds the number of remaining cannons he has pointing in the direction of the attacker. A ship with an admiral gets +1 to the boarding action roll. The player with the lowest total loses a crew counter."

The summary oversimplifies—doesn't mention the defender's cannon bonus, the admiral bonus, or that the *loser* (not winner) loses a crew counter (not that the winner "captures or damages"). The source's boarding action is attrition-based (crew counter loss), not a single "capture or damage" result. However, "the winner captures or damages" is not directly contradicted—it's a high-level summary of eventual possible outcomes.

### Finding 5 — Wind change on doubles (verified correct)

Summary: "Wind changes: The wind direction may shift during the game, affecting all ship movement."
Source: "On a double the wind changes one position; clockwise for evens, counter-clockwise for odds."
SUPPORTED.

### Finding 6 — Source is a player aid / rules summary, not full rulebook

The extracted source is from "The Esoteric Order of Gamers" — a third-party player aid. It does not cover faction-specific rules or full fleet-building details. This limits verification depth but the summary's claims match the player aid's content.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (1000 points, 25 point MOW cards, 9" cannon range, 2" turning template, 5 battle honours for admiral, wizard honours 1/2/3), SUPPORTED: 6

No outright contradictions. Oversimplifications in boarding combat and the phase-splitting are MINOR.

## manhattan

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Game length: 4-player vs. 2/3-player (MAJOR)

Summary: "The game lasts 4 rounds." (stated as universal rule)
Source: "Manhattan is played in 4 rounds." (4-player game) BUT: "2 PLAYER GAME RULES: The game still lasts **6 rounds**." AND "3 PLAYER GAME RULES: The game lasts **6 rounds instead of four**."

The summary states the game lasts 4 rounds without noting the 2- and 3-player variants play 6 rounds. The summary also says "player_count: 2-4" but gives only 4-round rules. A 2-player or 3-player game played with the summary's rules would be wrong.

### Finding 2 — Tiebreaker order (verified correct)

Summary: "Ties for tallest building: the 3 points are split (rounded down) or not awarded (depending on edition)."
Source: "If two or more buildings are tied for first place, no player scores 3 points."

The source is unambiguous: tied tallest = no one scores. The summary hedges with "split (rounded down) or not awarded (depending on edition)" — but the source says no points awarded in ties. The "split" option is unsupported.

Summary: "Ties for most buildings in a city: no one scores that city."
Source: "In a case of a tie, no player scores points in that city."
SUPPORTED.

### Finding 3 — End-of-game tiebreaker (verified correct)

Summary says "Ties broken by unused action cards." — this is NOT in the Manhattan source; this is from mall-of-horror. The Manhattan source says: "If there is a tie, the player with the highest building between the tied players wins. If there is still a tie, the player who owns the most buildings wins."

Wait — re-reading the summary: the Scoring/Victory section says "After 4 rounds, the player with the highest total score wins." and in Special Rules mentions "Ties for tallest building... Ties for most buildings in a city..." but the tiebreaker for the final score is NOT mentioned. The source's end-of-game tiebreaker (highest building → most buildings) is absent from the summary.

Actually re-reading more carefully: the summary says "Ties for tallest building: the 3 points are split (rounded down) or not awarded (depending on edition)." This refers to the mid-game scoring tie, not end-of-game. The source says no points for tallest if tied — the "split" option is invented/unsupported.

### Finding 4 — Cards per player in 3-player game (verified correct)

Summary: "Each player selects 6 building blocks they will use this round."
Source (3-player): "Every round, each player plays **4 Building blocks** instead of 6."
The summary states 6 blocks as the universal rule, missing the 3-player variant of 4 blocks.

### Finding 5 — Card draw mechanics (verified correct)

Summary: "Draw 4 new building cards for the next round."
Source: "After a player has placed their Building block, they draw another Building card from the face down pile... Note: Players keep the Construction cards they have in hand at the end of the round to start the next one."

The summary says "Draw 4 new building cards for the next round" but the source says you draw 1 card after each block placement (mid-round), and you keep cards between rounds. There is no round-end draw of 4 new cards. This is a MAJOR procedural error.

### Finding 6 — Scoring numbers (verified correct)

Summary: City majority = 2 points, Tallest building = 3 points, Each building = 1 point.
Source: "A. TALLEST BUILDING - 3 POINTS... B. MOST BUILDINGS OWNED IN A CITY - 2 POINTS PER CITY... C. INDIVIDUAL BUILDINGS OWNED - 1 POINT EACH."
SUPPORTED — all three scoring values are correct.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (4 rounds, 6 blocks per round, 4 cards dealt initially, 3 VP tallest, 2 VP city, 1 VP building), SUPPORTED: 4

The "Draw 4 new building cards for the next round" is contradicted by the source (you draw 1 per turn and keep cards between rounds). The tallest-building tie producing "split (rounded down)" is unsupported. Game length for 2/3 players is wrong.

## manila

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn structure: accomplice placement and dice rolls (MAJOR)

Summary: "Roll dice and move ships: Roll dice 3 times. Each roll moves ships forward. After each roll, more accomplices may be placed."

Source: "This phase has **three accomplice placing rounds, each followed by a punt movement round**. After the first accomplice placing round comes the first punt movement round, and so forth." And: "Beginning with the harbor master, and continuing in clockwise order, each player takes turns placing one of his accomplices on any vacant accomplice space and pays the amount shown."

The summary reverses the order: it says dice are rolled first and accomplices placed AFTER each roll. The source says accomplices are placed BEFORE each roll. This is a direction/order reversal — a MAJOR error for anyone trying to play the game.

### Finding 2 — Harbor Master shares-only privilege (partially verified)

Summary: "The harbor master also the only one able to buy a new share."
Source: "The harbor master is also the only one able to buy a new share as an investment in the future."
SUPPORTED.

### Finding 3 — Game end trigger (verified correct)

Summary: "The game ends when any good reaches maximum price on the track."
Source: "The game ends as soon as one ware value reaches 30."
SUPPORTED — the "maximum price" described is 30.

### Finding 4 — Victory condition (partially correct with omission)

Summary: "Players total: Cash on hand + Value of held shares (at current prices). The player with the most total wealth wins."

Source: "Each player counts his cash (PESOS) and adds to this the value of his shares (the value of the ware as shown on the black market table). From this sum, he subtracts **15 PESOS for each encumbered share** (loan not yet repaid). This final total is the players FORTUNE."

The summary omits the loan repayment deduction (15 PESOS per encumbered share). This is a scoring source omission that would cause incorrect final scores whenever loans were taken. MAJOR.

### Finding 5 — Punt starting positions constraint (verified correct)

Summary: "Ships start at different positions based on the goods' current price (cheaper goods start further back, making arrival harder)."
Source: "The harbor master places each punt on one of the sea routes... on one of the start positions on the sea route selected, numbered 0 to 5... the sum of the numbers of the 3 start position MUST always add to exactly 9."

The summary correctly implies differential starting positions. However it doesn't mention the sum-must-equal-9 constraint or the maximum-5 rule. These are significant constraints omitted from setup description — MINOR omission.

### Finding 6 — Insurance payout mechanism (verified with errors)

Summary: "Insurance: A ship fails; you collect insurance"
Source: The insurance agent receives 10 PESOS UP FRONT at placement, then must PAY OUT to players with accomplices at shipyard spaces when ships fail. The insurance agent profits only if no ship fails (keeping the 10 PESOS).

The summary's accomplice table says insurance pays out "A ship fails; you collect insurance" — but in reality the insurance agent PAYS (not collects) when ships fail. Collecting happens at placement (10 PESOS received), but paying happens on failure. The payout direction is reversed in the summary's description. MAJOR.

### Finding 7 — Pilot actions (verified correct)

Summary: "Harbor pilot: Help a ship arrive for a bonus"
Source: The small pilot moves one punt forward or backward by one space; the large pilot moves one punt up to two spaces or two punts each by one space.

The summary's description is extremely simplified but not contradicted.

### Finding 8 — Starting money and shares (verified correct)

Summary: "Each player receives starting money and accomplice pawns."
Source: "Each player takes 30 PESOS, 2 shares, 3 accomplices (game for 3: 4 accomplices)."
SUPPORTED — 30 pesos starting money verified.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (30 pesos start, 2 shares each, 3 accomplices, game-end at value 30, sum of start positions = 9, max start position 5, 10 pesos insurance payment, 15 pesos loan repayment), SUPPORTED: 6

The accomplice placement / dice roll order reversal (accomplices BEFORE rolls, not after) and the insurance payout direction reversal (agent PAYS when ships fail, not collects) and the missing loan-deduction from final score are all MAJOR.

## manoeuvre

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn sequence order (MAJOR)

Summary: "Each turn: 1. Restore a unit (if any were used for combat last turn). 2. Play cards to move units, attack, or use special abilities. 3. Draw cards back up to hand limit (5 cards). 4. Discard if over hand limit."

Source (rule 4 Sequence of Play): "Each Player Turn consists of 5 Phases, as follows: 1. Discard Phase, 2. Draw Phase, 3. Movement Phase, 4. Combat Phase, 5. Restoration Phase."

The summary places Restoration FIRST; the source places it LAST. The summary puts Discard LAST; the source puts it FIRST. The actual sequence is: discard unwanted cards → draw up to 5 → move → combat → restore. A player using the summary would attempt restoration before movement and combat, which is wrong.

### Finding 2 — Nightfall condition (MAJOR)

Summary: "Nightfall: When both players have cycled through their decks once, nightfall occurs."

Source (rule 2.3): "the game ends at the end of the Game Turn when **both players have drawn their last card from their first Action Deck**. The game does not end when just one player has drawn the last card in their first Action Deck. That player reshuffles all their discards to form a new Action Deck and the game continues. This player may reshuffle their Action Deck **as many times as necessary**. Once the other player draws the last card from their first Action Deck, the game is over."

The summary says nightfall occurs when both players cycle through their decks once. The source says: one player may reshuffle MULTIPLE times; nightfall only occurs after BOTH players have exhausted their FIRST deck (which may be at very different times). Also critically: the summary says "When both decks are exhausted" but the source clarifies it's specifically when the *second* player draws their last card from their *first* deck.

### Finding 3 — Attrition victory: eliminate 5 units (verified correct)

Summary: "Eliminate 5 enemy units (out of 8)."
Source: "If, at any point in the game, you have eliminated 5 (or more) of your opponent's units, you immediately win the game."
SUPPORTED.

### Finding 4 — Nightfall tiebreaker (MAJOR)

Summary: "If the battlefield is evenly split at nightfall with no unit advantage, the game is a draw."

Source (rule 2.2): "If there is a tie, whichever side eliminated more of his opponent's units wins. If there is still a tie, whichever side has fewer units at reduced strength wins. If there is still a tie, then the country first shown in the following list which is involved in the battle wins: United States, Ottoman Empire, Spain, Austria, Prussia, Russia, Great Britain, France."

The source says there is NEVER a draw—there are three additional tiebreakers (units eliminated, reduced strength, nation ranking). The summary's claim of a draw is directly contradicted.

### Finding 5 — Terrain table (cannot fully verify from extracted text)

Summary provides a specific terrain table:
| Clear | 1 | 0 |
| Forest | 2 | +1 |
| Mountain | 2 | +2 |
| Marsh | 2 | +1 |
| Town | 1 | +1 |

Source: "Refer to the Terrain Effects Chart on the Reference card for the specifics of each type of terrain."

The terrain table is on the Reference Card, not in the rules text. The specific values cannot be verified from the extracted text alone. The source mentions Hills, Towns, Woods, Marsh, Field, Lake — but does not give movement costs or defense bonuses in the rules text. "Forest" appears in the summary but the source mentions "Woods" (not "Forest"). "River" is mentioned in summary but not found in source. Cannot confirm or deny the specific values. Marked as unverifiable.

No support found for "Forest" terrain type (searched: Forest, forest, River, river). Source uses "Woods" and "Hills" instead.

### Finding 6 — Artillery (verified with error)

Summary: "Artillery can bombard at range (2 squares)."
Source: Artillery units are described via cards having a "Bombardment" value. "In a Bombardment, the defending unit may be up to two squares away." Also: "Unit Cards which have an artillery icon on them do not have an attack value" (they have Bombardment value instead).

The "2 squares" range for bombardment is SUPPORTED. However, source also describes "Volley" as another ranged combat form (not just Bombardment), and Volley is also a ranged attack distinct from Bombardment. The summary lumps all ranged attack into "Artillery bombards at range."

### Finding 7 — Combat procedure (verified with error)

Summary: "1. The attacker plays combat cards and rolls dice. 2. The defender may play defense cards. 3. Compare attack value (cards + dice) to defense value (cards + terrain bonus). 4. If attack exceeds defense, the defending unit is eliminated. 5. If defense holds, the attacker may be forced to retreat."

Source: The outcome table shows multiple levels (Attack < Defense = attacker takes a hit; Attack = Defense = no effect; Attack > Defense = defender retreats or takes hit; Attack ≥ 2x Defense = attacker chooses defender retreats or takes hit; Attack ≥ 3x Defense = defender takes hit AND retreats; Attack ≥ 4x Defense = defender eliminated).

The summary says "if attack exceeds defense, the defending unit is eliminated" — but the source requires attack to be 4x defense total for elimination. Merely exceeding defense causes a retreat-or-hit choice. And "if defense holds, the attacker may be forced to retreat" — the source says the attacker takes a hit (not retreat) when attack is less than defense. This is a MAJOR misrepresentation of the combat resolution table.

### Finding 8 — Cavalry charge (verified partially)

Summary: "Cavalry can charge (move and attack in one action) and pursuit (attack retreating enemies)."
Source: "Pursuit: If a defending unit Withdraws or Retreats, and a Cavalry unit advances into the vacated square, then that Cavalry unit may be able to inflict a hit or multiple hits on the Withdrawn/Retreated unit provided that specific unit played a card in the Combat."

Pursuit is SUPPORTED. "Charge (move and attack in one action)" — the source describes Infantry moving 1 square and Cavalry moving 1-2 squares, with combat declared separately in the Combat Phase. There is no single "charge" HQ card described in the extracted text; movement and combat are separate phases. The summary's "charge = move and attack in one action" appears to be unsupported or invented. No support found for "charge" mechanic (searched: charge, Charge).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (5 units for attrition, 8 units per army, 60-card deck, 5-card hand, 2-square bombardment range, 4x for elimination, 1-square infantry move, 2-square cavalry move), SUPPORTED: 6

The turn sequence inversion (Restoration first vs. last), the nightfall trigger misstatement ("both cycle once" vs. "both exhaust first deck"), the no-draw claim (source has multiple tiebreakers), and the combat outcome table (attack exceeds defense → eliminated, not → choose retreat/hit) are all MAJOR errors.
