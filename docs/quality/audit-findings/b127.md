## la-granja

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Farm Phase step order wrong; "Take Income" step omitted

Summary (Phase 1, Farm Phase steps):
> "1. Play a card and draw … 2. Harvest: Each field produces 1 harvest good … 3. Purchase roof marker … 4. Choose donkey marker …"

Source (section 6.0):
> "1. Play a farm card and draw new card(s)"
> "2. Take income — Each player takes his income from the pool."
> "3. Growth on fields and pig offspring — Each player receives new harvest goods on fields and offspring for his pigs."
> "4. Purchase roof markers"

The summary **omits the "Take income" step entirely** (step 2 in source). It also mislabels step 2 as "Harvest" when step 2 is actually "Take income" and step 3 is the growth/harvest step. This is a substantive omission — players would never know income is collected in the Farm Phase.

### Finding 2 — "Choose donkey marker" misplaced from Transportation Phase to Farm Phase

Summary (Farm Phase step 4):
> "4. Choose donkey marker: Each player selects 1 of their remaining donkey markers for this turn (determines deliveries and siesta advancement)."

Source (section 8.0–8.1):
> "8.0 TRANSPORTATION PHASE … 8.1 Choose Donkey Marker — Each player secretly chooses one of his available donkey markers and places it, flipside up, in front of him."

The summary places the donkey marker selection as the **fourth step of the Farm Phase**, but the source places it as **step 1 of the Transportation Phase**. This would cause players to misplay the sequence (they would choose their donkey marker before rolling revenue dice instead of after).

### Finding 3 — Invented tiebreaker (harvest goods)

Summary:
> "After 6 turns, the player with the most VP wins. Ties broken by silver, then remaining harvest goods."

Source (section 10.0):
> "The player with the most victory points is the winner. If there is a tie, among the tied players the player with the most silver remaining is the winner. If there is still a tie, there are several winners."

No mention of harvest goods as a tiebreaker anywhere in the source. "Several winners" is the final outcome for remaining ties. The summary invents harvest goods as a second-order tiebreaker.

### Finding 4 — Revenue Phase description oversimplifies die selection

Summary:
> "In game order, each player selects 2 dice and gains the corresponding revenue"

Source (section 7.0):
> "In game turn order each player takes one die and places it on the left die space of his farm. Afterwards the player immediately conducts the respective action! When each player has taken one die and conducted an action, each player, again in game turn order, takes a second die … Finally, one die will remain next to a revenue space. All players now conduct the action of that die in game turn order."

The Revenue Phase involves **three rounds of die selection** (first die for all, second die for all, then a shared remaining die for all), not simply "each player selects 2 dice." Players each actually engage with 3 dice effects per turn. The shared remaining die is omitted entirely.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 7 (component counts, victory point conversions, upgrade costs, dice per player count, silver tiebreaker, roof marker VP, siesta VP 0-3), SUPPORTED: 6 (silver tiebreaker not supported as described; harvest goods tiebreaker not supported at all)

## labyrinth

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn order reversed: Jihadist goes first, not US

Summary:
> "US player acts first, playing 1 card per action round."

Source (section 5.2.2):
> "In the first Action Phase, the Jihadist side plays two cards in succession from its hand. Then the US side does the same during its Action Phase, then the Jihadists, and so on."

Source (section 3.2):
> "begin play with the Jihadist Action Phase (see Sequence of Play, 5.0)."

The summary states the US acts first, but the source is unambiguous that the Jihadist side always goes first in each turn.

### Finding 2 — Cards per action phase: 2 cards, not 1

Summary:
> "US player acts first, playing 1 card per action round."

Source (section 5.2.2):
> "In the first Action Phase, the Jihadist side plays two cards in succession from its hand. Then the US side does the same during its Action Phase…"
> Glossary: "Action Phase: Alternating game segment in which a side (typically) plays two cards. (5.2.2)"

Each side plays **two cards** per action phase, not one. This is a core mechanic error.

### Finding 3 — Jihadist Caliphate victory condition fundamentally wrong

Summary:
> "Islamist Caliphate: If a country under Islamist Rule has 2+ adjacent countries also under Islamist Rule, Jihadist wins."

Source (section 2.1.2):
> "The Jihadists win the instant Islamist Rule governs countries totaling at least six Resources that include at least two adjacent countries"

The summary describes a hub-and-spoke adjacency requirement (one country with 2+ adjacent Islamist countries). The source requires a **total of 6+ Resources under Islamist Rule** spread across **at least two adjacent countries**. These are completely different conditions. The summary's version could be satisfied with 0 Resources; the source requires 6. Players using the summary's condition would trigger victory at wrong times.

### Finding 4 — Invented "Automatic Loss" condition

Summary (Special Rules):
> "Automatic Loss: If the US has no troops deployed and prestige is at 1, US loses."

Source (section 2.1 — complete victory conditions):
The Jihadist wins via: 6+ Islamist Resources with 2+ adjacent countries, OR Prestige "1" AND 15+ Muslim countries at Poor/Islamist Rule, OR WMD Plot resolves in US. There is no "no troops + prestige 1" automatic loss condition anywhere in the source.

No support found (searched: "no troops", "zero troops", "troop.*prestige", "automatic.*loss", "US loses", "defeat").

### Finding 5 — US victory condition adds "cadre" not in source

Summary:
> "Alternatively, if all cells and cadre are eliminated."

Source (section 2.1.1):
> "there are no Jihadist cells in any countries on the map."

The source mentions only cells, not cadres. Cadres are not mentioned in the US victory condition. This is a minor expansion of the condition.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (Prestige range 1-12, Funding range 1-9, Good governance =1, Fair=2, Poor=3, 12 Resources for US win, 15 Muslim countries threshold, 6 Resources for Jihadist win, WMD plot boxes =3 per box, 7-9 starting hand size, 120 cards, troop/funding tracks), SUPPORTED: 11 (Caliphate resource threshold wrong in summary)
