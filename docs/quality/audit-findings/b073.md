## doom-the-boardgame — MAJOR

### Findings

1. **Invented mechanic: defense dice.** Summary (Combat): "Defender may roll defense dice based on armor. Damage = attack results minus defense results." Source has no defense dice anywhere. Armor is a static rating: "In order to inflict damage to a figure (invader or marine both), the total damage must be equal to or greater than its armor rating. Thus, if a figure has 2 armor, it will take 2 points of damage to give it 1 wound... An attack deals 1 wound for every increment of damage equal to the target's armor rating." This is a fabricated combat system.

2. **Core action system omitted/misrepresented.** Summary (Marine Turn): "On your turn, you may perform actions using your action points (typically spending movement or attacks)." Source: a marine must declare exactly one of four actions — Sprint (move up to 8, no attack), Unload (up to 2 attacks, no move), Advance (move up to 4 + 1 attack), Ready (move up to 4 OR 1 attack, plus place a marine order). There are no action points. The entire Sprint/Unload/Advance/Ready structure — the heart of the marine turn — is missing.

3. **Core combat mechanic omitted.** Source: dice faces show range and damage; "The attack hits if the total range of the rolled dice is equal to or greater than the range needed for the attack," with miss faces on red/yellow dice and ammo expenditure when bullet icons are rolled. None of this (range-to-hit, miss results, ammo spend per bullet icon) appears in the summary; it is replaced by the invented attack-minus-defense formula.

4. **Wrong cost: doors.** Summary Player Reference: "Open Door | Free | Remove door token." Source: "It costs a figure 2 spaces of movement to either open or close a door."

5. **Marine orders subsystem omitted.** Aim / Guard / Dodge / Heal orders (a full page of the source, tied to the Ready action) are never mentioned.

6. **Victory condition drift.** Summary: "The Invader wins by killing all Marines or preventing them from completing their objective within the time/turn limit." Source: marines respawn when fragged; the invader's goal "in many scenarios" is to frag the marines a certain number of times (6 frags in KNEE DEEP IN THE DEAD). "Killing all Marines" is not how the invader wins.

7. **Respawn rule drift (smaller).** Summary: "Marines who die may respawn at designated points depending on the scenario." Source: respawn space must be a revealed empty space at least 8 and no more than 16 spaces from the frag location — not scenario-designated points.

### Numbers checked (10)
- 2-4 players: ✓ ("a game for 2 to 4 players")
- 120 min: ✓-ish ("playable in 1 to 2 hours")
- Marine turn = action points: ✗ (four declared actions: Sprint 8 / Unload 2 attacks / Advance 4+1 / Ready 4 or 1 + order)
- Open door = free: ✗ (2 movement spaces)
- Pick up item = free: ✓ ("does not cost him any movement points")
- Move 1 space = 1 MP: ✓ (implied by movement rules)
- Defense dice rolled by defender: ✗ (no such mechanic; static armor rating)
- Invader wins by killing all marines: ✗ (frag count, e.g. 6 frags in scenario 1)
- Invader hand: source 5 starting cards, draw 1/marine, hand limit 8 — absent from summary (omission)
- Invader kill threshold "wounds ≥ wound rating": ✓

**Verdict: MAJOR** — invented defense-dice combat, missing four-action system and range-to-hit mechanic, wrong door cost, wrong invader win condition.

## doomtown — MAJOR

### Findings

1. **Stud/draw bullets reversed.** Summary (Special Rules): "Stud bullets let you redraw that many cards; draw bullets do not." Source: "studs increase your draw hand's size, while draw dudes allow you to discard and replace cards from your draw hand." The summary inverts the central shootout mechanic: studs add cards to your draw hand (draw 5 + stud bonus); draw bullets grant discard-and-redraw. Also, the summary's shootout step "Each player draws 5 cards from deck" omits the stud bonus entirely (source: "the starting draw hand size is five cards, so draw that plus your posse's stud bonus").

2. **Cheatin' definition wrong.** Summary: "Cheatin' hands (duplicate values in a 5-card poker hand from non-Joker cards)." Source: "A draw hand is cheatin' if there are at least two cards in it (other than jokers) that share the same suit and value." Duplicate values alone (e.g., any pair) are legal; the summary's definition would make most hands cheatin'.

3. **Phase misattribution: unbooting and hand refill.** Summary places "Unboot all your cards" and "Discard down to hand limit" in the Upkeep phase. Source: both happen at Sundown ("each player unboots all of their booted cards" and refill/discard to 5 are Sundown steps); Upkeep is income + dude upkeep only.

4. **Movin' boot rules inverted/oversimplified.** Summary: "Move an unbooted dude to an adjacent location (boots them)." Source: "A dude may boot to move to any location, or move without booting to particular adjacent locations" (home→adjacent and town square→adjacent are boot-free). The summary makes adjacent moves cost a boot and omits boot-to-move-anywhere.

5. **Gamblin' drift (smaller).** Summary: winner "chooses to go first or second." Source: the Winner simply "makes the first play during High Noon & shootouts" — no choice. Summary also never states the Gamblin' hand is lowball (lowest hand rank wins).

### Numbers checked (10)
- Ante 1 ghost rock: ✓
- Starting/maximum hand 5 cards: ✓
- Gamblin' draw hand = top 5 cards: ✓
- Shootout draw hand = 5 cards flat: ✗ (5 + stud bonus)
- Casualties = hand rank difference: ✓ ("For every hand rank by which you lose a shootout, you suffer a casualty")
- Discard covers 1 casualty / ace covers 2: source ✓, summary omits the discard-vs-ace distinction (minor omission)
- Refill to 5 at Sundown: ✓
- 11 hand ranks, order High Card → Dead Man's Hand: ✓ (matches ranks 1-11)
- Win = control points > opponent influence at Sundown: ✓
- Bounty +1 for joining shootout at another player's Private location: source ✓, summary omits; "wanted... affects interactions with the Law Dogs outfit" is not supported by the source (wanted = collect bounty when you ace/discard the dude)

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the stud/draw mechanic (the core of every shootout) is stated backwards, and the cheatin' definition is wrong; plus phase and movement-boot errors.

## dorfromantik-the-board-game — MINOR

### Findings

1. **Game-end condition imprecise.** Summary: "At game end (when all tiles are placed)." Source: "As soon as you have to reveal and place a landscape tile, but all stacks of landscape tiles have been used up, the game ends immediately. Task tiles that have not yet been revealed and placed do not come into play." The summary's phrasing implies all tiles get placed; unrevealed task tiles do not. The continuation rule (if the last landscape tile completes a task, keep revealing task tiles until no more tasks complete) is also omitted.

2. **Flag scoring value omitted.** Summary lists "Closed flagged areas" as a scoring source but never gives the value. Source: "For each flag in a closed area, you receive as many points as the area consists of tiles."

3. **Task token scoring slightly vague.** Summary: "Completed task tokens (points vary by number)." Source: "Add up the numbers of all completed task tokens" — i.e., each token scores exactly its printed number (4/5/6). Supported but imprecise.

## dracula — MINOR

### Findings

1. **Vampire Perch pass-over rule inverted.** Summary (Moving Your Piece): "You cannot land on **or pass over** Vampire Perches." Source: "A player may pass over, but cannot land on, any of the six Vampire Perches." Passing over is explicitly allowed; the summary forbids it, which would needlessly restrict players' movement at the table.

2. **Omission (small): Green Vampire vault behavior.** Source: after conversion, the Green Vampire waits in the vault until a V roll sends him to a perch, or until his own turn — in which case "he does not take a spin... but simply moves to the Green Vampire Perch Number 1." Not in the summary.

3. **Omission (trivial):** captured players other than the first are released at the top of the vault steps "except the first" (the first becomes the Green Vampire) — the summary states both halves separately, consistent overall.
