## paleo

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Wrong ability names (Player Reference and Actions sections)

Summary: "People skills: Strength, Perception, Crafting"

Source (extracted/paleo-rules.txt, lines 280–282):
> Strength
> Awareness
> Skill

Two of the three ability names are wrong. "Awareness" is renamed "Perception" and "Skill" is renamed "Crafting." These names appear throughout the game on People cards; a player learning from this summary would be looking for "Perception" and "Crafting" on cards that say "Awareness" and "Skill."

Also repeated in the Actions section: "Use abilities | People cards have skills (perception, crafting, strength)."

### Finding 2 — "Draw 3 cards" mischaracterizes the mechanic

Summary: "Each player simultaneously draws **3 cards** from the Wilderness deck (choosing based on card backs showing terrain)."

Source (lines 179–182):
> Look at the backs of the top 3 cards of your deck, and choose 1 of them to place facedown in front of you. You can look only at the card backs; you cannot look at the card fronts. Then, return the other 2 cards to the top of your deck in the order of your choice.

Players do not "draw" 3 cards — they look at the backs of the top 3 and return 2 to their deck. The summary also omits that the unchosen 2 cards are returned to the top of the deck in the player's chosen order (a meaningful strategic decision).

### Finding 3 — Night phase reshuffle presented as conditional

Summary: "Discard and reshuffle if needed."

Source (lines 612–616):
> Shuffle all the cards in the faceup and facedown discard piles on the wilderness board together and deal them out facedown, as evenly as possible to the players... Once the cards are dealt, the night phase ends and the next day phase begins.

The reshuffle and redistribution happens every night as a mandatory step, not "if needed." This is a significant omission because the redistribution of cards between players is a core end-of-night mechanic.

### Victory/End condition check

Summary: "Win: Collect all required painting tokens (completing the cave painting) before defeat. Lose: Accumulate too many skull tokens (varies by scenario, typically 5)."

Source (lines 36–43):
> The object of the game is to complete the cave painting on the night board. To do so, you must collect 5 victory tokens as a tribe... Once you have collected all 5 victory tokens, you immediately win. If your tribe collects a 5th skull token, all players immediately lose the game.

The summary says "typically 5" for skull tokens but the source fixes this at exactly 5. The "typically" is unsupported and may mislead players into thinking this varies. However the win condition is correct (5 victory tokens).

### Procedural check — Night phase feeding

Summary: "Feed the tribe: each People card requires 1 food. Unfed people result in skull tokens."

Source (lines 595–597):
> Each player must feed all of the people in their group. For each person, pay 1 food from the storage area to the supply. If there is not enough food to feed everyone, place 1 skull token on the night board for each unfed person. Unfed people do not die.

This matches the summary. Confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4 (victory tokens=5, skull tokens=5, food per person=1, abilities=3), SUPPORTED: 3 (victory tokens, skull tokens, food per person), UNSUPPORTED/WRONG: 1 (two of three ability names wrong)

## pandemic

**Verdict: PASS**

### Victory/End condition check

Summary: "Players win immediately when cures for all 4 diseases are discovered. Defeat: 8 outbreaks, cube supply exhausted, Player Deck empty."

Source (lines 516–521):
> The players win as soon as cures to all 4 diseases are discovered. There are 3 ways for the game to end and the players to lose: if the outbreaks marker reaches the last space of the Outbreaks Track, if you are unable to place the number of disease cubes actually needed on the board, or if a player cannot draw 2 Player cards after doing his actions.

All three loss conditions match. Win condition matches.

### Procedural checks

**Epidemic card steps:**
Summary: "1. Increase: Move Infection Rate marker forward 1 space. 2. Infect: Draw the bottom card of Infection Deck. Place 3 cubes (or add to reach 3 + outbreak). 3. Intensify: Reshuffle only the Infection Discard Pile and place on top of Infection Deck."

Source (lines 436–452):
> 1. Increase: Move the infection rate marker forward 1 space. 2. Infect: Draw the bottom card from the Infection Deck... put 3 disease cubes... If the city already has cubes of this color, do not add 3 cubes to it. Instead, add just enough cubes so that it has 3 cubes of this color and then an outbreak... 3. Intensify: Reshuffle just the cards in the Infection Discard Pile and place them on top of the Infection Deck.

Matches summary exactly.

**Outbreaks:**
Summary: "Place 1 cube on every city connected to the outbreak city. Chain reactions: city can only outbreak once per chain."

Source (lines 479–488):
> Move the outbreaks marker forward 1 space. Then, place 1 cube of the disease color on every city connected to the city. If any of them already has 3 cubes of the disease color, do not place a 4th cube. Instead, in each of them, a chain reaction outbreak occurs after the current outbreak is done... do not add a cube to cities that have already had an outbreak (or a chain reaction outbreak) as part of resolving the current Infection card.

Matches.

**Eradication:**
Summary: "If the last cube of a cured disease is removed from the board, the disease is eradicated. Eradicated diseases: no new cubes are placed. Eradication is not required to win."

Source (lines 339–341):
> Eradicating a disease is not needed to win. However, when cities of an eradicated disease are infected, no new disease cubes are placed there. Removing the last cube of a disease that is not cured has no effect.

Matches.

**Player Reference — Infection Rate Track:**
Summary shows positions 1-2 = 2, 3-4 = 3, 5-7 = 4. The extracted source does not include the full track table (it is printed on the board); starting position is confirmed at the leftmost "2" space. Track values cannot be independently verified from this source text but are internally consistent.

**Roles:**
All 7 roles checked against source. Medic, Scientist, Researcher, Dispatcher, Operations Expert, Quarantine Specialist, Contingency Planner descriptions all match the source.

**Event cards:**
All 5 event cards (Airlift, One Quiet Night, Resilient Population, Government Grant, Forecast) checked. All descriptions match source.

**Key numbers:**
- Disease cubes per color: 24 — Source: "24 in 4 colors" — CONFIRMED
- Max cubes per city: 3 — Source: "If the city already has 3 cubes of this color, do not place a 4th" — CONFIRMED
- Hand limit: 7 — Source: "If you ever have more than 7 cards in hand" — CONFIRMED
- Research stations: 6 — Source: "6 Research stations" — CONFIRMED
- Cards to cure (standard): 5, Scientist: 4 — Source: "discard 5 City cards of the same color" / "needs only 4 (not 5)" — CONFIRMED
- Starting infection: 3+3+3 cities, 3+2+1 cubes, 18 total — Source: "flip over 3...3 cubes...flip 3 more...2 cubes...flip 3 more...1 cube...total of 18 disease cubes" — CONFIRMED
- Introductory/Standard/Heroic = 4/5/6 Epidemic cards — Source confirms.

## pandemic-legacy-season-0

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented action: Shuttle Flight

Summary (Actions table): "Shuttle Flight | Move between cities with safe houses"

Source (line 178–179):
> Note: Unlike Pandemic, there is no "Shuttle Flight" action (you cannot move directly from one safehouse to another).

The summary includes Shuttle Flight as a valid action but the source explicitly states this action does not exist in Season 0. This is a directly invented mechanic that would mislead players at the table.

### Finding 2 — Mop Up step: vague to the point of being unusable

Summary: "3. Mop Up: Resolve any end-of-action effects."

Source (lines 287–289):
> 3. MOP UP: Your teams do what they were sent to do. Remove all agents in each city with an active team.

The summary's description "Resolve any end-of-action effects" does not tell players what to actually do in this step. The actual mechanic (remove all agents from cities where you have active teams) is the primary offensive action in the game — it is the core purpose of assembling and positioning teams. A player reading the summary cannot execute this step.

### Finding 3 — Loss condition: agent supply running out is omitted

Summary: "Lose: Running out of Player cards, OR The Incident track reaches its limit, OR Other legacy-specific loss conditions"

Source (lines 390–396):
> The game ends when any of the following occurs: All objectives have a Completed or Failed token. You cannot draw 2 Player cards because there are not enough cards left in the Player deck. You need to place an agent, but the supply is empty. You need to place an Incident token, but the supply is empty.

Two omissions: (1) The agent supply running out is a specific loss condition not mentioned. (2) There is no "Incident track" in Season 0 — the loss condition is when you cannot place an Incident token (i.e., the Incident token supply is exhausted), not a track reaching an end. The summary mischaracterizes the mechanism.

### Finding 4 — Action names are wrong (minor within MAJOR verdict)

Summary uses "Share Knowledge" (Pandemic base term); source calls it "SHARE INTEL."
Summary uses "Remove Agent"; source calls it "NEUTRALIZE AGENT."
Summary uses "Acquire Team"; source distinguishes "ASSEMBLE TEAM" (build a team) from "ACQUIRE TARGETS" (complete an objective with a positioned team) — these are two distinct actions that the summary conflates into one entry.

### Victory condition check

Summary: "Win (per month): Complete all required objectives before [loss conditions]."

Source (line 391): "All objectives have a Completed or Failed token" — this triggers game end, then the After the Game section determines win/loss based on how many were failed. The summary is broadly correct about the objective-completion win condition.

### Procedural check — Surveillance

Summary: "Check if your current city has enemy surveillance that affects your alias."

Source (lines 130–133):
> If you start your turn in a city with surveillance, lose 1 cover for each surveillance symbol (®) in that city (max 3). Ignore this if there is a safehouse in your city.

The summary omits that surveillance affects cover (not just "affects your alias"), and omits the safehouse exception. Minor within the MAJOR verdict.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (funding level=5, hand limit=7, escalation piles=5, agents placed on initial setup=3/2/1, starting city=Washington), SUPPORTED: 5

## pandemic-legacy-season-1

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Research station destruction placed at wrong panic level

Summary (Panic Levels table):
- Level 2-3 (Rioting): "No Direct Flights or Charter Flights in or out"
- Level 4 (Collapsing): "Existing research stations destroyed; no new ones can be built; discard 1 card of city's color to Drive/Ferry into this city"

Source (lines 509–517):
> Rioting: Players cannot take Direct Flights or Charter Flights in or out. Research stations are destroyed and cannot be rebuilt.
> Collapsing: Discard 1 card of the city's color to enter this city with the Drive/Ferry action.

The source places research station destruction at Rioting (level 2-3), not at Collapsing (level 4). The summary moves this consequence one panic level later. Under the summary's version, players would keep research stations in rioting cities until level 4, dramatically changing gameplay by providing an extra level of use. The source also confirms this from the Structures section (line 1075): "Destroy a research station in a city if it is rioting."

Additionally, the source (line 1077–1078) says "You cannot build a research station in a city that is rioting, collapsing, or fallen" — the no-build restriction covers all three states, but the summary places it only at Collapsing.

### Finding 2 — Funding level: summary says 4, confirmed correct

Summary: "Funding: Starts at 4 and adjusts between games."

Source (lines 368–369):
> Your Funding Level is 4 at the beginning of the first game in January but will go up and down from game to game.

This matches — confirmed correct.

### Finding 3 — Win condition: mandatory objective for January

Summary: "Win condition: Complete the mandatory objective(s) and enough optional objectives as shown on the board for the current month. The mandatory objective in January is to discover cures for all 4 diseases."

Source (Legacy card LD 02 F, line 492–493):
> MANDATORY: Discover cures to the 4 diseases.

Confirmed — January's mandatory objective matches.

### Procedural checks

**Turn structure:**
Summary: "1. Do 4 Actions, 2. Draw 2 Player Cards, 3. Infect Cities"
Source (lines 642–645): "Each player turn is divided into 3 steps: 1. Do 4 actions. 2. Draw 2 Player cards. 3. Infect cities." — Confirmed.

**Epidemic cards:**
Summary: "increase infection rate, infect the bottom card of the Infection deck with 3 cubes, then shuffle the Infection discard pile and place it on top of the Infection deck."
Source (lines 864–880): Same three steps in same order. — Confirmed.

**Outbreaks and chain reactions:**
Summary: "When a 4th cube of a color would be placed in a city, an outbreak occurs instead. Each adjacent city receives 1 cube of that color."
Source (lines 949–956): Confirmed.

**Funding adjustment on win/loss:**
Summary: "Win a month... Lose a month..." does not mention funding change.
Source (line 1003–1011): "If you win: Funding Level is cut by 2. If you lose: Funding Level is increased by 2." The summary mentions this under "Funding" section ("More Funded Event cards are added to the Player Deck at higher funding levels") but does not state the +/-2 mechanics. This is an omission but less critical.

**Scars:**
Summary: "Characters in a city when it outbreaks gain a scar."
Source (line 405–406): "Characters are scarred when they are in a city that outbreaks." — Confirmed.

**Lost Characters:**
Summary: "If a character's card is full of scars or the character is in a Fallen city, the card is ripped up."
Source (lines 409–416): "If you have to put a scar on your character and it has no more scar slots, or if your character is in a city when it falls, your character is lost. When your character is lost, destroy (rip up) your Character card." — Confirmed.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 9 (funding=4, funding change±2, 4 diseases, 5 epidemic cards default, 8 outbreaks to lose, 24 cubes per color, 7-card hand limit, 5 cards to cure, 4 cubes triggers outbreak), SUPPORTED: 9

## pandemic-legacy-season-2

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented loss condition: "Infection deck runs out"

Summary (Lose conditions): "8 incidents occur / Infection deck runs out / Player deck runs out"

Source (lines 992–996):
> There are multiple ways for the game to end in a loss: Immediately when the Incident marker reaches the last space of the Incident track. If a player cannot draw 2 player cards after their actions.

The source does NOT include "Infection deck runs out" as a loss condition. In fact, the source explicitly provides the opposite (lines 943–946):
> In the rare case you get through the entire Infection deck, reshuffle it and move the Infection Rate marker forward 1 space on the Infection Rate track.

The summary states as a loss condition something that the source handles as a normal reshuffle. A group using this summary would end a game that should continue, or (if they remembered the reshuffle rule) would be confused by the contradiction.

### Finding 2 — Turn structure omits Step 1: Check for Exposure

Summary (Turn Structure): "1. Do 4 Actions, 2. Draw 2 Player Cards, 3. Infect Cities"

Source (lines 662–667):
> EACH PLAYER TURN IS DIVIDED INTO 4 STEPS: 1. Check for exposure. 2. Do 4 actions. 3. Draw 2 Player cards. 4. Infect cities.

The summary omits "Check for exposure" as the first step. Exposure is a core character-damage mechanic in Season 2 (starting a turn in a location with Plague cubes causes exposure, which can lead to scars or death). Players following the summary's turn sequence would never trigger exposure checks.

The Player Reference table in the summary only shows 3 phases and also omits this step.

### Finding 3 — "8 incidents" loss threshold is not supported by the source

Summary: "8 incidents occur" as a lose condition.

Source: The Incident track ends at a specific space shown by a skull symbol (line 954: "If the Incident marker reaches the last space (skull), the game ends and the players lose"). The number "8" for the incident track limit is not stated in the extracted source text. The source says "last space" without specifying a number. The claim of "8" cannot be verified from this source.

No support found (searched: "8.*incident", "incident.*8", "last space.*8", "track.*8").

### Victory condition check

Summary: "Win: Complete the mandatory objective(s) and required number of optional objectives for the current month."

Source (lines 987–990):
> You win as soon as you complete the necessary number of objectives (which must include all mandatory objectives) or you trigger a losing condition.

The summary's win condition matches the source. Confirmed.

### Procedural checks

**Infect step:**
Summary: "Draw Infection cards equal to the infection rate. For each city drawn, remove 1 supply cube. If a city has no supply cubes, place an Incident marker (like an outbreak in Season 1)."

Source (lines 933–938):
> To infect a city, remove 1 Supply cube from it and return it to the stockpile. If the city has no Supply cubes, instead place a Plague cube and record the incident... If the city already has 3 Plague cubes, do not place a 4th cube. Instead, an outbreak of the plague occurs in the city.

The summary says "place an Incident marker" but the source says "place a Plague cube and record the incident." These are different things — Plague cubes are physical components with ongoing effects (characters starting turns there get exposed), while an Incident marker is different. The summary also omits the 3-Plague-cube outbreak trigger.

**Build Supply Center:**
Summary: "Build Supply Center | Discard 5 cards of your city's color to build a supply center"

Source (lines 755–758):
> Discard 5 cards matching your current city's color. Take a supply center and place it into that city. It will last for the rest of the game. If there are no more available, move an existing supply center to your location instead. Each city can have only 1 supply center.

Confirmed for the cost.

**Epidemic cards:**
Summary: "Resolve any Epidemics immediately."

Source (lines 869–890): Same three-step Increase/Infect/Intensify process as other Pandemic games. The Infect step in Season 2 removes ALL Supply cubes from the affected city (not just 3 cubes like Season 1). Summary does not describe this distinction.

**Game end steps:**
Summary: "After each game, players check the Legacy deck, resolve game end cards, and spend production units on improvements."

Source (lines 1285–1317): Four-step Game End sequence: Adjust Population, Check Legacy Deck, Resolve Game End Cards, Spend Production Units. The summary captures the gist but omits the population adjustment step (step 1), which includes decreasing population for cities with Plague cubes and increasing for cities with supply centers.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (supply center cost=5 cards, hand limit=7, starting rationing=4, 5 epidemic cards, infection rate starts at 2), SUPPORTED: 5 (cost, hand limit, rationing level confirmed), UNVERIFIED: 1 ("8" incident track limit)
