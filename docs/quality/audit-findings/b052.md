## chaos-in-the-old-world — MAJOR

Component counts are accurate, but the gameplay sections contain multiple contradicted rules, invented mechanics, and a missing game-end condition.

### Findings

1. **Contradicted — hand limit.** Summary (Phase 2): "Each player draws 2 Chaos cards (hand limit: 7)." Source L660: "There is no Chaos card hand size limit." The draw amount is also per-Power (printed on each Power sheet, L653–655), not a flat 2.

2. **Contradicted — domination rule.** Summary: "the player with the most corruption tokens in a region dominates it and scores VP equal to the region's value. Ties broken by most total figures in the region." Source L1167–1194: Domination Value = sum of played Chaos card costs + quantity of figures (corruption tokens are irrelevant); the highest value must *exceed* the region's Resistance ("equalling it is not sufficient"); and "If two or more players tie for the highest domination value in a region, none of them dominate it, and none score victory points." The summary's basis (corruption), threshold (none), and tiebreaker (most figures) are all wrong.

3. **Contradicted — corruption phase order.** Summary resolves corruption placement first, then checks domination. Source L1156–1162: "the domination step, which takes place first... the corruption step, which is second."

4. **Wrong — dial advancement conditions table.** Summary: Nurgle "cultists in 2+ regions with at least 1 Peasant token"; Tzeentch "cultists in 2+ regions with at least 1 Warpstone token"; Slaanesh "cultists in 2+ regions with at least 1 Noble or Hero token"; Khorne "kills enemy figures in battle." Source: Khorne is "Kill 2+ enemy creatures in the same region" (L1503–04, one counter per region per round); Slaanesh is "Place 2+ corruption tokens in the same region" containing Noble/Hero tokens (L1483–86, L1808–13); Tzeentch advances from "corrupting areas containing at least two Warpstone tokens and/or magic symbols" (L1805–06, L1835–36); Nurgle's condition relates to *Populous* regions, not Peasant tokens (L1826–31 strategy text). The summary's "2+" is attached to the wrong noun (regions instead of corruption tokens/kills), and the token specifics are wrong.

5. **Invented — Hero token mechanic.** Summary: "Heroes in a region deal automatic hits to Chaos followers during battle." Source L1213–1218: Hero tokens are resolved in the *End Phase*: "the player with the greatest Threat who also has at least one figure in that region must choose one of his figures in that region, and remove it from the board." Not battle hits, not automatic against all.

6. **Contradicted — corruption supply.** Summary: "Corruption token supply is limited (29 per power); if depleted, you cannot place more." Source L299–302: "If any Ruinous Power ever runs out of corruption tokens, then any convenient markers... can be used to represent more; the supply of corruption tokens should be considered unlimited."

7. **Omitted — game-end condition.** Source L1405, L1430–33: "The game ends if five regions have been ruined" (player with most VP wins). This entire end condition is missing from the summary.

8. **Invented — VP victory tiebreaker.** Summary: "tiebreaker: most dial advancements, then most corruption on the board." Source L1426–29: ties among 50+ VP players are broken by most VP, then "the tied player with the highest Threat wins."

9. **Invented — start player rotation.** Summary End Phase: "Start player rotates." Source: action order is always fixed Power order (Khorne, Nurgle, Tzeentch, Slaanesh; L677–681) and the End Phase steps (L1199–1207) contain no rotation.

10. **Wrong/oversimplified — ruination scoring.** Summary: "all players with corruption there score bonus VP." Source: at ruination, only players who placed corruption *during that corruption step* score the ruiner value immediately (L1154–55, example L1270–82); then in the End Phase the players with most and second-most corruption score the card's "first"/"second" values with tie-splitting rules (L1237–1338). Summary misses the two-stage structure and the first/second-most scoring entirely.

11. **Wrong — ruined region restrictions.** Summary: ruined regions "may no longer be entered or corrupted normally." Source L1586–1603: no new Chaos cards, Old World tokens, corruption, or domination scoring — but figures still enter and "Battle still occurs in a ruined region each round."

12. **Omission/inaccuracy — battle details.** Summary: "hits on 4+ (5+ for some)." Source L910–917: hits on 4, 5, or 6, and each 6 "explodes" (roll an additional die, no limit) — explosions omitted. The "(5+ for some)" has no support; the actual exception is upgraded Daemonettes, which can only be damaged by results of 6 (L925–932). Figure defense values (a figure dies when hits equal its defense, L870–872) are also omitted.

13. **Minor — setup inaccuracies.** Summary: "Shuffle Old World cards; reveal one and place it on the track." Source L516–523: deal 7 cards (4p) or 8 cards (3p) to form the deck, return the rest to the box; no card is revealed during setup (the first is drawn in round 1's Old World phase). Starting tokens are exactly 2 Nobles + 3 Warpstone + 4 Peasants, one per region (L524–533), not "Peasants, Nobles, Heroes, Warpstone, Skaven."

14. **Omission — summoning placement restriction.** Source L643–651: figures must be placed in a region where the Power already has a figure or adjacent to one (first figure anywhere). Summary omits this core placement rule.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — accurate components, but the domination, dial-advancement, hero, and hand-limit rules as summarized would directly mislead players.

## cheeky-monkey — MAJOR

**The summary describes an entirely different game than the source document.**

- Summary: Reiner Knizia's *Cheeky Monkey* — push-your-luck token drawing from a bag: "52 animal tokens (8 different animals)", an animal distribution table (Elephant 3 / Seal 4 / Hippo 5 / Giraffe 6 / Panda 7 / Kangaroo 8 / Lion 9 / Monkey 10), 8 bonus tiles, draw-until-duplicate bust, stealing from stack tops, monkey swap ability, scoring = tokens + bonus tiles.
- Source (`extracted/cheeky-monkey-rules.txt`): Gamewright/Cheatwell-style children's game *Cheeky Monkeys* — a spinner game: "1 two-part 3D tree • 32 monkey cards • 1 pool board • 1 spinner board • 1 two-part plastic spinner arrow" (L6–7). Players spin for banana sections, collect monkey cards holding "0, 1, 2, 3 or 6 bananas" (L57), with cheeky-monkey/pool-monkey/? spinner results; winner is "the player with the most bananas" (L71–72); game ends when no face-down monkey cards remain (L74–76).

There is zero overlap in components, turn structure, or scoring. Not a single number in the summary (52 tokens, 8 bonus tiles, the entire distribution/bonus table, "duplicate = bust", "most of each animal type") is supported by the source. Either the wrong rulebook PDF was extracted for bgg_id 29773, or the summary was generated from prior knowledge while ignoring the source. Numbers checked: 10+ (all 8 animal quantities, 8 bonus values, 52 total, 8 tiles) — all unsupported; source's own numbers (32 cards, 6-banana monkey, 4 tree sections) absent from summary.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — summary is not derived from its source document; pipeline mismatch needs investigation.

### Findings
1. **MINOR — omitted variant:** the source's "Expert Play" scoring variant (L154–162: each tile worth 1–3 points by number of distinct colors, Chameleons worth 3; on placement score your tile's value plus the values of all tiles contacted — illustrated "2+1 = 3", "2+1+2 = 5", "3+3 = 6") is entirely absent, even though the summary has an "Alternative Versions" section listing the other variants. Also omitted: the Junior-play note about placing Chameleons perpendicularly to spread the layout (L151–153), and the Conundrum puzzle description (L163–168).
2. **MINOR — self-contradictory setup wording:** summary: players place their 8 tiles "face-up in front of themselves, hidden from other players." Source L37–39: "places them on the table in front of him without showing them to the other players." "Face-up" and "hidden" cannot both be true; the source says hidden.
3. **MINOR — unsupported rule:** "Tiles cannot be rotated or flipped once placed" appears nowhere in the source. Practically harmless but stated as a rule.
4. Core flow (Chameleon starter tile in center, draw-1-must-play-if-able-else-keep-and-pass, youngest first, clockwise, bag-empty pass) matches the source.

**Verdict: MINOR** — core rules faithful; an entire optional scoring variant omitted plus two small wording/invention issues.
