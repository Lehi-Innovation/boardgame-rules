### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Wrong harvest schedule.** Summary: "Harvest does not occur every round — only when a Harvest marker appears (typically rounds 6-12, randomized)" and Phase 3 "If the round has a Harvest marker, resolve the Harvest." Source: "In the first four rounds, there is only one harvest: at the end of the third round… At the end of round 4, there is no harvest but instead you have to pay 1 Food per Dwarf… By default there will be a harvest at the end of rounds 5 to 12" (markers on 6–12 only *modify* the default via red question marks). The summary drops the round-3 harvest, the round-4 forced 1-Food payment, and the default harvest every round 5–12 — players following the summary would feed far less often than the game requires.

2. **MAJOR — Invented "cooking furnishing" requirement for converting animals to Food.** Summary (Phase 3 and Player Reference): "animals (varies by cooking method)" / "Animals | Requires cooking furnishing". Source: "you may convert goods into Food at any time (without constraints)… Sheep are each worth 1 Food, Wild boars 2 Food, and Cattle 3 Food. A single Donkey is worth 1 Food, but 2 Donkeys are worth 3 Food." No furnishing is needed (that is an Agricola rule, not Caverna). The actual animal Food values are also omitted entirely.

3. **MAJOR — Wrong Ruby-to-Food value (stated 3 times).** Summary: "Ruby (trade for 1 Food)", "1 Ruby = 1 Food", reference table "Ruby | 1 Food". Source: "you can convert Rubies into 2 Food directly."

4. **MAJOR — Missing scoring category.** The source scoring list includes "-2 Gold per missing type of Farm animal: …you should have at least 1 Sheep, 1 Donkey, 1 Wild boar and 1 Cattle. You lose 2 Gold points for each of these types that you do not have." Absent from the summary's scoring table and Player Reference.

5. **MAJOR — Invented tiebreaker.** Summary: "Tiebreaker: most Rubies, then most Gold coins, then most Farm animals, then most Grain/Vegetables." Source: "In case of a tie, there are multiple winners." No such tiebreaker chain exists.

6. **MINOR — Dog/Sheep rule wrong in both count and location.** Summary: "Dogs can guard Sheep in non-pasture spaces (1 Dog per space can guard Sheep equal to Dogs present)." Source: "You can keep one more Sheep on the Meadow or Pasture than there are Dogs on it. (1/2/3/… Dogs can watch 2/3/4/… Sheep)" and "Dogs cannot watch Sheep on Forest spaces or Fields." So sheep = dogs + 1, on Meadows/Pastures (not "non-pasture spaces").

7. **MINOR — Ruby trading list inaccurate.** Summary: "1 Ruby = … 1 landscape tile, certain Furnishing tiles." Source: a single Cavern tile costs **2** Rubies (Tunnel/Field/Meadow cost 1); Cattle costs 1 Ruby **+ 1 Food**; Rubies cannot buy Furnishing tiles. Also omits the 1-Ruby "play an armed Dwarf out of order" use.

8. **MINOR — Scoring vagueness where source is exact.** "Ruby Mines | Variable" (source: 4 Gold each); "Pastures … varies (Large)" (source: Large pasture = 4 Gold); "Fields with crops | Variable" (no such category — crops on fields score in the Grain/Vegetable lines).

9. **MINOR — Omissions:** Dwarf play order (unarmed first, then ascending weapon strength), Imitation and Starting player action spaces, weapon initial-strength cap 8 / max 14 / +1 per expedition, round structure stated as 3 phases vs source's 5 (content mostly preserved).

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong harvest/feeding schedule, invented food-conversion mechanic, wrong Ruby food value, missing scoring penalty, invented tiebreaker.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Royal Favors table misrepresents all four tracks.** Summary gives fixed benefits: "Resources: Take 2 resource cubes", "Money: Take 6 deniers", "Prestige: 2/3/4/5 for positions 1/2/3/4". Source: each line has **5 columns** of increasing power; prestige line gives "between 1 and 5 prestige points, depending on the column"; deniers line gives "between 3 and 7 deniers"; resource line is col 1: 1 food, col 2: 1 wood or stone, col 3: 1 cloth, col 4: trade 1 cube for 2 (no gold), col 5: 1 gold; building line gives discounted carpenter/mason/lawyer/architect uses by column. The summary also omits column-unlock timing (cols 3–4 after Dungeon count, col 5 after Walls count) and the "multiple favors in one phase must go on different lines" rule. Players would play this central system entirely wrongly.

2. **MAJOR — End-game scoring wrong.** Summary: "End-game bonuses for gold cubes (3 prestige each) and favor track positions." Source: "3 points per gold cube… 1 point per 3 cubes they have left (no Gold), 1 point per 4 deniers they have left." The "favor track positions" bonus is invented; the cube and denier bonuses are omitted.

3. **MAJOR — Castle phase core mechanics omitted.** Source: a contribution is a **batch of 3 different cubes, one of which must be food**; each batch scores **5/4/3 prestige** (Dungeon/Walls/Towers); a player with a castle worker who provides no batch **loses 2 prestige**; the player providing the most batches in a turn gains **1 royal favor**. None of this is in the summary, which only says "Workers placed in the castle contribute resources. Each castle section requires specific resources" (also wrong — the batch is the same for every section).

4. **MAJOR — The Counts system is missing entirely.** Source: when the bailiff reaches/passes count spaces (or a section completes), each section is counted with favor/penalty thresholds — Dungeon: 0 houses -2 PP, 2+ houses 1 favor; Walls: 0 houses -3 PP, 2 houses 1 favor, 3–4 houses 2 favors, 5+ 3 favors; Towers: 0 houses -4 PP, 2–3 houses 1 favor, 4–5 2 favors, 6+ 3 favors. The summary never mentions counts. Section sizes (Dungeon 6, Walls 10, Towers 14 parts) also omitted.

5. **MINOR — Bailiff movement rule omitted; game end imprecise.** Source: bailiff moves 2 spaces if the provost is ahead of him, 1 if behind/same, then the provost is placed on the bailiff's space; game ends "right after the count of the Towers section (after the bailiff has reached the Towers count space or when the 14 parts of the Towers have been built)". Summary: "The bailiff advances along the road… The game ends when the bailiff reaches a designated space" — drops the speed mechanic and the Towers-completion trigger.

6. **MINOR — 2-player note inaccurate.** Summary: "2-player rules include some modifications to building setup." Source 2p changes are: both start with 5 deniers, alternating turn order (no stables), and opponents pay 3 deniers (not 2) after a pass — not building setup.

7. **MINOR — Omitted building effects** (marketplace sell 1 cube, pedlar buy limits, church 2d→3PP / 4d→5PP, tailor 1 cloth→2PP / 3→6PP, bank 2d→1 gold / 5d→2 gold, alchemist 2 cubes→1 gold / 4→2; stable max 3 workers/1 per player). Acceptable compression for a summary, noted for completeness.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — favor track values contradicted, end-game bonuses partly invented/omitted, castle batch system and the entire Counts scoring/favor system missing.
