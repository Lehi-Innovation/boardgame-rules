## legend-of-the-burning-sands

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented Honour Victory and Dishonour Victory (major)

Summary claims:
> "Honour Victory: Reach the honour threshold (typically 40)."
> "Dishonour Victory: Reduce opponent's honour to 0 or below."

Source "How To Win" section (lines 282–288):
> "Legend of the Burning Sands has several paths to victory. You can eliminate your rivals by destroying their City Sections or by outlasting them as available Water dwindles (a Military Victory), or you can win by creating a powerful legend through Story cards (a Story Victory). Some Story cards create and define other paths to victory (Alternate Victories)... You lose and are removed from the game if you end your turn with no Water tokens in your control."

No honour mechanic exists in LBS. The Honour Victory and Dishonour Victory are entirely invented — they come from L5R, not from LBS. The loss condition is running out of Water, not reaching 0 honour.

no support found (searched: "honour", "honor", "threshold", "dishonour", "dishonor")

### Finding 2 — Wrong Military Victory description (major)

Summary: "Military Victory: Destroy all of your opponent's provinces."

Source: "You can eliminate your rivals by destroying their City Sections or by outlasting them as available Water dwindles (a Military Victory)."

LBS uses City Sections, not provinces. There are no province cards in LBS. The summary grafts L5R province terminology onto LBS.

no support found (searched: "province", "all provinces", "destroy.*province")

### Finding 3 — Missing Story Victory (major scoring omission)

Summary omits the Story Victory path entirely (reaching 5 Story Points via Story cards). Source (lines 247–248): "Stories are worth Story Points; if you get cards with a total of 5 Story Points into play, you immediately win the game. This is called a Story Victory."

A player could win using only the summary's victory conditions and entirely miss the Story Victory path.

### Finding 4 — Wrong component list: Dynasty deck and Fate deck (major invented mechanics)

Summary says components include "Dynasty deck (characters, holdings, regions)" and "Fate deck (actions, items, spells, followers)."

Source (lines 79–83): "You need to have at least fifty-five cards in your deck, plus your Stronghold and City Section cards." LBS uses a single deck, not two separate Dynasty and Fate decks. The two-deck structure is L5R's design. No evidence of a separate Fate deck in LBS at all.

no support found (searched: "dynasty deck", "fate deck", "two deck", "dual deck")

### Finding 5 — Wrong component list: Gold tokens and Honour tokens (major)

Summary lists "Gold tokens" and "Honour tokens" as components.

Source uses "Water tokens" and "Copper" (produced by Holdings and the Stronghold). No gold or honour tokens exist. Source (lines 74–75): "Keep some tokens handy... to represent Water and other elements of the game."

no support found (searched: "gold token", "honour token", "gold", "honour")

### Finding 6 — Invented Turn Structure phases (major)

Summary Phase Order:
1. Straighten
2. Events (resolve events)
3. Action (alternate actions with opponent)
4. Dynasty (refill provinces, collect gold)
5. Discard (hand limit)

Source Turn Sequence (lines 5–40):
1. Dawn Phase (straighten)
2. Day Phase (play actions, bring cards into play, once-per-turn attack)
3. Night Phase (night actions, raids)
4. End Phase (draw cards, discard to hand limit)

The summary invented an "Events Phase," "Action Phase," and "Dynasty Phase" that do not appear in LBS. The "Dynasty" phase (which refills provinces) is an L5R concept. The actual Night Phase is entirely missing. The game also has no provinces to refill — it has City Sections which cannot be added during a turn.

### Finding 7 — Setup describes province cards (not in LBS)

Summary setup step 2: "Place province cards face-down in a row in front of the Stronghold."

Source (lines 291–300): Setup involves choosing City Sections (purchased with City Points before play), placing Stronghold into play, and drawing a starting hand. There are no province cards in LBS.

no support found (searched: "province card", "face-down.*row", "province")

## legend-of-the-five-rings-the-card-game

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Wrong win condition: "Break 3 provinces" stated as victory (major)

Summary: "Province Break Victory: Break 3 of your opponent's 5 provinces (including their stronghold province, which is the final one)."

Source (lines 199–213):
> "There are three ways by which a player can win the game: break the province which holds their opponent's stronghold, accumulate 25 honor, or have their opponent run out of honor."
> "If three of a player's four non-stronghold provinces are broken, attacks may be declared against that player's stronghold province. As soon as a player's stronghold province is broken, that player loses the game."

Breaking 3 provinces unlocks access to the stronghold province — it does not win the game. You must break the stronghold province itself to win. The summary conflates the access condition with the win condition.

### Finding 2 — Setup: First player determined by honour bid (major, contradicted)

Summary Setup step 6: "Determine first player (bid honour, lowest wins first player; difference in bids paid in honour)."

Source setup step 3 (lines 283–288): "Randomly select a player to be the first player. Place the first player token in front of this player. The other player gains 1 fate from the general token pool."

The honour bidding mechanic is in the Draw Phase to determine card draw, not to determine first player. The summary entirely invents this first-player determination method for setup.

### Finding 3 — Fate Phase: Fate collection placed in wrong phase (major procedural error)

Summary Fate Phase: "Each player collects fate from their Stronghold."

Source Dynasty Phase, Step 2 (lines 394–396): "Each player collects fate equal to the fate value on their stronghold card."

Fate collection from the Stronghold occurs in the Dynasty Phase, not the Fate Phase. The Fate Phase only: discards characters with no fate, removes 1 fate from remaining characters, and adds fate to unclaimed rings.

### Finding 4 — Fate Phase: Ring return placed in wrong phase

Summary Fate Phase: "Discard unclaimed rings; return all claimed rings to unclaimed."

Source Regroup Phase, Step 3 (lines 978–979): "Return All Rings to the Unclaimed Ring Pool — All rings are returned simultaneously."

Ring return happens in the Regroup Phase, not the Fate Phase. Additionally, the summary says "discard unclaimed rings" but the source says ALL rings are returned — there is no discarding of unclaimed rings, and unclaimed rings receive fate tokens during the Fate Phase (source lines 922–926).

### Finding 5 — Attacker wins province break condition (minor, but checking)

Summary: "If attacking skill > defending skill, the attacker claims the ring (gaining its elemental effect) and may break the province if the difference exceeds the province's strength."

Source (lines 784–791): "If the attacking player won the conflict by an amount of skill equal to or greater than the province's strength... the province is broken."

The summary says "may break" and "difference exceeds the province's strength" — source says "equal to or greater than." Slightly weaker wording but the summary's "exceeds" could cause players to miss ties at exact province strength. This is a borderline finding; recording as informational only since the core direction is correct.

### Ring Effects Table Verification

Summary table vs. source (lines 761–772):
- Air: "Take 1 honour from opponent OR gain 2 honour" — Source: "takes 1 honor token from their opponent, or gains 2 honor." MATCH.
- Earth: "Draw 1 card; opponent discards 1" — Source: "draws 1 card from their conflict deck and discards 1 card at random from their opponent's hand." MATCH (source specifies "at random" — omitted in summary but not a material error).
- Fire: "Honour or dishonour a character" — Source: "chooses a character in play and honors or dishonors that character." MATCH.
- Water: "Ready a character you control or bow a character with no fate" — Source matches. MATCH.
- Void: "Remove 1 fate from a character" — Source: "chooses a character and removes 1 fate from that character." MATCH.
