### Findings

1. **(MAJOR — missing lose condition)** Summary lists exactly two lose conditions ("Lose Conditions (any one): Temperature... Communities in Crisis...", repeated in Player Reference). The source has **three**: "If you do not win by the end of Round 6, you all lose the game!" (l.846-847) and the Game End list "Players haven't won by the end of Round 6" (l.866-868). The 6-round limit is never mentioned anywhere in the summary. Players using the summary would not know the game ever ends in a loss by time — a core lose condition omitted.
2. **(MAJOR — wrong numbers)** Summary, Growth Stage: "Increase Energy Demand: Each player increases their Demand by 1." Source: "By default, the United States and Europe grow their demand by 1, China by 2, and the Majority World by 3. In the solo game, grow your demand by 2" (l.852-855). Wrong for half the World Powers; directly affects difficulty every round.
3. **(MAJOR — invented/contradicted table)** Player Reference table "Starting Crisis Cards by Temperature Bands" (0-1 → 3, 2-3 → 4, 4-5 → 5, 6-7 → 6) is not in the source (the values live on the board graphic) and contradicts the one data point the rulebook gives: "e.g. from 1.5° to 1.6° you go from drawing 3 to 4 Crisis cards" (l.650-651). Since 8 bands = 2.0°C (l.654-655) and the game starts at 1.2°C with 0 bands, 1.5° = 3 bands and 1.6° = 4 bands — i.e., 3 bands → 3 cards and 4 bands → 4 cards, not the summary's 3 bands → 4 / 4 bands → 5. Also confirmed by l.663-668 (1 band → still 3 Crisis cards, consistent only with row "0-1 → 3").
4. **(MINOR — wrong threshold)** Drawdown stated as "total Carbon produced ≤ total sequestered". Source: Drawdown requires 0 cubes left in Recent Emissions **and** "there are remaining uncovered Trees, Oceans, and/or DAC tokens" (l.602-607), i.e. strictly less than sequestration capacity. An exact tie is not Drawdown. The summary also blurs the "flip the round marker / survive one final Crisis stage" sequencing (l.616-618, 860-863), though placing the victory check in the Growth stage preserves the net effect.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — omitted round-limit lose condition, wrong Energy Demand growth numbers, and an invented Crisis-card table contradicted by the source.

### Findings

1. **(MINOR — omission)** Friar/Cardinal tile benefits are an entire reference subsystem the summary skips: each of the 5 Friar and 5 Cardinal tiles grants distinct powers (l.1005-1096), including end-game VP adjustments listed in the rulebook's scoring section — Friar Raffaele +4, Friar Gigi −4, Cardinal Lanzuisi +6, Cardinal Shlasinger +4 (l.952-957). The summary mentions Cardinal Muret's 6 AP but omits these scoring effects and, notably, that **Cardinal Muret costs 70 Ducati instead of 40** (l.809-810, 1072-1074) — a player choosing Muret from the summary would underpay by 30 Ducati.
2. **(trivial)** Scoring section says the game "ends when the second red Papal Event tile is revealed (turns 12-16)". Papal tiles occupy spaces 12-16, but the second red tile can be revealed at earliest on turn 13; the source (and the summary's own overview) says the game lasts 13-16 turns (l.238-239).
3. **(trivial)** Special Rules says "Cardinals cannot take the Orient action"; source says "Friars and Cardinals cannot" (l.530). The Actions table's "(Merchants only)" is correct, so no practical impact.
4. **(trivial)** "Papal Library: Draw and keep Papal Library tiles at numbered positions" — source: draw 1/2/3 tiles per the position, keep only **1**, return the rest, and remove your marker from the track (l.577-584). The keep-1 limit is implied elsewhere ("You may swap a new tile for a previously kept one") but not stated.

**Verdict: MINOR** — all spot-checked numbers (30+) match; issues are limited to the character-tile benefit/cost omissions and small wording drift.
