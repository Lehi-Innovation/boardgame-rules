## commands-colors-ancients — MAJOR

The structure, movement table, dice symbols, and turn sequence are faithful, but the summary states the retreat rule — a core combat mechanic — incorrectly, plus several smaller contradictions.

### Findings

1. **[MAJOR] Wrong retreat distance per flag.**
   - Summary (Combat): "Flag = force target to retreat 1 hex"
   - Source: "For each flag rolled against the target unit, the unit must move one movement back toward its own side of the battlefield. A movement is the maximum number of hexes a unit may move when given an order. Exceptions: Warriors retreat 2 hexes. Elephants and auxilia retreat 1 hex." and the explicit example: "a light horse unit incurring one flag against it would retreat its maximum move of 4 hexes. If two flags were rolled against it, the light horse unit would have to retreat 8 hexes!"
   - Retreat distance drives block losses from blocked retreats; stating "1 hex" would substantially mislead at the table.

2. **[MINOR] Retreat into impassable terrain / off board.**
   - Summary: "Retreating into impassable terrain or off the board eliminates the unit."
   - Source: "one block must be removed from the unit for each hex of the mandated retreat movement that the unit cannot fulfill" — the unit loses one block per unfulfilled hex, it is not automatically eliminated.

3. **[MINOR] War machine "fires instead" after moving.**
   - Summary movement table: "War Machine | 1 | No (fires instead)"
   - Source: "Heavy War machine units may move one hex but may not battle when they move" and "A Heavy war machine unit that moves cannot use Ranged Combat." A war machine that moves cannot fire at all; "(fires instead)" implies the opposite.

4. **[MINOR] Victory banner target "typically 5-7".**
   - Source: "a set number of Victory Banners (usually 5 to 8)". Small drift (5-7 vs 5-8).

5. **[MINOR] "Leaders ... can absorb hits."**
   - Source has no hit-absorption: leaders trigger leader casualty checks when their unit takes hits, allow helmet symbols to score hits for attached/adjacent units, and let an attached unit ignore one flag (Bolster Morale). "Absorb hits" is unsupported.

6. **[MINOR] "Elephants can rampage when hit."**
   - Source: rampage occurs when an elephant retreats (flags rolled against it): "An elephant, when it retreats, goes on a Rampage before it is moved back." Not when hit.

7. **[MINOR] Omissions:** Momentum Advance / bonus Close Combat and the support / Bolster Morale system (disregarding flags when supported by two friendly units, attached leader, terrain) are absent from the summary. These interact directly with the (already wrong) retreat rule.

## commonspoly — MAJOR

The summary reads like a generic description of "a cooperative anti-Monopoly game" and matches the actual Game Master's Guide poorly: the victory condition is contradicted, the resource system and several components/actions are invented, and most core systems are missing.

### Findings

1. **[MAJOR] Victory condition contradicted.**
   - Summary: "Cooperative win: All properties are converted to community or public ownership before speculators take over."
   - Source: "This is how you win, by turning all goods common. If there is a mix of public and common goods, it's a draw. ... if there is even one private good you lose!" — public+common mix is a DRAW, not a win; only all-commons wins.

2. **[MAJOR] Loss condition / time limit wrong and missing.**
   - Summary: "Loss: Speculators gain control of too many properties or players run out of key resources."
   - Source: the game lasts exactly 20 rounds ("You have 20 rounds ... This is a race against time!"); you lose if any private good remains. Speculators do not acquire properties; they deduct Welfare Points, send players to the Detention Centre, or trigger the Tragedy of the Commons. The 20-round limit is absent from the summary.

3. **[MAJOR] Invented resource system.**
   - Summary lists "Resource tokens (time, wellbeing, money)" and a Player Reference table with Time / Wellbeing / Money.
   - Source resources are Welfare Points, Legitimacy Points, and per-character Skill Points (Caring/Occupying/Sharing/Regenerating). There is no "time" resource and no "money" in the source.

4. **[MAJOR] Invented components and setup.**
   - Summary: "Property cards", "Distribute starting property cards".
   - Source component list: board, 1 dice, 16 Player Sheets, 1 Game Master Sheet, 40 Global Cards, 50 Situation Cards, 8 Player tokens, 3 Speculator tokens, Goods tokens (37 each of black/grey/white), Welfare and Legitimacy coins. No property cards exist; setup is dice-rolled goods statuses (1-2 Private, 3-4 Public, 5-6 Commons) and random character allocation with 5 Welfare + 5 Legitimacy points each — none of which the summary mentions.

5. **[MAJOR] Speculators described as "an automated threat".**
   - Summary: "Speculators are an automated threat that advances based on player actions and events."
   - Source: the Speculator is a player role ("one person will have to play the role of The Speculator(s)", suggested to be the Game Master), rolls dice and moves like other players, and takes the last turn each round. Not automated.

6. **[MAJOR] Missing core systems:** Game Master role; the 10-point unlock mechanic (Skill Points + Welfare Points, private→public→commons, one step at a time); Central Agora / Collective Uprising Action (10 Legitimacy Points per player to commonalise an entire goods category); game modes (Mad Max / Transition / Commonsland from a 4-24 dice total, setting 1-3 Speculator tokens); special spaces (Assembly, Bureaucracy, Tragedy of the Commons, Crowdfunding, Mortgage, Detention Centre, Casino); Global Card read before each round; max two players per space.

7. **[MINOR] Invented actions.** "Invest in wellbeing" and a general "Trade and negotiate: Exchange resources with other players" have no basis in the source (closest is the Crowdfunding space and collectively paying to free a detained player).

8. **[MINOR] Player count.** Frontmatter "3-6"; source: "Ideally, Commonspoly is suitable for 6 to 8 players (including the Game Master). In theory, it can be played with as few as 4."

## conan — MAJOR

The asymmetric structure, gem-economy concept, Book of Skelos river idea, hindering, and dice colors are broadly right, but the summary contradicts the movement-cost rule and omits the stance/recovery system that governs the entire hero turn.

### Findings

1. **[MAJOR] Movement cost contradicted — free base movement omitted.**
   - Summary (Actions): "Move | Spend gems to move through areas (1 gem per area, more for difficult terrain)"
   - Source: "All characters have a base movement value... At the start of the Action phase, an aggressive hero gains a number of movement points equal to their base movement value. These movement points are free; they do not require the hero to assign gems to the Move action." Gems are only assigned for extra movement points (1 gem = 1 movement point). Telling players each area costs a gem would drastically change play.

2. **[MAJOR] Stance phase (and the heroes' four-phase turn) omitted; recovery quantities missing.**
   - Summary: "Gems spent go to a 'fatigue' zone and must be rested to recover" — there is no rest action.
   - Source: heroes' turn = Start Phase, Stance Phase, Action Phase, End Phase. In the Stance Phase each hero chooses aggressive (recover 2 gems; 3 if one hero dead; 4 if two+ dead; may take any action) or cautious (recover 5/6/7 gems; may ONLY Guard and Reroll). This recovery trade-off is the core resource loop and is entirely absent.

3. **[MINOR] Overlord activation limit omitted; river details unverifiable.**
   - Source: "On their turn, the Overlord may activate 0, 1, or 2 tiles in the River" — the 2-tile cap is not in the summary. The summary's claims about sliding tiles and far-end tiles being cheaper are consistent with the published game but are not in the extracted text (only the Heroes' Book was extracted; it defers to the Overlord's Book). Not counted as invented, but unverifiable from this source.

4. **[MINOR] Guard described as "defensive rerolls".**
   - Summary (Actions table): "Guard | Reserve gems for defensive rerolls during enemy attacks."
   - Source: Guard assigns gems to roll defense dice (plus parry/dodge equipment); it is not a reroll. (The summary's own Special Rules section states it correctly as "add defense dice", so the table entry is internally inconsistent.)

5. **[MINOR] Defense dice "shields".**
   - Summary: "Each hit symbol on attack dice minus each shield on defense dice = wounds."
   - Source: both attack and defense use the same axe-symbol dice; Damage = Attack Power − Defense Power (Guard + Armor). Net arithmetic is right; "shield" symbols don't exist.

6. **[MINOR] "Modular game boards" / "Assemble the game board".**
   - Source: "Double-sided Board x2" — boards are fixed double-sided maps chosen per scenario, not assembled/modular.

7. **[MINOR] Designer frontmatter.** "Frédéric Henry, Pascal Bernard, Antoine Bauza" — source credits "Game Designer: Frédéric Henry" (Pascal Bernard appears only among twelve scenario designers; Antoine Bauza does not appear at all).

8. Verified correct: dice color hierarchy (yellow weakest, then orange, then red — matches "yellow is the least likely to roll successes, followed by orange, and finally red"); minions "typically have 1 life point" (source: "Other characters controlled by the Overlord (Pict Hunters, Pirates, etc.) have 1 life point"); hindering costs extra movement; wound zone filling = hero death; exertion limits implied by "more gems = more dice"; turn-limited scenarios (turn track 0-15).
