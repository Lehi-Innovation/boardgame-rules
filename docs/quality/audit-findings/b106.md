# Audit Findings — Batch b106

## gloomhaven

**Verdict: MAJOR**

---

### Finding 1 — Summon Initiative Order (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Summon: Place a summon token with its own stats; it acts at the end of your turn."

**Source says (lines 1325–1326):**
> "A summon's turn in the initiative order is always directly before the character who summoned it, and is separate from that character's turn."

**Also from source (line 1334):**
> "Summoned figures never take a turn in the round they are summoned."

The summary is wrong in two ways: (1) summons act BEFORE the summoner's turn, not after it; (2) summons don't act the round they are summoned. A player relying on the summary would expect their summon to act immediately after them on the same round — the opposite of the actual rule.

---

### Finding 2 — Heal + POISON Interaction (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Heal: Remove damage and POISON/WOUND from target."

**Source says (lines 1172–1174):**
> "POISON – If a figure is poisoned, all enemies add +1 Attack to all of their attacks targeting the figure. If a Heal ability is used on a poisoned figure, the POISON token is removed, and the Heal has no other effect."

When a Heal is applied to a POISONED figure, the POISON is removed but NO hit points are restored. The summary implies a single Heal can simultaneously restore HP and remove conditions from a poisoned target, which contradicts the source. Players using the summary would incorrectly believe they can both cure POISON and restore HP with one heal action.

(Note: For WOUND the Heal does "continue normally" per source line 1176, which is correctly implied by the summary for WOUND.)

---

### Finding 3 — Exhaustion Condition Incomplete (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Exhaustion: A character is exhausted when they have no cards in hand or discard to play/rest."

**Source says (lines 1433–1438):**
> "A character can become exhausted in one of two ways: If a character ever drops below one hit point on the hit point tracker, or… If, at the beginning of a round, a player cannot play two cards from his or her hand (because they have one card or no cards in his or her hand) and also cannot rest (because they have one card or no cards in their discard pile)."

The summary omits the HP-based exhaustion path entirely. A character that drops below 1 HP is immediately exhausted — this is a core rule that determines when you lose the scenario. Omitting it is a MAJOR error. Additionally, the summary says "no cards" but the source specifies that having even ONE card in hand (not two) also triggers the card-based exhaustion path.
