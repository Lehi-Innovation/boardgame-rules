1. **MINOR — "may" vs "must" on the compulsory turn.** Summary: "If you can legally move one of your own workmen, you must do so. ## arborea — MAJOR

Source: full Arborea rulebook (~460 lines), good extraction including setup, turn steps, sages, creature scoring, and season tiles.

### Numbers checked (12 checked, 12 supported)
| Claim in summary | Source evidence |
|---|---|
| 8 pilgrimage track tiles, 2 per track | "place the 8 pilgrimage track tiles on the 4 tracks... 2 of them on the outermost spaces" |
| 6 borderland tiles | "Randomly place 6 of the borderland tiles" |
| 4 season tiles | "Randomly place 4 of the season tiles" |
| 8 gift cubes | "Place the 8 gift cubes on their designated space" |
| 2 elders, 3 young, 3 veterans per player | "the 2 elder villagers... and a young villager... the other 2 young... and the 3 veteran villagers" |
| 3 spirit for second pilgrimage option | "you may spend 3 spirit... to choose one of those options again" |
| Activate up to 3; 3rd costs 2 spirit | "activate up to 3... you must spend 2 spirit if you want to activate a third one" |
| Biome reserve max 8 | "If a biome tile reaches the '8' spot, it can't be increased anymore" |
| Max 3 current ecosystem tasks | "You can only have up to 3 current ecosystem tasks" |
| 2 extra turns after sun counter hits last spot | "all players play two more turns" |
| Inner tile swaps after 4 total track steps | "Once a track has moved 4 total steps... place it covering the first 4 spaces" |
| 6 biome types (tree, flower, mushroom, weed, sponge, water) | actions list enumerates exactly these |

### Findings
1. **MAJOR — invented villager-type abilities.** Summary: "Veteran villagers: Gain an extra reward when activated. Elder villagers: Gain powerful extra rewards." Neither claim exists in the source, and the elder claim is contradicted: the only reward-related difference is that the bottommost sage reward "can only [be chosen] if a young or a veteran villager is doing the action, **but not if it's an elder villager**." The real type differences — elders return to your supply after a trail while young/veterans return to reserves and must be retrained, and veterans advance their track 2 steps (vs 1) in turn step 4 — are entirely absent.
2. **MAJOR — wrong tiebreaker.** Summary: "Tiebreaker: player furthest on the spirit track." Source: tie broken in order by (1) most creatures on ecosystem, (2) most spirit, (3) most season-track steps, (4) shared victory.
3. **MAJOR — sage/gift system missing.** "Gift a Sage" (place a gift on a sage's '1' spot or advance it toward '3') and "Sage Rewards" (gain up to gift-value rewards, no repeats per villager, special bottom reward) are a core trail-reward system; the summary never describes them. Gift cubes appear only in components and as a vague scoring line ("RP from gift cubes spent during the game") — which itself misstates scoring: gifts only score via a specific season tile or the Wiseowl creature.
4. **MAJOR — turn step 4 track advancement rule missing.** Source: "For each of your elder or young villagers on a pilgrimage track, you must advance that track one step. For each of your veteran villagers... two steps." Summary reduces step 4 to "advance pilgrimage tracks" with no rule for which tracks or how far — players could not execute the turn.
5. **Omission — water action cost and wild-water rule.** "If you are doing the water action, you must first spend between 2 and 6 spirit" (with the all-villagers-stuck elder release) and water acting as a wild biome (only once the substituted biome is at 0) are both absent.
6. **Omission — biome reserve update RP.** Moving bottom halves up in step 4 awards RP for each spot moved through — an in-game scoring source the summary never mentions.
7. **Omission — creature placement and captivity.** Placement on intersections of 4 habitats, no orthogonal adjacency between creatures, captivity sliding, the place-one-from-captivity-when-inviting rule, and the -3 spirit per captive creature at game end are all missing; ditto ecosystem-card placement rules and the water-adjacency RP doubling for creatures.
8. **Garbled — season scoring.** Source: season tile value (condition count, capped at 8 RP) × your position on that season track. Summary splits this into two vague bullets ("RP from season tracks (positions reached)" and "Bonus RP from season tiles"), losing the multiplication and the 8 RP cap.
9. Minor: staggered spirit-track start (0/2/4...) by seat order omitted from setup.

**Verdict: MAJOR**

## archipelago — MAJOR

Source: full Ludically rulebook (~1,700 lines); heavy OCR damage to headers/card text, but all phase structure, actions, and scoring rules are legible. The summary appears to be written largely from general knowledge rather than this source.

### Numbers checked (5 checked, 1 supported, 4 wrong/unsupported — the summary contains almost no numbers, itself a red flag)
| Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|
| 2–5 players | action wheel sides for "2-4 players" and "5 players" | Yes |
| Resources: wood, stone, iron, cattle, fruit, **spices** | Source resources: "Exotic fruit, Wood, Cattle, Stone, **Fish**, Iron" — no spices; fish missing | **No** |
| "Crisis cards" component | No such component; crises are printed on the **backs of evolution cards** | **No** |
| "Player boards" (per player) | Players get a **screen aid**, ship, 2 citizens, 3 action discs, order marker, 10ƒ; boards I–IV are shared | **No** |
| "Rebellion track... reaches its maximum" | No maximum: "the rebellion marker moves **past the population marker**" (relative threshold) | **No** |

### Findings
1. **MAJOR — invented turn structure.** Summary phases: 1. Balance of Power (turn order), 2. Action Phase, 3. Crisis Phase, 4. Market Phase ("adjust market prices"), 5. Check End Conditions. Source: "Each turn is divided into **6 phases**: 1. Disengagement, 2. Order of play, 3. Population effects, 4. Balance of the archipelago [crisis], 5. Actions, 6. Evolution card purchase." The crisis comes BEFORE actions, not after; there is no market-price-adjustment phase and no end-check phase; Disengagement, Population effects, and Evolution card purchase are missing entirely.
2. **MAJOR — core action-disc/action-wheel system missing.** Each player has 3 action discs (growing to 4 then 5 as explorer token piles empty), placed round-by-round on an action wheel with limited zones (taxes, reproduction, exploration) and unlimited zones. The summary never mentions discs, the wheel, rounds, or these limits — players could not take a turn from it.
3. **MAJOR — wrong scoring mechanism.** Summary: "Each player's secret objective determines which accomplishments score them points." Source: "every player is ranked against the victory conditions of **all objective cards in play, including those of the other players**" (3/2/1 VP for top three), plus the trend card (2–4 VP) and VP from character/progress cards and built wonders. The shared-scoring twist is the game's signature rule and is inverted.
4. **MAJOR — invented crisis requirements.** Summary: "Each crisis has requirements (food, shelter, military, etc.)." Source crises demand one specific **resource type** for domestic consumption (each cube stands up N citizens, who were all laid on their backs) or N cubes of a resource for export; unmet needs create rebel citizens / raise rebellion. "Food, shelter, military" has no basis, and the citizens-laid-down/rebel-citizen mechanic is absent.
5. **MAJOR — wrong end-game triggers.** Summary lists "The evolution deck runs out" as a trigger — unsupported. Source: the game ends when any player's secret end-of-game condition is met (revealed by that player), or immediately on independence. Also wrong: "Variable Game Length... change the number of evolution cards used" — length is set by which 10-card objective deck (short/medium/long) is used.
6. **Wrong terminology/roles.** Source roles are the **Separatist** (wins alone on independence) and the **Pacifist** (scores 3VP if the population/rebellion gap is achieved). The summary's "sympathizer" approximates the Separatist; the Pacifist is missing.
7. **Component errors beyond resources:** no "natives" meeples (surplus workers are an abstract track), no "Markets and town tiles" as described (13 market/port + 13 town/temple double-sided tokens), and the list omits the action wheel, 51 florins, 24 explorer tokens, 25 region hexagons, 48 evolution cards, and player screens.
8. **Omissions:** turn #0 initial exploration (3 hexagons each, landscape continuity, 2 citizens disembark), secret simultaneous florin bid for turn order (highest bidder sets the whole order), explorer-token-to-resource conversion, engaged units, towns controlling regions, temples standing citizens up, tie-breaker (most money), and 2-player rules.

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Findings
- No invented mechanics or contradictions found. **Verdict: PASS**
