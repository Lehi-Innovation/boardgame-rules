### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Campaign victory condition misrepresented.** Summary (Scoring): "Players win by surviving and accomplishing objectives"; Special Rules: "Each character has a personal goal that provides bonus rewards when completed." Source: "To win the campaign players must complete the personal goal of at least 4 characters." Personal goals are not bonus rewards — they are the campaign win condition. A player using the summary would not know how to win the campaign.

2. **MAJOR — Campaign loss condition omitted.** Source: "If the [food] or [morale] dials on the campaign dashboard ever reach 0, the campaign has been lost" (dial icons stripped by OCR, but two specific dashboard dials reaching 0 = campaign loss is explicit; Infinite Lives mode resets those two dials to 5). The summary's only loss conditions are "all characters are defeated or mission-specific failure conditions" — the dashboard-dial campaign loss is entirely missing.

3. **MAJOR — Turn structure invented/contradicted; threat system omitted.** Summary: "After all players have acted, enemies activate according to their behavior patterns." Source: a player's turn is 5 fixed steps (Draw to 5 cards, Resolve Calamity, Place Threat, Perform Actions, Check Threat), and an enemy turn triggers during EACH player's Check Threat step "if the number of threat cards equals or exceeds the number of enemies on the threat track" (hostile) — not after a full player round. The threat track / threat (black) card system and the Calamity card mechanic — core pacing systems — are absent from the summary.

4. **MINOR — Skill test dice slightly misdescribed.** Summary: "Skill tests and combat use the 2 resolution dice. Players roll and compare results to difficulty thresholds." Source: regular tests roll only the white die (added to played card values + bonuses); only opposed tests also roll the black die, which is added to the difficulty. Also omits that card values are the main input.

5. **MINOR — "Rest: Recover from wounds."** Source's Recover action removes status effects ("attempt to recover from one of those [negative status] effects"); wounds heal via Nesting by spending food ("reduce [food] by 1 to remove 1 normal wound"). Conflated.

6. **MINOR — "Enemy behavior is deterministic based on enemy cards."** Source: the enemy's action each activation is selected by the random value on the threat card beneath it ("Check the value on the threat card under that enemy's card and match it to an action"). Card-driven, not deterministic.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — components and overview are accurate, but the campaign win/loss conditions are wrong/missing and the turn/threat structure is misstated.

## age-of-empires-iii-the-age-of-discovery — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Numbers checked (12 checked, 11 supported, 1 wrong)

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Wrong age boundaries.** Player Reference table: "II | 4-5" and "III | 6-8". Source: Age II is turns 4-6 and Age III is turns 7-8. This shifts building availability/costs and the Age II colony scoring round.

2. **MAJOR — End-of-age colony scoring omitted; scoring values missing.** Summary: "After 8 rounds, score: ... Colonies in regions | VP based on majority." Source: "New World colonies score victory points at the end of Age I (end of turn 3), Age II (end of turn 6) and Age III (end of turn 8)" — colonies score three times, with explicit values: 1st place 6 VP, 2nd place 2 VP, two-way tie for 1st = 2 VP each, ties for 2nd = 0. The summary's "score after 8 rounds" framing contradicts the interim scoring, and no VP values are given.

3. **MAJOR — Invented scoring sources.** Summary scoring table lists "Merchant fleet | VP from ships" and "Money | VP conversion" as general end-game scoring. Source end-game scoring is only: Discoveries, Colonies, Capital Buildings, and Economy ("victory points equal to the amount of Spanish Dollars that their trade goods and merchant ships generated at the end of turn 8"). Ships and money grant VP only via specific capital buildings (Navy: "4 VP per Merchant Ship"; Wealth: "1 VP per $5"), not as standing rules.

4. **MAJOR — Invented specialist abilities.** Summary: Captain "+1 VP per discovery; naval combat bonus"; Merchant "Extra income from trade goods". Source: Captain "counts as two (2) colonists in the Merchant Shipping event box or the Discovery event box" — no VP bonus, no naval combat (there is no naval combat in the game). Merchant "counts as two (2) colonists in the Merchant Shipping event box" and "generates five Spanish Dollars ($5) ... upon arrival in a New World colony" via the Colonist Dock — not income from trade goods.

5. **MAJOR — Event boxes mislabeled/conflated.** Summary: "Colonist Dock: Gain additional colonists/specialists from your unused supply" and a separate "Colonize" box ("Place colonists in discovered regions"). Source: the Colonist Dock is the box that moves placed colonists "to a discovered region in the New World"; gaining specialists is the Specialist event box (omitted by name in the summary); there is no "Colonize" event box. The summary also misstates Trade Goods ("Acquire trade goods from discovered regions" — actually chosen from a pool of 4 drawn goods; region goods are gained by being first to 3 colonists in a region) and Merchant Shipping ("Place merchant ships for trade income" — actually colonists compete by placement value; the highest total gains 1 ship).

6. **MINOR — Resolution order.** Summary says event boxes resolve "left to right"; source: "from top to bottom (Initiative first, Colonist Dock second, ...)". Same idea, wrong axis/no order given.

7. **MINOR — Warfare misdescribed.** "Warfare can displace opponents' colonists" — source: "each soldier eliminates one enemy unit"; also a full-scale War costs $10 (cost omitted). Capital building costs ($10/$14/$20 by age) also omitted.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong age rounds, invented specialist abilities and scoring sources, missing per-age colony scoring (6/2 VP), and a mangled event-box list.

## age-of-napoleon — VERDICT: MAJOR

### Numbers checked (summary contains few numbers; all checked)

| Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK? |
|---|---|---|
| 2 players | "Age of Napoleon 1805-1815 is a two-player game" | Yes |
| Covers 1805 to 1815 | title/turn track "from 1805 to 1815" | Yes |
| 3 scenarios: 1805, 1809, 1813 | "three different scenarios, starting in 1805 (the main scenario), 1809, and 1813" | Yes |
| 6 turn phases | Source Sequence of Play has 8 phases (Diplomacy, Insurrection, Strategy, Reinforcement, Campaign, Surrender, Winter Attrition, Victory Check) | **No** |
| play_time 180-360 min (frontmatter) | Not in source (source: experienced players finish the 1813 scenario in 45-60 min); presumably BGG metadata | n/a |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Entire economic system invented.** Summary: Components list "Economic/treasury markers"; Setup: "Initialize treasury levels for both sides"; Turn Structure: "Economic Phase: Collect income and manage treasury"; Actions: "Income depends on controlled territories and trade routes. Players spend money on armies, navies, and diplomatic efforts." The source contains no money, income, treasury, or purchasing of any kind. Components are: "1 gameboard, 1 rules booklet, 55 playing cards, 1 scenarios booklet, 162 game pieces, 2 dice, 2 player aid cards." Reinforcements are free, limited by Mobilization/Deployment charts (10.2.2), and actions are paid for with cards, not money.

2. **MAJOR — Card-driven core omitted.** The summary never mentions the 55-card deck, the central engine of the game: cards drive diplomacy ("Each of the following 'diplomacy' cards allows... Napoleon I, Talleyrand, English Gold, Pitt, Metternich and Alexander I"), insurrections, army moves and interceptions ("Every army move or interception requires the moving player to discard one card"), rallying spent corps, and events; hand size is 6-10 cards drawn in the Strategy Phase based on controlled countries.

3. **MAJOR — Turn structure wrong.** Summary's 6 phases (Diplomacy, Economic, Reinforcement, Movement, Combat, Political) vs source's 8 (Diplomacy, Insurrection, Strategy, Reinforcement, Campaign [6 bi-monthly rounds of movement+battle], Surrender, Winter Attrition, Victory Check). The Economic Phase is invented; Insurrection, Strategy, Surrender, and Winter Attrition phases are missing entirely — insurrection and surrender are core diplomatic-state systems (sections 8.0 and 14.0).

4. **MAJOR — Victory conditions misstated.** Summary: "Victory conditions vary by scenario" with per-scenario objectives. Source 6.0 gives one set of objectives for all scenarios: Marginal/Decisive French Victory (control/occupy countries adjacent to France plus Russia and/or Österreich/Preußen...), Marginal Coalition Victory ("France controls France and no more than three minor countries"), Decisive Coalition Victory ("France surrenders or Napoléon's corps is permanently eliminated"), and the tie-breaker "If at the end of the 1815 game turn neither side has met the victory conditions... the French player wins." None of these specifics appear in the summary, and the per-scenario variation claim is unsupported.

5. **MINOR — Invented supply-line and trade claims.** Special Rules: "Supply lines are critical for maintaining armies in the field" and "Sea power affects both trade income and the ability to project force." Source has no supply lines (losses come from march attrition 12.3 and winter attrition 15.0) and no trade income; sea movement exists only for the Coalition once Britannia is in effect (12.4.1, 12.4.4: "The French player may never move any corps by sea" — a notable asymmetry the summary misses).

6. **MINOR — Battle resolution loosely described.** "Battles resolved using unit strengths, terrain, and leadership bonuses" — source: battle strength = sum of engaged corps' Battle Ratings, modified +1 per corps fighting at home, -2 per corps attacking off a sea move, then a die roll on the Battle Results Table. Terrain affects attrition, not battle strength. Also omits the corps fresh/spent system and seniority-rating army-size limits (4.1.3) — but these follow from the larger structural problems above.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the summary describes a money/treasury wargame that does not match the actual card-driven game; invented economic phase/components, missing card system and four phases, and unsupported per-scenario victory conditions.
