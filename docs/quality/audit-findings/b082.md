## dungeons-dragons-the-legend-of-drizzt-board-game

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Poisoned condition effect is wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Poisoned: Speed reduced."

**Source (lines 701–704):** "While Poisoned, your Hero takes 1 damage at the beginning of your Hero Phase. Take this damage before using any Treasure cards and before checking to see if your Hero must spend a Healing Surge."

"Speed reduced" is the Immobilized condition, not Poisoned. Source (lines 685–686): "While your Hero is Immobilized, his or her Speed is reduced to 0—that Hero can't move!" No source text anywhere links Poisoned to a speed reduction.

### Finding 2 — Level-up trigger omitted (MINOR)

**Summary:** "When the group accumulates enough XP, Heroes level up (flip Hero card to level 2)."

**Source (lines 1139–1141):** "A Hero starts play at 1st level. Whenever a Hero rolls a natural 20 when making an attack roll or a disable trap roll, that Hero can choose to spend 5 Experience Points to become 2nd level!"

The summary omits the required natural-20 trigger. It implies mere accumulation of XP causes leveling up, which is incorrect. Players cannot level up whenever they want; they must first roll a natural 20.

## dust

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Production formula critically wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Production capacity = card's production value + number of production centers controlled on power source-adjacent areas (details vary)."

**Source (lines 624–633):**
> "Capitals: For each capital a player controls, he receives six production points."
> "PP = (3 x production centers*) + 6 + card value  
> * limited by power sources"

The summary completely omits capitals as a production source (6 PP per capital controlled). This is a large and consistent component of every player's production budget. The description of how production centers are counted is also wrong: the source says each production center gives 3 PP, but the total is capped at 3 × (number of power sources controlled); the summary describes it as "production centers controlled on power source-adjacent areas" which is a different and incorrect concept.

### Finding 2 — Victory condition missing capital-control requirement (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Win: Reach the VP threshold (varies by player count)."

**Source (lines 1060–1066):** "If, at the end of a given victory point phase, any player exceeds this threshold, that player wins the game if he controls at least one capital. If more than one player exceeds the threshold and controls at least one capital, the player among them with the highest total number of victory points wins."

The summary omits the requirement to control at least one capital to win. A player who exceeds the VP threshold but controls no capitals does not win.

### Finding 3 — VP thresholds unverifiable (NOTE, not rated separately)

The summary gives per-player-count VP thresholds (2p:45, 3p:35, 4p:30, 5p:25, 6p:20). The source's Victory table shows player-count ranges "3–4" and "5–6" but the actual VP numbers were lost to OCR. The "2-player" threshold is not visible in the extracted text at all. These numbers cannot be confirmed or denied from the extracted source; they are flagged as unverifiable, not as a confirmed error.

### Numbers checked: 8, Supported: 6

Verified: 60 tanks / 20 mechs / 20 fighters / 15 bombers / 15 submarines per army, 24 production centers, 16 power source tokens, 6 capitals, 3 majority tokens, 45-card deck, unit costs (tank 2, mech 5, fighter 3, bomber 6, sub 4, production center 6), 3 PP per production center (limited by power sources), 5 bonus dice for capital defense, 3 bonus dice for production center defense. NOT supported: capital component of production formula in summary.
