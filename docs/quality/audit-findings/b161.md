# Audit Findings — Batch b161

## pax-romana

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented Turn Structure (MAJOR)

Summary describes a 5-phase turn:
> "1. Political Phase … 2. Strategy Card Phase … 3. Operations Phase … 4. Revenue Phase … 5. Attrition and Supply Phase"

Source (3.2) shows 8 phases:
> "A. Income Phase … B. Maintenance Phase … C. Removal Phase … D. Leader Selection Phase … E. Manpower Phase … F. Activation Phase … G. Attrition and Isolation Phase … H. Victory Phase."

There is no "Political Phase", no "Strategy Card Phase", and no "Revenue Phase" in the source. The actual turn structure is completely misrepresented.

### Finding 2 — Invented Card Mechanic: "Operations Points (OPs) used to activate areas" (MAJOR)

Summary (Strategy Card Phase):
> "Cards have Operations Points (OPs) used to activate areas."

Source: No mention of "Operations Points" or "OPs" on cards anywhere in the text. Cards are Event Cards (Advanced Game only), drawn during the Card Segment of the Activation Phase. They trigger historical events or special effects; they do NOT have Operations Points or activate areas.

Searched: "Operations Points", "OPs", "card.*activate.*area", "activat.*area" — no matches found.

### Finding 3 — Invented "Colonization" Mechanic (MAJOR)

Summary (Actions — Political Actions):
> "Colonization: Establish colonies to extend territorial control and generate revenue."

Summary (Strategy Card Phase):
> "Players play strategy cards to activate their forces for movement, combat, colonization, or to trigger historical events."

Source: The word "coloni" does not appear anywhere in the extracted text. There is no colonization mechanic in Pax Romana. Searched: "coloni", "Colony", "colonies", "Colonization" — no matches found.

### Finding 4 — Invented VP Sources: "Winning major battles" and "Political and cultural achievements" (MAJOR)

Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions):
> "Victory is determined by Victory Points accumulated through: … Winning major battles … Political and cultural achievements"

Source (15.11): VP is earned from exactly three sources:
> "• The number of VP resulting from the GOP standings (15.2) plus • The number of VP resulting from the CVP standings (15.3) plus • The number of VP resulting from achieving Opportunity Objectives (15.4, only certain scenarios)"

"Winning major battles" and "Political and cultural achievements" are not VP sources anywhere in the source. Searched: "winning major battles", "battle.*VP", "political.*achievement", "cultural.*achievement" — no matches found.

### Finding 5 — Wrong Game End Date (MAJOR)

Summary (Overview):
> "covering the political and military history of the ancient Mediterranean from 300 BC to 30 BC"

Source (1.0):
> "Pax Romana is a game covering the struggle for power in the Mediterranean from c. 300 BC to 50 BC"

The end date is wrong: summary says 30 BC, source says 50 BC.

### Finding 6 — Wrong Turn Time Scale (MINOR)

Summary (Turn Structure):
> "Each game turn represents approximately 20 years in the full campaign."

Source (1.3):
> "Turns are 'generational', roughly 25 years of 'real' time."

25 years vs. 20 years.

### Finding 7 — Invented Player Reference Table for Cards (MAJOR)

Summary (Player Reference):
> | Card Use | Effect |
> | Operations | Activate forces for movement/combat |
> | Event | Trigger historical event |
> | Political | Spend influence for diplomacy/colonization |

Source (17.12): Cards are only of two types: "Play When Drawn" and "Hold in Hand". There are no card categories called "Operations", "Event", or "Political" in this game, and "political influence" spending via cards is not a mechanic in the source. The entire card reference table is fabricated.

### Verification Quota

**Victory / game-end conditions:**
- Source confirms "The player with the most VP at game end wins" and "Determine if anyone has won an Automatic Victory (see Scenario rules)" — summary's general claim that most VP wins is supported. However, summary's VP sources list is wrong (see Finding 4).
- Summary omits that VP is accumulated each turn from ranked standings of GOP (7/4/2/0 VP) and CVP (5/3/1/0 VP) — these specific scoring tables are entirely absent from the summary.

**Every number in Scoring / Victory Conditions:**
- Summary states no specific numbers in scoring. Source has specific VP award scales (7/4/2/0 for GOP; 5/3/1/0 for CVP). The summary provides no numeric VP breakdown at all. This is a significant omission.

**At least 5 procedural claims from Turn Structure/Actions:**
1. "Political Phase" — not in source (invented). FAIL
2. "Strategy Card Phase" — not in source as a standalone phase (ADVANCED GAME ONLY, within Activation Phase). FAIL
3. "Operations Phase" — source calls it Activation Phase with Operations Segment inside. FAIL
4. "Revenue Phase: Collect income from controlled provinces and trade. Pay maintenance for armies and fleets." — Split across Income Phase (A) and Maintenance Phase (B), not one phase. PARTIALLY supported.
5. "Attrition and Supply Phase: Check supply status; unsupplied forces suffer losses." — Source has "G. Attrition and Isolation Phase" with attrition for isolated land forces and fleets not in port. SUPPORTED (with different name and scope).

**Terms of art in victory/scoring:**
- "Control" (GOP): Source (1.4): "Land Units, Garrisons, Towns, and Cities occupy (and control) the spaces they're in." Summary does not define control.
- GOP: Summary mentions "controlling provinces and key cities" as a VP source, which partially aligns with the GOP calculation (provinces) and CVP (cities). However, the summary conflates these and attributes VP to "controlling key cities" rather than the ranked CVP standings.

**Exhaustion rules:**
- No deck/bag/supply exhaustion mechanic described in summary. Source reshuffles Event Deck each Victory Phase. No exhaustion rule claimed in summary. Not applicable.
