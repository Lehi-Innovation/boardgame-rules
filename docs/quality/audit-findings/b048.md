## catan-histories-rise-of-the-inka — MAJOR

The summary inverts the scenario's central "decline" (thicket/overgrowth) rules and omits the per-tribe culture-point thresholds and all build costs.

### Findings

1. **CONTRADICTION — declining settlements produce nothing.** Summary: "Overgrown structures block the spaces they occupy but produce no resources for any player." Source (Consequences of Decline): "Production: Settlements/cities in decline still produce resource/goods cards for their owners when anyone rolls the number of an adjacent hex." Declining settlements DO produce. This reverses a core economic rule.
2. **CONTRADICTION — building over declining settlements.** Summary: "Overgrown settlements/cities still occupy their intersections and block new building." Source: "Rebuilding: You can build over any declining settlement/city (your own or an opponent's)... pay the necessary construction costs for a settlement and remove the declining settlement/city and replace it with a settlement of your own." Building over declining pieces is a key strategic mechanic, explicitly allowed.
3. **CONTRADICTION — roads past declining settlements.** Summary: "Adjacent players can still build roads past overgrown structures." Source: "Adjacent paths: You can build a road up to, but not past, a settlement/city in decline. You must first build over the declining settlement/city... before you can build past."
4. **ERROR — founding a new tribe.** Summary: "The player takes over a new tribe of the next era with new settlements and roads" / "places new starting settlements and roads." Source: place exactly "1 new settlement (the 'founding settlement')... for free", "You do not place a free road with a founding settlement", and "Your turn ends immediately."
5. **MAJOR OMISSION — per-tribe apex thresholds.** Summary only says "specific culture point threshold" and 11 total. Source: 1st and 2nd tribes reach apex at 4 culture points each; 3rd tribe wins at 3 culture points (4+4+3 = 11). These numbers are the win condition and are absent.
6. **OMISSION — all build costs.** Road = 1 wood + 1 stone; Settlement = 1 wood + 1 stone + 1 potato + 1 wool; City = 2 potato + 3 ore; Development card = 1 potato + 1 wool + 1 ore. None appear in the summary or Player Reference.
7. **ERROR — potato classified as a good.** Summary: "feathers, coca, fish, potato (goods)". Source: "Separate the 5 types of resource cards and 3 types of goods cards"; "You may also buy resources with goods: feathers, fish, and coca." Potato is a resource (used in settlement/city/dev-card costs). The summary is also internally inconsistent (its Trade section correctly lists goods as feathers, fish, coca).
8. **OMISSION — 1 city per tribe limit.** Source: "A player may only build 1 city for each tribe!" Not mentioned.
9. **OMISSION — robber placement restriction.** Source: robber "may not be placed on a hex that produces goods (so, not on a jungle hex, nor on a fishing ground hex)." Summary says robber "works as in standard Catan."

### Numbers checked

- 27 hexes — supported
- 8 hex/terrain types — supported
- 2 starting settlements + 2 roads — supported
- 2 starting culture points — supported
- 11 culture points to win — supported
- 2 dice — supported
- Hand-limit on 7 (8+ cards discard half, round in your favor) — summary omits but does not contradict
- 4/4/3 per-tribe thresholds — OMITTED
- Build costs (4 sets of numbers) — OMITTED
- Resource/goods split 4/4 — WRONG (source: 5 resources / 3 goods)

**Verdict: MAJOR** — three direct contradictions of the tribal-decline system (the scenario's signature mechanic), plus omitted apex thresholds and build costs.

## catan-junior — MINOR

Faithful on essentially every number and mechanic; one structural drift.

### Findings

1. **DRIFT — fixed phase order.** Summary presents the turn as numbered phases "1. Roll the Die... 3. Build and Buy... 4. Trade", implying building precedes trading. Source: "After rolling... you may build, buy and trade in any order." Small flexibility loss; could mildly mislead.
2. **MINOR OMISSION — advanced player trading is 1-for-1.** Summary: "Players may trade resources directly with each other on the active player's turn." Source: "All trades must be 1 resource for 1 resource." The exchange-ratio restriction is omitted.
3. Trivial: "Move the Ghost Captain to any island of your choice" vs source "any other island."

### Numbers checked (all supported)

- 7 pirates' lairs / 8 ships per player (28/32 across 4 colors is a fair derivation)
- 2 starting lairs + 2 starting ships; 5 lairs + 6 ships in supply
- 5 resource types (cutlasses, goats, wood, gold, molasses)
- Starting resources: 1 wood + 1 molasses
- Lair cost: 1 cutlass + 1 molasses + 1 goat + 1 wood
- Ship cost: 1 goat + 1 wood
- Coco tile cost: 1 cutlass + 1 molasses + 1 gold
- Roll 6: take 2 resource tiles of the island's type
- Marketplace: 5 booths, 1 trade per turn, reset at 5 same-type tiles
- Stockpile trade: 2 identical for 1 any, unlimited
- Coco effect: receive the 4 resources shown
- Win: all 7 lairs; Spooky Island lair counts

**Verdict: MINOR** — turn-order presented as fixed when source allows any order; 1:1 restriction on player trading omitted.

## catan-portable-edition — MINOR

Standard Catan content is accurate; the edition-specific 2-player variant is entirely missing.

### Findings

1. **OMISSION — 2-player variant.** Source devotes a full section ("CATAN FOR TWO") plus components ("14 Trade Cards — These are only used in the 2 player game") to a 2-player ruleset: 2 neutral players, double dice rolls with differing results, trade-card actions (Forced Trade, Move Robber to desert), replenishment rules, 7-trade-card hand limit. The summary never mentions any of it, lists player_count as 3-4 only, and omits the 14 trade cards from Components. A complete additional ruleset of this edition is missing.
2. **MINOR — component counts omitted.** Source: 95 resource cards, 25 development cards, 4 building cost cards, 2 special VP cards, per-color sets of 5 settlements / 15 roads / 4 cities, 6 game board pieces, dice shaker, 2 card trays. Summary lists categories without counts (not wrong, just lossy).

### Numbers checked (all supported)

- Win at 10 VP; settlement 1 VP, city 2 VP
- Longest Road: 5+ segments, 2 VP
- Largest Army: 3 knights, 2 VP
- VP development card = 1 VP
- Road = 1 brick + 1 lumber
- Settlement = 1 brick + 1 lumber + 1 grain + 1 wool
- City = 3 ore + 2 grain ("2 wheat" in source)
- Dev card = 1 ore + 1 grain + 1 wool
- Maritime trade 4:1; harbors 3:1 generic / 2:1 special
- Roll 7: more than 7 cards (8+) discard half, rounded down
- 2 starting settlements + 2 roads; starting resources from second settlement (variable setup)

**Verdict: MINOR** — base-game content faithful; the included 2-player variant (and its 14 trade cards) omitted entirely.

## catan-scenario-crop-trust — MAJOR

Setup and components are accurate, but the Rule Changes are vague to the point of missing the core harvest mechanic, a VP-bearing reward system is omitted, and the end-game/lose condition is misstated.

### Findings

1. **CONTRADICTION — "all players lose" condition is invented.** Summary: "if too many crops are lost from the fields, all players can lose collectively" and "Lose condition: All players lose if critical crops are lost." Source (END OF THE GAME): the game ends when (1) a player reaches 10 VP — that player wins; (2) 3 of the 5 fields hexes contain no crop tokens; or (3) 2 of 5 plant species are extinct. Under conditions 2/3 the game still produces a winner: "If you are the player with the most crop tokens on your crop storage record, you win... In case of a tie, the tied player who has the most victory points wins. If the tie persists, both players win." Nobody-loses-collectively; only the single player who triggers condition 2/3 during the production phase loses individually ("the player with the 2nd most crop tokens... wins"). The actual alternate-win path (most stored crops) is entirely absent.
2. **MAJOR OMISSION — Crop Storage Record rewards (a scoring source).** Source: placing the 2nd crop token in a row of your Crop Storage Record grants 1 free development card; the 3rd, 4th, and 5th tokens each grant **1 victory point** (achievable twice via the second row). The summary says only "Crop storage records track each player's contributions" — omitting up to 6 VP and 2 free development cards.
3. **MAJOR VAGUENESS/ERROR — harvest mechanic.** Summary: "When you harvest grain (from a field hex), you may also interact with crop tokens." Source: receiving a food (grain) card from a fields hex REQUIRES removing a crop token from that hex ("After removing a crop token... take a food card"); choosing not to remove a token means no food card; cities may remove 1-2 tokens for 1-2 cards; harvesting proceeds in 1-2 harvest rounds in player order; when a hex has no tokens, no one adjacent can harvest food (crop shortage). The summary's phrasing reads as optional bonus interaction and misses that food production consumes crop tokens — the engine of the whole scenario.
4. **ERROR — "Four crop types (beans, maize, quinoa, rice) replace the desert."** Source: there are 5 crop varieties (wheat, beans, maize, rice, quinoa; "90 Crop Tokens in 5 diverse crop varieties"), and it is the extra fields hex that replaces the desert. The summary's own Components section says 5 varieties, contradicting its Overview.
5. **OMISSION — seed-deposit procedure.** Source: 4-step process — pay 1 lumber + 1 ore; place 1 crop token on Crop Storage Record (max 1 of each type per row); take 4 matching tokens to your vault space; replant the 4 tokens from your other vault space onto 4 different fields hexes (max 7 tokens per hex). Summary gives none of these numbers/steps.
6. **OMISSION — event token categories.** Regional / Small-scale (remove 2) / Large-scale (remove 3) crop loss and Monoculture (hex with only 1 crop type loses all tokens) are not described; summary says only "beneficial or harmful" — and per the source, event fronts show "various disastrous effects" (none beneficial), a further mild misstatement.

### Numbers checked

- 90 crop tokens, 5 varieties — supported (Overview's "four crop types" WRONG)
- 41 event tokens — supported
- 1 fields hex replacing desert, seed-vial watermark — supported
- 4 Crop Storage Records — supported
- 1 number token "9" — supported
- 7 event tokens per player; under remaining 3 settlements + 4 cities — supported
- 4 crop tokens on a seed vault space at setup — supported
- Robber starts on pasture "2" — supported
- Win at 10 VP — supported
- End conditions 3-of-5 hexes empty / 2-of-5 species extinct — OMITTED/misrepresented
- Storage cost 1 lumber + 1 ore — OMITTED
- Record rewards (dev card at 2nd, 1 VP at 3rd/4th/5th) — OMITTED

**Verdict: MAJOR** — invented collective-loss condition contradicting the actual alternate-win rules, omitted VP rewards, and the mandatory token-for-food harvest mechanic reduced to "may interact."

## cathedral — PASS

Accurate against the (very short) Magnetic Travel Edition source. A few component specifics are not in the source but match the published game and would not mislead.

### Findings (informational, not errors)

1. **Unsourced detail:** "10x10 grid" — board size never stated in the source; matches the real game.
2. **Unsourced detail:** "14 Light / 14 Dark pieces... occupying 1 to 5 squares each" — source says only "Distribute the 28 buildings accordingly" (14 each is a fair split); piece sizes are not in the source but are correct for the published game.

### Rules checked (all supported)

- Light places Cathedral first, anywhere, grid-aligned; Dark makes first and each alternate move
- Pieces immovable once placed; no space-claiming on either player's first move
- Enclose exactly ONE piece (Cathedral or opponent building) → MUST remove immediately; opponent may replay removed building; Cathedral never replayed
- Enclose TWO or more → nothing removed, space stays available to opponent
- Corner-to-corner contacts invalid for wall-to-wall connections
- Cathedral is neutral; cannot form part of your claiming wall
- Game ends when no further moves; fewest squares of unplaced buildings wins; rematch with Dark placing Cathedral, lowest combined score wins
- Tips (largest buildings first; don't build in own space while unclaimed space remains) — supported

**Verdict: PASS** — faithful; the only deviations are unsourced-but-accurate component details.

## cauldron-battle-of-gazala-may-1942 — MAJOR

Components, sequence of play, supply, minefields, LOC, and victory conditions are accurate, but the combat system and tactical chits contain multiple wrong numbers and invented mechanics.

### Findings

1. **WRONG — step counts.** Summary: "Units have 1-2 steps." Source (11.0): "Most units in the game have only a single step. All Allied and Italian armor units have two steps. **All German armor has three steps.**" German 3-step armor is central to Axis durability.
2. **WRONG — retreat distance.** Summary: "Units forced to retreat move 1-2 hexes away." Source (11.1): a retreating unit "must finish its retreat two hexes from its original location" — always 2 (or further only to avoid overstacking).
3. **CONTRADICTION — Disrupted units "cannot attack."** Source (12.0): disrupted attackers apply a -2 die roll modifier ("If one or more of the attacking units in a combat are disrupted, a -2 modifier is applied"), i.e., they CAN attack. It is *Isolated* units that may not attack (5.0). The summary's own supply table states this correctly, so the Disruption bullet contradicts both the source and itself.
4. **WRONG — ZOC cost.** Summary: "Entering an enemy ZOC costs extra movement points." Source (9.0): "To **leave** a hex in an enemy ZOC costs 2 movement points in addition to the normal terrain costs," and "A unit may not move from a hex in an enemy ZOC to another hex that is in an enemy ZOC" (prohibition omitted).
5. **INVENTED/WRONG — tactical chit effects.**
   - Air: summary "Air (modifier in combat)." Source (13.4): an immediate air attack on a single unit — Allied: 0-2 disrupts; Axis: 0-1 step loss, 2-6 disrupts, +2 to roll vs fortified hexes. Not a combat modifier.
   - Anti-Tank: summary "+2 vs. armor." Source (13.6): "increases Allied armor losses by one step in any combat it is used" — a step loss, not a die modifier; unusable if only Pavia/Brescia/Trieste units involved.
   - Combat: summary "+1 die roll modifier." Source (13.1): Axis +1 to own attack or -1 to opponent's; **Allied +2** to own attack or -1 to opponent's.
   - Extra Move: summary "activate 1 unit for movement only." Source (13.2): activates one additional unit that becomes "part of the group of activated units" — it moves and fights normally.
   - Replacement: summary omits "Replacements may only be used by armored units" (13.3).
   - Withdraw/Attack: summary omits that an unplayed chit gives the Axis 1 VP (13.7) — a small VP source.
6. **MISREPRESENTED — attack declaration.** Summary: "The attacker selects a target hex, declares all attacking units." Source (11.0): the active unit "attacks **all adjacent enemy units**" — mandatory inclusion of every adjacent defender; friendly units adjacent to those defenders may optionally join. No target-hex selection exists.
7. **OMISSION — attack intensities.** Source: "Combat has three levels of intensity: Light, Medium and Heavy attacks," each with an MP cost and combat modifier (e.g., heavy = 24 MP, +2 per the example). The summary never mentions intensity levels — a core decision in every attack.
8. **OMISSIONS — combat value usage and restrictions.** Hard values used when the opposing side includes armor, soft otherwise; infantry may never initiate/participate in attacks against armor; anti-tank units may not initiate attacks nor attack infantry. Summary says only "Both Hard and Soft combat values are used."
9. **MINOR — stacking limit unstated.** Source (10.0): max 2 units per hex; no stacking with Pavia/Brescia units. Summary: "Limits apply; excess units eliminated."
10. **MINOR — CRT result types.** D result (2 units disrupted), R = retreat 2 hexes, cumulative order (losses → disruption → retreat) not described.

### Numbers checked

- Map 22"x17"; 168 counters; d10 (0-9, 0=zero, not included) — supported
- 1 mile per hex; battalions except Brescia/Pavia regiments — supported
- Minefield Phase: up to 2 breach markers from May 28 — supported
- May 27 AM: Axis takes first two actions — supported
- Allied minefield crossing = 12 MP; Axis cannot cross unbreached — supported
- Axis LOC from May 29: 1 LOC to ≥8 units or -1 action; from June 1-2: 2 LOCs — supported
- Out of Supply: -1/3 MA, -2 attack DRM; Isolated: -2/3 MA, no attack, +2 to attacker's roll — supported
- Axis VP: 1/turn for 4 in-supply units on north edge; 1/turn for hex 2803; max 19; 1 VP per Allied step; Allies 2 VP per Axis step; replaced steps don't count — supported
- Scenario One: May 27 AM-June 1-2, Allies 1 VP/fortified hex, Axis starts +2 VP, ~3.5 hrs — supported
- Campaign: to June 13-14; 2 VP/hex for 1504/1605/2705; 3 VP/hex for 1118/1218/1219/1318; ~7 hrs — supported
- Step counts "1-2" — WRONG (1/2/3)
- Retreat "1-2 hexes" — WRONG (2)
- Anti-Tank "+2" — WRONG (+1 step loss)
- Combat chit "+1" — WRONG for Allies (+2)

**Verdict: MAJOR** — wrong step counts and retreat distance, an invented "disrupted cannot attack" rule, inverted ZOC cost, and several misdescribed tactical chits would all mislead at the table, despite accurate VP and supply rules.
