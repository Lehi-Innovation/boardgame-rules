## 1754-conquest-the-french-and-indian-war — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Victory scoring is misstated in a way that can change the winner; two component counts are wrong/unsupported (setup step numbers misread as counts); the actual water movement rule is replaced by a reference to other games.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Victory condition misstated (scoring).**
   - Summary: "The side controlling the most Victory Areas wins. Control requires having your side's units present and no enemy units in the area." (and same in Overview)
   - Source: "The side with the most Control Markers (4.2) on the map wins. If there is a tie, the game is a draw." (l.667-668) and "Players never place their side's Control Markers on their own Home Areas" / "A side's Control Markers only occupy Opponent or Native Home Areas, never its own Home Areas" (l.106-107, 669-670).
   - Impact: under the source rules, occupying your OWN home Victory Areas scores nothing — only markers on Opponent/Native home Victory Areas count. The summary's wording leads players to count their own home Victory Areas, which can flip the winner. The summary also omits tie = draw and the Louisbourg double Victory Symbol (worth 2 markers, l.467-469).

2. **MAJOR (wrong number) — Muster Marker count invented.**
   - Summary: "5 Muster Markers (2 British Colonial, 2 French Canadien, 1 additional)"
   - Source: "two grey Muster Markers... Then place the two yellow French Canadiens Muster Markers" (l.28-35) = 4 total. The "5" in the source heading (l.25) is the setup step number ("5 Muster Markers" = step 5), parallel to "1 Choose a Faction", "6 Cards", "9 Round Pawn". The "1 additional" marker is invented to reconcile the misread step number.

3. **Unsupported number — Fort Markers "4 ... per side, 8 total".**
   - Summary: "4 Fort Markers (per side, 8 total)"
   - Source: heading "4 Fort Markers" is setup step 4; the text says "The French and the English sides each place 3 Fort Markers" (l.42-44). Nowhere does the extracted text state 4 per side or 8 total. (Summary's own Setup step 4 correctly says 3 per side — internally inconsistent with its Components list.)

4. **MINOR/MODERATE — Boat movement rule replaced with a non-rule.**
   - Summary: "Water Movement and special movement cards work similarly to other Birth of America games."
   - Source 3.3 (l.327-335): only French/British **Regulars** armies may cross Open Water, Harbor-to-Harbor, Open Water counts as one area (2 moves total), armies may not stop in Open Water; Colonials/Canadiens/Natives may only ride along in Regulars armies. None of this is usable from the summary.

5. **MINOR — Battle details omitted:** dice rolled = number of units capped at the faction's dice count (l.418-421); Fort Die Block cancels one Hit (l.487-494); Hit/Flee resolved before Command (l.457); Command-move destination restrictions (friendly areas, empty allied home, empty Native home — l.448-453, 558-563); paired Native flee rule and Natives-only side loses/switches (l.475-484); destroying a Fort when the last defender command-moves out (l.495-499). The summary's "Fort Markers provide defensive advantages" gestures at #2 but gives no mechanic.

6. **MINOR — Armies must also stop on entering areas with independent Native units (l.322-323); summary only says "enemy-occupied areas".**

## 1775-rebellion — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — The summary invents a deck-reshuffle rule that directly contradicts the source's "discarded cards are never reused" (a core constraint the game-end pacing depends on), misstates the Event Card system, and wrongly extends allied colony control to Native Americans. Component numbers are all correct.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented mechanic: reshuffling the discard pile.**
   - Summary (Draw Cards Phase): "If your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck."
   - Source: "Discarded cards are not used again for the rest of the game. Manage your cards carefully. Once you have played a unique card, you will not draw it again." (l.705-708)
   - Impact: this is not a small drift — the designers' note explains that because each faction has exactly eight Movement Cards (incl. the Truce Card) and must play one per turn, "every player will inevitably play their Truce Card within the eight Rounds allotted" (l.739-741). A reshuffle rule lets players dodge Truce Cards and breaks the game-end mechanism.

2. **MAJOR — Event Card system misstated.**
   - Summary: "Event Cards have special effects and are played during the Movement Phase instead of (or sometimes alongside) Movement Cards."
   - Source: "You may play any number of Event Cards from your hand on your Turn... Each Event Card's text states the Turn Phase it should be played in: Reinforcement Event Cards / Movement Event Cards / Battle Event Cards" (l.722-736); the one Movement Card per turn remains mandatory ("play one (and only one) Movement Card", l.393-394).
   - Impact: players would wrongly restrict Events to the Movement Phase (Battle/Reinforcement events exist) and might skip their mandatory Movement Card.

3. **MODERATE — Allied colony control wrongly includes Native Americans.**
   - Summary: "Allied units (Hessian, French, Native American) can control a colony on behalf of their side even if no player faction units are present."
   - Source: "French or Hessian Units can control a Colony on behalf of their Side..." (l.317-323) — Native Americans deliberately excluded; moreover "If Native American Units are ever left alone in an Area, they return to being independent" (l.283-284), and independent Natives *block* colony control (l.292-293). The summary's rule can award a colony the source denies.

4. **MINOR — Command Move restrictions partially omitted:** the summary gives the water restriction but omits "you may not Command Move into an Area where there are only opposing Units" (l.578-579) and that command-moving into an independent Native area allies them (l.574-577).

## 1812-the-invasion-of-canada — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Two contradicted core rules: battle initiative (source uses homeland initiative, not defender-first) and the Truce Card structure (source: all five factions including Native Americans have Truce Cards; summary claims Native Americans have none). Component and enlistment numbers are all correct.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Battle initiative contradicted.**
   - Summary: "Defender rolls first." / "Alternate Defender/Attacker rolls until one side remains."
   - Source 3.3: "The side in whose Homeland Area (1.1) the battle takes place has the initiative and rolls first. In red areas the British players have the initiative and in blue areas the American players have the initiative, **regardless of which army is the attacker or defender**." (l.332-336). The turn example confirms it: the American Militia *attacks* Ogdensburg, and "The battle occurs in an American Homeland Area, so the American players have initiative and roll first" (l.408-410).
   - This appears to be copied from the sibling games (1754/1775 are defender-first); in 1812 it is simply wrong and affects every battle.

2. **MAJOR — Truce Card structure contradicted (game end).**
   - Summary (Scoring): "British side: both British Regulars and Canadian Militia Truce Cards... **Native Americans do not have a Truce Card.**"
   - Source: "Each faction has one Truce Card" (l.247) and "There are 3 Truce Cards for the British allies or 2 for the American allies" (l.536-537) — the Native American faction has a Truce Card and the British side's game-end trigger requires all three.
   - The summary also contradicts itself: its own Actions section says "Each faction has 1 Truce Card in their deck." Players using the Scoring section would end the game a full Truce Card too early for the British side.

3. **MODERATE — Command Decision rules imported from the wrong game / omitted.**
   - Summary: "Blank (Command Decision): Move 1 unit to adjacent area (not across water except at crossings)."
   - Source: the unit moves to an adjacent **Friendly Area** (defined l.368-383: own-side homeland that is empty or has allied units, or enemy homeland occupied by allied units); the only water restriction is "An army that moved into a battle by water may not move out by water with a Command Decision" (l.388-389). The summary's parenthetical is 1775's rule, not 1812's. Also omitted: the Native American Command Decision advantage — they alone may retreat into unoccupied enemy Homeland Areas (3.3.4, l.390-393).

4. **MINOR — "Native Americans: Are on the British side but are independent."** In 1812 the Native Americans are a full player faction (one of five, l.44-60); there is no independent-natives mechanic in this game (unlike 1754/1775). "Independent" is a carry-over error, though low impact since the summary otherwise treats them as a faction.

5. **MINOR — Water movement details:** "(Great Lakes, Atlantic)" — the Atlantic is not mentioned in the source (large bodies of water are aqua-marine lakes/large rivers, ending at greyed-out rivers, l.254-276). Canoe (5 Native units from up to 5 areas) and Fishing Boat (2 armies of up to 3 units) limits (3.2.5) are omitted. Fort Erie/Queenston double Objective (l.323-325) omitted.
