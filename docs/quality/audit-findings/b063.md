## cosmic-encounter — MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Cosmic Zap effect contradicted.** Summary (Special Rules): "Cosmic Zap: An artifact that negates another artifact or flare card as it is played." Source: "CosmiC Zap — Stops Power. Play this card at any time to cancel one use of any alien's power, including your own." The summary's Cosmic Zap entry actually describes Card Zap (which the summary also lists, correctly), so the two entries duplicate each other and Cosmic Zap's real function (cancelling alien powers) is lost.

2. **MAJOR — Wrong threshold for losing alien power.** Summary: "Losing all home colonies means you may not use your alien power until you regain one." Source: "If a player ever has fewer than three home colonies, that player loses their alien power... If a player who lost their alien power ever has three or more home colonies again, they immediately regain their alien power." Threshold is fewer than 3 home colonies (regain at 3+), not zero/one.

3. **MINOR — Regroup destination over-restricted.** Summary: "Regroup: Retrieve 1 ship from the Warp to any home colony." Source: "the offense retrieves one of their ships from the warp and places it on any of their home or foreign colonies."

4. **MINOR — Start Turn Phase omitted / empty-hand rule conflated.** Source lists 8 phases (1. Start Turn ... 8. Resolution); summary lists 7, dropping Start Turn. The summary's "Empty Hand" rule ("If you have no encounter cards in hand at the start of the Planning phase, discard your entire hand and draw 8 new cards") conflates three distinct source rules: the offense redraws during the Start Turn Phase; the defense reveals/discards/redraws during Planning; and the offense with no encounter card at Planning has their turn end immediately (ships return).

5. **MINOR — Unsupported details.** "Attack Cards (value 00-40)" — no value range appears in the extracted text (examples show 8, 10, 12 only). "Mobius Tubes: An artifact that frees all of a player's ships from the Warp" — no mention of Mobius Tubes anywhere in the extracted text (artifacts shown are Card Zap, Cosmic Zap, Ionic Gas, Force Field; the warp-freeing effect belongs to the Warpspace Key tech). Both are plausibly true of the real game but have no basis in this source.

## cry-havoc — MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Recruit destination wrong.** Summary: "Recruit: ... Place Units from Reserve onto Regions you control (including HQ)." Source: "Spend each Recruitment point to add one Unit from your Reserve to your Headquarters Region." Recruiting is to the HQ Region only — letting players recruit into any controlled region would substantially change the game.

2. **MAJOR — Enable Scoring / Scoring Phase misdescribed.** Summary: "Enable Scoring: Place your Scoring Enabled marker... A player cannot score crystals unless they have enabled scoring" and Scoring Phase "Score VP for Crystals in controlled Regions" (implicitly only for enablers). Source: "Each player has a single card in their deck that, when played, allows them to take the Enable Scoring Action... Scoring can only be enabled once per Round! At the end of the Round, the player who Enabled Scoring will score 1VP for every Region they Control. Then, all players will score 1VP for each Crystal in every Region they Control." So: enabling requires a specific card (not a free action), only once per round, the enabler gets the region-control VP bonus, and ALL players score crystals once anyone enables. The summary contradicts the all-players-score rule and omits the 1 VP/Region bonus and the card requirement.

3. **MAJOR — Battle resolution distorted/omitted.** Summary: "The side with the most units in each area wins that objective... Kill eliminates units." Source: Attrition is not majority-based — "Each player kills one enemy Unit in the Region... for each of their own Units placed on this Objective... Each player Scores 1 Victory Point per enemy Unit killed," resolved simultaneously. Also omitted from the summary: Step A "Add one Crystal to the Battle Region" before every battle; winning Region Control "immediately scores 2 Victory Points" (ties to Defender); tie in Prisoners = no capture; retreat rules for the loser. The 2 VP region-control award and 1 VP/kill are scoring sources missing from the Scoring section (only vaguely covered by "Various Tactics cards and Skills award VP").

4. **MINOR — Prisoners Phase ransom omitted.** Summary: "Score VP for captured Prisoners." Source adds: each player scores 1VP per prisoner held, then owners may pay 2 VP per prisoner to return them to their Reserve.

5. **MINOR — Final round scoring rule omitted.** Source: "Scoring is always Enabled in the final Round and cannot be Enabled by players... nobody will score VP for Region Control." Also tie-breakers (most Prisoners, then later Initiative) omitted.

6. Trivial: "Draw 2, keep 1" — the unchosen card is reshuffled per source, not discarded; the summary's Recruit wording "Must pay matching points per card shown" is garbled but harmless.

## cuba — MAJOR

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Invented "use a building" rider on three characters.** Summary appends "Also optionally use one of your existing buildings' abilities" to the Architect, Foreman, and Mayor actions. Source: building features are used only via the Foreman ("With the foreman, you can use the features of your buildings"); the Architect and Mayor have no such option. This invents extra actions every round.

2. **MAJOR — Foreman's actual mechanism missing.** Summary reduces the Foreman to "Convert products into goods at buildings" (distillery/cigar factory). Source: the Foreman chooses (1) use all buildings in the same row or column as the playing piece, or (2) use any one building — conversion is just one building feature among ~25. The warehouse use (storing products) is also a Foreman feature and is omitted.

3. **MAJOR — End-of-round product spoilage omitted.** Source (End of a round): "Players must give up all products that are still lying in the lot. Products that are in the warehouse may be taken into the next round. Resources and goods remain in the lot." The summary's phase E is just "Prepare for the next round" and its Special Rules even call the lot a "temporary depot" without the spoilage rule. This is a core economic rule; without it players would carry products over freely.

4. **MAJOR — End-of-game building VP omitted.** Source: "Players earn 2 additional victory points for each of their own buildings. Money does not bring any more victory points... In the case of a tie, the player with the most money wins." Summary says only "After 6 rounds, the player with the most VP wins" — a missing scoring source worth up to ~2x12 VP.

5. **MAJOR — Starting player rule contradicted.** Summary: "The starting player changes based on who wins certain parliamentary votes or card effects." Source: the new starting player is determined at the end of the action phase — the player whose fourth (last-played) card has the highest value (tie: the one who played last). Nothing ties it to parliament votes.

6. **MINOR — Black marking pieces given an invented use.** Summary (Tradeswoman): "Place black markers on vacated price slots if the product/good is sold out." Source: black markers are placed to lock out the once-per-round ALTERNATIVE uses of the tradeswoman/architect/mayor; they have nothing to do with market price slots. The alternative uses themselves (tradeswoman: free resource or cheapest product; architect: 2 VP first/1 VP second for skipping a build; mayor: 4/2 pesos for skipping shipping) are entirely omitted.

7. **MINOR — Market edge prices and shipping VP omitted.** Source: buy from general stock at 7 pesos when market is empty of a kind; sell to stock at 1 peso (products) / 3 pesos (goods) when market full; ships pay 1/2/3 VP per merchandise piece depending on dock, each ship holds max 5 pieces. Summary says only "pay its loading costs and earn VP per item shipped" (there are no peso loading costs — you supply the merchandise itself). Statute specifics (tax/duty compliance = 2 VP each, +1 bonus for both; vote buying at 1 peso/vote) are also generalized away, though the summary's generic wording does not contradict them.
