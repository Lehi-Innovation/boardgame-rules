## planet-steam

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Scoring formula is severely incomplete (MAJOR)

Summary: "Wealth = Credits held + Market value of all resources owned"

Source (lines 296–306): "Players calculate their final wealth by adding the credit value of their assets to the value of their credit tokens. Assets have the following credit values: Each resource has a credit value equal to that resource's current price listed on the resource terminal. 25 credits for each claimed zone. 25 credits for each tank. 50 credits for each supercharger. 50 credits for each luxury quarters certificate. Building licenses, carriers, and converters have no credit value."

The summary entirely omits: 25 credits per claimed zone, 25 credits per tank, 50 credits per supercharger, 50 credits per luxury quarters certificate. These are all significant scoring sources. Any final score computed from the summary will be severely incorrect.

### Finding 2 — Phase structure is fabricated (MAJOR)

Summary lists 7 phases: "1. Specialist Selection, 2. Claim Zones, 3. Purchase Equipment, 4. Deploy Equipment, 5. Resource Extraction, 6. Market Trading, 7. Support Airship"

Source (lines 327–343): "During each game round, players resolve the following phases in order: 1. Expansion Phase (place bonus resources, acquire specialist cards through an auction, place claim markers, place the support airship). 2. Tank Phase (buy and reorganize tanks, converters, superchargers; buy carrier upgrades). 3. Resource Phase (extract resources; buy and sell resources at the resource terminal). 4. End Phase (replenish local tank supply, advance round marker, prepare game board and specialist cards for next round)."

The summary invents phase names and splits/reorganizes phases in ways that misrepresent the actual structure (e.g., "Deploy Equipment" and "Support Airship" as separate phases do not exist; the Expansion Phase is broken apart incorrectly). The Player Reference table lists only 6 phases and omits the End Phase entirely.

### Finding 3 — Tiebreaker missing

Summary states: "The player with the most total wealth wins." (no tiebreaker mentioned)

Source (lines 308–310): "The player with the highest final wealth is the winner. If players tie for the highest final wealth, all tied players share a victory."

Minor omission but no active misdirection.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 6 (25/zone, 25/tank, 50/supercharger, 50/luxury quarters, credit denominations 1/5/10/20/50/100 match), SUPPORTED: 4 (credit denominations and resource pricing method supported; zone/tank/supercharger/luxury quarters values present in source but absent from summary)

## poison

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — "End of round" contradicts source "end of game" (MAJOR)

Summary: "You cannot examine your face-down collected cards until the round ends."

Source (line 24): "Note: You may not look at your face down cards at any point until the end of the **game**."

This is a direct contradiction. The summary implies players can examine cards between rounds; the source prohibits examining them until the entire game is over.

### Finding 2 — Scoring rules unverifiable; necessarily from outside knowledge (MAJOR)

Summary asserts:
- "Each remaining Potion Card in your collection scores 1 penalty point."
- "Each Poison Card in your collection scores 2 penalty points."
- Immunity rule: "the player who collected the most cards of that color discards all cards of that color"
- "Play multiple rounds. The player with the fewest total penalty points after the agreed number of rounds wins."

The extracted source text contains only the introduction/setup/gameplay sections (39 lines total) and entirely lacks any scoring section. None of the above scoring claims can be verified against the source. Per audit protocol, claims that have no support in the source and cannot be attributed to an OCR gap (the text simply ends after the card distribution note) must be treated as outside-knowledge fabrication. The summary provides a complete scoring system that is absent from the extracted rulebook text.

No support found (searched: score, penalty, point, immunity, round end, winner, fewest, end of game scoring).

### Finding 3 — Card distribution in Player Reference is inconsistent with summary text

Summary text says: "14 Potion Cards in each of 3 colors (42 total): 2 cards valued at 4, 3 cards each valued at 1, 2, 5, and 7"
Summary Player Reference table shows: "1, 1, 1, 2, 2, 2, 4, 4, 5, 5, 5, 7, 7, 7" (14 per color)

Source: "Fourteen Potion Cards in each of three colors (with two cards each valued at 4 and three cards each valued at 1, 2, 5, and 7)"

The numbers match (2×4 + 3×1 + 3×2 + 3×5 + 3×7 = 14), so the card counts are correct.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 3 (14 cards/color, 8 Poison cards, value 4 each), SUPPORTED: 3

## polis-fight-for-the-hegemony

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Actions per turn wrong: summary says 1, source says 2 (MAJOR)

Summary: "On each turn, a player performs one action: Diplomacy / Military / Trade / Projects / Wheat Collection / Population Growth / Fortification"

Source (lines 280–282, 296–297): "In each round, both players carry out alternately turns with two different actions to one another, choosing between the 12 possible actions... in each turn the player may perform two actions that must differ from one another."

A table of players would get the game fundamentally wrong: every turn they would do half the actions the game intends.

### Finding 2 — Final scoring formula is wrong: summary omits population as scoring element (MAJOR)

Summary: "After the final round: The player with the most prestige wins." / "Prestige is earned from: Winning battles, Completing projects, Controlling important poleis, Successful diplomatic actions."

Source (lines 1157–1165): "The final score is set by the addition of **three** elements of the game: The total population that the poleis, allied to a player, house. The player's current Prestige. And the Prestige 'for posterity' that is awarded by the completed Projects in his poleis."

The summary completely omits population as a scoring element. Population is described in the source as one of "the two main terms" of the game and is worth points directly in the final tally. This would cause players to mis-score the game.

### Finding 3 — Invented mid-game victory conditions (MAJOR)

Summary: "The game can end through: 1. Military victory: Control enough poleis to dominate Greece. 2. Prestige victory: Accumulate enough prestige through projects, military victories, and diplomatic achievements."

Source (lines 1143–1155) specifies exactly two early-end conditions:
1. A player loses their capital (Sparta or Athenai) because they cannot supply it.
2. After the six End of the Round phases, a player has zero Prestige.

Neither "control enough poleis" nor "accumulate enough prestige" appears as a victory condition in the source. These are entirely invented.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreaker wrong

Summary does not mention the tiebreaker.

Source (lines 1166–1168): "If there is a tie, it is resolved in favour of the player that has a greater amount of the 6 goods (metal, wood, wine, oil, silver and wheat)."

### Finding 5 — Turn order wrong

Summary does not specify who takes the first turn of a round.

Source (line 277): "The first turn of the round is for the player with less Prestige at the beginning of the round (in a tie, the Spartan player)."

### Finding 6 — Actions per type is wrong

Summary Player Reference lists 10 actions; source specifies 12 actions (4 Development, 4 Military, 4 Political).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (4 rounds, 2 players, 12 actions, 3 scoring elements, 6 goods for tiebreaker, 2 early end conditions, 18 poleis, 1 four-sided die), SUPPORTED: 5 (4 rounds, 2 players, 18 poleis, 1 four-sided die, 2 early end conditions verified but misrepresented)

## ponte-del-diavolo

**Verdict: PASS**

Note: Prefilter flagged SOURCE_SUSPECT (title tokens absent from first 3000 chars) but the extracted text is clearly the Ponte del Diavolo rulebook: it credits Martin Ebel as author, describes the 10x10 island/bridge game, and names the Alex Randolph connection — all consistent with the summary.

### Verification log

**Goal:** Summary: "The player with the most points from connected island groups wins." Source: "The players try to place as many islands on the board as they can and then connect them with bridges." and "The player with the most points is the winner!" ✓

**Island definition:** Summary: "Exactly 4 connected same-color squares (touching along at least one side)... never more or fewer." Source: "A grouping of 4 island squares of the same color connected to each other, touching at least along one side is called an island. An island must be exactly 4 island squares: never more or less." ✓

**Touching rule:** Summary: "An island or sand bank may NOT touch (even diagonally) another island or sand bank of the same color." Source: "An island may not touch (even diagonally) an island or sand bank of the same color." ✓

**Sand bank exception:** Summary: "Sand banks may touch each other diagonally." Source: "Sand banks may touch diagonally." ✓

**Bridge rule (blocked spaces):** Summary: "A bridge blocks the spaces beneath it (max 2 spaces blocked)." Source: "A bridge will block 2 spaces, at most." ✓

**Game end:** Summary correctly describes asymmetric end: light player can't place/bridge → dark gets 1 more turn then game ends; dark player can't place/bridge → game ends immediately. Source (lines 81–83) states the same. ✓

**Scoring — solitary island:** Summary: "A solitary island... scores 1 point." Source: "A solitary island, that is not connected by a bridge to any other islands or sand banks, scores 1 point." ✓

**Scoring table — verified via example:** Summary table: 1 island=1pt, 2 islands=3pts, 4 islands=10pts. Source example (lines 125–128): "light has 4 connected islands: 10 points. Dark has 2 (over a sand bank) connected islands (3 points) and 2 solitary islands (2 points): 5 points." The scoring table values for 1, 2, and 4 connected islands are confirmed. ✓

**Tiebreakers:** Summary: "Most islands, most bridges, shared victory." Source: "If the players tie, the player with the most islands is the winner. If players still tie, the player with the most bridges is the winner. If they still tie, they rejoice in their shared victory!" ✓

**Turn structure:** Summary: "Place 2 island squares on any 2 empty spaces OR place 1 bridge." Source Rule 1: "On his turn, the player places either — 2 island squares on any 2 empty spaces on the board... or — 1 bridge on 2 of his island squares." ✓

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (80 squares, 40 per player, 15 bridges, 10x10 board, 4 squares per island, 1-3 squares per sand bank, scoring values 1/3/10, tiebreaker order), SUPPORTED: 8

## potion-explosion

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Finding 1 — Game-end finish rule is imprecise (MINOR)

Summary: "When the required number of Skill Tokens have been awarded (based on player count), the end of game is triggered. Finish the current round."

Source (lines 364–366): "The game continues, in clockwise order, until the player to the right of the First Player completes their next turn (so that all players will have played the same number of turns during the game)."

"Finish the current round" and "until the player to the right of the First Player" are equivalent in most situations: both mean all players finish the round. This wording in the summary is imprecise but unlikely to mislead players meaningfully at the table. Treated as MINOR.

### Finding 2 — Second game-end trigger missing

Summary: "When the required number of Skill Tokens have been awarded (based on player count), the end of game is triggered."

Source (lines 362–363): "The end of the game is also triggered if, at the end of any player's round, there are no more Potion tiles available."

The summary omits this second trigger. Minor omission.

### Finding 3 — Tiebreaker missing

Summary: no tiebreaker mentioned.

Source (lines 373–375): "In case of a tie, all the tied players (starting from the First Player, in turn order) each take one marble from the Dispenser to try to trigger an Explosion. The player who takes the most marbles in this way is the winner."

### Procedural verification

**Taking a marble:** Summary: "Choose any marble from the dispenser. Remaining marbles slide down to fill gaps." Source (lines 168–170): "you simply have to choose and take one. You can freely choose any marble that is visible on the Dispenser, except for the 9th marble on each slide track." ✓ (Summary omits the 9th-marble exception but this is a minor detail)

**Little Help:** Summary: "take any 1 marble from the dispenser without triggering explosions. Take a Little Help token (worth -2 points at end of game)." Source (lines 200–206): "take a Little Help token and 1 Ingredient marble of your choice from the Dispenser. When you take an Ingredient marble using the Little Help from the professor, you cannot trigger an Explosion... For each Little Help token you lose 2 points at the end of the game." ✓

**Skill Tokens — condition 1:** Summary: "3 same type = 1 Skill Token." Source (lines 328–330): "when you complete your 3rd Potion of that kind (i.e., with the same power), you automatically receive a Skill token (but you don't gain another token if you complete 6 Potions of the same kind)." ✓

**Skill Tokens — condition 2:** Summary: "5 different types = 1 Skill Token." Source (lines 332–333): "when you complete your 5th different kind of Potion (i.e., completing Potions with 5 different powers), you automatically receive a Skill token." ✓

**Pool limit:** Summary: "Maximum 3 ingredients stored in your pool between turns." Source (line 223): "The Ingredient Pool can only hold up to 3 Ingredients at a time." ✓

**Scoring:** Summary: "Points from completed potion tiles (printed on each tile) + 4 points per Skill Token - 2 points per Little Help token." Source (lines 369–371): "all players add up the points they obtained from all the Potion tiles they completed and the Skill tokens they collected, then subtract 2 points for each Little Help token they have." ✓

**Exhaustion:** No deck to exhaust; Dispenser is refilled when marbles are returned. No reshuffle rule claimed in summary; consistent with source.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (80 marbles total, 20 per color, 64 potion tiles, 8 kinds, 15 Skill tokens, 21 Little Help tokens, 4 pts/Skill token, 2 pts per Little Help), SUPPORTED: 8
