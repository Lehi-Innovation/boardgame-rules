### Finding 1 — Turn Structure Phase 1 Misnamed (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "1. **Mandatory Events Phase** -- resolve any scheduled events."

**Source (line 1181):** "A. Mandated Offensive (MO) Phase (see 7.0) — Each player rolls one six-sided die (d6) and consults his Mandated Offensive Track to determine which (if any) MO he must perform this turn."

**No support found** for "Mandatory Events Phase" or "scheduled events" as a phase name (searched: "Mandatory Events", "mandatory event", "scheduled events"). The Mandated Offensive Phase is mechanically completely different from resolving scheduled events: each player rolls a die to see which enemy faction they are required to attack this turn, on pain of a VP penalty for failing. A player running the game from the summary would not run this phase at all.

### Finding 2 — Turn Phase Order Reversed: Replacement Phase and War Status Phase (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "4. **Replacement Phase** -- receive and place replacement points. 5. **War Status Phase** -- check for war entry, armistice, etc."

**Source (lines 1219–1252):** The sequence is:
- F. War Status Phase (check Extra VPs, check Automatic Victory, check Armistice, check War Commitment Levels, check National Collapse)
- G. Replacement Phase (each player spends RPs; AP goes before CP)

The summary puts Replacement Phase *before* War Status Phase — the reverse of the actual order. This matters: victory and armistice are checked (and the game may end) before replacements are taken in the source.

### Finding 3 — Action Rounds per Turn Wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Action Rounds -- alternating card plays (typically 6-7 rounds per turn)."

**Source (line 227):** "Action Phase: Each Action Phase consists of six Action Rounds." (Confirmed by line 1188: "Each Action Phase is divided into six identical Action Rounds." and line 1370: "Each Action Phase has six Action Rounds.")

There is no variant producing 7 rounds. The word "typically" and the "6-7" range are unsupported. Always exactly six.

### Finding 4 — Who Goes First in Action Rounds Reversed (MAJOR)

**Summary (Setup section):** "The Central Powers player goes first in Turn 1."

**Source (line 230):** "The AP Player plays first during each Action Round." (Confirmed by line 1189–1190: "The AP Player takes his action first in each Action Round" and line 1371: "the AP Player going first each time.")

The source is unambiguous and consistent: the AP (Allied Powers) player goes first in *every* Action Round of *every* turn, not the CP. The summary's claim reverses the player order.

### Finding 5 — Victory Condition: "Capturing Constantinople" Not a Win Condition (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "The Allies win by capturing Constantinople, controlling enough Ottoman territory, or reaching automatic victory thresholds at game's end."

**Source (lines 1090–1096, 5.2.1 Automatic Victory):**
"a. The AP Player wins if the Total VP Level is 0 or lower.
b. The CP Player wins if the Total VP Level is 20 or higher. Also, the CP Player wins if the CP Automatic Victory Marker and the VP Marker are in the same space on the General Records Track (or if the VP Marker is in a higher space)—the Allies have grown weary of the sideshow."

Capturing Constantinople is not a distinct AP automatic victory condition. Constantinople is a VP space (like others marked with a red border on the map), and AP units *in* Constantinople give –1 VP to the CP at Armistice (line 1143), but there is no rule awarding the AP an automatic victory or special win for capturing it. The summary invents "capturing Constantinople" and "controlling enough Ottoman territory" as named win conditions that do not exist in the source.

**No support found** for "capturing Constantinople" or "controlling enough Ottoman territory" as standalone victory conditions (searched: "Constantinople.*victor", "capture Constantinople", "AP wins", "AP Player wins", "Allied.*Constantinople").

### Finding 6 — Missing CP Automatic Victory Path via War-Weariness Marker (MAJOR)

**Summary (Scoring / Victory Conditions):** Mentions only that the CP wins "by controlling enough VP locations or forcing a political collapse of enemy will" — vague phrasing with no specifics.

**Source (lines 1092–1096):** "Also, the CP Player wins if the CP Automatic Victory Marker and the VP Marker are in the same space on the General Records Track (or if the VP Marker is in a higher space)—the Allies have grown weary of the sideshow and negotiated a separate peace with the Ottoman Empire."

This is a distinct second CP automatic victory condition (independent of VP ≥ 20), based on the position of a separate Automatic Victory Marker on the track. The summary gives no VP thresholds (0 for AP, 20 for CP) and does not describe this second CP trigger at all. A player relying on the summary would not know the specific VP thresholds or this marker mechanism.

### Finding 7 — Armistice Victory Table Completely Absent (MAJOR)

**Summary:** No mention of the Armistice victory table or its VP thresholds.

**Source (lines 1150–1161):** 
"To determine Victory after an Armistice, use the following table:
- 14 or more VPs → CP Marginal Victory
- 10–13 VPs → CP Victory of Endurance
- 7–9 VPs → AP Victory of Endurance
- 6 or fewer VPs → AP Marginal Victory"

The game ends in Armistice (either when the combined War Status reaches 40, determined by a die roll 3–5 turns ahead, or at end of Fall 1918). The armistice is a primary game-end path with its own separate victory determination table. The summary never describes this, instead saying only "The game can end prior to the last turn if a player achieves an Automatic Victory or if an Armistice occurs" — but gives no information on how victory is then determined. Players could not score a completed game that ends in Armistice.

### Finding 8 — Turn Structure Missing Phases: Siege Phase, Revolution Phase, Draw Strategy Card Phase (MINOR-to-MAJOR boundary)

**Summary (Turn Structure):** Lists only 5 phases. Omits:
- D. Siege Phase (die roll to reduce besieged Forts)
- E. Revolution Phase (Tribal Warfare Check, Jihad Revolts, Russian Revolution check)
- H. Draw Strategy Card Phase (discard CCs, draw to 7, reshuffle if depleted)

**Source (lines 1197–1267):** These three phases are explicitly listed in the Sequence of Play. The Russian Revolution check and reshuffle rule are important game mechanics. The summary references "Russian Revolution events can remove Russia from the war" in Special Rules, so the omission of the Revolution Phase from Turn Structure is at least partial — but the Siege Phase and Draw Phase are completely absent.

The Draw Phase rule is the exhaustion/reshuffle rule: "If a player's Draw Pile is exhausted, he reshuffles his discard pile and draws enough cards to complete his hand." The summary does not state this anywhere — omitted but not fabricated.
