## guild-ball

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented mechanic: scoring model removed after goal

**Summary:** "The scoring model is removed and returns during the next Maintenance Phase after being taken out."

**Source:** The source never states this. After a goal, the source says only: "During an activation, if a friendly model scores a goal, the activation immediately ends." (line 1169–1170). The taken-out condition in the source applies only to models reduced to 0 HP: "When a model is reduced to 0 HP, it suffers the taken out condition and is removed from the pitch." (line 852–854). Scoring a goal is not listed as a cause of the taken-out condition anywhere in the 83KB rulebook.

**Searched:** "scoring model", "goal.*suffers.*taken", "model.*goal.*removed", "goal.*taken out condition" — no matches. The only mention of "scoring model" in the source is "Run the Length! After scoring a goal, at the end of the current activation, the scoring model may spend 1 MP to make a 4" dodge." (line 1856–1858).

**Impact:** Players would incorrectly remove the scoring model each time a goal is scored, changing the game fundamentally (6v6 would drop to 5v6 temporarily after every goal).

### Finding 2 — Wrong/incomplete cost for Shot on Goal

**Summary:** "Shot on Goal (1 INF): Kick the ball at the goal post. TN 4+. If successful, score a goal (4 VP)."

**Source:** "In order to make a Shot, a model must spend 1 MP in addition to the influence cost for making a kick." (lines 1166–1168). The cost is 1 INF + 1 MP. The summary omits the mandatory 1 MP cost.

**Impact:** Players would attempt shots for only 1 INF, without spending the required momentum.

### Finding 3 — Initiative description oversimplified but not materially wrong

**Summary (Turn Structure / End Phase):** "Determine initiative for next turn using momentum (team with more momentum chooses who goes first)."

**Source:** "Both players look at their chosen game plans and secretly select one to play. Players reveal their chosen cards simultaneously and compare the initiative values of those cards. Each player additionally adds +1 to their initiative value for each of their unspent MP from the previous turn. The player with the highest net initiative decides which team has the initiative." (lines 736–749).

**Note:** The summary omits Game Plans entirely from the initiative mechanic and reduces it to "team with more momentum." This is inaccurate — unspent momentum adds only +1 per MP, and the game plan's own initiative value is the primary factor. However, this finding alone would be MINOR given the two MAJOR findings above already determine the verdict.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5 (12 VP win condition, 4 VP goal, 2 VP take-out, 1 VP mascot take-out, 1 INF sprint, 2 INF charge, 1 INF attack, 1 INF kick, 1 MP heroic play), SUPPORTED: 8/9 (shot cost unsupported as noted above)

## hacienda

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Wrong market scoring: flat "4 pts per market" is incorrect; source uses a progressive table

**Summary:** "Markets: 4 points per market connected"

**Source:** The scoring table was not fully extracted (lines 289–290 show only "Markets / Points" headers, table body lost). However, the scoring example directly contradicts the summary:
- Source example: "Red has connected to 5 markets... That results in 15 points." (lines 325–327). 5 × 4 = 20, not 15.
- Source text: "A player whose herds connect to 7 different markets receives 28 points." (line 291). 7 × 4 = 28 — this coincidentally matches, but 5 markets giving only 15 (not 20) proves the scale is NOT flat.

**Impact:** Players would compute incorrect scores for every player at the table. With 5 markets, the summary yields 20 pts vs. the correct 15 pts.

### Finding 2 — Wrong Hacienda scoring: "1 point each" is incorrect; source scores tile-count of the chain/herd

**Summary:** "Haciendas: 1 point each (placed on chains or herds)"

**Source:** "A Hacienda will score as many points during scoring as the size of the land chain or herd that it stands upon." (lines 157–158). The scoring section confirms: "Points for Haciendas. All land tiles in a chain, or all animal tiles in a herd, which has a Hacienda upon it are scored... Example: A player possesses 2 Haciendas; one stands on a 7-space land chain, and the other one stands on a herd which consists of 6 sheep. The player receives 7 + 6 = 13 points." (lines 304–309).

**Impact:** A hacienda on a 7-tile chain scores 7 points, not 1. Every scoring calculation using this rule would be badly wrong.

### Verified correct claims:
- Land chains: "2 points per tile in chains of 3+ tiles." Source: "Number of land tiles times 2 points" for chains of 3+ tiles. Matches.
- Water adjacency: "1 point per adjacent water tile." Source: "For each land and/or animal tile which borders a water panel tile, the owner scores 1 point." Matches.
- Money: "1 point per 10 pesos." Source: "For every 10 pesos a player has, he receives 1 point." Matches.
- Two scoring rounds (mid-game and end-game). Source confirms: first scoring when first animal stack depleted, final scoring when second stack depleted. Matches.
- Starting resources: 20 pesos, 8 land cards, 4 animal cards, 1 hacienda, 30 animal tokens. Source line 108: "Each player starts with a Hacienda (on the scoring track), 30 animal tokens, 8 land cards, 4 animal cards, 20 pesos, 1 rules summary." Matches.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 9 (20 pesos start, 8 land cards, 4 animal cards, 30 animal tokens, land chain 2pts/tile, water 1pt/tile, money 1pt/10 pesos, market pts for 5 markets, hacienda scoring), SUPPORTED: 7 (market table and hacienda scoring wrong)

## haggis

**Verdict: PASS**

### Verification

**Victory/game-end:** Summary: "Play rounds until a target cumulative score (250 for short, 350 for long) is reached. Tied scores: continue playing until one player leads." Source: "Continue playing rounds until an agreed upon cumulative score is reached (or passed) by at least one player. The player with the most points wins. If the scores are tied, continue playing rounds until there is a winner. For a shorter game, 250 points is suggested. For a longer game, 350 points is suggested." (lines 158–162). Matches.

**Scoring per round (all 3 sources verified):**
1. "The player who goes out scores 5 points per card remaining in the hand of the opponent who held the most cards." Source confirms verbatim. Matches.
2. "All point cards (3, 5, 7, 9, J, Q, K) captured during tricks score their point value." Source confirms. Matches.
3. "If the bettor went out first, they score the bet amount. If the bettor did NOT go out first, the bet amount goes to the player who went out first AND to any other opponent who did not bet." Source: "If the bet was not successful the amount bet will be added to the score of the player first to 'go out' and any other opponent that did not bet on this round." Matches.

**Bomb table (all 6 entries verified against source):** 3-5-7-9 in 4 suits / J-Q / J-K / Q-K / J-Q-K / 3-5-7-9 in 1 suit — exact order confirmed by source lines 68–73. Matches.

**Trick end conditions:** 2-player (one pass) and 3-player (two successive passes) — both confirmed.

**Procedural claims (5 verified):**
1. Lead any combination; others follow with same type/size at higher rank or pass.
2. Bombs beat any non-bomb; only higher bomb beats a bomb.
3. Winner of trick leads next trick. Source confirms.
4. Bomb trick: cards captured by an opponent (winner chooses). Source confirms.
5. End of round: last player captures final trick cards (unless bomb); all remaining hand cards + Haggis go to player who went out first. Source confirms.

**Dealer and first-lead rules:** Both confirmed by source.

**Setup:** "2-player game: Remove one complete suit, one Jack, one Queen, and one King." Source: "With two players, remove one complete suit, one Jack, one Queen, and one King from the deck." Matches.

**Exhaustion rules:** Summary says "Set aside remaining cards (8 for 2 players, 3 for 3 players) as the Haggis -- do not look at them." Source confirms. No invented reshuffle rule — the Haggis is not reshuffled; it is captured by the player who went out first at the end of each round. Summary correctly describes this.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (250 short game, 350 long game, 5 pts per card, point values 1/2/3/5 for 3-5-7-9/J/Q/K, 14 dealt cards, 3 wild cards, 8 Haggis for 2p, 3 Haggis for 3p, bet levels 0/15/30, 6 bomb types), SUPPORTED: 12
