## angola — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR — the summary is largely fabricated; it does not describe the game in the source rulebook (Angola! by Phil Kendall, MMP edition).**

1. **Invented turn structure.** Summary: "1. Political Phase ... 2. Command Phase: Activate units using command points. 3. Movement Phase ... 4. Combat Phase ... 5. Supply Phase ... 6. End Phase." Source (4.1): "Each Turn consists of three phases: Planning Phase ... Operations Phase ... End Phase," driven by Operations Cards/Packs ("each player turns over the top Operations Card in his Operations Pack"). There are no Political/Command/Movement/Combat/Supply phases and no "command points." The card-driven Operations system — the heart of the game — is entirely absent from the summary.
2. **Invented supply system.** Summary: "Supply Phase: Check supply lines and attrition," "Supply lines must be maintained from home bases or ports," "unsupplied units suffer attrition." The word "supply" (in the logistics sense) appears nowhere in the source; there are no supply-line or attrition rules.
3. **Wrong faction structure.** Summary: factions are "MPLA, FNLA, UNITA, and potentially Cuba/South Africa"; "Key Factions: MPLA (Soviet-backed), FNLA (Zaire-backed), UNITA (US-backed)." Source: four player factions — "FAPLA, MPLA, UNITA and FNLA"; "FAPLA and MPLA form the Soviet-backed alliance, and UNITA and FNLA form the Western-backed alliance." FAPLA is omitted entirely; Cuba/South Africa/Zaire are foreign-aid Major Units, not player factions.
4. **Wrong victory conditions.** Summary: "Victory is determined by control of key regions, especially Luanda ... The faction controlling the most strategic regions wins." Source (11.1): "Only an Alliance can win the game. A Faction cannot win"; victory is via the Victory Track, decisive victory checks turns 1–9, end-game victory on turn 10, driven by "capture of towns and cities (10.3) and ... winning the propaganda war (12.4.3)." Game "always ends after ten turns" (2.2) — the summary's "typically ends after a set number of turns" hides a fixed length.
5. **Invented components.** Summary: "Political markers, Supply and logistics markers, Combat resolution tables, Cards (event/action)." Source (1.3): one rule book, two counter sheets, one 34×22" map, four player aid cards, "One deck of 110 cards (65 playing cards plus 45 duplicates)." No political or supply markers exist.
6. **Wrong designer.** Frontmatter: "Nick Smith, Peter Wingrove, Jeremy Sherlock." Source credits: "Original Idea: Phil Kendall. Game Development: Phil Kendall, Roger Greenwood."
7. **Numbers:** the summary contains essentially no checkable numbers (itself a red flag). Source numbers absent from summary: 10 turns, 110 cards, 4 factions, 5 Victory Tokens and 4 Major Units per faction, 3 phases.

## anti-monopoly — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR — numbers are essentially perfect; two small rule omissions could cause table confusion.**

Numbers checked (all supported):
1. 3 blue + 3 green pawns; 25 Competitor + 25 Monopolist cards; 28 title cards = source contents.
2. Money 50×$1, 40×$5, 50×$10, 50×$50, 50×$100, 20×$500; 35 houses; 15 apartments = source contents; "amounts in thousands" = source.
3. $1,500 start: "two $500, three $100, two $50, seven $10, five $5 and five $1" = source Rule 5 verbatim.
4. $100 on landing/passing START = Rule 14.
5. $50 to leave Prison/Price War; doubles on first two turns = Rule 15.
6. Income Tax: $200 or Monopolist 20% cash + 10% property + 10% improvements; Competitor 10%/10%/10% = Rule 17.
7. Property Tax $75 = Rule 18.
8. Foundation: roll 1 die, 1→$25, 2→$50, else nothing; Monopolists pay $10 = Rule 21.
9. Utilities 4×/10×; transportation 10% vs doubling fares = Rules 19–20.
10. Houses: Competitors 4 then apartment; Monopolists 3 then apartment (monopolized cities only) = Rules 10–11. Mortgage = half price = Rule 12. Two-Hour Game keep 10%/20% = source.

Findings:
1. **Omission — "sightseeing."** Source Rule 15: landing on the Prison/Price War space "during a regular move ... they are just sightseeing ... they pay no penalties to leave"; penalties apply only when *sent* there. The summary presents Prison/Price War with no sightseeing case, which could make a player wrongly pay $50 after a normal landing.
2. **Omission — three-street cities.** Source Rule 10: "When the city has three streets, monopolists can charge double on that street also when acquired." Summary stops at "own any 2 streets ... double rent on those streets."
3. Trivial: "Dice (2)" — source contents just say "Dice" (2 implied by doubles); "Game board" not in the source contents list; voluntary early $50 payment (Rule 15 option 2) compressed.

## antike — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR — Components/Setup/Scoring are accurate, but the Actions section contains invented know-hows, wrong costs, and wrong production/combat math.**

Numbers checked that ARE supported: 35 personages (9 kings/8 scholars/7 generals/6 citizens/5 navigators); 12 nation cards; 30 coins; 20 temples; 150 city stones (25/color); 102 legions; 102 galleys; 36 game stones; 50 provinces per side; start 1 iron/2 marble/3 gold; 17 legions/17 galleys/25 city stones/6 game stones per nation; temple = 5 marble; arming = 1 iron, 1 per city / 3 with temple; win thresholds 7/8/9/10 for 6/5/4/3 nations; 2-player target 9; city defense 1 / 3 with temple; rondel 1–3 free, repeat = 8 wedges/5 resources; all-35-awarded tiebreak.

Findings:
1. **Wrong production bonuses.** Summary: "Market (+1 per city) and Currency (+2 per city)." Source: Market = "one extra unit" per production action, Currency = "two extra units" — totals, not per city. Source example: 5 gold production "rises to 6 [Market], and with CURRENCY, to 7." With several cities the summary massively overstates production.
2. **Invented know-how costs.** Summary table: "Technical 1st: 1G, 2nd: 4G; Economic 1st: 3G, 2nd: 8G; Political: various." Source: "7 gold units per each NEW basic know-how and 10 per each NEW secondary ... 3 gold units per each basic and five for each secondary that one or more of the nations already possess." None of the summary's costs exist.
3. **Invented know-hows / wrong structure.** Summary: "Political: Democracy, Republic, Theocracy (and more)," "8 know-hows across 3 tracks." Source: exactly 8 know-hows in 4 basic/secondary pairs — wheel→roads, sailing→navigation, market→currency, monarchy→democracy. There is no Republic or Theocracy; Wheel, Sailing and Monarchy are omitted entirely (Monarchy +1 / Democracy +2 city defense never mentioned).
4. **Wrong movement rates.** Summary: "Move all units 1 border (2 with Road/Navigation)." Source: base 1 maneuver action; WHEEL/SAILING = "up to two"; ROADS/NAVIGATION = "up to three" actions per unit.
5. **Wrong conquest threshold + omitted attacker cost.** Summary: "Attackers need more legions/galleys than the defense value." Source: units "must be at least equal to the defense strength" (≥, not >), and crucially "The conquering nation clears the board of its active ... military units equal to the defense strength of the city" — the attacker sacrifices units; the summary never mentions this core cost.
6. **Invented founding cost.** Summary: city founding costs "1 marble + 1 iron + 1 gold per city, plus 1 coin per adjacent city producing the same resource." Source: "the nation must pay the bank one marble, one iron and one gold unit" — no adjacency surcharge (that rule belongs to Antike Duellum, not Antike).
7. **Battles stated as automatic.** Summary: units of the same type "destroy each other 1:1" on meeting. Source: movement into an occupied province "may result in a battle if either of the nations involved wants it" — fights are optional.

## antike-duellum — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR — wrong temple and arming costs, wrong personage counts, and the recruitment-box economy (a core system) is missing.**

Numbers checked that ARE supported: 74 wooden pieces (24 galleys, 24 legions, 2 markers, 12 disks, 12 temples); 34 city tokens (10 gold/12 marble/12 iron); 12 town walls; 54 cards (21 personages, 25 events, 2 blank, 6 short rules); setup 12 galleys/12 legions/6 disks/1 town wall, 1 legion + 1 galley per recruitment box, 3M/3I/3G start, loser-of-start-roll gets 1 coin, 3-card open event display; rondel 1–3 free / 8 fields = 5 chips; founding 1M+1I+1G + 1 coin per adjacent same-resource city; defense = 1 (3 with temple) + wall +1 + Res Publica +1 + defenders; 9 personages to win; Kings 6, Generals 4, Citizens 4; town-wall milestones at 0/1/2/3/5/7.

Findings:
1. **Wrong temple cost.** Summary: "Build temples (5 marble each)." Source: "For each new temple, it pays the bank 6 marble chips" plus "1 coin must be paid for each temple that already exists in an adjacent region" — and the differences section stresses "Building a temple now costs 6 marble chips instead of 5 as in Antike." The adjacency coin cost is also omitted.
2. **Wrong arming cost.** Summary: "Recruit units (1 iron each)." Source: "This costs 2 iron chips per new military unit" ("instead of 1 as in Antike").
3. **Recruitment-box system omitted.** Source: "Only units that are inside the recruitment box at the beginning of the turn may be armed"; Scientia also lets you recruit from personal supply into the box at "1 gold chip for each legion ... 2 gold chips for each galley"; battle/conquest losses return to the recruitment box, not the supply. The summary's Scientia is "Buy know-hows with gold" only — the entire unit-cycling economy is missing.
4. **Wrong personage counts.** Summary: Scholars 4, Navigators 3. Source: "5 SCHOLARS" and "2 NAVIGATOR" (with 6 kings, 4 citizens, 4 generals = 21 total; the summary's 6+4+4+4+3 also sums to 21, masking the error).
5. **Navigator double-count omitted.** Source: "Sea regions without a city ... count double for the purpose of gaining Navigators ... 4 galleys could be enough." Summary just says "Every 7 seas with your galley at turn end."
6. **Production coin omitted.** Source: when producing, "In addition, he receives 1 coin, no matter which resource or how many he produces" (this replaces Antike's coin-per-round). The summary never says how coins are acquired.
7. **Conquest attacker losses omitted.** Source: "The conqueror removes as many military units from the city's region as the defense strength of the city, and puts them back into his recruitment box." Summary only says "Attacker needs units >= defense to conquer."
8. Smaller omissions: city-loss event card draw ("If the loss of at least of one city occurred, the loosing player chooses 1 event card ... not 1 per city!"); MONETA and COMMERCIUM (3-for-2 bank trade) know-hows; max 12 temples on board (count appears only in components).

2. Reserve 11 (3p) / 14 (4p) doeples = source.
3. Starting food pile 9 (5×1 + 4×2) for 3p; 12 (6×1 + 6×2) for 4p = source.
4. 2 starting antler pieces per stag = source.
5. End trigger 12 (3p) / 10 (4p) on doe track = source (intro and section D).
6. Max 5 food held; max 3 tokens committed per fight; max 2 food per space; doe sets of 3 (3p)/4 (4p), max 2 sets per round; max 3 wily tiles = all in source.
7. Scoring 3 pts centre / 1 pt middle / 0 outside; 1 pt per doe = source.
8. Tiebreakers: largest antlers, highest up island, condition (food value), player height = source order.
9. Antler math: total/2 rounded down; single token always = 1 = source (incl. examples).
10. Advanced game: 2 cubes (3p)/3 cubes (4p); bonus 1 pt all own cubes, 2 pts (3p)/3 pts (4p) for full set from opponents = source.
11. No-fight-won = zero points and automatic loss = source.

The summary states food loss only for draws; the loser's antler loss is only implied via the Diamond Antler tile description. A winner might wrongly keep committed food.
2. **Slight drift — loser placement.** Summary: "Winner places loser on a non-uphill space." Source: winner places loser on a stag-free space "no higher up the hill than their own piece, unless there are no other unoccupied spaces."
3. Trivial: attacker "commits 1-3 food tokens" — source says at least one *if they have one* (zero-token attackers possible); Speed tile's "may not pass through other stags" and Greedy's "must take at least one token normally" conditions omitted; advanced-game cube-selection details (own color vs loser's color, max 1 of any foreign color) compressed.
