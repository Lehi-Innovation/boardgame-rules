# Audit Findings — Batch b141

## marvel-heroclix-infinity-challenge

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Token removal timing REVERSED (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Beginning of Turn: Remove action tokens from characters that did not act last turn (allowing them to act this turn)."

**Source (line 767–768):**
> "At the end of your turn, remove the tokens from your figures that did not take an action this turn."

The summary reverses the timing: tokens are removed at the *end* of your turn, not the beginning. This matters because pushed characters (those with two tokens) have those tokens removed at the start of *your next turn* — a nuance consistent with the end-of-turn removal rule. A player using the summary would mistrack action token timing.

### Finding 2 — Scoring: Surviving friendly figures omitted (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Point-based: Score points equal to defeated characters' point values. Highest score wins."

**Source (lines 2130–2136):**
> "Every opposing figure that you defeat during the game is worth a number of victory points equal to its point value."
> "Every friendly figure that started the game on the battlefield and remained there until the end of the game is worth a number of victory points equal to its point value."

The summary omits the entire second scoring source: surviving friendly figures each score their own point value. This is a core scoring rule — a game could be decided by surviving figures — and its omission means players cannot correctly total victory points from the summary alone.

### Finding 3 — Regeneration mechanic wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Regeneration: Roll d6; on 5-6, heal clicks"

**Source (lines 3183–3187):**
> "REGENERATION. This character can heal himself up to 4 clicks. (Optional) Give this character a move action, but do not move him. Roll 1 six-sided die and subtract 2 from the result. Treat a negative result as 0. The result is the number of clicks that are healed on this character's combat dial."

The actual mechanic heals (die roll − 2) clicks, ranging from 0 to 4. The summary replaces this with a binary "5–6 = heal" trigger that does not exist in the source. The mechanic is completely wrong.

### Finding 4 — Object damage values conflated and incorrect (MAJOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Light objects (damage +1, range 6), heavy objects (damage +2, range 4)"

**Source (lines 1749–1765):**
> Close combat: "a light object inflicts 1 click of damage in addition to the damage inflicted by the character; a heavy object inflicts 2 additional clicks of damage."
> Ranged (thrown): "A light object has a range of 6 squares and will inflict 2 clicks of damage on a successful attack. A heavy object has a range of 4 and will inflict 3 clicks of damage."

The summary pairs the close-combat bonus values (+1/+2) with the range values (6/4), making it appear as though thrown objects deal +1 and +2 damage. The actual thrown damage is 2 clicks (light) and 3 clicks (heavy). Players using the summary would incorrectly apply close-combat bonuses to ranged throws and undercount thrown damage.

---

### Finding 5 — Ranged attack restriction mischaracterized (MINOR)

**Summary claims:**
> "Cannot target adjacent characters with ranged attacks."

**Source (lines 1183–1184):**
> "If the range value is greater than 0 and your character is not in base contact with an opposing figure, then you may give your character a ranged combat action."

The actual restriction is that a character *in base contact with an opposing figure* cannot make a ranged attack — not that adjacent figures cannot be *targeted*. An attacker not adjacent to enemies can target adjacent characters with ranged attacks. This distinction matters (e.g., hovering characters adjacent to opponents can make ranged attacks). However, in the most common ground-combat case it produces the same outcome. Classified MINOR rather than MAJOR because it does not affect scoring, but would mislead players in edge cases.
