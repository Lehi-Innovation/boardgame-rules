### Air ZOC Rule — FINDING (MINOR)

The summary conflates two different Air ZOC movement rules that differ by phase:

**Summary (Special Rules section):** "Enemy units entering an Air ZOC must stop (or move one additional hex if already in one)."

This partially mixes Sea Deployment and Operational Movement rules:

**Source — Sea Deployment (lines 944-948):** "Units may move into an enemy Air ZOC (3.3.2), but must stop there. If starting in an enemy Air ZOC, they may leave that hex, but must stop if they enter another hex under an enemy Air ZOC."

**Source — Operational Movement (lines 1083-1087):** "If a unit enters an enemy Air ZOC, it can either stop or move one more hex. If starting in an enemy Air ZOC, it may move one hex only (leaving the hex it started in), even if the destination is not in an enemy Air ZOC."

The summary description ("must stop (or move one additional hex if already in one)") reverses the conditional: it says "stop" when entering (correct for Deployment, wrong for Operational Movement where you can also move one more hex), and "one additional hex if already in one" (correct for Operational Movement when starting in ZOC, but wrong framing for Deployment). This is an imprecise merge that could mislead in Operational Movement scenarios.

This error is unlikely to completely derail play since the core mechanic (ZOC restricts movement) is correct, but it omits the "or move one more hex" option during Operational Movement. Verdict: MINOR — the simplified description understates the movement option in Operational Movement.

### Tile Actions — Market Tiles Missing Gold Coin Costs (MAJOR)

**Summary (Tile Actions table):**
> "Small Market (compulsory): Draw top 3 Resource cards; keep 1, discard others face-up."
> "Large Market (compulsory): Draw top 6 Resource cards; keep 2, discard others face-up."

**Source (lines 385-397):**
> "SMALL MARKET: Pay 3 Gold Coins and pick one Resource card of your choice from among the first 3 visible (at the start of the line)."
> "LARGE MARKET: Pay 6 Gold Coins and pick 2 Resource cards of your choice from among the first 6 visible (at the start of the line)."

The summary completely omits the gold coin costs (3 GC for Small Market, 6 GC for Large Market). It also incorrectly describes the action as "draw top X cards, keep Y" when the source says players choose from the face-up display without drawing new cards. Players using the summary would never pay gold for market visits, completely distorting the economy.

### Sacred Places — Wrong Cost (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Pay 1 or 2 Elders (or Fakirs as wild) to take 1 face-up Djinn or draw 2 to pick 1."

**Source (lines 398-403):**
> "SACRED PLACES: Pay either 2 Elders OR 1 Elder + 1 Fakir to take the Djinn of your choice, from among those visible."

Two errors:
1. The summary says "Pay 1 or 2 Elders" implying 1 Elder is sometimes sufficient. The source always requires 2 (either 2 Elders or 1 Elder + 1 Fakir).
2. The summary says "draw 2 to pick 1" — the source has no such option; players choose from the face-up visible Djinns.

**Source (lines 469-471):**

The source provides no Gold Coins tiebreaker. The summary invents one. This is a fabricated rule.

### Tiebreaker — Reversed Order (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Ties broken by: most unspent Coins (each worth 1 Reputation), then most Goods, then most Artisan Dragons in hand."

**Source (lines 493-497):**
> "In the case of a tie, the tied player with the most Artisan Dragons left in their hand wins. If still tied, the tied player with the most leftover Goods in their supply wins. If still tied, they share a well-deserved victory!"

The tiebreaker order is completely reversed. The source uses: 1) most Artisan Dragons, 2) most Goods, then shared victory. The summary inverts this to Coins → Goods → Artisan Dragons. Additionally, Coins are not used as a tiebreaker in the source — they are converted to Reputation at end of game before tiebreaking.

### End-of-Game Trigger and Final Turns

**Summary:** "Finish the current round so all players have equal turns."

**Source (lines 479-482):**
> "When the last card in either the Artisan deck or Enchantment deck is drawn or revealed, this triggers the end of the game. Each player gets 1 final turn, including the player who triggered the game end."

The summary says "finish the current round" (implying all remaining players in the round get turns), while the source says "each player gets 1 final turn" — meaning the triggering player also gets their final turn (they don't stop immediately). These are equivalent for most practical purposes: each remaining player in the round gets exactly 1 more turn.
