## circus-maximus — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Core turn-order mechanic contradicted, an invented speed-based second movement phase, several invented setup/optional rules, wrong Preparation Point categories, and a wrong lap count alternative. The summary would mislead players on how the game's central movement system works.

### Numbers checked (10; 5 supported, 2 wrong/unsupported, 3 partially supported)

| # | Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "typically 3 or 7" laps to win | Race is fixed at 3 laps: "The finish line, at the end of the third lap" (l.617); 7 laps only appears as a historical aside ("in reality, the Romans ran a [seven-lap race]", l.3162) | N |
| 2 | 2-12 players (frontmatter + Setup step 1) | "Between two and eight people can play" (l.391); a 9th is steward, 10th money changer (l.392-395); Enlarged Field allows >8 chariots/players (l.2163-2165), so 2-12 is BGG-style metadata, not the stated base range of 2-8 | Partial |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Movement order contradicted.** Summary: "Chariots move in order (fastest first, or by position)." Source: order is **random** — the steward draws color counters from a cup one at a time (l.622-625, 652-657). This is the game's core sequencing mechanic and the summary states it wrongly.
2. **MAJOR — Invented second movement phase.** Summary: "Some chariots may have a second movement phase if their speed warrants it." Source 5.4: the "Second Movement Phase" is simply the second random counter drawn; every faction moves exactly once per turn ("At this point, all factions have moved once and the turn is over", l.664-673). No speed-based extra phases exist.
3. **MAJOR — Wrong/invented Preparation Point categories.** Summary: allocate among "Horse Endurance, Driver Skill, Chariot Structure, and Equipment." Source 3.6: four Preparation Points are split among **Driver Modifier (3.6.1), Car type — light/normal/heavy (3.6.2), Team Speed (3.6.3), and Endurance (3.6.4)** (l.524-600). "Equipment" is invented, "Chariot Structure" is not a category (all cars have 10 hit boxes per wheel regardless of type, l.558), and Team Speed is missing.
4. **MAJOR — Invented optional rules.** Summary: "Optional rules add gladiatorial combat, team racing, and advanced chariot customization" and "Different track configurations are possible." Source optional rules (27.x) are: Detailed Specification Method, Special Weapons, Horse Carcasses, First Turn Acceleration, Voluntary Straining, Weather/Rain/Mud, plus WRECKUS MAXIMUS (l.224-244). No gladiatorial combat, no team racing, no alternate track configurations anywhere in the text (grep confirms zero hits).
5. **MINOR — Invented "bidding" for start positions.** Summary: starting positions "through a random process or bidding." Source: "Place the eight chariots randomly in their starting locations" (l.398-399). No bidding.
6. **MINOR — Lash attack target wrong.** Summary: "Whipping attacks target the opposing driver specifically." Source 9.4: "Lash attacks can be against either the horse team or the driver, depending on which element of the defender's chariot is beside the attacker's car" (l.1435-1437).
7. **MINOR — Sideslip mischaracterized as an attack.** Summary lists "Sideslip: Forcing an opponent into the wall or another chariot" under Combat. Source 6.7: sideslip is the *moving* chariot's own 3-MF escape move when blocked (l.945-972). The forced-sideways effect the summary describes is the "S = Swerve" combat result (l.1486-1503), a different rule.
8. **MINOR — Same-space claim contradicted.** Summary: "Multiple chariots in the same space create collision risks." Source 6.2.3: "At no time may more than one car or team counter occupy the same square" (l.740-741); blocking instead triggers involuntary ram (l.979-983).
9. **MINOR — Overview victory sentence garbled.** "The last chariot to complete the required laps (or the first to cross the finish) wins" — first across the finish line wins, with remaining-MF then draw-order tiebreaks (l.1702-1730). The Scoring section's "first chariot to complete the required number of laps wins" is correct.

Supported claims (for balance): secret simultaneous speed plotting (l.647-649), straining/braking/evading mechanics (6.5, 6.6, 9.3), corner strain and flips (6.3.1, 7.x), zero endurance slowing (8.4.3: team speed -1 per turn), wrecks remaining as obstacles (12.x, 14.x), running down a driver on the track (17.4), campaign game with money/experience/betting (19-26), Racing Steward role (3.9, 11.x), betting before the race and at end of laps 1 and 2 (l.2743-2744).

## cities-skylines-the-board-game — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component counts and the basic action/adjacency rules are accurate, but the summary misstates the Exchange action (omits its 2-money cost, wrong draw pile), invents a "deal new cards" step at milestone end in place of the actual paid exchange, omits the mandatory card draw after playing a construction card (core turn loop), and omits the happiness-based loss conditions and the stricter final-milestone requirement.

### Numbers checked (14/14 supported)

| # | Claim in summary | Source evidence | OK |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 game board pieces (A-F) | "6 Game board pieces (back sides A-F)" (l.22) | Y |
| 2 | 24 residential / 12 commercial / 12 industrial zones | l.23-25 | Y |
| 3 | 9 utility / 18 service / 10 unique buildings | l.26-28 | Y |
| 4 | 30 money tokens (4x 5-unit, 26x 1-unit) | l.31, 35-37 | Y |
| 5 | 75 construction cards (25 per stage) | l.33-34 | Y |
| 6 | 8 administration markers | l.38-39 | Y |
| 7 | 2 happiness indicators; 1 skyline | l.47-48, 52-53 | Y |
| 8 | 1 "End of a Milestone" card; 4 turn overview cards | l.49-51 | Y |
| 9 | 10 unique building / 10 role / 10 policies / 10 news cards | l.54-58 | Y |
| 10 | Starting capital 12 money | "starting capital of 12 money tokens" (l.98) | Y |
| 11 | Card deal: 4P=3, 3P=4, 2P=5, 1P=7 | l.107-110 | Y |
| 12 | Utility below 0: -1 Happiness per step at milestone end | l.126-127, 335-341 | Y |
| 13 | Employment: pay 1 money per step from 0 (above or below) | l.134-139, 435-443 | Y |
| 14 | Pollution/traffic/crime above 0 reduce overall Happiness at game end | l.144-145, 467-470 | Y |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Exchange action misstated.** Summary: "Swap one of your cards with a new one from the current stage's draw pile." Source: the player **pays two money tokens**, places the card on the exchange pile, then draws "a new card from **any of the three** Stage I, II, and III piles he or she likes" or takes any card from the exchange pile (l.320-329). The cost — the entire constraint on this action — is omitted, and the draw-pile restriction is invented.
2. **MAJOR — Invented end-of-milestone step.** Summary: "Deal new construction cards for the next stage." Source step 5: starting with the starting player, each player **may** put any number of cards on the exchange pile and draw an equal number from piles I-III, paying **1 money per card** (l.455-461). No free dealing of next-stage cards occurs.
3. **MAJOR — Core turn loop omission: draw a new card after playing.** Source: after playing a construction card, "The player adds to his or her card display by drawing one card from any of the three face-down piles... of the player's choice" or from the exchange pile (l.386-391). The summary never mentions this; players following it would deplete their hands and never choose draw stages (a central strategic decision per the tips, l.412-417).
4. **MAJOR — Loss conditions omitted.** Summary: "Losing: If the city treasury runs out of money, all players lose." Source also: Happiness marker at "-5" or overall Happiness at "-10" loses immediately (l.358-359, 382-383, 479-481), and "During a player's turn, he or she cannot perform any of the possible actions" (l.479-480).
5. **MINOR — Final milestone requirement omitted.** Summary says ending a milestone needs "at least one tile in each city district" (correct, l.183-184) but omits that ending the **last** milestone requires all board pieces revealed and "in each district there must be at least two tiles of any kind" (l.465-466).
6. **MINOR — Card-play limits omitted.** Cards may not be played if treasury can't pay, if a utility/pollution marker would pass the end of the red range, or if employment would pass ±5; Happiness caps at 25 with overflow to the Skyline (l.360-383).
7. **MINOR — Scenario content omitted.** Overview mentions scenarios and Scenario 4, and components list the cards, but the rules for Unique Buildings, Role, Policies, and News cards (Scenarios 1-4, l.512-574) and the scenario starting capital of 10 money (l.509; Scenario 5 difficulty 12/10/8, l.604-607) are absent. Layered optional content, but it is "all rules and elements" per the summary's own framing.
8. **Trivial —** "2 mounting feet" missing from components; end-of-milestone step order differs slightly from source (utilities → transfer happiness → employment → new board piece → exchange); empty-piles forced game end (l.393-395) omitted.

Supported claims (for balance): cooperative play and active-player-decides rule (l.8-9), youngest player starts (l.118-120), board flip on longitudinal axis preserving water (l.165-167), zone cards free / building cards cost money (l.194-205), any tile from matching zone category (l.217-219), placement restrictions (l.172-174), one utility building per district (l.226-229), Stage I adjacency vs Stage II/III same-district (l.231-235, 285-289), no retroactive benefits (l.212-214), tiles immovable (l.224-225), success table consultation (l.471-474).
