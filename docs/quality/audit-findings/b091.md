## fearsome-floors

### Prefilter
Prefilter returned WARN SOURCE_MISMATCH (no title tokens found), but manual inspection confirms the source IS the correct rulebook. The PDF is the English translation of the German "Fische Fluppen Frikadellen / Fairytale" sequel, which is Fearsome Floors. The theme, components, monster name (Furunkulus), and all mechanics match exactly.

### Finding 1 — Direction reversal: starting player tile passes left (counterclockwise), not clockwise

**Summary (line 52):** "Pass the starting player tile clockwise."

**Source (line 150):** "After moving the monster, the next player to the left gets the starting player tile and the game continues with phase 1."

In standard board game convention, "to the left" = counterclockwise; "clockwise" = to the right. This is a direction reversal that affects every round.

Numbers match. ✓

**Summary (line 78):** "Stage 2: Begins after the second-to-last monster movement tile is drawn. Reshuffle all tiles."

**Source (line 155):** "The second stage starts after the second to last monster movement card is drawn and executed. Now all monster movement cards are reshuffled and are placed face down as the new draw pile." ✓

**Summary (line 79):** "If after using all but the last card in stage 2 no one has won, all tokens still inside are eaten. The player with the most tokens that escaped wins."

**Source (line 158-163):** "After again using all but the last card monster movement without fulfilling the winning conditions, the game ends and all playing tokens inside the fortress count as eaten and killed. ...In this case the winning player is the one, who firstly moved the most playing tokens out of the exit." ✓

## federation-commander

### Prefilter
OK — source is confirmed as the Federation Commander: First Missions introductory rulebook.

### Finding 1 — Turn structure is wrong: summary describes a 4-phase turn, source describes Energy Allocation + 8 Impulses + End of Turn

**Summary (lines 38-57):** Describes 4 sequential phases per turn: "1. Energy Allocation Phase, 2. Movement Phase, 3. Combat Phase, 4. End Phase."

**Source (lines 276-361):** The actual sequence of play is:
- (1E1) Energy Allocation Phase (before impulses)
- (1E2) Impulse Procedure: **8 Impulses**, each containing: (a) Speed Change Phase, (b) Movement Phase, (c) Defensive Fire Phase, (d) Offensive Direct-Fire Phase, (e) Other Functions Phase, (f) Launch Phase
- (1E3) End of Turn Procedure

Source (line 290): "The Turn is divided into eight Impulses." There is no single "Movement Phase" or "Combat Phase" at the turn level — these happen within each impulse. The summary's description is fundamentally wrong about the turn structure.

### Finding 2 — Photon torpedoes misclassified as seeking weapons; drones are the seeking weapons

**Summary (line 52):** "Seeking weapons (photon torpedoes, drones/missiles) are launched and tracked."

**Source (lines 1614-1616):** "Photon torpedoes are fired during the Direct-Fire Weapons Phase of the Sequence of Play." 

**Source (3A2, line 1106-1109):** "Seeking weapons: These weapons (drones, plasma torpedoes) are covered in Federation Commander: Klingon Border and need not concern you now."

Photon torpedoes are direct-fire weapons, not seeking weapons. Drones/missiles are seeking weapons. The summary reverses this classification.

### Finding 3 — Movement is NOT "plotted or resolved simultaneously"; order of precedence exists

**Summary (line 46):** "Movement is plotted or resolved simultaneously."

**Source (lines 609-614):** "(2A5) ORDER OF PRECEDENCE: When two ships move at the same time, the slower ship moves first. If the speeds are equal, the unit with a better turn mode category moves last."

There is a specific Order of Precedence for movement — it is not simply simultaneous. The claim of simultaneous secret movement is not supported.

### Finding 4 — Cloaking devices: summary claims cloaking rules exist in this rulebook; source refers to Romulan Border for those rules

**Summary (line 91):** "Cloaking devices (Romulan): Ships can become invisible but cannot fire while cloaked."

**Source:** Cloaking is not described anywhere in this rulebook. The document is specifically "First Missions" (introductory), covering only Federation and Klingon ships. No cloaking rules are present. This is outside knowledge from the broader game system.

### Finding 5 — Empires list includes factions not present in this rulebook

**Summary (Player Reference):** "Empires: Federation, Klingon, Romulan, Gorn, Tholian, others."

**Source (line 101-102):** "Federation Commander: First Missions includes two different ships, the Federation heavy cruiser Constellation and the Klingon D7 battlecruiser Antagonist." The rulebook only covers Federation and Klingon. Other empires are mentioned in passing (miniatures catalog) but are not playable in this rulebook.

### Verification of victory conditions

**Summary:** "Per scenario — typically: Destroy or cripple enemy ships, Protect friendly ships or bases, Complete specific objectives."

**Source (lines 267-271):** "Each battle is fought as a series of turns. A given battle or scenario (Chapter 8) might have an unlimited number of turns, or might have a specific number of turns to accomplish a mission." The source confirms scenario-dependent victory. Acceptable as a generic description.

### Verification of turn structure (5 procedural claims)

1. **Summary:** "Energy allocation is simultaneous and secret." Source line 283-284: "Pick and pay for your baseline speed (2B1b) secretly and simultaneously with other players." ✓ (Energy allocation is correctly described as simultaneous/secret)
2. **Summary:** "Ships move based on allocated movement energy." Source line 204-207: "You pay the energy cost for movement...one-half point per hex for the Federation heavy cruiser or the Klingon D7." ✓
3. **Summary:** "Damage that penetrates shields is applied to ship systems using the SSD." Source (3D): confirmed by chapter reference. ✓
4. **Summary:** "Seeking weapons (photon torpedoes, drones/missiles) are launched and tracked." Source line 1615: photon torpedoes fire in Direct-Fire Phase; drones are seeking weapons but covered in Klingon Border. ✗ — MAJOR error
5. **Summary:** Turn is divided into Movement Phase, then Combat Phase. Source: turn is divided into 8 impulses each containing movement AND fire. ✗ — MAJOR structural error

### Numbers checked

- Shield facings: 6 per ship — Summary: "6 per ship". Source line 1169: "Each ship is surrounded by six shields." ✓
- 8 impulses per turn — Source line 290: "The Turn is divided into eight Impulses." Summary does not mention impulses at all. ✗ (major omission)

### Verdict

MAJOR — Turn structure is fundamentally misrepresented (4 sequential phases vs. Energy Allocation + 8 Impulses + End of Turn); photon torpedoes are misclassified as seeking weapons when the source explicitly fires them in the Direct-Fire Weapons Phase; cloaking device rules are stated as present in this rulebook when they are not. All three errors would cause players to play the game incorrectly.
