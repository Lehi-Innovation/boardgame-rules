contain several invented costs/mechanics and a contradicted scoring rule that would mislead
players at the table.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Contradicted scoring rule (invented subtraction, missing capture points).**
   Summary: "Subtract VP for locations lost." Source has no subtraction anywhere; instead the
   *capturer gains* points: "You also score two points for each of your opponents cubes that you
   captured and four points for each disc you captured." The summary both invents a penalty and
   omits an entire scoring source. Tie rule ("In the case of a tie then the French player is the
   winner") also omitted.

2. **MAJOR — Invented instant-victory variant; French targets omitted.**
   Summary: "British: Capture Quebec (or Louisbourg in some scenarios)" and "French: Capture
   certain British key locations." Source: "The British player wins immediately if he wins a siege
   in Quebec. The French player wins immediately if he wins a siege in Boston or New York."
   There are no scenarios in the rulebook; the Louisbourg clause is invented, and the French
   victory locations (Boston, New York) are concrete and should be named.

3. **MAJOR — Raid mechanics contradicted.**
   Summary: "Play: Native American card + location card connecting to enemy location. Remove 1
   enemy cube." Source explicitly: "You do not play any location cards to launch a raid and you do
   not need to play any transport symbols." Also omitted: 1 money paid per Native Americans card
   played; range = 1 connection per raid-capable card played; opponent may block; a raided disc is
   replaced by a cube (town becomes village) and the raider keeps the captured piece.

4. **MAJOR — "Take money" action wrong.**
   Summary: "Take 1 money from the bank (free action in some cases)." Source: "You play one
   location card and take an amount of money from the bank equal to the value indicated in the
   gold circle." It requires a location card, pays the card's printed value (not 1), and is never a
   free action.

5. **MAJOR — Intendant effect wrong.**
   Summary: "search through draw deck for a specific card." Source: "you pay two money to the
   bank... and then select one card of your choice from your discard pile and place it in your
   hand." Wrong pile (discard, not draw deck), missing 2-money cost, card goes to hand.

6. **MAJOR — Develop cost invented.**
   Summary: "location card... + settler card + 1 money." Source (rules text and player reference):
   "intended location card + settler symbol card" — no money cost. Also omitted: only locations
   with a printed VP value can be developed.

7. **MODERATE — Siege win thresholds wrong/incomplete.**
   Summary: "Attacker and defender compare total military strength. Defender wins ties...
   Defender checks siege at start of their turn." Source: each player checks *both* siege tracks
   at the start of his own turn; the **attacker wins with an advantage of two or more**, the
   **defender wins with an advantage of one or more**. "Defender wins ties" hides the attacker's
   +2 requirement, and attackers also check at the start of their turns.

8. **MODERATE — Core restriction on location cards omitted.**
   Source: "You cannot use a location card in any manner if you do not have control of that
   location or if it is not connected to either Boston (British) or Quebec (French), or if it is
   under siege." This supply-line rule constrains nearly every action and is absent from the
   summary.

9. **MINOR — Governor described as permanent removal.**
   Summary: "remove cards from your deck permanently." Source: removed cards "return... to the
   available cards" and "You can always regain a previously removed card in later turns."

10. **MINOR — Merchant mischaracterized.**
    Summary: "Play: merchant card." Source: "You play one card with a ship symbol on it. You can
    then play one or two cards which have a gold symbol on" — there is no "merchant card."

11. **MINOR — Discard cost omitted.**
    Source: first discard free, "you must pay one money for each additional card discarded."

12. **MINOR — Fortification benefits understated.**
    Source: fortified location starts a siege +2 to defense and "is also immune to raids" (raids
    cannot pass through it either). Summary only says "Fortification adds to defense."

## a-game-of-thrones-the-board-game — VERDICT: MAJOR

All component counts and unit/order numbers are accurate, and the round/phase skeleton is
correct. However, the end-of-game tie-breaker is contradicted, and two core systems (turn
order via the Iron Throne track, ship transport) plus the casualties system are missing —
enough to mislead a table playing from this summary.

### Numbers checked

| # | Summary claim | Source evidence | Status |
|---|---------------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | 138 plastic units: 60 Footmen (10/house), 30 Knights (5/house), 36 Ships (6/house), 12 Siege Engines (2/house) | Component list matches exactly | Supported |
| 2 | 81 large cards: 42 House (7/house), 30 Westeros, 9 Wildling | Matches exactly | Supported |
| 3 | 24 Tides of Battle cards | "24 Small Tides of Battle Cards" | Supported |
| 4 | 6 player screens, 2 reference sheets | "2 Reference Sheets / 6 House-specific Player Screens" | Supported |
| 5 | 266 tokens: 90 Order (15/house), 120 Power (20/house), 18 Influence, 6 Supply, 14 Neutral Force, 6 VP, 6 Garrison | Matches exactly | Supported |
| 6 | 10 rounds; control 7 castle/stronghold areas = instant win | "end of the 10th game round"; "controls seven such areas... immediately wins" | Supported |
| 7 | Westeros Phase skipped in Round 1 | "skipped game round 1" | Supported |
| 8 | Footman 1, Knight 2, Ship 1, Siege Engine 4 (only vs Castles/Strongholds) | Unit Combat Strengths section matches (Siege Engine otherwise 0) | Supported |
| 9 | March bonuses -1 / +0 / +1; Defense +1 / +2 | "one with a -1... another with a +0", Special "March +1"; two Defense "+1", Special "Defense +2" | Supported |
| 10 | Valyrian Steel Blade: once per round, +1 to combat | "may, once per game round, use the token in combat to grant himself a +1" | Supported |
| 11 | Consolidate Power: "Gain 1 Power token (+1 if in area with Crown icon)" | Source: "one Power token... plus one Power token **for each** Power icon printed on the assigned area" | Slightly wrong — per-icon, not a flat +1 |

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — End-of-game tie-breaker contradicted.**
   Summary: "Tie-breaker: Strongest position on influence tracks, then most Power tokens."
   Source: tied players compare, in order: "the tied player who holds the greater number of
   **Strongholds** wins. If there is still a tie, the tied player with the higher position on the
   **Supply Track** wins. If there is still a tie, the tied player with the **most available
   power** wins... [finally] the higher position on the **Iron Throne track**." The summary's
   first two criteria (Strongholds, Supply) are missing and its stated order is wrong — a tied
   game (common at round 10) would be awarded to the wrong player.

2. **MAJOR — Turn order system missing.**
   Source: "Any game mechanic that operates in 'turn order' is resolved in the order of House
   position on the Iron Throne Influence track," and the entire Action Phase is resolved by
   cycling player-by-player in turn order ("In turn order, each player resolves one of his Raid
   Orders... Keep cycling through the turn order"), as is mustering and supply adjustment. The
   summary never mentions turn order or that the Iron Throne track determines it (its Iron Throne
   row lists only tie-breaking) — a table cannot resolve simultaneous March Orders among 6
   players without this.

3. **MAJOR — Ship transport omitted.**
   Source devotes a full section: "Any two land areas are considered adjacent for the purposes of
   marching and retreating when they are connected by consecutive sea areas... each containing
   one or more friendly Ship units." This is the game's land-unit mobility engine ("a very
   important aspect") and is entirely absent; the summary's Ports/Ships section covers only
   support and mustering.

4. **MODERATE — Combat sequence misordered; casualties system omitted.**
   Summary combat order: initial strength → House cards → support → Tides of Battle → resolve.
   Source order: 1. Call for Support, 2. Calculate Initial Combat Strength (which already
   *includes* supporting units), 3. Choose and Reveal House Cards, 4. Valyrian Steel Blade,
   5. Final Strength, 6. Resolution. Support is declared *before* House cards are chosen (an
   information-timing rule that matters). The summary also omits Sword/Fortification icons and
   casualties ("destroy one unit... for each Sword icon of the victorious player minus the number
   of Fortification icons of the defeated player") — "Loser retreats or is destroyed" is not an
   adequate substitute. Third-party support (any adjacent player may support either side — "Not
   only may support be granted to a player's own combat needs, but to that of another player") is
   also lost by saying "Support orders from adjacent friendly areas."

5. **MINOR — Consolidate Power per-icon bonus understated.**
   "+1 if in area with Crown icon" vs. source "+1 for each Power icon printed on the area."
   Pillaging (raiding an enemy Consolidate Power order: raider gains 1 Power, victim discards 1)
   also omitted.

6. **MINOR — Wildling threshold wording.**
   Summary: "If total bids exceed Wildling strength." Source: Night's Watch wins if bids "equal
   **or** exceed" the wildling strength. Also: on a wildling win the threat token only falls back
   2 spaces (vs. reset to 0 on a Night's Watch win) — omitted.

7. **NOTE (not counted as an error) — Westeros deck contents unverifiable.**
   The summary assigns named cards to decks (Deck I: Mustering/Supply; Deck II: Game of
   Thrones/Clash of Kings; Deck III: Wildlings). The extracted text describes these cards but
   never states their deck numbers. The assignment matches the published game and is not
   contradicted, so it is not flagged.

8. **NOTE — Setup wording.** "Per scenario setup" is loose (setup is fixed per player screen with
   player-count adjustments, not scenarios); starting 5 Power tokens per player and Wildling
   token starting at "2" are omitted. House "Starting Area" table gives regions (e.g., Stark =
   North) where the source names home areas (e.g., Winterfell); not contradicted, but imprecise.
