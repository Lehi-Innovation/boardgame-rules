## a-game-of-thrones-the-card-game — MAJOR

Source is the 2nd Edition Rules Reference (glossary + timing appendix + core set component list).

### Findings

1. **Wrong win condition (Scoring section) — contradicted rule.**
   - Summary: "First player to accumulate **15 power tokens** on their faction card wins." (also Overview: "claim 15 power tokens and win the Iron Throne")
   - Source (lines 1804-1808): "The first player to have 15 (or more) power **on cards he or she controls** wins the game."
   - Power on characters (e.g., from the Renown keyword, which the summary itself lists as a power source) counts toward 15. A player following the summary would not count renown power and could miss their own win. Misleading at the table.

2. **Components list contradicts the source's component list.**
   - Summary: "4 pre-constructed House decks (Stark, Lannister, Baratheon, Targaryen)".
   - Source (lines 1843-1862): core set contains 28 Plot cards; 20 cards each for **8 factions** (Stark, Lannister, Baratheon, Greyjoy, Targaryen, Martell, Tyrell, Night's Watch); 31 Neutral cards; 1 Fealty Agenda card; 8 Faction/Agenda cards; 6 Melee title cards; 30 gold tokens; 30 power counters; 10 influence tokens; 1 first player token.
   - There are no "4 pre-constructed House decks" in the source; half the factions are dropped. Summary also omits agenda cards, influence tokens, title cards, and the first player token.

3. **First player determination — contradicted rule.**
   - Summary: "Compare initiative values; highest initiative is first player."
   - Source (lines 2012-2024): the player with highest initiative "wins initiative," then "The player who won initiative... **chooses a player to be the first player**" (choosing an opponent is legal and strategically relevant). Tiebreaker (lowest power total, then random) also omitted.

4. **Plot deck recycling — minor drift.**
   - Summary: "Once all 7 plots are used, shuffle them to form a new plot deck."
   - Source (lines 2038-2042): when the plot deck is empty, the used pile "returns to his or her plot deck" — no shuffle (plots are player-chosen, not drawn), and the just-revealed plot does not return.

5. **Military claim wording — minor ambiguity.**
   - Summary: "Win to choose and kill defending player's characters (claim value)" — reads as if the attacker chooses.
   - Source (lines 4.2.4, 2227-2231): "The **defending player** must choose a number of different characters under his or her control equal to the claim value, and kill those characters."

## a-house-divided-war-between-the-states-1861-65 — MAJOR

Source is the 2006 Living Edition Basic Game rules (Phalanx).

### Findings

1. **Turn order reversed — contradicted rule.**
   - Summary Turn Structure: "1. Confederate Movement Phase... 3. Union Movement Phase..."
   - Source 3.0 (l.322-324): "**The Union has the first Player Turn** each Game Turn, followed by the Confederate Player Turn."

2. **Player turn sequence wrong — contradicted rule.**
   - Summary: shared end-of-turn "5. Recruitment Phase -- Both players receive and place reinforcements. 6. Promotion Phase".
   - Source 3.0 (l.331-336): each Player Turn consists of four segments in this exact order: "1. Movement 2. Combat **3. Promotions 4. Recruitment**" — per player, with Promotions before Recruitment, not a shared phase after both players move.

3. **Core movement system (Marches) omitted entirely.**
   - Source 4.0 (l.359-362, 395-397): "the player rolls one six-sided die. The number rolled is the number of Marches... if he rolls a 1, he receives 2 Marches"; "no unit may participate in more than two Marches in a single turn." The die-rolled March action economy is the game's central movement mechanic and appears nowhere in the summary (summary just says "Units move along connected paths").
   - Also: "Infantry: move 1-2 spaces per turn (depending on type)" — source: distance depends on **transportation line** (1 box by road/unfriendly rail, 2 by river/friendly rail), not unit type.

4. **Control rule contradicted.**
   - Summary: "Players control locations... by having the last force to occupy **or pass through** them."
   - Source 8.0 (l.1352-1359): "Just 'passing through'... does **not** change control of that box... Control of boxes only changes at the end of a player's Combat Segment." Cavalry-only exception (control lapses on vacating) also omitted.

5. **Victory conditions misstated — contradictions and major omissions.**
   - Summary: "Automatic Victory: Capture of the enemy capital." True only for the Confederacy (capture Washington, l.1442-1444). The Union capturing Richmond does NOT win — Union wins only by simultaneously controlling "all seven Confederate Recruitment Cities with a Recruitment Value of 2 or 3" (l.1427-1430: New Orleans, Charleston, Wilmington, Atlanta, Memphis + 2 OCR-garbled names). A Union player following the summary would think taking Richmond ends the game.
   - Summary: "Victory is checked at the end of each game year." Source: both sides' capture conditions end play **immediately** (l.1437, 1443, 1448); otherwise victory is decided at the end of Game Turn 40 (l.328-330). Year-end checking appears only as the optional March-ending/sudden-death rules (11.3). Unsupported as stated.
   - Omitted: Confederate win #2 (Confederate Army Maximum Size exceeds Union's, l.1445-1449) and #3 (survive to end of Turn 40 = Confederate default win, l.1452-1456).
   - Summary: "Confederate Victory: Control enough key Union cities or maintain enough territory" — matches none of the three actual conditions.

6. **Recovery mischaracterized (minor).**
   - Summary: "Damaged units recover strength over time. Units not in battle may recover losses."
   - Source 5.7 (l.960-962): "After a player's Battle Segment is over, **all** units that are flipped to their reduced... sides are returned to their full-strength... sides" — automatic and immediate (the gradual version is optional rule 5.71).

7. **Retreat mischaracterized (minor).**
   - Summary: "Retreat (5.4): Losing side must retreat..."
   - Source 5.4 (l.876-878): from Battle Round 2 on, **either** player **may** retreat in lieu of firing; battles otherwise continue until one side is eliminated. Nobody is forced to retreat (an army that cannot retreat "must fight to the death").

8. **Recruitment mischaracterized (minor).**
   - Summary: "Each side receives new units on a schedule."
   - Source 7.0 (l.1154-1162): roll one die (1 treated as 2) for Recruitment Points, spent to recruit Militia from the Recruitment Pool into friendly Recruitment Cities (1 unit per city per segment). Only the Union Drafts (7.2) are scheduled.

9. **Player Reference internal contradiction (minor).**
   - "River crossing | +1 to attacker's die roll" vs. Actions section "+1 to defender" — source 5.34 (l.841-844): defender's units have Combat Values **increased by one** for the first two Battle Rounds when firing at units that crossed a river.

10. **Game turn length (minor).** Summary: "approximately 2 months." Source (l.319-321): "one month (or two in winter)."
