## dead-of-winter-a-crossroads-game — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Component list is perfect, but the Colony Phase has two wrong core numbers (food cost, zombie spawn rate), the overrun and bite-spread rules are misdescribed, frostbite timing is wrong, and the 3-wounds-kills rule is missing entirely.

### Major findings

1. **Pay Food rate wrong (doubles the food cost).** Summary: "Remove Food tokens = number of survivors at the Colony (helpless included)" and Player Reference "Food needed: 1 per survivor at Colony." Source (l.709-711): "Remove 1 food token from the food supply for every 2 survivors in the colony (rounded up)."
2. **Zombie spawn rate wrong at non-colony locations.** Summary: "Add 1 zombie per 2 survivors at each location (Colony included)" (repeated in Player Reference). Source (l.740-744): "Add 1 zombie to the colony for every 2 survivors... (rounded up). Add 1 zombie to each non-colony location for every 1 survivor present at that location." Summary halves the zombie pressure outside the colony. It also omits the noise-token spawn rolls: "One at a time remove each noise token at a location and roll an action die for each. Add a zombie... on each roll of 3 or lower" (l.744-746); summary only says "Noise attracts zombies."
3. **Overrun kill rule wrong.** Summary: "each overflow zombie kills 1 survivor there (player's choice) OR removes 1 barricade." Source (l.808-814): if entrance is full but has a barricade, destroy the barricade and remove the zombie; if full with no barricades, "remove that zombie and kill the survivor at the colony that has the **lowest influence value**" — not the player's choice.
4. **Missing death threshold.** Source (l.871-872): "When a survivor has 3 or more wound tokens it is killed." Nowhere in the summary — a core survival rule.
5. **Bite spread misdescribed.** Summary: "Roll for each survivor at that location — on bite, they die too (chain reaction)." Source (l.657-672): the bite spreads to the **lowest-influence** survivor sharing the location; that survivor's controller chooses: kill it (spread stops) OR re-roll exposure — a blank stops the spread, **any other result** kills and the spread continues.
6. **Frostbite timing wrong.** Summary: "At Colony Phase, each frostbitten survivor takes 1 additional wound." Source (l.645-653): "At the beginning of each of your turns, each survivor you control that has 1 or more frostbite wound tokens on it receives an additional wound token."

### Minor findings

- **First Player token direction.** Summary: "Pass First Player Token: to the left." Source (l.792-793): "passes the first player token to the player on his **right**."
- **Unsupported claim:** "If Morale hits 0, only the Betrayer (if any) can win." The Winning section (l.928-942) says only that whoever completed their secret objective wins; the claim is a plausible inference (morale 0 ends the game without checking the main objective) but is not stated in the rulebook.
- Omitted: crisis bonus "if the point total exceeds the number of players by 2 or more the colony gains 1 morale" (l.736-737); move limit "may move each survivor he controls once during his turn" (l.600-601); 2-player/co-op/hardcore variants.

## deadwood — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Components, turn structure, shootouts, Sheriff, and building abilities are accurate, but the end-game scoring is wrong: the fine is a progressive table (not $1/poster), ties are resolved by a Final Shootout (omitted; the summary invents tiebreakers), and the Train Station rule invents a "$5 per adjacent building" payout.

### Major findings

1. **Wanted-poster fine wrong.** Summary: "Each player pays a fine of $1 per wanted poster they hold." Source: the fine table (l.577-587, OCR-garbled) is progressive, proven by the worked example (l.588-602): Fastshot Arnie $18 / 3 posters → -6 → 12; Jim Teaspoon $20 / 2 posters → -3 → 17; Sally Read $21 / 4 posters → -10 → 11. So fines run 1/3/6/10... (triangular), not $1 each. With 4 posters the summary undercharges by $6.
2. **Tiebreakers invented; Final Shootout omitted.** Summary: "Ties: fewer wanted posters wins. Still tied: player with fewer cowboys on Boot Hill." Source (l.558-576): "If there is a tie, all tied players face off in The Final Shootout!" — a full mechanic (each tied player picks a surviving cowboy, unlimited cartridges, target declaration, etc.). Nothing in the source supports the summary's tiebreakers.
3. **Train Station payout invented.** Summary: "Builder earns $5 per building adjacent to the Train Station." Source (l.518-528): when the Train Station is placed, "Every cowboy who is at a building that is adjacent to the Train Station can immediately annex his building **twice**," and the Hotel controller gets $4. There is no $5-per-adjacent-building payment to the builder.
4. **Railroad adjacency annex omitted.** Source (l.511-514): every cowboy at a building adjacent to a newly placed railroad tile may immediately annex his building (placer chooses order). Missing from the summary's Railroad section.

### Minor findings

- Summary Special Rules: "Cowboys at the Abandoned Mine can only return to Ranch via 'Head Back to the Ranch'" — source (l.540-542) also allows the Dance Hall annex (which the summary itself lists). Internal contradiction.
- General Store: summary "move 1 other cowboy" is ambiguous; source (l.354-360): "he moves one of **his** other cowboys."
- Courthouse: omits the pay-or-take-a-poster option ("If a player cannot pay this fine, or if he chooses not to, he must take one wanted poster from the Crime Pool," l.441-444).
- Omitted: "If a player has no cowboys alive, he can still win the game if he has the most money" (l.554-555).

## decktet — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Deck structure, Adaman, Emu Ranchers, and Jacynth are all faithful, but the Nonesuch bid scoring is substantially wrong (invented values for two of the three bids and a wrong bid threshold), which would directly mislead at the table.

### Major findings

1. **"Most" bid misnamed and misdefined.** Summary: "**Lots** bid: expect to win the most tricks. Score 2 per trick if you win the most; lose 2 per trick if you don't." Source (l.503-509): the bid is "**Most**" and means "at least **half** the tricks: at least 6 with three players; at least 5 with four players." On failure "they lose **one** point for each trick **less than half** that they've taken" — not 2 per trick won.
2. **"None" bid scoring invented.** Summary: "Score 5 if successful; lose 5 if you win any trick." Source (l.510-515): success scores "a number of points equal to the number of tricks in the hand (**12** with three players, **9** with four)"; failure: "lose **one point for each trick** they've taken." The 5/-5 values appear nowhere in the source.

### Minor findings

- Special Rules: "The pairing of suits varies by rank and is unique across the deck" — contradicted by source (l.57-59): "The more common aAbB pair appears **three times**, at ranks 4, 8, and 9." (The Player Reference's narrower claim — unique pairing *at the same rank* — is consistent with the suit diagram.)
- Omitted Nonesuch rules: deal sizes (12 each at 3p, 9 each at 4p, l.486-487); the Excuse holder reveals/discards it and takes the up-card (l.516-521); no leading trump until trump is broken (l.525-527); recommended play to a cumulative 31 points (l.562-566).
- The rulebook also contains **Thricewise** (2-5 players, l.598-700), which the summary never mentions (it does hedge with "plus many more"). Frontmatter `player_count: "1-4"` understates the range given Thricewise supports 5.

## defenders-of-the-realm — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — Components, setup, actions, and combat thresholds are accurate, but the Overrun rule (the game's core threat system) is essentially invented in the summary, the Nighttime draw count is wrong, and the General healing/combat-skill system is omitted.

### Major findings

1. **Overrun mechanic contradicted on three points.** Summary: "When a 4th minion would be placed at a location... The 4th minion is placed, then all minions at that location move 1 space toward Monarch City (along the path shown). If the destination also has 4, chain overruns." Source (l.436-449): "The maximum number of minions... is 3. **Instead of adding the 4th minion**, a Tainted Crystal is placed on that location and... Add 1 minion of the same color that caused the overrun to **all adjacent** (connected by a line) locations... overrun minions that would increase the number... beyond 3, Taint the land but they do **NOT cause any additional overruns**." The 4th minion is not placed; existing minions do not move; spread is to all adjacent locations (not toward Monarch City); chain overruns explicitly cannot happen.
2. **Nighttime draw count wrong.** Summary (Turn Structure and Player Reference): "Draw 2 Darkness Spreads Cards and resolve them." Source (l.416-417, 466-478): draw "1, 2 or 3 (depending on the War Status)" — Early War 1 card; Mid War 2 (second card moves a General only); Late War 3 (third card moves the General only). At game start the summary doubles the threat rate.
3. **General healing system omitted.** Source (l.342-354, 371-378): after a failed attack the General heals 1 point at the end of each player's turn (Minor Wound) or after a full round (Major Wound/red space, which also stops his advance); "Sapphire heals **all** wounds immediately unless defeated." The summary says only "Multiple attacks may be needed" — losing the regeneration rules makes General fights play completely differently.
4. **General combat skills and defeat rewards omitted.** Balazarg corrupts committed red cards on rolls of 1; Gorgutt parries hits on 1s; Varkolak forbids re-rolls (l.355-370). Defeating a General grants the Character Card (VP + Slayer ability), 3 Hero Cards, and advances the War Status (l.326-333); failure also moves the hero to Monarch City (l.335-339). None of this appears in the summary.

### Minor findings

- Summary loss condition "5 enemy minions in Monarch City at any time" — supported (l.71, 460-462).
- "Complete Quest" listed as an action is a fair reading (quest cards instruct e.g. "Spend 1 action and roll 3 dice", l.619).
- Omitted: heroes may not heal at locations with minions; healed tokens unusable until next turn (l.192-194); quest re-draw rules (l.586-589).
