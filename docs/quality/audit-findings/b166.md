## project-l — Project L

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 (MAJOR): Completing puzzles — pieces return to personal supply, not the reserve

Summary states:
> "All pieces used to fill the puzzle return to the reserve."

Source (lines 197–198, COMPLETING PUZZLES section):
> "1 Return all the pieces from the puzzle to your personal supply."

This is a fundamental error in a core engine-building mechanic. Pieces returned to the personal supply can immediately be reused, whereas pieces returned to the reserve are gone. This would completely break the engine-building loop — players would be unable to accumulate pieces from puzzles they complete.

---

### Finding 2 (MAJOR): Finishing Touches phase entirely omitted

The summary's Final Scoring section states:
> "Each unfinished puzzle scores 0 points."

The source (lines 235–258) describes a mandatory post-game "Finishing Touches" phase and a deduction for incomplete puzzles:
> "After the final round ends, each player may place any number of pieces from their supply into the puzzles in front of them following the normal rules for the Place action. For each piece placed this way, 1 point is deducted from that player's final score."
>
> "Deduct points for each incomplete puzzle, equal to its point value."

The summary says unfinished puzzles score 0, but the source says incomplete puzzles incur a penalty equal to their point value. The entire Finishing Touches mechanic (placing pieces at -1 VP each, with the goal of completing or reducing penalties from unfinished puzzles) is absent from the summary. This is a missing game-end phase that affects every player's final score.

---

### Finding 3 (MAJOR): Tiebreaker rules wrong — summary invents "later in turn order"

Summary states:
> "Tiebreaker: the player who went later in turn order."

Source (lines 260–265):
> "In case of a tie, the player who has completed the most puzzles wins. If there is still a tie, the player with the most pieces leftover wins. If there is still a tie, all tied players share the victory."

The summary's tiebreaker (turn order) has no support in the source text. The source uses a three-tier tiebreaker (most completed puzzles → most pieces left → shared victory). The invented turn-order tiebreaker would produce a different winner in nearly every tie situation.

**No support found (searched: "turn order", "later in turn", "went later", "player order", "tiebreaker", "in case of a tie")**

### Summary

The summary has three MAJOR errors that would make the game unplayable as described:
1. Pieces from completed puzzles go to the personal supply (not the reserve) — this is the core engine-building mechanic.
2. The Finishing Touches phase is entirely absent, and the incomplete puzzle penalty is reversed (0 pts vs. a deduction equal to point value).
3. The tiebreaker is invented; the source uses most-completed-puzzles then most-pieces-left.
