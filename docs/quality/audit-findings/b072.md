## discworld-ankh-morpork — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (19/19 supported)
12 city areas; 4 sets of 12 minions; 4 sets of 6 buildings; 12 trouble markers; 4 demons; 3 trolls; 1 twelve-sided die; 48 green / 53 brown Player cards; 12 Random Event cards; 7 Personality cards; 12 City Area cards; 35 silver ($1) / 17 gold ($5) coins; 4 Player Aid cards; $10 starting money; hand size 5; Dragon King of Arms 8 trouble markers; Chrysoprase $50. All match the source. (Building cost range "$6–$18" is not stated in the extracted text — only Nap Hill's $12 appears — but is not contradicted.)

### Findings

1. **Contradicted rule — trouble marker removal (core mechanic).**
   Summary: "Removed only by specific game effects."
   Source: "As soon as a minion is moved or removed from an area, any trouble marker in the area is also removed (even if there are two or more minions remaining)."
   This automatic removal drives assassination/building timing; the summary's claim would mislead substantially.

2. **Misdescribed action — Place Minion.**
   Summary (Actions table): "Place one of your minions in an area containing one of your minions. Also place a trouble marker there."
   Source: place in an area you already occupy **or an adjacent area** (any area if you have no minions on the board), and a trouble marker is placed **only** "whenever a minion is placed or moved into an area that already has one or more minions in it."
   The summary omits the adjacency option entirely and makes the trouble marker unconditional. (The summary's own "Trouble Markers" bullet states the conditional correctly — internal contradiction.)

3. **Internal contradiction — victory-check timing.**
   Turn Structure: "You may check your personality victory condition at any time on your turn and declare victory." Scoring section: "Check at the start of your turn."
   Source: "All of the Personality cards apart from Commander Vimes state that the conditions on the card must occur at the start of your turn, not the end of it." Only the Scoring section is correct.

4. **Wrong control definition.**
   Summary: control = "have more minions there than any single other player AND no demons are present."
   Source: "You control an area if you have more **playing pieces** in it than any single other player (a playing piece being a minion **or a building**). You would also have to have more pieces there than the total number of trolls in the area." Buildings count; trolls must be exceeded.

5. **Omitted victory/scoring numbers.**
   - Vetinari thresholds: 11 areas (2P) / 10 (3P) / 9 (4P) — summary says only "varies by player count".
   - Selachii/Rust/de Worde control thresholds: 7 (2P) / 5 (3P) / 4 (4P) — also omitted.
   - Endgame point values: minion = 5 pts, building = its monetary cost, $1 = 1 pt, unpaid Dent/Bank card = −15 pts each, tiebreaker = highest-value City Area card — summary says only "based on buildings, cash, and minions".

6. **Omitted game-end condition and mechanics.**
   - "Riot" Random Event ends the game immediately at 8+ trouble markers (and Vimes does *not* auto-win in that case) — absent from the summary.
   - The **Interrupt** card symbol (playable any time, even off-turn) and the **Remove one trouble marker** symbol are missing from the Actions table.
   - 2-player setup changes (remove Chrysoprase personality; remove Hubert and Cosmos Lavish cards) omitted.

Verdict: MAJOR — contradicted core trouble-marker rule, misdescribed most-common action, plus omitted victory thresholds and a game-end condition.

## distilled — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (20+ supported)
Components all match: 5 clipboards / 20 tasting flights; 5 distillery boards; 1 shelf board; 1 basic market + 1 truck board; 88 basic market cards; 36 premium ingredient; 33 premium item; 30 upgrades; 18 identities / 18 signature ingredients / 18 signature labels; 60 alcohol / 40 flavor; 85 spirit labels / 23 awards; 20 goals; 10 starting items; 37 cubes; 59 money tokens (1/5/10); 5 scoring + 5 modifier tokens; 5 reference cards; 1 first-player token + 1 round barrel. Gameplay numbers: 7 rounds; 4 face-up cards per premium row; max 2 basic purchases/round; discard 1 (2 in 2P) rightmost premium per row; spirit awards = players + 1; labels 2/player Moonshine+Vodka, 1/player others; 3 goals dealt, discard 1 after round 3; warehouse max 2; tasting up to 4 SP; aged flavor bonus 2 flavors = 3 SP (confirmed by Báijiu example; 1/6/10 values not in text but not contradicted); endgame money 1 SP per 5; warehouse endgame 1 SP per flavor card. All supported.

### Findings

1. **Wrong scoring — bottle collection (endgame).**
   Summary: "SP for displaying bottles from multiple regions (1 region = 0; 2 = 2 SP; 3 = 5 SP)" (repeated in Player Reference).
   Source (player aid + example): two separate scores — bottles from the **same** region: "2 = 2, 3 = 4, 4 = 7, 5 = 10, 6+ = 15"; plus "If you have at least one bottle from three different regions, score 5 SP." Worked example: "three from Europe (scoring 4 SP), and at least one from three different regions (scoring 5 SP), for a total of 9 SP."
   The summary collapses these into a different (wrong) scheme and drops the entire same-region set track — endgame scoring would be done incorrectly every game.

2. **Omission — recipe sell value when selling.**
   Summary: "Collect money equal to total sell value on all cards."
   Source: "Count the total sell value on these cards, **as well as on the recipe (Moonshine, Vodka, and signatures only)**."
   Moonshine/Vodka earn most of their money from the recipe; recurring income error.

3. **Minor — spirit label bonus list.**
   Summary lists "Free card from premium market" twice and "Free card from either market" once; source has "Free from either market" twice and "Free from premium market" once.

4. **Minor — unverifiable/likely-wrong setup detail.** Summary names the 7 basic-market piles as "yeast, water, grains, fruits, plants, **metal barrels**, glass bottles". The source never enumerates the piles ("lay out... into 7 face-up piles as shown"); the metal barrel is the returning starting item, and the Rum example has the player *buying a wood barrel*, so "metal barrels" is dubious. Flagged as unsupported, not counted toward verdict.

5. **Minor omissions:** Yeast/Alcohol/starting items may not be traded in the Distill-phase trade; signature-recipe requirement is a *specific named* ingredient (signature or its premium equivalent), not any "premium ingredient of the same sugar type"; solo mode (47 solo goal cards + rules) entirely omitted despite "1-5 players" frontmatter.

Verdict: MAJOR — endgame bottle-collection scoring is garbled (wrong values, missing the same-region track) and sale income omits recipe sell value; both would mislead at the table. Otherwise a very faithful summary.

## dixit-journey — PASS

### Numbers checked (14/14 supported)
84 cards; 8 voting dials; 8 rabbit pawns; board with scoring track + 8 numbered card slots; deal 6 / hand size 6; storyteller scores 3 (some-but-not-all); correct guessers 3; all/none → storyteller 0, others 2 each; 1 bonus point per vote received on own card; game end at 30 points (end of turn, immediate); 3-player variant: 7-card hands, submit 2 cards, 5 cards displayed, draw 2. All match.

### Findings

1. Turn structure, voting restrictions (cannot vote for own card), storyteller rotation (left), discard/reshuffle rule, and tie = shared victory all match the source exactly.
2. **Trivial, non-gameplay:** the summary says "unique artwork by Xavier Collette" and "cards can be mixed with cards from any other Dixit edition" — neither appears in the source (the extracted PDF is the generic Dixit rulebook crediting Marie Cardouat, listing Journey as one of the 84-card expansion sets). Flavor-level drift only; no rules impact.
3. **Note (ambiguous source, not flagged):** for the 3-player bonus, the source reads "still score 1 bonus [point] if they have received a vote on their own card (regardless of which card)"; the summary renders this as "1 bonus point per vote on either of their cards." The source wording is ambiguous between per-vote and flat-1; the summary's reading matches the base-game per-vote rule.

Verdict: PASS.

## dog — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (13/13 supported)
30 pawns (5 each in 6 colors); 110 cards; 8 each blue 2,3,5,6,8,9,10,12; 8 each red 1/11, 4±, 7, 13, Swap; 6 Jokers; 4 pawns per player (partnership game); deal cycle 6,5,4,3,2 repeating; 8 team pawns to win; kennel exit via 1/11, 13, Joker; 7 split among pawns; 4 forward/backward. All match.

### Findings

1. **Invented exception — start-space blocking.**
   Summary: "Your own pawn on your start space blocks other pawns from passing over it **(except when using a 7 card)**."
   Source: "a pawn on its own start space ... cannot be landed upon, **passed** or swapped by any player, including its owner." There is no 7-card exception anywhere in the source; the 7's special property is burning passed pawns, not bypassing blocks. This invented exception directly contradicts the rule.

2. **Misstated 7-card burn rule.**
   Summary: "Each portion can send opponent pawns home."
   Source: "**Any pawn that the player passes** with any partial or full movement of the 7 card is sent back to its kennel, **even if it belongs to the moving player or her partner** ... The 7 is the only card that can send pawns to the kennels without landing directly on them."
   The summary fails to convey burn-on-pass and wrongly limits it to opponents. (Also omits: the 7 may not be used to leave the kennel.)

3. **Wrong Swap description.**
   Summary: "Exchange the position of one of your pawns with any **opponent's** pawn on the main track" and "cannot be used on pawns ... on **start spaces**."
   Source: swap with "a pawn of another color (**partner or opponent**)"; and only "any pawn on **its own** start space CANNOT be swapped" — "Pawns on start spaces of **other** players are treated as if they were on any normal space, and can be landed upon ... swapped, etc." Both halves of the summary's rendering are wrong.

4. **Major omission — non-partnership play (2/3/5 players) and 6-player structure.**
   Frontmatter says 2-6 players and the Overview claims teams of two throughout, but the source plays 2, 3, or 5 **without partnerships** under different rules: 5 pawns each (4 kennel + 1 start), pass a card to the left, a stuck player discards-and-draws instead of sitting out, and "the first player to get four of her five pawns home wins." 6-player = 3 teams of 2. None of this appears in the summary.

5. **Unsupported rule — "Cannot move backward out of home."**
   The source instead discusses a player whose only playable card is 4± with all pawns home/kennel being "NOT required to move a pawn from the safety of the home area back out onto the board" — which presupposes backing out of home is possible (the compiler flags the not-required part as uncertain). The summary's flat prohibition is not in the source.

6. **Omission:** a pawn that ended its turn on its own start space "may not move into the home area" on its next move (forcing another lap if you land exactly on your start space) — a frequently-relevant rule, absent from the summary.

Verdict: MAJOR — invented 7-card exception, contradicted Swap/blocking rules, misstated burn mechanic, and a whole player-count mode omitted.

## dominant-species — MAJOR

### Numbers checked (30+ supported)
Components: 27 cards (1 Survival + 26 Dominance incl. Ice Age); 31 large + 12 small tundra tiles; 330 cubes; 60 cylinders; 60 cones (10/animal confirmed: "Each animal has 10 dominance cones"); 120 element markers / 6 types; 6 initiative markers; 1 bag. Setup: 7 starting non-tundra tiles + tundra atop sea; 11-tile tundra stack; 3×8 face-down Wanderlust stacks; APs/cubes by player count (6p 3/35, 5p 4/40, 4p 5/45, 3p 6/50, 2p 7/55); 12 starting elements; Ice Age bottom + 25 shuffled + 5 face-up; 4 elements each to Adaptation/Abundance/Wanderlust; all starting species placements (all six animals) match exactly. Play: 12-action execution order matches verbatim; speciation limits 4 (sea/wetland), 3 (savannah/jungle/forest), 2 (desert/mountain), 1 (tundra); max 6 elements per display; Reset cascade (Adaptation→Regression, Abundance→Wasteland, Wasteland→Depletion; clear Regression/Depletion/Wanderlust) matches. Tile scoring spot-checks against source: wetland 8/4/2/1 (worked example), sea 4th = 2 / wetland 4th = 1, first-place 9 sea / 8 wetland / 7 savannah (Niche Biomes note). Endgame procedure and food-chain tiebreak match.

### Findings

1. **Wrong default elements for 4 of 6 animals (Player Reference).**
   Summary table: Mammal "Meat", Bird "Seed", Amphibian "Water", Arachnid "Grub" (one each), vs. Reptile "Sun, Sun" and Insect "Grass, Grass".
   Source: reptiles' "**two** default sun elements" and insects' "**two** default grass" are explicit; and the card clarification (Metamorphosis entry) reads "You cannot dump your **two** default (printed) elements" — generic wording implying every animal has exactly two printed defaults (the real displays show two per animal). Defaults feed the matching-elements computation that decides dominance — the game's core mechanic — so halving four animals' default counts would systematically mislead.

2. **Likely-wrong tundra scoring value (unverifiable from extract).**
   Summary: Tundra first place = 2 VP. The extracted text never states tundra's Tile Scoring value (it confirms only that tundra scores first place alone). The published table gives tundra 1 VP. Flagged as unsupported and probably wrong; not independently verifiable from this source.

3. Everything else checked is faithful: planning/execution/reset phases, all 12 action descriptions (incl. Glaciation's 7 steps, left-most-AP-only with shift during Reset; insect free speciation; arachnid free competition; reptile free Regression AP; bird 2-tile migration; mammal extinction save), Survival card possession/scoring, species-to-box vs Glaciation-to-gene-pool, dominance must be verbally/physically claimed, voluntary actions, never-eliminated rule, and the 2-3 player multi-animal variant (lowest-scoring animal = final score).

Verdict: MAJOR — the default-element reference table is wrong for four animals, corrupting the matching/dominance math at the heart of the game (plus a likely-wrong tundra scoring value). Otherwise an exceptionally accurate summary.
