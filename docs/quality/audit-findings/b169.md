## quiddler

**Verdict: PASS**

### Prefilter
SOURCE_SUSPECT (title absent from first 3000 chars), but reading the file confirms it is the Quiddler instruction sheet. Content fully matches.

### Victory / Game-end
Summary: "The player with the highest total score after Round 8 wins."
Source: "The player with the highest score at the end of the last round wins." ✓

### Key procedural claims

**Hand sizes:** Summary: rounds 1–8 = 3–10 cards. Source: "In the first hand of the game, 3 cards… In the last hand of the game, each player is dealt 10 cards." ✓

**Turn structure:** Draw then discard (optional go out). Source matches. ✓

**Going out requirement:** Summary: "arrange all cards in your hand into allowable words with exactly 1 card remaining (which you discard)."
Source: "a player must be able to arrange all the cards in his/her hand into allowable words… with one card remaining. He/she lays the cards down and discards one card." ✓

**After go-out:** Summary: "Each remaining player gets one final turn. Draw, then lay down as many words as possible. Cards not used in words count against your score."
Source: "the remaining players each have one last turn… The player will then lay down his/her words, and discard one card. He/she will score the number of points on the cards used to build words. Points on the unused cards are counted against the player's total." ✓

**Bonuses:** Summary: "+10 most words, +10 longest word, both can go to same player, tie = no bonus, 2-player: one bonus only."
Source: "The player with the most words in each round receives 10 additional points, and the player with the longest word receives 10 additional points. Both bonuses can go to the same player. When two or more players tie for a bonus, no bonus is given. When playing with two players, use only one of the bonuses." ✓

**Minimum score:** Summary: "Minimum score per round: 0 (cannot go negative in a single round)."
Source: "no player can score less than zero in any hand." ✓

**Challenges:** Summary: "If valid, challenger subtracts word's point value. If invalid, challenged player subtracts and may not rearrange."
Source: "If the word is in the dictionary and is allowable, then the challenger must subtract the number of points that the word is worth from his/her score. If the word is not in the dictionary… the player being challenged must subtract the number of points that the word is worth from his/her score. He/she does not get to rearrange the cards into other words." ✓

**Longest word by letters:** Summary: "by letter count, not card count."
Source: "the word that uses the most letters, not the most cards." ✓

**Double-letter cards count as 2:** Summary: "Double-letter cards (QU, IN, ER, TH, CL) count as 2 letters for longest-word determination."
Source states "most letters not most cards" but does not explicitly state that double-letter cards count as 2 for this purpose. No support found (searched: "two letters", "double letter", "counts as", "QU count"). This inference is reasonable but unsupported — noting as MINOR omission only.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 10, SUPPORTED: 10 (one claim unsupported by source but defensible by implication)

## quixo

**Verdict: PASS**

### Prefilter
SOURCE_MISMATCH warning triggered, but reading the file confirms it IS the Quixo rulebook (about placing crosses and circles on a 5x5 board of cubes). The title "Quixo" doesn't appear in the text itself (the PDF is the rules sheet with no prominent title), so the warning was a false positive.

### Victory / Game-end
Summary: "Be the first to make and announce a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line of 5 cubes bearing your symbol."
Source: "The winner is the player to make and announce that he/she has made a horizontal, vertical or diagonal line with 5 cubes bearing his/her symbol." ✓

Summary: "If you make a line of 5 cubes bearing your opponent's symbol, you lose — even if you simultaneously make a line of your own symbol."
Source: "The player to make a line with his/her opponent's symbol loses the game, even if he/she makes a line with his/her own symbol at the same time." ✓

### Key procedural claims

**Cube selection:** Summary: "You may take a blank cube or one showing your own symbol. You may never take a cube showing your opponent's symbol."
Source: "The player chooses and takes a blank cube, or one with his/her symbol on it… You are not allowed to take a cube bearing your opponent's symbol." ✓

**Symbol change:** Summary: "Change the face to show your symbol on top."
Source: "Whether the cube taken is blank or already bears the player's symbol, it must always be replaced by a cube with the player's symbol on the top face." ✓

**Replacement rule:** Summary: "You may never replace the cube in the position from which it was taken."
Source: "You can never replace the cube just played back in the position from which it was taken." ✓

**Skipping turns:** Summary: "You cannot skip your turn."
Source: "In no event can a player miss his/her turn." ✓

**4-player rules:** Summary: "Teams of 2, partners sitting opposite each other. Play clockwise."
Source: "The players are in teams of two, the team members facing each other. Going clockwise, each player takes his/her turn." ✓

**4-player cube ownership:** Summary: "Each player may only take a cube with their symbol pointing toward them."
Source: "takes a blank cube, or one with his/her symbol on it if the point is pointing towards him/her, from the board's periphery; as the direction of the point determines who in the team can play the cube." ✓

**4-player skip exception:** Summary says cannot skip; source has a 4-player exception: "A player must take his/her turn unless he/she is unable to take a blank cube, or one with his/her symbol on it if the point is pointing towards him/her, from the board's periphery." The summary omits this 4-player exception, but this is a minor edge case and doesn't affect the 2-player rules at all.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 5, SUPPORTED: 5

### Victory / Game-end

Source: "1. There is only 1 set of Plunder left in the Fortresses (1 of the 6 Fortress raids remains). 2. The Offering Draw Pile has been emptied. 3. There are no Valkyrie left on the board." ✓ (matches in substance)

### MAJOR FINDING: Scrambled building actions

The summary's "Town Actions" list is severely wrong — at least 4 of 5 buildings have incorrect actions:

**Summary:** "Long House: Hire crew (pay Silver cost, add to your crew area)"
**Source (lines 533–538):** "Long House — This allows players to take 1 of 2 actions: 1. Lose 1 Livestock to gain 2 Provisions. 2. Offer goods to the Chieftain — Pay the required Plunder and/or Silver shown on the desired Offering Tile…"
→ Hiring crew is done at the **Barracks**, not the Long House. The Long House exchanges Livestock or makes Offerings.

**Summary:** "Armoury: Collect Provisions"
**Source (line 522):** "Armoury — Lose 1 Iron to gain 2 Armour, or lose 2 Silver to gain 1 Armour."
→ The Armoury has nothing to do with Provisions; it grants Armour (which adds Military Strength).

**Summary:** "Treasury: Collect Silver"
**Source (line 512):** "Treasury — Discard 1 Hand Card to gain 2 Silver, or discard 2 cards to gain 1 Gold."
→ Treasury requires discarding hand cards to gain Silver/Gold; the summary omits the card-discard cost entirely, making it sound like free Silver collection.

**Summary:** "Barracks: Collect Provisions + Silver"
**Source (line 515):** "Barracks — Hire a new Crew Member."
→ Barracks is for hiring crew. Provisions are collected at the Mill. The summary's description is entirely wrong.

The summary omits the Town Hall (play 1 hand card), Mill (gain Provisions/Gold), and Silversmith (gain Silver) buildings entirely.

### Scoring section — Armoury Track omitted
**Summary** VP sources: "Raiding settlements, Collected Plunder, Making Offerings, Valkyrie collected, Coins earned, Hired crew with VP values, Harbour tile bonuses."
**Source** end-game scoring (lines 449–467): 1. Valkyrie Track (based on track marker position), 2. Armoury Track, 3. Collected Offering Tiles, 4. Hired Crew, 5. Plunder.
→ The Armoury Track scoring is missing from the summary. The summary mentions "Valkyrie collected" but the actual VP comes from the Valkyrie Track (which caps at 15 VP for 7 Valkyrie), not a raw count. "Coins earned" does not appear as a discrete end-game scoring category in the source; VP is tracked live on the Victory Point Track during play.

## raids

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
SOURCE_SUSPECT (title absent from first 3000 chars), but reading the file confirms it is the Raids Viking game rulebook (Longship boards, Voyage tiles, Glory points, 4 Voyages — all matching the summary).

### Victory / Game-end
Summary: "The player with the most Glory points after the 4th Voyage wins."
Source: "The player with the most Glory points at the end of the fourth Voyage wins the game." ✓

**Tiebreaker:** Summary has no tiebreaker.
Source: "If there is a tie, both players share the victory." — Omission is MINOR.

### MAJOR FINDING: Goods scoring error

**Summary (Scoring section):** Lists "Goods remaining on your Longship" as a Glory point source.
**Source (lines 431–434):** "Pennants, Goods, and Monsters: Score Glory points as shown on each tile you have on or next to your Longship board. **Exception: Goods tiles still on your Longship do not give you any Glory points.**"

The summary directly contradicts this rule. Only Goods that have been **sold at a Port** (and thus placed next to the Longship board) score Glory points. Goods tiles still occupying Longship spaces score 0. This would cause players to miscount end-game VP.

### MAJOR FINDING: Mjöllnir scoring omitted

The summary's Scoring / Victory Conditions section lists: Coins, Pennant tiles, Rune tile sets, Goods remaining (wrong per above), Harbor tile bonuses, Defeating Monsters. It does not mention Mjöllnir scoring.

**Source (lines 395–404 + 429–430):**
"Simple Mjöllnir: This tile gives you 1 Glory point per Viking on your Longship at the end of the game."
"Double Mjöllnir: This tile gives you 2 Glory points per Viking on your Longship at the end of the game."
"Mjöllnirs: Score Glory points by multiplying the number of Mjöllnirs you have by the number of Vikings on your Longship."

Mjöllnir is a significant scoring mechanic tied to crew size — its omission means players cannot compute final scores accurately.

Source: "To begin Combat, you must send 1 Viking… opponent now must choose to Retaliate or Flee. To Retaliate, your opponent must send 2 Vikings… Then it's your turn to Retaliate or Flee. Each time you Retaliate, you must discard as many Vikings as your opponent did plus one." ✓

**Turn order:** Summary: "The player furthest behind on the board (clockwise) takes the next turn."
Source: "the owner of the Longship marker furthest back (going clockwise) is always the player who takes the next turn." ✓

**First turn of each Voyage:** Summary: "On your first turn of each Voyage, skip Phase 1 (no tile to take) and go straight to Navigate."
Source: "During your first turn at the start of each Voyage, skip this step and go straight to the Navigate phase. There is no Voyage tile to take." ✓

## rail-baron

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Prefilter
OK.

### Victory / Game-end

**Win condition amount:** Summary: "accumulated the required cash amount (varies by number of players)."
Source: Standard game requires $200,000 in cash (stated six times, e.g., "a player must return to his 'home city' with $200,000 or more in cash"). "Varies by number of players" is Optional Rule No. 3 only, not the standard game. The summary omits the $200,000 threshold, which is the central win condition.

**Declare mechanic entirely omitted:** The source devotes a full section to the "Declaring" mechanic: a player must announce they are heading home **before rolling for a new destination**; they roll an "alternate" destination but head home instead; other players then have the opportunity to catch them via the "Rover Play" (collecting $50,000 from the declared player). None of this appears in the summary. The summary says only "That player declares 'Rail Baron' and wins," treating it as a name rather than a game mechanic.

### MAJOR FINDING: User fee rule incorrect

**Summary:** "When traveling on a railroad you don't own, pay the owner a fee. If no player owns it, pay the bank."
**Source (lines 226–234):** "1) You must pay the Bank $1000 if your pawn moved along any rail lines **that you own** or the Bank owns. You pay only $1000 per turn, no matter how many of your own or the Bank's rail lines you used that turn. 2) You must pay $5000 to each player who owned rail line that your pawn moved along that turn."

The summary's statement that fees only apply "for railroads you don't own" is wrong. You pay $1,000 to the bank even when using your **own** railroad lines. This is a core economic rule that affects every turn and all purchase decisions.

### Additional finding: Win condition completeness

**Source:** "Before a player can return to his 'home city' and win, however, he must warn the other players by 'declaring' to them at the start of the trip, that he has $200,000 and is returning home." A player cannot win simply by moving into their home city — they must first formally declare on a prior turn.

**Summary:** Completely omits the declaring rule, the rover play, and the $200,000 threshold.

### Numbers checked

**Starting cash:** Summary does not state starting cash.
Source: "each player is given $20,000 in cash." — Omission but not directly misleading.

**EXPRESS cost:** Summary mentions "Express movement rules may apply" (vague). Source: EXPRESS costs $4,000; SUPERCHIEF costs $40,000. The summary doesn't give these prices but also doesn't give wrong prices.

**Turn structure:** Summary lists move → pay fees → collect payoff → buy railroad → roll destination. Source order is broadly similar but the summary omits the Bonus Roll mechanic (extra movement for doubles or owning EXPRESS/SUPERCHIEF).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8, SUPPORTED: 5 (win condition number missing, user fee wrong, declare mechanic omitted)
