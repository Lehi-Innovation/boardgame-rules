## dune-imperium-uprising — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR** — Every number spot-checked is supported by the source, and the round structure, combat, spies, sandworms, ties, and CHOAM contract rules all track the rulebook closely. However, the summary contains material about Shaddam Corrino IV's "Sardaukar Commander" ability and two exclusive "Sardaukar contracts" (stated twice, presented as rulebook content) that has no basis anywhere in the extracted source, plus a multi-contract ruling imported from an external FAQ. These are unverifiable additions rather than contradictions, confined to an optional module/leader, so MINOR rather than MAJOR.

### Findings

1. **Unsourced: "Sardaukar Commander" ability and Sardaukar contracts (MINOR, main driver of verdict).** Summary (Leader clarifications and CHOAM Module sections): "His **Sardaukar Commander** ability: the two Sardaukar contracts are set aside as a private pile reserved exclusively for him… He still acquires a Sardaukar contract through the normal contract-taking mechanism… in place of one of the two face-up board contracts." The extracted source mentions "Sardaukar" only as flavor text ("his elite military—the Sardaukar," l.32) and as a board-space name (l.344). The CHOAM Module section (l.850-932) and the Clarifications section (l.935-954) contain no Sardaukar contracts, no private pile, and no ability named Sardaukar Commander. The source's only Shaddam clarification is the Signet Ring / "Emperor of the Known Universe" deploy-restriction timing (l.948-951), which the summary also reports correctly. The Sardaukar material may come from the leader card or external FAQ, but it cannot be verified against the source and is presented as rulebook content.
2. **Externally sourced ruling (minor).** Summary: "If you hold multiple contracts that name the same board space, sending one Agent there completes all of them… (official FAQ)." Not in the extracted text; at least it is explicitly attributed to the FAQ.
3. **Inference presented as rule (trivial).** Summary: "A sandworm is 'in the Conflict' from the instant it is summoned, so effects that count 'each sandworm…' include sandworms you summoned/deployed earlier the same turn." Not stated in the source; it is a logically safe derivation from l.557-558 and l.769 and does not contradict anything.
4. No contradictions found. Phases 1-5, Agent/Reveal turn rules, Combat Intrigue passing, ties (l.793-806), sandworm doubling incl. the 3-spice/6-spice example (l.779-787), battle icons + Endgame wild icon (l.770-778, l.1114-1120), Makers spaces incl. Habbanya Erg in 6P (l.832-834), tiebreakers spice→Solari→water→garrisoned troops (l.846-848), and the Rise of Ix / Immortality / base-game combination notes (l.1028-1061) all match the source.

### Findings

1. **Omission: traps place a Cleared token (the main finding).** Source: "Place one Cleared token in the room or chamber where the trap was drawn" (l.150-151). The summary's Traps section gives the treasure reward and the Cage/Slide effects but omits the token, so players following the summary would leave a trapped room uncleared (and respawn monsters there).
2. **Omission: surviving-monster bookkeeping.** Source: a monster that survives is placed by the first open numbered slot and "the corresponding Number token in its room or chamber" (l.199-201). Number tokens appear in the summary's component list but their use is never explained.
3. Trivial: Killed! result — source allows choosing "the same one if no other Hero is available" (l.205-207); summary just says "Choose a new Hero." Solo play variants (Appendix 2, l.278-309) and the Level-pile reshuffle rule (l.139-140) are omitted; acceptable summary scope.
4. No invented mechanics or contradictions found. Movement/stopping rules, chamber stacking exception, Lose-a-Turn discard, Fireball/Lightning Bolt no-strike-back, ESP Medallion/Crystal Ball (once per turn, adjacent / skip move-and-attack), dropped-treasure placement, and the Magic Sword replacement rule all match the source.

### Findings

1. **Omission: Treasure Chamber rooms (the main finding).** Source: "Treasure chambers — After the Encounter of this room, the Party receives the reward indicated on the room. If the reward is an Equipment, draw a random Equipment card… The Leader will decide who receives it" (l.274-278). The summary covers the other three special-room types (Shops, Healing Fountains, Dangerous Rooms) but never mentions Treasure Chambers, so players landing on one would find no rule.
2. **Simplified setup (trivial).** Summary: "draw 2 dungeon map boards." Source: the 2 map boards are drawn from a shuffle of the 2 map boards plus the 3 remaining boss boards, flipping any boss side down (l.119-125). Same practical result; detail lost.
3. **OCR-garbled component handled reasonably.** Source merges "14 scar tokens tower with 5 levels / Pieces for the tower with 6 levels" (l.36-37); the summary's hedged "Tower pieces (5 or 6 levels)" is a fair reading, not an error.
4. Trivial: the rule that identical special-shot requirements (e.g., weapon + room with the same icon) are not doubled (l.481-483) and the individual hero ability/shot lists (covered generically) are omitted; the difficulty table's per-pile card counts are unreadable in the source (l.90-97), so the summary's Easy/Normal/Hellish without counts is correct restraint.
5. No invented mechanics or contradictions found. Movement arrows/leader tiebreak, monster draw from topmost tier, gold-on-card, miss conditions (no bounce, hole, bone, off-board), die-on-boundary rule (more of the die / lower section if in doubt), Option A (white die from treasury) / Option B (all take damage, leader token to defeated), white dice any-color-after-throw one-time use, clean-up steps, final boss must-use-bonus-dice rule, and both loss conditions all match the source.
