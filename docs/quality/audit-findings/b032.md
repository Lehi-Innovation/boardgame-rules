## betrayal-at-house-on-the-hill — MAJOR

Component counts are accurate, but the attack rules contradict the source and several core post-haunt systems are missing.

**Numbers checked (14/15 supported):**
- 50 haunts ✓ ("This game has 50 haunts"); 1 rulebook ✓; 2 haunt books ✓; 44 room tiles ✓; 1 Entrance Hall tile / 3 rooms ✓; 6 figures ✓; 6 two-sided character cards ✓; 30 clips ✓; 8 dice ✓; 1 Turn/Damage Track ✓; 13 omen / 22 item / 45 event cards ✓; 291 tokens ✓; 4 clips per character ✓; haunt roll = 6 dice vs. total omens drawn ✓; Mystic Elevator roll 2 dice ✓.
- "custom: 0, 1, or 2 per die" — not stated anywhere in the extracted text (which only says "add the number of dots"). True of the published game, but unsupported by the source; not counted as an error.

**Findings:**
1. **MAJOR — contradicted attack rule.** Summary: "If defender's total >= attacker's: no damage." Source: "Whoever rolls a higher result inflicts physical damage to the other explorer or monster... The amount of damage equals the difference... If there's a tie, no one gets hurt." A defender who out-rolls the attacker damages the attacker. The summary makes attacking risk-free, which materially changes play.
2. **MAJOR omission — no pre-haunt death immunity.** Source: "Before the haunt starts, no one can die—that is, no trait can go below the lowest number on the list." Summary states unconditionally "If any trait reaches the skull symbol, the explorer dies" (twice), which would mislead players into killing explorers before the haunt.
3. **Omission — haunt turn order.** Source defines a full post-haunt phase structure (first hero to the traitor's left, all heroes, then traitor turn, then monster turn). Summary's Player Reference table covers pre/post haunt actions but never mentions this turn order.
4. **Omission — monster stunning.** Source: "Most monsters can't be killed. If a monster suffers any damage, it is stunned and misses its next turn." Summary's Monster Movement section omits stunning entirely and also omits monster movement (roll dice equal to monster Speed).
5. Minor: physical damage is split "Might and/or Speed" (source) — summary's "Defender chooses to reduce Speed or Might" reads as one-trait-only.
6. Minor omissions: moving past opponents costs +1 space per opponent; stealing items on 2+ damage; distance attacks (Revolver). Supported content otherwise checks out (companions Dog/Girl/Madman, Coal Chute, Collapsed Room, birthday start, locked front door).

## beyblade-trading-card-game — MAJOR

The summary invents a turn/battle structure that does not match the source and misses the game's actual core mechanic.

**Findings:**
1. **MAJOR — invented core mechanic (color matching).** Summary: "Compare the colors of the attack and defense cards. Matching colors may block or reduce damage." Source: stopping a card requires the **next color in a fixed cyclic order**: "The order of colors is red, yellow, green, then blue... If you played a red card... he would need a yellow card to stop it." Nothing blocks via matching colors.
2. **MAJOR — invented card taxonomy.** Summary components list "Attack cards, Defense cards, Spin cards, Special ability cards." Source: "There are four different card types in the game: Blade, Action, Character, and Event." There are no attack/defense/spin card types; the spin pile is built from your own deck cards equal to the Blade's spin strength ("Check the spin strength on your Blade card. Take that many cards... Put them face down in a spin pile").
3. **MAJOR — invented turn structure.** Summary: "1. Draw Phase: Draw cards from your deck. 2. Attack Phase... 3. Defense Phase... 4. Resolution." Source: a turn is play a card (starting a battle), battle goes back and forth following color order, then **refill your hand to five at end of turn**. There is no draw phase; a turn can also be passed (discard hand, redraw 5).
4. **MAJOR — invented loss condition.** Summary: "if a player cannot draw a required card, they lose." Source: "Any time you draw the last card from your deck, just shuffle your discard pile and start a new deck." No deck-out loss exists.
5. **Contradicted metadata.** Frontmatter designer "Mike Mulvihill"; source credits "Design: Evan Lorentz, Brad DeFruiter."
6. **Omissions:** 5-card hand size, 60-card minimum deck / max 4 copies per title / exactly 1 Blade, Character & Event "testing" mechanic, attacker-loses = nothing happens ("He can only hit you when you are the defender"), strike/empty battle-edge pieces, best-of-three match play.
7. Numbers: the summary contains almost none. Supported: 2003/Decipher ✓; spin-pile-empty loss ✓; Battle Edge hits removing spin cards ✓. "Discontinued after 6 sets" — not in source (source mentions only the 130-card Collision set); unsupported external claim.

## bismarck — MAJOR

The summary is a generic reconstruction that does not match this rulebook (Avalon Hill 1979 edition). Wrong designer, invented turn structure, wrong victory conditions, and several core systems missing.

**Findings:**
1. **MAJOR — contradicted designer.** Frontmatter: "Charles S. Roberts." Source design credits: "Game Design: Jack Greene, Jr. / Special Design and Development: Mick Uhl" (©1979 Avalon Hill).
2. **MAJOR — invented turn structure.** Summary lists "German Movement Phase / British Movement Phase / Search Phase / Contact Phase / Combat Phase." Source 4.0 sequence: Unit Availability, Visibility, Shadow Determination, Air Movement, Ship Movement, Search, Air Attack, Naval Combat, Chance — both sides act within the same phases; there are no per-side movement phases and no "Contact Phase."
3. **MAJOR — wrong victory conditions.** Summary: "British Victory: Sink the Bismarck. German Victory: Bismarck reaches a friendly port (France) or survives the time limit." Source 12.2: "both players total up the number of victory points... The player with the highest total is declared the winner." Sinking the Bismarck is worth 30 VP, not auto-win; the German player scores VP for sinking British ships (e.g., Hood 14, KGV/PoW 16 each, Victorious 24) and **convoys** (6/6/8/10/12 points); friendly ports include Trondheim and Bergen (Norway), not just France; British score VP even if the Bismarck reaches port (e.g., 14 down to 4 points by date, 6 points if it ports with a midships hit).
4. **MAJOR omission — convoy raiding and the Chance phase.** The German economic objective ("gain victory points by sinking enemy ships and convoys", Chance Table: General Search / HUFF-DUFF / Convoy Location) is entirely absent. The summary frames the German goal solely as evasion/escape.
5. **MAJOR omission — shadowing.** The entire shadowing system (sections 8.0-8.2, a central British mechanic with its own phase and table) is never mentioned.
6. **Contradicted search framing.** Summary: double-blind where "the German player plots secret movement while the British player searches." Source: both players move hidden on **two identical search boards** and both may call out search zones (7.21-7.22); the German player must search to find convoys and British ships.
7. **Component errors.** Summary: "Strategic game board... Search charts and plotting sheets." Source 2.1: Two Identical Search Boards, One Battle Board, counters, 6 Player-Aid Cards, 2 Range Finders and Battle Maneuver Gauges, Hit Record Pad, 2 dice. No plotting sheets.
8. **Setup error.** "The German player secretly places the Bismarck's starting position" — German ships start at Bergen per the order of appearance (12.33 penalizes "not left Bergen by the end of the 2000 May 22 turn"); the start is not freely chosen.
9. Supported bits: fuel tracking ✓ (5.2), weather/fog affecting search ✓ (7.1, 7.3), night restrictions ✓ (11.12), tactical battle board combat with salvoes and evasion ✓ (9.x), ship roster ✓. But the summary contains essentially no rule numbers to verify (only ~3 frontmatter values), itself a sign of low fidelity for a numbers-heavy wargame.

## black-friday — MAJOR

Setup and component numbers are excellent, but the action rules and the entire end-game scoring contradict the source.

**Numbers checked (17/17 supported in Components/Setup):**
- 140 shares (25×5 colors + 15 black) ✓; 5 share price tokens ✓; 2 gold tokens ✓; 20 big + 20 small gold bars ✓; big=5 small=1 ✓; 5 screens ✓; 5 double-sided level tiles ✓; 1 bag ✓; 4 bonus tiles ✓; money mix 30×$1, 15×$5, 20×$10, 15×$50, 10×$100, 10×$200 ✓; price tokens on 8th space ✓; gold tokens on "20" and "0" ✓; remove 1 share/color with 2-4 players ✓; 4 or 5 shares per market space ✓; draw 20 from bag ✓; scarcest→space 10, most common→space 6 ✓; 2 shares/color + 6 black on sale tracks, 9 black on price table ✓; 5 shares + $100 per player ✓; M.I.B.S. required at 2 players, optional 3-4 ✓.

**Findings:**
1. **MAJOR — wrong victory condition and tiebreaker.** Summary: "all remaining shares become worthless... The player with the most gold wins. Tiebreaker: most remaining cash." Source: "Now, sell all remaining shares you still own for the current prices... each small gold bar counts as $100, and each big gold bar $500. If you have the highest total in Dollars, you win... In the case of a tie, the player with the most money in gold bars is the winner." Shares are NOT worthless, the winner is total wealth (cash + gold), and the tiebreaker is gold (summary inverts it to cash).
2. **MAJOR — wrong purchase/sale limits.** Summary: "Buy 1-6 shares of one color" / "Sell 1-6 shares of one color." Source: limit starts at 1 ("You can only buy at most 1 share at the start of the game") and rises with level tiles to a max of 5; multi-color purchases/sales are explicitly allowed ("When you buy multiple colors on your turn..."). Both the range and the one-color restriction are wrong.
3. **MAJOR — wrong sell mechanics.** Summary: "Place them on the current share sale track." Source: sold shares go to the **share market**; you additionally **remove** 1 share from the share sale track into the market. Consequently the summary's crash trigger is backwards: the price change triggers when the sale track is sufficiently *emptied* ("there are only 5 colored shares on the current 'share sale' track"), not "when a share sale track fills up."
4. **MAJOR — invented market update.** Summary: "After each player's turn... Draw shares from the bag to replenish the share market." Source: bag draws happen only during a **price change**, which is triggered by filling the share-purchase/gold-purchase tracks (5/6 spaces) or depleting the sale track — not after every turn.
5. **Contradicted — level mechanics.** Summary: "As share sale tracks fill, the price level decreases." Source: levels only ever increase ("You never switch back to a lower level") and change when the most valuable share price enters a new area during a price change.
6. **Contradicted — black shares on the price table.** Summary: "when share prices drop to these levels, black shares enter the sale track." Source: when prices *rise* into a new level area, the area's black share goes **into the bag** (fueling future price-change draws).
7. **Omissions:** placing 1 share on the share-purchase/gold-purchase track with each buy action (the game's central manipulation mechanic, incl. "buy zero" option), gold price rising via drawn black shares and per-3-purchased-bars, game end at gold price 100, the three-track sale system with center-track refill, bonus tile specifics (tile N grants first N advantages). The M.I.B.S. description ("buys when prices are low, sells when high") is a loose paraphrase — actual rule: buys the cheaper of shares vs. gold while it can afford the full limit, sells only when short of cash — but directionally similar.
8. Supported: M.I.B.S. always starts ✓; black is not a share color ✓; exactly one action per turn from the 3 listed ✓; small/big bar exchange ✓.
