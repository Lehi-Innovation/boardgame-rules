# Audit b029: battlestar-galactica-the-board-game

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Compared `rules/battlestar-galactica-the-board-game.md` against `extracted/battlestar-galactica-the-board-game-rules.txt` (full rulebook text, 2015 lines, complete including attack table and quick reference).

## Major findings

### 1. FTL Control die mechanic is wrong (contradicts source)
- Summary (Actions): "FTL Control (Galactica): Risk an early jump (roll die; on 6-8 jump normally, on 3-5 lose 1 population, on 1-2 lose 3 population)"
- Source (lines 783-791): "The current player rolls the die, and if a '6' or lower is rolled, then a number of population is lost equal to the number listed on the current space of the Jump Preparation track." Also "Frequently Overlooked Rules" (lines 1780-1782): "population is lost according to the Jump Preparation track on a roll of 6 or less."
- The amount lost depends on the track space (-1 or -3), not on the die result; and a roll of 6 *does* lose population. The summary's tiered die results are invented and give the wrong outcome for rolls of 3-6.

### 2. Raider-attacks-viper numbers are wrong
- Summary (Combat): "Raiders attack vipers: Roll die; 1-4 miss, 5-6 damage viper, 7-8 destroy viper."
- Source attack table (lines 1439-1440, repeated 2000-2001): viper attacked: "5–7 = Damaged, 8 = Destroyed."
- A roll of 7 damages, it does not destroy. Wrong threshold that directly changes combat outcomes.

### 3. Nuke effect is invented
- Summary (Combat): "Nuke: Admiral plays to destroy up to 2 basestars or all ships in one area."
- Source attack table (lines 1447-1455): basestar attacked "With Nuke: 1–2 = Damaged Twice; 3–6 = Destroyed; 7–8 = Destroyed and destroy 3 raiders in the same area."
- The nuke is a die-rolled attack on a basestar; "destroy up to 2 basestars or all ships in one area" has no basis in the source.

### 4. Hangar Deck action is invented
- Summary (Actions): "Hangar Deck (Galactica): Repair 1 damaged viper if a pilot is here"
- Source (lines 1548-1553): a player "moves his character to the 'Hangar Deck' location and uses the action listed there. He then launches a viper as normal"; example (lines 1601-1606): "'Starbuck' uses the action in the 'Hangar Deck' location to launch herself in a viper... She may then take another action."
- Hangar Deck launches the character in a viper (plus another action). Repair is done "usually by an engineering card" (lines 1428-1429). The summary's repair action is invented.

### 5. Sickbay movement ban contradicts source
- Summary (Special Rules): "Sickbay: ... Cannot move to another location or take actions." (also "Sickbay/Brig ... no actions or movement" under Key Location Actions)
- Source, Frequently Overlooked Rules (lines 1785-1787): "Players may move out of 'Sickbay' without difficulty, but not the 'Brig.'"
- Only the Brig restricts movement. Telling a player in Sickbay they cannot move or act would seriously mislead at the table.

### 6. Sympathizer Brig outcome is swapped
- Summary (Scoring): "if humans are behind (any resource at half or less), the Sympathizer stays human. If resources are fine, the Sympathizer becomes a revealed Cylon (sent to brig, plays for Cylons)."
- Source (lines 1111-1120): "If at least 1 resource is half full or lower (in the red zone), then the player is moved to the 'Brig' location. This card is then treated as a 'You Are Not a Cylon' card. If every resource is more than half full... this player becomes a revealed Cylon player" (with Cylon Fleet / Super Crisis restrictions).
- The Brig applies to the stays-human case, not the becomes-Cylon case. Summary attaches it to the wrong branch.

## Minor findings

- Quorum draw location: summary credits the "Press Room"; source (lines 711-713) names the President Title Card or the "President's Office" location for drawing Quorum Cards. Wrong location name.
- Administration: summary says it "Gives president title to another player," omitting that it is a difficulty-5 politics/leadership skill check (source lines 1023-1031); presented as automatic.
- Brig "Draw only 1 Skill Card": unsupported by the source (source only documents the difficulty-7 politics/tactics escape check and the no-movement rule).
- Galactica destruction wording: summary "6+ damage tokens without unfilled locations" is garbled; source (lines 1073-1077, 1504-1505) says six or more *locations* on Galactica with damage tokens.
- Command: "Launch up to 2 unmanned vipers" — the count 2 and the launch-only framing are not in the source (source only says vipers "are usually activated by using the 'Command' location," line 1520; activation = launch, move, or attack). Plausibly from the board card, but narrower than activation.
- Omissions (minor): 10-card Skill Card hand limit (lines 900-904); partial-pass skill check results (lines 972-976); revealed Cylons discarding to 3 Skill Cards and losing titles on reveal (lines 1126-1130); FTL Control only usable when the fleet marker is on the blue track spaces (lines 778-780).

## Numbers checked (unsupported/wrong)

- FTL Control die outcomes (see Major 1)
- Raider vs viper: 7 = destroy (should be damage; see Major 2)
- Nuke: "up to 2 basestars" (see Major 3)
- Viper vs raider: summary "3-6 destroys, 7-8 also destroys" — source says 3-8 = Destroyed; functionally equivalent at the table, but the 3-6/7-8 split appears confused with the nuke table row. Not counted as a major error.

## Conclusion

Component counts, setup, loyalty deck composition, skill check procedure, victory thresholds, and turn structure are faithful. However, the Actions/Combat sections contain multiple wrong die thresholds and invented mechanics (FTL Control, raider attack results, nuke, Hangar Deck repair), plus the Sickbay movement contradiction and the swapped Sympathizer Brig outcome. These would mislead players at the table.

**Verdict: MAJOR**
