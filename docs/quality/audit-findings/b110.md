## grand-austria-hotel

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Die action mapping is completely wrong (MAJOR)

The summary maps die values to actions as follows:

| Summary die | Summary action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Take a Guest card |
| 2 | Take a Staff card |
| 3 | Prepare rooms |
| 4 | Take cake and strudel |
| 5 | Take wine and coffee |
| 6 | Gain krones (money) |

The source (extracted, lines 232–265) describes the actions in order:

- "For each die on this Action space, you can take 1 strudel or 1 cake... Example: There are 3 dice showing '1'." → Die 1 = Strudel/Cake
- "For each die on this Action space, you can take 1 wine or 1 coffee... If you understand action '1', you also understand this one" → Die 2 = Wine/Coffee
- "For each die on this Action space, you can prepare a room." (next action after "2") → Die 3 = Rooms ✓
- "For each die on this Action space, you can advance 1 space on the Emperor track or your Money track... Example: There are 2 dice showing '4'." → Die 4 = Emperor/Money track
- "You can play exactly one Staff card. For each die on this Action space, the cost is reduced by 1... Example: There are 4 dice showing '5'." → Die 5 = Staff card
- "Pay 1 krone and choose any of the 5 other actions (1-5). Take the action based on the number of dice showing '6'." → Die 6 = Wild (pay 1 krone, pick any action 1-5)

Correct mapping (from source):
- Die 1 = Strudel/Cake (summary says "Guest")
- Die 2 = Wine/Coffee (summary says "Staff card")
- Die 4 = Emperor track or Money track (summary says "Cake+Strudel")
- Die 5 = Play Staff card (summary says "Wine+Coffee")
- Die 6 = Pay 1 krone, perform any action 1-5 (summary says "Gain krones")

Only Die 3 (Rooms) is correct. This error is pervasive — the Player Reference also has the wrong mapping. Any player using this summary to take actions during play will be wrong on 5 of 6 die values.

### Finding 2 — Serve additional action costs 1 krone (MAJOR)

Summary (line 71): "Serve: Move up to 3 dishes/drinks from Kitchen onto Guest cards"
Source (line 275): "You can pay 1 krone to move up to 3 dishes and drinks from your Kitchen to the orders on your Guest cards."

The summary omits the 1-krone cost for the serve additional action.

### Finding 3 — Final scoring omits money/leftover resources and guest penalty (MAJOR)

Summary final scoring (lines 103–110) lists:
- VP from fulfilled guests
- VP from occupied rooms
- VP from Emperor scoring
- VP from staff cards with end-game bonuses
- VP from Politics cards

Source final scoring (lines 422–428):
- VP from Staff cards ✓
- VP for occupied rooms (1 VP/room row 1, 2 VP/room row 2, 3 VP/room row 3, 4 VP/room top row) ✓ (summary doesn't give row VP values, minor)
- "You receive 1 Victory Point for each krone and leftover dish and drink in your Kitchen." — MISSING from summary
- "You lose 5 Victory Points for each guest in your Café." — MISSING from summary

Both of these VP sources affect final score and are absent from the summary.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreaker wrong

Summary (line 110): "VP track goes to 75; use VP markers for scores above 75." No tiebreaker stated.
Source (line 429): "In case of a tie, the player who has more dishes, drinks, and krones left wins." — Missing from summary.

### Finding 5 — Passing mechanic misrepresented

Summary (lines 73–74): "Instead of taking an action, a player may pass, taking a Turn Order tile (lower numbers give first pick next round, but passing early means fewer actions this round)."

Source (lines 307–313): "When you pass, you must wait until every other player has either passed or taken two actions. When that happens, the player with the lowest number still showing on their Turn Order tile takes the remaining dice from all the Action spaces, removes one, and rolls the rest again..."

The summary doesn't describe the key mechanic: a player who passes triggers a dice reroll after everyone else has gone. This is a meaningful procedural omission.

## great-western-trail

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Game end trigger is wrong (MAJOR)

Summary (line 81): "Game end trigger: When a player places their last train station token or when the job market cannot be fully refilled."

Source (lines 1418–1425): "The game end is triggered when a player places a worker tile onto the last space of the job market while carrying out subphase 2 or 3 in Kansas City. As this makes the job market token move along the red arrow out of the job market..."

The source does not say the game ends when a player places their last train station token. There are no "train station tokens" mentioned as the game end trigger at all. The game ends when the job market is filled and the token moves out along the red arrow. The secondary trigger ("job market cannot be fully refilled") does not match the source either — the source says the trigger is the token moving out, not inability to refill. This is a fundamental misstatement of the game-end condition that would cause players to play a different number of rounds.

### Finding 2 — "Workers not hired = negative VP" has no source support (MAJOR)

Summary (lines 94–96):
```
Penalties:
- Undelivered cattle (negative VP for low delivery values)
- Workers not hired (possible negative VP from the job market)
```

No support found in source for "Workers not hired (possible negative VP from the job market)." Searched for: "worker.*penalty", "workers not hired", "job market.*VP", "negative.*worker", "penalty.*hire". The only negative VP sources in the source are: unfulfilled objective cards, certain city crests (Kansas City disc = -6 VP), and city crest VP that may be negative.

"Undelivered cattle (negative VP for low delivery values)" — also no direct support found in source. The source does not mention a delivery value penalty per se. Searched: "delivery.*penalty", "penalty.*delivery", "cattle.*negative", "undelivered". Not confirmed.

### Finding 3 — Delivering cattle describes discard and reshuffle as automatic (not supported for all cards)

Summary (line 76): "Discard all cattle cards; shuffle your discard pile into your draw pile."

Source (line 569-572): "Reveal your entire hand to the other players and calculate its total breeding value: To do this, sum the breeding values of each different type of cattle that you have in hand."

The source does not state at this point that all hand cards are discarded. The summary's claim about discarding "all cattle cards" and reshuffling needs a source citation. Actually examining further context would be needed but the source excerpt does not confirm automatic full discard at Kansas City. This may be inaccurate but is uncertain. I will not flag this as a confirmed finding.

## grind

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — White and red dice behaviors are reversed (MAJOR)

Summary (lines 79–81):
- "White dice: Used for actions, go to your dice well (unavailable for rest of turn)."
- "Red dice: Used for actions, given to opponent's dice pool after use."

Source:
- Action dice (white), line 222–224: "Action dice allow your steamjack to make attacks and perform other actions. Each time you make an attack, you must use at least 1 action die in the attack roll. You begin each turn with 10 action dice in your dice pool. After you spend or roll action dice, place them in your opponent's dice pool."
- Power dice (red), lines 235–240: "You can add any power dice from your dice pool to your rolls to increase your chance of success greatly. After you roll power dice, place them in your dice well for the rest of the turn."

The summary has the post-roll destinations exactly backwards: white action dice go to the **opponent's pool** (not your dice well), and red power dice go to **your dice well** (not opponent's pool). This is a critical reversal that would completely change the resource dynamics of the game.

### Finding 2 — Dice in the summary conflates "white" with actions dice and "red" with power dice incorrectly

The summary's Actions section says: "White dice go to the dice well (out for the turn); red dice go to opponent's pool." This is the reversed behavior as described above.

The Player Reference table also states:
- "White (to your well)"
- "Red (to opponent's pool)"

Both are wrong per source.

### Finding 3 — Game structure: turns per period not stated precisely

Summary: "The game is played in periods. Each period consists of multiple turns for each player." This is vague.
Source (lines 481–493): "A game of Grind consists of two periods. These have five rounds of play, each with one turn taken by each player... After five rounds, the first period of the game ends."

So the game has exactly 2 periods of 5 rounds each. The summary's "multiple turns" is imprecise but not technically wrong. However, "periods" with "multiple turns" is not a MAJOR error by itself.

### Finding 4 — Sudden Death tiebreaker missing

Summary says: "The player with the most goals when the game clock expires wins." and "Alternatively, reaching a target number of goals may end the game immediately." The second claim has no support in source. The source says nothing about "a target number of goals ending the game."

Source (lines 526–537): "The team with the most goals at the end of the game wins! Overtime: Sudden Death — If both teams have an equal number of goals after both periods are complete, the game goes into Sudden Death... The first team to score a goal during Sudden Death wins!"

The tiebreaker is Sudden Death, not described at all in the summary. The "target number of goals" alternate win condition is invented.

## guatemala-caf

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Blockade mechanic entirely missing (MAJOR)

The entire Blockade mechanic is absent from the summary. Source (lines 352–373):
"Each time before the victory points are counted, the players, starting with the player left of the one who initiated the scoring, are asked in clockwise fashion whether they want to block (prevent) the scoring... If a player wants to prevent the scoring, he replaces the coffee sack on that space with a sack of the same color from his stock. The current player still gets his money but the entire scoring for this turn is canceled. However, the current player may build plantations instead without moving the Buyer again."

This mechanic changes how scoring turns play out. A player can spend a coffee sack from their stock to cancel an opponent's VP scoring entirely (though they keep their 8 centavos). The summary says nothing about this rule.

### Finding 2 — Tiebreaker missing (MINOR)

Summary game end (line 84): "The game ends when a player's VP marker reaches the final coffee sack space on the victory point track. The player with the highest VP wins." No tiebreaker stated.

Source (lines 377–382): "The game ends when a player's victory point marker reaches or passes the last coffee sack that has been placed. The winner is the player who is ahead on the victory point track. In case of a tie, the winning player is the one with the most money."

Tiebreaker (most money wins a tie) is missing from summary.

### Finding 3 — Ship scoring: ships are removed after scoring (omission)

Summary describes doubling/tripling/quadrupling with ships but omits: after scoring, ships are removed. Source (lines 330–344): "If only one ship has been scored, it is removed from the game... only the cheapest ship of the scored color puts out to sea and is removed from the game."

This is a meaningful procedural rule: once a coffee type is scored with ships, those ships are consumed. The summary does not mention this.

### Finding 4 — "Selling Coffee" section describes unclear scoring (MINOR)

Summary (lines 79-81) has a "Selling Coffee" section that says: "compare your plantation's worker count to the coffee sack requirements. Score VP based on the number of workers connected to your shed and whether you have ships to export." This is vague and doesn't accurately represent the scoring rules (1 VP per worker, doubled with 1 ship, tripled with 2 ships, quadrupled with 3 ships). However, the scoring details are correctly given in the "Replenishing Money & Scoring" section of the summary and the VP sources table is correct. This is a minor redundancy/clarity issue.
