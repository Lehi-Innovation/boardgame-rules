## colt-express — MAJOR

Multiple wrong component counts, several invented rules (including an invented component), reversed movement rules, a contradicted tiebreaker, and an omitted core system (Events).

### Invented mechanics / components
1. **"1 cactus token (first player marker)"** (Components, Setup step 7, Round End "Pass the cactus"). No cactus token exists anywhere in the source. The first-player marker is the deck of Round cards ("The player on the First Player's left becomes the new First Player. He puts the remaining Round cards in front of him").
2. **"The player who last traveled by train goes first"** (Setup step 7). Source: a bandit pawn is drawn randomly — "draw one of them randomly. The player corresponding to the drawn Character is the First Player."
3. **"each car gets a number of purses equal to the number of players"** (Setup step 3). Source: "Place in each Car the number and type of Loot tokens indicated on its floor." No player-count rule.
4. **"Played cards go to each player's discard pile"** and **"draw their hand back up to 6 cards"** (Round End). In the base game, performed cards go back "on his personal deck," and at the start of each round players shuffle the whole deck and draw six. A discard pile and fill-to-six exist only in the Expert Variant; the summary presents them as base rules.

### Contradicted rules
5. **Move action reversed.** Summary: "Move your bandit 1-3 cars in either direction (inside the train)" and "Roof movement: ...you may move any number of cars (not limited to 3)." Source is the opposite: inside the train, move "from one Car to the next adjacent Car" (exactly 1); on the roof, "A distance ranging from one to three Cars." Both halves are wrong and would directly mislead at the table.
6. **Tiebreaker inverted.** Summary: "Ties broken by bullets fired (most wins)." Source: "In case of a tie, the winner is the tied player who has received the fewest Bullet cards from other players and Events."
7. **Gunslinger criterion (minor):** summary says "played the most Fire cards"; source measures "fewest Bullet cards of their colour left" (a Fire with no target keeps its bullet). Also omits that all tied players each get $1000.

### Major omissions
8. **Events system omitted entirely.** Round cards and the Train Station card trigger Events at the end of each round ("These Events always take place at the end of the Round, after Phase 2"); the source lists eight (Passengers' Rebellion, Take It All!, Braking, Swivel Arm, Angry Marshal, Pickpocketing, Marshal's Revenge, Hostage-Taking of the Conductor). The summary never mentions Events.
9. Train length scales with player count ("as many Cars as there are players") — summary implies the full train is always built.
10. Starting setup omits: each player takes 6 Bullet cards and a face-down $250 starting Purse; a Strongbox starts in the Locomotive with the Marshal, and the second Strongbox starts off the train; Train Station card goes on the bottom of the round deck.
11. 2-player special rules and Expert Variant omitted while frontmatter advertises 2-6 players (and elements of the Expert Variant leaked into the base round-end rules, see #4).

Supported items worth noting: Schemin'/Stealin' two-phase structure, tunnel face-down play, common-deck resolution in played order, Punch (drop loot, move 1 car), Rob, Marshal action (flee to roof + Neutral Bullet), roof line-of-sight vs adjacent-car shooting, bullet cards as dead cards, punched bandit with no loot still moves.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — swapped/wrong component counts (13/17, 36 vs 26), invented cactus token and first-player rule, reversed Move distances, inverted tiebreaker, fabricated loot-placement and round-card setup rules, Expert-Variant rules presented as base game, Events system missing.

## comanauts — MAJOR

The summary's core gameplay description is largely fabricated. The game's actual engine — a dice-bag system with colored attribute dice, clarity, a threat track, and a 7-step player turn — is entirely absent, replaced by an invented 4-phase round.

### Numbers checked
The summary contains almost no numbers to check — the entire Components list is uncounted, which itself obscures how little of the source it reflects.

| Claim in summary | Source evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 2-4 players (frontmatter) | "a cooperative adventure game for 2-4 players" | OK |
| Turn = 4 phases (Event/Action/Enemy/Status) | Player turn has 7 steps: "1. Draw Dice 2. Gain Clarity 3. Place Threat 4. Place Inner Child 5. Perform Actions 6. Discard Dice 7. Check Threat" | **WRONG — structure invented** |
| Component counts | Source: 38 Dice, 60 Item Cards, 11 Totem Cards, 6 Environment Cards, 22 Avatar Cards, 32 Hostile Cards, 13 I.D. Entity Cards, 55 Clue Cards, 11 Comazone Cards, 10 Vital Signs Cards, 24 Status Cards, 4 Player Sheets, 30 Clarity Tokens, 20 Life Force Tokens, etc. | **All omitted** — summary gives zero counts |

### Invented mechanics
1. **"Event Phase: Draw and resolve an event card"** — there are no event cards in the game. No "Event" anything appears in the source.
2. **"Status Phase: Check for ongoing effects and advance any timers"** — no such phase, no timers. Status cards apply immediately and are removed per their "removal" instructions.
3. **"Enemy Phase"** as a fixed per-round phase — enemy turns are conditional, triggered by the Check Threat step ("If the situation (token) is 'hostile'... and the number of dice on the threat track equals or exceeds the number of enemies in play, the enemies take a turn").
4. **"Rest: Recover health or remove negative conditions"** — no Rest action exists. Actual actions: Focus, Move, Reserve, Encourage, Equip/Trade, Skill Test, Group Task, Melee/Ranged Attack, Search.
5. **"If a comanaut is knocked out, they can be revived by teammates at a cost"** — invented. Source: a defeated avatar "ceases to exist in this comazone," its card is discarded, the player reveals their next avatar (the Morass), which returns at the start of that player's turn. No teammate revival, no cost.
6. **"Lose: ...or time runs out for the session"** — no time limit exists. Actual loss conditions: "If the Flatline vital signs card is revealed, if any player's third avatar is sent to the Morass or if all avatars are in the Morass at the same time, the players lose."
7. **"Collect enough clues to identify the I.D.'s weakness"** and "I.D.s have... weaknesses that must be discovered through clues" — clues identify the Prime I.D.'s **location** (which comazone), not a weakness: "Each clue hints at the emotions and memories associated with the Prime I.D. Entity... guide them to the correct comazone."
8. **"Dream space tiles"** (Components) and "dream spaces" throughout — there are no tiles; locations are **comazones**, pages in the Adventure Book. "Event cards" and "clue tokens" likewise do not exist (clue **cards**; clarity/insight tokens).
9. **"Items found in one session may carry over to the next in campaign mode"** — not stated anywhere in the source.
10. **Enemy combat misdescribed:** "Enemies counterattack" — enemies act only on enemy turns driven by threat dice rolled against their card's action table; players defend with a reserved die vs the attack's strength, losing 1 life force on failure. Enemies are defeated by a single successful attack roll vs their defense value (no enemy health attrition), except the Prime I.D., which loses 1 life-force token per defeat (tokens = player count).

### Major omissions (core systems entirely absent)
- The **dice bag / attribute dice** engine: draw 5 dice per turn; spend red (melee), green (ranged), yellow (search), blue (resistance), purple (wild), white (clarity) dice to act. This is the heart of the game and is never mentioned.
- **Clarity tokens** (gain via white dice, spend 3 for avatar abilities, 1 to re-roll).
- **Threat track / situation token (safe vs hostile)** and Check Threat.
- **The Nexus, comazone cards, totem cards** for traveling between comazones.
- **Vital signs deck / Flatline** loss condition.
- **The Morass / 3-avatars-per-player** structure (each player has a stack of avatars; losing the third loses the game).
- **Inner child die/standee** (the actual clue-gathering trigger: "Successful interactions with the inner child will provide clues to the Prime I.D.'s secret location").
- **Insight tokens** (3 insight = reveal a clue card), skill tests, group tasks, equipment slots/inventory limits.
- Setup omits everything specific: removing 2 Fear I.D. copies, revealing 5 I.D. Entities, secretly drawing the Prime I.D., stacking its 5 matching clue cards, dealing 2 face-down avatars + 1 chosen per player, bookkeeper/bookmark.

Accurate content is limited to the Overview (co-op, Dr. Strobal's coma, inner demons/I.D.s, book-driven, session-or-campaign play) and the campaign win framing.

**Verdict: MAJOR** — the Turn Structure, Actions, and most Special Rules are fabricated; the game's actual dice-driven engine, threat system, Morass, and vital-signs loss conditions are missing; loss conditions and the clue mechanic are contradicted.
