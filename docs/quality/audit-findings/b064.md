## cuba-libre — MAJOR

Source: official GMT rulebook text (3rd Printing, Living Rules March 2019), clean extraction. The summary's Components, Setup, Sequence of Play, and Propaganda Round skeleton are faithful, but the faction victory conditions and large parts of the Operations/Special Activities menus are wrong or invented.

### Finding 1 (MAJOR): Government victory condition wrong
- Summary: "**Government:** Total Support + Total Bases on map >= threshold" (and Player Reference: "Support + Bases >= threshold").
- Source (7.2): "Government: All Cities are at Active Support and Total Support exceeds 18." End-game margin (7.3): "Government: Total Support – 18."
- Government victory does not involve Bases at all; the summary also omits the "All Cities at Active Support" requirement and the 18 threshold.

### Finding 2 (MAJOR): Syndicate victory condition wrong (sum instead of dual condition)
- Summary: "**Syndicate:** Total open Casinos + Resources >= threshold."
- Source (7.2): "Syndicate has more than 7 open Casinos and Resources above 30." Margin (7.3): "Lower of open Casinos – 7 or Syndicate Resources – 30."
- These are two independent conditions (and the margin is the *lower* of the two), not a sum.

### Finding 3 (MAJOR): Directorio victory condition omits Bases
- Summary: "**Directorate:** Total Population in spaces they Control >= threshold."
- Source (7.2): "Directorio: Total Population in spaces Controlled by Directorio (1.7) plus the number of Directorio Bases exceeds 9."
- The "+ Directorio Bases" term is missing; threshold (9) unstated. (26July's condition is structurally correct in the summary — Opposition + Bases — though its threshold of 15 is also unstated.)

### Finding 4 (MAJOR): Invented Government Operation "Patrol"; Garrison missing
- Summary: "**Patrol:** Move cubes along adjacent spaces. May Activate enemy Guerrillas encountered."
- Source (3.2): "The Government chooses from Train, Garrison, Sweep, and Assault Operations." Garrison (3.2.2): "Move any number of cubes from any spaces to any ECs or Cities. Then, in each EC ... Activate 1 Guerrilla for each cube there. Then, if desired, Assault (3.2.4) in 1 EC at no added cost."
- "Patrol" does not exist in Cuba Libre (it belongs to other COIN volumes); Garrison is omitted entirely.

### Finding 5 (MAJOR): Government Special Activities largely invented
- Summary: "Air Strike ... Civic Action ... Govern ... Advise (US)."
- Source (4.2): "The Government may choose from Transport, Air Strike, or Reprisal Special Activities."
- Only Air Strike is correct. "Govern" and "Advise" do not exist in this game; Civic Action is a Train option / Support Phase activity (3.2.1, 6.3.2), not a Special Activity. Transport and Reprisal are omitted.

### Finding 6 (MAJOR): Directorio Special Activities invented
- Summary: "Student Protests, Kidnap, Ambush, Propaganda."
- Source (4.4): "Directorio may choose from Subvert, Ambush, or Assassinate Special Activities."
- Only Ambush is correct; "Student Protests", "Kidnap" (a 26July SA), and "Propaganda" have no basis as DR Special Activities; Subvert and Assassinate are omitted.

### Finding 7 (MAJOR): Syndicate given Attack; Construct omitted
- Summary: "**Syndicate Operations:** ... **Attack:** Remove pieces where Syndicate forces outnumber opponents."
- Source (3.3.3): Attack's executing faction "must be 26July or Directorio". Syndicate's fourth Operation is Construct (3.3.5): "Construct Operations—executed only by the Syndicate—build new Casinos or open closed ones ... pay 5 Resources per space."
- The Syndicate cannot Attack; Construct (the only way to build/open Casinos, 5 Resources/space, never free) is missing from the summary. Relatedly, the summary's "Rally: Place Guerrillas and open Casinos" is wrong — Rally does not open Casinos.

### Finding 8 (MAJOR): Syndicate Special Activities largely invented
- Summary: "Bribe, Extort, Assassinate, Gamble."
- Source (4.5): "The Syndicate may choose from Profit, Muscle, or Bribe Special Activities."
- Only Bribe is correct. "Extort" and "Gamble" do not exist; Assassinate is a Directorio SA. Profit (Cash markers) and Muscle (relocate Police/Troops) are omitted.

### Finding 9 (MINOR): 26July Special Activities — "Agitate" is not an SA
- Summary: "Infiltrate, Ambush, Kidnap, Agitate."
- Source (4.3): "26July may choose from Infiltrate, Ambush, or Kidnap." Agitation is a 26July Propaganda Round Support Phase activity (6.3.3), not a Special Activity.

### Finding 10 (MINOR): Attack mechanic mischaracterized
- Summary: "**Attack:** Remove enemy pieces where 26July Guerrillas outnumber enemy cubes."
- Source (3.3.3): Activate all Guerrillas, "roll a die: if the roll is less than or equal to the number of the executing Faction's Guerrillas there ... remove or close up to 2 enemy pieces." No outnumbering requirement; it is a die roll capped at 2 removals.

### Finding 11 (MINOR): Assault terrain modifier wrong terrain
- Summary: "In Forest/Mountain terrain, need more Troops."
- Source (3.2.4): Mountain only — "In Mountain, instead remove only 1 piece for every 2 Troops." Forest halves Sweep activation (3.2.3), not Assault.

### Finding 12 (MINOR): 2nd Eligible option restrictions and Pass income omitted
- Source 2.3.4: the 2nd Eligible's options depend on what the 1st did (LimOp if 1st did Op-only; Event if 1st did Op+SA; full Op+SA if 1st did Event). Source 2.3.3: Passing earns +1 Resource (+3 for Government). The summary presents both factions as having free choice and omits Pass income.

## cuboro-tricky-ways — PASS

Source: official multilingual rulebook (German first; English section at lines 209–387 used for comparison). The summary tracks the English text closely, including the variant rules.

### Findings
- No invented mechanics or contradicted rules found. One ambiguity noted (not counted as an error): the summary states modifications are "exactly 3" and "Players must use all 3 modifications each turn"; the English source says cubes "can be rearranged using a maximum of three types of changing," while the German original says "dürfen mit drei Veränderungen umgeordnet werden" ("may be rearranged with three changes"). The summary's exactly-3 reading matches the German original; the "must use all 3" emphasis is an interpretive addition, but given the translation ambiguity I am not confident it is an error.
- Trivial omission: the minus-points variant applies the penalties "for the 2nd and 3rd change" per source; the summary lists the penalty values without that qualifier (variant rule only).

## cyclades — MINOR

Source: official Matagot English rulebook, clean extraction. The summary is largely accurate — every component count and cost checked is correct — but a few small rule qualifiers are omitted.

### Finding 1 (MINOR): Temple discount once-per-cycle limit omitted
- Summary: "Temple (reduces Creature cost by 1 GP)" / "Temples reduce Creature cost by 1 GP each (min 1)."
- Source: "each Temple reduces the cost of buying a Mythological Creature by 1 GP. **The price reduction of each Temple can only be used once per cycle.**"
- A player buying multiple creatures in a cycle would wrongly apply the discount repeatedly.

### Finding 2 (MINOR): Battle bonuses are defender-only; summary's Battle section doesn't say so
- Summary (Battle): "Each player rolls 1 die and adds number of units. Fortresses add +1 per Fortress (land). Ports add +1 per Port (adjacent sea)."
- Source: "the **defender** adds «1» to his die value for each Fortress" / "the **defender** adds «1» to his die value for each Port."
- The buildings table does say "+1 defense"/"+1 naval defense", so the misread risk is limited (an attacker with a Port adjacent to the contested sea space could wrongly claim +1).

### Finding 3 (MINOR): Territory marker rule for vacated isles omitted
- Source: "If his Troops leave their starting isle empty, he must leave a territory marker there. This isle belongs to him as long as no opponent lands there. (An isle owned by a player can therefore never become neutral again)."
- The summary lists 3 territory tokens in Components and uses them in the mutual-annihilation rule, but never states the general vacated-isle rule.

- Trivial (not counted): "discard 2 GP creature" omits "if it wasn't used during the previous round" (a used creature is already discarded, so no practical effect); fleet movement's "add or abandon Fleets during movement" omitted; max-3-extra-units-per-turn is conveyed implicitly via the 2nd/3rd/4th cost listings.
