# Audit b075: dragon-eclipse

**Verdict: MAJOR**

Compared `rules/dragon-eclipse.md` against `extracted/dragon-eclipse-rules.txt` (3,792 lines, full read). Component counts and most structure are faithful, but two core Combat rules are contradicted by the source — both would mislead players at the table.

## Major findings

### 1. Player card-flow direction is inverted (Turn Structure, contradicted rule)

- **Summary (Turn Structure, Player Turn step 4):** "Slide remaining cards toward slot 1. Draw a new card to fill slot 4."
- **Source:** Cards slide toward slot 4 (the rightmost/highest slot) and new cards fill from slot 1:
  - "Slide all Action cards in the slots from left to right to fill empty slots. Then, draw new Action cards to fill the remaining empty slots, starting from the rightmost empty slot." (Draw step)
  - Prepare Bonus Action: "place it in the rightmost empty slot (usually, slot 1)" — i.e. new cards usually enter slot 1.
  - Interrupt definition confirms slot orientation: "discard the X rightmost cards from the opponent's track (starting from the highest numbered slot with a card)" — the highest-numbered slot is rightmost, so sliding "left to right" moves cards toward slot 4, not slot 1.
  - The worked Prepare example (one card remaining, played cards from slots 4 and 3) slides the remaining card to slot 4 and draws "1 new card to slot 3" — only consistent with new cards entering at the low-numbered end.
  - Enemy turn uses the same flow ("Draw Enemy attack card to 1st slot"), which the summary states correctly for the Enemy but reverses for the player.
- **Impact:** Inverts card aging. In the source, new cards enter slot 1 and drift toward slot 4 (which grants 3 Power, holds the oldest cards, and is what Interrupt strips first); "Slot X" conditional effects also resolve at the wrong time under the summary's scheme.

### 2. Fatigue gain attributed to the wrong deck's reshuffle (contradicted rule)

- **Summary (Special Rules & Edge Cases):** "Deck reshuffle: When your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile (including Fatigue) to form a new deck. Gain 1 Fatigue when reshuffling."
- **Source:** Fatigue is gained when the **Enemy** deck empties, not when yours does:
  - "If the Enemy deck becomes empty at any moment: You gain 1 Fatigue and place it on the top of your discard pile... Reshuffle the Enemy Attack discard and create a new Enemy deck" (Enemy Turn, step 1).
  - "You receive Fatigue cards during Combat as a result of reshuffling the Enemy deck" (Fatigue section).
  - Player Reshuffle keyword has no Fatigue clause: "when you must draw a card and your deck is empty, discard all your attached Upgrades, shuffle your discard pile, and create a new deck."
- **Impact:** Players following the summary would add Fatigue every time their own 16-card deck cycles (a significant difficulty change) and would never gain Fatigue from Enemy deck cycling, which is the actual pacing mechanism. Also note the source places the gained Fatigue on top of your discard pile, not directly into the shuffle.

## Minor findings

3. **Taming order omitted:** Summary says "Fulfill all conditions on the Taming card." Source: "you must fulfill all conditions on its Taming card in the presented order (from the first to the last)... you cannot fulfill the third Taming condition if you haven't fulfilled the 1st and 2nd ones yet." The in-order requirement is mechanically relevant.
4. **Power-per-slot simplified:** Summary: "Choose a card from one of 4 Action slots. Gain its Power marker." Source: "Gain as many Power as shown above that slot" (varies by slot; e.g. slot 4 grants 3 Power in the example). Singular "its Power marker" obscures that slots grant different amounts.
5. **Bonus Action once-per-turn limit omitted:** Source: "Each Bonus Action may only be resolved once per Player's turn." Summary mentions usability before/after the Action card but not the limit (the limit appears in the source's COMBAT ORDER reference card too).
6. **Omissions (acceptable for a summary, noted for completeness):** Elemental advantage icon system (effects printed on cards), Hard modes (Corruption / Unpredictable / Steel), Summons, Random Combat / Tier card setup procedure (Tier cards listed only as a component), Conceding rule, Mulligan during Combat setup, end-of-turn discard of 1 Condition token of each type.
7. **Not flagged:** "12 Enemy Attack cards per set" — the source component list is OCR-garbled here ("Q Enemy Attack cards / 12 Enemy Attack cards"); "12" is supported, "per set" is a plausible reading (solo vs 2-player decks exist) but unverifiable from the extraction.

## Conclusion

Components, resource caps, deck construction, scoring/end-of-combat conditions, and campaign structure are accurate. However, the player-turn card-flow direction is inverted and the Fatigue trigger is attached to the wrong deck — two confidently contradicted core Combat rules that would change how every Combat plays. Verdict: **MAJOR**.
