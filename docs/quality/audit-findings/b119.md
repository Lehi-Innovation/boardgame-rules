## imperium-horizons

### Verdict: MAJOR

### Finding 1 — Power card omitted from scoring (MAJOR)

**Summary (Scoring section):**
> "Score all cards in Hand, Play area, Draw deck, Discard pile, and History. Do NOT score un-played Nation deck or Development area cards."

**Source (lines 1317–1320):**
> "Score every card in your Hand, Play area, Draw deck, Discard pile and History (see page 38)/Legends (see page 46)/Flooded (see page 42) pile, **as well as for your Power card**. Do not score for un-played cards still in your Nation deck or your Development area."

**Analysis:** The Power card is listed as a distinct, separate scoring entity in the source ("as well as for your Power card"), clearly distinct from the Play area. The diagram in setup explicitly separates "power card" and "play area" as different zones (lines 774–778). Power cards can carry VP values — for example, the Wagadou A-side Power card explicitly scores "1 VP per 3 [Progress] in play" (lines 1644–1650), and the Cultists B-side Power card scores additional points based on ceremony stages (line 3352). A player following the summary's scoring instruction would not score their Power card's VP. This is a scoring omission that can change final scores.

**Supporting evidence for Wagadou Power card scoring:**
Source (lines 1644–1650): "1 VP per 3 [Progress] in play, except [Tang]" — this scoring line appears on the Wagadou Power card (3WAG1A/23).

### Finding 2 — Innovate turn restriction omitted (MINOR)

**Summary:**
> "No Actions or Exhausts. Instead: discard all cards from hand, then Break through for one card."

**Source (lines 1171–1177):**
> "An Innovate turn allows you to Break through (see page 36) for a card...Note that you may not use the Innovate turn to Break [through from the Main deck]."

**Analysis:** The restriction "you may not use the Innovate turn to Break through from the Main deck" is absent from the summary. This is a significant gameplay restriction — players could illegally break from the Main deck on Innovate turns. However, the Break through section in the source (and the summary's description of Break through) includes the full explanation, so it is a meaningful omission but arguably addressable by cross-reference. Given the summary's Actions section doesn't cover the restriction, this is a procedural error.

This could mislead players at the table but is in the Actions section rather than scoring, so I rate it MINOR compared to the MAJOR Power card scoring omission already found.
