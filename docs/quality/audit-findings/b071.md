## detective — MAJOR

**Verdict: MAJOR** — contradicted rule on investigator ability frequency, a denied mechanic (Write a Report), and a missing mandatory mechanic/scoring source (Signatures / Matching Evidence).

### Findings

1. **[MAJOR — contradicted rule] Investigator ability frequency.**
   - Summary (Actions): "Each investigator has a unique ability (once per case unless otherwise noted)." Player Reference: "Use Ability (once per case)."
   - Source: "Each investigator has a special ability that can be used once per **day**" (line 662); "All abilities can be used only once per **day**!" (line 798); "Mia's ability can also be used only once per **day**" (line 674). Used Ability tokens are removed each day: "Remove all Used Ability tokens from the investigator tiles" at day's end (lines 357-358, 800-801).
   - Impact: in a multi-day case players would use abilities once instead of once per day — directly misleads at the table. The summary's End-of-Day list also omits removing Used Ability tokens, reinforcing the error.

2. **[MAJOR — denied/contradicted mechanic] Write a Report.**
   - Summary: "Write a Report: Organize and discuss clues gathered. **No specific mechanics beyond team discussion.**"
   - Source: "Once per day, you may spend one of your working hours at any location to Write a report in order to receive an Authority token. If you do, move the Time marker one space forward and add 1 [Authority] to your Token pool. You cannot Write a report during Overtime hours" (lines 666-671).
   - Impact: the action's actual cost/benefit (1 hour → 1 Authority, once/day, not in Overtime) is explicitly denied. Also breaks Jack Coleman's ability (modifies this action).

3. **[MAJOR — missing mandatory mechanic + scoring source] Signatures / Matching Evidence.**
   - Summary: never mentions signatures or evidence matching.
   - Source: "When you encounter a Signature, you must input it into the Antares Database" (lines 378-380); "Inputting encountered Signatures into the Antares Database is **mandatory**!" (line 781-782); 12-character case-sensitive signatures in three categories SD/SDNA/SM (lines 750-758); "You will automatically receive additional points for each piece of matched evidence you've entered into the Antares Database (worth more than additional questions, but less than the main questions)" (lines 868-870).
   - Impact: an entire mandatory subsystem and an end-of-case scoring source are missing.

4. **[MINOR — missing scoring details] Final Report scoring.**
   - Summary: "Each correct answer earns victory points. Scoring Thresholds ... vary by case."
   - Source adds: report has two sections (main questions worth most; additional metaplot questions worth least, lines 858-867); "for each [Stress token] you will lose 1 point" (lines 871-873); "Each 'I don't know' answer is worth 0 points. Each wrong answer is worth -1 point" (lines 913-914); each case has a predetermined point total needed to win (line 865).

5. **[MINOR — omission] Plot cards.**
   - Source: Plot cards represent leads for future cases; added unread to the zip bag of the indicated future case (lines 830-849). The "ADD A PLOT CARD" instruction keyword (line 416) is also omitted. Summary never mentions Plot cards.

6. **[MINOR — omission] Lead resolution constraints.** Source: once you draw a lead card you MUST resolve it (lines 366-368); card sorting to left/right of board tracking back-side access (lines 384-392); Stress Levels (LSL/MSL/HSL) in questioning (lines 629-638). All absent from summary (the LSL/HSL system is flavor-adjacent but used throughout cases).

### Numbers checked (11): 36 cards/deck = 1 cover + 35 leads ✓ (39-40); skill tokens 3/3/3/3 + 5 wild ✓ (152-153); 15 Authority / 10 Stress / 5 Used Ability ✓ (151); 5 Special tokens ✓ (119); 3 wooden markers ✓ (149-150); 5 character tiles ✓ (112); 8-hour day 8AM–4PM ✓ (194); ~20 of 35 cards drawn ✓ (67-68); location durations HQ 1-2 / PD & Courthouse 2-3 / Lab up to 4 / Fieldwork 2+ ✓ (253-264); travel = 1 hour ✓ (342); 1 Stress per Overtime hour ✓ (322-324). All numeric claims supported; the failures are rule contradictions/omissions, not numbers.

## die-macher — MINOR

**Verdict: MINOR** — numbers are accurate across the board; a few real but small rule omissions in vote relocation, poll publishing, and the pay-money phase.

### Findings

1. **[MINOR — omitted constraint] Minimum 4 rallies to relocate votes in non-election states.**
   - Summary (9a) only says "In the election state, all rallies must be transformed."
   - Source: "Federal states not holding an election this round: If a player wants to transform party rallies into votes in such a state, they must transform **at least 4 party rallies**... If they are not able or willing to do so, they must pass" (lines 710-714). Also omitted: in the election state you need at least 1 rally to transform at all (720-721), and relocation order (reverse/counter-clockwise from the last-electing state, 705-709).

2. **[MINOR — omitted constraint] Poll publishing conditions.**
   - Summary (8): "Winner may publish it (adjust trends per card) or keep it secret (raise party base by 3)."
   - Source adds: publishing "may only be chosen, if the trend of the player's party is raised by the poll card" (728-730), and "The party of the player that influences the media in this federal state, is never lowered by a poll card!" (735-736). Auction mechanics (auctioneer = most votes, has final bid, 683-691) also compressed away.

3. **[MINOR — overbroad money rule] Pay Money.**

4. **[trivial] Backbencher/Vice-Chancellor double-marker action is "place **or remove** a double marker" (583-589, 1001); summary says only "place." Media-influence swap also can't introduce a duplicate topic and the display is never refilled (657-660); omitted.**
