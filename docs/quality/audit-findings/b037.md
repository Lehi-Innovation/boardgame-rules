## blue-gray — MAJOR

Source is the SPI Blue & Gray Standard Rules (Road to Richmond folio). Most of the summary is faithful, but several special rules are contradicted or invented.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Night Game-Turns contradicted.** Summary: "During night, combat is affected (reduced odds or modified CRT column) and movement may be restricted." Source 10.0/10.1: "There is no Combat Phase during a Night Game-Turn, and no combat is allowed... Artillery units may not bombard during Night Game-Turns." Night combat is omitted entirely, not modified. Movement restriction (cannot enter/exit enemy ZOC at night, 10.2) is real but the combat claim would have players fighting battles in a phase that does not exist.
2. **MAJOR — Attack Effectiveness recovery invented.** Summary: "Recovery occurs at the start of the owning player's turn for units outside enemy ZOC." Source 11.4: "The loss of Attack Effectiveness continues for a given unit until the end of any Night Game-Turn" (i.e., units are flipped face-up only at the end of a Night Game-Turn; if no night turn ensues, units stay ineffective until the end of the game, 11.0). Completely different recovery mechanism.
3. **MINOR — Bombardment CRT column invented.** Summary: "Bombardment uses its own column on the CRT." Source 8.0 PROCEDURE: "Determine the odds of the attack in exactly the same fashion as any other attack."
4. **MINOR — Voluntary odds reduction floor invented.** Summary: "may voluntarily reduce combat odds (but not below 1:6)." Source 7.9 states no 1:6 floor.
5. **MINOR — Victory conditions embellishment.** Summary's "comparing territorial control and/or casualties" is plausible but unsupported; source 4.0 only says Victory Conditions are in the Exclusive Rules and consulted after the final Game-Turn (hedged with "Generally," so low impact).
6. **MINOR — EX result simplified.** Player Reference says "Both sides lose equal strength"; source 7.6: all defenders eliminated, attacker eliminates units whose total printed strength *at least equals* the eliminated defenders'.

### Numbers checked (supported)

- 22" x 17" map ✓ (2.1); never more than 100 counters ✓ (1.0); Movement Allowance 6 MP ✓ (2.4/5.0); clear terrain 1 MP ✓ (5.21); road-to-road 1 MP regardless of terrain ✓ (5.22); trail-to-trail 2 MP forest/rough, 1 MP clear ✓ (5.23); hex = 400 meters ✓ (2.5); Strength Point = 250-350 men ✓ (2.5); stacking max 2 units/hex ✓ (5.32); retreat 1 hex ✓ (7.7); ford hexside +1 MP (not in summary, consistent with "other terrain costs more"); CRT results AE/AR/EX/DR/DE ✓ (7.6).

## blue-gray-four-american-civil-war-battles — MAJOR

The extracted source is NOT the game's rulebook: it is a Strategy & Tactics #100 (Nov/Dec 1984) magazine article, "Blue & Gray Variants — Expanded Rules for the Blue & Gray Game" by Steve Winter. It contains only optional variant rules (revised CRT, cavalry movement, leaders, Antietam/Chickamauga options) plus orders of battle. The bulk of the summary (Components, Setup, Turn Structure, Actions, base CRT, Scoring) describes the base game and has no basis in this source.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Core sections unsupported by source.** Components ("4 maps (22\" x 17\") each", "400 die-cut counters (100 per game)", die, charts), Setup, Turn Structure (Movement/Combat Phases, Game-Turn Record Interphase), Movement costs (6 MP allowance, clear 1, road 1, trail 2/1), maneuver/ZOC rules, retreat/advance rules, and victory conditions are nowhere in the extracted text. They appear to have been imported from the sibling Blue & Gray standard-rules summary. Whether or not they are true of the real game, they cannot be verified from this source, and the specific counts (4 maps, 400 counters) are pure invention relative to it.
2. **MAJOR — Diversionary attack odds invented.** Summary: "may make a diversionary (soak-off) attack at 1:6 odds." Not in the source at all (the article's CRT runs 1:5 to 6:1, with no 1:6 column shown), and the standard rules (7.5) specify no fixed odds.
3. **MAJOR — Night Game-Turns claim unsupported and wrong.** Summary: "night turns... Movement is reduced and combat results may be modified." Not in this source; the standard rules say night turns have NO combat phase and movement is restricted only re: enemy ZOC. Misleads at the table.
4. **Supported — Variant CRT.** "Two-dice CRT adds Attacker Shattered (AS) and Defender Shattered (DS) results, reducing units to half-strength permanently. Requires 3:2 odds for even chances of dislodging the defender" ✓ — source: "the new CRT requires at least 3:2 odds before the attacker has a roughly even chance to dislodge the defender"; "the unit's combat strength is reduced to one-half of its full strength (round fractions up)... Shattered units never recover"; "restructured for two-dice resolution."
5. **Supported — Cavalry variant.** "Cavalry units outside enemy ZOC for an entire turn have 9 movement points. If expending more than 6 points, they cannot enter enemy ZOC" ✓ — matches source verbatim in substance.
6. **Supported — Leader variant.** Command radii via command number ✓; out-of-command units "cannot move into an enemy zone of control" ✓; "If all of the units participating in an attack are stacked with a leader, the attacker receives a combat bonus" (raise or lower the dice roll by one) ✓. Summary omits that all-out-of-command attackers give the *defender* a bonus (minor).
7. **MINOR — Omissions of source content.** The article's "Little Mac" Antietam variant (only 15 Union units move turn 1, 10 thereafter; corps committed by random counter draw), the Longstreet/Chickamauga rule, leader elimination/replacement (die roll of 1, VP = command number), and shattered-retreat escalation (blocked DR becomes DS, DS becomes DE) are absent from the summary.

### Numbers checked

- 3:2 odds threshold ✓; two-dice CRT ✓; half strength, round up ✓; cavalry 9 MP / 6 MP threshold ✓; combat bonus +/-1 ✓; two-units-per-hex stacking (leaders exempt) ✓; "less than two hours" play ✓ (summary play_time 60-120 min consistent); 4 battles (Antietam, Cemetery Hill, Chickamauga, Shiloh) ✓. UNSUPPORTED: 4 maps 22"x17"; 400 counters; 1:6 diversionary odds; 6 MP base allowance (only inferable); victory point targets.

## bomb-busters — MAJOR

Components, setup, turn actions, equipment, communication, and mission progression are all accurate — but one Special Rules bullet invents a rule that reverses the game's central loss condition.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Red wire explosion rule contradicted (and internally inconsistent).** Summary Special Rules: "A red wire that is incorrectly pointed at (teammate says it's not the guessed value) does NOT explode — the bomb only explodes if the active player points at a red wire and the action would succeed (i.e., the teammate confirms it)." Source: "But if they are wrong, the action fails: If the wire in question is red, the bomb explodes, and the mission ends in failure." Pointing at a red wire during a Dual Cut always explodes the bomb (a red wire can never match a stated number, so the guess is always wrong and the bomb always blows). The bullet also contradicts the summary's own Turn Structure ("Incorrect guess (red wire): The bomb explodes immediately") and Player Reference. A team playing by this bullet would treat red wires as safe to probe — game-breaking.
2. **MINOR — Setup omission.** Summary doesn't state that you take "as many Equipment cards as there are bomb disposal experts" (source setup step 8); it just says "Place Equipment cards faceup over the checkmark."
3. **Note (not an error):** "66 missions" in Overview is supported indirectly ("You will need it for mission 66!").

## bommerz-over-da-sulphur-river — MAJOR

Components, setup, defence counters, damage, bombing, second wave: all accurate. The victory table is misassigned.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Victory results table wrong.** The source chart has six cells (rows: 1 wave / 2 waves; columns: bridges damaged / 1 bridge destroyed / 2 bridges destroyed) with exactly these six outcome texts: one "Ork Major Victory!", two "Ork Minor Victory", two "Imperial Minor victory", one "Imperial Major victory!". The summary assigns EIGHT outcomes, including TWO Ork Major cells ("1 destroyed" and "2 destroyed" on 1 wave) and an invented "no bridges damaged" row ("Imperial Major (1 wave) or Imperial Minor (2 waves)") — a tally that cannot match the source chart under any cell mapping. The flavor text fixes several cells the summary gets wrong: "Imperial Major victory! The Ork Bommer-leader is reduced to the status of Gretchin slave" pairs with the failed single-wave raid (1 wave / bridges damaged), where the summary awards "Ork Minor Victory"; "Ork Minor Victory: The Orks succeed in destroying the bridges, but they are too severely mauled..." pairs with a destroyed-bridges cell, not with mere damage. Net effect: an Ork player who merely damages bridges in one wave would claim a victory the source awards to the Imperial side as a major win.
2. **MINOR — Multi-player winner caveat omitted.** Source: most bridge hits decides the individual winner "(assuming the Orks gained a victory at all)"; summary drops the parenthetical.
3. **MINOR — Driven-off exception to backtracking omitted.** Source: a Fighta-Bommer "can't enter a space it has just left *unless it is Driven Off* by Flak or a Dogfight"; summary states the prohibition without the exception.
4. **MINOR — Solo driven-off direction omitted.** Source: in solo play, driven-off direction is "away from the bridges for Orks"; summary says only "Imperial player's choice."

## bonaparte-at-marengo — MAJOR

Components, setup, sequence of play, command limit, movement, and fog of war are accurate, but the summary invents a maneuver-combat mechanic, guts the assault system, and misstates the victory/demoralization rules.

### Findings

1. **MAJOR — Maneuver attack resolution invented.** Summary: "The attacker's strength is compared to the defender's blocking strength, modified by terrain penalties... If attack strength exceeds defense, the defender must retreat. If attack fails, the attacker must retreat back." Source section 9: there is NO strength comparison in a maneuver attack. Outcomes are: (1) Blocked — defender moves reserve pieces to block the approach; the attacker then stays in place, advances to block the opposite approach, or splits (movement ends; attacker never retreats); or (2) Successful — if the defender cannot/will not block, the defenders retreat. Strength is irrelevant.
2. **MAJOR — Assault system misstated/omitted.** Summary: "Total assault strength = all assaulting pieces' strength minus terrain penalties... Losses are distributed based on strength comparison." Source section 11: assault strength = strengths of the declared LEADING pieces only (max 1 narrow / 2 wide approach; strength >= 2; all-infantry or all-cavalry; strength must exceed terrain penalty), minus the approach terrain penalty; defense subtracts only the defender's leading pieces. Losses: winner loses exactly 1 SP; loser loses 1 + 1 per point of the differential, applied to leading pieces first. The summary omits leading pieces, the artillery-defense step, and cavalry pursuit entirely — the heart of the game's combat/bluffing system.
3. **MAJOR — Victory conditions wrong.** Summary: "Three color-coded star objectives... The Austrian player wins by controlling objectives... If an army's morale reaches zero, that army is demoralized and loses." Source section 14: there are EIGHT starred objective locales in three colors; territory is only a TIE-BREAKER used "if neither or both armies are demoralized"; the Austrian wins the tie-breaker by occupying at least 2 of the 8 locales in at least 2 of the 3 colors. Demoralization does not lose the game by itself (a demoralized army loses only if the other army is not also demoralized at game end); demoralized armies keep playing with penalties (cannot assault, -1 SP per piece on assault defense) and the other side gains +5 morale — all omitted.
4. **MAJOR — Retreat rules wrong.** Summary: "pieces move to an adjacent locale (or off-map if no retreat path)... Retreats through enemy zones may cause additional losses." Source section 12: if there is no legal retreat locale the pieces are ELIMINATED (never moved off-map; "Once on, pieces may not move off the map again," section 8). Actual retreat losses (all omitted): retreating artillery is eliminated; infantry retreating from reserve loses 1 SP; cavalry from reserve loses nothing; each blocked approach retreated from costs 1 SP. "Retreats through enemy zones" is invented (retreating into an enemy-occupied locale is simply prohibited).
5. **MINOR — Artillery movement restriction reversed.** Summary: "Artillery that bombards cannot move on the same turn it declares bombardment." Source section 10: "An artillery piece may move in the same turn in which it declares a bombardment, but it may not move in the same turn in which the bombardment is resolved."
6. **MINOR — Bombardment "succeeds/fails" framing.** There is no success check: the target automatically loses strength points equal to the (terrain-adjusted) bombardment strength, defender's choice of piece. Summary's "If bombardment succeeds, defender loses strength; if it fails, nothing happens" implies a resolution roll that doesn't exist (though no dice exist in the game, as the summary itself notes).
7. **MINOR — Bombardment position requirement omitted.** Artillery must be blocking an approach (not in reserve) to bombard; target priority (blocking pieces, then reserve, then other approaches) omitted.

### Numbers checked (supported)

1. 80 wooden pieces ✓; 2. 22" x 30" board ✓; 3. 3 markers ✓; 4. 2 rules booklets ✓; 5. 16 rounds, 1 hour each, June 14 1800 ✓; 6. start marker at 6:00 AM ✓; 7. activation: 1 piece first turn, then 2/turn once a set-up locale was ever enemy-occupied or from 11:00 AM ✓; 8. command limit 3 (groups + assaults combined), exemptions for blockers/artillery/retreats/primary roads ✓; 9. strengths inf 1-3 / cav 1-2 / art 1 ✓ (examples + "one, two or three"; each side has exactly one 1-strength artillery piece); 10. road move up to 3 locales, road capacity 3 pieces per approach ✓ (capacity detail omitted but consistent); 11. four reinforcement displays (1 Austrian, 3 French) ✓; 12. capacity is per-locale with no sub-limits ✓.
