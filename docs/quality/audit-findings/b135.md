# Semantic Audit — Batch b135

Games: mage-knight, mage-knight-dungeons
Audited: 2026-06-13

---

## mage-knight

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented turn phases (MAJOR)

Summary: "Clear Phase: Remove action tokens from figures that acted last turn. Action Phase: Give actions to your figures."

Source: No phases exist anywhere in the source text. The source describes a single continuous turn: "During your turn, you assign your actions to your warriors." (p. 4 / lines 266–270). The words "Clear Phase" and "Action Phase" have no support — no support found (searched: "phase", "clear phase", "action phase", "step.*turn").

### Finding 2 — Token removal rule reversed / wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "Clear Phase: Remove action tokens from figures that acted last turn."

Source (lines 295–298): "If you give an action (other than pass) to one of your warriors, mark him with an action token … At the end of your turn, remove all tokens from your figures **not given an action this turn**."

Tokens are removed at the **end** of a turn from figures that **did not** act — not at the start from figures that did act. Tokens persist on figures that did act so the pushing system tracks consecutive activations. The summary's description would make pushing tracking impossible.

### Finding 3 — Critical hit trigger wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "Critical Hits: Rolling doubles (except double 1s) may trigger critical hit effects."

Source (lines 386–392): "Whenever you give a ranged or close combat action to a warrior and roll a '2,' you automatically miss the target. This is called a critical miss … If you roll a '12,' then you have automatically hit the target. This is called a critical hit."

Critical hit = rolling 12 (not rolling doubles). Critical miss = rolling 2 (not double 1s). The summary's "doubles" mechanic does not exist in this ruleset. No support found (searched: "double", "doubles", "boxcars").

### Finding 4 — Victory conditions incomplete / wrong (MAJOR)

Summary: "Elimination: Destroy all enemy figures … The standard victory condition is having figures remaining when your opponent does not."

Source (lines 756–768): "At the end of the game, all players tally their victory points. Whoever scores the most victory points wins … Victory point total = (eliminated opposing figure points) + (captured opposing figure points x 2, but only if they are in your starting area) + (friendly non-captured figure points that have survived the entire game, but not if all your figures are captured and/or demoralized)."

The actual standard victory is **most Victory Points**, not simple elimination. The summary omits entirely: captive scoring (2x point value for captives in starting area) and surviving-friendly-figure scoring. These are substantial scoring sources. The game can also end by time limit or mutual agreement (line 754), neither mentioned.

Tiebreaker source (line 773): "the winner is the player who built his army with the fewest points." — no support found in summary.

### Finding 5 — Ranged combat restriction overstated (MAJOR)

Summary: "A figure adjacent to an enemy cannot make ranged attacks."

Source (lines 423–424): "If the range value is greater than 0 and your warrior is **not in base contact with an opposing figure**, then you may give your warrior a ranged combat action."

The restriction applies only to base contact (bases physically touching), not to "adjacent" (which in a miniatures game commonly means within some distance). "Base contact" is explicitly defined in the rules (line 290): "A figure is in base contact with another figure if their bases are touching." A figure near an enemy but not touching can still fire.

## mage-knight-dungeons

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Victory condition wrong: "collected" vs "carried out" (MAJOR)

Summary: "The player whose team has collected the most gold wins."

Source (lines 920–922): "Once all the Heroes have left the dungeon or been eliminated, the players add up their gold. The player with the highest total of gold **successfully carried out of the dungeon** wins."

Only gold carried out of the dungeon counts. A Hero eliminated before exiting loses their treasure (Advanced Rules lines 1146–1148 also confirm this). The summary's "collected the most gold" omits the critical requirement that the Hero must escape alive with it.

### Finding 2 — Knockback mechanic invented (MAJOR)

Summary: "Knockback: Some attacks cause knockback, pushing the target figure away from the attacker."

Source: No support found. Searched: "knockback", "knock back", "push.*away", "pushed.*away". No such mechanic exists anywhere in the extracted text.

### Finding 3 — Door cost wrong: "free action" vs speed points (MAJOR)

Summary: "Figures may open or close doors as a free action when adjacent."

Source (lines 1027–1028): "Doors cost **2 speed points to open**. Wooden doors can be closed for **1 speed point**."

Doors are not free. Opening costs 2 speed points; closing a wooden door costs 1 speed point. Iron doors cannot be closed by players (they "swing closed after each use," line 1022).

### Finding 4 — Turn structure wrong: phases vs activations (MAJOR)

Summary: "Players alternate turns. On each turn, a player may activate each of their figures once: 1. Move 2. Attack 3. Interact."

Source (lines 308–315): "**Each player receives 2 activations per turn.** A Hero, Mage Spawn, or wandering monster token can only be activated once per turn … Activate A Hero: An activated Hero may move through the dungeon, fight and open treasure chests. **The Hero's activation ends immediately after a combat** or according to the use of a special ability."

The summary misrepresents the activation system entirely: players get 2 activations (not "activate each figure once"), and an activation ends immediately after combat (a Hero cannot move, then attack, then also interact in the same activation — combat ends it). "Interact" is not a separate numbered step; opening chests is part of movement/activation but requires speed points (2 speed points per line 769).

### Finding 5 — Gold dropped on death: unsupported in basic rules (MINOR)

Summary: "Defeated Hero figures lose any gold they were carrying (it drops in the dungeon space)."

Source: The basic game ending section (lines 912–922) does not describe what happens to gold when a Hero is eliminated. The Advanced Rules (lines 1146–1155) describe treasure redistribution when a Hero dies in multi-Hero teams, but that is about returning extra chests to the dungeon — not gold "dropping in the dungeon space." No support found in basic rules (searched: "drop.*gold", "lose.*gold", "gold.*drop", "gold.*lost", "eliminated.*treasure").

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8, SUPPORTED: 4
