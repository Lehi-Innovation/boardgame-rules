## nyctophobia

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Invented Hunted win condition (wrong procedure)

Summary: "Hunted win: If any Hunted player reaches the car and then escapes off the board edge."

Source (lines 164–166): "Hunted Win – The car is found and the call for help action is taken and all players survive through the end of the current round and one additional round of play (the game ending when the player who found the car finishes their next turn)."

The summary invents "escapes off the board edge" — no such mechanic exists. The actual win requires a specific "Call for Help" action taken inside the car, followed by ALL players surviving one additional round. The summary would lead players to play out a completely wrong endgame sequence.

### Finding 2 — Hunter turn structure reversed/wrong

Summary:
"1. Move the Hunter piece up to 2 spaces.
2. Play 0-1 action card from hand.
3. Draw 1 action card."

Source (lines 257–260, turn summary lines 478–495):
"The Hunter player chooses one of the two cards in hand to resolve. After resolving, discard the used card and draw back up to two."
Turn summary: "1) Play 1 card from hand; 2) Resolve card; 3) Draw back up"

The summary inverts the Hunter turn: in the source, the card is played first and its text determines movement; there is no separate free movement before card play. The summary also says "Draw 1 action card" but the source says draw back up to 2. The movement is entirely card-driven, not a separate action.

### Procedural verification

## obsession

**Verdict: MINOR**

### Finding 1 — Final courtship mechanics omitted/conflated

Summary: "Courtship Round: Compare VP totals in the current theme category. Sole winner takes a Fairchild card. Each winner draws 1 VP card. Discard 1 objective card. Pass first-player pawn."

Source (lines 159–168):
- Regular courtships 1–3: "A sole winner adds their choice of Fairchild card... If there is a tie, nobody takes a Fairchild card."
- Final courtship: "In the final courtship, VP are added for tiles in each of the 4 theme categories. Repeat VP of repeated themes. A sole winner takes a Fairchild card (for its 8 VP). If tied, the tied player with the highest reputation takes a Fairchild."

The summary's single description conflates the two different courtship procedures. The final courtship (season 4) scores all 4 categories (not just the current theme) and has a different tiebreaker (reputation). A table playing the final courtship with the summary's description would use the wrong scoring method. However, a sophisticated player could infer the distinction from the season table. This is MINOR because the regular courtship is the one repeated 3 times and the summary is correct for those, but the final courtship error could affect the final scoring.

## odysseus

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Turn structure wrong: sequential, not simultaneous; cards played face-up, not face-down

Summary: "1. Play Wind Cards: Each player simultaneously plays Wind cards face down onto colored spaces on the board. 2. Reveal and Resolve: All played cards are revealed."

Source (lines 109–120): "The player to the left of Zeus always begins a placement round and play then continues clockwise. When it is his turn, a player must choose one of the four possible options: 1. Play a Wind card... The player chooses a Wind card from his hand and places it face-up on one of the nine colored spaces behind the three ships."

Card placement is sequential (clockwise from Zeus's left), not simultaneous. Cards are played face-up, not face-down. There is no "Reveal and Resolve" step. The summary's description of the core mechanic is wrong on both counts, which would make the game unplayable as described.

### Finding 2 — Ship movement is up to 3 steps in potentially different directions, not one move "in the winning direction"

Summary: "3. Move Ship: Move the ship in the winning direction."

Source (lines 77–102): "In each round, Odysseus' ship moves, at most, three spaces over the colored lines (red, green, yellow)... Three ships (1, 2, & 3) are depicted along the edge of the gameboard. The numbers correspond to each movement step. Movement direction (that is, which color line will be used) is determined by the colored spaces in the background of each ship."

There are 3 separate movement steps (one per "ship" area), each evaluated independently for color dominance. The ship can move up to 3 spaces and can change direction with each step. The summary collapses this to a single movement in one direction.

### Finding 3 — Divine powers entirely absent from summary

The source (lines 183–229) describes 6 unique divine powers (Zeus, Hades, Aphrodite, Hermes, Poseidon, Athena) that are core to the game. Players use them as a main action type each turn. The summary does not mention divine powers anywhere. They affect: ending rounds early, playing cards face-down, doubling wind at a space, moving played cards, blocking movement options, and countering other gods. Omitting them makes the game unplayable as described.

### Finding 4 — Wrong wind card dealing: 3 cards, not "a hand"

Summary: "4. Shuffle Wind cards and deal a hand to each player."

Source (line 59): "The 42 Wind cards are shuffled and three are dealt to each player."

The summary vaguely says "a hand" with no number; the source specifies exactly 3. Minor in isolation but symptomatic of shallow sourcing.

### Finding 5 — Player Reference states wrong core mechanic

Summary (Player Reference): "Core Mechanic: Simultaneous Wind card play"

Source: Sequential card placement. This consolidates the wrong core description as a reference claim.

### Victory condition

Summary: "The first player to score 10 or more points wins."

Source (line 70): "Whoever first earns 10 or more points wins the game." SUPPORTED.

### Tiebreaker

Summary: (no tiebreaker mentioned)

Source (lines 238–239): "If more than one player has reached this victory condition simultaneously, the player with the higher total wins. If there is still a tie, the player that scored the most points on the last completed adventure wins." MISSING from summary.

### Task card scoring

Summary: "Each Task card shows 3 different adventures worth varying points."

Source (line 69): "Each player tries to steer the ship to the adventure disks which match those on his Task card and thus earn the corresponding 1, 2, or 3 points." SUPPORTED (each adventure has value 1, 2, or 3).

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 4, SUPPORTED: 2 (victory threshold and task card point range supported; core mechanic wrong, tiebreaker missing)
