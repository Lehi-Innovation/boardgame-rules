## dungeonquest

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Sun Track advancement timing is wrong (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Sun Track: At the end of each round (after all players have taken a turn), advance the Sun Token one space."

**Source (line 863–864):** "When the First Player takes his turn, before he performs any actions, he advances the sun token one space on the sun track."

**Source (lines 271–273, First Player Token description):** "advance the sun token along the sun track at the start of each of that player's turns."

The source is unambiguous: the **First Player** advances the sun token **at the start of their own turn**, not at the end of the round. The summary's description ("end of each round, after all players have taken a turn") misidentifies both who advances the token and when. In practice this makes no difference in turn count but is factually wrong about the procedure and who is responsible.

### Finding 2 — Turn structure is wrong: Move and Search are mutually exclusive (MAJOR)

**Summary Turn Structure:** "1. Move: Move your Hero to an adjacent space... 2. Encounter: Draw a Dungeon card... 3. Search (optional): In many chambers, you may choose to search... 4. Sun Track: At the end of each round..."

The summary presents these as sub-steps of a single turn, implying a hero can both move and search in the same turn.

**Source (lines 396–399):** "When a Hero takes his turn, he must choose one of the following actions and resolve its consequences: Move into an adjacent space OR Search the chamber he is in"

**Source (lines 562–564):** "Once a Hero has moved or searched, the Hero's turn ends. Heroes cannot move and search in the same turn."

This is MAJOR: the entire turn structure is misrepresented. Each turn is an exclusive choice between moving or searching — not a sequence of steps. Players reading the summary would never understand that searching is an action that uses your entire turn.

### Finding 3 — Winning condition omits required Loot card to exit (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Escaping: Heroes must find their way back to any Tower Room before the sun sets."

**Source (lines 920–924):** "It is important to note that a Hero may not exit the dungeon unless he possesses at least one Loot card (no Hero would leave the dungeon empty-handed; otherwise, what was the point?). However, any Loot card will do; it does not even need to have a gold value to fulfill this requirement."

The summary says nothing about needing at least one Loot card to exit. This is a hard game rule — a hero with no loot cannot escape.

### Finding 4 — Tiebreakers entirely omitted (MINOR)

**Summary:** "The player with the surviving Hero holding the greatest total value in Loot wins." (No tiebreaker mentioned.)

**Source (lines 941–945):** "If two or more surviving Heroes are tied for the highest gold total, the tied player with the single Loot card worth the highest gold value wins the game. If it is still a tie, the tied player with the most Loot cards wins the game. If it is still a tie, then all tied players win the game."

Three-stage tiebreaker entirely omitted from summary.

### Finding 5 — Sun-ending described as deterministic rather than probabilistic (MINOR)

**Summary:** "Heroes must find their way back to any Tower Room before the sun sets (Sun Token reaches the final space)."

**Source (lines 867–874):** Once the sun token reaches numbered spaces, "the First Player must roll one die to see if the game ends. If the number rolled is higher than the number or range of numbers listed on the space, the Tower Rooms' doors remain open... If the number rolled matches the number or falls within the range of numbers listed on the space, the giant stone doors of every Tower Room close and the game immediately ends."

The game can end before the sun token reaches the final space — it's probabilistic once numbered spaces are reached.

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 12 (component counts matching source exactly), SUPPORTED: 11 (1 not supported — sun track timing wrong)

## dungeons-dragons-castle-ravenloft-board-game

**Verdict: MAJOR**

### Finding 1 — Level Up requires natural 20, not just 5 XP (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Spending 5 XP lets you level up (flip your hero card)."

**Source (lines 703–706):** "Whenever a Hero rolls a natural 20 when making an attack roll or a disable trap roll, that player can choose to spend 5 Experience Points to become 2nd level!"

The summary completely omits the requirement of rolling a natural 20. Players reading the summary would believe they can level up at any time by spending 5 XP, when in fact the natural 20 is the gating trigger. This would fundamentally change how players approach leveling.

### Finding 2 — Villain Phase encounter card trigger: black triangle case omitted (MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Villain Phase: If you did NOT explore a new tile, draw an encounter card (usually something bad)."

**Source (lines 418–421):** "If you didn't place a Dungeon Tile in your Exploration Phase, **or if you placed a Dungeon Tile with a black triangle**, draw and play an Encounter Card."

The summary omits the black triangle case. Even if you successfully explored, placing a black triangle tile still requires drawing an Encounter Card. Players would miss half the trigger conditions for this phase.

### Finding 3 — Healing Surges are a shared group resource, not per-hero (MAJOR)

**Summary Setup:** "Each hero starts with hit points as shown on their hero card and 2 healing surges."

**Source (lines 572–576):** "The Heroes begin the game with two Healing Surge tokens. These tokens are a resource that the entire group shares. You use Healing Surge tokens to revive a Hero that has been reduced to 0 Hit Points."

The summary says each hero has 2 healing surges, implying a per-hero resource. The source says the group has 2 Healing Surge tokens total as a shared pool. This is a critical distinction — with 4 heroes, the summary implies 8 surges total; the source gives 2.

### Finding 4 — Losing condition: timing is wrong (MINOR-to-MAJOR)

**Summary:** "Losing: If any hero is reduced to 0 HP and has no healing surges remaining, all players lose."

**Source (lines 559–561):** "A Hero that starts his or her turn at 0 Hit Points must spend a Healing Surge token. If there are no Healing Surge tokens remaining, the Heroes lose the adventure."

The summary implies the loss trigger fires immediately when a hero reaches 0 HP without surges. The source says it fires at the **start of that hero's turn**. A hero reduced to 0 HP mid-turn is not yet eliminated — they can be healed by other heroes before their next turn.

### Finding 5 — Component count: adventure book listed as "13 adventures" (unsupported)

**Summary:** "Adventure book with 13 adventures"

Source lists components but does not state the number of adventures in the extracted text. The table of contents mentions adventures but no count is stated.

No support found (searched: "13 adventures", "adventure", "13 scenarios").

NUMBERS_CHECKED: 8 (components, HP, surges, XP thresholds), SUPPORTED: 6

## dungeons-dragons-computer-labyrinth-game

Source (lines 489–493): "When any player succeeds in getting the treasure back to his or her Secret Room without being attacked by the dragon or robbed by the other warrior, that player wins." ✓

- **Dragon wake condition:** "within 3 squares (any direction) of the Treasure Room." Source (lines 281–282): "If either player is 3 squares away or closer, in any direction, the dragon wakes completely." ✓

- **Dragon movement:** "Moves 1 square/turn, moves diagonally and over walls, chases nearest warrior or treasure holder." Source (lines 286–292, 296–298): confirmed ✓

- **Attacked with treasure = immediate elimination.** Source (lines 557–558): "A warrior is out of the game if attacked by the dragon a third time or while carrying the treasure." ✓

- **Movement maximum progression:** 8→6→4→eliminated on 3rd attack. Source (lines 313–322): confirmed ✓

- **Warrior-vs-warrior combat sub-cases (4 cases):** verified against source lines 428–458. All four cases correctly described. The summary omits case 2 of the treasure-holder (has used all 4 moves), but the omitted case is an edge case that would not cause players to make a wrong move in the common flow. MINOR omission at most.

- **Secret Room safety:** "dragon cannot detect you in your own Secret Room. You are NOT safe in your opponent's Secret Room." Source (lines 538–544): confirmed ✓

- **Dragon targets weaker warrior when both on same square.** Source (lines 396–403): confirmed ✓

- **Doors (Advanced):** "all doors eventually open, but not always on the first attempt." Source (lines 486–488): confirmed ✓

- **Strength factor:** "Moving costs strength. Computer periodically restores warrior strength." Source (lines 383–389): confirmed ✓
