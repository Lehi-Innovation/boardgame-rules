## Overview

A streamlined 2-player version of Agricola focused on animal husbandry. Players are farmers raising sheep, pigs, cows, and horses over 8 rounds. Workers are placed on action spaces to collect resources, build fences and buildings, and acquire animals. Each action space can only be used once per round. The player with the most animals and most valuable buildings wins.

## Components

- 1 Game board (action spaces)
- 2 Farm boards (forest top, path bottom)
- 4 Farm expansions
- 4 Stall tiles (stables on reverse)
- 4 Special buildings (Half-Timbered House, Storage Building, Shelter, Open Stables)
- 9 Goods tiles
- 1 Start player tile
- 2x3 Workers (blue and red)
- 26 Borders (yellow fences)
- Animals: 22 Sheep, 16 Pigs, 13 Cows, 14 Horses
- Resources: 17 Wood, 15 Stone, 5 Reed
- 10 Feeding troughs (yellow)
- 1 Score pad

## Setup

1. Place game board in center. Each player takes a farm board.
2. Lay out the 4 special buildings.
3. Place farm expansions, feeding troughs, and stall tiles off to the side.
4. Sort building materials and animals into general supply.
5. Give each player 9 borders. Place remaining 8 borders near the "Expand" action space.
6. Each player starts with 3 workers.

## Turn Structure

The game lasts **8 rounds**. Each round has 4 phases:

1. **Refill Phase**: Replenish accumulating action spaces with resources/animals.
2. **Work Phase**: Place workers alternately (starting player first). Each player places 1 worker at a time on an unoccupied action space and takes the action. The work phase ends once all 6 workers have been placed.
3. **Home Phase**: All workers return to their farms.
4. **Breeding Phase**: If you have at least 2 animals of the same type, you receive 1 more of that type. You can gain at most 1 animal per type per breeding phase (max 1 sheep, 1 pig, 1 cow, and 1 horse). Newborn animals that cannot be housed are returned to the general supply.

## Actions

Workers are placed on action spaces to gain resources, animals, or build improvements.

### Resource Collection
- Collect wood, stone, reed from accumulating spaces
- Collect animals from accumulating spaces

### Building
- **Build Fences**: Use borders to create enclosed pastures on your farm
- **Build Stalls**: Place stall tiles (hold 3 animals each, or 6 with feeding trough)
- **Upgrade Stalls to Stables**: Flip stall tiles to hold 5 animals (or 10 with feeding trough)
- **Build Special Buildings**: Construct one of the 4 available special buildings
- **Expand Farm**: Take a farm expansion tile and borders

### Animal Capacity
| Location | Capacity | With Feeding Trough |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Pasture (fenced area) | 2 animals per space | 4 animals per space |
| Stall | 3 animals | 6 animals |
| Stable (upgraded stall) | 5 animals | 10 animals |
| Cottage (your house) | 1 animal | - |
| Unenclosed feeding trough | 1 animal | - |

- Each pasture holds animals of only **one type**.
- A fully enclosed pasture holds 2 animals per space, so a 2-space pasture holds 4 animals, a 3-space pasture holds 6, and so on. Feeding troughs double the capacity of the space they occupy.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

At game end, score:

| Category | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Each animal | 1 point |
| Bonus animals (above type thresholds) | 1 extra point each (after 13th sheep, 11th pig, 10th cow, or 9th horse) |
| Special buildings | Points as printed |
| Farm expansions (fully utilized, all 3 spaces used) | 4 points each |
| Half-Timbered House | 5 points |
| Each animal type with 3 or fewer animals | -3 points |

Highest score wins. In case of a tie, the player who was **not** the start player in round 1 wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Each animal type must be kept in separate pastures.
- The cottage holds exactly 1 animal (a house pet).
- The Half-Timbered House is built over the cottage (on the same space) and holds up to 2 animals of the same type.
- When a building is placed adjacent to existing borders, those borders do not provide additional benefit.
- You cannot return previously built borders to your supply.
- Animals received that cannot be housed must be returned immediately.
- Feeding troughs placed outside a pasture/building can hold exactly 1 animal.

## Player Reference

### Animal Housing Quick Reference
- Cottage: 1 animal
- Unenclosed feeding trough: 1 animal
- Pasture: 2 per space (4 per space with trough)
- Stall: 3 (6 with trough)
- Stable: 5 (10 with trough)

### Game Length
8 rounds, 3 workers per player = 24 worker placements total per player
