## Overview

Cleopatra and the Society of Architects is a game where players are architects competing to build Cleopatra's palace. Players collect resources from the market and use them to construct parts of the palace, earning Talents (VP). However, some actions involve corruption (using tainted cards or character cards). At game end, the most corrupt player is fed to Cleopatra's sacred crocodile. Among survivors, the richest architect wins.

## Components

- 3D palace model (built during play): 9 Column walls, 6 Sphinxes, 2 Door frames, 1 Throne & 1 Pedestal, 2 Obelisks, 12 Mosaics of the Gods
- A deck of 110 cards: 75 Standard Resource cards (Artisans, Stone Blocks, Marble Slabs, Wooden Logs, Lapis Lazuli) and 23 Tainted Resource cards (3 doubled-value copies of each standard resource, worth double, plus 8 Trompe-l'oeil), plus 11 Character cards (all Tainted): 2 Beggars, 2 Courtesans, 2 Envoys, 2 Scribes, 2 Smugglers, 1 Vizier
- 89 Corruption Amulets
- 5 Pyramids of Corruption (one per player, hides amulets)
- 108 Talents (32×1, 32×2, 24×5, 20×10)
- 15 Nile Merchants (3 per player)
- 10 Statues of Anubis (2 per player)
- 1 Cleopatra figure
- 5 Dice of the Great Priest; 1 Altar of the Great Priest
- Palace's Garden and Plaza of the Sphinx game boards

## Setup

1. Place the upside-down game box in the center. Lay the Palace's Garden on top and the Plaza of the Sphinx adjacent.
2. Place 5 Dice of the Great Priest (blank face up) near the Plaza; place the empty Altar of the Great Priest in front of the Plaza.
3. Place the Cleopatra figure at the start of the Plaza of the Sphinx (before step 1).
4. Shuffle the 110-card deck. Deal 3 cards face-down to each player. Cut the deck in two halves, flip one half face-up, shuffle both halves together. Draw the top 3 cards and lay them side-by-side to form the initial **Market** (maintaining each card's orientation).
5. Each player receives: 1 Summary Sheet, 1 Pyramid of Corruption, 2 Statues of Anubis (matching color), 3 Nile Merchants, and 5 Talents.
6. The player with the best Egyptian credentials (or youngest) goes first; play proceeds clockwise.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform **one and only one** of the following two actions:

### Option A: Visit the Market
Pick up **all cards** from one Market stall (column) of your choice and take them into your hand.

- If you end your turn with more than **10 cards** in hand, you must either:
  - (a) Discard any cards to keep only 10, and take **1 Corruption Amulet**, OR
  - (b) Keep ALL cards in excess of 10, taking **1 Corruption Amulet per excess card**.
- After taking your cards, replenish the Market stall by drawing the top 3 cards from the draw pile and placing them (one at a time, maintaining face orientation) in the stall.
- When the draw pile is depleted, reshuffle the discard pile using the same half-face-up mechanism.

### Option B: Visit the Quarry
Build one or more Palace pieces this turn.

For **each piece built:**
1. Play from your hand any combination of Resource cards and/or Nile Merchants that meets or exceeds the piece's construction requirements (see Building Elements below). Discard them to the Market discard pile. Nile Merchants played are removed from the game.
2. Take the piece from the Quarry and place it in its designated spot on the Palace grounds.
3. Collect Talents as shown on your Summary Sheet.

**Multi-piece bonus:** If you build 2 pieces in one turn, add +2 Talents. If you build 3 or more, add +5 Talents instead.

**Category completion:** Whenever the last piece of a category (Sphinxes, Obelisks, Throne & Pedestal, Door frames, Column walls, or Mosaics) is built, advance Cleopatra 1 step on the Plaza of the Sphinx.

**Dice roll:** After building, roll all Great Priest dice still showing blank. Move any die landing on the Great Priest symbol to the Altar. If all 5 dice now fill the Altar, trigger an **Offering to the Great Priest** (see below).

**Tainted cards:** Any tainted Resource or Character card played adds Corruption Amulets (as shown on the card) to your Pyramid of Corruption the moment it is played.

**Nile Merchants:** Can substitute for any single missing resource (including an Artisan). Once used, removed from game.

**Overpayment:** The resource count paid never needs to be exact; change is never given. Unused portions of a resource card are lost. Resources can be split across multiple pieces built in the same turn.

### Character Cards (usable any time during your turn)
Discard the card onto the Market discard pile, apply the effect, and add the stated Corruption Amulets to your Pyramid. Can be played during either a Market or Quarry visit, even if received during the current turn.

- **Beggars (cost: 2 amulets):** Each opponent must give you either 2 Talents or one Standard Resource card (non-tainted).
- **Courtesans (cost: 1 amulet):** Take any one card from the Market discard pile.
- **Envoys (cost: variable):** Ask any/all opponents for Standard Resource cards. For each card you accept, take 1 Corruption Amulet from that card's owner. Opponents are never obligated to offer; you are never obligated to accept.
- **Scribes (cost: 2 amulets or 0):** For 2 amulets: choose any Mosaic from the Quarry pile (not just the top one). Alternatively, for 0 amulets: change the face of one Great Priest die (move a blank die to the Altar, or return an Altar die to blank).
- **Smugglers (cost: 1 amulet):** Play when you have more than 10 cards in hand; keep ALL your cards for just 1 amulet total.
- **Vizier (cost: variable):** Draw the top 5 cards from the Market draw pile. Keep any you wish (1 amulet per card kept); discard the rest.

## Actions

### Building Elements

| Piece | Qty | Cost | Talents Earned |
|-------|-----|------|----------------|
| Sphinxes | 6 | 1 Artisan | 2 Talents (1st of each pair); 5 Talents (2nd Sphinx placed directly across from the first in the pair) |
| Column Walls | 9 | 1 Artisan + 1 Wood | 3 Talents + 1 Talent per adjacent Mosaic square |
| Door Frames | 2 | 2 Artisans + 1 Wood + 1 Lapis Lazuli | 4 Talents + 1 Talent per Column Wall directly connected to that Door frame's corner |
| Obelisks | 2 | 3 Artisans + 2 Woods + 2 Stones | 12 Talents each |
| Throne + Pedestal | 2 | 3 Artisans + 2 Marbles + 2 Lapis Lazuli | 12 Talents each |
| Mosaics of the Gods | 12 | 2 Artisans + 1 Lapis Lazuli | 4 Talents + 1 Talent per palm square covered; right to form Sanctuary |

*Mosaics must be taken from the top of the pile (unless using a Scribe). After each Mosaic is built, check if the next Mosaic can still fit; if not, remove it and check the next, until a fitting piece is on top or the pile is empty.*

### Offerings to the Great Priest
Triggered when all 5 dice display the Great Priest symbol on the Altar.

All players secretly choose Talents to bid, then reveal simultaneously:
- **Highest bidder:** Removes 3 Corruption Amulets from their Pyramid (or as many as they have).
- **2nd place:** Adds 1 amulet.
- **3rd place:** Adds 2 amulets.
- **4th place:** Adds 3 amulets.
- **5th place:** Adds 4 amulets.

Ties are broken by rank order (two tied for 1st each remove 3 amulets; the next player is 3rd, etc.). **All Talents bid are lost** regardless of rank, returned to the supply. The 5 dice are then returned to blank face-up position. Multiple offerings may occur during a game.

### Sanctuaries (Mosaics)
If a player builds a Mosaic such that a new, closed garden area is formed in which no further Mosaic can be placed, a **Sanctuary** is created. The player who created it may (optionally) place one of their Statues of Anubis in it to claim ownership. Unclaimed Sanctuaries cannot be claimed later.

At game end, a player who owns a Sanctuary reduces their Corruption Amulet count by 1 for each grid square of the Sanctuary (including the square with their Statue).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when Cleopatra reaches step 5 — i.e., when **5 out of 6 categories** of Palace pieces have been fully built.

At game end:
1. Players discard all cards still in hand, taking 1 Corruption Amulet for each tainted card discarded.
2. Each player places 1 Corruption Amulet on each Sanctuary grid square they own (reducing their amulets held). Then players lift their Pyramids to reveal their total amulets.
3. The player(s) with the **most Corruption Amulets** are eliminated (fed to the crocodile). If no player has any corruption, no one is eliminated.
4. Surviving players count their Talents. Each remaining Nile Merchant is worth **3 Talents**.
5. The wealthiest surviving architect wins. **Ties broken by fewest Corruption Amulets remaining.**

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Corruption Amulets are hidden inside players' Pyramids; you never know exactly how corrupt opponents are.
- Tainted resource cards provide extra resources (worth double) but add corruption when played.
- A card must always be at least partially visible in each stall if it is face-up; the Market stall replenishment maintains card orientations.
- You can never look at opponents' Talents (kept face-down in a pile).
- Nile Merchants remaining in hand at game end are worth 3 Talents each (added to score at game end).

## Player Reference

| Key Tension | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Market stalls | Take all cards from one stall per turn; over 10 cards = corruption |
| Tainted cards | Powerful but add corruption when played; also cost 1 amulet if discarded at game end |
| Offering | Talent auction triggered when all 5 Great Priest dice fill the Altar; winner removes amulets, others gain them |
| Sanctuary | Build a closed Mosaic area; each grid square owned removes 1 amulet at game end |
| Elimination | Player(s) with most Corruption Amulets at game end are eliminated |
| Victory | Most Talents among surviving players; fewest amulets breaks ties |
