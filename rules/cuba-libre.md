## Overview

Cuba Libre is a 1-4 player COIN (Counter-Insurgency) series game depicting the Cuban insurgency of 1957-1958. Each player controls one of four factions: the Government (Batista regime), the 26th of July Movement (Castro's leftists), the Revolutionary Directorate (anti-communist students), or the Syndicate (organized crime). Players use military, political, and economic actions to influence the population, extract resources, and achieve their faction's victory conditions. A card-driven system regulates turn order, events, and victory checks. Non-player rules enable solitaire, 2-player, and 3-player games.

## Components

- 1 mounted game board (17" x 22") showing Cuba divided into Provinces, Cities, and Economic Centers
- 52-card deck (Event cards and Propaganda cards)
- 86 wooden force pieces (Government Troops/Police, Insurgent Guerrillas, Bases/Casinos)
- 8 embossed cylinders
- 6 white and 6 black pawns
- Sheet of markers (Support/Opposition, Control, Terror, Sabotage, Cash, Capabilities)
- 3 six-sided dice (red, yellow, green)
- Sequence of Play sheets, Faction player aid foldouts, Non-player aid foldout

## Setup

1. Place the game board. Set up forces per the setup chart: Government Troops and Police in cities and provinces, Insurgent Guerrillas and Bases per faction starting positions.
2. Place Support, Opposition, and Control markers per the setup chart.
3. Set faction Resources on the edge tracks.
4. Shuffle the deck. Propaganda cards are pre-mixed into the deck at set intervals.
5. Reveal the top card of the deck for all to see (the "upcoming" card).

## Turn Structure

Cards are played one at a time from the deck. Each Event card shows:
- The order in which factions become Eligible (printed on the card).
- The Event text (which any eligible faction may choose to execute).

On each card:
1. The 1st and 2nd Eligible factions (in the order printed on the card) decide: execute the Event, perform an Operation (+ optional Special Activity), or pass.
2. Executing an Event or Operation makes that faction Ineligible for the next card. Passing keeps the faction Eligible.
3. At most 2 factions act per card. The remaining factions are automatically Eligible next card.

**Propaganda Rounds:** When a Propaganda card is drawn, all factions execute a special sequence: Victory Check, Resources, Support/Opposition shifts, Redeploy, and other maintenance.

## Actions

**Government Operations** (Train, Garrison, Sweep, Assault):
- **Train:** Place up to 4 cubes (Troops/Police) in each selected Province with a Government Base and in each selected City. Optionally, in 1 selected space replace 2 cubes with 1 Government Base, or conduct Civic Action (shift toward Support if the Government also has Troops, Police, and Control there).
- **Garrison:** Move any Troops or Police to any ECs or Cities; Activate 1 Guerrilla for each cube at each EC; optionally Assault in 1 EC at no added cost. Costs Resources total (not per space).
- **Sweep:** Move adjacent Troops into selected Cities or Provinces, then Activate Guerrillas there (1 per cube in non-Forest; 1 per 2 cubes in Forest).
- **Assault:** Remove 1 Active Guerrilla per Troops cube in each selected space; also 1 enemy piece per Police in a City or EC; in Mountain, only 1 piece per 2 Troops. Bases are removed only after all the targeted Faction's Guerrillas are gone.

**Government Special Activities** (Transport, Air Strike, Reprisal):
- **Transport:** Move up to 3 Troops from a City or Base to any 1 other space.
- **Air Strike:** Remove 1 Active Guerrilla (or 1 Base if the targeted Faction has no Guerrillas there) in a Province or EC. May only accompany Garrison, Sweep, or Assault. Not available if US Alliance is Embargoed.
- **Reprisal:** Place a Terror marker in 1 Govt-Controlled space; shift Opposition 1 level toward Neutral; relocate 1 Guerrilla from there to an adjacent space. May only accompany Garrison, Sweep, or Assault.

**26th of July Movement Operations** (Rally, March, Attack, Terror):
- **Rally:** Place 1 Available Guerrilla in each selected space; may instead replace 2 Guerrillas with 1 Base. If a 26July Base is already present, may Flip all own Guerrillas Underground or place up to twice the number of Bases plus twice the space's Population in Guerrillas. 26July may only Rally in Neutral or Opposition spaces.
- **March:** Move any Guerrillas into adjacent spaces. Pay 1 Resource per destination City or Province. Guerrillas moving to a space with Support or an EC flip to Active if the combined total of movers and cubes there exceeds 3.
- **Attack:** Select spaces where 26July has at least 1 Guerrilla and 1 enemy piece. Activate all own Guerrillas and roll; on a result ≤ number of own Guerrillas, remove or close up to 2 enemy pieces. On a roll of "1," place 1 available own Guerrilla.
- **Terror:** Select spaces with at least 1 Underground Guerrilla. Activate 1 friendly Underground Guerrilla; place a Terror marker and shift Support/Opposition 1 level toward Neutral (or 26July may shift toward Active Opposition); in ECs, place a Sabotage marker instead.

**26July Special Activities** (Infiltrate, Ambush, Kidnap):
- **Infiltrate:** Replace 1 cube (Police if any, else Troops) with a 26July Guerrilla in 1 space without Support that has or is adjacent to an Underground 26July Guerrilla. Accompanies Rally or March only.
- **Ambush:** In 1 space selected for Attack with at least 1 Underground 26July Guerrilla, automatically remove 2 enemy pieces without rolling; also place 1 Available Guerrilla as if a "1" had been rolled.
- **Kidnap:** In 1 space selected for Terror that is a City, EC, or has an open Casino, with more 26July Guerrillas than Police: roll a die and transfer that many Resources from the targeted Faction (Syndicate in spaces with open Casinos, Government in Cities/ECs) to 26July; also close 1 open Casino in the space.

**Directorate Operations** (Rally, March, Attack, Terror) with Special Activities (Subvert, Ambush, Assassinate):
- Operations are identical to 26July with minor differences: Directorate may Rally only in Neutral or Passive spaces (not Active Support or Active Opposition); Rally places up to Bases + Population in Guerrillas (not twice).
- **Subvert:** Add the Province's Population to Directorate Resources and set the space to Neutral. Accompanies Rally or March; occurs in 1 Province with Directorate Control only.
- **Ambush:** Same as 26July Ambush using Directorate forces.
- **Assassinate:** In 1 space selected for Directorate Terror where Directorate Guerrillas outnumber Police, remove or close any 1 enemy piece.

**Syndicate Operations** (Rally, March, Terror, Construct):
- **Rally:** Place 1 Available Guerrilla; if an open Casino is present, may Flip all own Guerrillas Underground instead.
- **March:** Move Guerrillas; same procedure as other Insurgents.
- **Terror:** Same as other Insurgents: place Sabotage markers on ECs; place Terror markers and shift Support/Opposition in Provinces/Cities.
- **Construct:** Build new Casinos or open closed ones in Cities or Provinces that the Government or Syndicate Controls. Costs 5 Resources per space. Never free.

**Syndicate Special Activities** (Profit, Muscle, Bribe):
- **Profit:** Place 1 Cash marker under any Guerrilla or cube in each of 1–2 spaces with an open Casino; or close any Casinos and remove any Syndicate Cash for +3 Resources each. Accompanies Rally, March, or Construct.
- **Muscle:** Relocate 1–2 Police from any spaces to 1 City with an open Casino, or 1–2 Troops to 1 Province or EC with an open Casino or an EC. Accompanies Rally, March, or Construct.
- **Bribe:** Spend 3 Resources; remove up to 2 cubes in a space, or remove/flip up to 2 Guerrillas, or remove 1 enemy Base there. May occur in any 1 space.

**Forces:**
- **Government:** Troops (dark blue cubes) and Police (light blue cubes). Troops are strong in combat; Police enable civic actions.
- **Guerrillas:** Can be Underground (hidden) or Active (revealed). Set up and placed Underground. Certain actions flip them Active.
- **Bases:** Represent camps, administration, or casinos. At most 2 non-Casino Bases + 2 Casinos per space.
- **Casinos (Syndicate Bases):** Open or Closed. Cannot be removed by enemy actions (only closed, not destroyed). The Syndicate may voluntarily remove its own Casinos.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Each faction has a unique victory condition checked at the start of each Propaganda Round:
- **Government:** All Cities are at Active Support AND Total Support exceeds 18.
- **26th of July Movement:** Total Opposition plus the number of 26July Bases on the map exceeds 15.
- **Directorate:** Total Population in spaces Controlled by Directorate plus the number of Directorate Bases exceeds 9.
- **Syndicate:** Syndicate has more than 7 open Casinos AND Resources above 30.

All conditions for a given player's faction(s) must be met simultaneously at a Victory Check for that player to win.

If no faction wins during any Propaganda Round, play continues. After the final (4th) Propaganda card's Victory Check fails, the game ends and the faction with the highest **victory margin** wins:
- **Government:** Total Support − 18
- **26July:** Total Opposition + 26July Bases − 15
- **Directorate:** DR-Controlled Population + Directorate Bases − 9
- **Syndicate:** Lower of (open Casinos − 7) or (Syndicate Resources − 30)

Ties go to Non-players, then Syndicate, then Directorate, then 26July.

**Key Terms:**
- **Total Support:** (2 × Population in Active Support) + (1 × Population in Passive Support).
- **Total Opposition:** (2 × Population in Active Opposition) + (1 × Population in Passive Opposition).
- **Opposition + Bases:** Total Opposition + the number of 26July Bases on the map.
- **DR Pop + Bases:** Population of all spaces Controlled by Directorate + the number of Directorate Bases.
- **Control:** A faction Controls a Province or City if its pieces there exceed those of all other factions combined. Open Casinos count; Closed Casinos do not.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Eligibility:** A faction that acts on a card becomes Ineligible for the next card. Passing preserves Eligibility.
- **Underground/Active Guerrillas:** Underground Guerrillas cannot be Activated by Assault; Sweep (or Garrison at ECs) is used to Activate (locate) them first. Only Active Guerrillas can be removed by Assault. Assault cannot remove a targeted Faction's Base until all that Faction's Guerrillas in the space are gone.
- **Stacking:** Max 2 non-Casino Bases + 2 Casinos per Province or City. Bases and Casinos cannot occupy Economic Centers.
- **Casinos:** Cannot be removed by enemy actions; they are only "closed" (flipped). The Syndicate can voluntarily remove them.
- **Cash Markers:** Special markers that stack with specific pieces on the map.
- **Non-Player Factions:** Detailed automated decision flowcharts allow 1, 2, or 3 human players by running non-player factions through prescribed priorities.
- **Propaganda Rounds:** Include: Victory Check, Faction Resource collection, Adjust Support/Opposition, Government Redeployment, and other upkeep.
- **Events:** Each card has a unique event that can benefit one or more factions, often with historically themed effects.
