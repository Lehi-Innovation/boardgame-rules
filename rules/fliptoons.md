## Overview

FlipToons is a fast, small-box deck-building card game where players are talent agents casting an animated show. Each round every player simultaneously shuffles their deck and flips cards into a 3-by-2 grid to "audition" a cast of six toons. The toons' abilities interact and generate **fame**, which is spent in a market to hire new toon cards or dismiss unwanted ones from your grid. As soon as any player's flip generates **30 fame**, all players do one **Final Flip** — the player who generates the most fame on that last flip wins.

## Components

- 24 Starting cards (gray toons, rank 0)
- 53 Toon cards (the market deck)
- 5 Price cards
- 4 Reference cards
- 4 Fame cards
- 1 Critic's Choice card
- 1 First Player card

**Toon card anatomy:** each card shows a **Name**, a **Rank** (used to order the market), a **Fame** value, a **Card Count** (how many copies are in the deck), an optional **Special Effect**, and an optional **Ability**.

## Setup

1. Give each player one reference card and one fame card.
2. Give each player a starting deck of six cards: **2 caterpillars, 1 skunk, 1 dragonfly, 1 bee, and 1 snail**. All starting cards are gray and have a rank of 0. Leave space for dismissed cards. Return unused starting cards, reference cards, and fame cards to the box.
3. Place the five price cards face up in the center of the table in ascending order (left to right).
4. Shuffle the toon cards face down to form the toon deck.
5. Reveal the top five toon cards to form the market. Sort them by rank, placing the lowest-rank card under the leftmost (cheapest) price card and the highest-rank card under the rightmost price card.
6. Place the critic's choice card face up near the toon deck.
7. Give the First Player card to whoever most recently watched a cartoon.

## Turn Structure

FlipToons is played over a series of rounds. Each round has four phases: **Flip → Check Fame → Market → Cleanup**.

### 1. Flip (simultaneous)
Each player shuffles their deck face down, then reveals cards one at a time, placing them into a **3-by-2 grid** — left to right, top row first, then bottom row — until all six slots are filled. Remaining cards stay face down in the deck.
- Some toons have abilities that activate **when placed**, before the next card is revealed.
- If a player lacks enough cards to fill the grid, they place as many as possible.
- Some abilities change how many cards end up in the grid.
- Unless stated otherwise, effects and abilities only affect that player's own grid.
- If multiple abilities resolve at the same time, resolve them **left to right, top row first**.

### 2. Check Fame (simultaneous)
Each player totals the fame generated by the toons in their grid and marks it by overlaying their reference card on their fame card so the arrow points to the total. Once calculated, the amount doesn't change until spent in the Market phase.
- If any player generated **at least 30 fame**, the player who generated the most takes the **critic's choice card** (worth +3 fame during the Final Flip) and the end of the game is triggered. If multiple players tied at 30+ for the most, return the critic's choice card to the box instead. Either way, proceed to the Final Flip (after Cleanup).

### 3. Market
Starting with the first player and going clockwise, each player spends the fame they earned in Check Fame to perform **up to two actions**. You may do both action types once, one action twice, or skip either/both. See **Actions** below.
- **Refill the market:** after a player finishes their actions, if fewer than five cards remain in the market, reveal cards from the toon deck until there are five, then re-sort the market by rank (lowest left, highest right).
- Fame is not recalculated during this phase, and **unspent fame does not carry over** to the next round.
- If the **toon deck is depleted**, stop adding cards; arrange remaining market cards under the highest price cards by rank. If the **market is also depleted**, finish the round and proceed to the Final Flip.

### 4. Cleanup
All players collect the cards from their grid back into their deck.
- **2-player games:** after the last player refills the market, discard the leftmost and rightmost market cards, then refill and re-sort by rank.
- If no player reached 30 fame, pass the First Player card clockwise and begin a new round.
- If a player reached 30 fame during Check Fame, the end of the game is triggered — proceed to the **Final Flip**.

## Actions

During the Market phase, each of your (up to two) actions is one of:

- **Hire a Toon:** Take one toon card from the market, paying fame equal to the cost on the **price card above it**. Add the hired card to your deck of unrevealed cards.
- **Dismiss a Toon:** Dismiss one face-up card in **your own** grid for **5 fame** (some cards cost less). A dismissed card is permanently removed — it is never shuffled back into your deck. Place it face up next to your deck.
  - You cannot dismiss cards that are in your deck or face down in your grid.
  - You may only dismiss cards in your own grid.
  - You may not dismiss a toon if you have fewer than four cards total in your deck and grid.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Final Flip (end of game)
Once the end is triggered, all players complete one last Flip phase and check their fame. A player holding the **critic's choice card adds +3** to their total. **The player who generates the most fame on the Final Flip wins.**

**Tiebreaker:** tied players collect all their cards, reshuffle, Flip, and Check Fame again; the tied player with the most fame wins. Repeat until a winner is determined.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Keywords
- **Adjacent:** cards that share a side (left, right, top, bottom) in a grid. Diagonal cards are **not** adjacent.
- **Dismiss:** remove a card from a grid and place it face up in that player's area. Any player may examine any player's dismissed cards at any time.
- **Flip:** turn a card over in a grid. A face-down card has no name, rank, special effects, or abilities and generates no fame. A face-down card can be flipped face up again by another ability, but doing so does **not** re-activate its abilities.
- **Stack:** place a card on top of another card in the grid so all cards' effects/abilities remain visible. New stacked cards go on top. Stacked cards share one grid slot, and all face-up cards in a stack are active (names, fame, ranks, effects, abilities).
- **Stack Adjacency:** cards **within** a stack are not adjacent to each other, but all cards in a stack are adjacent to cards in neighboring grid spaces.

### Card-specific rulings (FAQ, by rank)
- **Skunk (0):** Only one player benefits per round; ties = no effect. Can dismiss itself. The ability is mandatory and costs no action; benefiting from it still leaves you your two Market actions.
- **Ostrich (1):** If placed as the final grid card, don't reveal another. The next revealed card is stacked before its ability resolves; if that card then moves after stacking, it doesn't stack on the ostrich after all.
- **Eagle (2):** If placed as the final grid card, don't reveal another. Ignore any special effect/ability of the card it flips face down.
- **Donkey (3):** If placed in the upper row, it is not dismissed.
- **Butterfly (4):** May only dismiss a face-up caterpillar in your grid.
- **Dog (5):** Its fame is not affected by market changes during the Market phase.
- **Goat (6):** Its ability still activates if a monkey created a new row above the upper row.
- **Camel (8):** On a tie for most camels across players' grids and/or the market, all tied camels generate four fame.
- **Horse (10):** When hired, immediately discard any number of market cards, reveal an equal number of new ones, and re-sort by rank; place discards face up next to the toon deck.
- **Snake (11):** If the top deck card can't be dismissed, place it to the right of the snake (or return it to the deck if the snake is in the final grid space). "When Hired" abilities triggered this way resolve after the Flip phase; a stacked peacock adds fame and grants an extra Market action; a stacked rabbit/turkey stacks on the snake. If the deck is empty when the snake is revealed, still stack a card even though none is dismissed.
- **Elephant (12):** If it's the first card placed, ignore its ability. It flips the last placed card even if that card has moved.
- **Alligator (15):** Its target generates fame in Check Fame (spendable in Market) even though the target is dismissed at end of Market. If the target is a stack, dismiss any one card in the stack. If the alligator is dismissed during the market phase, it no longer dismisses a card.
- **Monkey (17):** When placed in the upper row, move the card above the upper row to create an additional row, and place the next revealed card in the monkey's original space. This extra row is part of the grid but does not fill one of the six slots and is not the "upper row."
- **Pig (18):** Can be placed in any player's deck or back in the toon deck (shuffle the toon deck if placed there).
- **Turkey (20):** If it's the first card placed, ignore its ability. It stacks on the last placed card even if that card has moved.
- **Bear (24):** Counts all face-up stacked cards as well as itself.
- **Cow (25):** If a stack is adjacent to a cow, you may copy any one card's fame in the stack.

### Solo Play
Goal: generate **30 fame in a single Check Fame phase** before the toon deck runs out.
- **Solo starting deck:** 1 dragonfly, 1 bee, 1 snail, and **3 caterpillars** (instead of 2). The **skunk is not used** in solo.
- Return the **pig** to the box. The First Player card and critic's choice card are unused.
- After setup, discard cards from the toon deck to a face-up discard pile — this sets difficulty:
  - **Slapstick Starter (Easy):** 17 cards
  - **Wacky Workload (Normal):** 20 cards
  - **Maniacal Mayhem (Hard):** 23 cards
- Play the four phases as normal. After refilling the market at the end of the Market phase, discard the leftmost and rightmost market cards, then refill and re-sort by rank.
- If there aren't enough cards to refill the market, the game is over and you lose.
- If you generate at least 30 fame at the end of a Check Fame phase, you win.

## Player Reference

**Round flow:** Flip (fill 3×2 grid) → Check Fame (total your grid) → Market (up to 2 actions) → Cleanup (grid back to deck).

**Market actions (up to 2 per turn):**
- Hire a toon — pay the price-card cost above it; goes into your deck.
- Dismiss a toon — pay 5 fame (or less); face-up grid card only, permanently removed. Not allowed below 4 total cards.

**Ability resolution order:** left to right, top row before bottom row.

**End trigger:** any player generates 30+ fame in Check Fame → finish Cleanup → everyone does the Final Flip. Critic's choice card holder gets +3. Most fame on the Final Flip wins.

**Fame reminders:** fame is locked once Check Fame is done; unspent fame does not carry over between rounds.
