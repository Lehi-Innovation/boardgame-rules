## Overview

Archipelago is a semi-cooperative game of exploration and colonization set during the Age of Discovery. Players explore a modular archipelago, gather resources, build settlements, and manage native populations. Each player has a secret objective that determines victory, but all players share the risk of colony-wide rebellion. If the rebellion marker moves past the population marker, the game ends and the Separatist player (if present) wins alone, while everyone else loses. The game blends Euro-style resource management with negotiation, hidden objectives, and variable game length.

## Components

- Modular hex tiles for the archipelago
- Exploration tokens
- Player boards
- Citizen meeples (player-colored)
- Resource cubes: Exotic fruit, Wood, Cattle, Stone, Fish, and Iron
- Ships
- Market, port, town, and temple tokens
- Cards: trend cards, character cards, progress cards, evolution cards, objective cards
- Action wheel and action discs
- Colony stability board (tracking population and rebellion markers)
- Surplus workers board
- Domestic market board and export market board
- Rulebook

## Setup

1. Place the four boards (domestic market, export market, colony stability, surplus workers) at the end of the table. Set up each board as directed.
2. Each player takes a player screen aid, 1 ship, 2 citizens, 3 action discs, and a turn order marker, plus 10 florins placed behind their screen.
3. Shuffle and deal secret objective cards (one per player); keep these hidden at all times.
4. Prepare the evolution deck (48 cards), place 5 cards face-up on the evolution track, and shuffle the trend cards, drawing one to remain visible throughout the game.
5. Set the rebellion and population markers to their starting positions (both at zero).
6. Determine first player randomly.

## Turn Structure

Each turn is divided into **6 phases** played in this order:

1. **Disengagement:** Disengage (rotate 90° counterclockwise) all engaged character and progress cards; move citizens and ships off resource icons; stand rebel citizens up. (Skipped on turn 1.)
2. **Order of Play:** Players secretly bid florins; highest bidder arranges turn order for all players. Tied bids are re-bid.
3. **Population Effects:** Apply effects from each of the four boards in order—domestic market, export market, colony stability, and surplus workers—adjusting the surplus workers marker and rebellion marker accordingly.
4. **Balance of the Archipelago:** Resolve the crisis or event shown on the back of the top card of the evolution deck (domestic consumption crisis, export consumption crisis, or event). (Skipped on turn 1.)
5. **Actions:** Starting with the first player, each player takes turns placing one **Action Disc on the Action Wheel** to perform the corresponding action. Players continue in rounds until all action discs are used or no actions can be taken.
6. **Evolution Card Purchase:** Starting with the first player, each player either purchases one evolution card from the five on the evolution track (paying its current cost) and rotates one remaining card, or rotates two different cards. Empty slots are refilled from the evolution deck; if a newly revealed card's back shows a red zone, that crisis or event is resolved immediately.

## Actions

On your turn, place an Action Disc on the Action Wheel to perform one of the 13 available actions:

- **Explore:** Draw a hex tile and place a new region adjacent to at least two existing regions. Gain an explorer token and add resources to the domestic market.
- **Recruit Workers:** Pay the current recruitment cost to bring surplus workers into the colony as citizens.
- **Harvest Resources:** Deploy active, non-engaged units onto matching resource icons (citizens for land resources, ships for fish) to collect resource cubes from the bank.
- **Build:** Construct a port, town, market, or temple in a region where you have an active non-engaged citizen (or a ship, for a port). You may also build a ship. There are no fortresses.
- **Reproduce:** In each region where you control exactly 2 citizens, add 1 new citizen; increase the population marker accordingly.
- **Transaction (Buy/Sell at Market):** Purchase or sell one resource cube on the domestic or export market. Price is determined by the row from which the cube is taken or placed.
- **Migration:** Move all of your active, non-engaged units—citizens by land, ships by sea—each to an adjacent region or an unoccupied building on the same or adjacent region.
- **Use Evolution Cards:** Pay the activation cost, engage the card (rotate 90° clockwise), and apply its effects. Character cards and progress cards may be used once per turn.
- **Taxes:** Collect florins from the bank for each active citizen, ship, town, and temple you control; the rebellion marker moves up 1 each time this action is taken.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Game End Triggers (variable by objective card):**
- A player reveals their objective card as soon as its end-of-game condition is met (e.g., a certain number of buildings in play, resources running out at the bank, explorer token piles exhausted, population reaching a certain level, etc.).
- The game ends at the end of the active player's round when an end-of-game condition is revealed.
- The game ends immediately if the rebellion marker moves past the population marker (war of independence).

**Scoring:** At the end of the game, **all players are ranked against every objective card in play** (not just their own). For each card, the highest-ranking player scores 3 VP, second scores 2 VP, and third scores 1 VP. Players are also scored against the trend card in play (earning 2–4 VP). Certain character cards (e.g., King, Pope) and all built wonders also award VP.

**Tiebreaker for grand winner:** If players are tied on total VP, the tied player with the most florins behind their screen is declared the grand winner.

**Rebellion Loss:** If the rebellion marker moves **past** (not merely to the same level as) the population marker, the war of independence is declared and the game ends immediately. If a player holds the Separatist objective card, that player alone wins. All other players lose. If no Separatist is present, all players lose.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Semi-cooperative:** Players must manage crises collectively or risk rebellion, but compete for individual victory.
- **Separatist and Pacifist:** Two secret objective roles have unique win conditions. The Separatist wins alone if the colony declares independence. The Pacifist scores 3 VP alone if, at game end, the gap between the population marker and the rebellion marker meets the condition on the Pacifist card.
- **Citizen states:** Active citizens can perform actions. Rebel citizens (lying on their backs) cannot work, use buildings, or contribute to taxes. Citizens become rebels when crises go unresolved. Rebels are stood up again during Phase 1 (Disengagement).
- **Crisis resolution (Phase 4):** The crisis type is shown on the back of the top evolution deck card. For a domestic consumption crisis, all citizens are laid on their backs; players in turn order provide resources to stand citizens up. For an export consumption crisis, players in turn order provide the required resources to the export market. Unresolved citizens remain rebels and raise the rebellion marker; missing export resources each raise the rebellion marker by 1.
- **Action Disc limits:** Some actions (taxes, reproduction, exploration) have limited slots on the Action Wheel per turn. Others (harvest, recruitment, construction, transaction, migration) are unlimited and can receive stacked discs.
- **Explorer token piles:** There are three piles of 8 explorer tokens. When the first pile empties, each player gains a 4th action disc. When the second pile empties, each player gains a 5th action disc.
- **Variable Game Length:** Objective card decks are sorted into short, medium, and long game sets, chosen before play.
- **3-citizen limit:** No player may have more than 3 citizens (active or rebel) in a single region.
- **Surplus workers:** Tracked by a marker only—surplus workers are not physically represented on the board until recruited.
- **Negotiation:** Trading, deal-making, and negotiation with other players are permitted during a player's round (Phase 5: Actions), during crisis resolution, and when determining turn order. These are general activities, not discrete Action Wheel actions.

| 5. Actions | Place Action Discs on Action Wheel; perform actions in turn order |
| 6. Evolution Card Purchase | Each player buys or rotates evolution cards; resolve any immediate crises |

|----------|-------------|

| Domestic crisis unresolved | Remaining citizens become rebels; rebellion marker rises |
| Export crisis unresolved | Rebellion marker rises 1 per missing resource |
| Surplus workers too high | Rebellion marker rises each population phase |
