## Overview

Artus is a game of political maneuvering at King Arthur's round table. Players lead groups of Knights, competing for the most prestigious seats around the rotating table. Since the King changes positions and new Kings can be crowned, players must constantly reposition their Knights. The player with the most prestige points wins.

## Components

- 1 game board (3 sections)
- 1 rotating board ("table surface") with suckling pig marker
- 12 point tiles (marked "50")
- 28 playing figures: 6 Knights per player color (4 colors) + 2 Princes (silver and bronze)
- 10 golden rings
- 88 cards (22 per player): Knight cards, King cards, and scoring cards
- 1 plastic stand

## Setup

1. Assemble the game board. Place Princes on the 4 crown-marked seats (identical colors opposite each other).
2. Place 3 rings on the Prince at the larger crown seat (this becomes King Arthur). Place 1 ring on each other Prince.
3. Position the rotating table so the crown plate is adjacent to King Arthur.
4. Each player takes 6 Knights (5 with 4 players), places 1 on the 0/50 prestige track.
5. Each player sorts their 22 cards into Knight cards (helmet backs) and King cards (crown backs). Set aside scoring cards for beginner game.
6. Shuffle each pile separately. Draw 2 from each for 4 hand cards.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, play 1 card from your hand, execute its effect, then draw 1 replacement card (from either Knight or King pile).

## Actions

**Knight Cards:**
- Play a Knight card to move one of your own Knights clockwise (forward) around the table by as many spaces as indicated by the card. The numbers on the card give you options for how far to move.
- Only one special Knight card permits backward (counter-clockwise) movement of 1–5 spaces; this is the only card that allows deliberate backward movement.
- Moving a Knight onto an occupied seat displaces the figure already there. The displaced figure moves to the next empty space in counter-clockwise direction. Displaced figures do not score any points.
- Prestige points are determined by the value of the space the Knight **left**, not the space it lands on.

**King Cards:**
- Play a King card (with number values) to move a Prince or the King forward (clockwise) as many spaces as indicated by the card. Cards are colour-restricted: the card's background colour determines which figures (silver, bronze, or either) may be moved.
- Only one special King card permits backward (counter-clockwise) movement of a Prince or the King by 1–5 spaces.
- When the King is moved, the rotating table surface is immediately rotated so the crown plate stays adjacent to the King.
- Moving a Prince scores prestige points equal to the value of the space the Prince **left**. Moving the King scores 0 points (the King is always next to the crown, which is worth 0).
- If a Prince or King lands on an occupied seat, the figure there is displaced to the next empty space in counter-clockwise direction.

**Add a Ring (King Cards):**
- Some King cards allow you to add a ring from the supply to a Prince (never the King). Colour restrictions apply.
- When a Prince receives its third ring, it is immediately crowned the new King. The old King becomes a Prince, losing all but one ring (returned to supply). Rotate the table surface to place the crown adjacent to the new King.
- For adding a ring (including a third one), the player scores prestige points equal to the value of the space the Prince is currently standing on. Princes are not moved by a ring action.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- Knights earn or lose prestige points based on the value of the space they **leave** when moved.
- The highest-value seats are directly behind the King (clockwise); seats in front of the King have negative values.
- When a player's scoring marker reaches or passes the 50 space, they take a point tile (marked "50") from the supply. If their score drops below 50 again, the tile is returned.
- The beginner game ends after **16 rounds** (all cards played). The advanced game ends after **11 rounds** (all cards played). There is no end-game position scoring.
- The player with the most prestige points wins. In the event of a tie, there are multiple winners.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Rings on a Prince track proximity to being crowned King (3 rings = new King). Rings do not add any direct point bonus.
- When the King is displaced by another figure landing on its seat, the table surface is rotated immediately afterward so the crown is next to the King again.
- Negative prestige points are possible; track carefully (marker placed on its side if below 0).
- The advanced game adds scoring cards and increases hand size to 6 cards, with players playing 2 cards per turn.
- Knights pushed by another figure landing on their seat do not score any points from being displaced.

## Player Reference

| Card Type | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Knight card | Move your own Knight clockwise; one special card also allows backward movement |
| King card (move) | Move a Prince or the King clockwise (colour-restricted); one special card also allows backward movement |
| King card (ring) | Add a ring to a Prince (colour-restricted); third ring crowns a new King |

| Seat Value | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| Highest positive | Directly behind King |
| Decreasing positive | Further behind King |
| Negative | In front of King |
