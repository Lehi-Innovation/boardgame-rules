## Overview

Boonlake is an engine-building strategy game where players develop an abandoned lakeside region by exploring landscapes, settling, breeding cattle, hiring inhabitants, modernizing production, and building along a river. Players choose actions from a shared action board of sliding strips, which also determines ship movement. The game plays over 2 rounds, each ending when a player's ship reaches the river's end. Victory points come from settlements, projects, cattle, levers, tier bonuses, and scoring tiles.

## Components

- 1 Gameboard (with river, victory-point track, coin/card tracks)
- 1 Action board with 7 action strips
- 165 Project cards
- 4 Double-layer player boards
- Per player: 10 inhabitants, 6 houses, 4 settlements, 5 cattle, 2 boats, 1 ship, 4 production sites, 4 scoring markers, 1 100-/200-marker, 3 player markers
- 42 Building tiles (including 10 starting tiles, 7 cattle pastures)
- 16 Scoring tiles, 36 Levers, 14 Vases
- 62 Coins (1s, 5s, 20s)
- 10 Solo tiles, 4 Region markers, Starting player token

## Setup

1. Place gameboard and action board. Shuffle action strips randomly onto the action board.
2. Place building tiles based on player count (1-2P: 6 tiles, 3P: 8 tiles, 4P: 10 tiles).
3. Stack gray building tiles as facedown mound. Place cattle pastures nearby.
4. Place region markers. Deal 6 project cards to each player.
5. Mix scoring tiles; each player takes 2, keeps 1 (place in their color area at gameboard bottom), returns the other. In 1-3P, fill remaining areas with 1 random tile each so 4 are always in play.
6. Each player takes colored pieces: 6 houses, 4 settlements, 5 cattle covering board recesses; 3 inhabitants on ranch, 2 in recesses, 5 in supply.
7. Ship on river: 1st space (1-2P) or 3rd space (3-4P). Boats on canal in wood-production area.
8. Player markers on VP track (0), coin track (2), card track (2).
9. Starting player (last person to travel to a place with <100 inhabitants) gets 6 coins; next gets 7; others get 8.
10. Each player chooses 1 production site and places it (level-1 side up) in the top-left recess of their board.

## Turn Structure

Played over **2 rounds**. A round ends when any player's ship reaches the last river space (current round continues until all players have had equal turns; then the 4th interim scoring and final scoring occur).

**Each turn has 3 phases:**

### Phase A: Choose 1 Action
Select an action strip from the action board and place it at the bottom. If selecting one of the two bottom-most strips, move your VP marker back as indicated.

The action strip has three sections:
1. **Card play** (left): Play a project card of the matching type (day/sunset/night) by paying its cost, OR discard a card for 2 coins (3 coins with the Builder action), OR skip.
2. **Active player effects** (middle, left of arrow): Execute effects left to right. You may omit some.
3. **All-player effect** (right of arrow): All players (including active) may use this effect. (Exception: with Pioneer, only the *other* players may use the right-side effect.)

### Phase B: Advance Ship
Move your ship along the river up to the number of steps shown on the just-uncovered action board space (minimum 1 step). Collect the bonus of the space where your ship lands.
- Harbors hold unlimited ships; all other spaces hold only 1 ship.
- Occupied non-harbor spaces are skipped and do not count toward movement; harbor spaces always count.
- Ship moves only downstream (never backwards). Cannot move beyond the final space.
- In round 1, take the upper fork; in round 2, the lower fork.
- If you are the **first** player in a round to cross a lock, immediately trigger an interim scoring for all. If someone already crossed that lock this round, no scoring is triggered.

### Phase C: Push Action Strips Together
Slide the remaining strips up to close the gap.

## Actions

The 7 action strips are: **Pioneer, Settle, Cattle-breeding, Region Scoring, Hire, Builder, Progress (Modernize).**

### Core Effects Available on Strips

**Explore:** Reveal a gray building tile from the mound and place it on an unoccupied gameboard space adjacent to an existing tile (or connected via a bridge). Receive the space's reward (coins, cards, 1 inhabitant, or 1 production site).

**Develop:** Place 1 inhabitant from your ranch onto an unoccupied building site on any building tile (paying any cost or taking any reward shown on the site). Cattle pastures cannot hold inhabitants.

**Upgrade:** Either upgrade 1 of your inhabitants on a building site to a house (give up 1 ranch inhabitant; take a house from the topmost non-emptied tier of your board), or upgrade 1 of your houses to a settlement (give up 2 ranch inhabitants; house must be adjacent to 3+ presences; 3rd settlement costs 1 extra inhabitant or 4 VP; 4th costs 2 extra inhabitants or 4 VP each; building a settlement in a region where you already have one costs 5 VP). Emptying a tier earns a **tier bonus** (place the inhabitant shown left of that tier onto your ranch).

**Cattle-breeding (place cattle):** Choose a cattle pasture with ≤3 cattle. Give up 1/2/3/4 ranch inhabitants if the pasture holds 0/1/2/3 cattle. 4th cattle costs 1 extra inhabitant or 4 VP; 5th costs 2 extra inhabitants or 4 VP each. Take 2 coins per house (any owner) on adjacent building tiles. Removing cattle from your board increases your interim-scoring income.

**Region Scoring (action strip):** Choose 1 region whose reward is not yet covered. Place a region marker on its reward and gain it. Then all players score 1 coin per presence (inhabitant, house, settlement, or cattle) in the other 3 regions. (Previously covered regions are ignored for the coin scoring.)

**Hire (take inhabitants):** Pay 3 coins or discard 3 cards per inhabitant taken from the supply to your ranch (you may pay differently for each when taking multiple).

**Modernize:** Pay the coins shown on an unoccupied space in your modernization area, place a lever tile there (reward side visible). Lever effects may be triggered at any time (flip lever down to use; levers reset upward at each interim scoring).

### The 7 Named Action Strips in Brief

| Action | Active-player bonus (left of arrow) | All-player effect (right of arrow) |
|--------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Pioneer** | Play/discard card; explore twice; then develop or upgrade once | Others draw 2 cards, play 1 card of any kind (or discard for 2 coins) |
| **Settle** | Play/discard card; develop or upgrade once | All players (starting with active, clockwise) may develop, upgrade, or play 1 card of any kind |
| **Cattle-breeding** | Play/discard card; place 1 cattle pasture on an unoccupied adjacent space and take its reward | All players (starting with active, clockwise) may place 1 cattle or play 1 card of any kind |
| **Region Scoring** | Play/discard card; perform Region Scoring once | After region scoring, all players score 1 coin per presence in the 3 unscored regions |
| **Hire** | Play/discard card; take 1 inhabitant and explore once | All players may pay 3 coins or discard 3 cards to take 1 inhabitant |
| **Builder** | Receive 3 coins (instead of card play); then play 1 card of any kind or draw 2 cards | All players may play 1 card of any kind |
| **Progress** | Play/discard card; modernize once (active player may modernize once more on their own) | All players may modernize once, but must give up 1 inhabitant; otherwise draw 1 card |

### Resources
Production sites produce resources virtually (stone, iron, loam, wood). Resources are not spent when used — you need them "available." Each production site shows 1 resource (level 1) or 2 resources (level 2) of its type.

Each of your 2 boats provides 1 resource of the type next to its canal position. Moving boats downstream is free; each upstream move costs 2 coins.

### Project Cards
Cards cost coins, vases, and/or resources. Played cards provide immediate effects (lightning symbol), end-game VP (star symbol), or permanent abilities (book symbol). Cards come in three types: day, sunset, night. Discarding any card from hand yields 2 coins (3 coins with Builder action).

### Special Projects (Scoring Tiles)
Instead of playing a card, pay the coins + resources shown on a scoring tile's upper area. Gain 2 inhabitants (place 1 on the tile's color-matching space), advance 1 step on the shown income track, and earn the VP shown. If this is your chosen scoring tile, earn double VP. Each project can be done only once per game per player.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Interim Scoring (4 times during the game)
Triggered when the first player in a round crosses each of the 3 locks, and when a player's ship reaches the final space (4th scoring). All players participate in each interim scoring.

**Interim scoring steps (all players simultaneously):**
1. **Card/Upgrade opportunity:** Once or twice, each player may play 1 card of any kind (discarding for coins is not allowed here), or upgrade (develop is not allowed). You may do one of each.
2. **Score for a scoring tile:** Take your scoring marker numbered 1/2/3/4 (matching this interim scoring). Choose one of the 4 scoring tiles not yet marked by your color. Place your marker there and earn the VP shown if you meet the condition; otherwise subtract those VP. Your chosen scoring tile doubles the result. (You must score even if you cannot meet the condition.)
3. **Income:** 
   - Receive coins/cards/VP per your markers' positions on the two income tracks.
   - Receive coins and VP for each space vacated on your player board (inhabitants via tier bonus, houses, settlements, cattle).
   - Cattle scoring: for each of your cattle on the gameboard, earn 1 VP per settlement on adjacent building tiles (including across bridges).
   - **At the 4th interim scoring only:** players receive only VP — coins and cards from income go to waste.
4. **Levers:** Reset all used levers upward. Earn 1 VP per lever you did **not** use.
5. **Region markers:** Remove all region markers from rewards (so regions can be scored again).
6. **End of round 1:** After the 2nd interim scoring, all ships return to the starting space for round 2.

### Final Scoring
After the 4th interim scoring:
1. If you have 4–6 levers on your board, earn 2 VP per lever. If 7+, earn 3 VP per lever.
2. Earn VP for each card you played that shows an end-game effect.
3. Earn 2 VP for each recess in the 3rd board tier from which you removed a piece; 4 VP per 4th-tier recess; 6 VP per 5th-tier recess.
4. **Leftover coins and cards give no VP.**

**Winner:** Most VP. Ties: all tied players win. Use 100-/200-markers to track overflow.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Lever effects:** Activated at any time (including during other players' actions and during interim scoring, but not after levers are reset at the 4th interim scoring). Move lever down to use; reset up at each interim scoring.
- **Top-row levers:** May only be used if you forgo the all-player right-side effect of the action strip you chose. Only 1 of these 3 levers may be used per action.
- **Tier bonuses:** Emptying the 1st tier (2 houses + 1 cattle removed) places the adjacent inhabitant onto your ranch immediately. Same for 2nd tier. Vacated spaces pay income at each interim scoring.
- **Resource availability:** Virtual — not consumed, just verified present on production sites and boats.
- **Upstream boat movement:** Costs 2 coins per boat moved any number of steps upstream. Downstream is free.
- **VP can go below 0:** Track with the marker.
- **Wooden pieces are limited:** If the supply is depleted you go empty-handed.
- **Houses removed from game:** A house used in an upgrade (to a settlement) is removed from game permanently but may be reused if you have no houses left on your board but need to place one.
- **Parallel play:** Most actions resolve simultaneously. Settle and Cattle-breeding resolve in clockwise order starting with the active player.
- **Income track end:** If your marker is on the last space and would advance, the effect is wasted; instead earn 2 VP per forfeited step.
- **4th interim scoring timing:** Triggered when a ship reaches the final space, but the current round continues until the player to the right of the starting-player token has taken their turn. Only then is the 4th interim scoring (and final scoring) conducted.

## Player Reference

**Turn Sequence:**
1. Phase A: Choose action strip (lose VP if choosing bottom rows), play/discard card, execute active-player effects, all-player effect
2. Phase B: Move ship (min 1 step), collect river bonus
3. Phase C: Push strips together

**River Bonuses:**
| Symbol | Reward |
|--------|--------|
| Coins | Take X coins |
| Cards | Draw X cards |
| Inhabitants | Take X inhabitants to ranch |
| Vase or Production | Take 1 vase OR build 1 production site (level 1 or upgrade to level 2) |
| Vase | Take 1 vase |
| VP | Gain X victory points |

**Upgrade costs summary:**
| Upgrade | Ranch inhabitants required |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Inhabitant → house | 1 (from ranch) |
| House → settlement (1st or 2nd) | 2 |
| House → settlement (3rd) | 2 + 1 extra (or 4 VP) |
| House → settlement (4th) | 2 + 2 extra (or 4 VP each) |
| Same-region settlement penalty | −5 VP |

**Starting Resources:** 6–8 coins (by turn order), marker at 2 on coin track, marker at 2 on card track, 1 level-1 production site of chosen type, 2 wood (from boats in wood area)

**Game Length:** 2 rounds, 4 interim scorings total. Final scoring follows the 4th interim scoring.
