## Overview

Discworld: Ankh-Morpork is a card-driven area control game set in Terry Pratchett's Discworld. Lord Vetinari has disappeared, and players secretly vie for control of the city. Each player has a hidden Personality card with unique victory conditions. On your turn, play a card and follow its instructions, then refill your hand to 5.

## Components

- Game board (city of Ankh-Morpork divided into 12 areas)
- 4 sets of 12 minion pieces (4 colors)
- 4 sets of 6 building pieces (4 colors)
- 12 trouble markers
- 4 demon pieces, 3 troll pieces
- 1 twelve-sided die
- 48 green-bordered Player cards, 53 brown-bordered Player cards
- 12 Random Event cards
- 7 Personality cards
- 12 City Area cards
- 35 silver coins ($1), 17 gold coins ($5)
- 4 Player Aid cards

## Setup

1. Each player selects a color and takes 12 minions + 6 buildings.
2. Each player places 1 minion in The Shades, The Scours, and Dolly Sisters. Place trouble markers in these areas.
3. Each player receives $10.
4. Shuffle Personality cards; deal 1 to each player (keep secret). Set remainder aside.
5. Shuffle brown Player cards and place face-down. Shuffle green Player cards and place on top.
6. Deal 5 cards to each player.
7. Shuffle Random Event cards; place as face-down deck.
8. Place City Area cards face-up near the board.

## Turn Structure

Starting with the first player, each player takes a turn:

1. **Play a card** from your hand. Follow all instructions on the card (symbols and text).
2. Cards may chain -- some allow you to play additional cards.
3. After finishing all card plays, **draw cards** to bring your hand to 5 (do not discard if you have more than 5).
4. You may check your personality victory condition at any time on your turn and declare victory.

## Actions

Cards contain symbols that trigger specific actions (in order, left to right):

| Symbol | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Place Minion | Place one of your minions in an area where you already have a minion OR in an area adjacent to one where you have a minion. If you have no minions on the board, you may place in any area. If all 12 minions are on the board, remove one and place it per these rules. |
| Assassinate | Remove one minion (not your own), troll, or demon from an area containing a trouble marker. This also removes the trouble marker. |
| Remove Trouble Marker | Remove one trouble marker from any area of your choice. |
| Build | Pay the area's building cost; place one of your buildings in an area where you have a minion and there is no building or trouble marker. Take the matching City Area card. |
| Take Money | Take the shown amount from the bank. |
| Play Another Card | Play another card from your hand. |
| Random Event | Draw and resolve the top Random Event card (must be performed; not optional). Place it aside after resolution — each event can only occur once. |
| Interrupt | Can be played at any time, even on another player's turn. |
| Scroll (text) | Follow the card's specific text instructions. |

### Trouble Markers
- Placed whenever a minion is placed or moved into an area that already has one or more minions (including trolls and demons) in it. An area can only contain one trouble marker.
- **Removed** as soon as any minion is moved or removed from the area (even if other minions remain).
- Buildings can only be placed in areas without trouble markers (and without an existing building).
- Assassinations require a trouble marker to be present in the target area.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when: (a) a player achieves the conditions on their Personality card, (b) the Riot Random Event card is drawn and its conditions are met, or (c) the draw pile runs out.

All Personality conditions (except Vimes) are checked at the **start of your turn**:

- **Lord Vetinari:** Have minions in at least 11 different areas (2P), 10 areas (3P), or 9 areas (4P). Areas must be free of demons.
- **Lord Selachii / Lord Rust / Lord de Worde:** Control at least 7 areas (2P), 5 areas (3P), or 4 areas (4P). You control an area if you have more playing pieces (minions + buildings) there than any single other player AND more pieces than the total number of trolls in the area. Demons in an area prevent control.
- **Dragon King of Arms:** 8 or more trouble markers are on the board at the start of your turn.
- **Chrysoprase:** Your net worth (cash + monetary cost of each building you have, minus $12 per loan) is $50 or more. (Note: Chrysoprase is removed in 2-player games.)
- **Commander Vimes:** Wins if nobody else wins by the time the draw pile is exhausted.

**Tiebreaker/draw-pile end (no Vimes):** The player with the most points wins. Each minion on the board = 5 points; each building = its monetary cost; each $1 in cash = 1 point; each loan card = −$12. If tied, the tied player with the highest-value City Area card wins. If still tied, those players share victory.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Area control:** You control an area if you have more playing pieces (minions + buildings) there than any single other player AND more pieces than the total trolls present; demons in the area prevent control. Trouble markers do not affect control.
- **Buildings:** Each area can have at most one building. Costs vary ($6–$18). When you place a building, take the matching City Area card (return it if the building is removed). You may have up to 6 buildings; if already at 6, remove one to place a new one.
- **City Area cards** grant abilities usable once per turn (exception: Small Gods can be used on other players' turns). You may not use a card's ability on the same turn you gained it.
- **Demons and trolls** are placed via card effects. Trolls count as minions for trouble and control purposes. Demons prevent area control and also count as minions for trouble purposes.
- **Players cannot hide their money** — all cash is public.
- **Random Events** use the 12-sided die to determine which area is affected. Each Random Event card can only occur once.
- **2-player setup:** Remove Chrysoprase Personality card and the Hubert and Cosmos Lavish Player cards before setup.
- Most card actions are optional; the **Random Event action is mandatory**.
