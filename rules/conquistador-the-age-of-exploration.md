## Overview

Conquistador! is a 1-3 player grand strategy game covering the exploration and conquest of the New World from 1495-1600. Players take the roles of European nations (Spain, England, France, and optionally Portugal) competing to accumulate wealth, land, and prestige through Discovery Points, Political Control of areas, and Gold/Resources. Players send expeditions with colonists, soldiers, explorers, and missionaries to settle, explore, and exploit the riches of the Western Hemisphere.

## Components

- 1 game map (22" x 32") of the Western Hemisphere with hexagonal grid
- 1 sheet of die-cut counters (200 pieces) including units for Spain (green), France (blue), and England (light tan); Portugal has no dedicated counters (players must supply their own if using the Portugal option)
- Unit types: Colonists, Soldiers, Explorers, Privateers, Conquistadors, Missionaries, Ships; Yellow ships and Brown/Yellow Gold Markers are not tied to any nationality
- Charts and tables printed on the map
- 2 six-sided dice
- Rules booklet

## Setup

1. Place the game map on a large flat surface.
2. Each player selects a nationality (Spain, England, or France). In the optional 4-player variant, a fourth player controls Portugal.
3. Place starting units in designated home port hexes according to the scenario being played.
4. Set the National Treasury for each country at starting levels.
5. Place the game turn marker on turn 1.

## Turn Structure

Each game turn represents approximately 5 years. The Campaign Game covers 21 Game-Turns (1495-1600). Each Game-Turn has five phases, each divided into segments:

1. **Council Phase (A)** -- Performed simultaneously by all players. Segments: Random Events (taxation, colonist availability, political events), Initiative Determination, Planning (purchases, expedition plans, loans, treaties). All actions in the Council Phase are simultaneous.
2. **Initial Naval Phase (B)** -- Players act in Initiative order. Segments: Trans-Oceanic Naval Movement (expeditions to/from Europe; units may not be unloaded), Hemispheric Naval Movement (within the New World; units may be loaded/unloaded, naval combat occurs here), Naval Attrition.
3. **Land Phase (C)** -- Players act in Initiative order. Segments: Gold Segment (mine gold, collect loot, check for depletion), Land Movement and Combat (move land units, combat, land discovery credit recorded), Native Combat, Native Uprising, Land Attrition, Resource Segment (load gold onto ships, credit resources to treasury).
4. **Final Naval Phase (D)** -- Trans-Oceanic movement only (no hemispheric movement, no combat). Segments: Trans-Oceanic Naval Movement, Naval Attrition, Discovery Credit (for expeditions returned to a friendly port), Gold/treasure added to national treasuries.
5. **Maintenance Phase (E)** -- Players pay maintenance costs for each unit they wish to keep; costs deducted from National Treasuries.

## Actions

**Royal Council Phase:**
- **Monarchs:** Each nation's monarch affects the country's capabilities (some monarchs are more supportive of exploration than others).
- **National Treasury:** Collect income from colonies, mines, and trade. Fund expeditions.
- **Random Events:** Roll for events that may affect any nation (storms, epidemics, political upheaval).
- **Outfitting Expeditions:** Purchase new units (Ships, Colonists, Soldiers, Explorers, etc.) using treasury funds.
- **Initiative:** Determines player order for the turn.

**Naval Phases:**
- **Trans-Oceanic Movement:** Ships move across the Atlantic to/from Europe. During the Initial Naval Phase, units may not be unloaded at the landing hex.
- **Hemispheric Movement:** Ships move within the New World. Units may be loaded/unloaded; naval combat and naval invasions of ports occur during this segment (Initial Naval Phase only; the Final Naval Phase has no hemispheric movement and no combat).
- **Naval Combat:** When opposing ships engage, naval combat is resolved using the Naval Combat Results Table.
- **Naval Attrition:** Ships at sea may suffer losses due to storms and provisioning failures. Checked against the Naval Attrition Table after all movement in a phase.

**Land Phase:**
- **Land Movement:** Units move through hexes; movement costs vary by terrain type. Zones of Control restrict movement near enemy units.
- **Land Combat:** Resolved using the Land Combat Results Table. Combat strength modified by terrain and fortifications.
- **Discovery:** When units enter unexplored hexes, check the Discovery Table for what is found (native populations, geography, resources).
- **Gold Mining and Resource Collection:** Established mines produce gold; colonists collect resources from controlled areas.

**Special Units:**
- **Colonists:** Settle areas and collect resources. A settlement is any land or partial-land hex containing a Colonist unit.
- **Soldiers:** Provide military strength for combat against natives and rival nations.
- **Explorers:** Required to lead Voyages of Discovery (naval expeditions earning discovery credit). Explorer-led expeditions spend 3 Movement Points per hex being discovered.
- **Privateers (English "Sea Dogs"):** May lead Voyages of Discovery like Explorers; also raid enemy shipping.
- **Conquistadors:** Named historical leaders (Spanish only) with enhanced combat abilities against natives.
- **Missionaries (Spain only):** Spain must have a Missionary in every Area where it has Colonists or Soldiers and the Native Level is at least 1. Missionaries have a Conversion Rating (+/-); plus-rated Missionaries subtract from the combat die-roll vs. Natives (up to 3 total reductions including Conquistadors). Their rating is added to the Uprising Level calculation when checking for Native Uprisings. Missionaries are not involved in Political Control; they may not be attacked by European forces. Maintenance costs 1 Ducat per turn per Missionary, paid automatically before all other maintenance.

**Settlements and Ports:**
- A settlement is any land or partial-land hex containing a Colonist unit (no separate building cost).
- Ports are settlements on coastal or island hexes; they enable naval access.
- Settlements allow unlimited stacking. Settlements do not provide general combat bonuses; ports double defending units against Naval Invasions only.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The Player with the most Victory Points at the end of the game is the winner. Victory Points are awarded from three sources:

1. **Wealth:** 2 Victory Points for each Ducat in the Player's Treasury at game end. (Uncollected gold on the map does not count.)
2. **Political Control:** 150 Victory Points for each Area under the Player's Political Control at game end.
3. **Discoveries:** Variable Victory Points as per the Discovery Table.

**Political Control** of an Area requires satisfying ALL five conditions simultaneously:
1. At least 50% of all Colonists in the Area must be the Player's; and
2. At least 75% of all Soldiers in the Area must be the Player's; and
3. There may be no enemy ports in the Area; and
4. There must be at least one friendly port (unless there are no coastal hexes in the Area); and
5. There must be at least three friendly Colonist units and at least two friendly settlements (one of which may be the port in condition 4).

Only one Player may control a given Area; if no Player satisfies all five conditions, no one controls it. Native Level has no effect on Political Control.

**Spain bonus:** The Spanish Player starts with 25 Victory Points (credit for discovering the New World).

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Native Levels:** Each Area has a Native Level affecting combat difficulty and uprising checks. Higher Native Levels mean stronger resistance.
- **Native Uprisings:** Areas may experience uprisings; the Uprising Level for the check is affected by the Conversion Rating of any Missionaries present (plus ratings help Spain, minus ratings hurt).
- **Treaty of Tordesillas (Optional):** Historical treaty dividing the New World between Spain and Portugal. Affects which areas each nation may claim. Cannot be used if the Portugal option (23.0) is in play.
- **Portuguese Fourth Player (Optional, rule 23.0):** Adds Portugal as a playable nation. There are no Portuguese counters in the base game; players must supply their own.
- **German Bankers (Optional, rule 24.0):** A non-national player (requires at least 3 active country players) controls German Banking Houses. The Banker player cannot send expeditions or make active moves on the map. At game end he receives 3 Victory Points for every Ducat in his Treasury; he receives NO Discovery credit for any Discovery. He is considered the winner if he has the most Victory Points at game end. In addition, regardless of his Victory Point total, if his Treasury is greater than the combined Treasuries of any two other countries, he has scored a "technical victory." Separately (24.3), if the Banker receives at least 50% of an Area's profits (Gold + Resources) via loan agreements, he — not the Player who otherwise satisfies Political Control there — is considered "in Control" of that Area for Victory purposes (the standard 150 VP/Area rate, not a per-Area VP bonus).
- **Solitaire Scenario (21.2):** Spain-only; the Non-Player countries (England, France, and Portugal) do not roll for Random Events, send no Expeditions, and have no land units — instead they colonize Areas via an abstract Non-Player Colonization Table keyed to the Random Events roll. The Spanish Player scores Victory Points as per Section 20.0, except he receives no Discovery credit for North America, Hudson's Bay, the St. Lawrence and Mississippi Rivers, or the Great Lakes. In the Solitaire Scenario, the Player is considered to "control" an Area only if its European Level has been reduced to "0". If the Player has 4500 or more Victory Points at the end of the Game, he is considered to have won.
- **Attrition:** Both naval and land units face attrition. For naval attrition, Players purchase Bounds (spending limits) that define how far expeditions may travel without increased attrition risk; unused Bounds do not carry over between turns.
- **Gold Transportation:** Gold must be physically loaded onto ships (during the Resource Segment) and transported back to Europe (Final Naval Phase) to be added to the national treasury and count toward Victory.
- **Treasure and Looting:** Conquering native Treasure Cities may yield loot.
- **Settlements:** A settlement is any land/partial-land hex with a Colonist unit present; ports are settlements on island or coastal hexes. Settlements offer no general combat strength bonus, except that units in a port hex are doubled on defense against Naval Invasions.
- **Diplomacy:** Players may negotiate any agreements (political, military, financial) during the Council Phase, but none may violate the rules.
- **Secrecy:** Expedition plans and purchases made in the Planning Segment may be kept secret until the end of the turn.
