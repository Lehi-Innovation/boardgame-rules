## Overview

Commonspoly is a cooperative board game that promotes commoning as an alternative to privatisation — the conceptual opposite of Monopoly. Players work together to convert privately and publicly owned goods into commons. One player acts as Game Master and also plays The Speculator(s), who move around the board trying to block the players. The game must be won within 20 rounds or the players lose. Ideally played with 6–8 players (Game Master included); possible with as few as 4 but very difficult.

## Components

- 1 game board
- 1 die
- 16 Player Sheets (characters)
- 1 Game Master Sheet
- 40 Global Cards (affect all players each round)
- 50 Situation Cards (affect individual players)
- 8 player tokens (non-white colours)
- 3 Speculator tokens (white)
- Goods Tokens (37 each of black/grey/white, representing Commons/Public/Private)
- Welfare Points Coins (15×5, 20×2, 30×1)
- Legitimacy Points Coins (15×5, 20×2, 30×1)

## Setup

1. Place the board centrally.
2. Decide who will be Game Master; the Game Master also plays The Speculator(s).
3. Roll the die once for each of the 4 types of goods (Health, Urban, Intangible, Environmental) to determine their starting status: 1–2 = Private (white token), 3–4 = Public (grey token), 5–6 = Commons (black token). Place Goods Tokens on the board accordingly.
4. Total the 4 dice results to determine the Game Mode (see below) and the number of Speculator tokens.
5. Each player picks a Player Sheet at random (no peeking) and receives 5 Welfare Points and 5 Legitimacy Points. Skill Points are fixed values printed on each Player Sheet.
6. Players choose non-white tokens; The Speculator(s) use white tokens. All tokens start at GO!
7. Place both card decks face down near the Game Master.

**Game Mode (sum of 4 good-status dice):**
| Total | Mode | Speculator tokens |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| 4–10 | Mad Max | 1 |
| 11–17 | Transition | 2 |
| 18–24 | Commonsland | 3 |

## Turn Structure

The game lasts a maximum of **20 rounds**. Each round:

1. **Global Card (from round 2 onwards):** Game Master draws and reads a Global Card; its effects apply to all players for this round.
2. **Players take turns** in order, rolling the die and moving in any direction. Players may move 1 at a time; there is a **maximum of 2 players per space** at any time.
3. **The Speculator(s) take their turn last**, rolling once per Speculator token; the Game Master may move any token(s) in any order.

**On your turn:**
- Roll the die and move to a space.
- Resolve the space's effect.
- Optionally spend points to change the status of a good on your space (see Actions).

## Actions

### Landing on a Good Space
When you land on a Good space, receive points based on its current status:
- **Private:** Receive no points.
- **Public:** Receive 1 Welfare Point and 1 Legitimacy Point.
- **Commons:** Receive 2 Welfare Points and 2 Legitimacy Points.
- **Inalienable Common Goods (pink):** Receive 2 Welfare Points and 2 Legitimacy Points.

### Unlocking / Changing a Good's Status (Individual Action)
You can shift a good from Private → Public, or from Public → Commons, by spending a total of **10 Points** combining your Skill Points (must match the good type) + Welfare Points.

- **Skill Points** are fixed per character and never lost; they apply only to their associated good type.
- **Welfare Points** are spent (lost) when used; minimum 0.
- You gain **1 Legitimacy Point** automatically when you successfully change a good's status.
- You can only shift one good at a time as an individual (Private → Public, or Public → Commons — not Private → Commons in one step).

**Skill types and associated goods:**
| Skill | Good type |
|-------|-----------|
| Caring | Health (purple) |
| Occupying | Urban (red) |
| Sharing | Intangible (yellow) |
| Regenerating | Environmental (green) |

### Collective Uprising Action (Central Agora)
All goods of **one entire type** can be shifted directly to Commons via the Central Agora:
- Enter through one of the 4 column spaces (must roll the exact number).
- At least **2 players** must be in the Agora to begin the action; once 2 are present, other players can join instantly.
- You may stay up to **3 rounds** in the Agora; each round costs 1 Welfare Point and gains 1 Legitimacy Point. If you run out of Welfare Points you must leave.
- The group must collectively spend at least **10 Legitimacy Points × number of players in the Agora** to perform the action.
- On success, all goods of the chosen type (regardless of current status) become Commons. Remaining Legitimacy Points are distributed among players by group decision.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Win condition (cooperative):** All goods on the board become Commons before 20 rounds are completed. If all goods are Commons, the players win outright. If there is a mix of Public and Commons goods at game end, it is a **draw**. If any Private good remains, the players **lose**.

**Loss condition:** 20 rounds elapse and at least one Private good remains, OR players are forced to lose if they cannot prevent the Speculators from maintaining privatisation.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### The Speculator(s)
The Speculator(s) are played by the Game Master (or a separate designated player). They have the last turn each round and roll once per token. If a Speculator lands on a space occupied by a player:
1. **Deduct Welfare Points** (roll to determine amount): 1–2 = −1 WF, 3–4 = −2 WF, 5–6 = −3 WF.
2. **Send to Detention Centre** (roll to determine duration): 1–2 = 1 round, 3–4 = 2 rounds, 5–6 = 3 rounds. Cost to be freed: 1 WF per round if citizen, 2 WF per round if non-citizen (can be paid collectively). Players in Detention cannot join the Central Agora.
3. **Send to Tragedy of the Commons** (requires rolling the same number twice in a row).

**Speculator in the Central Agora:** If only 1 player is in the Agora, a Speculator may enter and roll: 1–3 = kicks the player out (both go to Detention; player freed for free next round); 4–6 = Speculator must go to Mortgage. Once 2 or more players are in the Agora, the Speculator cannot stop the action.

### Space Occupancy Limit
A maximum of 2 players may occupy any space at any time. If a space is full, you cannot enter it and must move elsewhere.

### Scores Cannot Go Negative
Welfare Points and Legitimacy Points cannot go below 0; any excess deduction is ignored.

### Special Spaces
| Space | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Central Agora | Collective Uprising Action (see above) |
| Assembly | Each round you stay: spend 2 WF, gain 4 LP |
| Bureaucracy | Change one Private Good to Public for 6 WF |
| Tragedy of the Commons | 3 goods of your choice drop one status level |
| Crowdfunding | Donate WF to any player; you gain 2 LP per WF donated |
| Mortgage | Lose 2 Welfare Points automatically |
| Detention Centre | Miss 1 round; cannot move |
| Casino | Lose 4 Legitimacy Points |
| Inalienable Common Goods (pink) | Gain 2 WF and 2 LP; status cannot change |
| Situation Card space (light blue S) | Draw a Situation Card; affects only you for 1 round |

## Player Reference

**Starting resources:** 5 Welfare Points, 5 Legitimacy Points, Skill Points per character sheet.

**To change a good's status:** Skill Points (matching type) + Welfare Points = 10. Skill Points are kept; Welfare Points are spent. Gain 1 LP on success.

**Status chain:** Private → Public → Commons (individual, one step at a time). Private → Commons in one step requires the Collective Uprising Action.

**Speculator token count by mode:** Mad Max = 1, Transition = 2, Commonsland = 3.

**Win:** All goods Commons. Draw: mix of Commons and Public. Lose: any Private good remains after 20 rounds.
