## Overview

Chandragupta is a hex-based tactical wargame in the Great Battles of History (GBoH) series by GMT Games. It simulates battles fought during the rise of the Mauryan Empire in ancient India (covering Chandragupta's victories over the Nanda clan through to Ashoka's conquest of Kalinga, 319–261 BC). Players command armies featuring the traditional Indian "four-fold" military division (chaturangabala): foot soldiers, chariots, elephants, and cavalry, along with distinct military grades (Maula, Bhrta, Sreni/Guild, and Tribal). The game emphasizes leadership, command and control, and the tactical use of diverse troop types. Each hex represents approximately 60–70 yards; each game turn represents roughly 15–20 minutes of real time.

## Components

- 2 double-sided 22" × 34" map sheets and 1 22" × 17" map sheet (five battle maps total; scenarios specify which maps to use)
- 3 counter sheets (840 counters): combat units, leaders, and information markers
- 1 Rules Book
- 1 Scenario Book (orders of battle, setup, and victory conditions per battle)
- 4 player aid cards
- 1 ten-sided die
- Plastic counter storage bags

There are no cards in the game.

## Setup

Setup varies by scenario. Generally:
1. Select a scenario from the Scenario Book.
2. Place the map(s) specified for that scenario.
3. Deploy units according to the scenario's starting hex assignments.
4. Place leaders with or near their designated units as given in the scenario.

## Turn Structure

The GBoH system uses an initiative-based activation sequence. Players take turns activating leaders one at a time; the player whose lowest-Initiative unactivated leader has the lowest Initiative rating activates that leader first. After all leaders have been activated, the Rout and Recovery Phase and Withdrawal Phase conclude the turn.

**Sequence of Play (in order):**

**A. Leader Activation Phase**
1. The player with the lowest-Initiative-rated unactivated leader activates that leader. If the leader is eligible for Line or Wing Command, the player rolls for that ability now.
2. Non-Momentum Trump attempts may be made here.

**B. Orders Phase**
1. *Movement and Missile Fire Segment.* For each Individual Order issued by the activated leader, the player may move and/or fire missiles with one combat unit; or, if the leader has Line Command capability, an entire Line of units may Move and/or Missile Fire; or Wing Commanders may issue Wing Commands to subordinate Nayaka Commanders.
2. *Shock Combat Segment.* After the leader finishes issuing orders, eligible units engage in Shock combat: Shock Designation → Pre-Shock TQ Check (The Charge) → Possible Leader Casualties → Resolve Shock Combat → Check for Collapse and Rout.

**C. Momentum Phase OR Return to "A"**
The player who just completed an Orders Phase may attempt a Momentum die roll to give that leader another Orders Phase, or play returns to Phase "A" for the next unactivated leader. The opposing player may attempt a Momentum Trump.

**D. Rout and Recovery Phase**
1. Remove "Rallied" markers.
2. Rout Movement: All currently-Routed units move their full printed Movement Allowance toward their Retreat Edge (11.24) — this is distinct from the initial 2-hex move a unit makes the instant it first Routs (11.22), which happens earlier in the turn (e.g., during the Shock Combat Segment).
3. Reload Segment: Eligible missile units may replenish missiles.
4. Resolve Breach Attempts.
5. Flip all "Moved" units and "Finished" leaders to their front (active) sides.

**E. Camp Orders Phase**
Artillery units and individual units that are "out of command" but inside camps may move, fire, and then Shock attack.

**F. Withdrawal Phase**
Each player totals Rout Points to check for Army Withdrawal (see Victory Conditions). If an army reaches or exceeds its Withdrawal Level, the game ends. Otherwise the game turn concludes and a new turn begins.

## Actions

### Movement
Units move through hexes paying Movement Point costs for each hex entered and some hexsides crossed. Moving a unit for the first time in a turn flips it to its "Moved" side; each subsequent move of a "Moved" unit costs 1 Cohesion Hit (does not apply to Orderly Withdrawal or Advance After Combat). Tribal Leaders have a Movement Allowance of 6; non-Tribal Leaders have 9 MP and use Cavalry terrain costs unless mounted on an elephant or chariot.

**Indian unit grades (affect command eligibility and combat effectiveness):**

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Maula (m) | Royal standing army; highest loyalty and training |
| Bhrta (b) | Mercenary corps; reasonably trained, loyalty proportional to pay |
| Sreni / Guild (s) | Trade guild forces; powerful but independent |
| Tribal (t) | Unpredictable tribal warriors |

**Four-fold army types:**

| Unit Type | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Patti (Foot soldiers / Infantry) | Backbone; hold ground |
| Rathin (Chariots) | Shock troops; powerful charges; eliminated (not routed) when TQ exceeded |
| Hasti (Elephants) | Dominant assault units; Rampage instead of routing |
| Ashva (Cavalry) | Mobile flanking forces |

**Orderly Withdrawal (OW):** A friendly (unrouted) unit not in an enemy ZOC may avoid contact with an approaching enemy unit the instant that enemy moves within two hexes of it, as long as the withdrawing unit's own Movement Allowance is higher than the approaching unit's MA. It may withdraw up to a number of Movement Points equal to (withdrawing unit's MA − approaching unit's MA) — e.g., an Elephant unit (MA 7) approached by a Phalanx (MA 4) may withdraw up to 3 Clear hexes (7−4=3 MP). Foot units and Chariots incur 1 Cohesion Hit automatically after withdrawing; Skirmishers, Cavalry, and Elephants do not. All withdrawing units take a TQ check at the conclusion.

### Leadership
Combat units may not move or fire missiles without receiving an Individual Order, Line Command, or Wing Command from a leader. Units within a leader's Command Range that received orders may also conduct Shock Combat.

Each leader has: Command Range, Initiative (determines activation order and number of Individual Orders), and — for eligible Indian leaders — a Line Command rating ("L") and possibly Wing Command capability.

**Individual Orders:** One order per leader per Orders Phase (equal to leader's Initiative rating). Each order allows one unit to: move, fire missiles, remove 2 Cohesion Hits, or attempt to Rally a Routed unit.

**Line Command:** Allows an entire line of eligible units to Move and/or Missile Fire as one order.

**Wing Command (Indian Mauryan leaders):** Overall Commanders with Wing Command may issue commands through subordinate Nayaka commanders to activate large sections of the army.

**Momentum:** After an Orders Phase, a player may roll for Momentum to grant the same leader another Orders Phase. The opponent may attempt a Momentum Trump to prevent it.

**Trump:** A leader can be used to interrupt an opponent's activation, transferring the Orders Phase to a friendly leader.

### Combat

**Missile Combat:** Missile units may fire as part of an Order/Line Command, as Reaction Fire (Entry Reaction Fire when an enemy enters range, or Retire Reaction when leaving ZOC), or — for Cavalry/Chariot archers only — during Orderly Withdrawal. Each missile type has its own supply (Low/No markers track ammo). Missile units fire individually; combined fire is not allowed.

**Shock Combat (melee):** Shock combat uses the Shock Combat Table. Results are Cohesion Hits applied to attacker and/or defender. Superiority (relative weapons/armor matchup) determines who takes fewer hits. When a unit accumulates Cohesion Hits equal to or exceeding its TQ rating, it Routs (see special exceptions below).

**TQ Check:** Many game actions require a TQ Check — roll one die; if the result exceeds the unit's TQ, the unit suffers Cohesion Hits per the Cohesion Hit and TQ Check Chart.

### Cohesion and Routing

- A unit **Routs** when its accumulated Cohesion Hits equal or exceed its TQ rating.
  - **Exception:** Chariots, Skirmishers, and Artillery are **eliminated** (not routed) when their TQ is exceeded.
  - **Exception:** Elephants do **not** rout; they **Rampage** instead (see Special Rules).
- A Routed unit is immediately moved 2 hexes toward its Retreat Edge the instant it Routs (taking the path of least resistance). If it cannot retreat the full distance, it is eliminated. Place a "Routed" marker on it.
- Later, during the Rout Movement step of the Rout and Recovery Phase, all currently-Routed units move again — this time their full printed Movement Allowance toward the Retreat Edge — even if they already moved earlier in the turn.
- Routed units have a TQ of "1" for all purposes.
- A leader may use an Individual Order to attempt to Rally a Routed unit.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Winning:** A player wins by causing his opponent's army to Withdraw (13.11). An army Withdraws when its accumulated **Rout Points (RPs)** equal or exceed its scenario-specified **Withdrawal Level** at the end of the Withdrawal Phase.

**Rout Points are tallied from eliminated units, units that Fled, units that Routed off the map, and killed leaders.** RP values:

| Unit/Leader | RP Value |
|-------------|----------|
| Most combat units | Equal to their TQ |
| Elephant (EL) units | Equal to their TQ |
| Indian Tribal combat units | 1 RP |
| Indian Sreni (Guild) combat units | 2 RPs |
| Skirmisher (SK) units | 2 RPs |
| Indian Maula and Bhrta Light Infantry | ½ TQ, rounded up |
| Phalanx and Hoplite/Heavy Infantry (Size 10) | 2× TQ |
| Artillery (AT) units | 0 RPs |
| Sreni, Tribal, and Nayaka Leaders | Initiative Rating in RPs |
| All other Leaders | 5× Initiative Rating in RPs |

**Withdrawal Level** for each army is specified in the scenario instructions.

**Tiebreaker:** If both armies reach their Withdrawal Level at the end of the same turn, the player whose army has the **fewest RPs above** its Withdrawal Level wins (a narrow victory). If both sides are exactly equal above their levels, it is a draw.

**Game Length:** Most battles run an indeterminate number of turns until one side Withdraws. Some scenarios use a Turn Record Track to limit game length; those scenarios specify their turn limit.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

**Elephant Rampage (10.14):** When an Elephant unit would otherwise Rout (Cohesion Hits ≥ TQ), it does not rout — it **Rampages** instead. The owning player rolls one die:
- **0:** The elephant charges toward the nearest friendly unit.
- **1–6:** The elephant charges in the direction indicated by the compass on the map (by hexside, toward the die-roll direction).
- **7–9:** The mahout stops the rampage; the unit is eliminated.

A Rampaging elephant moves 3 hexes per Rampage die roll, attacking all units in its path (friendly and enemy):
- Frontal attack: 1 Cohesion Hit each.
- Flank or rear attack: 2 Cohesion Hits each.
- Cavalry and Chariots always take 2 hits when attacked by a rampaging elephant.
- Any unprotected leader in the elephant's path is automatically eliminated.

Rampage repeats (rolling again) until: the Elephant exits the map, the mahout stops it (7–9 roll, eliminated), it is 8+ hexes from the nearest unit (eliminated), or after the 4th consecutive Rampage roll (eliminated). Rampaging elephants have no ZOC and may not be rallied. Only one Rampaging Elephant may be active at a time. Rampaging elephants do not draw Reaction Fire of any kind.

**Elephant Proximity (10.16):** If an Elephant moves adjacent to an enemy Cavalry or Chariot unit, that unit must attempt Orderly Withdrawal if possible; if it cannot, it undergoes an immediate TQ check.

**Pass-Through Attacks (10.13):** After completing Shock combat, if the Elephant has not Rampaged and the defending unit remains in place, the attacking Elephant is placed in one of the defender's vacant rear hexes.

**Cascade Rout (11.23):** If an Indian Overall Commander (OC) or Wing Commander (WC) riding an Elephant or Chariot unit sees that unit rout, the rout may cascade to adjacent friendly units.

**Grades and Command:** Only Indian units have grade designations. Maula and Bhrta units can be combined with Elephant or Chariot units in Combined Lines. Guild (Sreni) forces are independent; Tribals are unpredictable.

**Chariots:** Powerful shock troops; when their TQ is exceeded, they are **eliminated** (not routed). Dismounted chariot crews use the counter's reverse side. Chariots may Orderly Withdraw 1 MP before an Elephant unit even though they have the same Movement Allowance.

**Terrain:** Movement Point costs and possible Cohesion Hit penalties apply to most terrain types. Indian Elephant units may avoid forest Cohesion Hits by moving at half or less of their printed MA through forest hexes. Units in Column have their TQ reduced by –2.

**Missile Supply:** Each missile type has its own supply levels tracked by Low/No markers. Artillery is worth 0 RPs and may fire from inside camps during the Camp Orders Phase.

**Fortifications (12.0):** Camp and city rules apply in applicable scenarios; covered separately in section 12.

**Optional Rule — Dharmayuddha (14.1):** Only Indian players may adopt this rule. Before play begins, an Indian OC secretly pledges "yes" or "no" to abide by Dharmayuddha (a historical code of just warfare). Restrictions: Shock and missile attacks may only target enemy front hexsides; Orderly Withdrawal is prohibited (except Cavalry/Chariots vs. Elephants); and certain unit-type matchups are prohibited from initiating Shock. If a player who pledged "yes" violates the restrictions, his Withdrawal Level is reduced by 10% (rounded up) at the subsequent Withdrawal Phase. If a player who pledged "yes" reaches Withdrawal Level without having violated the code, his Withdrawal Level is increased by 15% (rounded up). Tribal units are exempt from Dharmayuddha entirely.

## Player Reference

**Turn phases (in order):** A. Leader Activation → B. Orders (Movement/Missile + Shock Combat) → C. Momentum / Return to A → D. Rout and Recovery → E. Camp Orders → F. Withdrawal

**Activation order:** Lowest-Initiative unactivated leader activates first (within each player's leaders).

**Individual Order options (per order):** Move one unit OR fire missiles with one unit OR remove 2 Cohesion Hits from one unit OR attempt to Rally one Routed unit.

**Rout trigger:** Cohesion Hits ≥ unit TQ → Rout (except: Chariots/Skirmishers/Artillery → eliminated; Elephants → Rampage).

**Win condition:** Force opponent's army to reach its scenario Withdrawal Level in RPs.

**Tiebreaker:** Fewest RPs above Withdrawal Level wins; exact tie = draw.

**Unit grades (Indian only):** Maula (m) > Bhrta (b) > Sreni/Guild (s), Tribal (t) — affect command eligibility and RP values.
