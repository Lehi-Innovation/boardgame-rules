## Overview

Caesar & Cleopatra is a two-player card game where one player takes the role of Caesar and the other Cleopatra. Both compete to sway powerful Roman patricians to their side. Players send influence cards to influence five groups of patricians. When a vote of confidence is called, the player with greater influence wins a patrician's voice. Victory points come from winning individual patricians, achieving majority in a group, influencing an entire group, and a secret influence bonus. The player with the most victory points determines Egypt's fate and wins.

## Components

- 135 cards total:
  - 50 Roman (Caesar) cards: 37 influence cards (numbered 1–5 plus "P" philosopher cards), 13 action cards
  - 50 Egyptian (Cleopatra) cards: 37 influence cards, 13 action cards
  - 35 other cards: 6 influence bonus cards, 21 patrician cards (5 groups), 8 vote of confidence cards

## Setup

1. **Secret influence bonus:** Shuffle 6 influence bonus cards (2 Senators, 2 Praetors, 2 Quaestors). Deal 1 face-down to each player (keep secret). Return 4 to box.
2. **Patrician cards:** Place 21 patrician cards in 5 rows between players: 5 Senators, 5 Praetors, 5 Quaestors, 3 Censors, 3 Aedilis.
3. **Vote of confidence cards:** Shuffle 8 vote cards and place them face-down in a draw pile next to the patrician cards.
4. **Player decks:** Each player takes their 37 influence cards and sets aside two each of numbers 1–5 (10 cards total). The remaining influence cards are shuffled into a face-down **influence deck**. The 13 action cards are arranged in the order the player chooses, forming a face-down **action deck**. Neither deck may be examined during play.
5. **Starting placement:** Each player places 5 of the 10 set-aside cards (one each of values 1–5) face-down on their side of the patrician groups, one card to each group.
6. **Starting hand:** Each player takes their remaining 5 set-aside cards (values 1–5) as their starting hand.

## Turn Structure

Cleopatra goes first. Players alternate turns. On your turn, choose **active** or **passive**:

### Active Turn
1. **(Optional) Play one action card** from your hand at any point before drawing.
2. **(Required) Play 1 or 2 influence cards:**
   - **1 influence card face-down** on any one patrician group, OR
   - **2 influence cards face-up** on 1 or 2 groups of your choice.
3. **(Required) Refill hand to 5 cards** by drawing from either your influence deck or action deck (player's choice per card). Skip if no cards remain.
4. **(Required) Draw the top vote of confidence card.** If it names a patrician group, a vote of confidence is called for that group. If it is an Orgy card, no vote is called (see Orgy Card). If it is the reshuffle Orgy card, reshuffle all vote of confidence cards not removed from the game.

A player may look at their own face-down influence cards at any time. A player may have a maximum of **5 influence cards** on their side of a patrician group. The combined total for both players on any group may not exceed **8 cards**.

### Passive Turn
If a player cannot add influence cards to any group (no influence cards, or player and total maximums are reached on every group) and has no action card to change the situation, the player must be passive. On a passive turn: discard (face-up) up to 5 cards from your hand, then refill your hand to 5 cards by drawing from either deck. The player does **not** draw a vote of confidence card.

If a player has no more influence cards in hand or influence deck, they may no longer play; their opponent continues taking turns normally until the game ends.

### Special Vote of Confidence (8-Card Rule)
After a player finishes playing influence cards (before refilling hand), if any patrician group has a combined total of 8 influence cards from both players, a special vote of confidence is called immediately for that group.

## Actions

### Action Cards (13 per player, 6 types)
Each player may play at most one action card per turn (before, between, or after playing influence cards, but before drawing).

- **Assassination (4 each):** Remove one of your opponent's **face-up** influence cards from the group of your choice. The card is placed on your opponent's discard pile. Cannot be used if opponent has no face-up influence cards.
- **Spy (2 each):** Search your opponent's hand, select one card, and remove it from the game (placed on their discard pile). Your opponent then draws a card from either their influence deck or action deck to replace it.
- **Castling (2 each — Roman or Egyptian):** Redistribute all your influence cards from two of your own groups face-down. Pick up all your influence cards from both groups and distribute them face-down as you choose across those two groups. You may place no cards on one group. You may not place more than 5 on a group or exceed the 8-card combined maximum. At least one card must be on at least one of the two groups when you play the card.
- **Scout (2 each):** Turn all influence cards from one of your opponent's groups face-up. The cards remain face-up until a subsequent action changes that. Your opponent must have at least one face-down card in the selected group.
- **Wrath of God (1 each):** Remove all influence cards (both yours and your opponent's) from one patrician group. Cards are placed in the respective players' discard piles. At least one card must be on the group to play this card.
- **Veto (2 each — Caesar's or Cleopatra's):** When your opponent plays an action card, play this card to prevent it. Both cards are discarded. You may then draw one card from either your influence or action deck. Veto cannot be used against a veto.

### Vote of Confidence

**Triggering:** A vote is called when (a) the drawn vote card names a patrician group, or (b) the combined influence on a group reaches 8 cards.

**Resolving:**
1. All face-down influence cards for that group are turned face-up.
2. Each player sums their influence cards on that side.
3. **Philosopher cards (value "P"):** A philosopher card has a value of 0, but it "reinterprets" the result — the player with the **smaller** sum wins the vote. See Philosopher Rules below for ties and multiple philosopher cards.
4. The player with the higher (or lower, if philosopher is in play) sum **wins** the voice of one patrician card from that group (takes the topmost card).
5. The **winning player** discards their **highest**-numbered influence card from that group. The **losing player** discards their **lowest**-numbered influence card from that group. Philosopher cards are also discarded per the philosopher rules.
6. All remaining influence cards for the group stay face-up; play continues.
7. If players are **tied** in influence (and no philosopher is present), neither wins. All cards remain face-up.
8. When the last patrician card is taken from a group, all remaining influence cards on that group are removed to their owners' discard piles.

### Philosopher Card Rules

- A philosopher has value 0; the player with the **smaller total wins** the vote. The losing player (higher total) discards their highest influence card; the winning player (philosopher holder) discards the philosopher card.
- If only philosopher cards are on both sides, no decision is made; philosopher cards remain face-up.
- If equal philosopher cards are on both sides (1 vs. 1, or 2 vs. 2), they cancel out and the player with the **higher** numbered influence wins normally. Both philosopher cards are discarded with the appropriate numbered cards.
- If one player has 1 philosopher card and the other has 2, the player with **smaller** influence wins. All philosopher cards and appropriate numbered cards are discarded.
- If one player has 2 philosopher cards and the other has none, it is resolved as if the player had only 1 philosopher card.
- If both players tie with philosopher cards, all cards remain face-up and no patrician is won.

### Orgy Card

The vote of confidence deck contains three Orgy cards. When an Orgy card is drawn, no vote of confidence is called. One of the three Orgy cards is a "reshuffle" card: when drawn, all vote of confidence cards not removed from the game are shuffled back into a new deck.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Game End
The game ends when any of the following occur:
1. All patrician cards have been won.
2. Both players have no more influence cards (in hand or deck).
3. Neither player can play on any group due to maximums, and neither has nor can draw action cards to change the situation.

### Scoring
- Each patrician card won = **1 VP**
- Majority in a patrician group = **1 VP** (based on total group size; unclaimed cards not counted)
- All patricians won in a group = **1 VP** (in addition to majority)
- Achieve secret influence bonus (win at least 3 cards from the group on your bonus card) = **2 VP**

*Example: All 5 Quaestors won + influence bonus for Quaestors = 5 (cards) + 1 (majority) + 1 (all) + 2 (bonus) = 9 VP.*

**The player with the most VP wins. Ties are broken by the player who won the most patrician cards.**

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Face-down cards may be placed one per active turn (as part of the 1 face-down option). Two face-up cards may be placed on the same or different groups.
- You can only influence one group per turn with a single face-down card; two face-up cards may go to 1 or 2 groups.
- Action cards may be played in addition to influence cards on the same active turn.
- A player may look at their own face-down cards at any time.
- Player maximum: 5 influence cards per side per group. Combined maximum: 8 total per group.
- When a group is emptied of patrician cards, subsequent vote cards drawn for that group are removed from the game (placed in the box).

## Player Reference

**Patrician Groups:**

| Group | Cards | Majority at |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Senators | 5 | 3+ cards won |
| Praetors | 5 | 3+ cards won |
| Quaestors | 5 | 3+ cards won |
| Censors | 3 | 2+ cards won |
| Aedilis | 3 | 2+ cards won |

**Active Turn Order:**
1. (Optional) Play 1 action card
2. (Required) Play 1 face-down card on one group OR 2 face-up cards on 1–2 groups
3. (Required) Refill hand to 5 cards
4. (Required) Draw top vote of confidence card

**Hand size:** 5 cards (refill to 5 after each active turn). **Group limits:** 5 per side; 8 combined.

**Vote resolution:** Winner takes 1 patrician card, discards their **highest** card; loser discards their **lowest** card.
