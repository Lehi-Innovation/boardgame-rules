## Overview

Alien Frontiers is a dice-placement, worker-placement game where players colonize an alien planet (Planet Maxwell). Players roll dice (representing ships) and assign them to orbital facilities to gather resources, build new ships, and establish colonies. Territory control bonuses come from placing colonies in regions on the planet. The player with the most victory points when any player places their last colony wins.

## Components

- 1 Game board (planet with territories and orbital facilities)
- Custom dice in player colors (ships)
- Colony tokens per player (6 for 4-player, 7 for 3-player, 8 for 2-player)
- Fuel tokens (orange), Ore tokens (gray)
- Alien Tech cards (three displayed face-up near the Alien Artifact at all times)
- Relic Ship (clear die, starts on Burroughs Desert territory)
- Territory bonus tiles
- Score track board and scoring rockets

## Setup

1. Place board centrally. Place the scoring track board nearby; each player places their scoring rocket on 0. Place the Relic Ship (clear die) on the Burroughs Desert territory.
2. Place Territory tiles face-up on matching territories.
3. Shuffle Alien Tech cards; deal three face-up near the Alien Artifact (the alien tech stock). Deal one Alien Tech card face-up to each player. Place remaining cards as a draw pile.
4. Place field generator counters near the board.
5. Each player chooses a color, takes 3 ships (dice) and places them on the Shipyard Maintenance Bay area. Take colony tokens: 6 each for 4-player, 7 each for 3-player, 8 each for 2-player. Return unused tokens to the box.
6. Roll for starting player (highest roll goes first). Compensating resources for turn order: player 2 receives 1 fuel; player 3 receives 1 ore; player 4 receives 1 fuel and 1 ore. (2-player: player 2 receives 1 fuel. 3-player: player 2 receives 1 fuel, player 3 receives 1 ore.)

## Turn Structure

1. **Gather and Roll Fleet:** Gather all your ships from the board and roll them.
2. **Use Alien Tech Cards and Assign Fleet (in any order):** You may use Alien Tech cards at any time during your turn. You must dock all your ships at orbital facilities if legally possible; ships that cannot be legally docked go to the Maintenance Bay. You may only use one discard power from Alien Tech cards per turn.
3. **Earn Benefits:** Gain resources, build ships, place colonies, or take other facility effects immediately (unless noted otherwise). Costs are paid to the stocks.
4. **End of Turn:** Leave ships docked until your next turn. If you have more than 8 total resource tokens, return excess of your choice to the stocks.
5. **Victory Check:** Scoring is tracked continuously. The game ends as soon as one player places their last colony.

## Actions

### Orbital Facilities

| Facility | Dice Required | Effect |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| Solar Converter | Any dice (up to 8 ports) | Gain fuel = half each die's value, round up per die |
| Lunar Mine | Each new die ≥ highest die already docked (up to 5 ports) | Gain 1 ore per die docked |
| Orbital Market | Matching pair (equal value) | Pay fuel equal to docked die value to receive 1 ore; may trade multiple times |
| Colonist Hub | Any die(s), one track per player | Advance colony token 1 circle per die docked (7 circles total); launch when reaching circle 7 by paying 1 fuel + 1 ore |
| Colony Constructor | Matching triple (3 equal dice) + pay 3 ore | Place 1 colony immediately |
| Terraforming Station | One die showing 6 + pay 1 fuel + 1 ore | Place 1 colony immediately; the docked die is returned to stock at start of your next turn |
| Shipyard | Matching pair (equal value) | Build 1 new ship; costs: 4th ship = 1 fuel + 1 ore; 5th = 2 fuel + 2 ore; 6th = 3 fuel + 3 ore |
| Raiders' Outpost | Sequential straight (3 consecutive values) | Steal 4 resources (any mix from any players) OR 1 Alien Tech card from one player |
| Alien Artifact | Any die(s); cycling is optional | Each die may cycle the 3 displayed Alien Tech cards; claim 1 card by docking ships totaling 8+ (count resets per card claimed) |

### Alien Tech Cards

Provide ongoing bonuses, one-time effects, or special abilities that modify dice placement rules, resource generation, or colony placement. You may not possess more than one copy of any card. Most cards have a fuel-cost power (usable once per turn) and a discard power (only one discard per turn across all your cards). Cards may be used immediately after acquiring them (except the Resource Cache, which cannot be used on the turn you acquire it).

### Territory Control

When you place a colony, you choose which territory. The player with the most colonies in a territory controls it and gains that territory's bonus (extra die manipulation, resource generation, etc.).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Scoring is tracked continuously on the score track; it is a snapshot of the current state, not cumulative totals.

**Victory Points:**
- 1 VP per colony token on a territory
- 1 VP per territory you control (more colonies there than any other single player)
- 1 VP for possessing the Alien City card
- 1 VP for possessing the Alien Monument card
- 1 VP for controlling the territory with the Positron Field

**Game End:** The game ends immediately when any player places their last colony. All players then tally their VPs; the player with the most VPs wins.

**Tiebreakers (in order):** most revealed Agenda cards (if used) → most Alien Tech cards → most ore → most fuel → play again.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- You must dock all ships if legally possible; ships that cannot be legally docked go to the Maintenance Bay (no benefit). Maintenance Bay ships cannot be moved by Alien Tech cards.
- Dice placed at a facility must meet the exact requirements (matching pairs must be equal value, straights must be three consecutive values, etc.).
- Alien Tech cards may override standard placement rules. Each Alien Tech card may be used only once per turn (fuel power once; only one discard per turn across all your cards).
- The Relic Ship is a colorless die that can be purchased for 1 fuel + 1 ore by the player controlling Burroughs Desert; it otherwise behaves as a normal ship, but when it would return to the ship stock, it returns to the Burroughs Desert territory instead.
- If two or more players are tied for the most colonies in a territory, no one controls it and no one gets the bonus.
- Building a new ship gives you an additional die starting on your next turn (placed in the Maintenance Bay until gathered).
- Terraforming Station: the docked die is consumed (returned to stock at start of your next turn). You cannot use it if doing so would leave you with fewer than 3 ships of your color.
- Resource limit: no more than 8 total resource tokens at end of turn; excess returned to stocks.

## Player Reference

**Turn Flow:** Gather + Roll fleet → Use Alien Tech cards / Assign ships to facilities (any order) → Earn benefits → End turn (discard excess resources above 8)

**Colony Placement Methods:**
- Colonist Hub: dock any ships to advance a colony along 7-circle track; launch from circle 7 by paying 1 fuel + 1 ore
- Colony Constructor: 3 equal-value dice + 3 ore → place 1 colony immediately
- Terraforming Station: 1 die showing 6 + 1 fuel + 1 ore → place 1 colony; that die is consumed

**Victory Points:** 1 per colony on territory; 1 per territory controlled; 1 each for Alien City / Alien Monument cards; 1 for controlling territory with Positron Field

**Resource Uses:** Build ships at Shipyard (matching pair + fuel/ore by ship number); trade at Orbital Market (matching pair, pay die value in fuel for 1 ore); Alien Tech card costs vary

**Shipyard Costs:** 4th ship = 1F+1O; 5th = 2F+2O; 6th = 3F+3O

**Solar Converter:** Fuel = half each die value (round up per die)

**Lunar Mine:** 1 ore per die; each new die must be ≥ highest die already docked

**Raiders' Outpost:** Straight (3 sequential dice); steal 4 resources or 1 Alien Tech card; a higher-value sequence can bump a lower one
