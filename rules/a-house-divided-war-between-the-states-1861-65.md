## Overview

A House Divided is a strategic-level wargame of the American Civil War (1861-1865) for two players. One player commands the Union (North) and the other the Confederacy (South). Using point-to-point movement on a map of the eastern United States, players maneuver armies, fight battles, and attempt to capture key cities to achieve victory. The game features simple rules but deep strategic decisions.

## Components

- 1 mapboard (eastern United States with point-to-point connections)
- Cardboard unit counters (infantry, cavalry) for Union and Confederate forces
- Dice
- Rules booklet with multiple scenarios

## Setup

Per scenario (1861 Campaign Game is the standard). Place units on designated starting locations as specified in the scenario setup charts. The Union player sets up first, then the Confederate player.

## Turn Structure

Each game turn represents approximately one month (or two in winter). The Union has the first Player Turn each game turn, followed by the Confederate Player Turn. Each Player Turn consists of exactly four segments conducted in this order:

1. **Movement** — The active player rolls a die to determine how many Marches (box activations) are available, then moves units.
2. **Combat** — Resolve battles where opposing forces occupy the same location.
3. **Promotions** — Eligible units may be promoted.
4. **Recruitment** — The active player recruits new Militia units.

## Actions

### Movement (4.0)
- To move, the active player rolls one six-sided die. The number rolled is the number of Marches available that turn. If a 1 is rolled, the player receives 2 Marches instead.
- A March consists of activating a box containing friendly units; any or all units in that activated box may then move.
- No unit may participate in more than two Marches in a single turn.
- Infantry: move 1 box by road or unfriendly railroad, or 2 boxes by river or friendly railroad per March.
- Cavalry: move 2 boxes by road and/or railroad (friendly or unfriendly), or 2 boxes by river per March.
- **Cavalry Jump Moves (4.4):** Cavalry making a two-box March may move through a box containing enemy units (with restrictions).
- **Union Sea Movement (4.6):** Union units can move by sea between port boxes; each unit moved counts as one March.
- **Union Naval Invasions (4.7):** If the Union rolls a 6 for Marches, units starting in Washington D.C. may move to any Confederate-controlled port.
- **Union Rail Movement (4.9, optional):** Union units may move three boxes per March if entirely along friendly rail lines.
- **Entrenchment (4.3):** Units may use a March activation to entrench at their location rather than move, gaining defensive bonuses.
- **First Turn Restriction (4.1):** On the first game turn, neither player rolls for movement; each side automatically receives two Marches.
- **Potomac River Restriction (4.2):** Confederate units may never move along the Potomac River; only the Union may use it.

### Combat (5.0)
- Battles occur when opposing forces occupy the same location at the end of a player's Movement Segment.
- The player who moved into the box is the attacker; the other is the defender. The defender fires first each Battle Round.
- Each unit fires once per Battle Round by rolling a die; if the result equals or is less than that unit's Combat Value, the targeted enemy unit is hit.
- A hit flips a unit to its reduced side; a hit on an already-reduced unit eliminates it.
- **Battle rounds** continue until one side retreats or is eliminated.
- **Die Roll Modifiers:**
  - Entrenchment: subtract 1 from the firing unit's Combat Value when firing at an entrenched unit.
  - Crack Infantry: subtract 1 from the firing unit's Combat Value when firing at a Crack Infantry unit.
  - Attacking across rivers: during the defender's first two Battle Rounds only, the defender's Combat Values are increased by 1 when firing at units that attacked across the river.
- **Retreat (5.4):** Beginning with the second Battle Round, either player may retreat all his units to an adjacent connected box (with restrictions), ending the battle.
- **Reinforcing battles (5.5):** Beginning with the second Battle Round, both sides may bring in one unit per adjacent connected box per round.
- **Cavalry withdrawal (5.6):** If the defending player has only Cavalry units in a box when the attacker designates it for battle, those Cavalry units may withdraw before any Battle Rounds are conducted.

### Recovery (5.7)
- After a player's Battle Segment is over, all units that were flipped to their reduced (red number) sides are automatically returned to their full-strength (white number) sides.
- **Refitting the Army (5.71, optional):** Units do not recover automatically; instead, a player must spend a Recruitment Point or a March to recover one reduced unit.

### Capturing and Controlling Boxes (8.0)
- A player captures a box by having at least one unit there at the end of his friendly Combat Segment.
- Passing through a box during movement does NOT capture it; control only changes at the end of a player's Combat Segment.
- If a player completely vacates a captured enemy-color or tan box, a Control marker is placed there to maintain ownership (exception: Cavalry units alone cannot hold a box once they vacate it).

### Recruitment (7.0)
- Each side rolls one die (a roll of 1 is treated as 2) to determine Recruitment Points, then spends them to place Militia units from the Recruitment Pool into friendly-controlled Recruitment Cities.
- **Maximum Army Size (7.1):** The sum of all controlled Recruitment City shield values of a player's color determines the maximum number of units that player may have in play.
- **Union Army Drafts (7.2):** Four additional Union Militia Infantry units are added to the Recruitment Pool at the start of the 04 (April) Game Turn in each of 1862, 1863, and 1864.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Victory Conditions (10.0)
Victory is checked continuously and triggers immediately when conditions are met.

- **Union Victory:** The Union player wins immediately upon simultaneously controlling all seven specific Confederate Recruitment Cities with a Recruitment Value of 2 or 3 (New Orleans, Charleston, Wilmington, Atlanta, Memphis, and two others listed in the rules).

- **Confederate Victory:** The Confederate player wins immediately by achieving any one of the following:
  1. Capturing Washington D.C.
  2. Capturing sufficient Recruitment Cities to make the Confederate Army Maximum Size greater than the Union's Army Maximum Size.
  3. Surviving to the end of Game Turn 40 without the Union achieving its victory condition (Confederate wins by default).

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Kentucky Neutrality (4.12, optional):** Kentucky starts neutral; special rules restrict entry until certain conditions are met.
- **Fort Monroe (4.71, optional):** If Confederates control Fort Monroe, the Union may not conduct Naval Invasions against Yorktown or Norfolk.
- **Crack Infantry (4.8, optional):** Crack Infantry units move like Cavalry units (up to two boxes by road or railroad per March).
- **Combat Experience (6.1, optional):** Units that survive battles may be promoted; Veteran-to-Crack promotion requires being a victorious survivor.
- **Confederate Replacement Training (6.2, optional):** Confederate player rolls during Promotion Segment; on a 1 or less, he may promote two units instead of one.
- **Draft Riots of 1863 (7.3, optional):** The four 1863 drafted Militia Infantry units enter the Recruitment Pool one per month (April through July) rather than all at once.
- Multiple scenarios: 1861 Campaign, 1862–64 scenarios, short game options.
- **Extended Game (9.1, optional):** By mutual consent, the game length may be extended beyond 06 (June) 1865 to Game Turn 45.
