# Solstis

## Overview

Solstis is a tile-capture game for 1-2 players in which you explore a mountain, assembling land tiles into a panorama. On each turn you play a tile from your hand to capture a matching tile from a shared display. Completing 2×2 squares in your panorama earns mystical Spirit tiles. The player with the most stars (earned from panorama size, lit fires, and spirits) wins.

**Goal:** Prove your mettle by getting the highest star count through:
- **Exploring the mountain** — build the biggest panorama section possible.
- **Encountering spirits** — each spirit earns at least 1 star (beware the Evil Spirit).
- **Lighting fires on peaks** — create a path of tiles from the valley to fire icons.

## Components

- 48 Land tiles (each shows a number 1–7 in a colored box; the number = column, the box color = row/landscape type)
- 12 Rainbow tiles (wild cards)
- 15 Spirit tiles (plain/brown background = immediate effect; blue background = end-game effect)
- 1 player aid (shows the complete panorama layout)

## Setup

1. Place all 48 Land tiles face down in a shuffled heap; set aside as the **Land draw pile**.
2. Draw 6 tiles face down and form a line in the center — this is the **display**.
3. Draw 6 more tiles and place 1 face up on each face-down tile (6 stacks of 2, top face up).
4. Each player draws 3 tiles as their **starting hand** (keep hidden). 30 tiles remain in the draw pile.
5. Shuffle the 15 Spirit tiles into a face-down **Spirit draw pile**. *First few games: remove the Evil Spirit tile.*
6. Place Rainbow tiles in a supply pile.
7. Keep the player aid visible to both players.

Decide who goes first. Play alternates.

## Turn Structure

Each turn has four steps, performed in order:

### Step 1 — Select a Tile from Your Hand
Choose one Land tile from your hand and place it face up in the middle of the play area.

### Step 2 — Check for Capture

**Capture possible:** Your tile can capture a face-up display tile if it shares the same **column** (same number in the number box) OR the same **row** (same box color and same landscape type). If one or more display tiles qualify, you **must** capture exactly one. Take both your played tile and the captured tile and place them in your panorama (using the player aid to find the correct positions). If the captured display tile had a face-down tile beneath it, immediately flip that tile face up.

**No capture — Second Chance:** If your played tile matches nothing in the display, add it to the display, then **draw one tile from the Land draw pile**, flip it face up, and check if it can capture using the normal rules.
- If the Second Chance tile can capture: capture one display tile; place both tiles in your panorama.
- If the Second Chance tile also cannot capture: add it to the display too, and **take 1 Rainbow tile**, placing it orthogonally adjacent to any Land tile already in your panorama.

### Step 3 — Check for Spirits
If you completed one or more **2×2 squares** of orthogonally adjacent tiles in your panorama this turn, you encounter one Spirit (only one, even if multiple squares were formed in the same turn — note: the same Land tile can contribute to different squares):

- Either draw the top 2 tiles from the Spirit draw pile, choose 1, and place the other face up beside the Spirit draw pile (forming a face-up collection).
- Or choose any tile from the face-up Spirit collection.

Place the chosen Spirit tile at the center of one of the 2×2 squares formed this turn.

### Step 4 — Draw a New Land Tile
Draw 1 tile from the Land draw pile to restore your hand. Then the next player takes their turn.

## Actions

### Capture (Step 2, capture possible)
Play a hand tile that matches a face-up display tile by column number OR row color/landscape. Take one matching display tile. Place both the played tile and the captured tile in their correct positions in your panorama.

### Second Chance (Step 2, no capture)
When your played tile cannot capture: add it to the display, draw one tile from the draw pile, and attempt to capture with it. On success, proceed as a normal capture. On failure, add the drawn tile to the display and take a Rainbow tile.

### Place a Rainbow Tile
When you earn a Rainbow tile (from a failed Second Chance or a Spirit effect), place it orthogonally adjacent to an existing Land tile in your panorama. Rainbow tiles are wild cards that can stand in for any Land tile position.

**Rainbow blocked by a Spirit:** If a Spirit tile covers an intersection that overlaps a Rainbow's space, the Rainbow is "blocked." When you later acquire the Land tile that belongs in the Rainbow's position, keep the Rainbow in place and return the Land tile to the box (out of play).

**Rainbow free:** If the Rainbow is not covered by a Spirit, when you later acquire the matching Land tile for that position, move the Rainbow to any other valid position in your panorama and place the Land tile in the vacated spot.

### Encounter a Spirit (Step 3)
Triggered by completing a 2×2 square. Choose one Spirit from the draw pile (draw 2, keep 1) or the face-up collection. Place it at the center of the completed square.

- **Plain/brown background:** Apply the effect immediately upon placing.
- **Blue background:** The effect triggers at end of game scoring.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Count stars at end of game. Most stars wins. On a tie, players share the win (replay to break it if desired).

### 1. Biggest Panorama
Count every Land tile in your **single largest contiguous section** of the panorama. Each Land tile = **1 star**. Rainbow tiles in that section = **0 stars**.

### 2. Fires Lit on Peaks
Some Land tiles have Fire icons. A fire is "lit" if there is a continuous orthogonal path of tiles connecting it to any tile in the **bottom row** (tiles with a red number box). Each lit fire = **1 star**.

### 3. Spirits Encountered
Each Spirit tile = **1 star**, plus any bonus stars from Spirit effects (blue background). The Evil Spirit overrides this — see Special Rules.

*Scoring example: 9-tile panorama section with 1 Rainbow = 8 stars. 1 lit fire = 1 star. 3 Spirits (Marmot + Butterfly + Evil-Spirit-covered Spirit) where Evil Spirit cancels one = 2 stars from Spirits plus Butterfly's bonus. Total: 12 stars.*

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Evil Spirit
When you draw the Evil Spirit, you are forced to keep **both** the Evil Spirit and the other Spirit you drew at the same time. Place the normal Spirit on your panorama as usual; keep the Evil Spirit in front of your panorama (not on it).

- The next time you (or your opponent) encounter a new Spirit, give the Evil Spirit to your opponent. It passes back and forth each time a Spirit is encountered.
- At end of game, the player holding the Evil Spirit must cover one of their own Spirits with it (chosen by the opponent). The Evil Spirit and the covered Spirit each earn **0 stars**.

### End of Game
When the Land draw pile is empty, stop proceeding to Step 4 (no more drawing). From this point, a player may only take a turn if they can capture a display tile with one of the tiles in their hand. If you cannot capture, skip your turn. The game ends when **neither player can capture** a display tile. Then calculate scores.

### Multiple Squares in One Turn
If you complete multiple 2×2 squares in a single turn, you still encounter only **one** Spirit. A single Land tile can form part of multiple squares simultaneously.

### Display Gaps
When both tiles in a display stack are taken, that slot remains empty for the rest of the game.

### Solo Mode
Solo mode is included with the game (noted on box as 1-2 players). Specific solo rules are not included in the main rulebook — refer to the separate solo rules insert.

## Player Reference

### Turn Order
1. Play a tile from hand.
2. Capture if match (column or row) — or Second Chance → capture or Rainbow.
3. Spirit if 2×2 square formed.
4. Draw 1 tile from draw pile.

### Capture Conditions
| Match Type | Condition |
|---|---|
| Same column | Same number in number box |
| Same row | Same box color AND same landscape type |

### Spirit Tile Effects

**Plain/brown background — immediate:**
| Effect |
|---|
| Take a Rainbow tile and place it in your panorama |
| Add a tile from your hand to your panorama; draw 2 tiles at end of turn |
| Add a tile drawn at random from the Land draw pile to your panorama |
| Immediately draw a Land tile (hand size increases by 1 for rest of game) |
| Take a random tile from opponent's hand for your panorama; opponent draws 1 replacement |
| Take any face-up display tile of your choice and add it to your panorama |
| After finishing your turn, take another full turn (2 turns total) |

**Blue background — end of game:**
| Effect |
|---|
| +1 star per Spirit with a plain background in your panorama |
| +1 star per Rainbow tile in your panorama |
| Before scoring, add all remaining hand tiles to your panorama |
| +1 star per separate panorama section (including single-tile sections) |
| +1 star per Spirit with a blue background in your panorama, except this one |
| +1 star per Fire symbol that has NOT been lit |
| +1 star per lit Fire symbol |

### Scoring Summary
- Each Land tile in largest contiguous panorama section: 1 star
- Each lit fire (path to bottom row): 1 star
- Each Spirit tile: 1 star + any blue Spirit bonuses
- Evil Spirit + covered Spirit: 0 stars each
- Rainbow tiles: 0 stars
