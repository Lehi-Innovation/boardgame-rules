## Overview

A standalone game in the Azul series. Over **4 rounds**, players draft hexagonal tiles and garden expansions from a shared display and arrange them around their fountain board to build the most beautiful garden. Each drafted "hexagon" carries one of **6 patterns** and one of **6 colors**; placing a tile costs other tiles from your storage. Points are scored in an interim scoring at the end of each round (driven by a rotary wheel) and in a large **final scoring** that rewards groups of matching patterns and colors. Most points wins.

## Components

- 4 Garden boards (A) — one per player; shows the placement cost of each of the 6 patterns along the top
- 4 Storages (B) — personal holding area (12 spaces for tiles + 2 spaces for garden expansions)
- 4 Fountain boards (C) — placed in the middle of a player's garden
- Jokers (D / P) — each player starts with 3; more are gained in play (supply is unlimited — use substitutes if you run out)
- 1 Scoring board (E) — double-sided (normal side / variant side); holds the rotary wheel and evaluation track
- 1 Rotary wheel (F) — indicates the 3 patterns/colors scored each round
- 1 Evaluation marker (G) — moves down the evaluation track during final scoring
- 4 Scoring markers (H) — one per player, in player color
- 1 Bag (I)
- 108 Colored tiles (J) — hexagons showing a pattern + color
- 36 Garden expansions — hexagon-shaped mini-boards providing 5 or 7 extra tile spaces
- 1 Shield token (M) — marks the top of the supply
- 1 First player marker (N)
- 1 Tower (O) — holds discarded tiles
- Point tokens (Q) — used to track scores beyond one lap of the track

## Setup

1. Each player takes a color and receives: 1 garden board, 1 storage placed beside it, 1 fountain board placed in the middle of their garden, and **3 jokers** placed on empty storage spaces.
2. Place the scoring board in the middle of the table with the **central icon side visible** (this is the normal game; see Special Rules for the variant). Assemble the rotary wheel on its starting position. Put the evaluation marker at the **top** of the evaluation track.
3. Each player places their scoring marker on the **15th space** of the scoring track.
4. Fill the bag with all **108 colored tiles**.
5. Shuffle the **36 garden expansions** and build **4 face-down round stacks**, sized by player count:
   - 2 players: **5** garden expansions per stack
   - 3 players: **7** garden expansions per stack
   - 4 players: **8** garden expansions per stack
   Create the **display area** by placing the 1st round stack in the middle of the table. Place the other 3 round stacks in a row above the scoring board. Fill the **top** garden expansion of the 1st round stack with exactly **4 tiles** drawn randomly from the bag (that expansion's own spaces are not used yet).
6. Build a **supply** next to the stack row from all remaining garden expansions and put the **shield token** on top of it.
7. Place the first player marker in the display area.
8. Place the tower next to the scoring board and the joker reserve next to it.
9. Set the point tokens aside for scoring.

Return unused garden boards, storages, fountain boards, and scoring markers to the box.

## Turn Structure

The game lasts **4 rounds**. Each round has 3 phases:

- **Phase 1 — Players' Actions:** starting with the first player and continuing clockwise, players take turns until **all players have passed**. (For the very first round, the youngest player starts.)
- **Phase 2 — Scoring:** performed 4 times per game (once each round).
- **Phase 3 — Prepare the Next Round:** performed at the end of rounds 1–3 only (not after the 4th round).

On your turn, choose exactly one action: **(A)** Acquire tiles and garden expansions, **(B)** Place a tile, **(C)** Place a garden expansion, or **(D)** Pass.

### Key terms

- **Pattern:** one of 6 designs, each with a fixed **value 1–6** that is *both* its placement cost *and* its point worth. The value is hinted by the art (tree = 1, bird = 2 [two wings], butterfly = 3 [three butterflies], flower = 4 [four petals], plus two more patterns worth 5 and 6). The 6 patterns are distributed evenly across the 6 colors.
- **Hexagon:** any pattern + color combination — found on a tile or on a garden expansion.
- **Group:** two or more adjacent hexagons that share **the same pattern OR the same color, but not both**. One hexagon can belong to a pattern-group and a color-group at the same time.

## Actions

### A) Acquire tiles and garden expansions

Choose a single pattern **or** a single color from the display area, then take, according to one option only:

- **ALL** tiles and garden expansions of the chosen **pattern** (across different colors), **OR**
- **ALL** tiles and garden expansions of the chosen **color** (across different patterns).

If two or more of the selected hexagons are **identical** (same pattern *and* color), you take only **one** of them (your choice). Everything acquired goes into your storage. Storage holds **12 tiles + 2 garden expansions**; if the full selection won't fit, **you cannot make that selection**.

After acquiring, if you **took at least one tile from the current round stack**:
- Move the **topmost garden expansion** off the round stack (along with any tiles left on it) and set it beside the stack to **extend the display area**.
- Then **refill** the new top garden expansion of the round stack with exactly **4 tiles** from the bag.

Also, any garden expansions in the display area that are now **empty** (no tiles left on them) are turned **face up**. A face-up garden expansion shows a **pavilion** in its center plus a hexagon, and becomes available for players to acquire (by matching that hexagon's pattern or color).

### B) Place a tile

Select a tile from your storage and pay its cost to place it in your garden.

**Paying a cost:** every pattern has a placement cost (its value 1–6, shown along the top of your garden board). Pay by discarding that number of hexagons **from your storage, including the tile you are placing**. The discard set must follow one option:
- all discarded hexagons share the **same pattern but different colors**, **OR**
- all discarded hexagons share the **same color but different patterns**.

Discarded **tiles** go into the tower; discarded **garden expansions** go face down to the **bottom of the supply**.

**Paying with jokers:** if you lack (or don't want to use) enough matching hexagons, you may complete payment with jokers. You must minimally have the actual hexagon you want to place (**a joker can never be placed in your garden**); for each *other* hexagon the cost requires, you may discard **one joker** instead. Discarded jokers return to the supply.

**Placement rules:** place the tile on a free space of your garden (not occupied by a hexagon or garden element), either:
- with **no adjacent hexagons**, **OR**
- adjacent to one or more hexagons, where the placed tile's **pattern or color matches at least one** adjacent hexagon.

You may **not** create or extend a group that would then contain **2 identical hexagons** (same pattern and same color) — this applies whether the duplication is with the tile you place or via connecting existing hexagons together.

**Surrounding an element:** if you completely surround a **fountain, statue, bench, or pavilion**, you immediately gain jokers from the supply based on the element and place them in your storage:
- Fountain → **1 joker**
- Statue → **2 jokers**
- Bench → **2 jokers**
- Pavilion → **3 jokers**

Jokers that don't fit in your storage are **lost**.

### C) Place a garden expansion

Garden expansions add **5 or 7** extra tile spaces. Either:
- select a garden expansion **from your storage** and pay its cost (like a tile), **OR**
- take a **face-down** garden expansion from the supply (always provides **7 free spaces**) by spending exactly **6 points** on the scoring track (your score cannot go negative).

Place it on a free expansion area around your fountain board, in any orientation, following the **same placement rules** as tiles (adjacency by matching pattern/color, no duplicate hexagons in a group).

### D) Pass

You **may** pass if you don't want to acquire or place any more hexagons, and you **must** pass if you cannot take any other action. When you pass you may discard any number of hexagons from your storage, scoring their values as **minus points**.

The **first player to pass** takes the first player marker and moves their scoring marker **back 1 space**. A player who has passed takes no further turns this phase. Phase 1 ends when all players have passed.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Phase 2 — Round scoring (end of each round)

Starting with the first player, each player scores:
- The rotary wheel shows **3 scored patterns/colors** for the round. For **each hexagon in your garden** whose pattern or color matches one indicated by the wheel, score points equal to that hexagon's **pattern value**. A single hexagon can score **twice** (once for its pattern, once for its color) if both are indicated.
- Then **+1 point for each visible pavilion** in your garden.

The scoring track is continuous; use point tokens to track totals beyond one lap.

### Phase 3 — Prepare next round (after rounds 1–3)

Turn the rotary wheel clockwise to its next position. In the display area, discard all remaining tiles from face-down garden expansions into the tower and return all remaining garden expansions to the supply (face down). Place the next round stack in the display area and fill its top expansion with exactly **4 tiles** from the bag. The holder of the first player marker places it near the next round stack and starts the new round.

### End of the game & Final Scoring

The game ends after all players pass during the **4th round**. A normal end-of-round (Phase 2) scoring happens first, then the **final scoring**:

1. **Empty your storage.** Each remaining **joker** = **+1 point**. Each remaining **tile or garden expansion** = **minus** its pattern value (tree = −1, bird = −2, etc.).
2. Move the evaluation marker to the top of the evaluation track (a blue hexagon). The track lists the **6 colors, then the 6 patterns**. Working top to bottom, evaluate each color, then each pattern.
3. For each evaluation, score every **group of at least 3 hexagons** in your garden matching it. There is no limit to how many groups score per evaluation. A group's value is the **sum of the pattern values** of its hexagons (see the values on the right side of the scoring board).
4. **Bonus: +6 points for each group of exactly 6 hexagons.**

Groups of only 2 hexagons do **not** score (minimum group size for final scoring is 3).

The player with the **most points** wins. On a tie, tied players **share the victory**.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Storage limits are hard.** 12 tile spaces + 2 garden-expansion spaces. A selection that would overflow storage simply cannot be taken. Excess jokers gained from surrounding an element are lost if storage is full.
- **Duplicate-in-selection rule:** when acquiring, identical hexagons in the selection collapse to a single one you keep.
- **Group formation vs. group scoring:** a group *forms* at 2 adjacent matching hexagons (needed for the "no duplicate hexagon in a group" placement restriction), but *scores* only at 3+ hexagons in final scoring.
- **No duplicate hexagons in a group, ever** — you cannot place a tile or expansion if doing so puts two identical (same pattern + color) hexagons in the same group, directly or by bridging.
- **Jokers** are wild only for *paying costs* and are never placed in the garden. Supply is unlimited — substitute if you run out.
- **Tile shortage:** if the bag lacks enough tiles to fill a garden expansion, return **all tiles from the tower** to the bag. If still short, play continues with fewer or no tiles on the stack; in that case any empty garden expansions from the current round stack are placed **face up** in the display area.
- **Buying an expansion with points** costs exactly 6 and can never take your score below 0.
- **Game Variant:** turn the scoring board to its other side (different central icon) and assemble the rotary wheel on that side during setup. All rules are identical except: (1) patterns **score differently in Phase 2**, and (2) **more** bonus points are scored for groups during final scoring.

## Player Reference

**Turn — pick ONE:**
1. **Acquire** — take all hexagons of one chosen pattern (diff. colors) OR one chosen color (diff. patterns); keep one of any duplicates; must fit storage (12 tiles / 2 expansions). If you took a tile from the round stack: slide off the top expansion to extend the display, then refill the stack top with 4 tiles. Turn emptied expansions face up.
2. **Place a tile** — discard hexagons equal to the pattern's value (1–6), all same-pattern/diff-color OR same-color/diff-pattern, including the placed tile; jokers cover extra costs. Place on a free space, alone or matching an adjacent hexagon's pattern/color. Never duplicate a hexagon within a group.
3. **Place a garden expansion** — from storage (pay cost) or face-down from supply for 6 points (7 spaces). Same placement rules.
4. **Pass** — optionally dump storage hexagons for minus points. First to pass: take first-player marker, −1 space.

**Pattern values (cost = points):** tree 1 · bird 2 · butterfly 3 · flower 4 · (pattern) 5 · (pattern) 6.

**Surround rewards (jokers):** fountain 1 · statue 2 · bench 2 · pavilion 3.

**Round scoring (Phase 2):** each garden hexagon matching one of the wheel's 3 patterns/colors scores its pattern value (can score twice); +1 per visible pavilion.

**Final scoring:** remaining jokers +1 each; remaining tiles/expansions minus their value. Then per color (×6) and per pattern (×6): each group of 3+ scores the sum of its hexagons' pattern values; +6 for each group of 6. Most points wins; ties shared.

**Start:** scoring markers on space 15; 3 jokers each; 4 rounds.
