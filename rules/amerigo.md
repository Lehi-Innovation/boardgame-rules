## Overview

Amerigo is a strategy game where players explore islands, build settlements, and collect goods in the New World. The game features a unique cube tower mechanism that determines available actions each round. Players sail ships to discover islands, place landscape tiles to settle them, and trade goods for victory points. After 5 rounds, the player with the most points wins.

## Components

- 1 Game board (ocean grid with islands)
- 1 Cube tower
- Action cubes in 7 colors (each color = different action type)
- Player boards
- Ships, settlement markers
- Landscape tiles in various shapes
- Goods tokens
- Progress tokens
- Victory point track
- Pirate tiles
- Cannons

## Setup

1. Place the game board centrally. Each player takes a player board, ships, and settlement markers.
2. Load the cube tower with an initial set of cubes.
3. Place landscape tiles, goods, and progress tokens in designated areas.
4. Ships are not placed during setup; each player places their ships on the frame the first time they choose the Move Ships action during the game.

## Turn Structure

The game is played over 5 rounds, each with 7 phases. Each phase:

1. Drop cubes of the current phase's color into the cube tower.
2. Cubes that fall through determine which actions are available and the number of action points (AP) for each action.
3. The largest number of cubes of any single color that fell out determines how many AP can be spent on a single available action this phase.
4. Each player takes one action per phase in player order.

## Actions

Actions depend on the color of cubes that emerge from the tower:

| Cube Color | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Blue | Move ships across the ocean |
| Black | Load cannons (advance cannon disc) |
| Red | Plan (take landscape/village tiles from supply into personal supply) |
| Brown | Progress (advance progress disc and collect progress tokens) |
| Green | Build (place tiles from personal supply onto islands) |
| Yellow | Buy production tokens |
| White | Special action (change player order or take another color's action) |

### Exploration and Settlement

- Move ships to reach new islands.
- Use the Plan action (red) to take landscape or village tiles into your personal supply.
- Use the Build action (green) to place tiles from your personal supply onto islands where you have a trading post.
- Complete islands (fill all spaces) for bonus points.

### Production and Goods

- Collect commodity tokens by building over commodity spaces on islands.
- Buy production tokens using the yellow action.
- At final scoring, multiply commodity tokens by production tokens of each type for victory points.

### Pirates

- At the end of each round, pirates attack. Players must use cannons (advanced via the black action) to defend or lose victory points equal to the full pirate strength.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

After 5 rounds, players score:
- **Gold:** 1 VP per gold on the gold track (treasure chests and one-time gold progress tokens are converted to gold first)
- **Goods:** For each type of good, multiply commodity tokens owned by production tokens owned of that type
- **Progress:** VP equal to the highest value reached or passed on the progress track, plus end-game progress token bonuses
- **Special actions:** VP equal to the highest value reached or passed on the special action track
- Victory points from landscape tile placement and completed island bonuses are scored immediately during the game

Highest total wins. In case of a tie, the tied player who is furthest ahead in player order wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- The cube tower is the central randomizer — cubes dropped in may be retained by the tower (caught on internal ledges) and fall out in later rounds, creating unpredictability.
- Players share the same pool of available action cubes each phase.
- Landscape tiles must fit legal positions on islands.
- Progress tokens provide permanent, one-time, or end-game advantages.
- Some islands are "big islands" (20 or more land spaces) and award larger tile scoring values and a treasure chest when completed.

## Player Reference

**Round Structure:** 7 phases per round, 5 rounds total.

**Phase Flow:** Drop cubes → Largest single-color count sets AP limit → All players take one action of an available type per phase.

**Key Actions:** Move ships (blue), Load cannons (black), Plan/take tiles (red), Progress (brown), Build/place tiles (green), Buy production tokens (yellow), Special action (white).
