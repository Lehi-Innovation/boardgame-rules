## Overview

D-Day at Omaha Beach is a solitaire (or cooperative 2-player) wargame simulating the US assault on Omaha Beach on June 6, 1944. The player controls US forces attempting to establish a beachhead against a game-system-controlled German defense. The game covers the first twelve hours of the battle across two scenarios: the "First Waves" (turns 1-16) and the "Extended Game" (turns 17-32). No dice are used; the game is driven by a deck of event cards that control German fire and actions. Each turn represents 15 minutes (early game) or 30 minutes (extended game).

## Components

- 1 mounted game board (22" x 34") depicting Omaha Beach
- 352 die-cut counters
- 1 deck of 55 event cards
- 3 player aid cards
- Rules booklet and campaign analysis

## Setup

1. Place the game board. It shows the beach, pavilion, bluffs, draws (valleys), and inland high ground divided into east and west sectors.
2. Set up German Widerstandsnest (WN) units in their positions as indicated on the map.
3. Prepare the US landing schedule showing which units arrive on which turns and at which beach sectors.
4. Shuffle the event card deck.
5. Place the turn marker on turn 1.

## Turn Structure

**First Waves (Turns 1-16):**

Each turn follows this sequence:
1. **US Amphibious Operations Phase** -- Draw two landing cards (one per sector); land units in Beach Landing Boxes; place newly arriving units in Beach Landing Boxes for next turn.
2. **Event Phase** (skip on Turn 1) -- Draw an event card and implement the event listed for the current turn.
3. **German Fire Phase** -- Draw two fire cards (one per sector); occupied German positions matching the card's colors fire at US units in their fields of fire; apply disruption and step losses; remove disruption from German units whose color appeared.
4. **US Engineer Phase** (skip on Turn 1) -- Place Cleared markers in beach hexes with obstacles cleared by engineers.
5. **US Action Phase** -- Each division may have two units or stacks perform actions; eligible units may also act for free (see Actions section).
6. **End of Turn** -- Move cards to discard pile; reshuffle deck if indicated on the Turn Track; advance Turn marker.

**Extended Game (Turns 17-32):**

The extended game sequence adds a Second Event Phase (after German Fire), replaces US Engineer Phase with a US Engineer & HQ Phase (establish/expand engineer bases and command posts), and expands the US Action Phase to three actions per division. German positions may now perform actions (not just fire) as indicated by action letters on fire cards.

## Actions

**US Amphibious Operations:**
- **Landing Checks:** Draw a landing card per sector; apply the result (from the Landing Table) to all units in that sector's Beach Landing Boxes. Units may arrive safely, be displaced, be delayed, lose steps, or be eliminated.
- **Landing Units:** Move all units in Beach Landing Boxes to their corresponding beach hex, then place newly arriving units from the Turn Track into the appropriate Beach Landing Boxes.

**German Fire:**
- Controlled by fire cards (not event cards). Each fire card shows position colors; all non-disrupted occupied positions of those colors fire at US units in their fields of fire.
- **Fields of Fire:** Each German WN has defined fire dots (intense, steady, sporadic) indicating hexes it can fire into. Fire effectiveness depends on the fire dot type and the US unit's target symbol.
- **Hit Limits (6.31):** A German position may hit a number of US units equal to the number of German units and depth markers in the position per fire. This limit doubles starting Turn 17 in the Extended Game (see Extended Game Additions, 14.11).
- **Disruption (US units, 6.33):** A US unit hit by fire may become disrupted instead of or in addition to losing a step. An already-disrupted unit that incurs another disruption result is NOT further affected (it may still lose a step from a different position in the same phase).
- **Step Loss Limit (6.34):** A given US unit may not lose more than one step in a single German Fire Phase (this limit does not apply in the extended game).
- **German Artillery (Turn 4+):** If the fire card shows an artillery result, check whether the number of undisrupted artillery units in the sector meets the threshold; if so, one US unit with the matching target symbol loses a step.

**US Actions:**
- Each turn, two units or stacks per division may perform one action each. Certain units act for free (do not count against the two-action limit), but a unit may only perform one action per turn regardless:
  - Ranger infantry always act for free.
  - Units with a hero, inspired, climb, climb-cliff, or disrupted marker act for free.
  - HQ units and Generals act for free.
  - Units in command of an HQ or General (stacked with or adjacent at start of Action Phase) act for free.
  - Infantry conducting a self-preservation move (moving to an adjacent beach hex closer to a protective hexside) act for free.
- **Available actions:** Move one hex (all units); Climb a Bluff (infantry, HQs, Generals only); Climb a Cliff (infantry only); Remove disruption (any unit with disrupted marker); Attack; Barrage (tank units only); Naval artillery barrage (naval fire marker only).
- **Stacking Limits:** Maximum units per hex vary by terrain type.

**US Combat Actions:**
- **Attack:** Must include at least one infantry unit adjacent to the German-occupied hex. Other eligible units (tanks, artillery, etc.) may join under specific conditions. Attack strength (US) is compared to German defense strength (modified by terrain). If US units possess all required weapons, use the favorable section of the Attack Table; otherwise use the unfavorable section.
- **Barrage (tank units only):** A tank unit not adjacent to the target may barrage if it (or an observing undisrupted infantry unit) is in the target's field of fire; draw a fire card and consult the Barrage Table. A German position may not be both attacked and barraged in the same Action Phase.
- **Naval Fire:** Naval fire markers (received via event cards) may be expended as part of an attack or as a free Naval Artillery Barrage. Naval barrage places a disrupted marker on the German unit and removes any depth marker; German units are not eliminated by naval barrage.
- **German Withdrawal:** Defeated 352nd Division reinforcement units in German communication withdraw to the Division Reinforcement Box instead of being eliminated. WN units and 716th Division units do not withdraw.

**Extended Game Additions:**
- **German Actions:** German positions may now perform actions (Re-Occupy, Re-Supply, Redeploy, Reinforce, Mortar, Patrol, Artillery Fire, Advance, Ambush) as called for by action letters on fire cards, in addition to or instead of firing.
- **Doubled German Hit Limit (14.11):** Starting Turn 17, the 6.31 hit limit is doubled — a position occupied by a single unit without a depth marker can hit 2 US units per fire (instead of 1), and a position with a unit and a depth marker can hit 4 (instead of 2).
- **Artillery Barrage (US, Turn 17+):** Artillery and SP artillery units may barrage if: in range but not adjacent to target; target is a WN or revealed reinforcement position; unit is in command of an HQ or CP (not a General); an observer (the artillery unit itself or an undisrupted infantry unit) is in the target's field of fire and in command of the same HQ or CP.
- **US Command Posts:** HQ units may convert to CPs (starting Turn 17) to extend command range up to four hexes. CPs do not move but provide free actions to all units within command range.
- **Engineer Bases:** Established by engineer units on the beach; enable non-infantry units to move off the beach and provide traffic management; range increases over time.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Control Definition (for VP purposes, rule 13.22):**
A hex counts as US-controlled for VP purposes only if: (a) it is occupied or controlled by a US unit AND in US communication AND NOT in any German unit's field of fire, including a disrupted German unit's field of fire; OR (b) US communication can be traced to it, German communication cannot, and it is not in a German field of fire (again including disrupted German units). Simply occupying a hex is not enough — it must also be safe from German fire, and a disrupted German unit's field of fire still counts as unsafe for this purpose.

**US Communication (12.3):** A hex is in US communication if a path of hexes of any length can be traced from it to any beach hex, not passing through hexes occupied by or in the field of fire of a German unit (a disrupted German unit's field of fire still blocks the path per 12.31; an empty German position, however, has no field of fire for tracing purposes), nor through rough hexes or across bluff/cliff hexsides.

**Catastrophic Loss:**
- Turns 1–16: If 8 regular infantry units (full-strength counter) from either division have been reduced to one-step, the division suffers catastrophic loss — you lose immediately.
- Turns 17–32: The catastrophic loss threshold increases to 14 infantry units per division.
- Ranger infantry eliminated and non-infantry units do not count toward catastrophic loss. Certain events allow you to remove counters from the loss box, reducing your loss total.

**First Waves Scenario Victory (end of Turn 16, rule 13.2):**
If neither division suffered catastrophic loss by end of Turn 16, count VPs:
- 1 VP per WN position you control. A two-hex WN is worth 2 VP but scores 0 until both hexes are controlled.
- 1 VP per German reinforcement position you control (including positions not marked "VP").
- 5 VPs per draw you control. You control a draw if all its draw hexes south of the anti-tank barrier are in US control.

**Win threshold: 19 or more VPs.**

**Extended Game Victory:**

*First Victory Check — end of Turn 16 (rule 20.2):*
Using the same VP count as rule 13.2:
- 25 or more VPs: Decisive US victory — game ends.
- 11–24 VPs: Continue play through Turn 32.
- 10 or fewer VPs: Decisive US defeat — game ends.

*Second Victory Check — end of Turn 32 (rule 20.3):*
Use these VP awards instead of those in 13.2:
- 1 VP per German reinforcement position controlled in a hex on or adjacent to a major road, or a minor road leading off the south edge (42 total hexes marked with VP symbol).
- 4 VPs per draw opened to US traffic (you control every hex of the draw and engineers have completely cleared German obstacles from the draw).
- 1 VP per non-infantry, non-HQ unit on the high ground at end of play.
- 1 VP per armor or regular infantry step that exits from a lettered exit hex (A–G), if you can trace US communication to that hex at end of play.
- 1 VP per ranger infantry step that exits from exit F or G (communication not required).
- Subtract 1 VP per WN hex you do not control.
- Subtract 1 VP per German unit in the Kampfgruppe Meyer box (not depth markers).

**Win threshold: 40 or more VPs.**

Victory levels (Extended Game end):
- 19 or fewer VPs: Decisive US defeat.
- 20–29 VPs: Operational US defeat.
- 30–39 VPs: Tactical US defeat.
- 40–49 VPs: Tactical US victory (historical result).
- 50–59 VPs: Operational US victory.
- 60 or more VPs: Decisive US victory.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **No Dice:** All randomness comes from the card deck. Fire cards control German fire; landing cards control landing checks; event cards implement turn events.
- **Heroes (11.1):** Created by certain events. A hero provides a weapon "wild card" (stands in for any one weapon requirement, except flanking) when attacking from an adjacent hex, or can increase attack strength by 1. Hero wild card is not cumulative (only one named per attack regardless of hero count). When a hero is killed or sacrificed, the marker flips to "inspired" and remains with the unit — inspired units act for free each turn but do not provide the wild card.
- **Headquarters (11.2):** Each HQ commands all US units in its hex and all adjacent hexes at the start of the Action Phase. Commanded units act for free; units attacking from an adjacent hex while commanded by an HQ are considered to possess a radio. HQs move for free, one hex per turn. Starting Turn 17, HQs may convert to Command Posts.
- **Generals (11.3):** Similar command radius to HQs. Can enable tanks, anti-tank, and anti-air units in command to conduct ranged fire (not artillery units). A General alone in a hex does not block German fire or communication.
- **US Communication (12.3):** During play, US units are NOT affected by being out of communication. Communication only matters at end-of-scenario for VP purposes (see Scoring section).
- **German Communication (12.2):** German positions must trace communication (path to any exit hex A–G, not passing through US-occupied or US-controlled hexes) to receive reinforcements, receive depth markers, withdraw after defeat, or perform certain extended game actions. A disrupted German unit does not fire and, for purposes of firing and hit-limit determination, projects no field of fire (6.4). However, per 12.31, a German position's field of fire extends into all hexes with fire dots emanating from that position even if the occupying unit is disrupted — a disrupted German unit's field of fire still blocks tracing of US communication (an empty German position, by contrast, has no field of fire for this purpose). Per 13.22, a disrupted German unit's field of fire likewise still counts against US control of a hex for VP purposes — a hex in a disrupted German unit's field of fire is NOT safe from German fire for VP scoring.
- **Disrupted US Units (7.7):** A disrupted unit has a disrupted marker; it may perform the "Remove disruption" action (for free, since it counts as having a disrupted marker) to recover. Disruption from a single fire event does not add up — an already-disrupted unit receiving another disruption result is unaffected (but may still lose a step from a separate position). Disrupted units CAN attack (disruption is not a prohibition on attacking per se), but they act as normal units for combat purposes.
- **Beach Obstacles:** Block landing craft; US engineers clear beach obstacle hexes during the US Engineer Phase. All beach obstacles in mid-tide waterline hexes are automatically considered cleared beginning on Turn 24.
- **Terrain:** Bocage terrain provides defensive advantage (German strength modifier). Bluff and cliff hexsides restrict movement and attack eligibility. US low-ground units cannot conduct ranged fire against high-ground German positions unless that position projects a field of fire into at least one beach hex.
- **Concentrated Target (6.35):** A hex with US units totaling 5 or more steps is a concentrated target; all units in it are considered to have the target symbol shown on the German Fire card, regardless of their actual symbol.
- **German Reinforcement Positions:** Positions that receive reinforcement units as called for by event cards. Division Reinforcement Pool units are placed with depth markers; Tactical Pool units are placed without. Positions must be in German communication to receive a unit.
- **Kampfgruppe Meyer (9.5):** A special German reinforcement box. German units placed here subtract from your VP total at end of the Extended Game.
- **Two-Player Cooperative:** One player controls 1st Division (east sector), the other 29th Division (west). A unit that moves one hex across the sector boundary remains under original division control; more than one hex and it comes under the other player's control.
- **Beyond the Beach Scenario (section 21):** Covers Turns 17–32 only; sets up surviving German WNs from the historical position at 1000 hrs. VP thresholds differ (see rule 21.2 and section 20 — win at 40+ VPs using the Turn 32 VP awards, but with adjusted thresholds: 24 or fewer = decisive defeat, 25–34 = operational defeat, 35–44 = tactical defeat, 45–54 = tactical victory, 55–64 = operational victory, 65+ = decisive victory).
- **Optional Historical Variants (sections 22–23):** Effective Allied Bombardment (−10 VP adjustment), First Wave Tanks Land Safely, Rangers Reinforce Pointe du Hoc (+5 VP), Improved German Reaction (+5 VP), German Armor (+1 VP per armor unit that enters). VP adjustments are added/subtracted from the final total at end of game.
