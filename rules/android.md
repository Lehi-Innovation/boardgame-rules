## Overview

Android is a thematic board game of noir investigation set in a futuristic city. Players are detectives investigating a murder while managing their personal lives, pursuing conspiracy leads, and navigating the dark side of a cyberpunk world. Each detective has their own storyline, personal demons, and goals. Players score points for advancing the murder case, uncovering conspiracies, achieving personal goals, and resolving their character's storyline. The player with the most victory points after the investigation period wins.

## Components

- 1 Game board (New Angeles and surrounding areas)
- 5 Detective character sheets with unique storylines
- Murder case sheet and suspect cards
- Evidence tokens (numbered +5 to –4, plus special tokens: surprise witnesses, perjuries, and alibis)
- Conspiracy puzzle pieces
- Twilight cards (separate light and dark decks) and plot cards for each detective
- Event cards
- Favor tokens
- Time track
- Various tokens and markers

## Setup

1. Place game board centrally.
2. Each player selects a detective and takes their character sheet, tip sheet, cardboard figure, twilight cards (two separate light and dark decks), plot cards, special cards, hero markers, vehicle ruler, warrant markers, twilight marker, and NPC status/favor tokens.
3. Set up the murder case: choose a victim, determine suspects.
4. Place evidence tokens in a pool.
5. Prepare conspiracy board.
6. Deal starting plot cards.

## Turn Structure

The game plays over a fixed number of days. Each player receives 6 Time per turn and spends it freely on actions (1 Time each) until Time runs out. There is no fixed sequence of sub-phases within a turn. Available actions include:

- **Move** to a new location
- **Follow up a lead** at your current location
- **Draw or discard a twilight card**
- **Play a light twilight card**
- **Use a location ability**
- **Get a jump on the case** (at the scene of the crime)

## Actions

### Investigation

- Follow leads at locations to gather evidence.
- Place evidence tokens on suspects. Evidence tokens have numeric values; positive values are incriminating and negative values are exonerating.
- The suspect with the highest numeric evidence total (after adjustments for strong/weak files, alibis, surprise witnesses, and perjuries) is found guilty at game end.

### Conspiracy

- Reveal information to draw and place conspiracy puzzle pieces on the board.
- Completing a row, column, or diagonal of the puzzle earns conspiracy tokens worth +4 VP each.
- Puzzle links also modify the VP values of favors, sad endings, and other scoring elements at game end.

### Personal Storyline (Plots and Twilight Cards)

- Each detective has a light twilight card deck (helpful events) and a dark twilight card deck (harmful events). These are separate from plot cards.
- Playing a light card costs 1 Time plus a twilight cost (dark shifting). Playing a dark card costs 0 Time (played on another player's turn) plus a twilight cost (light shifting).
- Plot cards are not played directly — they resolve automatically at the end of day 3 and day 6 of each week based on accumulated good and bad baggage tokens.
- Resolving your personal storyline positively grants VP; negative outcomes lose VP.

### Favors

- Favors are a currency spent to use location abilities or play cards. They have no inherent abilities of their own.
- Gain favors at various locations on the board.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

At game end, players score VP from multiple sources:

- **Guilty hunch correct:** +15 VP
- **Innocent hunch correct:** +5 VP (scored independently from the guilty hunch)
- **Personal Storyline:** Points based on plot ending cards achieved (positive or negative outcomes)
- **Conspiracy tokens:** +4 VP each (earned by completing rows, columns, or diagonals of the puzzle)
- **Haas tokens:** +3 VP each
- **Jinteki tokens:** +3 VP each
- **Trauma counters:** –1 VP each
- **Favors:** +0 VP normally, but puzzle links may cause certain favor types to be worth VP

The player with the most VP wins. In the event of a tie, if only one of the tied players had a correct guilty hunch, that player wins. Otherwise, the tied players share the victory.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Each detective has a unique special ability and starting location.
- Dark twilight cards can be played on other players during their turns to hinder them — politics and negotiation matter.
- The murderer is the suspect with the highest numeric evidence total after removing the lowest-value token from each suspect's strong evidence file and the highest-value token from each suspect's weak evidence file, then accounting for alibis, surprise witnesses, and perjuries.
- Players can attempt to frame or exonerate suspects based on their personal hunch cards.
- Time is a limited resource — choosing where to spend your actions is crucial.

## Player Reference

**Turn:** Spend 6 Time freely on actions (1 Time each): move, follow lead, draw/discard twilight card, play light card, use location ability, get a jump on the case.

**VP Sources:** Correct guilty hunch (+15) + correct innocent hunch (+5) + plot endings (varies) + conspiracy tokens (+4 each) + Haas tokens (+3 each) + Jinteki tokens (+3 each) + trauma (–1 each) + favors (0, modified by puzzle links)

**Detectives:** Each has unique abilities, starting positions, twilight card decks, and plot cards.
