## Overview

A two-player strategic board game covering the Napoleonic Wars in Europe from 1805 to 1815. One player controls the First French Empire and its allies, while the other controls the Coalition countries led by Great Britain. The game includes three scenarios starting in 1805, 1809, and 1813, each with different strategic situations and victory conditions. Players manage armies and diplomacy across a map of Europe using a shared deck of 55 playing cards.

## Components

- Game board (map of Europe)
- 162 game pieces (corps counters and markers)
- 55 playing cards (shared deck)
- 1 scenarios booklet
- 2 player aid cards
- 2 dice
- Rules booklet

## Setup

Choose a scenario (1805, 1809, or 1813). Place units according to the scenario's historical starting positions. Set up diplomacy markers for neutral nations.

## Turn Structure

Each turn represents a year of the Napoleonic Wars and proceeds through eight mandatory phases in order:

1. **Diplomacy Phase**: Play cards to change the diplomatic alignment of enemy or neutral countries, or counter the other player's diplomatic actions.
2. **Insurrection Phase**: Determine the outcome of existing insurrections; the Coalition player may play cards to trigger new insurrections.
3. **Strategy Phase**: Discard remaining cards, reshuffle the deck if required, and draw new cards.
4. **Reinforcement Phase**: Deploy reinforcements from the reserve, rally spent corps, and repatriate prisoners.
5. **Campaign Phase**: Move armies and fight battles across multiple bi-monthly campaign rounds.
6. **Surrender Phase**: Check whether enemy countries must surrender.
7. **Winter Attrition Phase**: Determine and remove winter attrition losses.
8. **Victory Check Phase**: Determine whether either side has won.

## Actions

### Diplomacy
Both players may play diplomacy cards (Napoleon I, Talleyrand, English Gold, Pitt, Metternich, Alexander I) to bring neutral or enemy nations to their side. The opposing player may immediately play a counter card to prevent the change. Certain countries are immune to diplomacy depending on their alignment and situation.

### Military Operations
- Armies move by land between adjacent areas or, once the Britannia card is in effect, the Coalition player may move one corps per campaign round between coastal areas by sea.
- Moving an army costs a card discard (except each player's first move of the Campaign Phase).
- Battles are resolved by summing the Battle Ratings of engaged corps, applying situation modifiers, and rolling dice on the Battle Results Table.
- Napoleon and other commanders contribute their Battle Rating, Movement Rating, and Seniority Rating to their army per the standard rules like any other corps commander.
- Historical events and cards can shift the balance of power throughout the game.

### Insurrection
The Coalition player may play Insurrection cards to turn French dominions or French-occupied neutral countries into insurgent countries. Insurgent countries may later become Coalition members or revert to French dominions depending on the military situation.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Victory conditions are defined globally across all scenarios as follows:

**French Marginal Victory**: France controls or occupies all countries adjacent to France and either Russia or Österreich, while neither Russia nor Österreich is a Coalition member, insurgent country, or Coalition-occupied neutral. Additionally, either French-controlled corps invaded Russia prior to the current game turn, or the current game turn is 1811 or later.

**French Decisive Victory**: France controls or occupies Russia, Österreich, Preußen, and all countries adjacent to France, and no country except Britain is a Coalition member, insurgent country, or Coalition-occupied neutral.

**Coalition Marginal Victory**: France controls France and no more than three minor countries.

**Coalition Decisive Victory**: France surrenders, or Napoleon's corps is permanently eliminated.

**Tie-Breaker**: If neither side has met any victory condition at the end of the 1815 game turn, the French player wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- The Britannia card enables Coalition sea movement and is critical for moving British corps; it immediately comes into effect if neither Österreich nor Preußen nor Russia is a Coalition member.
- Insurgent corps can only fight to free their own home country and cannot move or retreat more than one area away from it.
- March attrition affects corps entering a third or fourth area in a move, mountain or marsh areas, any area during a winter campaign round, or during a Forced March.
- Permanent losses (from winter campaign rounds or certain battle outcomes) remove corps from the game entirely; temporary losses are placed in the Prisoners Box and returned at the end of the Reinforcement Phase.
- Diplomatic alignment applies to an entire country; different areas of a major country cannot have different alignments.
