## Overview

Bloody April is a detailed tactical, regimental-level simulation of the Battle of Shiloh (Tennessee), April 6–7, 1862. Published by SPI in 1979, it uses the award-winning system first introduced in Terrible Swift Sword (Gettysburg). The game tracks morale, stragglers, soldier fatigue, ammunition, regimental assignments, and individual unit strengths. Each hex represents approximately 200 yards, each unit represents a regiment or battery, and each daylight game turn represents 20 minutes (night turns represent one hour).

## Components

- Two 22" × 34" mapsheets that together form the Shiloh battlefield
- 120 playing pieces ("counters"), including organizational counters (Strength Counters, Pinned Counters, Routed Counters, Column Formation Counters, Limbered Counters, Encamped Counters, Prone Counters) and unit counters (regiments, artillery batteries, leaders, aides-de-camp, supply wagons, gunboats, etc.)
- Rules booklet using the SPI Case System
- Charts and Tables (on separate sheets): Fire and Melee Combat Results Tables, Terrain Effects Chart, Range Effects Chart, Stacking Restriction Chart, Density/Fire Adjustment Chart, Straggler Table, Leader Casualty Table, Fire Coordination Table; plus Turn Record Track, Casualty and Captured Tracks, and Terrain Key printed on the game map
- Orders of Battle and Reinforcement Schedules (in rules booklet)
- Roster Sheets (for tracking unit strength and ammunition)
- Two dice

## Setup

Players consult the Initial Deployment Charts (Cases 29.5–29.6) for the chosen scenario. All Union units begin encamped and unalerted. Confederate units deploy in designated hexes (all batteries begin limbered; most Confederate units may not move until 0600). The Game-Turn marker is placed on the Turn Record Track.

## Turn Structure

Each Game-Turn consists of a First Player-Turn (Confederate) and a Second Player-Turn (Union), followed by the Game-Turn Record Interphase. Each Player-Turn has these phases, in order:

1. **Union Alert Phase** (Union Player only; applies only until all Union units are alerted): The Union Player attempts to alert officers or commanders using pickets or aides.
2. **Picket and Patrol Phase**: The Union Player (if Phasing) may move pickets. Aides-de-camp for both sides also move in this phase.
3. **Initial Command Phase**: The Phasing Player checks the Effectiveness Radii of all his officers to determine whether infantry units in that officer's command may move and fire this turn. (Cavalry, artillery, and leaders are not affected by this check.)
4. **Movement Phase**: The Phasing Player moves any or all of his units within their Movement Allowances. Reinforcements may enter per their schedule.
5. **Defensive Fire Phase**: The non-Phasing Player may fire with any of his eligible units at Phasing Player units. Neither player moves units during this phase (except as mandated by combat).
6. **Offensive Fire Phase**: The Phasing Player fires, using the same procedures as the Defensive Fire Phase.
7. **Retreat Before Melee Phase**: The non-Phasing Player may retreat any of his units that are in danger of being meleed (one or two hexes), thus avoiding melee. Retreating units may draw withdrawal fire and must check for possible rout at the end of such retreat. The Phasing Player may advance into vacated hexes.
8. **Melee Phase**: The Phasing Player may use any Phasing infantry or cavalry units that begin this phase adjacent to an Enemy combat unit (other than one that has already retreated before melee) to enter that Enemy-occupied hex and engage in melee combat. The Melee Differential is determined and retreats and/or casualties are applied.
9. **Final Command Phase**: The Phasing Player may attempt to rally routed units, round up stragglers, and resupply units needing ammunition.

**Game-Turn Record Interphase**: The Game-Turn marker is advanced one space (20 minutes for daylight turns; one hour for night turns).

## Actions

- **Movement**: Units move according to their Movement Allowance (not printed on counters; listed in Case 2.4 and the charts). Movement is modified by terrain and formation. Road/trail use requires Column formation for infantry. Encamped units are stationary until alerted and broken camp.
- **Formations**: Infantry is in Line or Column; artillery is Limbered or Unlimbered; cavalry is Mounted or Dismounted. Changing formation costs Movement Points (Infantry Line↔Column: 2 MP; Artillery Limber/Unlimber: 4 MP; Cavalry Mount/Dismount: 3 MP). Infantry in Column gains mobility but may not initiate melee; only 1 Strength Point of Column infantry may fire. Line infantry melees and fires normally. Infantry in Column may use roads, trails, fords, and bridges; Line infantry may not.
- **Facing**: Units have a facing direction affecting movement costs and combat (enfilade hexsides, etc.). Units in Column have different facing from Line units (two enfilade hexsides and only one frontal hexside).
- **Fire Combat**: Infantry and artillery fire at range. Fire Strength is the unit's Combat/Gun Strength, modified by range, terrain, formation, facing, and stacking. Results from the Fire CRT may cause casualties, morale checks, or retreat.
- **Melee Combat**: Melee uses a *differential* CRT, not an odds-based CRT. The Melee Differential = total attacking Base Strength Points (Phasing Player) minus total defending Strength Points (adjusted for terrain, leaders, and morale). Defender's points are always subtracted from attacker's regardless of who has more. Differentials lower than −4 or higher than +7 are treated as −4 or +7 respectively. A defender may adjust up to 4 total points (2 for terrain, 1 for a Leader, 1 for Morale). Results produce retreats and/or casualties.
- **Morale and Rout**: Units check morale in certain combat situations. Failed checks may cause rout. Routed units may be rallied in the Final Command Phase.
- **Stragglers**: Units generate stragglers under certain conditions. Straggler recovery occurs in the Final Command Phase using the Straggler Table.
- **Battle Fatigue**: Units accumulate Fatigue Points through combat and forced marching. Fatigue reduces unit effectiveness (see Section 19.0).
- **Artillery**: Batteries are either Limbered (can move, cannot fire) or Unlimbered (can fire, cannot move). Gun Strength represents the type and number of guns in a battery. Artillery has specific weapon types, ranges, and ammunition limits tracked on Roster Cards.
- **Zones of Control (ZOCs)**: Combat units exert ZOCs into adjacent hexes, restricting enemy movement and affecting ammunition resupply.
- **Ammunition**: Units track ammunition on Roster Cards. Supply wagons resupply infantry and artillery. Confederate supply rules differ from Union supply rules (Sections 11.3–11.4).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Victory is expressed in levels determined at the end of the game/scenario (Case 30.1 applies to all scenarios except the Confederate Attack scenario, Case 29.1, which uses Case 30.2). Both players first note unit locations, then total Casualty Points.

**Casualty Points**: Each Strength Point (men) killed/wounded or captured = 1 Casualty Point. Each gun lost = 1 Casualty Point; a captured gun = 2 points for the player losing it. Officers below Colonel rank = 1 point killed/wounded, 2 points captured. Higher-ranking officers have escalating values (see the Casualty Point table in Case 30.13). Grant, Buell, Johnston, and Beauregard are not counted.

**Standard Victory Levels (Case 30.14)**:
1. **Confederate Total Victory**: No Union combat units remain west of the Tennessee River (and south of the Snake/Owl Creek barrier) within Effectiveness or Command range of any Union officer.
2. **Confederate Strategic Victory**: The Confederate Player controls (has a combat unit in or passing through) either or both of the two Landing hexes (Pittsburg Landing), regardless of losses. Such units must be within range of a leader and able to face a path free of Enemy units/ZOCs to hex A0102.
3. **Confederate Tactical Victory**: Have at least 40 Strength or Gun Points within 10 hexes of a Landing hex and have fewer Casualty Points than the Union.
4. **Union Marginal Victory**: The Confederate Player has at least 40 Strength/Gun Points within 10 hexes of Landing hexes but the Confederate Player has the same number or more Casualty Points.
5. **Union Tactical Victory**: Union losses (Casualty Points) exceed those of the Confederacy, but the Confederates have fewer than 40 SPs within 10 hexes.
6. **Union Strategic Victory**: Confederate losses equal or exceed those of the Union (in Casualty Points) and the Confederacy does not have 40 SPs within 10 hexes of the Landing (this is the historical outcome of the battle).

**Confederate Attack Victory Conditions (Case 30.2)**: Used only for the Confederate Attack scenario (Case 29.2). Victory is determined by the number of named Fields (e.g., Rea Field, Adamson Field, etc.) the Confederate Player controls. A Confederate player controls a field if there is at least one Confederate combat unit in or adjacent to that field and no unrouted Union combat units in or adjacent to it. Victory levels: 1–3 fields = Union Strategic Victory; 4–6 = Union Tactical; 7–8 = Confederate Marginal; 9–10 = Confederate Tactical; 11+ = Confederate Strategic.

**Victory Adjustments**: Hypothetical scenarios (Case 29.4) can adjust the victory schedule by plus or minus one level.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Union Alert System (Section 20.0)**: All Union units begin the game encamped and unalerted. Unalerted Union units may not move or fire. All Union units are automatically fully alerted at the beginning of the 0900 turn. Before that, units may be alerted by pickets, aides, or by enemy contact (visual or physical). A brigade alerted by picket must roll 1–4 on a die to actually alert (1st consecutive turn with picket stacked with officer); on the 2nd consecutive turn the alert is automatic. A division may also be alerted if a picket stacks with a division commander. Units alerted by enemy contact (spotted, fired on, or meleed) become alerted the following Friendly Player-Turn but with halved Movement Allowance for that first turn. Units automatically alerted at 0900 have no movement restriction.
- **Pickets**: Certain Union brigades (Peabody and Miller of 6th Division, and any one brigade from 5th Division) begin the game with picket counters, formed by subtracting 1 Strength Point from a regiment in that brigade. Pickets have no Zone of Control; they may move only 1 hex per turn until they spot or encounter an enemy unit.
- **Encampment**: Union units begin encamped. Encamped units are stationary and defend poorly. They must be alerted before they can move normally.
- **Lew Wallace's Division**: Special reinforcement rules govern the delayed and confused arrival of Lew Wallace's division (Case 22.3).
- **Night (Section 23.0)**: Night turns last one hour each. Night rules restrict certain actions, allow recovery and reorganization, and govern the arrival of Buell's Army of the Ohio.
- **Leaders and Effectiveness Radius**: Each officer has a Command Radius (range within which his infantry units may move and fire) checked during the Initial Command Phase. Cavalry, artillery, and other leaders are not affected by this check. Leaders may also provide melee bonuses when stacked with units.
- **Leader Casualties**: Leaders may become casualties via the Leader Casualty Table. Replacement officers exist for some positions (Case 16.6).
- **Grant's Bad Leg (Very Optional Rule, Case 16.8)**: Several days before the battle Grant's horse fell on him, severely bruising his leg. Players may optionally lower Grant's Movement Allowance to represent this historical limitation.
- **Ammunition Supply (Section 11.0)**: Infantry and artillery units track ammunition on Roster Cards. Supply wagons are used to resupply units (Case 11.2). Confederate and Union ammunition supply rules differ (Cases 11.3–11.4). Union camps also provide a source of Union ammunition (Case 11.5). Berdan's Sharpshooters have a special ammunition rule (Case 11.6).
- **Cavalry Charge (Case 17.2)**: Mounted cavalry may conduct a charge; charging units attack with double their Base Strength but are automatically routed after completing the charge (unless already routed in the melee).
- **Union Gunboats and Ferries (Section 21.0)**: Union gunboats may fire on Confederate units; ferries provide a transport option. Gunboats have restricted movement along the river.
- **Fire Coordination (Section 24.0)**: Optional rule allowing coordinated fire from multiple units via the Fire Coordination Table.
- **Exploding Caissons (Case 18.4)**: Optional rule; artillery caissons may explode if hit.
- **Stacking**: Units have stacking limits per hex. Different terrain types impose different limits on melee as well.

## Player Reference

| Formation | Who Uses | Movement Effect | Combat Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Line | Infantry | Reduced (may not use roads/trails/fords/bridges) | Full fire and melee capability |
| Column | Infantry | Full (may use roads, trails, fords, bridges) | Only 1 SP may fire; may not initiate melee |
| Limbered | Artillery | May move; may not fire | N/A |
| Unlimbered | Artillery | May not move | May fire normally |
| Mounted | Cavalry | Full movement (treated as Column) | May charge (double strength, auto-routed after) |
| Dismounted | Cavalry | Reduced (treated as Line; may use bridges/fords) | Full fire/melee; horse-holders penalty to fire strength |

| Formation Change | Cost |
|---|---|
| Infantry Line ↔ Column | 2 MP |
| Artillery Limber/Unlimber | 4 MP |
| Cavalry Mount/Dismount | 3 MP (only at end of Movement Phase) |

|---|---|---|
