## Overview

7 Ages is an epic civilization game covering 6,000 years of human history from 4000 BC to 2000 AD. Players lead dynasties that guide the fate of empires across seven ages. Using cards to start, manage, and develop empires, players compete for glory points. The game features simultaneous management of multiple empires per player across a map of the entire world.

## Components

- 2 A1 (594x840mm) full-color world maps
- 6 full-color countersheets (576 x 5/8" counters, 300 x 1/2" counters)
- 110 full-color cards
- Rules and players' guide

## Setup

1. Lay out both maps to form the world.
2. Shuffle cards; place face-down.
3. Sort large counters into 15 empire colors.
4. Put unnamed leaders and "no leader" markers into a cup.
5. Deal 7 cards face-down to each player.
6. Determine first player: each player plays 1 card face-down, reveal simultaneously. Highest card goes first (ties play additional cards). Discard all played cards.
7. In order of cards played (highest to lowest), each player picks a glory marker and matching empire counters (dedicated for the entire game). Remaining empire colors are available for anyone.

## Turn Structure

At the start of each turn, players place action markers face-down on each empire they have in play (and optionally one extra marker not on any empire). Each action phase is then reached in sequence; when a phase begins, all players who chose that action turn over that action marker and perform the action before moving on to the next phase.

### Action Sequence
1. **Start Empire** — Play a card to create a new empire on the map.
2. **Production** — Empires earn income and spend it to maintain existing units and build new ones.
3. **Trade and Progress** — Empires trade with neighboring empires to advance along the progress track, swap cards, or exchange financial aid.
4. **Manoeuvre** — Move military units and resolve conflicts.
5. **Destiny** — Discard unwanted cards and refill hand to 6 cards.
6. **Civilise** — Add improvements: build cities, play event or artefact cards, promote leaders, adopt religions or governments, modernise units, and remove disorder.
7. **Discard Empire** — Remove one of your empires from the map to free up space for another.

## Actions

### Starting Empires
- Play a card to start a new empire using one of the available empire colors.
- The card determines the empire's starting characteristics: location, type, and initial strength.
- Each player can control multiple empires simultaneously (maximum varies by player count: 7 empires each in a 2-player game, 5 each with 3 players, 4 each with 4 players, 3 each with 5 or more players).

### Progress
- Empires advance along a progress track representing technological and cultural development.
- Higher progress enables more powerful units and abilities.
- The game ends when any empire progresses beyond the 49th progress level (the Age of Aquarius threshold) or acquires the internet. Empires can progress beyond the 49th level but cannot go backwards in progress once they have done so.

### Combat
- Conflict is resolved when units from different empires occupy the same area.
- Each side draws a card from the deck; the card's value contributes to that side's conflict total alongside the front-line and support values of committed units, elite markers, age difference, terrain modifiers (for the defender), and other factors. There are no dice.
- Leaders called tacticians allow their owner to discard their drawn conflict card and draw a replacement before committing units. Tacticians also expand retreat options: normally a player may only retreat if the opponent has acquired a disorder marker, but a player with a tactician in the area may retreat if either side has acquired a disorder marker.

### Trade & Production
- Empires generate income based on controlled territories and cities.
- Resources are used to build units and advance progress.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- **Glory Points:** The winner is the player with the most glory points when the game ends.
- Glory is earned through empire achievements, territorial control, cultural advancement, and military victories.
- **Game End:** When any empire exceeds the 49th progress level or acquires the internet (7 bonus glory points to that player).
- Alternatively, players may agree on a predetermined end time.
- **Tie-breaker:** The player with the most advanced empire wins; if more than one empire has passed the 49th progress level, the first to progress past it wins; if still equal, all tied players share the victory.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Players have **dedicated empire colors** chosen at game start, plus access to remaining neutral colors.
- Each player manages multiple empires across different ages simultaneously.
- Cards serve multiple purposes: starting empires, events, combat modifiers, and more.
- Leaders come in two types. Named leaders are specified on an empire's card for a particular age and are chosen directly (not drawn randomly). Unnamed leaders are drawn randomly from a cup; a drawn unnamed leader may be returned to the cup if undesired, and the player may draw again up to the number of times permitted by the empire's leader value. If a "no leader" counter is drawn, it must always be returned.
- **Civil wars** can break empires apart, with portions possibly falling to other players.
- Empires can rise and fall through the ages; defunct empires are removed.
- The progress track represents 7 distinct ages of human civilization.
- Variable game length: can be very short (30 minutes with limited ages) or very long (6+ hours for full game).
