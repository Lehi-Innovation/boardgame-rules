## Overview

Catan Histories: Rise of the Inkas follows the rise and fall of three successive civilizations in ancient Peru. Players lead tribes through three cultural eras, building settlements, roads, and cities to accumulate culture points. When a tribe reaches its cultural apex (4 points for the 1st and 2nd tribe; 3 points for the 3rd tribe), it falls into decline and the player begins with a new tribe. The first player to bring their third tribe to its cultural apex (3 culture points with the 3rd tribe, totaling 11 culture markers overall) wins. The game uses familiar Catan mechanics (dice rolling, resource collection, trading, building) with 5 resource types and 3 goods types, plus a unique tribal progression and decline system.

## Components

- Game board with 27 hexes (framed)
- 5 resource card types (wood, ore, stone, wool, potato) produced by: Forest, Mountains, Quarries, Pasture, Farmland
- 3 goods card types (feathers, coca, fish) produced by: Jungles, Jungle Plantations, Fishing Grounds
- Number tokens
- Resource and goods cards
- Settlements (8 per player), roads (7 per player), cities (2 per player) in player colors
- Thicket tokens (to cover fallen civilizations)
- Culture boards and culture markers (11 per player)
- Trading & building tiles (player aids)
- 2 dice, robber figure, development cards (two decks: "1" and "2")

## Setup

1. Assemble frame and place hexes (fixed for beginners).
2. Place number tokens on hexes.
3. Each player places 2 starting settlements and 2 roads.
4. Each player starts with 2 culture points.

## Turn Structure

The highest-rolling player goes first. On your turn, take the following steps in order:

**1. Production Phase:** Roll 2 dice. All hexes matching the rolled number produce for adjacent settlements/cities: 1 card per settlement, 2 cards per city. Resources and goods are collected.

**2. Trade and Build Phase (any order, as many times as you like):**
- **Trade with players:** On your turn, trade resource/goods cards with any other player. On others' turns, you may only trade with the active player.
- **Trade with the supply:** 3:1 for 3 matching resources; 2:1 for 2 matching goods; 3:2 for 3 different goods (takes any 2 resource/goods cards).
- **Build:** Roads, settlements, cities, and development cards. You may also play 1 development card per turn (before your dice roll or during this phase), but not on the turn you bought it.

## Actions

**Building costs:**
- **Road:** 1 wood + 1 stone
- **Settlement:** 1 wood + 1 stone + 1 potato + 1 wool (earns 1 culture point when placed)
- **City:** 2 potato + 3 ore (replaces one of your settlements; earns 1 additional culture point — city = 2 total, but replaces settlement's 1, so net +1 on the board)
- **Development Card:** 1 potato + 1 wool + 1 ore

**Each player may only build 1 city per tribe.**

**Cultural apex and tribal decline:** When a tribe reaches its cultural goal, it falls into decline:

1. Remove all of your roads from the board (return to supply).
2. If you held the Longest Trade Route advantage card, lose it immediately.
3. Cover each of your settlements and city with a thicket token.
4. Place a free founding settlement for your new tribe (no road, no starting resources). Your turn ends immediately after placement.

**Founding settlement placement restrictions:** Must respect the distance rule (applies to both active and declining settlements/cities). May not be placed on an active tribe's settlement site (an intersection adjacent to an active player's road). May not be placed on a declining settlement/city intersection. May not be placed in the jungle or sea.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Players accumulate culture points by building settlements (1 point each) and cities (1 additional point, since a city replaces a settlement). Each tribe has its own cultural goal tracked on a personal culture board:

- **1st tribe cultural goal:** 4 culture points (requires 2 settlements + 1 city, or 4 settlements)
- **2nd tribe cultural goal:** 4 culture points (same requirements)
- **3rd tribe cultural goal:** 3 culture points (requires 1 settlement + 1 city, or 3 settlements)

The first player to reach 3 culture points with their third tribe wins — this is 11 total culture markers on their culture board across all three tribes.

The Longest Trade Route (first to build a continuous route of 3+ roads uninterrupted by opponent settlements/cities) grants a 2:1 supply trade bonus once per turn. The Mightiest Combat Arts advantage (first to play 2+ Combat Arts cards face-up) lets you return the robber to the jungle frame once on your turn if it blocks one of your hexes, taking 1 resource from that hex.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

**Declining (thicket-covered) settlements/cities:**
- They **still produce** resource/goods cards for their owner when the adjacent hex number is rolled.
- They **can be built over:** any player may pay settlement construction costs to replace a declining settlement/city with their own new settlement (return the old piece and thicket to the owner's supply).
- Roads may **not** be built past a declining settlement/city. You must build to that intersection, then build over the declining piece before you can extend roads beyond it.
- You may not add roads, settlements, or cities to your own tribe in decline.

**Rolling a 7:** All players with 8 or more resource/goods cards must discard half (round in their favor). The active player moves the robber to another hex or the jungle frame. The robber may not be placed on a goods-producing hex (jungle or fishing ground). Rob 1 random resource/goods card from one opponent adjacent to the new robber location.

**Combat Arts cards (development cards):** Each face-up Combat Arts card you've played increases your discard threshold by 1 (e.g., 3 face-up cards → must discard if you have 10+).

**Development Cards "2":** These cards only enter the supply once ALL players have begun their 2nd tribe (placed their 1st settlement of their 2nd tribe).

**Robber restriction:** The robber may not be placed on a hex that produces goods (jungle or fishing ground hexes).
