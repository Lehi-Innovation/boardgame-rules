## Overview

BattleLore is a two-player miniatures board game that combines medieval warfare with fantasy elements. Using the Commands & Colors system, players lead armies on a hex-based battlefield, playing Command cards to order units in different sections of the board. The game features infantry, cavalry, and archers, enhanced by mercenary races (Dwarves, Goblins), mythical creatures, and a Lore magic system. Players fight pre-designed Adventures (scenarios), and the first player to capture a set number of Victory Banners wins.

## Components

- 1 Double-sided game board (hex grid with terrain)
- Over 100 plastic miniatures (various troop types and creatures)
- 60 Command Cards (Section and Tactic cards)
- 60 Lore Cards (15 per Lore Master class: Wizard, Cleric, Rogue, Warrior)
- 42 Terrain tiles and Landmark pieces
- Battle dice (custom 6-sided dice)
- Adventure booklet with multiple scenarios
- Summary cards, War Council sheets
- Banner Bearers and unit figures
- Lore tokens and Council tokens

## Setup

Each Adventure specifies:
1. Place terrain tiles on the board as shown in the scenario map.
2. Place unit figures on starting hexes. Each unit has a Banner Bearer (always last to be removed) plus additional figures.
3. Determine starting Command cards per adventure rules (typically 4-6 per player depending on Commander level).
4. Set up War Council if using advanced rules.
5. One side is Standard Banner, the other Pennant Banner.
6. The board is divided into three sections (Left Wing, Center, Right Wing) by two red dotted lines.

## Turn Structure

Players alternate turns. Each turn has these phases:

### 1. Command Phase
Play one Command Card from your hand. Command Cards determine which units you can order (activate) this turn.

**Section Cards**: Order a number of units in a specific section (Left, Center, or Right).
**Tactic Cards**: Special orders that may affect units across sections or provide unique effects.

### 2. Order Phase
Activate the units specified by your Command Card. Ordered units may Move and/or Battle.

### 3. Movement Phase
Move each ordered unit up to its movement allowance:

**Foot units:**

| Banner Color | Move & Battle |
|--------------|---------------|
| Green (Irregular) | Up to 2 hexes and battle |
| Blue (Regular) | Up to 1 hex and battle, OR up to 2 hexes and not battle |
| Red (Heavy) | Up to 1 hex and battle |

**Mounted units:**

| Banner Color | Move & Battle |
|--------------|---------------|
| Green (Irregular) | Up to 4 hexes and battle |
| Blue (Regular) | Up to 3 hexes and battle |
| Red (Heavy) | Up to 2 hexes and battle |

- Units cannot move through hexes occupied by other units (friendly or enemy).
- Terrain may restrict or modify movement.

### 4. Combat Phase
Each ordered unit that is in range may battle one enemy unit.

**Melee Combat**: Adjacent units attack. Roll battle dice equal to the unit's combat strength.
**Ranged Combat**: Archers (and some special units) may attack at range. Range limits and line of sight apply.

**Battle Dice Results**:
- Colored Helmet (Green/Blue/Red): Scores 1 hit if the helmet color matches the target unit's banner color; other colors are misses
- Sword on Shield: A hit only when specified on the attacking unit's weapon card (e.g., Short Sword and Long Sword hit on matching helmet color AND Sword on Shield; Common Bow does NOT hit on Sword on Shield)
- Bonus Strike: Scores a hit only when specified on the attacking unit's weapon card; otherwise a miss
- Flag: Forces target to retreat 1 hex per flag (Frightened units retreat 2 hexes per flag); if retreat is blocked, lose 1 figure per blocked hex
- Lore: Generates a Lore token (in Lore Adventures)

For each hit, remove 1 figure from the target unit. When the last figure (Banner Bearer) is removed, the attacker captures a Victory Banner.

### 5. End of Turn
- Draw a new Command Card.
- Collect Lore tokens if using advanced rules.
- Check for victory conditions.

## Actions

### Retreat
When a unit takes flag results, it must retreat 1 hex per flag toward its own side. If it cannot retreat (blocked by terrain, board edge, or friendly units), it loses 1 figure per un-retreated flag.

### Pursue (Follow-on Actions)
Some units may advance into the vacated hex after forcing a retreat or eliminating a unit.

### Terrain Effects
There are only four basic Terrain Types in the core game: Countryside, Wooded Terrain, Elevated Terrain, and Waterways (Fords and Bridges are waterway features). Retreat moves are not subject to terrain movement restrictions.

| Terrain | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Countryside | Default open terrain; no movement or battle restrictions |
| Wooded Terrain | A unit entering must stop and may move no further that turn (follow-on moves are subject to the same restriction), but may still battle that turn; an attacker targeting an enemy unit on a Wooded Terrain hex battles with a maximum of 2 dice; blocks line of sight |
| Elevated Terrain | A unit on Elevated Terrain battles with a maximum of 3 dice; a unit not on Elevated Terrain attacking an enemy unit on Elevated Terrain battles with a maximum of 2 dice; blocks line of sight except between contiguous Elevated Terrain hexes of the same height |
| Waterways | Impassable except at Fords and Bridges; does not block line of sight |
| Ford (Waterways) | A unit must stop when entering a Ford (gaining ground and pursuits remain possible); a unit may enter a Ford hex and still battle, capped at a maximum of 2 dice; a unit attacking an enemy unit on a Ford hex battles with no dice restriction; does not block line of sight |
| Bridge (Waterways) | No movement or battle restrictions; a unit on a Bridge is Bold and may ignore 1 flag; does not block line of sight |

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Each Adventure specifies a target number of Victory Banners (typically 4-7, varies by adventure). Earn 1 Victory Banner each time the last figure (Banner Bearer) of an enemy unit is eliminated; the Banner Bearer is placed on the victor's Victory Track. Additional Victory Banners (Victory Blazon tokens) may be earned by capturing certain terrain hexes or accomplishing adventure-specific objectives. The game ends the very moment a player reaches the required number of Victory Banners, even mid-turn (e.g., on a successful Battle Back). The first player to reach the target wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

### Mercenaries (Advanced)
- **Iron Dwarves** (Green/Blue/Red Banner foot units — Crossbowman, Swordsman, Swordsman (Heavy) — moving and battling per the normal per-banner rules like any other unit): Special power **Iron Morale** — all Iron Dwarf foot units are Bold: they may ignore 1 flag rolled against them in battle, making them a fearsome foe even when caught alone and unsupported. If a dwarf unit is supported, it becomes even bolder and may ignore 2 flags rolled against it in any battle dice roll — effectively Bold2 for as long as it remains supported.
- **Goblinoids**: Two special powers. **Goblin Rush** — all Goblinoid foot units (including Blue and Red Banner units) may rush up to two hexes to engage an enemy unit in Melee, and must then engage that enemy in battle the same turn (unless the enemy dies first); if they don't rush, they're subject to the usual movement and battle restrictions of their banner color. **Goblin Run** — all Goblinoid units are considered Frightened the moment they are forced to retreat: they must retreat 2 hexes for each flag rolled against them that they turn out unable to ignore, and must always check for Panic Losses during that retreat.

### Creatures (Advanced)
Creatures are single-figure units (neither foot nor mounted) with special rules:
- **Critical Hits (only way to kill a Creature)**: Creatures are immune to normal combat casualties. When an attack scores one or more hits (helmet color matching the Creature's banner, or Sword on Shield if applicable), set those dice aside and re-roll them. Only if the Creature's own banner color comes up on the re-roll is a Critical Hit scored — the Creature is instantly eliminated and placed on the opponent's Victory Track. The attacker also collects 1 Lore token. All other re-roll results (other helmet colors, Sword on Shield, Flag, Lore) are ignored.
- **Morale**: Creatures are Bold; they must always ignore 1 flag rolled against them (they may never opt to retreat instead). Creatures retreat 2 hexes per flag they must take; if a retreat is blocked, the attacker rolls 1 die per blocked hex and each banner-color match triggers another Critical Hit re-roll.
- **Trampling**: When a Creature's retreat is blocked by units, each unit in the theoretical retreat path adjacent to the Creature loses 1 figure per hex the Creature cannot retreat.
- **Special Powers**: Each Creature has unique powers triggered by rolling a specified number of Lore symbols during its battle dice roll (described on the Creature's Summary card). A Creature may also store Lore symbols as Power-ups toward higher-cost special powers.

### Lore System (Advanced)
- Each side has a Lore Master (Wizard, Cleric, Rogue, or Warrior).
- Lore tokens are collected from battle dice.
- Lore Cards are played by spending Lore tokens for magical effects (healing, combat bonuses, terrain manipulation, etc.).
- Each Lore Master type has unique cards and abilities.

### War Council (Advanced)
Players compose a War Council of Commander + Lore Masters:
- **Commander** level determines number of Command Cards: No Commander = 3 cards; Level 1 = 4 cards; Level 2 = 5 cards; Level 3 = 6 cards.
- **Lore Masters** (Wizard, Cleric, Rogue, Warrior) determine access to Lore Card types and the starting number of Lore tokens and Lore cards in hand. The highest-level Lore Master on the War Council sets these values.

### Bold and Battle Back
- **Bold**: A unit that can ignore at least one flag rolled against it. Bold units hold their ground more easily and are eligible to Battle Back.
- **Battle Back**: Any defending unit that is Bold at the time of an enemy Melee attack may fight back immediately after the attacker's dice roll is fully resolved — provided the defending unit survived and did not vacate its hex. Units battling back cannot gain ground or make pursuit actions. No further battle back is possible from the initial attacker.

## Player Reference

### Turn Sequence
1. Play 1 Command Card
2. Order specified units
3. Move ordered units
4. Battle with ordered units (roll dice)
5. Draw 1 Command Card; collect Lore

### Battle Dice Symbols
| Symbol | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Green Helmet | Hit on Green Banner unit |
| Blue Helmet | Hit on Blue Banner unit |
| Red Helmet | Hit on Red Banner unit |
| Sword on Shield | Hit when weapon card specifies; ignored by mounted units vs. Short Sword |
| Bonus Strike | Hit when weapon card specifies; otherwise miss |
| Flag | Force retreat (1 hex per flag for Normal units; 2 hexes per flag for Frightened units); blocked retreat = 1 figure lost per blocked hex |
| Lore | Gain 1 Lore token (in Lore Adventures) |

### Victory
- Eliminate enemy units to earn Victory Banners
- First to target number (per scenario) wins
- Typical target: 4-7 banners (varies by adventure)
