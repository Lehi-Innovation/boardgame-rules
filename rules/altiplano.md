## Overview

Altiplano is a bag-building game set in the South American highlands of Bolivia and Peru. Players generate wealth by fishing, mining, breeding alpacas, and trading goods from limited resources. Players draw goods tiles from personal bags, plan actions at locations, and move around a circular route of 7 locations to execute those plans. The player who amasses the most points through goods, orders, houses, boats, and warehouse storage wins.

## Components

- 7 Location tiles (Village, Market, Road, Harbor, Farm, Mine, Forest)
- 5 Action boards, 5 Warehouses, 5 Game figures, 5 Markers, 5 Containers, 5 Cloth bags
- 209 Goods tiles (food, corn, wood, stone, ore, cacao, silver, cloth, wool, alpaca, fish, glass)
- 56 Game cards (10 house cards, 10 boat cards, 16 order cards, 20 mission cards)
- 7 Role tiles (shepherd, trader, stonemason, fisherman, farmer, miner, woodcutter)
- 28 Extensions, 15 Carts, 50 Coins
- 1 Extension strip, 1 Starting player figure, 1 Score pad, 5 VP overviews

## Setup

1. Place 7 location tiles in a random circle.
2. Stock locations with goods/cards based on player count.
3. Place food tiles and coins in the center.
4. Prepare extension pile (A on top, then B, C, D on bottom), reveal top 5.
5. Each player takes: 1 action board, 1 warehouse, 1 game figure, 1 marker, 1 container, 1 cart, 1 cloth bag.
6. Each player receives a role tile determining their starting goods and special ability.
7. Place starting goods tiles in your bag.

## Turn Structure

Each round has 4 phases:

1. **Draw Phase:** Draw goods tiles from your bag (up to the number of available planning spaces). If your bag is completely empty when you need to draw, first transfer all tiles from your container into your bag, then draw.
2. **Planning Phase:** Place drawn tiles on action spaces of your action board to plan actions at various locations.
3. **Action Phase:** Players act in clockwise order, starting with the starting player. On your turn, carry out one action (or pass). Once you pass, you may not act again this round. This continues until all players have passed.
4. **Clean-up Phase:** Pass the starting player figure left, reset carts, and refill the extension strip as needed.

## Movement

Movement is not a separate action. Before and/or after your action each turn, you may move your game figure between locations:

- **Cart (free):** Each player starts with 1 cart, usable once per round for free. A cart allows travel of up to 3 steps (each step moves to an adjacent location). Move the cart token to the right after use to show it is spent.
- **Food (extra steps):** You may also spend 1 food tile per step by placing it on a movement action space. This allows 1 step per food spent. Additional carts (up to 4 total) can also be used, each requiring 1 food and allowing up to 3 steps.

Road construction at the Road location expands your planning spaces (first road: 5 spaces) or yields corn — it is not a movement mechanism.

## Actions

Actions depend on the location visited. Each location offers specific production/trade options:

| Location | Key Actions |
|----------|------------|
| Village | Build houses (2 stone → 1 house card, worth 4 pts), store goods in warehouse, buy carts (1 coin) |
| Market | Sell goods for coins, buy extensions, acquire orders (1 coin), deliver goods to orders |
| Road | Road construction (1 stone + 1 wood → expand planning spaces or gain corn) |
| Harbor | Produce food (1 fish + 1 food → 1 extra food), exchange fish for stone, build boats (2 wood → 1 boat card, worth 2 pts) |
| Farm | Produce food (alpaca → food), produce wool (alpaca + food), produce cloth (wool + food) |
| Mine | Produce stone (2 food), produce silver (1 ore + 1 food) |
| Forest | Produce wood (2 food), trade cacao (cacao → food, cloth, or glass) |

Players can also:
- **Buy extensions** (permanent ability upgrades) from the extension strip at the market
- **Store goods** in the warehouse for end-game scoring
- **Fulfill orders** by delivering required goods to your order card

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when all tiles and cards on at least one location are used up, or an empty space at the extension strip cannot be refilled. When triggered, the current round is completed and then **one additional complete round** is played before scoring.

Players score points from:

- **Goods tiles** (scored by type: food and corn are 0 pts; other goods range from 1–4 pts each)
- **Boats** (2 points each)
- **Houses** (4 points each)
- **Completed orders** (points as shown on each order card; unfulfilled orders score nothing and any goods on them are lost)
- **House bonuses** (each house card boosts one type of good; score 1 additional point per tile of that good type you have)
- **Warehouse row completion** (each completed row scores the points indicated at the end of that row; individual goods in the warehouse are also scored at their per-tile value like all other goods)

Coins are **not** worth any points. In case of a tie, the tied player with the most coins wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Tiles and cards are limited; when depleted, they cannot be replaced. Coins are unlimited.
- Container tiles are transferred to the bag only during Phase 1 (Draw phase) when the bag is completely empty.
- Each role tile provides a unique starting setup and special ability.
- Extensions marked with player count restrictions are removed for smaller games.
- Players may check the contents of their own bag and container at any time. Any player may also count how many goods tiles remain in the supply and how many extensions are left in the pile (without revealing or reordering them).
- All players may inspect what orders, boats, and houses are available on locations or with other players.

## Player Reference

**Round Flow:** Draw tiles from bag → Plan actions on action board → Move and execute actions (turn-based, clockwise) → Clean up

**Role Tiles:** Shepherd, Trader, Stonemason, Fisherman, Farmer, Miner, Woodcutter — each has unique starting goods and ability.
