## Overview

Colt Express is a programmed-action game set on a train in the Old West. Players are bandits robbing the Union Pacific Express on July 11, 1899. Over 5 rounds, players simultaneously program actions by playing cards into a common deck, then execute them in order. The richest bandit at game end wins.

## Components

- 1 locomotive and 6 train car tiles (3D cardboard construction)
- 6 bandit character cards and 6 bandit figures
- 6 identical decks of 10 action cards per bandit
- 6 sets of 6 Bullet cards (one set per bandit color)
- 17 Round cards (7 for 2–4 players, 7 for 5–6 players, 3 Train Station cards)
- 13 Neutral Bullet cards
- 26 loot tokens: 18 Purses (worth $250–$500 each), 6 Jewels ($500 each), 2 Strongboxes ($1000 each)
- 1 Marshal pawn

## Setup

1. Assemble train cars in a line with the locomotive at front, using as many cars as there are players.
2. Each player chooses a character, takes the matching Character card, 10 action cards (shuffled into a personal deck), and 6 Bullet cards arranged in order.
3. Each player also takes a $250 Purse and places it face-down on their Character card (value hidden).
4. Place loot tokens in cars as indicated on each car's floor.
5. Place the Marshal pawn and one Strongbox inside the locomotive. The second Strongbox is placed off the train near the locomotive (may enter play via an Event).
6. Players with odd numbers place their bandit in the Caboose; players with even numbers place their bandit in the car next to the Caboose.
7. **First Player:** Randomly draw one bandit pawn from among all players' pawns. The corresponding player is First Player for Round 1 and takes the Round card deck.
8. **Round deck:** Randomly draw 4 Round cards from the 7 corresponding to the player count (2–4 or 5–6), then randomly select 1 Train Station card. Shuffle these 5 cards together with the Train Station card on the bottom as a face-down deck.

## Turn Structure

There are **5 Rounds**. Each round has two phases:

### Phase 1: Schemin'
- The First Player draws the top Round card and places it face-up for all to see.
- At the beginning of each round, each player shuffles all their cards (action cards plus any Bullet cards received) and draws **6 cards** (Doc draws **7**).
- Starting from the First Player and going clockwise, each player either:
  - Plays one action card **face-up** (or face-down on a Tunnel turn) onto the common deck, OR
  - Takes 3 additional cards from their personal deck into their hand.
- Repeat for the number of turns indicated on the Round card. Any unplayed cards are placed back on top of the player's personal deck.

### Phase 2: Stealin'
- The First Player takes the common deck and flips it over (without changing order). Cards are now resolved from top to bottom (i.e., in the order they were played).
- Each card's action is executed by the owning bandit based on current positions.
- Any action programmed during Schemin' is obligatory — each player must use their action if it is still possible.
- After resolution, each performed action card is returned to its owner, who places it back on their personal deck.

### Round End
- The player on the First Player's left becomes the new First Player and takes the remaining Round cards.

## Actions

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Move** | If **inside** the train: move 1 car forwards or backwards. If **on the roof**: move 1 to 3 cars (your choice) forwards or backwards. The Locomotive counts as a car. You must move — you cannot stay in place. |
| **Floor Change** | Move your bandit between the interior and the roof of their current car. |
| **Fire** | Give one of your Bullet cards to a target bandit. **Inside:** target must be in an adjacent car (not your own car or the same spot). **On roof:** target any bandit on the roof in your line of sight (not your own car; no other bandit between you and the target). If no valid target exists, keep your Bullet card. |
| **Punch** | Choose a bandit on the same car and same floor. That bandit loses one loot token (placed on the floor where you are); if no loot, nothing is dropped. Then move the targeted bandit to the same floor of an adjacent car (either forwards or backwards, your choice in most cars; forced backwards if you are in the Locomotive, forced forwards if you are in the Caboose). |
| **Rob** | Take one loot token of your choice from your current location (same floor). Place it face-down on your Character card. No effect if no loot is present. |
| **Marshal** | Move the Marshal 1 car in the direction of your choice. Any bandit in the car where the Marshal arrives (or is already present when a bandit enters) must immediately flee to the roof of that car, and receives 1 Neutral Bullet card placed on top of their personal deck. |

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

After 5 rounds, count loot:
- Purses: $250 or $500 (values hidden until end)
- Jewels: $500
- Strongbox: $1000

**Gunslinger bonus:** The player(s) who fired the most bullets — i.e., who have the **fewest Bullet cards of their own color remaining** — receive the Gunslinger title, worth **$1000** each.

The richest bandit wins. **Ties are broken by the player who received the fewest Bullet cards from other players and Events.**

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Bullet cards** clog your deck. When drawn, they have no effect and waste an action slot.
- **Tunnel turns:** Cards are played face-down; opponents cannot see what you programmed.
- **Speeding-Up turns:** During the current turn, each player plays twice (two action cards, or take 3 cards and play one, etc.).
- **Switching turns:** The current turn is played counter-clockwise starting with the First Player.
- **Marshal:** The Marshal never goes on the roof. Bandits can never stay inside a car where the Marshal is.
- **Neutral Bullets:** If the Neutral Bullet card deck is depleted, bandits no longer receive Neutral Bullets from the Marshal or Events; remove the deck from the game.
- Each bandit has a unique special ability on their Character card (e.g., Doc draws 7 cards; Ghost plays first card face-down; Belle cannot be targeted if another bandit can be; etc.).
- If a bandit has no loot when punched, no loot is dropped but the bandit still moves.
- You can never leave the train; movement stops at the Caboose or Locomotive.

### Events (on Train Station Round cards)
Events occur at the **end of a Round, after Phase 2**. Examples include:
- **Passengers' Rebellion:** Bandits inside cars each receive 1 Neutral Bullet.
- **Take It All!:** Place the second Strongbox inside the car where the Marshal is.
- **Braking:** All bandits on roofs move to the roof of the Caboose.
- **Swivel Arm (Angry Marshal):** Marshal shoots bandits on the roof of his car (each gets a Neutral Bullet), then moves 1 car toward the Caboose.
- **Pickpocketing:** Each bandit alone on their spot may take 1 Purse token from that spot.
- **Marshal's Revenge:** Each bandit on the roof above the Marshal loses their least valuable Purse.
- **Hostage-Taking of the Conductor:** Each bandit in the Locomotive or on its roof receives $250.
