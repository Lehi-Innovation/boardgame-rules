## Overview

Civilization: The Boardgame (Eagle Games, 2002) is a civilization-building game based on Sid Meier's video game franchise. Players guide civilizations from ancient times through the modern era, building cities, researching technologies, raising armies, and competing for territory. The game can be played with Standard Rules (simpler) or Advanced Rules (more complex). Victory is determined by scoring the most victory points at game end; there are no "culture points" — the in-game currency is gold.

## Components

- Large game board (36" x 46" world map)
- Plastic settlement pieces (villages, towns, cities, metropolises) in six player colors, plus tan-colored pieces
- Military unit miniatures (infantry, cavalry, artillery, fleets, aircraft — each era has distinct units)
- Settler pieces and flag bearer pieces
- Technology cards (53 total, across four eras) and Wonder cards (25 total)
- City cards (used for resource tracking in Standard rules; represent individual settlements in Advanced rules)
- City improvement cards (Advanced rules only)
- Exploration markers (face-down on land regions: resources, events, terrain, or no encounter)
- Coin/gold tokens
- Four dice (two red, two white)
- Reference card and technology flow chart card

## Setup

1. Place the game board centrally. Mix all exploration markers and place one face-down on each named land region; return unused markers to the box (remove two "no encounter" markers per player before mixing for more resources).
2. Each player receives 20 gold, 2 villages, 2 swordsmen, and 2 settlers in their chosen color.
3. Roll two dice; highest roller goes first (reroll ties). The first player selects a starting region and places a village, swordsman, and settler there. Continue clockwise until all players have one region, then reverse order so the last player picks second, continuing counterclockwise until all players have two starting regions.
4. (Advanced rules only) Deal each player one ancient technology without prerequisites from a shuffled subset.
5. Flip exploration markers in all starting regions immediately; ignore (remove) plagues, deserts, and minor civilizations found during setup.
6. Roll two dice again to determine first player for the game turn. Play continues clockwise.

## Turn Structure

A game turn has exactly four phases. During each phase all players act in order (starting player first, then clockwise); all players complete a phase before the next phase begins. After the turn ends, the player to the left of the current starting player becomes the new starting player.

1. **Movement and Battles Phase:** Move any or all military units and settlers (each has a movement point allowance). Battles are declared and resolved after all movement is complete. Once you start resolving battles you cannot move any more pieces.
2. **Trade Phase:** Negotiate resource trades and other deals with other players (in Advanced rules, only players within trade range may trade).
3. **Production Phase:** Calculate gold income from cities, critical resource roll, resources, and monopolies. Collect gold from the bank.
4. **Purchase Phase:** Spend accumulated gold to buy military units, settlers, settlements (villages), settlement upgrades, technologies, and (in Advanced rules) city improvements.

## Actions

- **Move and Explore:** Move settlers into land regions with face-down exploration markers to reveal them. Exploration markers contain resources, events (free technology, treasure, minor civilization, plague), terrain types, or no encounter. Settlers are also required to found new settlements.
- **Fight Battles:** When you move military units into a space where another player has units, either side may declare battle. Units fight one-on-one (each player secretly selects one unit, then both roll dice with modifiers). Battle continues until one side is eliminated; no retreat once battle starts.
- **Build Settlements:** During the purchase phase, replace a settler with a village (pay the cost). Settlements can be upgraded one size per turn (village → town → city → metropolis) with technology and gold.
- **Research Technology:** Purchase technology cards during the purchase phase. In Standard rules all same-era techs are generic. In Advanced rules each has a specific cost, prerequisites, owner bonuses, and fee charges.
- **Acquire Wonders:** In Standard rules, wonders are earned by being first to announce reaching era milestones. In Advanced rules, wonders are rewards granted when certain technologies are purchased.
- **Trade:** In the trade phase, exchange resource city cards and other items with other players.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Standard Rules
The game ends at the end of the turn in which any player owns their third modern era technology. All players then count victory points; the player with the most wins.

**Standard victory points:**
- Each village: 1 point
- Each town: 2 points
- Each city: 3 points
- Each metropolis: 4 points
- Each technology owned: 2 points
- Each wonder of the world owned: 3 points

### Advanced Rules
The game length is chosen before play:
- **Short game:** Ends in the medieval era. When a player purchases a medieval technology, roll one die; if the result is ≤ the total number of medieval technologies owned by all players, the game ends at the end of that turn.
- **Medium game:** Ends in the gunpowder/industrial era. Same trigger mechanic using gunpowder/industrial technologies.
- **Long game:** Ends when one of these four events occurs: (1) Total conquest — only one player has settlements remaining; (2) Diplomatic victory — a player who owns the United Nations wonder declares the game over; (3) Military victory — a player who owns the Apollo Program wonder declares the game over; (4) Technology/space victory — any player purchases the Alpha Centauri Colony Ship wonder (costs 200 gold after Fusion is purchased).

Unless the game ends by total conquest, all players tally victory points; the player with the most wins.

**Advanced victory points:**
- Settlement size: villages 1 pt, towns 2 pts, cities 3 pts, metropolises 4 pts
- Each wonder of the world owned: 2 points
- Each seminal discovery owned: 4 points
- Bonus points if the game ended with a diplomatic victory: the United Nations owner gets +5 points
- Bonus points if military victory: every player gets +1 point per military unit they own
- Bonus points if technology/space victory: every player gets +1 point per technology card they own

Note: Technologies are NOT worth victory points in the Advanced rules except via seminal discoveries and the technology/space victory bonus.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Standard vs. Advanced Rules:** The standard rules treat all same-era technologies as identical (no prerequisites, generic bonuses) and wonders as milestone achievements. The advanced rules give each technology a specific cost, prerequisites via a tech tree, individual owner bonuses, and fee charges to non-owners who build associated units or improvements.
- **Eras:** The game is divided into four eras (Ancient, Medieval, Gunpowder/Industrial, Modern). In Standard rules an era ends when any player purchases the third technology of the current era or the last remaining technology in the era. In Advanced rules an era ends when a player purchases the first technology of the next era. Units, technologies, and wonders from ended eras become unavailable.
- **Terrain exploration markers:** Desert (cannot build settlements), Mountains (village only, cannot grow), Jungle/Forest (town maximum), Fertile/Productive (settlement counts as one size larger for Standard rules production, or uses the parenthetical value in Advanced rules). Terrain effects do not prevent settling but restrict growth.
- **Minimum gold production:** A civilization can never receive less than 10 gold per production phase.
- **Wonders (Standard rules):** Only one player may own each wonder. Wonders are earned by announcing a milestone first — any late announcement loses the wonder to the faster player.
- **Wonders (Advanced rules):** Wonders come as owner bonuses when specific technologies are purchased; they are not separately constructed or announced.
- **Government types:** The source notes that government-type advantages are not included in this game and are planned for a future expansion. There are no Government cards in the base game.
- **Barbarians:** The source does not include barbarian rules. Minor civilizations (from exploration markers) are competed for by all players rolling dice (adjusted by number of settlements owned); the winner places a village and army unit there.
- **Military unit upgrades (Advanced rules):** Units automatically upgrade to the best type available within their era when a new technology enabling a better same-era unit is purchased. Exception: catapults upgrade to trebuchets when Engineering is purchased.
- **Losing your last settlement (Advanced rules):** You are eliminated. The player who eliminated you takes all your gold and wonders; your technologies enter a common pool where they can be used as prerequisites and allow building associated units and improvements but are not owned.

## Player Reference

**Standard Rules Production Formula (per turn):**
1. City production: village = 1 gold, town = 2 gold, city = 3 gold, metropolis = 4 gold (fertile region: treat settlement as one size larger)
2. Critical resource: starting player rolls 2 dice, consults table; if you own the matching resource city card, double your total city production
3. Technology × unique resources: multiply total technologies owned by total unique resource types owned
4. Monopoly bonus: 3 of same resource = +20 gold; 4 = +40 gold; 5 = +80 gold
5. Minimum: always receive at least 10 gold

**Standard End Condition:** Game ends when any player owns 3 modern era technologies (or last tech of modern era is purchased).

**Battle Resolution:**
- Each player secretly selects one military unit; both reveal and roll
- Ancient = 1 die; Medieval = 2 dice; Gunpowder/Industrial = 3 dice; Modern = 4 dice
- Modifiers: battlefield superiority (Cavalry beats Infantry; Infantry beats Artillery; Artillery beats Cavalry) adds current era number (1–4) to winner's roll; scientific superiority (Standard only) adds +1 per 2 technologies owned; defending a settlement (Advanced only) adds +1; aircraft adds extra dice
- Highest total wins; tie = both eliminated
- No retreat once battle begins; continue until one side has no units

**Standard Victory Points:** village 1, town 2, city 3, metropolis 4; each technology 2 pts; each wonder 3 pts

**Advanced Victory Points:** village 1, town 2, city 3, metropolis 4; each wonder 2 pts; each seminal discovery 4 pts; bonus points depend on game-end condition
