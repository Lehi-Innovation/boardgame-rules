## Overview

Bismarck is a two-player naval wargame (designed by Jack Greene, Jr.) simulating the historic hunt for the German battleship Bismarck in May 1941. One player commands the German forces (Bismarck and Prinz Eugen), the other controls the British Royal Navy. The game is composed of three games of increasing complexity: the Basic Game, Intermediate Game, and Advanced Game. The Basic Game uses a double-blind search system on two identical search boards; both players secretly move ships and air units, then call out zones to search for the opponent. Combat between located ships is resolved on a separate Battle Board. The German player earns victory points by sinking enemy ships and convoys; the British player earns victory points by sinking or damaging the Bismarck and Prinz Eugen and preventing convoy losses. The player with the most victory points at the end of play wins.

## Components

- Two identical search boards (one per player; each is a map of the North Atlantic divided into square zones)
- One Battle Board (for resolving ship-to-ship combat)
- One sheet of die-cut counters (ship counters, air unit counters, and markers)
- Six Player Aid Cards (Order of Battle, Time Record Track, Visibility Tables, Search and Shadow Tables)
- Two Range Finders and Battle Maneuver Gauges (Advanced Game only)
- Hit Record Pad (for tracking ship damage and salvoes)
- Two dice (one colored, one white)
- Rules Manual

## Setup

1. Each player takes one search board (kept hidden from the opponent), one Player Aid Card with the Basic Game side up, and one sheet from the Hit Record Pad.
2. Each player places the ships and air units listed in the Basic Game Order of Appearance on their own search board per the Order of Appearance chart. All other counters are set aside.
3. Place one Battle Board counter for each battleship, cruiser, and aircraft carrier aside with the Battle Board.
4. Place the Basic Game Tables card in a convenient shared location.
5. Both players start with their visibility markers at level '4' on the Visibility Track. The first turn begins at '1200 May 22'.

## Turn Structure

Each turn represents four hours of real time. The nine phases are performed in this order:

1. **Unit Availability Phase:** The British player places any ships entering play this turn on the search board. Air units available after rest and refit are returned to their bases.
2. **Visibility Phase:** The German player rolls two dice (except turn 1, which is automatically level '4') and adjusts the visibility marker on the Visibility Track; the result determines which zones can be searched this turn. Ships may also enter or break from task forces or convoys during this phase.
3. **Shadow Determination Phase:** Either player may designate one ship, task force, or air unit in the same zone as a located enemy ship to shadow it. The shadowing unit must meet evasion requirements (ships) or endurance/search requirements (air units). The shadowed ship moves, then one die is rolled on the Shadow Table to determine whether contact is held or lost.
4. **Air Movement Phase:** All air units in play may move up to their maximum speed. Air units that must return to base are removed from the search board and placed on the Time Record Chart.
5. **Ship Movement Phase:** Any ships not moved in the Shadow Determination Phase may move up to their maximum speed.
6. **Search Phase:** Each player may call out zones in which they have sufficient search strength (ships and/or air units meeting the current visibility level). For each zone called, the opposing player must state whether any ships are present and, if so, their number and general type.
7. **Air Attack Phase:** Bomber air units in attack mode and in the same zone as a located enemy ship may launch air strikes. British bombers may make two strikes per day turn; German bombers make one. Air attacks cannot be made at night.
8. **Naval Combat Phase:** If both players have located ships in the same zone, either player may initiate combat on the Battle Board.
9. **Chance Phase:** The British player rolls two dice for each German ship and consults the Chance Table; results may force a German ship to reveal its position (General Search), announce a nearby zone (HUFF-DUFF), or allow a German ship to locate and destroy a convoy for victory points. At the end of this phase, both players advance their time markers by one turn.

## Actions

### Ship Movement
Cruisers may move up to two zones per turn. Battleships and aircraft carriers with maximum speed '2' may also move two zones, but not in consecutive turns, and must expend one fuel factor to do so. Battleships with maximum speed '1' (Rodney, Ramillies, Revenge) move up to one zone per turn. Ships that exhaust their fuel enter emergency movement (up to one zone every second turn, on turns labelled 'C'). Ships may also switch to patrol mode (flip counter to back side) for increased search strength but cannot move while patrolling.

**German Breakout Bonus (Turn 1 only):** Each German ship may move three zones free, four zones for one fuel factor, or five zones for two fuel factors.

### Search
During the Search Phase, players call out zone grid-coordinates where their ships or air units have combined search strength meeting or exceeding the current visibility level. The opposing player must confirm whether any ships (never air units) are in the called zone and state their number and general type. British coastal zones (Faeroe Islands, Shetland Islands, coast of Ireland, coast of Great Britain, Hvalfiord) have an inherent search strength of '4' (reduced to '3' at night). German coastal zones (Norway and France) also have '4' inherent search strength. Fog completely blocks search in affected zones.

### Shadowing
Only the British player may shadow located enemy ships. To shadow, assign one ship, task force, or air unit in the same zone as the located enemy. Ships must have an evasion rating equal to or greater than the ship they are shadowing. After the shadowed ship moves, one die is rolled on the Shadow Table to determine if contact is held. Shadow must be reaffirmed each turn. A shadowing unit cannot search in the same turn.

### Tactical (Naval) Combat on the Battle Board
When opposing ships are located in the same zone, either player may choose to fight. Combat is initiated by one ship or task force against one opposing ship or task force; multiple separate combats may occur in the same zone but each requires the ships involved to be committed. The defending player places ships in the center hex; the attacker rolls one die to determine entry edge. Combat proceeds in rounds:
- Ships move in order of lowest to highest evasion rating (attackers first if tied), up to the maximum allowed by the Combat Movement Chart.
- After movement, each ship designates its salvoes against targets within 'A' range (1–3 hexes) or '6' range (4–6 hexes). Guns must be in the correct sector (bow, stern, port or starboard secondary) to fire at a given target. Salvoes at 'A' range use the full count; at '6' range, halve the salvo count (round down). Each salvo: roll two dice and consult the battle board for hit results. Damage is recorded on the Hit Record Pad.
- All fire is simultaneous within a round; damage takes effect at the end of the round.
- A ship is sunk when all its midships boxes are marked out. When the Bismarck is sunk, the game ends immediately.
- After the third round, each player may attempt to bring reinforcing ships in by rolling one die (a '4' or higher allows entry, with successive rolls gaining a cumulative '1' bonus).
- Either player may attempt to disengage; if successful the withdrawing ship makes a bonus withdrawal move.

### Air Combat
Bomber air units in attack mode may strike any ship located by search in their zone during the Air Attack Phase. British bombers make two strikes per day turn; German bombers make one strike. Air attacks are not allowed at night. LR recon units cannot attack. For each strike, the attacker rolls two dice and consults the Bomber Table.

### Convoys
Two British battleships start the game assigned to convoy escort duty. During the Chance Phase, if a German ship meets location and mode requirements and has not been located or shadowed that turn, it may destroy a convoy. The first convoy destroyed earns 6 German victory points; subsequent convoys earn 6, 8, 10, and 12 points respectively. Only one convoy may be destroyed per day. A German ship that destroys a convoy cannot move on the search board the following turn.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when any of the following occur:
1. The Bismarck is sunk.
2. The Bismarck enters a friendly port (Trondheim, Bergen, Brest, or St. Nazaire).
3. The German player has accumulated more victory points than it is possible for the British player to score.
4. The time limit is reached (end of the '1200 May 27' turn in the Basic Game).

At the end of play, both players total their victory points. The player with the most points wins; equal totals is a draw.

**British Victory Point Schedule:**
- Sinking the Bismarck: 30 points
- Sinking the Prinz Eugen: 10 points
- Bismarck enters port with at least one midships hit: 6 points
- Prinz Eugen enters port with at least one midships hit: 2 points
- Bismarck enters a friendly port on a May 22 turn (or has not left Bergen by end of 2000 May 22): 14 points; May 23: 12 points; May 24: 10 points; May 25: 8 points; May 26: 6 points; May 27: 4 points
- Bismarck at sea at end of game with evasion level 21 or less: 6 points
- Prinz Eugen at sea at end of game with evasion level 21 or less: 2 points
- Each German air unit unable to return to base: 2 points
- No convoy sunk: 4 points

**German Victory Point Schedule:**
- Sinking the Victorious: 24 points
- Sinking the Ark Royal: 20 points
- Sinking King George V or Prince of Wales (each): 16 points
- Sinking the Hood: 14 points
- Sinking the Rodney: 12 points
- Sinking Repulse or Renown (each): 10 points
- Sinking Revenge or Ramillies (each): 8 points
- Each heavy cruiser sunk: 6 points
- Each light cruiser sunk: 4 points
- British player mobilizes a ship on escort duty: 1 point
- Each British air unit unable to return to base: 2 points
- Convoys destroyed: 1st=6, 2nd=6, 3rd=8, 4th=10, 5th=12 points
- Bismarck at sea with damage but evasion level 22+ at game end: 2 points
- Bismarck at sea with no midships damage at game end: 4 points
- Prinz Eugen at sea with no midships damage at game end: 2 points

**Damage to surviving ships:** Each player also receives 2 points per midships hit on surviving enemy battleships or aircraft carriers, and 1 point per midships hit on surviving enemy cruisers.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Fog:** At the start of turn 1 Bergen is automatically in fog (no search). Each subsequent turn, when the visibility change would also result in fog, neither player may search any fog zone that turn, and no combat may occur in a fog zone.
- **Night:** The '2000' turn is a night turn in zones south of the 55° line; '2400' is always night. At night, ships in naval combat may only fire at 'A' range, using the 'B' range tables. Air units cannot attack at night. The Chance Table only produces HUFF-DUFF results at night.
- **Fuel:** Battleships and aircraft carriers with maximum speed '2' must expend one fuel factor each time they move two zones. When all fuel is expended they enter emergency movement (one zone per 'C'-labelled turn only). Cruisers are exempt from fuel rules.
- **Task Forces:** Two or more friendly ships in the same zone may combine into a task force during the Visibility Phase. The task force moves at the speed of its slowest ship. Only the task force's collective search strength (a single ship's patrol strength if patrolling) is used in search; individual ship strengths within the task force are ignored.
- **Evasion:** A ship can only initiate naval combat against an enemy ship or task force whose evasion rating is equal to or less than its own (unless the opponent accepts combat).
- **Air Endurance:** Each air unit has a maximum number of turns airborne. If it cannot return to base by its last airborne turn, it is eliminated and the opponent scores 2 victory points.
- **Port Safety:** Ships in port cannot be located by sea search; they can only be located by air search. German ships cannot enter Irish Sea zones or British port zones.
- **Simultaneous Fire:** All naval fire within a combat round is resolved simultaneously; damage takes effect only after all fire is completed.

|-------|----------|
| German | Bismarck (BB), Prinz Eugen (CA), German air units |

**Game End Triggers:**
1. Bismarck sunk
2. Bismarck enters friendly port
3. German VP total exceeds maximum British can score
4. Time limit reached (end of '1200 May 27' turn, Basic Game)

**Winner:** Most victory points; draw if equal.

**Key British VP sources:** Sinking Bismarck (30 pts), Sinking Prinz Eugen (10 pts), convoy intact bonus (4 pts), damage/evasion bonuses.

**Key German VP sources:** Sinking British capital ships (8–24 pts each), destroying convoys (6–12 pts each), Bismarck surviving undamaged (4 pts).

**Visibility:** Level 1–9 plus 'X' (no search). Determined each turn by German player's dice roll on the Visibility Change Table. Starting level: '4'.

**Night turns:** '2000' south of 55° line; always '2400'. Air cannot attack; naval combat limited to 'A' range; Chance Table gives only HUFF-DUFF.
