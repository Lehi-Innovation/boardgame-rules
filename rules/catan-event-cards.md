## Overview

The Catan Card Game is a two-player card game where each player builds a principality by expanding settlements into cities, constructing buildings, and recruiting knights. Players manage resources (grain, wool, ore, brick, lumber) obtained through dice rolls and card play. The game uses an event die and a production die each turn, with 62 expansion cards providing buildings, knights, and special abilities. The first player to reach a set number of victory points wins.

## Components

- 120 cards total:
  - 9 red shield starting cards (Player A)
  - 9 blue shield starting cards (Player B)
  - 11 region cards, 7 road cards, 5 settlement cards, 7 city cards
  - 10 event cards
  - 62 expansion cards (yellow, green, red text fields)
- 1 event die, 1 production die
- 1 windmill token, 1 knight token

## Setup

1. Sort cards by back symbols.
2. Each player arranges their 9 starting cards: 2 settlements connected by a road, with regions flanking each settlement.
3. Place event cards face-down in the center.
4. Place region, road, settlement, and city development card stacks in a row.
5. Shuffle expansion cards and divide into 5 roughly equal stacks.

## Turn Structure

Each turn has five steps in order:
1. **Event:** Roll both dice. Resolve the Event Die first. Events affect both players.
2. **Production:** Both players receive resources. All Region Cards in both principalities whose number matches the Production Die roll produce 1 resource (rotate the matching card to the next higher value).
3. **Actions:** Build roads, settlements, cities; play expansion cards; trade resources. May be taken in any order, as many times as desired.
4. **Draw:** Check your hand size. Your hand limit is normally 3 cards (increased by buildings like Abbey or Library). If you have fewer cards than your limit, draw up to the limit. Each draw you may either: (a) take the top card of any expansion stack for free, or (b) pay 2 resources of any type to search any one stack and pick any one card. If an Event Card causes you to exceed your hand limit when it is not your turn, discard excess cards immediately.
5. **Pass the Dice:** Your turn ends; pass to your opponent.

## Actions

- **Build roads:** Connect settlements (cost: 2 brick + 1 lumber per road).
- **Build settlements:** Place at road endpoints (cost: 1 each of brick, lumber, grain, wool). Worth 1 VP.
- **Upgrade to cities:** Convert settlements (cost: 3 ore + 2 grain). Worth 2 VP.
- **Draw expansion cards:** Pay gold or resources to draw from expansion stacks.
- **Play expansion cards:** Buildings and knights attach to your settlements/cities. Each provides ongoing benefits.
- **Trade:** 3:1 with the supply, or use harbor/counting house for better rates.

**Special tokens:**
- **Windmill Token:** Awarded to the player who (1) has more Commerce Points than their opponent AND (2) has at least one city. If tied for Commerce Points, or if the leading player has no city, the token returns to the center. Commerce Points come from certain buildings (e.g., Marketplace, Harbor, Mint) indicated by a windmill icon.
- **Knight Token:** Awarded to the player with the highest total Strength Points (black number next to the iron glove on each Knight card). If tied, the token returns to the center. Note: Strength Points (for the Knight Token) are distinct from Tournament Points (red number next to the knight's head, used when the Tournament event is rolled).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The first player to accumulate the required victory points wins (typically 12 VP). Points come from settlements (1 VP), cities (2 VP), VP expansion cards, and special achievements.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Each settlement can have up to 2 expansion cards; each city can have up to 4.
- Event die results include: Year of Plenty (extra resources), Brigands (attack), Tournament (knight comparison), Trade (commerce), and others.
- The knight token provides extra strength and VP.
- Players can only hold a limited number of resources; excess must be discarded.

## Player Reference

| Building | Cost | VP |
|----------|------|----|
| Road | 2 brick + 1 lumber | 0 |
| Settlement | 1 brick + 1 lumber + 1 grain + 1 wool | 1 |
| City | 3 ore + 2 grain | 2 |

**Win condition:** First to 12 VP on their turn.

**Knight Token:** Highest total Strength Points (black iron-glove number). Tied = no one holds it.

**Windmill Token:** Most Commerce Points AND at least 1 city. Tied or no city = no one holds it.

**Hand limit:** 3 cards by default; Abbey/Library each add +1. Draw up to hand limit at end of turn.
