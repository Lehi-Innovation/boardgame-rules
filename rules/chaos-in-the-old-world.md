## Overview

Chaos in the Old World is an asymmetric area-control strategy game set in the Warhammer Fantasy universe. Each player takes the role of one of four Ruinous Powers (Chaos Gods): Khorne (blood and battle), Nurgle (plague and disease), Tzeentch (magic and manipulation), or Slaanesh (pleasure and temptation). Players summon followers, play Chaos cards, and corrupt regions of the Old World to earn victory points or advance their unique Threat dial to win. If no player wins before the Old World deck runs out, all players lose.

## Components

- 1 game board (9 regions, Threat dials, VP track)
- 4 Threat Dials with plastic connectors
- 4 Power Sheets (one per Chaos Power)
- 196 cardboard tokens (Power markers, VP markers, Dial Advancement counters, Corruption tokens, Old World tokens)
- 149 cards (96 Chaos cards, 20 Upgrade cards, 5 Ruination cards, 28 Old World cards)
- 5 dice
- 45 plastic followers:
  - Khorne: 1 Bloodthirster, 6 Bloodletters, 4 Cultists
  - Nurgle: 1 Great Unclean One, 5 Plaguebearers, 6 Cultists
  - Tzeentch: 1 Lord of Change, 3 Horrors, 8 Cultists
  - Slaanesh: 1 Keeper of Secrets, 3 Daemonettes, 6 Cultists

## Setup

1. Attach Threat dials to the board (Khorne top, Nurgle right, Tzeentch bottom, Slaanesh left).
2. Each player takes a Power sheet, followers, Chaos cards (draw 3), power marker, VP marker, corruption tokens, and dial advancement counters.
3. Separate 2 Noble tokens, 3 Warpstone tokens, and 4 Peasant tokens; randomly place one in each of the 9 regions.
4. Shuffle all Old World cards; deal 7 facedown as the Old World deck (4-player) or 8 (3-player). Return remaining cards to the box.
5. Place Ruination cards nearby.
6. Set VP markers to 0.

## Turn Structure

Each game round has 6 phases:

### Phase 1: Old World Phase
Draw a new Old World card and place it face-up on the Old World track. Apply effects of expiring cards. Populate regions with tokens as indicated.

### Phase 2: Draw Phase
Each player draws Chaos cards according to his Power sheet's draw phase instruction. There is no hand size limit. Each player also resets his power marker to the highlighted starting space on his power point track (unused power points do not carry over).

### Phase 3: Summoning Phase
Players take turns (Khorne > Nurgle > Tzeentch > Slaanesh) spending power points to summon followers and play Chaos cards in regions. Each follower and card has a power point cost. Continue until all players pass.

- **Cultists:** Cheap; place corruption tokens
- **Warriors:** Mid-cost; strong in battle
- **Greater Daemons:** Expensive; very powerful

### Phase 4: Battle Phase
Resolve battles in each region (in numbered order) where opposing followers coexist. Each player rolls dice equal to their total battle value in that region. Hits scored on 4+ (5+ for some); assign hits to enemy figures. Removed figures return to owners. Khorne excels here.

### Phase 5: Corruption Phase
The corruption phase has two steps, resolved in standard region order (Norsca first, Badlands last):

**Step 1 — Domination:** For each region, each player calculates their domination value: the quantity of their figures in the region plus the sum of the printed costs of their Chaos cards played in the region. The player with the highest domination value compares it to the region's Resistance. If it strictly **exceeds** the Resistance (equaling it is not sufficient), that player scores VP equal to the region's Conquest Value. If two or more players tie for the highest domination value, no one scores for that region.

**Step 2 — Corruption:** Each player places 1 corruption token in the region for each of their Cultists there. Then count all corruption tokens (including Warpstone tokens) in the region. If the total is 12 or more, the region is ruined: place the next Ruination card on it; each player who placed at least one corruption token this step scores points as indicated on the Ruination card. More points will be scored from that Ruination card in the End Phase.

### Phase 6: End Phase
Steps in order:
1. **Remove Chaos Cards:** Each player removes their Chaos cards from the board to their discard pile.
2. **Resolve Hero Tokens:** In each region with a Hero token, the player with the greatest Threat who also has at least one figure there must remove one of their figures from the board. Repeat for each Hero token in that region.
3. **Resolve Old World Cards:** Carry out "When Old World cards are resolved…" effects on the Old World track.
4. **Score Ruined Regions:** For each Ruination card placed this round, the player with the most corruption tokens in the ruined region scores the "first" value; the player with the second-most scores the "second" value. Then turn the Ruination card facedown and return all corruption tokens in that region to their owners.
5. **Advance Threat Dials:** Every player with at least one dial advancement counter advances their dial one tick and carries out the revealed instruction. The player with the most counters advances one additional tick. Then return all counters to the stockpile.
6. **Check for Game End** (see Victory Conditions).

Start player does not rotate — Power order (Khorne, Nurgle, Tzeentch, Slaanesh) governs turn order.

## Actions

### Summoning
Spend power points to place figures on the board or play Chaos cards from hand.

| Follower Type | Typical Cost | Role |
|---------------|-------------|------|
| Cultist | 1 PP | Places corruption; cheap |
| Warrior | 2-3 PP | Strong in battle |
| Greater Daemon | 4-5 PP | Very powerful; unique abilities |

### Chaos Cards
Each power has unique cards that manipulate battles, corruption, movement, and more. Cards are played during the Summoning phase and may have instant or persistent effects.

### Upgrade Cards
Each power has 5 Upgrade cards revealed through dial advancement. These provide permanent bonuses.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Game end conditions are checked in this order at the end of each round:**

1. **Threat Dial Victory:** If one or more players' dials show "Victory!", that player wins. If tied, the tied player with more VP wins; remaining ties are shared.
2. **50 Victory Points:** If one or more players have 50 or more VP, the player with the most VP wins. If tied, the tied player with the highest Threat wins.
3. **Five Regions Ruined:** If five regions have been ruined, the player with the most VP wins. If tied, the tied player with the highest Threat wins.
4. **Old World Deck Exhausted:** If no cards remain in the Old World deck, all players lose.

**Threat Dial Advancement Conditions** (each dial advances once per triggering event per round, regardless of excess):
| Power | Advances When... |
|-------|-----------------|
| Khorne | Kills 2+ enemy figures in the same region (in a single battle) |
| Slaanesh | Places 2+ corruption tokens in the same region (in a single corruption step) |
| Tzeentch | Corruption step: the source text refers to magic symbols as Tzeentch's dial advancement condition; exact wording is on the Power sheet |
| Nurgle | Exact condition is on the Power sheet |

Each time a condition is met, place one dial advancement counter on the dial. A single event yields at most one counter regardless of how large the excess. In the End Phase, each player with one or more counters advances their dial one tick; the player with the most counters advances an additional tick.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Each region has both a Resistance value and a Conquest Value. Initially they are the same number, but game effects that modify one do not necessarily affect the other (e.g., Skaven tokens lower Resistance only; Noble tokens raise Conquest Value only).
- **Hero Tokens (End Phase, not Battle Phase):** In each region with a Hero token, the player with the greatest Threat who has at least one figure there must remove one of their own figures. This is resolved in the End Phase, not during battle.
- **Ruined Regions:** Once a Ruination card is placed in a region (face-up initially, turned face-down after End Phase scoring), no new Chaos cards may be played there, no new Old World tokens may be placed there, domination is not checked there, and no corruption tokens are placed there. Figures may still be present and battle still occurs in ruined regions.
- If five regions have been ruined and a sixth would be ruined, it is not ruined (no ruination card remains); no ruination VP are scored for it.
- The Threat dial tracks victory progress and also unlocks Upgrade cards and other bonuses at specific positions via the revealed dial instruction.
- Populous regions (marked) are referenced by various game effects.
- **3-player game:** One Power is not used. Deal Power sheets randomly or by consensus; unused components return to the box. The Old World deck is 8 cards (vs. 7 for four players).
- **Corruption token supply:** The supply of corruption tokens should be considered unlimited — substitute convenient markers if the 29-token supply is exhausted.

## Player Reference

**Round phases:** Old World > Draw > Summoning > Battle > Corruption > End

**Victory (check in this order each End Phase):**
1. Threat dial shows "Victory!" → that player wins (tie: most VP)
2. Any player has 50+ VP → most VP wins (tie: highest Threat)
3. Five regions ruined → most VP wins (tie: highest Threat)
4. Old World deck empty → all players lose

**Battle:** Roll dice equal to total attack values of your figures in the region; hits on 4, 5, or 6; a 6 explodes (roll one extra die). Assign hits to enemy figures; a figure is killed when hits equal or exceed its defense value. Tipped figures are removed after all players have rolled. Excess hits are lost.

**Domination (Corruption Phase, Step 1):**
- Domination Value = quantity of your figures + sum of printed costs of your Chaos cards in the region
- You dominate only if your value **strictly exceeds** the region's Resistance (equaling it is not enough)
- Ties for highest domination value = no one dominates
- Dominator scores VP equal to the region's Conquest Value

**Corruption (Corruption Phase, Step 2):**
- Each Cultist places 1 corruption token in its region
- Count all corruption tokens + Warpstone tokens in the region; 12+ = Ruination

**Dial Advancement Counters:** Collected during the round; cashed in during the End Phase. Every player with counters advances one tick; player with most counters advances one additional tick.
