## Overview

A Few Acres of Snow is a two-player deck-building war game about the struggle between Britain and France for control of North America in the 17th-18th centuries. Players use cards representing locations and empire resources to settle new territories, wage sieges, raid opponents, and trade for money. Cards cycle through draw deck, hand, and discard pile. The game ends through conquest of key locations, exhaustion of settlement pieces, or accumulated territorial control.

## Components

- 1 game board (map of North America)
- 12 British town discs, 9 French town discs
- 18 British village cubes, 18 French village cubes
- 2 siege location markers, 2 siege strength markers
- 12 fortification discs
- 110 cards (British, French, and neutral empire cards)
- Money: 15 silver (1 each), 15 gold (5 each)
- 2 player aid cards

## Setup

1. Each player takes: initial draw deck (dark border), available location cards (light border), available empire cards (light border), wooden pieces.
2. Place pieces on starting locations (red squares = British villages, red circles = British towns; blue = French).
3. British starts with 12 money; French starts with 5 money.
4. Each player shuffles initial draw deck, draws 5 cards.
5. Neutral empire cards placed face-up beside the board.
6. British player takes the first turn. Both players perform only 1 action on their respective first turns; 2 actions thereafter.

## Turn Structure

1. **Check siege victory** (if applicable).
2. **Perform 2 actions** (1 on each player's first turn). Same action may be taken twice. Free actions do not count.
3. **Draw up to 5 cards** from draw deck. When draw deck is empty, shuffle discard pile to form new deck. You may have more than 5 cards (no discard required).

## Actions

### Expansion Actions
| Action | Requirements |
|--------|-------------|
| **Settle a location** | Play: connecting location card + transport card (bateaux/ship/wagon) + settler card (if location requires). Place village cube. Take that location card from available pile. |
| **Develop a location** | Play: location card for a village you control + settler card. Replace village cube with town disc. |
| **Fortify a location** | Play: location card + fortification card + pay 3 money to the bank. Place fortification disc. |

### Military Actions
| Action | Requirements |
|--------|-------------|
| **Besiege a location** | Play: connecting location card + transport card + military cards. Place siege marker. |
| **Reinforce a siege** | Play additional military cards to an ongoing siege. |
| **Raid** | Play one or more cards that state they can be used to raid (no location card played). One card raids a location directly connected to one you control; each additional raid-capable card extends range by one connection. Opponent may block with a card that states it can block a raid, or the location card for the raided location. If unblocked, take the cube or disc from the raided location. |
| **Ambush** | Play a card that has the ability to ambush. If unblocked, the opponent must return one card with the ambush-vulnerable symbol from their hand or reserve to their empire deck. |
| **Priest/Indian leader** | Special card effects for managing Native Americans. |

### Economic Actions
| Action | Requirements |
|--------|-------------|
| **Take money** | Play one location card and take money from the bank equal to the value shown in the gold circle on that card. |
| **Merchant** | Play: one card with a ship symbol, then one or two cards with gold symbols. Earn the amount indicated. |
| **Trader** | Play: trader card + fur cards. Earn 2 money per fur card. |
| **Piracy** | French only. Play the Louisbourg card and a ship symbol card. Take 2 money from the British player (shortfall paid by the bank). |

### Card Management Actions
| Action | Requirements |
|--------|-------------|
| **Draft empire card** | Take 1 card from available empire cards or neutral cards (pay cost if any). Goes to discard pile. |
| **Discard cards** | Remove cards from hand to discard pile (first card free; each additional card costs 1 money). |
| **Place in reserve** | Set 1 card aside (up to 5 cards maximum in reserve). |
| **Retrieve reserve** | Free action: take all cards from your reserve back into your hand, paying 1 money per card retrieved. |
| **Governor** | Play governor card: return 1 or 2 cards from your hand to the appropriate available cards pile (they may be re-drafted later). |
| **Intendant** | French player only. Pay 2 money to the bank, then take one card of your choice from your discard pile and place it in your hand. |
| **Home Support** | Free action: draw 3 cards from your draw deck. |
| **Withdraw from siege** | Free action: abandon a siege; treated as losing the siege. |
| **Pass** | Do nothing with an action. |

### Siege Resolution
- **Attacker wins** if the siege strength marker shows the attacker has an advantage of 2 or more.
- **Defender wins** if the siege strength marker shows the defender has an advantage of 1 or more.
- Both players check the siege tracks at the start of their own turn.
- Fortification adds 2 to the defender's starting strength. Location defence modifier also adjusts starting strength.
- After a siege ends, the loser must select one empire card from their siege card space to return to their available empire deck (or neutral empire display) before placing the remainder onto their discard pile. The loser cannot choose to lose a location card this way.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

### Instant Victory
- **British:** Win a siege in Quebec.
- **French:** Win a siege in Boston or New York.

### End Game Triggers
The game ends when, at the start of a player's turn with no sieges in progress:
- One player has placed all of their discs **or** all of their cubes onto the board, **or**
- One player has captured 12 or more points worth of enemy cubes/discs (cubes = 2 pts each, discs = 4 pts each).

### Point Scoring
When the game ends without instant victory, count victory points:
- Each controlled location is worth its printed VP value.
- Towns (discs) double the VP of their location.
- Score 2 points for each enemy cube captured and 4 points for each enemy disc captured.
- Highest total wins. In the case of a tie, the French player wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- British and French have asymmetric location cards with different connections and symbols.
- Native American cards can be drafted by either player.
- Indian trails can only be used for raids, not settling or attacking.
- Ship symbols connect all sea locations to each other.
- Cards played to a siege go to the siege card space, not the discard pile.
- Cards vulnerable to ambush are marked with the ambush symbol.
- Reserve allows saving cards between turns (maximum 5 cards).
