## Overview

Asia Engulfed is a grand strategic wargame covering the entire Pacific and Asian theater of World War II from 1941-1945. One player controls the Allies (USA, Commonwealth, China, Philippines, Dutch East Indies) while the other commands Japan. Using block units for fog of war, players manage naval fleets, air forces, and ground armies across a vast operational theater. The game features unique supply, oil, and strategic bombing systems.

## Components

- Game board covering the Pacific and Asia
- Wooden block units (labeled with stickers)
- Resource/supply markers
- Oil markers
- Turn track
- Combat results tables
- Dice
- Rules booklet and playbook

## Setup

Follow scenario-specific instructions for unit placement. The standard campaign begins with the Nov/Dec 1941 turn. Place Japanese and Allied blocks on their historical starting positions. Set up resource tracks, supply chains, and initial oil reserves.

## Turn Structure

Each game turn represents approximately two months of historical time. The turn uses an igo-ugo structure: the Japanese player completes all sub-phases first, then the Allied player repeats the same phases. There is no initiative determination step.

**Japanese Player-Turn (then repeated by Allied Player):**

1. **Naval Movement Phase:** Move fleets and troop transports; non-phasing player may react; oil and transport points are expended.
2. **Initial Supply Determination Phase:** Resolve jungle effects (Jul/Aug turns); Japanese player sets Supply Network; check supply status of all phasing-player units.
3. **Production Phase:** Calculate WERPs, resolve strategic warfare effects, purchase replacements and new units.
4. **Air & Naval Combat Phase:** Resolve naval interception, naval combat, port/airbase attacks, and submarine fire.
5. **Operational Movement Phase:** Move ground and LBA units; move amphibious invaders ashore.
6. **Strategic Movement Phase:** Move a limited number of ground and air units strategically across the map.
7. **Ground Combat Phase:** Fight an initial round of ground combat in all required or desired areas; optionally purchase Breakthrough Combat Phases with Special Actions.
8. **Rebasing Phase:** All fleets and troop transports at sea return to port; overstacked marine units rebase.
9. **Final Supply Determination Phase:** Check all friendly areas, units, and fleets for supply; eliminate those without at least Partial Supply.

After both players have completed their turns, advance the turn marker (Turn Advancement Phase).

## Actions

**Naval Operations:**
- Move fleet groups across sea zones.
- Conduct naval battles (surface combat, carrier strikes, submarine warfare).
- Amphibious invasions to land troops on enemy-held islands/coasts.
- Japanese Supply Network is maintained via transport points; Allied submarine warfare interdicts Japanese supply and production.

**Ground Operations:**
- Move ground forces along land connections.
- Conduct ground combat for territory control.

**Air Operations:**
- Strategic bombing against Japanese production.
- Air support for naval and ground operations.
- Air superiority (dogfighting) missions.

**Supply and Oil:**
- Units need supply to maintain combat effectiveness.
- Japan depends on oil from conquered territories.
- Cutting supply lines weakens enemy forces.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Japanese Decisive Victory:** Achieved if, at the end of an Allied Player-turn, any of the following are true: (a) Japan controls Hawaii; (b) Japan controls 12 or more supplied Victory Point (VP) areas/sea-zones; or (c) Japan is unconquered at the end of the Jul/Aug 1945 turn, controls at least six supplied VP areas, and the Allies control no strategic airbase land areas. There is no "force Allied surrender" mechanic.

**Japanese Operational Victory:** Japan controls 6 or more supplied VP areas at game end.

**Japanese Marginal Victory:** Japan controls 3–5 supplied VP areas at game end.

**Allied Marginal Victory:** Japan controls 2 supplied VP areas at game end.

**Allied Operational Victory:** Japan controls 0–1 supplied VP areas at game end.

**Allied Decisive Victory:** Japan (Honshu) is conquered before game end.

The game ends at the conclusion of the Jul/Aug 1945 turn unless Japan is conquered earlier or Japan fulfills its Decisive Victory conditions before then. The core victory conditions (VP thresholds and Japan conquest) are fixed rules; scenario variations, if any, are in the Play Book.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Fog of War:** Block units face the owning player; opponents cannot see unit types or strength until combat.
- **Oil System:** Japan must capture and maintain oil-producing regions. Running low on oil cripples naval operations.
- **China Theater:** Chinese forces have special limitations and reinforcement rules.
- **Strategic Bombing:** US bomber points attack Japanese production, reducing WERPs received each turn.
- **Carrier Operations:** Carrier air groups have distinct rules for strikes and defense.
- **Submarine Warfare:** The USA conducts a strategic submarine campaign against Japanese production and transport capacity; both sides also have tactical submarine units for fleet combat.
- **Combined Game:** Can be linked with Europe Engulfed for a global WWII experience.
