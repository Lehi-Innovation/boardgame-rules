## Overview

Cuba is a strategic economic and political game set in pre-revolutionary Cuba. Players manage villages, cultivating plantations, producing goods, trading on the market and shipping from the harbor, erecting buildings, and influencing parliament. Over 6 rounds, players earn victory points through shipping merchandise, constructing and using buildings, and abiding by enacted laws. The player with the most victory points wins.

## Components

- 1 gameboard with market, harbor, parliament, and victory point track
- 5 plantation boards (double-sided: one side is identical for all players; the other side is unique per player)
- 25 character cards (5 per player color: Worker, Tradeswoman, Architect, Foreman, Mayor)
- 15 ship cards
- 1 starting player card with stand
- 24 statute cards (4 types x 6 acts each: Tax, Duty, Subsidy, Other)
- 25 building tiles
- 1 voting tile (town hall), 1 veto tile (church)
- 5 playing pieces and 5 playing markers in player colors
- 54 product pieces: 18 each of citrus fruit (orange octahedra), sugar cane (white octahedra), tobacco (green octahedra)
- 30 goods pieces: 15 rum bottles (red), 15 cigar boxes (brown)
- 45 resource pieces: 15 each of stone (red-brown cubes), wood (natural cubes), water (blue cubes)
- 60 coins (1, 3, and 5 pesos)
- 6 black marking pieces

## Setup

1. Place the gameboard centrally. Place 25 building tiles face up on appropriate spaces on and next to the gameboard.
2. Sort products and goods by color as general stock. Place 3 each of citrus fruit (orange), sugar cane (white), and tobacco (green) on the market spaces labeled with prices 6, 5, and 4. Place 2 each of rum bottles (red) and cigar boxes (brown) on the market spaces labeled with prices 6 and 5.
3. Sort resources by color as general stock.
4. Shuffle 15 ship cards; place face down. Lay the 2 top cards face up on the first 2 harbor docks. Lay 1 more card face up near the harbor (the approaching ship). The third dock remains empty in the first round.
5. Form 4 statute card piles (I–IV, 6 cards each), shuffled, face down on the board (I=Tax Acts, II=Duty Acts, III=Subsidy Acts, IV=Other Acts).
6. Determine a starting player; give that player the starting player card.
7. Each player chooses a color and receives: a playing piece and playing marker, a plantation board, 10 pesos, 2 resource pieces (wood, stone, or water) and 2 product pieces (citrus fruit, tobacco, or sugar cane) of their choice, and 5 character cards.
8. Place playing markers on the lighthouse (start of VP track). Place playing pieces in the warehouse on plantation boards.

## Turn Structure

The game lasts 6 rounds, each with 5 phases:

1. **A. Bills** — Turn over the top card of each of the 4 statute piles. These are proposals (bills) visible to all.
2. **B. Action Phase** — Starting with the starting player, each player plays 1 character card and executes its action. Continue clockwise until each player has played 4 cards. The 5th card is kept in hand until Parliament.
3. **C. Parliament Phase** — Players vote on which bills become law.
4. **D. Statute Phase** — Enacted statutes take effect.
5. **E. End of Round** — Prepare for the next round.

## Actions

**The Worker (1 vote):** Move your playing piece on your plantation in any direction, or leave it where it is. Then activate the 6 fields in the same row and column as the piece. For each activated field (except the warehouse or buildings), collect the resource or product piece from the stock. The worker may use a maximum of 2 product fields per turn; choose which 2. By giving up 1 water piece per additional product field, the worker may use more than 2 activated product fields (water pieces collected in the current turn may be used this way; no field may be used twice). Resource fields have no limit. All collected resources and products go to the lot in front of your warehouse.

**The Tradeswoman (2 votes):** Execute as many of the following transactions as you want and can:
- **Buy:** Take merchandise (products or goods) from the market at the marked price.
- **Sell:** Place merchandise on available market spaces and receive the indicated price from stock.
- If no piece of a given merchandise kind is on the market, buy from general stock for 7 pesos per piece.
- If the market is full, sell products to stock for 1 peso each and goods for 3 pesos each.
- **Alternative use (once per round):** Instead of market trading, either take 1 resource of any kind for free, or take 1 unit of whichever product currently has the lowest market price for free. Cover the appropriate gameboard space with a black marking piece to show this alternative has been used this round.

**The Architect (3 votes):** Choose one building tile from the gameboard (or adjacent area) and place it on any field of your plantation board (including the lake, mountain regions, or under the worker piece), but not in the warehouse or on top of another building. Pay the building cost in resources to the stock. If you build on a resource or product field, you lose that field's production permanently.
- **Alternative use (once per round, first player):** If you play the Architect without building, the first player to do so earns 2 VP; the second player earns 1 VP. Cover the appropriate space with a black marking piece.

**The Foreman (4 votes):** Use the features (abilities) of your buildings. You have 2 choices:
1. Use, in any order, **all** buildings in the same row or column as your playing piece (including a building beneath the piece).
2. Use **any one** of your buildings.

The warehouse is a building: using it with the Foreman lets you store all products currently in your lot into the warehouse (products in the warehouse carry over to the next round; products left in the lot are lost at the end of the round). Goods and resources do not need to be stored in the warehouse.

Conversion examples at buildings: a distillery turns any amount of sugar cane into rum (1:1); a cigar factory turns any amount of tobacco into cigars (1:1). Each building's feature may be used once per round.

**The Mayor (5 votes):** Supply one ship of your choice in the harbor with any number of merchandise pieces and earn VP. You may supply only one ship per turn; you cannot split a supply between ships. Place the appropriate products or goods on the spaces marked on the ship card (each ship holds a specific combination of up to 5 merchandise spaces). Earn 1, 2, or 3 VP per merchandise piece supplied, depending on the ship's harbor position.
- **Alternative use (once per round, first player):** If you play the Mayor without supplying a ship, the first player to do so earns 4 pesos; the second player earns 2 pesos. Cover the appropriate space with a black marking piece.

**Changing the Starting Player (end of Action Phase):** After all players have played their 4th card, the player whose last (4th) card has the highest vote value becomes the new starting player and takes the starting player card. If multiple players tie on the highest value as their 4th card, the player among them who played their card last becomes the new starting player.

**Parliament Phase:** Each player reveals the character card kept in hand. Its vote value (1–5) is that player's base parliament votes. Whoever built the Town Hall and used it with the Foreman this round gets 2 additional votes. Players then simultaneously bid pesos (hidden in fist) to buy additional votes at 1 peso each; all players pay what they bid whether or not they win. The player with the most total votes selects exactly 2 of the 4 bills to pass. Each selected act is placed on its parliament space (replacing any prior statute there); the other 2 bills are discarded. If there is a tie, the tied players bid again; if still tied, the starting player (or the next player clockwise among those tied) wins.

Note: If the Corruption Act (IV) is in force, buying votes is not permitted.

**Statute Phase:** Resolve enacted statutes in order I–IV:
- **Tax Acts (I):** Pay the current tax (initially 2 pesos) to earn 2 VP. If you cannot or do not wish to pay, you earn nothing.
- **Duty Acts (II):** Give the required resource or product to stock to earn 2 VP. If you comply with both the tax and duty acts, earn 1 additional VP (total 5 VP).
- **Subsidy Acts (III):** Earn 1 VP for each qualifying item (e.g., each building, each open resource field, each open product field, each water piece (max 7 VP), each vote value of your parliament card, or every 3 pesos (max 7 VP)) — no payment required.
- **Other Acts (IV):** Includes Market Acts (add or remove products from market), Drought Act (worker may use only 1 product field), Building Act (architect pays 2 extra pesos to build), Harbor Act (completed ships leave immediately), and Corruption Act (no vote buying).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- Players earn VP by shipping merchandise from the harbor, using building abilities (via Foreman), complying with enacted statutes, and from certain building features.
- Record VP immediately on the victory point track when earned. If you reach 80 VP before the game ends, restart counting from the beginning of the track.
- **End of Game (after Round 6):** Each player earns **2 additional VP for each of their own buildings** (including the starting warehouse). Money does not earn additional VP.
- The player with the most VP wins. In case of a tie, the player with the most money wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Merchandise = products + goods. Resources are NOT merchandise and cannot be shipped.
- Products: citrus fruit, sugar cane, tobacco (octahedra). Goods: rum, cigars (shaped pieces). Resources: stone, wood, water (cubes).
- The lot on your plantation is a temporary depot; it is never activated by the Worker.
- **Product spoilage:** At the end of every round, players must give up all products still in the lot (return to general stock). Products stored in the warehouse are safe and carry over. Goods and resources in the lot remain; they do not spoil.
- Buildings placed on resource/product fields replace that field's production permanently.
- **Fully loaded ships** (all 5 spaces filled) leave the harbor at the end of the round. All remaining ships move one dock closer (from the ship pile toward the sea to the docks). A new approaching ship is placed at the sea position.
- From Round 2 onward, a ship also occupies the 3rd dock; it leaves the harbor at end of round regardless of its load.
- Black marking pieces track which alternative uses have been taken per round; all pieces are removed at the end of the round.
- Players may not trade among each other.
