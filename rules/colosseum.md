## Overview

In Colosseum, each player is a Roman impresario producing great spectacles in her arena, hoping to attract the most spectators. For 3–5 players, ages 10 and above, 60–90 minutes. The game lasts 5 game turns, so each player has the opportunity to produce up to 5 events. Money earned from events is used to buy more ambitious Event Programs, win auctions for Event Asset Tokens, and improve the arena with expansions, Season Tickets, or an Emperor's Loge. The winner is the player who attracts the highest number of spectators to any single one of her events — the title of Grand Impresario goes to the best single show, not a cumulative total.

## Components

- 1 Rules Booklet and 1 Game Board
- 10 Arena pieces (5 different colors) and 10 Arena Expansion pieces (5 different colors)
- 5 Emperor's Loges
- 10 Season Tickets (each worth 5 additional spectators)
- 5 Scoring Markers
- 6 Nobles: the Emperor, 2 Consuls, and 3 Senators
- 2 Dice
- 80 Roman Coins (in denominations of 1, 2, 5, 10, 50)
- 4 Podiums (each worth 3 additional spectators)
- 152 Event Asset Tokens (40 with a green back and 112 with an orange back)
- 7 Star Performer Awards
- 30 Event Programs (numbered 1 to 30)
- 18 Emperor Medals
- 6 Event Summary Sheets
- 1 First Player Token, 1 Game Turn Counter, 1 Storage Bag

The standard asset types are: 20 Gladiators, 12 Comedians, 12 Musicians, 12 Horses, 12 Torches, 11 Priests, 11 Ships, 10 Lions, 10 Scenery, 10 Decorations, 10 Chariots, and 10 Cages. There are also three Special Event Asset Token types: 4 Jokers, 4 Emperors, and 4 Additional Actions.

## Setup

1. Place the game board in the center of the table. The Roman coins form the public bank.
2. Put the Podiums, Emperor Medals, Star Performer Awards, 2 dice, 10 Arena Expansion pieces, 5 Emperor's Loges, and 10 Season Tickets within reach next to the board.
3. Hand each player a Summary Sheet, a Scoring Marker, 2 Arena pieces of matching color, and coins worth a total of 30. Place the Scoring Markers at the starting position of the spectator scoring track along the outside of the board.
4. Separate the green-backed Event Asset Tokens from the orange-backed ones. Mix the green-backed tokens and fill the 5 markets on the board with 3 tokens face up in each market.
5. Distribute green-backed Event Asset Tokens to each player according to player count: 3 players — 8 tokens each; 4 players — 6 tokens each; 5 players — 5 tokens each. Tokens are placed face up in front of each player. In a 3- or 4-player game, place the one remaining green-backed token face down next to the board. Put the orange-backed tokens in the bag next to the board.
6. Place each player's Arena on the board in the starting position indicated for the number of players. Place the Emperor, the 2 Consuls, and the 3 Senators on their respective starting positions (the colored squares on the path); these starting positions also serve as resting areas.
7. Mix the Event Programs numbered 1 to 5 and hand one to each player; repeat with programs numbered 6 to 10. Each player thus has 2 starting programs — one numbered 1–5 and one numbered 6–10 — placed face up in view of the other players. Remove any remaining programs numbered 1–10 from the game. Put programs 11 to 30 in numbered order in 2 stacks of matching length next to the board.
8. Place the game turn counter on the first position on the board.

Star Performer Awards may be given at the beginning of the game if, after the distribution of Asset Tokens, a player has 3 or more tokens of the same kind and no other player also has the same number of those tokens.

## Turn Structure

The game consists of 5 game turns, each composed of 5 distinct phases. Each phase is optional; a player can pass without doing a specific phase.

- **Phase 1: Investing** — each player may make one (and only one) investment.
- **Phase 2: Acquiring Event Asset Tokens** — auctions on the 5 markets of 3 tokens each.
- **Phase 3: Trading Event Asset Tokens** — trade money and assets with the active player.
- **Phase 4: Producing an Event** — move Nobles, produce an event, count spectators, collect income.
- **Phase 5: Closing Ceremonies** — Podium award, Event Clean-up, Asset Donation. No Closing Ceremony occurs after the final turn.

On the first game turn, the player whose arena is immediately to the left of the Emperor takes the First Player token and performs her Phase 1 actions first; play proceeds clockwise. Once all players have completed a phase, the starting player begins the next phase. At the start of each new game turn, the game turn counter advances and the player immediately to the left of the previous starting player takes the First Player token. The game ends after Phase 4 of the 5th game turn.

## Actions

### Phase 1: Investing

Each player can make one and only one of the following investments per turn (a second investment is possible by turning in 2 Emperor Medals, or by using an Additional Action special token):

- **Buy a new Event Program** — pay the price displayed on the program to the bank immediately. The new program must have a higher number than the previous one the player has produced. The program shows its purchase cost, required arena size, the type and number of assets required, the earnings if all assets are available, and the earnings if assets are missing.
- **Expand the Arena** — pay 10 coins for an Arena Expansion piece, added in either direction. Arenas start at 2 squares, grow to 3 after the first expansion, and can be expanded twice to a maximum of 4 squares. The benefit is more space to attract Nobles and hold larger events; Nobles may end up inside an arena as the result of an expansion. Only one expansion per turn (unless spending 2 Emperor Medals for a second investment).
- **Purchase a Season Ticket** — pay 10 coins; the ticket ensures 5 additional spectators for each upcoming event. Only 1 per turn, but a player can accumulate multiple Season Tickets while they remain available.
- **Construct an Emperor's Loge** — pay 5 coins and add it to your arena. The Loge lets you roll 2 dice instead of 1 when moving Nobles before producing your event. An arena can have a maximum of 1 Emperor's Loge.

### Phase 2: Acquiring Event Asset Tokens (Auction)

The starting player points to one of the 5 markets and makes an opening bid, which must be **8 coins or more**. Moving clockwise, each player can make a higher bid; a player who steps out of a bidding round cannot rejoin the bidding for that market. The winner pays the bank her winning bid and takes the 3 face-up tokens from that market. Having won an auction, she can no longer participate in subsequent bidding rounds this turn. New auctions are held until every player has had a chance to initiate a bidding round. Participation is never mandatory: a player who passes on her opportunity to initiate a bid can still join rounds initiated by others, but loses her chance to initiate for the rest of the turn.

**Refill:** If the auction winner is the active player (the one who initiated the bidding), empty markets are refilled before the next player initiates — drawing any remaining green-backed token first, then orange-backed tokens from the bag, placed face up. If the winner is not the active player, the tokens are not replaced until the active player wins another bidding round this turn or passes on her opportunity to initiate.

### Phase 3: Trading Event Asset Tokens

Beginning with the starting player and moving clockwise, each player in turn makes open offers to the rest of the players. The active player can buy, sell, or exchange with any other player, but non-active players can only trade with the active player, not among each other. Only money and event assets can be used to make trades; all trades must be delivered immediately, and no trades can be based on future promises. It is possible that no trades occur at all.

### Phase 4: Producing an Event

Each player, beginning with the starting player, may produce an event in 3 steps.

**Step 1: Moving Senators, Consuls and the Emperor.** Before producing an event, each player rolls one die (or two dice if her arena has an Emperor's Loge) and must move one Noble clockwise per die, the number of squares indicated. The 1-3 face lets the player move a Noble 1, 2, or 3 squares forward. With two dice, a player may move two different Nobles (one per die) or one Noble using the sum of both dice. If a Noble ends his movement on a resting area, the player earns 1 Emperor Medal; when combining two dice on one Noble, the medal is earned only if the Noble finishes (not merely pauses) on the resting area. Multiple Nobles may occupy the same square. Each Noble present in your arena brings spectators: the Emperor 7, each Consul 5, each Senator 3.

**Step 2: Producing your Event.** Choosing only from the Event Programs in your possession, announce the event and place the program face up for all to see. You can only present one event per turn. First check that your arena is large enough for the program's required arena size. Display the Event Asset Tokens needed; you do not need all listed assets, but your score is reduced for each missing asset, and you must have at least the minimum number of assets listed on the program that still gives a score. You may repeat an event produced in a previous turn by turning that older program face up again.

**Step 3: Counting Spectators.** Add up:
- the spectators on the Event Program (full value only if all assets are present; attendance is reduced for each missing asset token — e.g., the Circus Maximus program missing 1 Horse and 1 Chariot draws 24 instead of 32);
- +5 spectators for each previously produced Event Program (turned face down);
- +5 spectators for each Season Ticket in your arena;
- +4 spectators for each Star Performer Award you possess, but only if an Asset Token of that type is used in the performed event;
- Noble bonuses for each Noble in your arena (Emperor +7, Consul +5 each, Senator +3 each);
- +3 spectators for each Podium earned in previous turns;
- +3 spectators per Emperor Medal turned in (medals are then discarded).

If this score is higher than any of the player's previous events, her scoring marker moves to the new higher position; otherwise it stays, always showing her highest score. The player then **receives coins from the bank equal to the number of spectators that attended** this turn's event.

### Phase 5: Closing Ceremonies

- **Podium:** At the end of each turn except the last, the player with the highest overall score **since the beginning of the game** (not necessarily the most spectators this turn) receives a Podium and places it in her arena; it adds 3 spectators to any of her subsequent events. Ties go to the richest player; if still tied, the tied players roll the two dice and the highest total wins the Podium.
- **Event Clean-up:** At the end of every turn, each player must discard 1 of the Event Asset Tokens used in her latest event and remove it from the game.
- **Asset Donation:** The player in last position on the scoring track asks the player who just received the Podium to donate one Asset Token of a type she names (no Special tokens — Additional Action, Emperor, or Joker). If tied for last place, the poorest player receives the donation; if still tied, roll the dice until decided.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Scoring is not cumulative: each player's scoring marker always shows the highest spectator count achieved by any single one of her events. The game ends when each player has had a chance to produce an event in the fifth and final turn (the game ends after Phase 4 of turn 5, with no Closing Ceremony). The winner is the player who attracted the most spectators to one of her events during the game. If two players are tied, the richest player wins; if players are still tied, the one who holds the most Star Performer Awards wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Previous Events:** When a new event is presented, each previously produced Event Program is turned face down and counts for 5 additional spectators until the game ends (e.g., a third event gets 5+5 = 10 bonus spectators from 2 old programs). A player repeating an old event turns that program face up; other previously produced programs still count +5 each, but programs never yet produced are set aside and add nothing.
- **Jokers (x4):** A Joker can replace any standard asset token; the player decides which asset it represents when the event is performed. Jokers do not count toward earning a Star Performer Award.
- **Emperor tokens (x4):** When acquired, an Emperor Asset Token is immediately replaced by an Emperor Medal; if no medals remain, the token is lost.
- **Additional Action tokens (x4):** Can be used to make 2 investments instead of 1 in the investment phase (the second still costs the appropriate payment, and may be the same investment type twice), or exchanged for an Emperor Medal at any time.
- **Star Performer Awards:** The live-performer asset types are Gladiators, Priests, Comedians, Musicians, Lions, Horses, and Ships. The first player to collect 3 or more matching tokens of one of these types — while having more of that type than any other player — receives that type's Star Performer Award. The award changes hands if another player collects more of that asset type, or if the holder drops below 3 tokens (in which case it goes to a player with 3+ and the most; on a tie among others, no one gets it). Each award adds 4 spectators when an event uses that asset type — a player with 3 awards using assets from 2 of them adds 8 (4+4).
- **Emperor Medals:** Earned when a Noble ends his dice-roll journey on a resting area, or via the Emperor and Additional Action special tokens. A medal used to move a Noble onto a resting area does NOT earn a new medal — medals only come from dice movement. Playing 1 medal does exactly one of: add 3 spectators when producing an event; move any Noble 1, 2, or 3 squares forward or backward (after the dice movement); or receive 6 coins. Playing 2 medals allows a second investment during the investment phase. There is no restriction on how many medals can be played — several can be played in a row to move a Noble multiple times or move a second Noble. Medals can be used immediately when earned or saved, and are collected only as long as they remain available.
- **Intense Auctions variant:** Changes Phase 2 so that players who already won an auction may bid again once the initiating player changes; the opening bid is still 8 coins or higher, markets refill when the initiating player wins or passes, and the phase ends when every player has had a chance to initiate a bidding round.
