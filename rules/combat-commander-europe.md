## Overview

Combat Commander: Europe is a card-driven tactical infantry combat wargame set in World War II. One player commands the Axis (Germany) while the other leads the Allies (America or Russia). Players take turns playing Fate cards from their hands to activate units for various military functions on a hex map. There are no physical dice; all die rolls are resolved by revealing the top card of a player's draw pile and reading its two six-sided dice icons. The game features scenarios with different maps, force compositions, and victory conditions.

## Components

- Six back-printed 17″ × 22″ game maps (twelve maps total)
- 352 large (⅝″) die-cut counters
- 280 small (½″) die-cut counters
- 220 cards (Fate card decks: German, American, Russian)
- One 24-page Rulebook; one 24-page Playbook
- One Track Display (VP/Victory Track, Casualty Track, Time Track, Artillery Boxes, OB Display, Objective Box, Data Boxes)
- Four double-sided player aid sheets
- Unit counters: Leaders, Squads (10-man), Teams (5-man), Weapons, Radios
- Markers: Objective chits, Control markers, Time marker, Sudden Death marker, Suppressed, Veteran, Smoke, Blaze, Wire, Mines, Foxholes, Spotting Round, VP marker, Attack Total marker, Surrender markers

## Setup

1. Select a scenario; set up the designated map and place units per the scenario's Order of Battle.
2. Each player takes the appropriate Fate card deck, shuffles it, and draws a hand equal to their Hand Size (determined by Posture: Attack = 6 cards, Recon = 5 cards, Defend = 4 cards).
3. Place Objective chits (open and secret) in the Objective Box per the scenario.
4. Place the Time marker and Sudden Death marker on the Time Track as directed by the scenario.
5. Place the VP marker on the Victory Track as directed by the scenario.
6. Place each player's Surrender marker on the Casualty Track as directed by the scenario.
7. Determine which side begins with the Initiative card per the scenario (or by comparing Initiative numbers in Random Scenario Generation).

## Turn Structure

Turns alternate strictly: one player takes a complete Turn, then the other. A Turn consists of the active player choosing either to perform one or more Orders (by playing cards) or to discard any number of Fate cards up to that nationality's Discard Limit (a Pass).

**Order Capability:** The maximum number of Orders per Turn is listed in the scenario's OB or on the OB Display (1–6). Orders require playing a card; the card is placed in the discard pile after use.

**Actions:** Either player may play a card for its Action at any time during either player's Turn (subject to prerequisites). There is no limit on Actions per Turn other than the number of cards in hand.

**Hand Refill:** At the end of every Turn, the active player draws up to their Hand Size. If the last card of the draw pile is drawn during a Hand Refill, a Time Marker Advancement is triggered first, then the Hand Refill resumes after the deck is reshuffled.

**Events:** Events are never played from hand; they occur only when a die roll result is enclosed in a red "Event!" trigger box — the player making the roll then reveals the top card of their draw pile and executes the Event listed on it.

## Actions

### Orders (played during active player's Turn only)

| Order | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Advance** | Activated unit moves one hex (including into enemy-occupied hexes); immune to Op Fire while advancing |
| **Artillery Denied** | Breaks opponent's Radio; if already broken, eliminates it |
| **Artillery Request** | Either fixes own broken Radio (Battery Access) or calls a Fire For Effect bombardment via an unbroken Leader Spotter |
| **Command Confusion** | Cannot be played as an Order; acts as a dead card |
| **Fire** | Activated units/Weapons make Fire Attacks against enemy units in LOS and Range |
| **Move** | Activated units move hex to hex, spending MPs equal to terrain Move Costs |
| **Recover** | Player removes all Suppressed markers from friendly units; makes a Rally Roll for each broken friendly unit |
| **Rout** | Active player makes a Rout Roll for each broken unit belonging to the chosen player (self or opponent) |

### Actions (played by either player at any time)

Actions are listed alphabetically in rules A25–A41. Examples include Ambush (A25), Assault Fire, Crossfire, Dig In, Marksmanship, Mines, Opportunity Fire (A33), Sustained Fire, Wire, and others. Each Action's card specifies its prerequisite and effect.

### Fire Resolution

1. The active player announces a Fire Order; at least one activated unit (or its Weapon) must have an enemy unit within both its LOS and Range.
2. Ordnance Weapons (white-band stats) must first make a Targeting Roll (multiply the two dice): the product must exceed the range to hit; a miss cancels the attack.
3. For non-Ordnance fire: determine final FP = base FP of firing piece(s), modified by Leader Command and any Hindrance penalties (largest Hindrance only; FP cannot be reduced to 0 or less or the shot cannot be made).
4. Fire Groups: two or more units/Weapons may combine. Group FP = FP of one base piece + 1 per each additional piece. All pieces must have LOS to the target; Ordnance cannot join a Fire Group.
5. Fire Attack Roll: firing player reveals top card (adds dice together). Attack Total = final FP + roll.
6. Fire Defense Roll: each unit in the target hex adds its Morale (modified by Cover and Leader Command) to a separate die roll. Defense Total = Morale + roll.
   - Defense Total < Attack Total → unit **breaks**.
   - Defense Total = Attack Total → unit becomes **Suppressed** (or **breaks** if it is currently activated to Move).
   - Defense Total > Attack Total → no effect.
7. A "Jammed!" trigger on a Fire Attack Roll breaks all firing Weapons (does not cancel the attack).

### Melee

Melee occurs at the end of any Order or die trigger that leaves units of both sides in the same hex.

1. Both players resolve any Ambush Actions (A25) first.
2. Each side sums the current FP of its units in the hex (not Weapons), adding +1 for each unit with a boxed FP ("Melee FP").
3. The **inactive player** makes a die roll first; adds it to Melee FP → Melee Total. Then the **active player** does the same.
4. The side with the **lower Melee Total has all its units eliminated**. On a tie, **both sides are eliminated** — unless one side started the Melee in a Bunker or Pillbox, in which case only the other side is eliminated.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

VPs are tracked on the VP Track. The VP marker moves toward a player as that player gains VPs.

### VP Sources

**1. Elimination VPs (7.1):** When any non-Hero unit is eliminated, the opponent immediately gains:
- Squad: 2 VPs
- Team: 1 VP
- Leader (not Hero): 1 VP + 1 VP per point of Command on its unbroken side
- Hero: 0 VPs

**2. Exit VPs (7.2):** A unit voluntarily exiting off the opponent's board edge via a Move or Advance Order earns the same VP value as elimination (above) for its owner. Units involuntarily forced off their own friendly board edge via Rout are eliminated and award Elimination VPs to the opponent.

**3. Objective VPs (7.3):** Each of the five map Objectives can be worth VPs based on which Objective chits are in play. Open Objectives award VPs immediately when they change control (subtract from former controller, add to new controller). Secret Objectives are typically scored at game end. Some special Objective chits may double Elimination VPs or Exit VPs for both players, or grant an automatic win to a player controlling all five Objectives prior to each Sudden Death Roll.

**Time Marker Advancement (6.1.2):** Each time the Time marker advances, the triggering player first shuffles their deck and makes a Sudden Death Roll if appropriate; only if that roll does not end the game does the Defender then gain 1 VP — if the Sudden Death Roll ends the game, this VP is not awarded.

### Game End (6.3)

A game ends in one of four ways:

1. **Surrender:** A player is forced to place an eliminated unit on the Casualty Track space occupied by their own Surrender marker → that player **immediately loses** regardless of VP total.
2. **Last unit eliminated:** A player's last remaining unit on the map is eliminated → that player **immediately loses**.
3. **Last unit exits:** A player's last remaining unit voluntarily exits the map → proceed to VP comparison (see below).
4. **Sudden Death Roll:** Whenever the Time marker advances into or beyond the Sudden Death marker's space, the triggering player makes a Sudden Death Roll (reveal top card, add both dice). If the result is **less than the number currently shown on the Time marker**, the game ends immediately → proceed to VP comparison.

For situations 1 and 2: the player in that situation loses instantly. If both players lose simultaneously, the player **holding the Initiative card wins**.

For situations 3 and 4: reveal any secret Objective chits and award their VPs. The player whose VP marker is on their side of the "0" space wins. If the marker is exactly on "0", the player **holding the Initiative card wins**.

### Initiative Card (9)

The Initiative card is never part of a player's hand and does not count against hand size. Its holder may cancel the last die roll (before any results are applied) and force a re-roll — but must then give the Initiative card to the opponent. This can happen an unlimited number of times. In any tie game-end situation the player holding the Initiative card wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **No physical dice:** All die rolls are made by revealing the top card of the rolling player's draw pile and reading the two dice icons (added together; multiplied only for Targeting Rolls).
- **Die triggers:** Rolls enclosed in a red box trigger an Event!, Jammed!, Sniper!, or Time! pause; triggers are resolved entirely before the roll result is applied. A roll during a die trigger cannot itself produce a trigger.
- **Reshuffling / Time Advance:** Whenever a player draws the last card of their deck (during any draw or die roll), the Time marker advances one space. First, only that player shuffles their discard and draw piles together, and then makes a Sudden Death Roll if the marker is at or past the Sudden Death marker. Only if the game does not end from that roll do the remaining steps occur, in order: the Defender gains 1 VP; the triggering player removes one Smoke marker from the map; reinforcements (if any) enter play; and both players may play "Dig In" Actions.
- **Broken units:** A broken unit has a red bar across the top and white Morale number. Broken units cannot fire Weapons. A broken unit that breaks again is eliminated.
- **Suppressed units:** Have −1 FP, −1 Range, −1 Movement, and −1 Morale; cannot fire any Weapon. Suppressed markers are removed at the start of a Recover Order.
- **Leader Command:** A Leader's Command number extends its activation radius to friendly units within that many hexes. It also directly adds to the FP, Range, Movement, and Morale of all friendly Squads and Teams in the same hex (not other Leaders). It also adds to FP and Range of non-Ordnance Weapons carried by units in the same hex.
- **Stacking:** Up to 7 total soldier figures may stack in a hex without penalty. Violations must be remedied by eliminating units at end of Turn (one free Deploy is permitted first). Unit stacking is not enforced mid-Turn.
- **Posture/Hand Size:** Attack = 6-card hand; Recon = 5; Defend = 4. This affects hand size only, not the deck contents.
- **Ordnance:** Weapons with white-band stats must Targeting Roll before a Fire Attack; cannot join Fire Groups; cannot participate in Opportunity Fire.
- **Hindrances:** The single largest Hindrance modifier applies (not cumulative). Smoke and Blaze affect LOS into, out of, and through their hex. Non-Ordnance Fire Attack FP is reduced by the largest Hindrance; Targeting Rolls are similarly reduced.
- **Retreat (Rout Order):** A Rout Roll greater than a unit's Morale forces a Retreat equal to the difference in hexes toward the unit's own board edge. A unit retreating off its own board edge, into an enemy-occupied hex, into an impassable hex, or across a Cliff hexside is eliminated.
- **Objective Control:** Control markers track which side controls each Objective. A building Objective spanning multiple hexes requires all those hexes to be free of enemy units for control.

|--------------------|--------|

|-------------|--------|

|-----------------|-----------|
| Surrender marker reached on Casualty Track | That player loses immediately |
| Last unit on map eliminated | That player loses immediately |
| Last unit voluntarily exits map | VP comparison; tie goes to Initiative card holder |
| Sudden Death Roll < Time marker number | VP comparison; tie goes to Initiative card holder |
