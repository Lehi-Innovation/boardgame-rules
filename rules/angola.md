## Overview

Angola is a multi-player wargame simulating the Angolan Civil War of July 1975 to April 1976. Players control four factions — FAPLA, MPLA, UNITA, and FNLA — divided into two alliances: FAPLA and MPLA form the Soviet-backed alliance, while UNITA and FNLA form the Western-backed alliance. The game is best played by four players, with each player taking one faction. Games with fewer players are possible but are not as well-balanced. Factions vie for control of towns and cities, including the capital Luanda, to accumulate Victory Tokens and advance their alliance's position on the Victory Track. Cuba and South Africa are not playable factions; they appear as Major Units (foreign aid troops) that can enter play through the Direct Foreign Aid system.

## Components

- 1 Map of Angola (divided into regions)
- Two sheets of cardboard counters, including:
  - Unit counters for each faction (infantry, armored cars, tanks, artillery, engineers, airgroups, mercenaries)
  - Equipment counters (anti-aircraft missiles, anti-tank missiles, mines, minefields)
  - Informational markers: Column markers, Control Markers for Towns and Cities, Victory Tokens, Victory Track Marker, First Player Marker, Turn Marker, +1 Jungle Markers
- One deck of 110 cards (65 Operations and Reinforcement Cards plus 45 duplicates)
- Four player aid cards
- Dice

## Setup

1. Players sit around the map: FAPLA to the West (near Luanda), FNLA to the North (near Zaire), MPLA to the East (near Luso), UNITA to the South (near South-West Africa).
2. Sort Units, Operations Cards, and Victory Tokens by faction.
3. Set aside Operations Cards marked with a turn number; these are added to the Operations Deck at the start of the indicated turn.
4. Place each faction's five Victory Tokens and four Major Units off-map; turn Major Units face down.
5. Place Equipment counters (mines, minefields, missiles) in a central pool.
6. Place both Victory Track Markers on the "20" space of the Victory Track; place the Turn Marker on "Turn 1"; shuffle the Reinforcement Card deck.
7. Each faction takes its designated Control Markers and draws additional ones randomly from an opaque container; deploy starting units and columns on the map as directed by the setup rules.
8. Place the five remaining Control Markers face up on their corresponding towns/cities on the map as uncontrolled.

## Turn Structure

Each turn represents one month of real time. The game always ends after ten turns unless one alliance wins a decisive victory earlier. Each turn consists of three phases:

1. **Planning Phase:**
   - Check for new Operations Cards to add to the Operations Deck (new cards are added on turns 3, 4, 5, and 6).
   - Each player secretly assembles an Operations Pack from their Operations Deck (the number of cards allowed is shown on the Turn Record Track).
   - Determine the First Player by rolling on the First Player Chart.

2. **Operations Phase:**
   - Beginning with the first player and proceeding clockwise, each player turns over the top card of their Operations Pack and takes the permitted actions:
     - **Blank Card:** No action; play passes immediately to the next player.
     - **Column Card:** Move the designated Column; combat is resolved immediately if the Column enters an enemy-occupied region.
     - **5th Column Card:** Move one or more non-Column Units from the same region; they may not enter an enemy-occupied region.
     - **Command Card:** Reassign a Column marker to a different Force, or swap Column markers between two Columns, without moving either Force.
   - Play continues until all Operations Cards have been turned over.

3. **End Phase:**
   - **A) Victory Determination:** Check for decisive victory; total Victory Tokens by alliance to determine who won the turn; move the winning alliance's Victory Track Marker accordingly; check for end-of-game victory on turn 10.
   - **B) Reorganization:** Form Infantry Brigades and reorganize Columns.
   - **C) Direct Foreign Aid:** If an alliance lost the turn, its factions consult the Direct Foreign Aid Table to receive foreign troops (Major and Minor Units).
   - **D) Recruitment:** All factions recruit and deploy infantry Units.
   - **E) Covert Foreign Aid:** All factions secretly bid for Reinforcement Cards to receive Units and Equipment.
   - **F) Clean Up:** Return airgroups from the Air Mission Completed Box; return Victory Tokens to their owning factions; turn all Control Markers face up; advance the Turn Marker.

## Actions

### Movement

Units are activated for movement by playing an Operations Card. A Column Card activates a specific lettered Column; a 5th Column Card activates non-Column Units. Forces move into adjacent regions. Normally a Force moves one region and stops, but exceptions allow:
- **Road Movement:** Up to six regions along a continuous road.
- **Clear Terrain Movement:** Up to three regions through connected clear terrain.
- **Combination Road/Clear:** Up to three regions using a mix of roads and clear terrain.
- **Jungle Movement:** A Force attempting to exit a jungle region without a road must roll equal to or greater than the region's printed jungle rating; failure ends movement and places a +1 Jungle Marker on the Force.

Tank Units and Major Units that are artillery may only move through clear terrain or along roads; they are destroyed if forced to move or retreat through savannah, jungle, or hills.

### Combat

Combat occurs when a Column enters a region containing Enemy Forces. The Column stops and combat is resolved immediately. The full combat procedure is:

1. Check for Automatic Victory (attacker has at least 6× the defender's combat strength and defender has no Column).
2. Commit and reveal Airgroups.
3. Reveal Combat Units.
4. Resolve Air-to-Air Combat.
5. Resolve Air Strikes (and anti-aircraft missile fire).
6. Fire Artillery (attacker fires first; each side may fire one artillery Unit).
7. Use Minefields (engineer removes minefield or attacker rolls for each armored Unit).
8. Fire Anti-Tank Missiles.
9. Resolve Combat (calculate odds, compute Net Combat Dice Bonus, roll Combat Dice, apply Adjustment Table, consult Result Table, apply losses and retreats).
10. Resolve Straggling (attacking Column loses one Unit, or two if it has eight or more Units).
11. Transfer Control Markers and Victory Tokens.

### Foreign Aid and Recruitment

There is no political influence mechanic. Foreign support is handled through three systems in the End Phase:
- **Direct Foreign Aid:** Factions that lost the turn receive Major and Minor Units (representing Zairois, South African, and Cuban troops) based on the Direct Foreign Aid Table. If insufficient Major Units are available, the faction rolls on the Crisis Table.
- **Recruitment:** Each faction recruits infantry Units based on set amounts and the number of face-up Control Markers they hold.
- **Covert Foreign Aid:** Factions secretly bid 0–5 for Reinforcement Cards, then draw and receive the indicated Units and Equipment.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

Only an **Alliance** can win the game; individual factions cannot. Victory depends on the alliance's Victory Track Marker position on the Victory Track (spaces numbered 10–20, starting at 20).

- **Winning a Turn:** The alliance with more total Victory Tokens at the end of a turn wins that turn and moves its Victory Track Marker down the track (1–3 spaces depending on token count). A tie means neither alliance wins the turn.
- **Decisive Victory:** An alliance wins immediately if, during the End Phase Victory Determination step, it controls Victory Tokens equal to or greater than the number on the space where its Victory Track Marker sits.
- **End of Game Victory:** If no decisive victory has occurred after turn 10, the alliance whose Victory Track Marker is closer to the "10" space wins.

Victory Tokens are gained by capturing towns (1 token), cities other than Luanda (2 tokens), and Luanda (3 tokens) from the enemy. The UNITA/FNLA alliance also gains a Victory Track movement bonus for capturing Cabinda. Covert Foreign Aid bidding can generate propaganda victories, moving the enemy alliance's marker one space down the track.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Alliances:** Allied Forces may occupy the same region and must defend it together. However, a faction never provides a Supporting Region for its ally.
- **Cabinda:** Not a port and has no Control Marker. MPLA Forces defending Cabinda convert retreat results into "½ of the Defending Force Eliminated." Capturing Cabinda moves the UNITA/FNLA Victory Track Marker one space toward 10 instead of exchanging Victory Tokens.
- **Zaire and South-West Africa:** FAPLA and MPLA Forces may never enter these areas. FNLA Forces deploy and recruit through Zaire; UNITA through South-West Africa.
- **Crisis Table:** When a faction cannot draw a required Major Unit, it rolls on the Crisis Table, which can restrict or withdraw Major Units, reduce Reinforcement Cards, or cut off all foreign aid. All Crisis Table results apply for the rest of the game.
- **Infantry Brigades:** Three infantry Units in the same region may be combined into a single Infantry Brigade during End Phase reorganization.
- **Counter Limitations:** The counter mix is the absolute maximum; no additional counters of any type may be created.

## Player Reference

**Turn Flow:** Planning Phase → Operations Phase → End Phase

**Key Factions:** FAPLA & MPLA (Soviet-backed alliance) vs. UNITA & FNLA (Western-backed alliance)

**Victory:** Alliance-based — accumulate Victory Tokens to win turns and move your Victory Track Marker toward 10; achieve a decisive victory or have your marker closer to 10 at game end.
