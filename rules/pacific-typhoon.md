## Overview

Pacific Typhoon (GMT Games, designed by Ben Knight) is a strategy card game for 3–7 players, ages 10 to adult, with many similarities to Atlantic Storm. It is best suited for 4–6 players; it can be played with 3 or 7, but the rules note this is "not as satisfying an experience." The game covers the naval and air war in the Pacific theatre during World War II, from the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor (7 Dec 1941) to the formal surrender of Japan (2 Sep 1945). Players are not assigned to permanent sides: each holds a hand mixing Allied and Japanese force cards and freely chooses which side to fight on each battle. Each battle is one round of the table; players win battles to collect spoil cards (battle cards and destroyed enemy force cards), and the spoil victory points (VPs) on those cards are the only way to win the game. Table talk and negotiation are encouraged — but a player never has to keep a promise.

## Components

- **40 Battle cards**, numbered 1–40 in chronological order (the number only matters for end-game tiebreaking). Each shows: battle name, location or campaign, year of battle (1941–'45), date, time of day (Day = sunny sky/blue sea background; Night = moonlit sky/black sea background; Either = twilight sky/gray sea background, leader's choice of day or night), resource value (full resource = one extra hand card; half resource = half an extra card; no resource), victory point value from 0–8, photograph, caption, and victim status (a symbol if the battle card is fated by a force card). One special **Renewed Battle** card lets its drawer take a battle card from another player's spoils to fight over again.
- **110 Force cards.** Each shows: card name; side (Axis = Japanese; Allied = U.S., British, Australian, and Dutch; Both = playable with either side); three combat values — **Air**, **Surface**, and **Sub/Anti-sub**; years available (e.g., Iowa is playable in a '44 or '45 battle but not '41–'43); time of day (day, night, or either, by background); type by photograph frame (Aircraft, including aircraft carriers = windscreen frame; Ship = binoculars frame; Sub = circle frame; Any = square frame); restrictions or history text; "fated against" victim name (on Fate cards); and a victory point value of 0–6, or a Recycle symbol meaning the card may not be destroyed.
- Force cards fall into three play categories: **Regular** cards (any card that is not a bonus or event card), **Bonus** cards (with "+" combat values that add to a parent regular card; the Kamikaze bonus has "*" values that replace its parent's values), and **Event** cards (no combat values, or negative values in the case of Gremlins).
- Some combat values are "?" values resolved with a die roll (a ?1 is a die roll plus 1, a ?2 is a die roll plus 2, etc.), and cards may carry sun or moon symbols worth 1 extra combat point in day or night battles respectively.

## Setup

1. Randomly determine the dealer (and, if desired, seating order) — by dice roll, or by each player drawing a battle card and sitting in order of card numbers from lowest (dealer) to highest, returning the cards afterwards.
2. The dealer shuffles the Force Deck and deals each player a hand of 6 cards. The remaining force cards go face down as the Draw Pile.
3. Battle Deck: remove the Renewed Battle card, divide the deck into halves of 20 and 19 cards, add Renewed Battle to the pile of 19 (making two piles of 20), shuffle each pile separately, and put the stack without Renewed Battle on top of the stack with it. This way Renewed Battle cannot appear until the second half of the game.
4. Etiquette: never show your cards to other players except when playing or discarding them. Open conversation is encouraged; don't discuss the game in private, whisper, or keep notes.

## Turn Structure

The game is played in rounds, also called Battles. Each player plays once per round in clockwise order, starting with the round leader. The dealer leads the 1st round; the player on his left leads the 2nd, and so on clockwise for the entire game.

1. **Leader selects battle:** The round leader draws **two** cards from the Battle Deck, flips them face up, **selects one for battle and discards the other** face up to the Battle Discards. (Skip this step if the battle is continuing from a tied previous round.) He must announce the battle card's year — only force cards matching the year of battle may fight; force cards that appear after that year may not.
2. **Leader selects time:** The leader chooses and announces "Day" or "Night" (never "Either," even on an Either battle card). Day-background force cards may only be played in day battles, night cards only at night; twilight ("either") cards may be played day or night.
3. **Leader selects suit:** The leader chooses and announces the **suit** (operation type) of the battle: **Air, Surface, Sub, or Combined**. The suit determines what combat values apply to the battle's outcome — values that don't match the suit are treated as 0. If the suit is Combined, all three combat values apply. The leader always calls suit and time, even if he discards instead of plays.
4. **Each player, in clockwise order starting with the leader, must play or discard** (see Actions — passing is not allowed).
5. **Resolve the battle outcome** and divide spoils (see Scoring), then all players refill their hands to their hand size, and the next player to lead starts a new round.

## Actions

On your turn you must do exactly one of the following — **you may not pass** (rule 7.7):

1. **Play face up at least one legal card**, consisting of:
   - **One Regular card** at most (you may not play more than one regular card unless you use the Admiral event ability);
   - **Any number of playable Bonus cards** — each must attach to your regular card (its parent) or be playable without a parent. A bonus card must match its parent's type (aircraft, ship, or sub); "any"-type bonus cards (e.g., Ocean Patrol, Coast Watchers) may attach to any of the three types. Shinyo Flotilla, Ha-10, Ohka, and PT 109 may be played without a parent. A bonus card may never attach to another bonus card, to an event card, or to a card someone else played; two or more bonus cards may attach to the same parent.
   - **Any number of Event cards** playable per their restrictions.
2. **Or discard one card** from your hand to the Discard Pile instead of playing.
3. **Or discard your entire hand** to the Discard Pile instead of playing.

You may only play one side: never play Allied and Japanese cards together (exact Allied nationality doesn't matter — a U.S. bonus card may attach to an Australian parent). All plays must match the year of battle and fit the declared time. Playing a card with a 0 combat value in the declared suit is allowed.

**Illegal plays:** If an illegal play is noticed before the next player plays or discards, you take it back into your hand and re-do your turn; if noticed later, the illegal play is discarded and does not affect the battle. Illegal plays include mixing sides (both cards treated as illegal), a force card whose earliest year is later than the battle's year, a card whose time is opposite the declared time, a bonus card not fitting its parent's type, or violating a card's printed restriction.

**Fate cards:** A force card listing another card's name is a Fate card, fated against that victim. It only affects its victim if played **after** the victim in the **current** round, and you must announce the victim when you reveal the fate card — if you fail to announce it before the battle outcome is determined, the fate does not occur. A fate card targets its victim even if it has a 0 combat value in the declared suit.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Determining the outcome:** When the last player has played or discarded, roll a die for each "?" in the declared suit (?1 = die roll +1, etc.). A sun symbol in the declared suit counts as 1 extra combat point in day battles; a moon symbol counts as 1 extra point in night battles. Total the combat values in the declared suit for each side (Allied vs. Japanese); in a Combined battle count all three values. Three outcomes are possible:

- **No combat:** If no force cards are in play, or those in play have only 0 combat values in the declared suit (or no combat values), discard the battle card to the Battle Discards and discard all force cards in play (including engaged cards from a previous round).
- **Tied combat:** If Allied and Japanese totals are equal, the force cards become **Engaged**: place them (including Recyclable ones) underneath the battle card and fight a new round for the same battle. Players refill hands, and the next round leader declares time and suit (same or different). Players may change sides. Engaged cards don't affect the new round's outcome and can't be victimized by fate cards; Typhoon, Day or Night, and Tokyo Rose effects don't carry over. A battle can extend this way over multiple rounds as long as each ends tied. (Exception: if Little Boy has taken the battle card, a tie ends with the engaged cards discarded as no combat.)
- **One side wins:** The side with the higher total wins. The losing side's cards are **destroyed** (except Recyclable ones); the winning side's cards are discarded. The winning-side player who contributed the highest combat value is the battle winner; if tied, the one who played his card(s) first wins ("Getting there firstest with the mostest").

**Spoils (rules 11.1–11.2):** Spoils are the battle card itself plus all losing-side force cards except Recyclable ones (including engaged losing-side cards). Battle cards are worth 0–8 VPs; force cards 0–6 VPs. The battle winner **must divide the spoil cards among himself and every player who has a combat value of 1 or higher in play on the winning side** at the end of the round (a 0 combat value, or a play removed by fate or Typhoon, gets nothing). Each eligible player must receive one spoil card before anyone receives a second, a second before anyone receives a third, etc. — the cards are distributed evenly, but the VPs need not be; the divider generally keeps the highest-VP spoils. Promises about spoils are never binding. Fate-card victims, Rosie the Riveter, and a Little Boy battle card don't count against your share.

**Keeping spoils:** Place spoils in front of you — resource spoils (battle cards and Rosie the Riveter) face up so their resource and VP values are visible; other spoil cards face down. Players may not record or inspect the hidden VP values of others' spoils until the game ends.

**End of game (rule 11.5):** The game ends after 20 battles, when there are no more battle cards to select. The player with the highest total spoil VPs wins. If tied, the tied player with the **most battle card VPs** wins; if still tied, the one holding the battle card with the **lowest card number** wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Hand size (rule 12):** At the end of each round, draw from the Draw Pile up to your hand size. Base hand size is **6 cards**; each full-resource spoil adds 1 card, each half-resource spoil adds 1/2 card (two half-resources are needed for an extra card); maximum **9 cards**. If your hand already equals or exceeds your hand size (e.g., after Renewed Battle stole a resource from you), you draw nothing but never discard down. When the Draw Pile runs out, shuffle the Discard Pile into a new Draw Pile.
- **Kinds of fate victims (8.2):** A VP force-card victim is immediately destroyed and placed face down in front of you as your spoil. A Recyclable force-card victim is immediately discarded face up. A **battle-card** victim instead **doubles your fate card's combat value** in that battle (double the die roll for a "?", not two dice; never double attached bonus cards, and don't double a parent if the attached bonus card is the fate). If a destroyed victim was a parent, its attached bonus cards are discarded, not destroyed.
- **Renewed Battle (6.3/7.2):** If selected, the leader exchanges it for one battle card from another player's spoils, then announces that card's printed year or chooses a later year (never one preceding the printed year).
- **Recycle:** Most bonus and event cards carry the Recycle symbol instead of a VP value — they are discarded at the end of the round played, whatever the outcome, and may not be destroyed.
- **Admiral events (Yamamoto, Halsey):** Played alone or with other plays. Either change the declared suit to **Combined** (affecting all players; the suit stays Combined even if the admiral is later discarded by Typhoon) **or** play 2 regular cards instead of 1.
- **Tora, Tora, Tora! / Cryptanalysts:** Play last in the round, but you may only play that side or discard. They are fated against each other — if one appears after the other, the first is discarded and its player loses his turn.
- **Shangri-La / Decisive Battle:** Must be your first card; discard any number of cards and refill your hand before finishing your play, but you may only play that side (or discard the event to end your turn). The leader must pick the battle and declare suit and time before playing one.
- **Rosie the Riveter:** If the Allies win the battle, keep Rosie as a face-up resource spoil; discard her if the Allies lose or the outcome is no combat.
- **Little Boy:** You must keep it as a spoil regardless of outcome ("you lose 3 VPs for unleashing atomic weapons upon the world"). At the end of the round, roll 1–5 to take the battle card as a free spoil, even if the Japanese win.
- **Tokyo Rose:** Played alone or with a Japanese play; select 2 players after you who may not play Allied this round (the restriction survives even if Tokyo Rose is discarded by Typhoon).
- **Typhoon:** Not playable before 1944. Playable alone or with either side; stops as soon as it is effective or at the end of the current round; never carries into a new round, even on a tie.
- **Day or Night:** May switch the battle time; previously played cards that become illegal are discarded (never the battle card). The switch persists even if the card is later removed by Typhoon. A player who loses his turn passes it on without rolling.
- **Fog:** Hide one regular card and/or attached bonus cards face down until all others have played; your turn-order position is unchanged, so Fog does not let you escape fate or win first-played ties any differently.
- **Catalina / Amagiri:** From your hand (not if deployed for combat values), discard the card to "rescue" a spoil card — denying it to someone and saving it from destruction — when a fate victim is announced or before spoils are divided. Usable even if you played on the other side.
- **Gremlins:** Played against another player; reduces the combat value of his play, but never below 0 in any of the three suits.
- **Variants:** A Chronological Version fights the 37 remaining battles in historical order (Pearl Harbor first, Home Islands always last) with year-staged hand sizes (1941: 3 cards plus 1 per resource spoil; 1942: 4 plus 1 each, max 7; 1943: 5 plus 1 each, max 8; 1944–45: 6 plus 1 each, max 9). Short Versions end after 13 battles by having the leader flip three battle cards and pick one. A Team Version for 4 or 6 players splits into two fixed teams with separate Allied and Japanese draw piles, removing Fog, Day or Night, Gremlins, and Typhoon.

## Player Reference

- **Round flow:** Leader flips 2 battle cards, keeps 1, discards 1 → announces **year** → declares **time** (Day/Night) → declares **suit** (Air / Surface / Sub / Combined) → each player in clockwise order plays at least one legal card, discards one card, or discards entire hand (no passing) → resolve combat in the declared suit → winner's highest contributor divides spoils → refill hands → next leader.
- **Plays per turn:** max 1 regular card (2 with an Admiral), any number of legal bonus and event cards, all on one side only; plays must match the battle's year and time.
- **Combat:** Only declared-suit values count (Combined = all three). "?" = die roll + printed number; sun = +1 in day battles; moon = +1 in night battles. Highest side total wins; tie = engaged cards go under the battle card and the battle continues next round.
- **Spoils:** Battle card (0–8 VP) + destroyed losing force cards (0–6 VP), divided card-by-card among every winning-side player with combat value 1+.
- **Hand size:** 6 base, +1 per full resource (+1/2 per half), max 9.
- **Game end:** After 20 battles; most spoil VPs wins. Tiebreakers: most battle card VPs, then lowest battle card number.
