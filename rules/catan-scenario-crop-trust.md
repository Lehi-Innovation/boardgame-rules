## Overview

Catan: Crop Trust is a semi-cooperative scenario for the Catan base game, developed in collaboration with the Global Crop Diversity Trust. Players settle Catan while managing crop diversity. Five crop types (wheat, beans, maize, quinoa, rice) appear on field hexes, and players must balance personal victory point goals with the collective need to store seeds in a seed vault. There is always an individual winner: either the first player to 10 VP, or — if crop loss triggers an early end — the player who stored the most crops. The scenario adds event tokens, crop tokens on field hexes, and the seed vault display.

## New Components

- 1 fields hex (replaces desert, watermarked with seed vial)
- 90 crop tokens (5 varieties)
- 41 event tokens
- 1 seed vault display
- 4 crop storage record player aids
- 1 number token "9"

## Setup Changes

Follow standard Catan setup with these modifications:
- Replace the desert with the extra fields hex.
- Place crop tokens on field hexes as shown.
- Each player places 4 crop tokens of one plant type on a seed vault space in their color.
- Each player takes 7 face-down event tokens and places 1 under each of their remaining 3 settlements and 4 cities.
- Place event tokens on Longest Road and Largest Army cards.
- Robber starts on the "2" pasture hex.

## Rule Changes

The core Catan rules apply with these additions:

**Crop Tokens:** Fields hexes have crop tokens representing different plant types. When someone rolls the number of a fields hex adjacent to your settlement or city, you may choose to harvest or not to harvest food. To harvest, remove 1 crop token of your choice from the hex and return it to the supply; then take 1 food card (grain card). For a city, you may remove 1–2 crop tokens and take 1–2 food cards. If you choose not to remove a crop token, you receive no food card for that hex. Each eligible player may remove at most 1 crop token per harvest round; harvesting stops when the hex runs out of crop tokens.

**Seed Vault:** Once per turn, a player may make a seed deposit by paying 1 lumber and 1 ore. To deposit: (1) Pay 1 lumber and 1 ore. (2) Take 1 crop token from a fields hex adjacent to one of your settlements/cities and place it on your Crop Storage Record. You receive no reward for the first token in a row; placing the second token earns 1 free development card; placing the 3rd, 4th, or 5th token earns 1 victory point each. You may fill both rows (earning rewards twice). You may not place more than 1 of each crop type per row, and may not store a crop type already in both rows. (3) Take 4 crop tokens of the same type from the supply and place them on your empty seed vault space. (4) Take the 4 crop tokens from your other seed vault space and replant them — 1 token each on 4 different fields hexes (max 7 tokens per hex).

**Event Tokens:** When you build a settlement or city (or claim Longest Road/Largest Army), reveal the event token placed there. Events can be beneficial or harmful, affecting crop availability and gameplay.

**Crop Loss:** Crop tokens are removed from fields hexes through harvesting and event tokens. If a crop type has no tokens on the board and none in the seed vault, it becomes extinct (all remaining supply tokens returned to box). If 3 of 5 fields hexes empty out, or 2 of 5 species go extinct, the game ends immediately with the player who stored the most crops winning. See Scoring / Victory Conditions for full details.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when any one of 3 conditions occurs:

1. **A player reaches 10 VP on their turn** — that player wins. Note: if reaching 10 VP triggers revealing an event token that causes end game condition 2 or 3, those conditions take precedence (see below).
2. **3 of the 5 fields hexes no longer contain crop tokens** — the game ends immediately.
3. **2 of 5 plant species are extinct** — the game ends immediately.

If conditions 2 or 3 occur, the player with the most crop tokens on their Crop Storage Record wins (they contributed most to preserving diversity). Tiebreaker: most victory points; if still tied, both players win.

**Exception — Losing:** If during the production phase you personally remove a crop token causing condition 2 or 3, you lose the game even if you have the most tokens on your Crop Storage Record. In that case, the player with the 2nd most tokens on their record wins.

Standard Catan VP sources (settlements, cities, Longest Road, Largest Army, VP cards) apply. Additional VPs may be earned from the Crop Storage Record (see Seed Vault rules above).

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Extinction:** If no crop tokens of a given type remain on the board AND none are stored in the seed vault, that species is extinct. Return all tokens of that type from the supply to the box; tokens on Crop Storage Records remain. Extinct crops cannot be planted, harvested, or stored. If an event token depicts an extinct species, return it to the box and draw a new one.
- **Event tokens — Crop Loss types:** Regional Crop Loss removes all tokens of the depicted type from fields hexes with the depicted numbers. Small-scale Crop Loss removes 2 tokens of the depicted type (starting from hexes adjacent to your settlements/cities). Large-scale Crop Loss removes 3 tokens similarly. Monoculture removes all tokens from any fields hex that contains only 1 crop type.
- **Robber:** The robber starts on the pasture hex marked "2" (not the desert, which is replaced). The robber does not affect seed vault storage.
- **Event token placement:** Place event tokens face-down under settlements, cities, and on the Longest Road/Largest Army cards at setup. Reveal when those buildings are built or cards are first claimed or change hands. Replenish from the supply stack; shuffle and reuse if the supply is exhausted.
- The game uses a fixed starting setup for the first game; variable setup rules are provided for subsequent games.
- All Catan base game rules apply except where modified by this scenario. Grain resource cards are called "food cards" in this scenario.

## Player Reference

**Standard Catan VP sources:** Settlements (1), Cities (2), Longest Road (2), Largest Army (2), VP cards

**New elements:** Crop tokens on fields, seed vault storage, event tokens under buildings

**Win condition:** First to 10 VP; OR most crop tokens on Crop Storage Record if end game condition 2 or 3 triggers
**Lose condition:** You personally lose if you remove a crop token during production that triggers end game condition 2 or 3
