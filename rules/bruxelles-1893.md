## Overview

In Bruxelles 1893, players are famous architects establishing the reputation of Art Nouveau in the Belgian capital. Over 5 rounds, players place assistants to perform actions on two boards: the Art Nouveau board (with auctions for Bonus cards) and the Brussels board (free actions but risk losing assistants to the Courthouse). Players construct buildings, create and sell works of art, recruit Public Figures, and gain influence at City Hall and the Royal Palace. The architect with the most victory points wins.

## Components

- 1 Brussels gameboard
- 5 Action strips + 1 Bonus strip (forming the Art Nouveau board)
- 5 personal Architect boards (1 per player color)
- 26 Public Figure cards
- 25 Bonus cards
- 12 Stock Exchange cards
- 76 one-BF coins and 12 five-BF coins
- 1 Bracket tile, 1 Workshop cursor
- 6 Compass tiles and 2 compass needles
- 1 Manneken Pis pawn (first player)
- 30 Building tiles (6 per color), 20 discs (4 per color), 35 Assistant pawns (7 per color)
- 30 Noble Materials cubes (10 wood, 10 iron, 10 stone) + 15 white Joker cubes
- 30 Works of Art tiles (5 colors + black)
- 5 Exhibition turn-counting tiles

## Setup

1. Randomly assemble 5 Action strips above the Bonus strip (use non-star side for first games).
2. Each player takes their Architect board, 6 Building tiles, 4 discs, and 7 Assistants.
3. Place 5 Assistants near your board; place 2 on the Courthouse space.
4. Place discs on: City Hall track (first shield), Royal Palace track (first coat of arms), personal Architect track (first space), Scoring track (space 0).
5. Each player receives a 5-BF Georges Brugmann Public Figure card.
6. Sort Exhibition tiles by year (1893–1910) face-down; flip the first tile. Place Workshop cursor at center of the Workshop on the Brussels board. Set up Stock Exchange deck.
7. Place 2 compass needles as shown; randomly place 6 compass tiles around the compass.
8. Shuffle and deal 4 Public Figure cards face-up in the Royal Theater display (rightmost spaces); remaining cards form a draw deck at the leftmost space.
9. Shuffle Bonus cards; place 1 face-up on each Bonus strip space.
10. Each player draws 1 color Work of Art tile at random.
11. First player gets Manneken Pis pawn and 5 BF. Other players receive 6, 7, 8, 9 BF (clockwise from first player).

## Turn Structure

The game is played over **5 rounds**, each with 4 phases:

### A. Stock Exchange Phase
The first player reveals the top Stock Exchange card. Each card line corresponds to a player count and shows 2 pairs of numbers, each defining a shield position (row, column) on the Art Nouveau board. The first player chooses one shield and places the Bracket tile on it, oriented to designate the **largest of the 4 resulting areas** (most action spaces). That area is available for this round.

### B. Action Phase
Starting with the first player and going clockwise, each player performs one action per turn (Art Nouveau board or Brussels board). Players continue until all have passed.

**Passing:** The **first player to pass** takes the Exhibition tile for this year, places it in front of themselves, and receives 1 BF + 1 BF per color of Work of Art they own (maximum 6 BF). All other players who pass each earn 1 BF per color of Work of Art they own (maximum 5 BF). Exhibition tiles allow drawing additional Work of Art tiles in future Workshop actions.

### C. Resolution of the Turn Phase
Resolved in this order:

1. **Bids on the Art Nouveau board:** For each column in the available area, the player with the highest total money bid wins that column's Bonus card. In case of a tie, each tied player benefits from the Bonus card's central power, but the card is discarded.
2. **Designation of the new first player:** Each player counts Manneken Pis symbols on Bonus cards won this round plus 2 Manneken Pis on the Exhibition tile (if they took it). The player with the highest total becomes the new first player and takes the Manneken Pis pawn. Ties are broken by the tied player who is first clockwise from the current first player. (Only current-round cards and tiles count.)
3. **Using Bonus cards:** Each player who won Bonus cards chooses for each card: (a) use its central power (then discard the card), or (b) tuck it under one row of their personal board for an endgame VP multiplier. A player cannot place two cards in the same row in the same round. Cards with no VP symbols cannot be tucked.
4. **Shield Majorities on the Art Nouveau board:** Examine each City of Brussels shield. If a shield has 4 Assistants surrounding it, the player with the most of those 4 Assistants earns VPs equal to their City Hall track level. Ties: each tied player earns their full VPs. Bid money has no effect.
5. **Majority on the Brussels board:** The player with the most total Assistants on the 4 Brussels board action spaces must send one of those Assistants to the Courthouse. Ties: each tied player sends one Assistant.

### D. End of Turn Phase
(Skipped after the 5th round — proceed to final scoring.)

- Retrieve all Assistants from Art Nouveau board (except those at the Courthouse).
- Bids on Art Nouveau board return to general supply.
- Rotate all players' used (rotated) Public Figure cards back upright.
- Reveal Exhibition tile for the new round.
- Discard the Public Figure card from the leftmost (space 0) Royal Theater slot; shift remaining cards right; reveal top deck card to space 3.
- Discard any remaining Bonus cards on the board; draw 5 new Bonus cards.
- Discard the used Stock Exchange card.

## Actions

### Art Nouveau Board Actions (require 1 Assistant + BF bid)
Place an Assistant on an action space **in the available area** and bid at least 1 BF (bid stacked under the pawn). Each action space can hold only 1 Assistant per round. The bid participates in that column's auction.

A player may place an Assistant just to participate in the auction without performing the action.

- **Workshop:** Draw 1 color Work of Art tile (draw additional tiles equal to Exhibition tiles you own; keep 1, return rest).
- **Sale:** Sell one Work of Art. Proceed in order: (1) Move the Workshop cursor horizontally and/or vertically up to a number of spaces equal to total works you own. (2) Choose a Work of Art whose color differs from both works currently in the shop window — selling a color displayed in the shop is forbidden. (3) Immediately earn money and VPs shown by the cursor dot matching your sold work's color (row = money income, column = VP income). (4) Place sold work on one of the two shop spaces (first 2 sales of the game must use empty spaces; afterward, you may cover either). The covered work's color becomes sellable again at the next sale. If the color Work of Art stack or black Work of Art stack runs out, make two new stacks from shop tiles, leaving the top 2 tiles in place.
- **Royal Theater:** Choose one of the 4 face-up Public Figure cards. The rightmost card is free; second costs 1 BF, third costs 2 BF, fourth costs 3 BF (money goes to general supply). Immediately perform that card's action. Then either discard the card or keep it (placed rotated 90° clockwise). Kept cards must remain until game end and will cost money then (failing to pay costs you the card and 5 VP). You cannot keep two identical cards. After taking, shift remaining display cards right and reveal the top deck card to space 3.
- **Construction:** Build one of your 6 Building tiles on the Art Nouveau board. Steps: (1) Pay construction cost as determined by the compass (see Compass Rules). Lower-row tiles cost 2 units, middle-row cost 3, upper-row cost 4; must build lower before middle, middle before upper. (2) If you paid no white Joker cubes, immediately gain 5 VP; if any Joker cubes were used, gain no bonus VP. (3) Rotate one compass needle one space clockwise (the two needles may never point at the same space). (4) Place the built tile on any empty action space on the Art Nouveau board (need not be in the available area; no Assistant may be on the space). Each of the 2 top-row tiles immediately gives 5 VP when built. At game end, each built tile scores VPs equal to your Architect track level. Opponents who place an Assistant on a space with your building get you a secondary action (see Secondary Actions).
- **Materials:** Take exactly 2 Noble Materials cubes of your choice (wood, stone, or iron, in any combination). Joker cubes cannot be chosen. If none remain in the general supply, you cannot take them.

**Compass Rules:** The compass has 6 tiles showing different construction units (wood, stone, iron, 1 noble material of your choice, 3 BF, or empty space) and 2 needles always pointing at 2 different spaces. When constructing, you must pay using both types of unit indicated by the needles. If paying more than 2 units, any split is allowed as long as both types are used. A "1 noble material of your choice" needle means you pay that unit with any material but must still pay at least one unit of the other needle's type. An "empty space" needle means you pay the entire cost with the other needle's unit. Any construction unit may be replaced 1-for-1 with a white Joker cube (but using any Joker forfeits the 5 VP bonus).

### Brussels Board Actions (free, no bid required)
Brussels actions do not require money. The first time a player uses a Brussels action in a round, they place **1 Assistant**. The next player (or the same player again) to use that same action must place **2 Assistants**; the third use requires 3 Assistants, and so on. In 4- and 5-player games, each Brussels action is available twice, so the second player to use an action also places only 1 Assistant.

The 4 Brussels board action spaces are:

- **Market:** Take 3 white Joker material cubes (can replace any Noble Material construction unit).
- **Stock Exchange:** Gain money equal to the value shown at the top of the current round's Stock Exchange card.
- **Fiftieth Anniversary Park:** Perform any 1 action from the 5 Art Nouveau board actions immediately (no Assistant placed on Art Nouveau board, no auction participation). Any action may be chosen even if not currently in the available area.
- **Grand Plaza:** Activate any number of your upright (not rotated) Public Figure cards up to your current Royal Palace track level. Each activated card is rotated 90° clockwise.

### Secondary Actions on the Art Nouveau Board
When a player places an Assistant on an Art Nouveau action space where an **opponent has built a Building tile**, the owner of that tile immediately receives a secondary action after the active player completes their primary action:

- **Workshop space:** Owner takes 1 black Work of Art tile (may be sold later; black works follow the same sale rules as color works, with profits from the cursor's center dot).
- **Sale space:** Owner earns 1 VP per Work of Art tile they own (including black works).
- **Royal Theater space:** Owner may activate 1 upright Public Figure card (rotate it 90°).
- **Materials space:** Owner takes 1 Noble Material cube of their choice.
- **Construction space:** Owner earns 1 VP per Building tile they have already built.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Game end:** After the 5th round, resolve majorities on the Brussels board (step 5 of Resolution phase), then proceed directly to final scoring (skip End of Turn phase).

**Final scoring proceeds in two steps:**

**Step 1 – Pay Public Figure card costs:** Each player must pay the BF amounts shown on every Public Figure card they kept. For each card whose cost cannot or is not paid, the player loses that card and 5 VP.

**Step 2 – Calculate total VP:**
- a. VPs accumulated on the scoring track during the game.
- b. Construction VPs: each built Building tile scores VPs equal to your current Architect track level.
- c. Bonus card row multipliers: each tucked Bonus card row scores (VPs per item) × (number of VP symbols in that row, counting 1 built-in symbol plus any card symbols). Row 1 = sets of 4 BF; Row 2 = Works of Art still owned; Row 3 = Public Figure cards kept; Row 4 = Assistants beyond 2 (excluding those at the Courthouse).
- d. 1 VP per Noble Material cube (Joker cubes are worthless).
- e. The player holding the Manneken Pis pawn at game end earns 5 VP.

**Highest VP total wins.**

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- A player may place an Assistant on the Art Nouveau board just to participate in the auction, without performing the action.
- Each Action space on the Art Nouveau board can hold only 1 Assistant per round.
- Construction requires building lower tiles before middle, middle before upper; each tile comes from the player's personal board.
- Using any white Joker cube in a Construction action forfeits the 5 VP quality bonus (using multiple Jokers has no additional effect).
- The two compass needles may never point at the same space.
- When Work of Art stacks are empty, remake them from shop tiles (leave top 2 in place).
- Public Figure card deck exhausted mid-game: shuffle the discard pile to form a new deck.
- In a 2-player game, each player has a neutral-color Assistant pawn. At the start of each Action phase, each player places their neutral Assistant on an Art Nouveau action space in the available area (no Building tile on the space; no bid). If no empty space exists, skip placement. Neutral Assistants count for shield majorities; if the neutral color wins a majority, no players score VP (ties still pay out normally).
- First game: use the non-star side of Action strips.

## Player Reference

**Round Phases:** Stock Exchange → Actions (with passing for BF + Exhibition tile) → Resolution (Auctions → New First Player → Bonus Cards → Shield Majorities → Brussels Majority) → End of Turn

| Board | Action | Cost |
|-------|--------|------|
| Art Nouveau | Workshop | 1 Assistant + ≥1 BF bid |
| Art Nouveau | Sale | 1 Assistant + ≥1 BF bid |
| Art Nouveau | Royal Theater | 1 Assistant + ≥1 BF bid |
| Art Nouveau | Construction | 1 Assistant + ≥1 BF bid |
| Art Nouveau | Materials | 1 Assistant + ≥1 BF bid |
| Brussels | Market | 1 Assistant (free; +1 per repeat use) |
| Brussels | Stock Exchange | 1 Assistant (free; +1 per repeat use) |
| Brussels | Fiftieth Anniversary Park | 1 Assistant (free; +1 per repeat use) |
| Brussels | Grand Plaza | 1 Assistant (free; +1 per repeat use) |

**Auction Tie:** Tied bidders each use the Bonus card's central power; the card is then discarded.

**Brussels Majority:** Player with most Assistants on Brussels board sends 1 to Courthouse; ties = all tied players send 1.

**Shield Majority:** A shield with 4 surrounding Assistants: majority holder scores VPs = City Hall track level; ties pay full VPs to all tied players.

**First Player:** Determined by Manneken Pis symbols on Bonus cards won this round + Exhibition tile (2 symbols); highest total wins.

**Final Scoring:** Pay Public Figure card costs (or lose card + 5 VP) → score track VPs + construction (tiles × Architect level) + Bonus row multipliers + 1 VP/Noble Material + 5 VP for Manneken Pis holder.
