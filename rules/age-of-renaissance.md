## Overview

A strategic board game of trade, exploration, and cultural development set in Renaissance Europe. Players represent major European powers competing to advance their civilizations through market control, expansion, and acquiring advances. Players must balance economic development, expansion, and cultural progress while managing misery.

## Components

- Game board (2-part mapboard)
- Six sets of colored playing pieces, each containing: 36 square tokens, 25 round dominance markers, 12 market indicators, 1 ship, 2 status markers
- Three shortage/surplus markers
- Six player mats
- A pad of Advance logs
- 64 History cards (commodity cards, event cards, and leader cards)
- Three six-sided dice (white, black, and colored)
- Play money in denominations of 1, 5, 10, 20, and 100

## Setup

Players secretly bid cash (up to $40) to choose their starting capital province. The highest bidder gets their first choice; ties are broken by die roll. Each player then receives $40 minus the amount spent in the capital bid and places a dominance marker on their capital. All remaining dominance markers go on the player mat; all tokens go to the player's stock. Misery markers are placed at 0.

## Turn Structure

Each turn follows seven phases in this sequence:

1. **Determine Order of Play**: Players secretly write down how many expansion tokens they wish to purchase (at $1 each). The player who buys the fewest tokens goes first; ties are broken by capital bid standing. The Renaissance [Q] advance allows one swap per turn with an adjacent player in turn order.
2. **Draw Card Phase**: The first player may pay to remove an existing shortage or surplus. Then each player draws one history card.
3. **Buy/Discard Cards**: Players with Urban Ascendancy [V] may buy one extra card for $10. Players with Master Art [P] may discard one card.
4. **Play Cards**: Players play commodity cards, event cards, and/or leader cards from their hands.
5. **Purchase**: Players may buy ship upgrades, advances, pay stabilization, and purchase misery relief.
6. **Expansion**: Players place tokens and dominance markers on the map to gain and contest market control, or spend tokens to buy extra history cards.
7. **End of Turn**: Award expansion bonus, apply Interest & Profit [L], collect income, reduce misery (New World [U]), and roll for shortage/surplus.

## Actions

### Playing Cards

**Commodity cards**: When played, all players who control provinces producing that commodity are paid from the bank according to the commodity log — the more matching provinces controlled, the higher the payout. Shortage increases payment by one box on the commodity log; surplus decreases it by one box. The shortage or surplus marker is removed once the card is played.

**Event cards**: Trigger a historical event as described on the card. Effects generally last for the remainder of the turn. Event cards can be voided by other cards or advances, becoming unplayable misery burdens.

**Leader cards**: Played to offer discounts on advances purchased in the following Purchase phase. The first leader card played goes into the protected leader card box and benefits only its player. Subsequent leader cards may be claimed by players holding Patronage [E], unless the original owner places cash on the card as protection.

### Expansion

Players expand from controlled territory (or their capital) by placing square tokens and round dominance markers on the map. Expansion can be overland (adjacent provinces) or by sea using trade fleets:

- **Galleys**: Limited to coastal waters and limited transport capacity/distance.
- **Seaworthy Vessels**: Can reach any province on the map (including Iceland and West Africa) but not overseas provinces; transport capacity still limited.
- **Ocean Navigation**: Can reach any province on the map with unlimited tokens, plus the East Indies, India, and China (limited by shipping display number). The New World [U] advance additionally allows control of North and South America.

To dominate a market, a player must place tokens equal to or exceeding the province's market number. The tokens are then replaced by a single dominance marker.

**Conflicts**: When a player wishes to expand into a province already held by another player, a market competition may be triggered. The attacker must commit a required number of tokens (based on market number and tokens/markers already present). Three dice are rolled: the attacker wins if the black die beats the white die, or the colored die exceeds the attacker's position number on the turn order display.

### Advances

There are exactly 26 advances (A through Z) in six categories: Science, Religion, Commerce, Communication, Exploration, and Civics. Advances cost cash; Religion advances also increase misery by one index. Previously purchased advances provide credits that reduce the cost of further advances in the same category. Leader card discounts may further reduce costs.

When a player holds at least one advance of every category, they complete a "tier." Each tier allows the player to buy back one misery index step per turn during the Purchase phase.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The game ends when a player acquires all 26 advances, the last card of the agreed number of epochs is drawn, or an agreed time limit is reached. Before final scoring, a final card play phase is conducted.

**Final score = value of all acquired advances + cash on hand − misery**

The player with the highest score wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Misery**: Each player tracks a misery index. Passing the 1000 square eliminates the player immediately. Misery is deducted from the final score. Stabilization (paying cash each turn based on number of cards held) prevents misery from rising; failure to pay fully converts the owed amount into misery index increases.
- **Satellites**: Provinces with a market number of 1 (inside a square) cannot hold dominance markers. Controlled satellites can support adjacent markets in defense (and with Cosmopolitan [Y], in attack).
- **Capital defense**: Tokens in a player's own capital count double for both attack and defense.
- **Military advantage cards**: Four event cards (Stirrups, Armor, Long bow, Gunpowder) grant a −1 token modifier when attacking and +1 when defending for the turn they are played. Later cards can cancel earlier ones.
- **Nationalism [W]**: Grants a −1 modifier when attacking and +1 when defending within the player's home area.
- **Map changes**: Designer-recommended map corrections are available on BoardGameGeek.
