## Overview

Asara is a card-driven worker-placement and tower-building game set in a magical realm. Players compete as architects building towers from colored sections (bases, trunks, windows, turrets) in 5 different materials. Players dispatch buyer cards to market areas to purchase sections, visit the bank, or build towers. After 4 rounds (years), the player with the most prestige points wins.

## Components

- Game board with market areas, bank, and building circle
- 4 player screens
- Buyer cards (multiple colors per player)
- Tower sections: bases, trunks, windows, turrets in 5 colors (brown, green, red, black, white), some with gold decorations
- Asari (currency)
- Prestige point tokens

## Setup

1. Place the board centrally. Set up tower sections in their respective market areas face up.
2. Each player takes a screen and buyer cards of their color.
3. Distribute starting Asari to each player.
4. Deal starting tower sections per the setup rules.
5. Youngest player starts.

## Turn Structure

Play proceeds clockwise. Each turn:

1. **Dispatch buyer** (place 1 buyer card face up on an empty space in any area of the board).
2. **Execute move** (buy a tower section, get money, build a tower, bribe, take the Caliph's patronage, etc., based on where the buyer was placed).

## Actions

**Dispatching Buyers:**
- If no other buyers in the area: place any color buyer card.
- If buyers already present: must follow suit (match the color already there).
- Cannot follow suit: place any 2 cards face down on 1 space instead.
- Following suit applies to ALL areas (bank, building circle, market areas, etc.).
- Exception: The colored building next to the bank (Extra Funds space) — any buyer card may be placed face down here; no need to follow suit, and any number of cards may be stacked there.

**Possible Moves:**

| Area | Move |
|------|------|
| **Base market** | Buy 1 base section, pay its color's cost |
| **Trunk market** | Buy 1 trunk section, pay its color's cost |
| **Window market** | Buy 1 window section, pay its color's cost |
| **Turret market** | Buy 1 turret section, pay its color's cost |
| **Bank** | Take Asari from the kitty equal to the amount shown on the bank space |
| **Colored building (Extra Funds)** | Take 5 Asari from the kitty (no follow-suit required; face-down buyer) |
| **Building circle** | Build towers using sections from behind your screen |
| **House of Spies (Bribery)** | Pay 3 or 5 Asari; search one pile of tower sections and take 1 section from it, paying that section's color cost |
| **Caliph's Patronage** | Take the Caliph's Patronage tile from whoever holds it; become starting player for next year |

**Buying Sections:** Take 1 face-up section from the market area. Pay the cost in Asari (price depends on color, same for all section types of that color). Gold-decorated sections cost the same as plain ones.

**Building Towers (Building Circle):** The building circle has 7 plots costing 1–7 Asari. When placing a buyer on a plot, pay that many Asari and use (assemble or add to towers) that many tower sections. In reward, receive prestige points equal to the number of sections used.
- Each tower may consist of one color only.
- Each tower has exactly one base and one turret; any number of trunk sections and windows may be added in between.
- A player may build any number of towers simultaneously (new or existing, same or different colors).
- Completed towers may be added to (inserting trunk sections or windows), but parts may not be swapped between towers.
- At end of turn, only complete towers (at minimum a base and a turret) may remain in front of the screen.
- If the desired plot is occupied, place the buyer on the next highest vacant plot (pay more, use fewer sections; prestige = sections used).

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**End-of-year evaluation** (after each of the 4 years):
- 1 prestige point for each tower built so far (any color or height).
- 1 prestige point for each gold-decorated section in any built tower.
- 1 prestige point for holding the Caliph's Patronage.

**Final evaluation** (after the 4th year, immediately following end-of-year scoring):
- Per color (Brown, Green, Red, Black, White): highest tower (most sections) earns 4/6/8/10/12 points; second highest earns 2/3/4/5/6 points.
- Highest tower overall (regardless of color): 4 points for 1st, 2 points for 2nd.
- Largest total number of towers: 12 points for 1st, 6 points for 2nd.
- 1 prestige point for every 10 Asari remaining.
- Gold decorations and Caliph's Patronage do **not** earn points in the final evaluation.

**Ties in final evaluation:** Points for tied places are added together, divided by the number of towers (or players, for the "most towers" category), and rounded up, then awarded to each tied player. In the event of a tie after all evaluation, there is more than one winner.

Highest prestige total after final evaluation wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- Tower sections of the same color always cost the same amount regardless of section type.
- Gold-decorated sections cost the same as plain ones but score 1 prestige per year in end-of-year evaluation (not in final evaluation).
- Unsold sections are refreshed between rounds (fill empty market spaces from top-up piles).
- At the start of each new year: remove all buyer cards, reshuffle all 45 and redistribute, fill market spaces, distribute 20 Asari to each player.
- The player with the Caliph's Patronage begins each new year.
- **Professional variant:** Adds "Recruit buyers" (pay to draw extra buyer cards) and the "House of lighted windows" actions. Lighted windows earn 1 prestige per year (like gold decorations) and serve as tiebreakers in the final evaluation.
