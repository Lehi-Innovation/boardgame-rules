## Overview

At the Gates of Loyang is an economic strategy game set in ancient China where players are farmers supplying vegetables to the flourishing city of Loyang. Players plant and harvest six types of vegetables, then sell them to regular customers, casual shoppers, or at market stalls. The goal is to advance as far as possible along the Path of Prosperity by spending cash to move forward. The game is played over exactly 9 rounds, each with harvest, card drafting, and action phases.

## Components

- 4 T-shaped game boards (with Path of Prosperity and Shop)
- 12 Satisfaction markers (blue/red)
- 24 small 1-Cash coins
- 14 large 5-Cash coins
- 237 wooden vegetable tokens: 48 Wheat (yellow), 46 Pumpkins (orange), 44 Turnips (red), 36 Cabbages (white), 32 Beans (light green), 25 Leeks (dark green)
- 4 Scoring markers
- 2 Starting player tokens (1 larger, 1 smaller)
- 120 Cards including:
  - 4 "Storehouse / Cart" cards
  - 4 "Turn Summary" cards
  - 6 "Loan" cards
  - 36 Private fields (green backs): 4 Home fields (9 spaces), fields with 3/4/5/6 spaces
  - 70 Action cards (brown backs): 6 Common fields, 14 Market stalls, 14 Regular customers, 14 Casual customers, 22 Helpers

## Setup

1. Place money, vegetables, Satisfaction markers, and Loan cards in the supply.
2. Shuffle Action cards; place face-down as the Draw pile.
3. Each player receives: 1 T-shaped board, 10 Cash, 1 Home field, 1 Scoring token (bottom of Path of Prosperity), 1 Storehouse/Cart card, 1 Turn Summary card.
4. Stock each player's Shop with: 3 Wheat, 2 Pumpkins, 2 Turnips, 2 Cabbages, 1 Bean, 1 Leek.
5. Each player receives 8 Private fields (2 sets of 4 with 3/4/5/6 spaces), shuffled and stacked face-down.
6. Choose a Starting player (takes larger token).
7. Starting with the Starting player, each player buys 1 vegetable from their Shop and plants it on their Home field (only Wheat, Pumpkins, or Turnips; max 2 players per vegetable type).

## Turn Structure

The game lasts exactly **9 rounds**. Each round has 3 phases:

### Phase 1: Harvest Phase (Simultaneous)
1. Turn over the top Private field from your Field pile (no new field in the last round).
2. Harvest exactly **1 vegetable** from each of your fields. Place harvested vegetables and any vegetables left in your Storehouse from previous turns on (or beside) your Cart.
3. Remove fully harvested Private fields from the game. Common fields from the Action deck go to the Discard pile.

### Phase 2: Card Phase
1. Shuffle the Discard pile into the Draw pile.
2. Deal each player **4 Action cards**.
3. **Distribution round:** Each player plays exactly 2 Action cards (1 from hand + 1 from the Courtyard):
   - Starting player places 1 card from hand face-up in the Courtyard.
   - Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, either: (a) place 1 card from hand into the Courtyard, OR (b) take 1 card from the Courtyard + 1 card from hand, play both, and place remaining hand cards in the Courtyard (you are done).
4. When a Regular customer is played, place a Satisfaction marker (blue side up) on it.
5. When a Market stall is played, immediately fill it with the indicated vegetables.
6. When a Common field is played, pay 2 Cash immediately.
7. Remaining Courtyard cards form the Discard pile.
8. **New Starting player:** Last player to play 2 cards gets the large Starting player token; second-last gets the small token.

### Phase 3: Action Phase
Players take turns in Starting player order. On your turn, you may perform any actions in any order, as many times as desired (except "Buy a Two-pack" which is once per round).

## Actions

### A) Sow Vegetables as Seed
- Take 1 vegetable from your Cart and place it on an empty field.
- All remaining spaces on that field are then filled with the same vegetable type from the supply.

### B) Buy Vegetables from the Shop
- Pay the dark price tag amount on your board to buy a vegetable from your Shop.

### C) Sell Vegetables to the Shop
- Sell a vegetable from your Cart to your Shop for the pale price tag amount. Place the vegetable back in the empty Shop slot.

### D) Trade Vegetables at a Market Stall
- Trade 1 or 2 vegetables from your Cart for 1 vegetable from a Market stall, as shown on the stall card.

### E) Use or Discard Helpers
- Play a Helper card's ability. Helpers can be discarded at any time (unique among card types).
- There are 20 different Helpers with various abilities. Each appears on one card, except the Trader, who appears on three.

### F) Deliver to Regular Customers
- Regular customers require two specific vegetables delivered each round in a set sequence.
- **First missed delivery:** If you cannot or do not wish to deliver, turn the Satisfaction marker to its red side. No penalty is paid yet.
- **Second consecutive missed delivery:** If you miss a delivery while the marker is already red, you must pay a **2-Cash penalty** to the supply.
- Regular customers provide increasing income over four rounds of delivery.

### G) Deliver to Casual Customers
- Casual customers buy three specific vegetables for a one-time payment.
- The price is modified: +2 Cash if you have fewer Casual than Regular customers; −2 Cash if you have more Casual than Regular customers.

### H) Buy a Two-Pack (Once Per Round)
- Draw 2 Action cards from the Draw pile. The cost equals the greater of the number of Helpers or Market stalls you have in your played area (cards covered by another card do not add to the cost).
- You may keep 0, 1, or 2 of the drawn cards; any not kept are discarded.
- If you keep both cards, place them one on top of the other (top card's bottom line must remain visible). Common fields in a Two-pack still cost 2 Cash immediately.

### Storing and Scoring (End of Action Phase)
- At the end of your turn, store leftover vegetables from your Cart in your Storehouse. Your Cart has unlimited capacity but the Storehouse is limited; excess vegetables must be sold to your Shop or discarded.
- **Move your Scoring marker** along the Path of Prosperity by paying the indicated cost for each step.
- Each step costs progressively more money.

### Loans
- At any time during the game, take a Loan card to receive **5 Cash**.
- Each outstanding Loan moves your Scoring marker back **1 space** along the Path of Prosperity at the end of the game. Loans can never be repaid.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- After 9 rounds, the game ends.
- Each player's position on the Path of Prosperity determines their score.
- Subtract 1 step for each outstanding Loan.
- The player furthest along the Path of Prosperity wins.
- **Tiebreaker:** The player with the most Cash remaining wins.
- **Second tiebreaker:** If still tied, the player with the most vegetables remaining in their fields and Storehouses wins.
- **If still tied:** The tied players are equal and share the win.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Vegetable hierarchy:** Wheat is cheapest/most common; Leek is rarest/most expensive. Shop prices increase with rarity.
- **Field capacity:** Fields produce 1 vegetable per round until depleted. Home fields hold 9 vegetables.
- **Card restrictions:** Once played, Action cards cannot be discarded until used (exception: Helpers can be discarded anytime).
- **2-player variant:** During the Distribution round, a player may flip the top Action card from the Draw pile into the Courtyard before choosing.
- **4-player variant:** The Starting player chooses one of the two non-Starting players as his partner; the other non-Starting player is paired with the Second player. The Starting player and Second player take their Action phase simultaneously first; only after they finish do their respective partners take their turns. "Other player" effects only target your partner.
- **Common fields cost 2 Cash;** Private fields are free.
- **Satisfaction markers:** Blue = delivery not yet missed (marker placed when card is played); Red = one delivery already missed. A 2-Cash penalty is only paid when a delivery is missed while the marker is already on its red side.
- **Path of Prosperity costs escalate:** Early steps are cheap; later steps are very expensive, forcing careful financial management.
- **Solo game:** Separate rules section available.

## Player Reference

**Round Structure:** Harvest → Card Phase (deal 4, draft 2) → Action Phase

**Vegetables (cheapest to rarest):** Wheat → Pumpkins → Turnips → Cabbages → Beans → Leeks

**Key Actions:** Sow (from Cart), Buy from Shop, Sell to Shop, Trade at Market Stalls, Use Helpers, Deliver to Regular Customers, Deliver to Casual Customers, Buy Two-Pack (1/round)

**Loans:** 5 Cash received, -1 Path of Prosperity step at game end

**Game Length:** Exactly 9 rounds

**Victory:** Furthest on the Path of Prosperity (minus loans); tiebreak by most Cash, then most vegetables in fields/Storehouses
