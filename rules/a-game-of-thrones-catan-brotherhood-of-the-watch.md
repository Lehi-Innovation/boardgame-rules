# A Game of Thrones: Catan - Brotherhood of the Watch

## Overview

You are the Night's Watch — the only thing holding back the wildlings trying to migrate to the warmer southern lands. As a natural leader overseeing a small cadre, you must keep the Wall's defenses properly manned with guards, fed by resources flowing from settlements, keeps, and roads in the Gift — the ancient territory bounded by the Starks' kingdom to the south, the seas east and west, and the Wall to the north. Do your job well and you become the new Lord Commander.

The rulebook contains two games: a **base game** (the classic Catan rules system; the rulebook recommends new Catan players play it first) and the **full Brotherhood of the Watch game**, which uses all the base rules plus wildlings, guards, hero cards, and new end-of-game conditions. Each settlement is worth 1 victory point and each keep 2; you start with 2 settlements and 2 roads (2 VPs). In the full game you win with 10 victory points at the **end** of your turn — but the game can also end immediately if the Wall is breached 3 times or 8+ wildlings enter the Gift, in which case the player with the most guards on the Wall wins.

## Components

- 21 terrain hexes of 6 terrain types: forest (produces lumber), hills (brick), pasture (wool), fields (grain), mountains (ore), and **Ice Fields (produces nothing)**. All 21 hexes are used in a 4-player game; 16 in a 3-player game.
- Frame pieces that hold the board together, with spaces for the areas north of the Wall: the **Frostfangs**, 3 clan areas (Hornfoot Clan, Ice River Clan, Cave People Clan) with 5 camps each, 6 trails numbered 1–8, 4 clearings, the wall breach track, and **Tormund's Camp**.
- The Wall, made of 4 wall sections with 5 guard spaces each, plus a breach marker.
- Round number tokens (taller numbers with more pips are rolled more often).
- Resource cards in 5 types: lumber, brick, wool, grain, ore (sorted into face-up supply stacks).
- Development cards of 3 kinds: ranger patrol (purple banner), progress (green), and victory point (orange).
- Special cards "Longest Road" and "Largest Patrol" (each has a 2 VP side and a 1 VP side).
- **11 hero cards** (each with an A-side and a B-side, e.g. "A2" Bowen Marsh, Lord Steward: "You exchange a single resource card with the supply at a 1:1 rate") and 2 "New Recruits" cards ("Add 1 of your guards to the Wall for free").
- Per player: 5 settlements, 4 keeps, 15 roads, **7 guard pieces**, and a building cost card (with wildling tokens depicted).
- Wildling figures of 3 types — climbers, giants, and regular wildlings — plus face-down wildling tokens.
- 8 VP tokens.
- Tormund the Robber figure.
- Two 6-sided production dice and one **12-sided wildling die**.

## Setup

**Base game setup (page 2):** Assemble the frame, place the hexes face up as shown in the standard set-up, and place 1 number token on each hex. Sort the resource cards into 5 face-up supply stacks. Shuffle the development cards and place them face down. Set the 11 hero cards and 2 "New Recruits" cards aside (not used in the base game), and leave the 12-sided die, gray figures, wall, and guards in the box. Place the "Longest Road" and "Largest Patrol" special cards **2 VP side up** and the two 6-sided dice beside the board. Each player chooses a color, takes the pieces and a building cost card, places 2 settlements and 2 roads as shown, and receives 1 resource for each terrain hex adjacent to their starting settlement (the one marked with a white star). The oldest player goes first.

**Full game additions:**
- Place the wildling die beside the production dice and the 4 wall sections on the board.
- Shuffle **all** the development cards to form the stack.
- Place the Longest Road and Largest Patrol cards **1 VP side up**.
- Place your build cost card face up (wildling tokens depicted).
- Place the breach marker on the "0" space of the wall breach track and the 8 VP tokens beside the board.
- Each player rolls the production dice; highest roll goes first.
- **Wildlings:** Place the wildling figures on the Frostfangs. Mix all wildling tokens face down so nobody knows which is which. Place 1 wildling token face down on each settlement space of your build cost card and 1 of your settlements on top of it; place 2 wildling tokens and 1 keep on each keep space. Remaining tokens form a face-down pool beside the board.
- **Hero cards:** Place all hero cards next to the board, A-side up, as a display. In a 4-player game: player 1 takes "A4" Yarwick, player 2 "A3" Tarley, player 3 "A2" Marsh, player 4 "A1" Mormont. In a 3-player game: player 1 takes "A3" Tarley, player 2 "A2" Marsh, player 3 "A1" Mormont.
- Each player takes **7 guard pieces** in their color.
- Placement runs **3 rounds instead of 2**: rounds 1 and 2 are as in the base game (place 1 settlement and 1 road each); in round 3, each player places **1 guard on the Wall**, starting with player 1 and continuing clockwise.

Experienced players can use a variable board — randomize the terrain hexes, but never the number tokens (see the Rules Reference under Set-Up, Variable).

## Turn Structure

On your turn, perform these steps in order, then pass the dice to the player on your left:

1. **Roll the 3 dice** — the two 6-sided production dice and the **12-sided wildling die** together. First resolve production: the sum of the production dice determines which hexes produce, for all players. A hex blocked by a wildling (or occupied by Tormund) does not produce. Then resolve any **wildling advance** indicated by the wildling die: each trail is a numbered connection between a camp and a clearing, and if the die result (1–12) matches a trail number and a wildling sits in the camp connected to that trail, it advances down the trail to the clearing. If a clan area holds multiple wildlings, the one in the camp closest to the numbered trails advances, and the others each move 1 camp closer to fill unoccupied camps.
2. **Trade** resource cards with other players and/or with the supply. The standard supply rate in the full game is **3:1 (instead of 4:1)**.
3. **Build** roads, settlements, and/or keeps, **recruit guards**, and/or buy development cards.

You may also play 1 development card at any time during your turn (not one bought that same turn, except victory point cards).

## Actions

### Building Costs
| Item | Cost | VP |
|------|------|----|
| Road | 1 Brick + 1 Lumber | 0 |
| Settlement | 1 Brick + 1 Wool + 1 Grain + 1 Lumber | 1 |
| Keep (upgrade a settlement) | 3 Ore + 2 Grain | 2 |
| **Guard** | **1 Brick + 1 Wool + 1 Lumber** | see VP tokens |
| Development Card | 1 Ore + 1 Wool + 1 Grain | varies |

You cannot build more pieces than your pool allows: maximum 5 settlements, 4 keeps, and 15 roads (plus 7 guards).

### Resource Production
Each settlement bordering a producing hex yields 1 resource card; each keep yields 2. Settlements and keeps usually border 2–3 terrain types and can harvest up to 3 different resources per roll.

### Roads, Settlements, Keeps
- A new road must connect to one of your existing roads, settlements, or keeps; only 1 road per path.
- A settlement requires an unoccupied intersection, one of your roads leading to it, and the **distance rule**: all 3 adjacent intersections must be vacant (no settlements or keeps, even yours).
- A keep is built only by upgrading one of your settlements (return the settlement piece to your supply).

### Recruiting Guards
After paying for a guard, place it on the **lowest-numbered unoccupied space** on a wall section of your choice. While you have 3 guards anywhere on the Wall, you receive 1 VP token; while you have 5+ guards, you receive 1 additional VP token (2 VPs total). If your guard count falls below a threshold, return the corresponding VP token to the supply.

### Wildling Tokens (Creating New Wildlings)
When you build a settlement, reveal the wildling token under it on your build cost card and migrate a wildling of the shown type (in blue) from the Frostfangs to the shown clan area (in red). When you build a keep, do this twice, then place a wildling token from the pool under the settlement you return to your build cost card. Each time you take the Longest Road or Largest Patrol special card, draw a random wildling token from the pool to migrate a wildling. Used tokens go face up beside the board; if the face-down pool is depleted, re-mix the face-up tokens into a new pool.

### Trading
- **With players:** Only with the player whose turn it is; the other players may not trade among themselves.
- **With the supply:** 3:1 in the full game (the base game's standard rate is 4:1). With a settlement/keep on a trade route, trade at the better rate the route indicates: 3:1 or 2:1.

### Rolling a 7 / Tormund the Robber
On a "7", no one receives resources. Each player with more than 7 resource cards returns half (rounded down) to the supply. Then you must move Tormund immediately to a number token on any other terrain hex and steal 1 random resource card from 1 opponent with a settlement or keep adjacent to that hex (your choice if several qualify). While Tormund occupies a hex, that hex does not produce.

### Development Cards
- **Ranger patrol** (purple): stays face up in front of you after play. In the base game, the only option you can use is to move Tormund as if you had rolled a "7". The first player with 3 ranger patrol cards face up receives the **Largest Patrol** special card; a player who accumulates more ranger patrol cards than the current holder immediately takes the card.
- **Progress** (green): follow its instructions, then remove it from the game.
- **Victory point** (orange): keep hidden; reveal only during your turn when you are sure you have 10 victory points. You may play any number of VP cards, even ones bought that turn, if playing them wins you the game.

Development cards never go back into the supply, and you cannot buy any if the deck is empty.

### Hero Cards
Your hero card gives you the specific advantage described on it. The first time you use it, choose one: (a) return it to the display A-side up and take any other hero card from the display, or (b) flip it to its **B-side** to use the ability again on a subsequent turn — after that second use you must return it A-side up and take another hero of your choice. You may not use a hero card the same turn you take it, and hero cards cannot be traded, given away, or stolen.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

The full game can end in 3 ways:

1. If the Wall has been **breached 3 times**, the game ends immediately, and the player with the most guards on the Wall wins.
2. If the number of wildlings in the Gift **exceeds 7 (8 or more)**, the game ends immediately, and the player with the most guards on the Wall wins.
3. If you have **10 VPs at the END of your turn**, you win. Unlike most Catan games, you only win at the end of your turn — you must still advance the wildlings as usual throughout your entire turn, which could trigger an immediate end under conditions 1 or 2.

**VP sources:** settlements (1 each), keeps (2 each), victory point development cards, **Longest Road (1 VP in the full game)** for the first continuous road of at least 5 segments (not counting forks, not interrupted by a foreign road or keep), **Largest Patrol (1 VP in the full game)** for the first player with 3 ranger patrol cards face up, and guard VP tokens (1 VP at 3 guards on the Wall, 2 VPs total at 5+).

**Resolving ties for most guards:** the tied player with the most VPs wins; if still tied, the tied player with the oldest guard on the Wall (the guard on the lowest number in any wall section) wins; if still tied, the player with the guard in the most western location (closest to the Frostfangs) wins.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Wildling types on reaching a clearing:** A **climber** immediately jumps over the Wall and blocks the first hex not occupied by a wildling directly south of that wall section — this does **not** count as a wall breach. A **giant** removes itself and the guard on the "1" space of the defending wall section (the guard returns to its owner, the giant to the Frostfangs); remaining guards on that section each move to the next lowest number; if there is no guard on that section, the giant breaches the Wall. A **regular wildling** stays in the clearing if the number of regular wildlings there is equal to or less than the guards on the defending wall section; if wildlings outnumber guards, they breach the Wall.
- **Wall breach:** Remove the guard on the "1" space of that wall section (return it to its owner); the other guards on that section fill the lowest-numbered unoccupied spaces from lowest to highest. Then each wildling in the clearing jumps the Wall one at a time, blocking the first wildling-free hex directly south. A blocked hex does not produce. Move the breach marker 1 space on the breach track — **3 breaches end the game**.
- **Wildling rush:** When a fifth wildling moves into a clan area that already holds 4, the wildlings in the two camps closest to the trails immediately advance — the one in the lowest camp uses the lower-numbered trail, the next closest uses the higher-numbered trail. The 3 remaining wildlings then fill in: 3rd camp to 1st, 4th camp to 2nd, 5th camp to 3rd.
- **Longest Road swing:** If another player builds a longer road than the current holder's, they immediately take the special card and its victory points.
- If the supply lacks enough of a resource to fulfill everyone's production, consult the Rules Reference.
- The "New Recruits" card reads "Add 1 of your guards to the Wall for free"; the 2 New Recruits cards are set aside and not used in the base game.
- Numbers 6 and 8 are rolled most often and make strong settlement spots — but those hexes are also prime targets for Tormund.
