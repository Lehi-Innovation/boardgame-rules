## Overview

City of the Great Machine is an asymmetric one-vs-many steampunk board game set in a flying city ruled by a steam-powered artificial intellect — the Great Machine. One player controls the Great Machine, commanding Servants and mechanical Guards. The other 1–3 players are Revolutionaries, each controlling a Hero. The Great Machine wins by completing its Master Plan (marker reaches XII on the track). The Revolutionaries win by achieving 3 successful Riots (or 2 successful Riots plus reaching Riot Stage on the Discontent track).

## Components

- 5 Minor District tiles + 4 Major District tiles (modular city)
- 1 Progress Board (combined Master Plan track I–XII and Discontent track with 18 slots + Riot Stage)
- 3 Servant miniatures (Lex Hecate, Judgementor, Phantasmer) + 18 wooden Guard figures
- 6 Hero miniatures + 6 oversized Hero cards
- 54 Access cards (9 per Hero)
- 30 Famous Citizen tokens (5 each of Artists, Scientists, Merchants, Industrialists, Nobles; plus Traitors)
- 9 Directive cards
- 21 City Event cards (includes "The Countdown")
- Trust/Bond tokens (double-sided; 1s and 4s)
- 2 Riot tokens, 3 Raid tokens, 3 Servant Order tokens
- 1 Assault die, 1 Escape die
- Reference cards for both sides

## Setup

1. Arrange the District tiles so each District connects to at least 2 others and the city stays connected (Two Route Principle).
2. Shuffle and place Famous Citizen tokens face-down in the blue-highlighted squares on each District (4 per Minor District and on the Central Square; 1 each on Grand Citadel, Tower of L.A.W., and Control Nexus — 27 total). Set 3 aside face-down near the Progress Board (arrested).
3. Place 2 Guard figures in each District (in the smaller squares on the left).
4. The Great Machine takes the 3 Servant miniatures and places them all in the Grand Citadel. Takes Directive deck (shuffled face-down), 3 Raid tokens, and Reference cards.
5. Set the Master Plan pointer to III (experienced Great Machine vs. new Revolutionaries may use II or I). Set the Discontent pointer to the starting slot marked with the icon for 1, 2, or 3 Revolutionaries.
6. Build City Event deck: take 6 of 10 "City Event II" cards and place face-down on "The Countdown" card; take 6 of 10 "City Event I" cards face-down on top. Final deck is 13 cards (6×I on top, 6×II in middle, The Countdown at bottom).
7. Each Revolutionary chooses a Hero, places their miniature in the District shown at the bottom of their Hero card, takes all 9 of their Access cards, randomly discards 1 (forfeited, face-down), and takes 2 Revolutionary Reference cards.
8. Place Riot tokens, dice, and Trust/Bond tokens within reach as common supply.

## Turn Structure

Each round has 5 phases:

### I. Opening Phase
- Great Machine reveals the top City Event card (only the topmost is in effect).
- Great Machine receives Bonds from the common reserve based on the current Stage of Discontent (6 Bonds at Zero Stage, 7 at Stage 1, up to 12 at Stage 5 and Riot Stage).
- Each Famous Citizen provides 1 Trust to the Hero(es) in their District (shared if multiple Heroes present; Revealed Traitors provide no Trust). Each Hero cannot hold more Trust than their Hero card maximum; excess is discarded.
- Each Hero secretly selects one Access card and places it face-down. The card's District is the Hero's destination for this round. The Great Machine must not see the chosen cards.

### II. (Implicit — no separate phase; Heroes' Access card selection happens in Phase I)

### III. The Great Machine's Phase
The Great Machine moves Guards and Servants (in any order) and Servants take Actions.

- **Guard movement:** Pay 1 Bond per District a Guard exits.
- **Servant movement:** Pay 2 Bonds per District a Servant exits. A Servant may not move after taking an Action.
- **Servant Actions (cost 2 Bonds each, except as noted):**
  - *Arrest* (Standard): Remove 1 revealed active Famous Citizen from the District; place face-down with arrested Citizens.
  - *Repair* (Standard): Put 1 broken Guard figure upright.
  - *Raid* (Standard): Place a Raid token on the District to try to detain Heroes arriving there this round.
  - *Grand Citadel* (Special): Draw 1 Directive card.
  - *Tower of L.A.W.* (Special): Publish 1 Directive from hand (max 3 published at a time).
  - *Control Nexus* (Special): Reposition one District tile (free — costs no Bonds).
  - *Central Square* (Special, free): Receive Bonds equal to (Citizens in Central Square − operational Guards).

### IV. The Heroes' Turns
Heroes take turns in Initiative order (lowest Initiative number first). Each Hero fully completes their turn before the next begins.

**Part 1 — Movement:** Reveal the chosen Access card. The Hero must move to the destination District shown. Heroes may pass through any connected Districts; they ignore Raid tokens in Districts they pass through (only the destination triggers a Raid). Pay 1 Trust for each operational Guard in each District the Hero exits. If a Hero cannot pay: discard all remaining Trust and roll the Escape die — success icon means proceed; handcuffs icon means the Hero is detained.

**Part 2 — Action:** After arriving, a Hero may take one Action in their destination District.

- *Incitement* (Minor Districts only; costs 1 Trust per Guard in the District): Discontent rises by 1.
- *Attack a Guard* (Standard, any District): Pay any amount of Trust and roll the Assault die. Success if the die result ≤ Trust paid (and no shield rolled) — the Guard is broken (laid on its side). Trust is spent regardless.
- *Identify Famous Citizens* (Standard): Pay 1 Trust per Guard in the District for each Citizen identified. Peek at each chosen token secretly; rotate token to show the green indicator. Identified Citizens may be viewed by any Revolutionary at any time.
- *Move Famous Citizens* (Standard): Pay 1 Trust per District each Citizen exits. Move 1 or more Citizens from the Hero's District to other Districts.
- *Start a Riot* (Standard): Pay 1 Trust per Guard for each unidentified Citizen in the District. Reveal all Citizens. **Successful Riot:** more active Citizens than (Guards + Traitors) — place a Riot token; break all Guards in the District; Heroes cannot start another Riot here. **Failed Riot:** Master Plan advances by 1; Great Machine may immediately arrest any or all active Citizens.
- *Special Actions* (Major Districts only; cost 1 Trust per Guard): See Access card for the 2 available options per Major District. (Central Square: Discontent rises by Citizens minus Guards. Control Nexus: reposition a District OR give a Hero 3 Trust. Tower of L.A.W.: cancel a published Directive OR retrieve a forfeited Access card. Grand Citadel: discard a Directive from Great Machine's hand OR return 1 arrested Citizen to any District.)

**Detainment:** A Hero is detained if they (a) end movement in a District with a Raid token or (b) are captured by the Escape die roll. The Great Machine chooses: *Option A* — advance Master Plan by 1 (Hero continues their turn as normal, but is considered detained for card effects); or *Option B* — Hero's turn ends, and apply 1 of 2 Punitive Measures: "No Trust!" (Hero discards all Trust; Great Machine gains same number as Bonds) or "No Access!" (Hero forfeits their chosen Access card).

Each Hero can only be detained once per round.

### V. Closing Phase
1. **Failed Raids:** For each Raid token with no detained Hero, Discontent rises by 1. Remove all Raid tokens.
2. **City Event — End of Round:** Resolve the "At the end of this round only…" condition on the current City Event card, if any.
3. **City Event — Master Plan Advancement:** Check the "The Master Plan advances if…" condition. If satisfied, Master Plan advances by 1. When the Countdown card is active, Master Plan advances by 1 each round automatically.

## Actions

See Turn Structure above for all actions. Key additional rules:

- **Broken Guards:** Laid on their side. Do not count for Trust costs, Riot results, or movement costs until repaired by a Servant.
- **Directives:** Published Directives are face-up and in effect for all players to see. Max 3 published at once. The Directive deck is never reshuffled.
- **Access cards:** A Hero can only move to a District for which they have an Access card. Forfeited Access cards are face-down; Heroes may view their own forfeited cards but cannot use them; Great Machine may not view them.
- **Famous Citizens:** Active if their class number ≤ current Stage of Discontent. Traitors never support Riots. Reaching the Riot Stage on the Discontent track itself counts as 1 successful Riot.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

- **Great Machine wins:** The Master Plan pointer reaches XII. This can happen via detainment (Option A), a failed Riot, or the City Event Closing Phase condition.
- **Revolutionaries win:** Achieve 3 successful Riots. Two ways to reach 3: (a) 3 successful Riots in 3 different Districts, or (b) 2 successful Riots plus reaching Riot Stage on the Discontent track (which counts as the 3rd Riot).
- The winner is whichever side achieves their objective first. Victory is immediate.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Food chain / Initiative:** Heroes act in ascending Initiative number order (lowest first).
- **Hero movement through Districts:** Heroes may freely move through Districts they have no Access card for, but must end at their destination District (the one on their chosen Access card).
- **Famous Citizen states:** Face-down = unknown; turned to show green indicator = identified (can be seen by all Revolutionaries); face-up = revealed (e.g., after a Riot). Arrested Citizens are removed from the City (placed face-down near Progress Board). A Citizen cannot provide Trust if they are a revealed Traitor.
- **Trust limit:** Each Hero has a maximum Trust shown on their Hero card. Excess Trust is immediately discarded.
- **Riot Stage:** If the Discontent pointer ever reaches the red circle at the end of the Discontent track (Riot Stage), this itself counts as 1 successful Riot. Discontent cannot rise or fall after reaching Riot Stage; the Great Machine continues to receive 12 Bonds.
- **Solo rules:** 1 Revolutionary controls all 3 Heroes.
- **2 Revolutionaries:** One controls 2 Heroes, the other controls 1 (or they jointly control one Hero together).
