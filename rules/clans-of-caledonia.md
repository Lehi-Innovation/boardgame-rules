## Overview

Clans of Caledonia is a strategic economic game set in 19th-century Scotland. Players represent unique historical clans competing to produce, trade, and export agricultural goods and whisky. Over 5 rounds, players expand their settlements, produce goods, fulfill export contracts, and score points. The clan with the most Victory Points (VP) wins.

## Components

- 4 double-sided Map modules (form a modular board)
- 4 Player boards with clan tokens
- 1 Market board (flip to side matching player count)
- 1 Export board (flip to side matching player count)
- 9 Scoring tiles, 9 Port bonus tiles, 9 Starting tiles, 9 Clan tiles
- 50 Export contract tiles, 4 Export box tiles, 4 Glory tiles
- Goods tokens: Milk (10), Wool (14), Cheese (10), Grain (10), Bread (10), Whisky (10)
- Import tokens: Cotton, Tobacco, Sugar cane
- Clan components per player: Cows (16), Sheep (16), Fields (16), Bakeries (16), Distilleries (16), Cheese dairies (16), Workers (32), Merchants (28), Shipping tokens, Turn order tokens, Glory tokens
- 8 Technology tiles, 6 transparent Price markers
- 70 Coins, Scoresheet pad, 4 Player aids

## Setup

1. Randomly assemble the modular map (4 modules arranged so module letters A–D are in clockwise alphabetical order).
2. Choose 4 Port bonus tiles randomly and place one at each corner of the map (position depends on player count).
3. Set up the Market board (appropriate side for player count); place Price markers on starting prices; place Goods tokens below the Market.
4. Set up the Export board (appropriate side for player count); randomly select 5 Scoring tiles and place face-up; deal one face-up Export contract to each box (one box remains empty in solo/3-player). Place players' Glory tokens and Import tokens (Cotton, Tobacco, Sugar cane) off the board.
5. Each player chooses a color and places all tokens on their Player board. Place 8 Workers on the 4 forest (Woodcutters) and 4 mountain (Miners) locations. Fill the Merchant track with 5 Merchants; place 2 Merchants in stock. Set Shipping token on the first Shipping track space. Flip Technology tiles to the arrow side. Take 1 Export box tile and 4 Port markers.
6. Deal Clans: randomly draw one more Clan tile than the number of players, plus one Starting tile per Clan. In reverse turn order, each player selects a Clan and its Starting tile, taking the shown Goods and money. The starting player chooses last.
7. Determine turn order randomly; place Turn order tokens on the Export board track.
8. Place first Workers: in turn order, each player places one Worker (Woodcutter on a forest space, Miner on a mountain space) on the map, paying the land cost + unit cost. Then in reverse turn order, each player places a second Worker (need not be adjacent to the first). Each space can only ever hold one unit.

## Turn Structure

Each of the **5 rounds** has 4 phases:

### Phase 1: Preparation (skip in round 1)
- Flip the previous round's Scoring tile face-down.
- Refill any empty Export board boxes with Export contracts from the draw pile.
- Players retrieve their Merchants from the Market and place them next to their Player boards.

### Phase 2: Actions
Each player takes exactly one Action per turn in turn order. Continue until all players have passed. Most actions can be taken multiple times.

### Phase 3: Production
All players simultaneously produce Income and Goods from units deployed on the map (see Production table below).

### Phase 4: Scoring
Players earn Glory according to the current round's Scoring tile if they meet its requirement.

## Actions

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **1. Trade** | Place Merchants from your stock onto the buy or sell area of one type of Good on the Market. Pay or receive the current price × quantity. After trading, adjust the Price marker up (buy) or down (sell) by the number of Goods traded. Only one type of Good per Trade action; take multiple Trade actions to trade different types. |
| **2. Obtain an Export Contract** | Pay the current round cost (in round 1, receive £5 instead) to take any Export contract from the Export board and place it on your empty Export box. You can hold only one unfulfilled Export contract at a time (exception: Clan Buchanan). You must fulfil a contract before obtaining a new one. |
| **3. Expand** | Place one unit from your Player board onto an empty space of the map that is neighbouring (adjacent, no river between) or within Shipping reach of any of your units. Pay the unit cost (shown above the unit on the Player board) plus the land cost (£1–£6 shown on the space). Land restrictions: Grasslands accept Sheep, Cows, Cheese dairies, Bakeries, Fields, and Distilleries; Forests accept only Woodcutters; Mountains accept only Miners. **Neighbourhood bonus:** Expanding adjacent to an opponent's unit lets you immediately buy that Good at a £2 discount (Basic goods) or £3 discount (Processed goods). |
| **4. Upgrade Shipping** | Pay £4 to advance your Shipping marker one space, allowing you to expand across rivers (River-crossing), then one loch space (1-loch), then two loch spaces (2-loch), etc. |
| **5. Upgrade Technology** | Pay £10 (or £5 with a Bonus Upgrade) to flip a Technology tile, increasing that Worker type's income by £2 per Production phase. |
| **6. Hire a Merchant** | Pay £4 to take an additional Merchant from your Player board into your stock for use in future Trade actions. |
| **7. Fulfil an Export Contract** | Pay the Goods shown on the left side of your Export contract tile to gain the benefits on the right (money, Import goods, Direct export bonuses). Move Import tokens (Cotton, Tobacco, Sugar cane) on the Export board track when imported; reaching a marked step earns £1. Contracts requiring Meat require slaughtering Cows or Sheep (remove from map, return to Player board). |
| **8. Pass** | Move your Turn order token to the leftmost available position on the next round's track and collect the Pass bonus money shown. The passing order determines turn order next round. |

**Free Action — Port bonus tile:** If a Port bonus tile is neighbouring or within Shipping reach of any of your units, you may use that tile's bonus once per game (before or after your main Action on any turn). Mark it with one of your Port markers.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

After 5 rounds, calculate final VP. The player with the most VP wins. Ties are broken by remaining money (most money wins); if still tied, those players share the victory.

**Final Scoring categories:**

| Category | VP |
|----------|----|
| Glory | 1 VP per step on Glory track |
| Basic goods in stock | 1 VP each (Wool, Milk, Grain) |
| Processed goods in stock | 2 VP each (Bread, Cheese, Whisky) |
| Money | 1 VP per £10 (leftover money also breaks ties) |
| Hops (from fulfilled Export contracts) | 1 VP per Hops unit |
| Cotton, Tobacco, Sugar cane | 3, 4, or 5 VP each based on rarity (the most-imported Import good is worth 3 VP each; second-most is 4 VP; least-imported is 5 VP; ties follow fixed order: Cotton rarest, then Tobacco, then Sugar cane) |

**Export Scoring (majority of fulfilled Export contracts):**
- 3–4 players: Most fulfilled contracts: **12 VP**; second-most: **6 VP**. Tied players split the combined VP of tied ranks (round down).
- 2 players: Most fulfilled contracts: **8 VP**; second player scores 0 VP. Tied players each receive **4 VP**.

**Settlement Scoring (number of Settlements within Shipping reach of each other):**
A Settlement is a cluster of neighbouring same-colour units (adjacent, no river between). Single isolated units are also Settlements. Count only those Settlements within Shipping reach of each other; if not all are connected, use the largest reachable group.
- 3–4 players: Most Settlements: **18 VP**; second-most: **12 VP**; third-most: **6 VP**. Tied players split VP of tied ranks (round down).
- 2 players: Most Settlements: **12 VP**; second player: **0 VP**.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

- **Market prices** shift dynamically: buying raises the price; selling lowers it. Each Price marker moves by the number of Goods traded.
- Each clan has a unique special ability that significantly affects strategy (see Clan tiles).
- **Whisky production** is not a two-step aging process for most players — a Distillery simply processes 1 Grain into 1 Whisky during the Production phase. (Whisky aging is exclusively Clan MacKenzie's special ability.)
- **Expanding across water** requires Shipping upgrades: River-crossing allows adjacent river crossings; each additional Shipping level adds one more loch space of reach. A player may never hop across land spaces.
- **Slaughtering:** Cows and Sheep on the map can be slaughtered (returned to Player board) only as part of Fulfilling an Export Contract requiring Meat. This frees the space and reduces that animal's production.
- **Building bonus:** Deploying your 4th building of one type (Cheese dairy, Bakery, or Distillery) with an empty Export box lets you draw 3 Export contracts and optionally take 0 or 1 (paying the normal cost if you take one).
- **Direct Export Bonuses** (received when fulfilling contracts): money, a free land-cost Expand action, or a Bonus Upgrade (discounted Technology upgrade at £5, free Shipping upgrade, or free Merchant hire).
- Money and goods tokens are unlimited — use substitutes or Goods tiles if supplies run out.
- Players may never hide any information (all information is public).
