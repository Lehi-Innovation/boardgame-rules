## Overview

Assault on Hoth is a two-player tactical wargame recreating the Battle of Hoth from The Empire Strikes Back. One player controls the Imperial Ground Assault Group; the other commands Echo Base Hoth Ground Defense Forces. The Imperial player must destroy the Shield Generator before five Rebel Transports escape; the Rebel player wins by delaying the Imperial advance long enough for five Transports to get away, or by destroying all Imperial forces on the map.

## Components

- One map board (with Walker/Snowspeeder displays, damage tables, and fire/armor charts)
- 59 cardboard counters (units and markers)
- 60 plastic stands
- 40 markers (damage and Force Point markers)
- 8 fire dice and 2 damage dice (10 dice total)
- 42 cards: Action Deck, Event Deck, and 1 Luke Hero Card

## Setup

1. Lay the board between players. The Rebel player sits on the side with the Snowspeeder Displays; the Imperial player sits opposite.
2. Remove blank cards; give the Rebel player the Luke Hero Card. Separate remaining cards into the Action Deck and the Event Deck; shuffle each separately.
3. **Imperial setup:** Place Walkers 1–4 and two AT-STs in the Imperial Reinforcement Sector (one per hex; Walkers may face any direction). Assign all 8 light Snowtroopers and 4 heavy Snowtroopers to Walkers (3 per Walker; record in Walker Carried Troops boxes).
4. **Rebel setup:** Place Snowspeeders 1–5 in Rebel Reinforcement Sector 4 (one per hex). Place 10 light Troopers, 4 heavy Troopers, and 6 Laser Towers anywhere south of the Rebel Forward Line (one per hex; no units in cliff hexes; any Troopers may go in the Base Box). Secretly assign Luke to a Snowspeeder by placing the Luke Hero Card face-down under that Snowspeeder's display.

## Turn Structure

The game is played in turns; each turn consists of multiple **Action Rounds**.

Each Action Round:
1. **Draw an Action Card** from the top of the Action Deck.
   - If it is a **Draw Event Card**: draw and resolve the top Event Card (see Special Rules).
   - Otherwise: all units of the type listed on the card may perform the action(s) shown. A player is never required to act.
2. Set the used card aside and draw again for the next Action Round.
3. When no Action Cards remain, shuffle the deck and begin a new turn.

Play continues until one player wins.

## Actions

Action Cards activate one of the following unit types and allow move and/or fire actions:

- **Move units:** Each activated unit receives 5 Movement Points (MPs). General cost: 1 MP per hex entered. Walkers cost 3 MPs to enter rough hexes and 1 MP to turn one hexside (must face destination hex before entering). Snowspeeders pay 1 MP for cliff hexes; no other unit may enter cliff hexes.
- **Fire at enemy:** Roll fire dice equal to the firing unit's modified fire strength. Imperial player looks for Vader symbols; Rebel player looks for Saber symbols. Hit if relevant symbols rolled ≥ target's armor strength. Fire strength is reduced by range (number of macrohexes to target) and by 1 if target is a Laser Tower, AT-ST, or Trooper in rough terrain. Walkers may only fire at targets within their forward field of fire.
- **Harpoon attack (Snowspeeders):** Only on "Snowspeeders Move, Fire, or Harpoon" Action Cards. A Snowspeeder with a live gunner that is adjacent to a Walker may attempt a harpoon attack. Roll 5 fire dice if in the same macrohex as the Walker, or 4 if in a different macrohex. If Saber symbols ≥ Walker's armor strength, the Walker is destroyed immediately.

## Scoring / Victory Conditions

**Imperial Victory:** The Imperial player wins if he destroys the Shield Generator before the fifth Transport Away! Event Card is drawn.
- The Shield Generator has armor strength 3 (reduced to 2 if the Power Grid is destroyed first). The Power Grid has armor strength 3.
- Destroying it follows the same fire rules as destroying any unit.

**Rebel Victory:** The Rebel player wins if the fifth Transport Away! Event Card is drawn before the Imperial player destroys the Shield Generator. Alternatively, the Rebel player wins if he destroys all Imperial forces currently on the map.

There is no point scoring; the game ends immediately when one victory condition is met.

## Special Rules & Edge Cases

**Event Cards:** Two Action Cards say "Draw Event Card." When drawn, reveal the top Event Card (never reshuffled). There are three types:
- **Reinforcements:** Place listed units in the indicated Reinforcement Sector or Base Box immediately. Imperial Troopers arriving with a Walker go in that Walker's Carried Troops box.
- **Transports Away!:** A Rebel Transport escapes. If the fifth such card is drawn before the Shield Generator is destroyed, the Rebel player wins.
- **Special Effects:** Unusual occurrences (Force events, Combat Repair Droids fixing Walkers, etc.) with instructions on the card.

**Walker damage (Damage Dice):** When a Walker is hit, roll 2 damage dice and add results:
- 2–3: Blaster hit. First hit reduces fire strength by 3; second hit destroys the blasters entirely (can no longer fire). Subsequent blaster hits are treated as body hits.
- 4–9: Body hit. First hit: place Body Damage marker. Second hit: additional marker; remove all carried Snowtroopers (destroyed); Walker may no longer mount Snowtroopers. Third body hit: Walker destroyed.
- 10–11: Leg hit. First hit reduces movement by 2 MPs. Second hit immobilizes the Walker (cannot move or turn). Subsequent leg hits are body hits.
- 12: Critical hit. Walker (and all carried Snowtroopers) is destroyed immediately.

**Snowspeeder damage (Damage Dice):** When a Snowspeeder is hit, roll 2 damage dice:
- 2–4: Gunner killed. Reduces Speeder's fire strength by 2 permanently; Speeder may no longer use Harpoons. Subsequent gunner hits are body hits.
- 5–10: Body hit. Second body hit destroys the Snowspeeder.
- 11–12: Critical hit. Snowspeeder destroyed immediately.

**Luke Skywalker and Force Points:** Luke begins with 10 Force Points tracked on the Force Points Track. He may spend Force Points in three ways (declared after opponent announces firing, before dice are rolled unless noted):
- **Increase fire strength:** Spend 1 point per +1 fire strength for one fire action (including harpoon).
- **Increase armor strength:** Spend 1 point per +1 armor strength against all enemy fire in the current Action Round.
- **Reduce a critical hit:** Spend 1 Force Point to reduce a critical hit to a body hit.
When all 10 Force Points are spent, Luke may no longer use The Force.

**Walker transport of Snowtroopers:** A Walker carries up to 3 Snowtroopers with no effect on movement or combat. Snowtroopers pay 1 MP to mount or dismount. Snowtroopers aboard Walkers may not fire or be fired at.

**Walker movement rules:** Walkers must always face a specific hexside and may only move into the hex directly in front of them. Turning costs 1 MP per hexside. A Walker may not move through another Walker. Moving a Walker through a Trooper, Tower, or AT-ST destroys that unit; moving through a Snowspeeder displaces it to an adjacent empty hex of the Rebel player's choice at no MP cost.

**Rebel Base (tunnel system):** Rebel Troopers only may use the tunnel system. A Trooper pays 1 extra MP to enter a Base entry hex and move into the Base Box (and may move no further that Action Round). It costs 1 MP to leave the Base Box to any Base entry hex; the Trooper may then continue with remaining MPs. A Trooper may not enter or leave a Base entry hex occupied by an Imperial unit.

**Firing restrictions:** Rebel Troopers in the Base Box and Snowtroopers aboard Walkers may not fire or be fired at. Snowspeeders in cliff hexes may neither fire nor be fired at. No unit is required to fire.

**Impossible shots:** If the target's armor strength exceeds the firer's modified fire strength, the shot cannot possibly hit and is not attempted.
